      <p><b>Haiti</b> (French: <i>Haïti</i>; <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Creole_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Haitian Creole language">Haitian Creole</a>: <i>Ayiti</i>), officially the <b>Republic of Haiti</b> (French: <i>République d'Haïti</i>; Haitian <a href="/wiki/Creole" class="mw-redirect" title="Creole">Creole</a>: <i>Repiblik d Ayiti</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>. The other country on the island is the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. Haiti has two <a href="/wiki/Official" title="Official">official</a> <a href="/wiki/Languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Languages">languages</a>: French and <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Creole_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Haitian Creole language">Haitian Creole</a>, or "<i>Kreyol</i>", which is a simple version of French mixed with African languages. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>. </p>
      <p>Haiti has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climate</a>. In French, the country is called <i>"La Perle des Antilles"</i> (The pearl of the Antilles), because of its natural beauty. There are many <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a> in Haiti. The country used to be covered with <a href="/wiki/Forests" class="mw-redirect" title="Forests">forests</a>. However, it no longer is, because of <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poorest</a> country in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>. </p>
      <p>Haiti is divided into ten <i>departements</i>. The main religion is <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholicism</a>. However, many Haitians also practice <a href="/wiki/Voodoo" title="Voodoo">Voodoo</a>. This is a religion which came from African folk beliefs in <a href="/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a>. Haiti has many holidays; the largest and most important is the <i>Mardi Gras</i>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Haiti" title="Geography of Haiti">Geography of Haiti</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Haiti has a total area of 27,750 <a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">km²</a>. Most of it is in the western third of the Hispaniola island. There are also smaller islands near the Haitian coast, like <i><a href="/wiki/Gona%C3%AFves" title="Gonaïves">Gonâve</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tortuga_(Haiti)" title="Tortuga (Haiti)">Île de la Tortue</a></i>, <i>Les Cayemites</i>, <i>Île-à-Vache</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Navassa_Island" title="Navassa Island">La Navase</a></i>. </p>
      <p>Haiti has many mountains. There are only some coastal <a href="/wiki/Plain" title="Plain">plains</a> and few <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valleys</a>. The largest valley is the <i>Cul-de-Sac</i>. Port-au-Prince is in the western end of this valley. The country's main river is the <a href="/wiki/Artibonite_River" title="Artibonite River">Artibonite</a>, which is also the longest in Hispaniola. Haiti's biggest city is <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>, with more than 3 million people in its <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">metropolitan area</a>. The second largest city is <a href="/wiki/Cap-Ha%C3%AFtien" title="Cap-Haïtien">Cap-Haïtien</a>. </p>
      <p>Haiti has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical">tropical</a> climate. The <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">rainy</a> <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">season</a> lasts from April to June, and from October to November. <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical cyclones">Hurricanes</a> are common during summer. In the past, hurricanes have caused a lot of damage and killed many people. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Taino" class="mw-redirect" title="Taino">Taino</a> people were a tribe of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arawak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arawak (not yet started)">Arawak</a> Amerindians. They lived on the island of Hispaniola before <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> found the island and started a European <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> there. Columbus found the island of Hispaniola on his first trip to the Americas.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Within twenty-five years after Columbus arrived, all of the Arawaks had been killed by Spanish <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">conquistadors</a>. </p>
      <p>In the early 17th century, the French set up a colony on Hispaniola. In 1697, Spain sold the western third of the island - Haiti - to the French. The French colony<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> was based on <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forestry</a> and making <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>. It became one of the wealthiest in the Caribbean. However, to do this, the colony brought many <a href="/wiki/Slave" class="mw-redirect" title="Slave">slaves</a> over from Africa and destroyed much of the environment. </p>
      <p>By the late 18th century, there were nearly half a million slaves in Haiti. They revolted, led by <a href="/wiki/Toussaint_L%27ouverture" title="Toussaint L'ouverture">Toussaint L'Ouverture.</a> After a hard and bloody struggle, they won their <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a>. In 1804, Haiti became the first independent black <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Today there are many monuments in Haiti remembering the Haitian Revolution. One of the largest is <i>La Citadelle Laferriere</i>. </p>
      <p>On January 12, 2010, in the afternoon of a Tuesday, <a href="/wiki/2010_Haiti_Earthquake" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Haiti Earthquake">Haiti was struck</a> by a <a href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude_scale" title="Moment magnitude scale">magnitude</a>-7.0 <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a>. This was the worst earthquake to hit the country in the past 200 years.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The quake's <a href="/wiki/Epicenter" title="Epicenter">epicenter</a> was just outside the Haitian capital of <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The quake caused major damage to Port-au-Prince and nearby areas. Over 200,000 people were thought to have been killed, but it was hard to be sure because many people were buried in mass graves before they could be identified.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Departments">Departments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Departments">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Departments">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Haiti" title="Departments of Haiti">Departments of Haiti</a>

These departments are further divided into 41 <i>arrondissements</i>, and 133 <i>communes</i>. These are the second and third level units of <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">administration</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Republic of Haiti is divided into 10 departments, but the central government has control over most political affairs. The president is head of state and is normally elected by popular vote for a five-year term. The president cannot serve consecutive terms. However, because of inconclusive election results in 2015, Haiti's current interim president was elected by the legislature. The prime minister is head of government and is appointed by the president and confirmed by the bicameral National Assembly. The National Assembly's upper house is the 30-seat Senate, and the lower house is the 118-seat Chamber of Deputies. Members of both houses are elected through a majoritarian system. Senators serve six-year terms and deputies serve four-year terms. There have been efforts toward constitutional reform to ensure that more women are represented in politics on the national level, but these reforms have not yet resulted in concrete changes. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays">Holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Holidays">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Holidays">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>Official holidays</b> (on the same day every year) </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>1 January: Jour de l'Indépendance ("Independence Day")</li> 
       <li>2 January: Jour des Aïeux ("Ancestors' Day")</li> 
       <li>1 May: Fête de l'Agriculture et du Travail ("Agriculture and Labor Day")</li> 
       <li>18 May: Fête du Drapeau et de l'Université ("Flag and University Day")</li> 
       <li>17 October: Anniversary of the Death of Jean-Jacques Dessalines</li> 
       <li>1 November: All Saint's Day</li> 
       <li>2 November: All Soul's Day</li> 
       <li>18 November: Battle of Vertières' Day and Armed Forces Day</li> 
       <li>25 December: Christmas Day</li>
      </ul> 
      <p><b>Traditional and religious holidays</b> (dates vary according to the liturgical calendar of the Catholic Church) </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Carnival (Only Mardi Gras is an official holiday)</li> 
       <li>Good Friday</li> 
       <li>Corpus Christi</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Bas-Ravine.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Bas-Ravine.jpg/220px-Bas-Ravine.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Bas-Ravine.jpg/330px-Bas-Ravine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Bas-Ravine.jpg/440px-Bas-Ravine.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Bas-Ravine.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Bas-Ravine, in the northern part of <a href="/wiki/Cap-Ha%C3%AFtien" title="Cap-Haïtien">Cap-Haïtien</a>.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>Haiti is the least developed country in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>. It is also one of the least developed and poorest countries in the world. </p>
      <p>There are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_indicator&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Economic indicator (not yet started)">indicators</a> that can be used to compare social and <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economic</a> situations between countries. Some indicators show that Haiti has fallen behind other poor <a href="/wiki/Developing_country" title="Developing country">developing countries</a> since the 1980s. In 2006, Haiti ranked 146th of 177 countries in the United Nations <a href="/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a> (2006). About 90% of the Haitian people were living in poverty in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-14">[14]</a></sup> Haiti is the only country in the Americas on the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> list of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Least_Developed_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Least Developed Countries (not yet started)">Least Developed Countries</a> and it is the poorest country in the Americas. The economy was staying even or falling behind even before their big earthquake. </p>
      <p>About 66% of all Haitians work in <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>. Most of them do small-scale <a href="/wiki/Subsistence_agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Subsistence agriculture">subsistence farming</a><sup id="cite_ref-Haiti_in_CIA_World_Factbook_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Haiti_in_CIA_World_Factbook-15">[15]</a></sup> (meaning that they are able to grow just enough to survive). This does not bring in much money. </p>
      <p>Very few jobs were created in the last ten years. However, the <a href="/wiki/Informal_economy" title="Informal economy">informal economy</a> is growing. <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">Mangoes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> are two of Haiti's most important <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exports</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Haiti_in_CIA_World_Factbook_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Haiti_in_CIA_World_Factbook-15">[15]</a></sup> Haiti has consistently ranked among the most <a href="/wiki/Corruption" title="Corruption">corrupt</a> countries in the world on the <a href="/wiki/Corruption_Perceptions_Index" title="Corruption Perceptions Index">Corruption Perceptions Index</a>. </p>
      <p>About one third of the national government's budget is money given to them by other countries. The United States gives the most money. Canada gives the second largest amount of money. The <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, Venezuela and Cuba also give and help Haiti's economy in different ways. Haiti has renewed its <a href="/wiki/Alliance" title="Alliance">alliances</a> with Venezuela and Cuba in 2006 and 2007. </p>
      <p>From 2001-2004, the United States stopped giving aid to Haiti completely. The aid was cut off after Haiti's 2000 election. The election's results were questioned, and President <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aristide&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aristide (not yet started)">Aristide</a> was accused of cheating to win the election. Aristide was overthrown in 2004. After that, the United States started giving aid to Haiti again. The United Nations led a peacekeeping operation called the <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Stabilization_Mission_in_Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="United Nations Stabilization Mission in Haiti (not yet started)">United Nations Stabilization Mission in Haiti</a>. (The Mission is called called MINUSTAH in Haiti; this is an <a href="/wiki/Acronym" title="Acronym">acronym</a> for the Mission's name in French). The Brazilian army led the peacekeeping operation. </p>
      <p>Even after President Aristide was overthrown, corruption continued to be very common in Haiti.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Haiti has a large amount of foreign debt (money owed to other countries and international institutions to repay loans). The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heavily_Indebted_Poor_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Heavily Indebted Poor Countries (not yet started)">Heavily Indebted Poor Countries</a> (HIPC) program planned to forgive about $525 million of Haiti's debt by mid-2009.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haiti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of Haiti (the number of people living in the country) was about 10,090,190. Figures from the DNA Nationwide Studies Institute say that the <a href="/wiki/Race" class="mw-disambig" title="Race">racial</a> makeup of the population is: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>94.2%: Black (Descendants mainly from the Fon, Ewe, Yoruba, and Bantu tribes of West/Central Africa)</li> 
       <li>5.4%: <a href="/wiki/Mulatto" title="Mulatto">Mulatto</a> (European and African descent).</li> 
       <li>0.4%: White (mainly of French, Polish, and Arab origin)</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Some East Asians also live in the country. </p>
