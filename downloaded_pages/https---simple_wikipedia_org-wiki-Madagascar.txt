      <p><b>Madagascar</b> is a large island nation in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>. It is off of the east coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Twenty-two million people live there; its capital is <a href="/wiki/Antananarivo" title="Antananarivo">Antananarivo</a>. It is the world's fourth largest island.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The official languages are Malagasy and French. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Geologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Geologist">Geologists</a> think that about two million years ago, Madagascar was a part of a big landmass that included what is now the continent of Africa, but it broke off. Madagascar would later break off of the <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> subcontinent.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madagascar&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Environment">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madagascar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Environment">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Madagascar is home to many species that were not known about until around 1679 when Dutch explorers went there. They do not even exist elsewhere in Africa. They only exist in Madagascar. In fact, most of the <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a> living in Madagascar do not live anywhere else in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, many of the <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> in Madagascar are in danger because many of the forests have been cut down.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> A big reason that forests have been cut down is so that land can be used to grow crops such as <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, which is one of the most important crops that is grown in Madagascar. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madagascar&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madagascar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">Agriculture</a> is a big part of the <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> in Madagascar, including the growing of coffee and <a href="/wiki/Vanilla" title="Vanilla">vanilla</a>. Madagascar sells more vanilla than any other country in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Madagascar also makes money from <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madagascar&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madagascar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>People have probably lived in Madagascar for at least 2000 years.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> took over the city of Antananarivo in 1895, and added Madagascar as a colony two years later.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Madagascar became independent from France, which meant it became its own country, on 26 June, 1960. On March 17, 2009, President Marc Ravalomanana quit because of pressure from the <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a>. Andry Raejolina became the next president.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
