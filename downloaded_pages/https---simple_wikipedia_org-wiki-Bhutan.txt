      <p><b>Bhutan</b> (officially called <b>Kingdom of Bhutan</b>) is a small country in the <a href="/wiki/Himalaya" class="mw-redirect" title="Himalaya">Himalaya</a> mountains of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. It is ruled by King <a href="/wiki/Jigme_Khesar_Namgyel_Wangchuck" title="Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck">Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck</a>, who has been king since 2006. Bhutan was founded in 1644 by <a href="/wiki/Shabdrung_Ngawang_Namgyel" title="Shabdrung Ngawang Namgyel">Shabdrung Ngawang Namgyel</a>. The Bhutanese people are proud that they have always been an <a href="/wiki/Independent" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent">independent</a> country. Bhutan's capital city is <a href="/wiki/Thimphu" title="Thimphu">Thimphu</a>. The official language is <a href="/wiki/Dzongkha" title="Dzongkha">Dzongkha</a>. </p>
      <p>About 700,000 people live in Bhutan. The people and government of Bhutan are proud of their culture which is based on <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Buddhism" title="Tibetan Buddhism">Tibetan Buddhism</a>. 97% of Bhutan's people are Buddhist. </p>
      <p>Until 1974, Bhutan was closed to the outside world. Now people can visit the country, but only in small numbers. The only airport is in Paro district. The country is bordered on the south by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of India">Republic of India</a> and on the north by <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The Indian state of Sikkim separates Bhutan and <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>. The main export of Bhutan is <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydroelectricity</a> which is sold to India. The <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> of Bhutan is very small but is growing quickly. The currency is the <a href="/wiki/Ngultrum" title="Ngultrum">Ngultrum</a>, which is pegged at par with the Indian <a href="/wiki/Rupee" title="Rupee">rupee</a>. </p>
      <p>Bhutan is the only carbon-neutral and carbon-negative country in the entire world. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhutan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhutan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Royal Bhutan Army is Bhutan's military service. It includes the Royal Bodyguard and the Royal Bhutan Police. Membership is voluntary, and the minimum age for recruitment is 18. The standing army numbers about 16,000 and is trained by the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Army" title="Indian Army">Indian Army</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Being a <a href="/wiki/Landlocked_country" title="Landlocked country">landlocked country</a>, Bhutan has no navy. It also has no air force or army aviation corps. The Army relies on Eastern Air Command of the Indian Air Force for air assistance. </p>

      <p>Bhutan's national sport is <a href="/wiki/Archery" title="Archery">archery</a>. Competitions are held regularly in most villages. <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a> has gained popularity in Bhutan, particularly since the introduction of television channels from India. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhutan_national_cricket_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bhutan national cricket team (not yet started)">Bhutan national cricket team</a> is one of the more successful affiliate nations in the region. <a href="/wiki/Association_Football" class="mw-redirect" title="Association Football">Football</a> is also an increasingly popular sport. </p>
