      <p>The <b>Republic of Serbia</b> (Serbian: <i>Република Србија</i> / Republika Srbija), or usually just <b>Serbia</b> (Serbian: <i>Србија</i> / Srbija), is a country in Central Europe. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>. To the north of Serbia is the country <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. To the east of Serbia are the countries <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>. To the south of Serbia are countries <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Macedonia">North Macedonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>. To the west of Serbia are the countries <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> (the country that was once united with Serbia), <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The oldest form of writing comes from an ancient civilization which inhabited Serbia called the Vinča culture and the symbols are called the Vinča script.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Serbia is also home to the earliest known copper smelting sites and the birth of the Copper Age which lead humanity out of the Stone Age and into regular use of metallurgy.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The same culture contains the earliest evidence of tin alloy bronze which replaced the much weaker arsenic bronze.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medieval_history">Medieval history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Medieval history">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Medieval history">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>White Serbs, an early <a href="/wiki/Slavs" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavs">Slavic</a> tribe from Northern Europe, came to Northern Greece in the 6th century. By the 8th century they had created the Serbian Principality, a Serbian country, in the Balkans.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Serbs became Christian around the 10th century. For 200 years, the Nemanjić dynasty ruled. They made Serbia a kingdom, built new towns, monasteries, and forts, and made Serbia bigger. In 1371 the Nemanjic Dynasty died out. Serbia became unsafe and local leaders fought each other for control. In 1389 the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> invaded Serbia. They fought against Serbia, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kingdom_of_Bosnia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kingdom of Bosnia (not yet started)">Bosnia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights Hospitaller</a>, and the forces of many other local leaders, and won. The Turks fought the Serbs for 70 years until in 1459 the Ottoman Turks conquered Serbia.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ottoman_period">Ottoman period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Ottoman period">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Ottoman period">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Once the Ottomans conquered Serbia, they got rid of the Serbian upper class. Most Serbs worked as farmers on land owned by Turks. They had to pay high taxes to the Turks. Some Serbs were forced to become <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Muslims</a>. But the Serbs had their own laws in the Ottoman Empire through the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Millet_system&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Millet system (not yet started)">millet system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERunciman1968204_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERunciman1968204-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKia2011115_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKia2011115-12">[12]</a></sup> During the 19th century Serbia gradually became independent. During World War I Austria conquered Serbia but lost the bigger war. Serbia joined other Balkan countries to form <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Serbia is found in the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkan peninsula</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pannonian_Plain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pannonian Plain (not yet started)">Pannonian Plain</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> passes through Serbia. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0ar_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Šar Mountains (not yet started)">Šar Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a> form the border with <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>. Over 31% of Serbia is covered by forest.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">National parks</a> take up 10% of the country's territory.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Serbia has 5 <a href="/wiki/National_parks" class="mw-redirect" title="National parks">national parks</a> and 22 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nature_reserves&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nature reserves (not yet started)">nature reserves</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Serbia is classed as an upper-middle income economy.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The major processed vegetable crops in Serbia are <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potatoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tomato" title="Tomato">tomatoes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_pepper" title="Black pepper">pepper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Serbia is one of the biggest world producers and exporters of <a href="/wiki/Raspberry" title="Raspberry">raspberries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> They are a leading exporter of frozen fruit.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are five international airports in Serbia: <a href="/wiki/Belgrade_Nikola_Tesla_Airport" title="Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport">Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ni%C5%A1_Constantine_the_Great_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Niš Constantine the Great Airport (not yet started)">Niš Constantine the Great Airport</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Morava_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Morava International Airport (not yet started)">Morava International Airport</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vr%C5%A1ac_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vršac International Airport (not yet started)">Vršac International Airport</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pristina_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pristina International Airport (not yet started)">Pristina International Airport</a>. </p>
      <p>89% of households in Serbia have fixed telephone lines. There are over 9.60&nbsp;million cell-phones users. This is larger than the number of the total population of Serbia itself by 30%. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Serbia has a total of eight sites on the <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">UNESCO World Heritage list</a>: The Early Medieval capital <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stari_Ras&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stari Ras (not yet started)">Stari Ras</a> and the 13th-century monastery <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sopo%C4%87ani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sopoćani (not yet started)">Sopoćani</a>, and the 12th-century monastery <a href="/w/index.php?title=Studenica_monastery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Studenica monastery (not yet started)">Studenica</a>, and the endangered <a href="/w/index.php?title=Medieval_Monuments_in_Kosovo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Medieval Monuments in Kosovo (not yet started)">Medieval Monuments in Kosovo</a> group, comprising the monasteries of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Visoki_De%C4%8Dani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Visoki Dečani (not yet started)">Visoki Dečani</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Our_Lady_of_Ljevi%C5%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Our Lady of Ljeviš (not yet started)">Our Lady of Ljeviš</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gra%C4%8Danica_Monastery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gračanica Monastery (not yet started)">Gračanica</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Patriarchate_of_Pe%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Patriarchate of Peć (not yet started)">Patriarchate of Peć</a> (former seat of the Serbian Church, mausoleum of Serbian royalty) and finally the Roman estate of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamzigrad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gamzigrad (not yet started)">Gamzigrad–Felix Romuliana</a>. There are two literary memorials on UNESCO's <a href="/wiki/Memory_of_the_World_Programme" title="Memory of the World Programme">Memory of the World Programme</a>: The 12th-century <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miroslav_Gospel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Miroslav Gospel (not yet started)">Miroslav Gospel</a>, and scientist <a href="/wiki/Nikola_Tesla" title="Nikola Tesla">Nikola Tesla</a>'s valuable archive. </p>
      <p>The most prominent museum in Serbia is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Museum_of_Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Museum of Serbia (not yet started)">National Museum of Serbia</a>. It was founded in 1844. It houses a collection of more than 400,000 exhibits, over 5,600 paintings and 8,400 drawings and prints, and includes many foreign masterpiece collections, including Miroslav Gospel. </p>
      <p>The official language, <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>, is written in both the <a href="/wiki/Cyrillic" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyrillic">Cyrillic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_alphabet" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin alphabet">Latin alphabets</a>. </p>
      <p>Composer and <a href="/wiki/Musicology" title="Musicology">musicologist</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stevan_Stojanovi%C4%87_Mokranjac&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stevan Stojanović Mokranjac (not yet started)">Stevan Stojanović Mokranjac</a> is said to be one of the most important founders of modern Serbian music.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the 1990s and the 2000s, many <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a> performers rose to fame. Željko Joksimović won second place at the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>. Marija Šerifović won the 2007 Eurovision Song Contest. Serbia was the host of the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2008" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2008">Eurovision Song Contest 2008</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The most popular sports in Serbia are <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>, basketball, volleyball, <a href="/wiki/Team_handball" class="mw-redirect" title="Team handball">handball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_polo" title="Water polo">water polo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a>. </p>
      <p>The three main football clubs in Serbia are <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star</a> and <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" class="mw-redirect" title="FK Partizan">Partizan</a>, both from the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=FK_Vojvodina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="FK Vojvodina (not yet started)">Vojvodina</a> from <a href="/wiki/Novi_Sad" title="Novi Sad">Novi Sad</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Novak_Djokovic" title="Novak Djokovic">Novak Djokovic</a>, a multiple Grand Slam-winning <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> player and current number one, is from Serbia. Other tennis players from Serbia include <a href="/wiki/Ana_Ivanovic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ana Ivanovic">Ana Ivanovic</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jelena_Jankovic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jelena Jankovic (not yet started)">Jelena Jankovic</a>. </p>
