      <p>The <b>Republic of Equatorial Guinea</b> is a nation in west <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">central Africa</a>, and one of the smallest countries in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It borders <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> on the north, <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a> on the south and east, and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> on the west. In the past, it was the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_Guinea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spanish Guinea (not yet started)">Spanish Guinea</a>. The country's territory is both on the continent and on islands. The continental part is known as <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Muni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Río Muni (not yet started)">Río Muni</a>. On one island, <a href="/wiki/Bioko" title="Bioko">Bioko</a>, is the capital, <a href="/wiki/Malabo" title="Malabo">Malabo</a>. </p>
      <p>Equatorial Guinea is the only country in Africa where <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> is an official language. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provinces">Provinces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equatorial_Guinea&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Provinces">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equatorial_Guinea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Provinces">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Equatorial Guinea is divided into seven <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a> (capitals appear in parentheses): </p> 
      <ol>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Annob%C3%B3n" class="mw-redirect" title="Annobón">Annobón</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Antonio_de_Pal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Antonio de Palé (not yet started)">San Antonio de Palé</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Bioko_Norte" title="Bioko Norte">Bioko Norte</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebola,_Equatorial_Guinea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rebola, Equatorial Guinea (not yet started)">Rebola</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Bioko_Sur" title="Bioko Sur">Bioko Sur</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Luba,_Equatorial_Guinea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Luba, Equatorial Guinea (not yet started)">Luba</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Centro_Sur" title="Centro Sur">Centro Sur</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Evinayong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Evinayong (not yet started)">Evinayong</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Ki%C3%A9-Ntem" title="Kié-Ntem">Kié-Ntem</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ebebiy%C3%ADn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ebebiyín (not yet started)">Ebebiyín</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Litoral_(Equatorial_Guinea)" title="Litoral (Equatorial Guinea)">Litoral</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Bata,_Equatorial_Guinea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bata, Equatorial Guinea (not yet started)">Bata</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wele-Nzas" title="Wele-Nzas">Wele-Nzas</a> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongomo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mongomo (not yet started)">Mongomo</a>)</li>
      </ol> 
      <p>The provinces are further divided into districts. </p>
