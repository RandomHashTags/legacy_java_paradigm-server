      <p><b>Uzbekistan</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and largest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Tashkent" title="Tashkent">Tashkent</a>. There are about 32 million people living in Uzbekistan, now. The neighbouring countries are <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>. It is a doubly <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> country, of which there are <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">only two</a> in the world. Most people in Uzbekistan speak a language called <a href="/wiki/Uzbek_language" title="Uzbek language">Uzbek</a>, a Turkic language similar to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uyghur&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Uyghur (not yet started)">Uyghur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>. In the Uzbek language, Uzbekistan is called "O‘zbekiston" and it means "the land of the true nobles". Uzbekistan has a long history. Humans first lived in Uzbekistan from before the 2nd <a href="/wiki/Millennium" title="Millennium">millennium</a> BC.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The current president today is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shavkat_Mirziyoyev&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shavkat Mirziyoyev (not yet started)">Shavkat Mirziyoyev</a>. Human rights in Uzbekistan are 'atrocious' according to <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.hrw.org/europecentral-asia/uzbekistan">Human Rights Watch</a> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> is its largest religion. </p>
