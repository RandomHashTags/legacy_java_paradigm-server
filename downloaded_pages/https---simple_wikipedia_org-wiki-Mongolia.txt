      <p><b>Mongolia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> between <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. </p>
      <p>Mongolia is a <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> country in <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. It borders Russia to the north and the People's Republic of China to the south, east and west. Mongolia's <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">political system</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliamentary</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. </p>
      <p>Until recently, most of the people there were <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhists</a>. Many of them are <a href="/wiki/Nomads" class="mw-redirect" title="Nomads">nomads</a> (people who always move from place to place and do not stay in one home), but this is changing. The largest city, and <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Ulaanbaatar" class="mw-redirect" title="Ulaanbaatar">Ulaanbaatar</a>. It is where about 38% of the population live. This has been spelled <i>Ulan Bator/ Ulaan Battor</i> and other ways in the past. The north and east parts of the country have many mountains. Part of the south part is the <a href="/wiki/Gobi_Desert" title="Gobi Desert">Gobi Desert</a>. There are 2,791,272 people living in Mongolia. The country is the 18th biggest country in the world, with an area of 1,564,116&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (603,909&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). </p>
      <p>Mongolia is mainly rural with the lowest <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of all independent countries in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-unpop_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unpop-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[note 1]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The area which is Mongolia has been ruled by various nomadic empires. This was until the great '<a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a>' was founded by <a href="/wiki/Genghis_Khan" title="Genghis Khan">Genghis Khan</a> in 1206. After the <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan Dynasty</a> collapsed, the Mongols became nomads again. After the 16th century, Mongolia were influenced by <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibetan</a> <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>. By the end of the 17th century, most of Mongolia was part of an area ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a>. When the Qing Dynasty collapsed in <a href="/wiki/Xinhai_Revolution" title="Xinhai Revolution">1911</a>, Mongolia declared independence. But they had to fight against the Chinese. They were helped by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. In 1921, the world accepted its independence. Shortly after the death of Bogd Khaan, the last monarch of Mongolia the monarchy was replaced by a communist government in 1924, and the country was renamed the Mongolian People's Republic. Up until the fall of the Soviet Union, Mongolia was a <a href="/wiki/Satellite_state" title="Satellite state">satellite state</a> for the Soviets. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolian_Red_Cross_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mongolian Red Cross Society (not yet started)">Mongolian Red Cross Society</a> was set up in 1939. It has its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Ulan_Bator" title="Ulan Bator">Ulaanbaator</a>. Following the break-up of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> in 1991, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>'s interest in Mongolia has declined. <a href="/wiki/PRC" class="mw-redirect" title="PRC">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> are currently Mongolia's main trade and political partners. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Mongolia is a&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliamentary</a>&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. People vote for their&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a>. The&nbsp;President of Mongolia&nbsp;is elected to a four-year term, and cannot be elected President more than twice. The current&nbsp;President&nbsp;is&nbsp;Khaltmaagiin Battulga. He was first elected as President on July 7, 2017. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p> Mongolia has a <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppe</a> climate. It has very cold winters and mild summers. Recently, winters have become very cold. This has killed many people and cattle. On June 2, 2008, 52 people and 200,000 head of <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a> died in heavy <a href="/wiki/Blizzard" title="Blizzard">blizzards</a> in Mongolia.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On March 1–2, 2008, a heavy <a href="/wiki/Dust_storm" title="Dust storm">dust storm</a> hit northeast <a href="/wiki/PRC" class="mw-redirect" title="PRC">China</a> and parts of Mongolia. and ended over <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> on the March 4 .<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Twenty one people died in a rural Mongolian blizzard on May 8, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup> Parts of the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> were also affected on the May 8, 2008. </p>
      <p>On June 2, 2009, it was said that 15 people and 10,000 head of <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a> had died by this date in Mongolia. </p>
      <p>April 1–2, 2010 had temperatures plummet to -50 degrees in Mongolia’s <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuul_valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tuul valley (not yet started)">Tuul valley</a>, A nomad named Urna said she bought 400 bundles of grass to feed the animals in preparation for more bad weather. The Mongolian Red Cross has said that about 4,500,000 livestock died as a result of the bad weather this year [2010].<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dw-world.de_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dw-world.de-21">[20]</a></sup> Tume, who lives in <a href="/wiki/Ulan_Bator" title="Ulan Bator">Ulan Bator</a>, said that he had noticed that there were several really harsh winters in a row too. He blamed <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, but experts said that overgrazing by cattle had also killed off most of the country’s grassland.<sup id="cite_ref-dw-world.de_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dw-world.de-21">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Mongolia known as the "Land of the Eternal Blue Sky" or "Country of Blue Sky" (<a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a>: <span lang="mn">Mönkh khökh tengeriin oron - Мөнх хөх тэнгэрийн орон</span>) because it has over 250 sunny days a year.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Mongolia is the world's 19th-largest country (after <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>). It is significantly larger than the next-largest country,&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. Mongolia does not share a border with&nbsp;Kazakhstan but its westernmost point is only 38&nbsp;kilometers (24&nbsp;mi) away from <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>. </p>
      <p>The geography of Mongolia is varied, with the&nbsp;Gobi <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">Desert</a>&nbsp;to the south and with cold and mountainous regions to the north and west. Most of Mongolia consists of&nbsp;steppes, with forested areas comprising 11.2% of the total land area.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup> &nbsp;The highest point in Mongolia is the&nbsp;Khüiten Peak&nbsp;in the&nbsp;Tavan bogd&nbsp;massif, at a height of 4,374&nbsp;m (14,350&nbsp;ft). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Provinces">Provinces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Provinces">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Provinces">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Mongolia is divided into 21 <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a> called <i>aimags</i>. The <i>aimags</i> are divided into 329 districts called <i>sums</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Languages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Languages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The official language of Mongolia is&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a>, and is spoken by 95% of the population. A variety of&nbsp;dialects&nbsp;of&nbsp;<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oirat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Oirat (not yet started)">Oirat</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="/w/index.php?title=Buryatare&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Buryatare (not yet started)">Buryatare</a> are spoken across the country. <a href="/wiki/Turkic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Turkic language">Turkic languages</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Kazakh" class="mw-redirect" title="Kazakh">Kazakh</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuvan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tuvan (not yet started)">Tuvan</a>, are also spoken in the western part of the country. </p>
      <p>Today, Mongolian is written using the&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Cyrillic_alphabet" title="Cyrillic alphabet">Cyrillic alphabet</a>, although in the past it was written using the&nbsp;Mongolian <a href="/wiki/Writing_system" title="Writing system">script</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Traditional" class="mw-redirect" title="Traditional">traditional</a> <a href="/wiki/Alphabet" title="Alphabet">alphabet</a> is being slowly reintroduced through schools recently.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[27]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Russian&nbsp;is the most frequently spoken foreign language in Mongolia due to their diplomatic ties as former <a href="/wiki/Communist_state" title="Communist state">communist states</a>. However English has been gradually replacing Russian as the second language in order to become part of the world economy.&nbsp;Korean&nbsp;has gained popularity as tens of thousands of&nbsp;Mongolians work and study in South Korea. Interest in&nbsp;Chinese has been growing because of the neighboring power. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Swine_flu">Swine flu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Swine flu">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Swine flu">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Mongolia is currently free of bird and <a href="/wiki/Swine_flu" class="mw-redirect" title="Swine flu">swine flu</a>, but 103 air travelers who were suspected victims, and the plane crew of 6, were <a href="/wiki/Quarantine" title="Quarantine">quarantined</a> for 7 days in Ulaan Bator(Ulaanbaatar) in May 2009. </p>
      <p>It may have come over the border from the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>. </p>
