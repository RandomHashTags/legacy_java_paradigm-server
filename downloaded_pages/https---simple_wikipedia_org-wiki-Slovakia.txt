      <p><b>Slovakia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>: <i>Slovensko</i>) (Official name <b>The Slovak Republic</b>, <i>Slovenská republika</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> with no access to the ocean in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> in the southwest, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> in the south, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> in the east, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> in the north and <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> in the northwest. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a>, the only capital of the world that borders with other two countries. Other main cities are <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%A1ice" title="Košice">Košice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pre%C5%A1ov" title="Prešov">Prešov</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C5%BDilina" title="Žilina">Žilina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bansk%C3%A1_Bystrica" title="Banská Bystrica">Banská Bystrica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tren%C4%8D%C3%ADn" title="Trenčín">Trenčín</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nitra" title="Nitra">Nitra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trnava" title="Trnava">Trnava</a>. Slovakia has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> since 2004 and its official currency is the Euro. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovakia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovakia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Celt" class="mw-redirect" title="Celt">Celts</a> started to settle since 450&nbsp;BC. The coins named <i>Biatec</i> represent the first use of writing in Slovakia. At the turn of millenniums, many different Germanic tribes like <a href="/w/index.php?title=Quadi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Quadi (not yet started)">Quadi</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcomanni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marcomanni (not yet started)">Marcomanni</a> started to overtake the area. The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> established many outposts along the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> river. They even fought Germanic tribes, with the most northern presence being in <a href="/wiki/Tren%C4%8D%C3%ADn" title="Trenčín">Trenčín</a> (<i>Laugaritio</i>), during the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcomannic_Wars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marcomannic Wars (not yet started)">Marcomannic Wars</a>. </p>
      <p>Great Moravia was at first in constant quarrels with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Francia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eastern Francia (not yet started)">Eastern Francia</a>, and at the beginning of the 10th century, with the <a href="/wiki/Magyars" class="mw-redirect" title="Magyars">Magyars</a> who arrived from Asia. The Magyars conquered Great Moravia in 906, and established the Hungarian Principality, resulting later in the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a> in year 1000. Slovaks continued to live in the north, while most Hungarians were in the south. </p>
      <p>A huge population loss resulted from the <a href="/wiki/Mongol" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongol">Mongol</a> invasions in 1241. The Hungarian kings started to invite other settlers, for example Germans in the 13th century, and many others started to arrive in the kingdom, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vlachs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vlachs (not yet started)">Vlachs</a> from <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> in the 14th century and <a href="/wiki/Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Jews">Jews</a>. </p>
      <p>In the 16th century the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> occupied the southern part of the kingdom including the two most important towns, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Buda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Buda (not yet started)">Buda</a> (capital) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Szekesfeh%C3%A9rv%C3%A1r&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Szekesfehérvár (not yet started)">Székesfehérvár</a> (coronation capital). Many Hungarian nobles moved to Slovakia and the king moved to <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a> (<i>Pressburg</i>, <i>Pozsony</i> at that time). The wars against the Ottomans and an uprising against the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a>s caused much destruction. After the Ottomans started to <a href="/wiki/Retreat" class="mw-redirect" title="Retreat">retreat</a> back from Hungary, Bratislava continued to be capital until 1848, when it was moved back to <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>. </p>
      <p>The creation of a dual monarchy, <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a>, enabled the Hungarian government to initiate a policy of suppressing the teaching of languages other than the Hungarian language in state schools. The official use of other languages than Hungarian was also discouraged. During this time, a nationalist movement arose among Slovakians. A part of this movement joined forces with a part of the Czech nationalist movement. During World War I, this movement convinced the future victorious powers to recognise a new state of Czechoslovakia after the war. </p>
      <p>The territory of today's Slovakia was a part of <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> from 1918 to 1938 and again from 1945 until 1989. Czechoslovakia was one of the states which came into existence after the breakup of Austria-Hungary at end of World War I in 1918. Czechoslovakia split up in 1939, when Slovakia, under <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Hitler</a>'s influence and pressure, declared independence and became an independent state. The state existed during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. It was a one-party totalitarian state which was a puppet ally of Nazi Germany. Under the regime, the state fought in the war on the side of Nazi Germany and deported about 70,000 of its Jewish citizens to Nazi extermination camps as part of the Holocaust. </p>
      <p>The territory of Czechoslovakia was liberated by the Soviet Union at the end of World War II in 1945. After the liberation, Czechoslovakia was reunified and briefly existed as a democratic country. The Communist Party successfully performed a state coup in 1948 and ruled Czechoslovakia as a totalitarian one-party state which was a satellite of the Soviet Union. In 1989, the one-party rule of the Communist Party was overthrown during the <a href="/wiki/Velvet_Revolution" title="Velvet Revolution">Velvet Revolution</a>, which was a series of large and peaceful demonstrations by the citizens in the streets. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> again became a democratic country. However, it split up into two independent countries (Slovakia and <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>) on January 1, 1993 in what became known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Velvet_Divorce&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Velvet Divorce (not yet started)">Velvet Divorce</a>. Slovakia has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> since May 1, 2004. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovakia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovakia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Slovakia is <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a>. It is noted primarily for its mountainous nature, with the <a href="/wiki/Carpathian_Mountains" title="Carpathian Mountains">Carpathian Mountains</a> being in the north and various lowlands mostly in the south. The highest mountain range are the <a href="/wiki/Tatra_mountains" title="Tatra mountains">Tatra mountains</a> with the highest peak, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerlachov_Peak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gerlachov Peak (not yet started)">Gerlachov Peak</a> (Slovak: <i>Gerlachovský štít</i>; 2,654&nbsp;m). Major Slovak rivers besides the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> are the <a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A1h&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Váh (not yet started)">Váh</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hron&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hron (not yet started)">Hron</a>. </p>
      <p>At low altitudes, Slovakia's climate is <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">humid continental</a> (<i>Dfb</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Koeppen_climate_classification" class="mw-redirect" title="Koeppen climate classification">Koeppen climate classification</a>, with warm summers and cloudy, cold and humid winters. At high altitudes it is <a href="/wiki/Subarctic_climate" title="Subarctic climate">subarctic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alpine_climate" title="Alpine climate">alpine</a>. </p>

      <p>About 5.5 million people live in Slovakia.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Most people are <a href="/wiki/Slovaks" title="Slovaks">Slovak</a> (86%), but in southern regions of Slovakia live <a href="/wiki/Hungarians" class="mw-redirect" title="Hungarians">Hungarians</a> (10%), with some municipalities even being with the Hungarian majority (for example, <a href="/wiki/Kom%C3%A1rno" title="Komárno">Komárno</a> or <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunajsk%C3%A1_Streda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dunajská Streda (not yet started)">Dunajská Streda</a>), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruthenian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ruthenian language (not yet started)">Ruthenian</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a> are spoken in the northeast. A minority <a href="/wiki/Roma_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Roma people">Roma</a> population speak versions of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Romany&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Romany (not yet started)">Romany</a> language and are scattered mainly in the east. </p>
      <p>The Slovak constitution guarantees a freedom of religion. The majority of Slovaks are <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholics</a> (69%), the next are the <a href="/wiki/Atheists" class="mw-redirect" title="Atheists">atheists</a> (13%). Other religions include <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheranism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greek_Orthodox" class="mw-redirect" title="Greek Orthodox">Greek Orthodox</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calvinism" title="Calvinism">Calvinism</a>. There are about 5,000 <a href="/wiki/Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> in Slovakia and 2,000 <a href="/wiki/Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Jews">Jews</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovakia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Transport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovakia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Transport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Rail</b>: Slovakia has 3,662&nbsp;km of rails, of which 3,512&nbsp;km are in standard gauge. Rail network currently undergoes a modernisation.</li> 
       <li><b>Road</b>: Slovakia has 42,993&nbsp;km of roads, of which 316&nbsp;km are motorways (2003). The motorway network is currently under construction.</li> 
       <li><b>Air</b>: Slovakia has 6 international airports, with the most important ones being in <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%A1ice" title="Košice">Košice</a>, others are near <a href="/w/index.php?title=Slia%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sliač (not yet started)">Sliač</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poprad" title="Poprad">Poprad</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C5%BDilina" title="Žilina">Žilina</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pie%C5%A1%C5%A5any&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Piešťany (not yet started)">Piešťany</a>.</li> 
       <li><b>Marine</b>: Slovakia has no direct access to the sea. The most important waterway is the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>, with the ports in Bratislava and Komárno. The Danube and the Rhine-Main-Danube canal connects Slovakia to the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>.</li>
      </ul>
