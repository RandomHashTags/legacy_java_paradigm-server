<small>IPA:&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:IPA_for_Thai_and_Lao" class="extiw" title="en:WP:IPA for Thai and Lao">[râːt.tɕʰā ʔāːnāːtɕàk tʰāj]</a></span> <span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Th-pratheidthai_raachaanaajakthai.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20"></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/77/Th-pratheidthai_raachaanaajakthai.ogg" class="internal" title="Th-pratheidthai raachaanaajakthai.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span>), is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. Its neighbours are <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>. Its name was <b>Siam</b> until <a href="/wiki/June_24" title="June 24">June</a> <a href="/wiki/1939" title="1939">1939</a><sup id="cite_ref-ThaiCSM_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ThaiCSM-9">[9]</a></sup> and between 1945 and <a href="/wiki/May_11" title="May 11">May 11</a> <a href="/wiki/1949" title="1949">1949</a>. The word Thai (ไทย) comes from the ethnic group in the center of Thailand. </p>
      <p>Thailand is a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>. It has a <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">King</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>, who is <a href="/wiki/Vajiralongkorn" title="Vajiralongkorn">Vajiralongkorn</a> at the moment. </p>
      <p>Most people here (95%) follow the <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> called <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>. Smaller number of people (4.4%), mostly to the south, follow <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>. Other religions in Thailand are <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sikhism" title="Sikhism">Sikhism</a>. Some Muslims in the south have begun fighting the government because they want to be more <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thailand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thailand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A Buddhist kingdom named <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sukhothai_kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sukhothai kingdom (not yet started)">Sukhothai</a> was founded here in <a href="/wiki/1238" title="1238">1238</a>. </p>
      <p>A century later, a bigger kingdom named <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayuthaya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ayuthaya (not yet started)">Ayuthaya</a> appeared south of Sukhothai. Later Sukhothai became a part of Ayuthaya. Ayuthaya existed for more than 400 years before its fall by the attack of a <a href="/wiki/Burma" class="mw-redirect" title="Burma">Burmese</a> kingdom. </p>
      <p>A soldier of <a href="/wiki/Chinese" class="mw-disambig" title="Chinese">Chinese</a> origin then founded a new capital city at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thonburi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thonburi (not yet started)">Thonburi</a>, and became King <a href="/w/index.php?title=T%C4%81ksin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tāksin (not yet started)">Tāksin</a>. </p>
      <p>The current era, Rattanakosin, started on founding <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a> as the capital city by <a href="/w/index.php?title=King_Rama_I&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="King Rama I (not yet started)">King Rama I</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chakri_Dynasty&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chakri Dynasty (not yet started)">Chakri Dynasty</a>. </p>
      <p>Before <a href="/wiki/1932" title="1932">1932</a>, Thailand was an <a href="/wiki/Absolute_monarchy" title="Absolute monarchy">absolute monarchy</a>. On <a href="/wiki/June_24" title="June 24">June 24</a>, 1932, a group of people did a <a href="/wiki/Coup" class="mw-redirect" title="Coup">coup</a> and changed Thailand to a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>. It was not until <a href="/wiki/1973" title="1973">1973</a> that Thai people voted for a <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> in an <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">election</a>. There were <a href="/wiki/Coup" class="mw-redirect" title="Coup">coups</a> both before and after this year. </p>
      <p>In 1951, there was a failed coup - the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manhattan_Rebellion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manhattan Rebellion (not yet started)">Manhattan Rebellion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/September_19" title="September 19">September 19</a>, <a href="/wiki/2006" title="2006">2006</a>, the army did a coup d'état and took control from <a href="/wiki/Thaksin_Shinawatra" title="Thaksin Shinawatra">Thaksin Shinawatra</a>'s government. </p>
      <p>In May 2014, a new military coup d'état removed another government. </p>
      <p>In October <a href="/wiki/2016" title="2016">2016</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vajiralongkorn" title="Vajiralongkorn">Rama X</a> become the new king. </p>
      <p>Between 1932 and 2014, Thailand had 12 coup d'etats. </p>
