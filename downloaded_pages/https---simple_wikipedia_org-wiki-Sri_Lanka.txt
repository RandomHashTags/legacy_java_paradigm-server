      <p>The <b>Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka</b> (ශ්‍රී ලංකා in <a href="/wiki/Sinhala_language" title="Sinhala language">Sinhala</a>, இலங்கை in <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>) is a tropical <a href="/wiki/Island_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Island nation">island nation</a> off the <a href="/wiki/Southeast" title="Southeast">southeast</a> coast of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a>. The capital of the country is <a href="/wiki/Sri_Jayawardenepura_Kotte" title="Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte">Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte</a> while the largest city is <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">Colombo</a>. </p>
      <p>It was known as <b>Ceylon</b> before 1972, and <b>Serendib</b> and <b>Sinhale</b>, among other names, before that. It was South Asia's oldest <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_make-up">Ethnic make-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Ethnic make-up">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Ethnic make-up">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Sri Lanka has three main ethnic groups. They differ in their religions and origins, and there has often been difficulties between the groups. The total population of the island is over 20 million people, and rapidly expanding. </p>
      <p>The largest of the three groups is the population of <a href="/wiki/Sinhalese_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinhalese people">Sinhalese people</a>. Most of them are <a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a>. Their language is <a href="/wiki/Sinhala" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinhala">Sinhala</a>. They make up about 75% of the population. </p>
      <p>The second largest group is the <a href="/wiki/Tamil_people" title="Tamil people">Tamil</a> people. They are <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a>. There are about 2,271,000 Tamils in Sri Lanka. The <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Civil_War" title="Sri Lankan Civil War">Sri Lankan Civil War</a> against Tamil separatists lasted 28 years. </p>
      <p>The third largest group is the Sri Lankan Moors. They are <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>. There are over one and a half million people in this group. They use <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a> as their language today. </p>
      <p>Other than these three main groups , there are burghers (descendants of colonial ancestors) , malays and chinese. </p>
      <p>In areas such as Dabana , Mahiyangaya&nbsp;; lives the indigenous group called 'veddas' who are said to descend from Kuweni and Vijaya's son 'Jeewahaththa'. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Buddhist missionaries arrived from India in 250 BC during the time period of King Devanampiyatissa. The kings of the island, and eventually most of the Sinhalese people, became Buddhists. Legend has it that Buddha visited the island three times. </p>
      <p>The island was subject to waves of European settlement and eventually <a href="/wiki/Colonisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonisation">colonisation</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese</a> in the 16th and 17th centuries, the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch</a> in the 17th and 18th centuries, and lastly the <a href="/wiki/British" title="British">British</a> from 1796 to 1948. The British traded as the others had done, but also developed <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a> of <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a>. Tea was planted after a <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">fungus</a> destroyed the coffee plantations in 1869. The tea plantations were the basis of Sri Lankan prosperity for a hundred years. Lastly, <a href="/wiki/Rubber" title="Rubber">rubber</a> plantations were started in the early 20th century. In 1965, Ceylon became the world's leading exporter of tea, with 200,000 tonnes of tea being shipped internationally annually.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Independence from the British was got in 1948, together with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dominion (not yet started)">Dominion</a> status in the British <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. In 1972, Ceylon changed its name officially to the Republic of Sri Lanka, and is still a member of the Commonwealth.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2009 came the end of a long <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Civil_War" title="Sri Lankan Civil War">Sri Lankan Civil War</a> between Sinhalese and Tamils. Since then <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a> has rapidly grown as a source of foreign <a href="/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">investment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag">Flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Flag">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Flag">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Sri Lanka flag is also known as the Lion flag because of the Lion on it. The Lion is holding a sword in its right paw. There is a crimson background with four leaves in each corner. There is a yellow border. On the left of the flag there are the colors green and saffron. </p>
      <p>The lion represents bravery. The four leaves represents Karuna, Meththa, Muditha and Upeksha. The orange stripe represents the Sri Lankan Tamils and the green stripe represents the Sri Lankan Moors. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Easter_Sunday_bombings">Easter Sunday bombings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Easter Sunday bombings">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sri_Lanka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Easter Sunday bombings">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/2019_Sri_Lanka_Easter_bombings" title="2019 Sri Lanka Easter bombings">2019 Sri Lanka Easter bombings</a>
      </div> 
      <p>On April 21, 2019, bombings hit churches and hotels and left at least 290 people dead and 500 injured in the cities of cities of <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">Colombo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Negombo" title="Negombo">Negombo</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Batticaloa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Batticaloa (not yet started)">Batticaloa</a>. According to officials a Sri Lankan group called <i>National Thowheed Jamath</i> was behind the attacks. </p>
      <p>Most of those killed were Sri Lankan nationals but there were also thirty one foreigners who died, including eight British, eight Indian, two Turkish, two Australian, one Bangladeshi, one Portuguese and four American nationals.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
