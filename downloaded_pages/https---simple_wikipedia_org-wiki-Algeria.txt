      <p><b>Algeria</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/dʒ/: 'j' in 'jam'">dʒ</span><span title="/ɪər/: 'ear' in 'near'">ɪər</span><span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span> <span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:En-us-Algeria.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20"></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/25/En-us-Algeria.ogg" class="internal" title="En-us-Algeria.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span></span> or <span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ɔː/: 'au' in 'fraud'">ɔː</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span></span>-/</a></span></span>), officially the <b>People's Democratic Republic of Algeria</b>, is a former French <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> and the largest country in Africa. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a> region of Northwest Africa. <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a> is the capital. It is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> in the north, <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> in the northeast, <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> in the east, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> in the southeast, <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> in the southwest, <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a> in the west and <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> in the northwest. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>People have lived in Algeria for for thousands of years, which can be seen in the cave paintings at <a href="/wiki/Tassili" class="mw-redirect" title="Tassili">Tassili</a> National Park that date to around 7000BC. By 600 BC, <a href="/wiki/Phoenician" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenician">Phoenicians</a> were in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hippo_Regius&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hippo Regius (not yet started)">Hippo Regius</a> (now called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Annaba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Annaba (not yet started)">Annaba</a>), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rusicade&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rusicade (not yet started)">Rusicade</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Skikda" title="Skikda">Skikda</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Tipasa" class="mw-redirect" title="Tipasa">Tipasa</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Central" class="mw-disambig" title="Central">central</a> <a href="/wiki/Coastal" class="mw-redirect" title="Coastal">coastal</a> part of the country. The Romans would annex (take over) the region by the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Punic_Wars" title="Punic Wars">Punic Wars</a> in 146BC, with what is now modern day Algeria staying in Roman hands till the fall of the Roman Empire in the 5th century and the region being taken over by the Germanic <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a>. </p>
      <p>Algeria would become part of the islamic world when the first <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> <a href="/wiki/Arabs" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabs">Arabs</a> came to Algeria in the mid-<a href="/wiki/7th_century" title="7th century">7th century</a>, resulting in many people converting to the new religion of islam. In the <a href="/wiki/11th_century" title="11th century">11th century</a>, the Arab tribes of <a href="/wiki/Banu_Hilal" title="Banu Hilal">Banu Hilal</a> and Banu Sulaym were living between <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> and eastern Algeria (<a href="/wiki/Constantine" class="mw-disambig" title="Constantine">Constantois</a>). The famous <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fibonacci" title="Fibonacci">Fibonacci</a> (1170—1250) lived in Algeria as a teenager. This is where he learned the <a href="/wiki/Hindu%E2%80%93Arabic_numeral_system" title="Hindu–Arabic numeral system">Hindu–Arabic numeral system</a>. Algeria was made part of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> in 1517. <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">Enslavement</a> of <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black people</a> was practiced there as it was throughout the empire.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the 1500s and 1700s, the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> ruled a lot of Algeria. <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> colonized Algeria starting in 1830. In 1954, the <a href="/wiki/National_Liberation_Front" title="National Liberation Front">National Liberation Front</a> (Front de Libération Nationale or FLN) wanted freedom from France. They fought a war. It became independent from <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in July 5, 1962. </p>
      <p>In 1963 <a href="/wiki/Ahmed_Ben_Bella" title="Ahmed Ben Bella">Ahmed Ben Bella</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_Algeria" title="President of Algeria">President of Algeria</a>. </p>
      <p>The Algerian Civil War started in 1991. It ended in 2002. the government stopped the state of <a href="/wiki/Emergency" title="Emergency">emergency</a> on 24 February 2011. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A large part of southern Algeria is the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a>. The Aures and Nememcha mountain ranges are in the north. The highest point is Mount Tahat (3,003 m). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Languages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Languages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The official languages of the country are <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> as a first language and <a href="/wiki/Berber" class="mw-disambig" title="Berber">Berber</a> as a second language. Many people speak <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, too. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Algeria's population is about 45 million people. There are over 40 <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Algeria" class="mw-redirect" title="List of cities in Algeria">cities</a> with more than 100,000 people. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UNESCO_World_Heritage_Sites">UNESCO World Heritage Sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: UNESCO World Heritage Sites">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Algeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: UNESCO World Heritage Sites">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are several <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Sites" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Sites">World Heritage Sites</a> in Algeria<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> including <a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Qal%27a_of_Beni_Hammad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Al Qal'a of Beni Hammad (not yet started)">Al Qal'a of Beni Hammad</a>, the first capital of the <a href="/wiki/Hammadid" class="mw-redirect" title="Hammadid">Hammadid</a> empire; <a href="/wiki/Tipasa" class="mw-redirect" title="Tipasa">Tipasa</a>, a Phoenician and later Roman town; and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dj%C3%A9mila&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Djémila (not yet started)">Djémila</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Timgad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Timgad (not yet started)">Timgad</a>, both <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> ruins; <a href="/w/index.php?title=M%27Zab_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="M'Zab Valley (not yet started)">M'Zab Valley</a>, a limestone valley containing a large urbanized <a href="/wiki/Oasis" title="Oasis">oasis</a>; also the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Casbah&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Casbah (not yet started)">Casbah</a> of Algiers is an important citadel. The only natural <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Sites" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Sites">World Heritage Sites</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Tassili_n%27Ajjer" title="Tassili n'Ajjer">Tassili n'Ajjer</a>, a mountain range. </p>
