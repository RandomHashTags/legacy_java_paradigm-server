      <p><b>The Bahamas</b> (officially called <b>Commonwealth of The Bahamas</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">group of islands</a> in the West Indies. The country's capital, <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a>, is on New Providence Island. </p>
      <p>The Taino were the first people living there. In 1492, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> found the Americas by landing on another of the islands, <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador</a>. The Eleutheran Adventurers soon came along, making a home in Eleuthera. </p>
      <p>The islands' mostly black population speaks <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, the country's main language. </p>
      <p>The Bahamas are a popular place for people to visit for holidays, the 700 islands and cays attract many visitors from nearby <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and other countries. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucayans&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lucayans (not yet started)">Lucayanss</a> were the first people to arrive in the Bahamas. They moved into the southern Bahamas from <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> around the 11th century AD, having come there from South America. They came to be known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucayan_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lucayan people (not yet started)">Lucayan</a>. About 30,000 Lucayan lived the Bahamas when <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> arrived in 1492. Columbus' first landfall in the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a> was on an island named San Salvador, which some scholars believe to be present-day <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador Island</a>. </p>
      <p>The Spanish forced much of the Lucayan population to move to Hispaniola. They were used for <a href="/wiki/Forced_labour" class="mw-redirect" title="Forced labour">forced labour</a>. This and the exposure to foreign <a href="/wiki/Infectious_disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Infectious disease">diseases</a> led to most of the population of the Bahamas dying.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">Smallpox</a> alone wiped out half of the population in what is now the Bahamas.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1670, <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">King Charles II</a> rented out the islands to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carolinas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carolinas (not yet started)">Carolinas</a>, along with rights of trading, tax, and <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">governing</a> the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Anglican_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Anglican-11">[11]</a></sup> During this time, the Bahamas became a haven for <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">pirates</a>, including the infamous <a href="/wiki/Blackbeard" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackbeard">Blackbeard</a>. To restore proper government, Britain made the Bahamas a <a href="/wiki/British_overseas_territories" title="British overseas territories">crown colony</a> in 1718. The first governor was <a href="/w/index.php?title=Woodes_Rogers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Woodes Rogers (not yet started)">Woodes Rogers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>After the <a href="/wiki/American_War_of_Independence" class="mw-redirect" title="American War of Independence">American War of Independence</a>, the British resettled some 7,300 <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Loyalists</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Slave" class="mw-redirect" title="Slave">slaves</a> in the Bahamas from New York, Florida, and the Carolinas. The first group of loyalists left <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">St. Augustine</a> in East Florida in September 1783. These Loyalists established <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a> on several islands. <a href="/wiki/British_America" class="mw-redirect" title="British America">British Americans</a> were outnumbered by the African-American slaves they brought with them, and ethnic Europeans remained a minority in the territory. On <a href="/wiki/10_July" class="mw-redirect" title="10 July">10 July</a> <a href="/wiki/1973" title="1973">1973</a> The Bahamas gains full <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> within <a href="/wiki/British_Commonwealth" class="mw-redirect" title="British Commonwealth">British Commonwealth</a>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Languages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Languages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The official language of the Bahamas is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, but they also speak a local dialect called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahamian_Creole&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bahamian Creole (not yet started)">Bahamianese</a>. The Bahamian dialect is based based on the West Country England accents along with South Hiberno English dialects with strong influences from <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West African</a> languages. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_the_Bahamas" title="List of cities in the Bahamas">List of cities in the Bahamas</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In 1864 the Governor of the Bahamas reported that there were 29 islands, 661 cays, and 2,387 rocks in the colony.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The closest island to the United States is <a href="/wiki/Bimini" title="Bimini">Bimini</a>. The southeasternmost island is <a href="/wiki/Inagua" title="Inagua">Inagua</a>. The largest island is <a href="/wiki/Andros_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Andros Island">Andros Island</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a>, capital city of The Bahamas, is on the island of <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a>. </p>
      <p>All the islands are low and flat. The highest point in the country is <a href="/wiki/Mount_Alvernia" title="Mount Alvernia">Mount Alvernia</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cat_Island,_Bahamas" title="Cat Island, Bahamas">Cat Island</a>. It is 63 metres (207&nbsp;ft) high. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The climate of The Bahamas is subtropical to tropical. The <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" class="mw-redirect" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a> can be very dangerous in the summer and autumn. This is when hurricanes pass near or through the islands. <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">Hurricane Andrew</a> hit the northern islands during the <a href="/wiki/1992_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1992 Atlantic hurricane season">1992 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Floyd" title="Hurricane Floyd">Hurricane Floyd</a> hit most of the islands during the 1999 Atlantic hurricane season. </p>
      <p>There has never been a freeze reported in The Bahamas. The temperature can fall as low as 2–3&nbsp;°C (35.6–37.4&nbsp;°F). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bahamas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Bahamas does not have an army or an air force. The Royal Bahamas Defence Force (RBDF) is the navy. The Defence Force has a fleet of 26 coastal and inshore patrol craft along with 2 aircraft and over 850 personnel including 65 officers and 74 women. </p>
