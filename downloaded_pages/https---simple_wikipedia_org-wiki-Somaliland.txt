      <p><b>Somaliland</b> (<a href="/wiki/Somali_language" title="Somali language">Somali</a>: <i lang="so">Somaliland</i>; <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">صوماليلاند</span>‎ <i><i lang="ar-Latn" title="Arabic-language romanization">Ṣūmālīlānd</i></i>, <span lang="ar" dir="rtl" title="Arabic language text">أرض الصومال</span>‎ <i><i lang="ar-Latn" title="Arabic-language romanization">Arḍ aṣ-Ṣūmāl</i></i>), officially the <b>Republic of Somaliland</b> (<a href="/wiki/Somali_language" title="Somali language">Somali</a>: <i lang="so">Jamhuuriyadda Somaliland</i>, <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">جمهورية صوماليلاند</span>‎ <i>Jumhūrīyat Ṣūmālīlānd</i>), is an <a href="/wiki/Unrecognized_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Unrecognized state">unrecorgnized state</a>, which the international community considers part of <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a>. The territory covers the former <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Somaliland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="British Somaliland (not yet started)">British Somaliland</a>. De facto, the territory is independent from the rest of Somalia. It <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/border" class="extiw" title="wikt:border">borders</a> <a href="/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a> to the west, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Ethiopia" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Republic of Ethiopia">Federal Republic of Ethiopia</a> to the south and <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> to the east.<sup id="cite_ref-Profile_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The area used to be the Somali area which was part of the British empire along with <a href="/wiki/Jubaland" title="Jubaland">Jubaland</a>, which was called <a href="/wiki/Trans-juba" class="mw-redirect" title="Trans-juba">Trans-juba</a>. It was called the British Somali Coast Protectorate before 26 June 1960. That year it united with Somalia called the "Somali Republic".<sup id="cite_ref-PDF_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDF-10">[10]</a></sup> In May of <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, after a war, Somaliland regained independence. </p>
      <p>Currently, Somaliland is an <a href="/wiki/Unrecognized_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Unrecognized state">unrecognized state</a>. This means that no country with membership in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> or <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/international" class="extiw" title="wikt:international">international</a> organization views Somaliland as an independent country.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-11">[11]</a></sup> Instead, they see Somaliland as a part of Somalia. </p>
      <p>Somaliland has a republican government with free elections. The capital is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hargeisa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hargeisa (not yet started)">Hargeisa</a>. Berbera is a beautiful city on the coast. Aboput 55% of the people of Somalilands are <a href="/wiki/Nomads" class="mw-redirect" title="Nomads">nomads</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Most Somalis are <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni</a> <a href="/wiki/Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>. Some people are part of <a href="/wiki/Sufism" title="Sufism">Sufi</a> orders. </p>
      <p>A territory in the west, called <a href="/wiki/Khatumo_State" title="Khatumo State">Khatumo State</a> has been disputed between Somaliland and <a href="/wiki/Puntland" title="Puntland">Puntland</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somaliland&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somaliland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="***REMOVED***">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somaliland&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Language">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somaliland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Language">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Most people in Somaliland speak Somali and Arabic. Article 6 of the Constitution of 2001 says the official language of Somaliland is Somali,<sup id="cite_ref-PDF_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDF-10">[10]</a></sup> but Arabic is a <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/mandatory" class="extiw" title="wikt:mandatory">mandatory</a> subject in school. English is also spoken and taught in schools. </p>
      <p>The main Somali <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">dialect</a> is Standard Somali. Standard Somali is spoken in most of Somalia and in countries that border it. Standard Somali is used by almost all of the media in the Somaliland region. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somaliland&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somaliland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Almost all Somalilanders are <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This is because <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> is the state religion, and practicing a religion other than Islam is against the law.<sup id="cite_ref-PDF_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDF-10">[10]</a></sup> Small amounts of non-Islamic <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/tradition" class="extiw" title="wikt:tradition">traditions</a> exist in Somaliland, but Islam is very important to the Somali sense of national identity. </p>
