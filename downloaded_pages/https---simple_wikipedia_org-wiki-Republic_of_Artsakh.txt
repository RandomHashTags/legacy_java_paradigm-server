      <p>The <b>Nagorno-Karabakh Republic</b> (<b>NKR</b>) <sup id="cite_ref-Constitution_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Constitution-3">[3]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy">Լեռնային Ղարաբաղ Հանրապետություն</span> <i><i lang="hy-Latn" title="Armenian-language romanization">Lernayin Gharabaghi Hanrapetut’yun</i></i>) or <b>Artsakh Republic</b><sup id="cite_ref-Constitution_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Constitution-3">[3]</a></sup> is a <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh" title="Nagorno-Karabakh">Nagorno-Karabakh</a> region of the <a href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">South Caucasus</a>. It is internationally recognised to be part of <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>. </p>
      <p>The Nagorno-Karabakh region has been populated by <a href="/wiki/Armenian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Armenian people">Armenians</a> for a long time. The area has been disputed by Armenia and Azerbaijan since 1918. After the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> established control over the area, in 1923 it formed the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagorno-Karabakh_Autonomous_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast (not yet started)">Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast</a> (NKAO) within the <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan_SSR" class="mw-redirect" title="Azerbaijan SSR">Azerbaijan SSR</a>. In the final years of the Soviet Union, the region again became a source of dispute between Armenia and Azerbaijan, resulting in the <a href="/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_War" title="Nagorno-Karabakh War">Nagorno-Karabakh War</a> from 1988–1994. </p>
      <p>On 10 December 1991, a referendum was held in the NKAO and the neighboring <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shahumian&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shahumian (not yet started)">Shahumian</a> region. This resulted in a <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_independence" title="Declaration of independence">declaration of independence</a> from Azerbaijan as the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic. The country is not recognized by any <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">UN</a> member state, including Armenia. Representatives of the governments of Armenia and Azerbaijan have since been holding peace talks mediated by the <a href="/wiki/OSCE" class="mw-redirect" title="OSCE">OSCE</a> Minsk Group. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagorno-Karabakh_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagorno-Karabakh_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Artsakh Republic has a lot of mountains. It is 11,500&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (4,440&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) in area, and borders <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, Azerbaijan, and <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. The highest peaks in the country are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Mrav&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Mrav (not yet started)">Mount Mrav</a>, 3,340 metres (10,958&nbsp;ft), and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Kirs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Kirs (not yet started)">Mount Kirs</a> 2,725 metres (8,940&nbsp;ft). The major rivers are the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Terter_river&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Terter river (not yet started)">Terter</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khachen_river&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Khachen river (not yet started)">Khachen</a> rivers.<sup id="cite_ref-Overview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Overview-4">[4]</a></sup> Most rivers in the country flow towards the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Artsakh_valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Artsakh valley (not yet started)">Artsakh valley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Geography_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geography-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The climate in the Artsakh Republic is mild. It is <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">foggy</a> for over 100 days a year. More than 36% of the country is <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forested</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagorno-Karabakh_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nagorno-Karabakh_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Nagorno-Karabakh_conflict" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Nagorno-Karabakh conflict">2020 Nagorno-Karabakh conflict</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In 2005, the country's population was 137,737. The ethnic composition was 137,380 (99.74%) Armenians, 171 (0.12%) <a href="/wiki/Russians" class="mw-redirect" title="Russians">Russians</a>, 21 (0.02%) <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ukrainian people">Ukrainians</a>, 6 (0.00%) <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijanis" class="mw-redirect" title="Azerbaijanis">Azerbaijanis</a> and 159 (0.12%) others. </p>
      <p>The first demographic <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> in the Artsakh Republic took place in 1769. It was a letter from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heraclius_II_of_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Heraclius II of Georgia (not yet started)">Heraclius II of Georgia</a> to Russia's <a href="/w/index.php?title=Petr_Ivanovich_Panin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Petr Ivanovich Panin (not yet started)">Petr Ivanovich Panin</a>, and said, "Seven families rule the region of Khamse. Its population is totally Armenian." <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2014, the life expectancy for males was 71.6 years, and females 76.8 years.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Nearly all of the Azerbaijani population was killed or thrown out by Armenian forces since the war. Azerbaijani refugees who have survived not allowed to return and have become displaced.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
