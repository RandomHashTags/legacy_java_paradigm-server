      <p><b>Indonesia</b>, (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">ɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="/ʒ/: 's' in 'pleasure'">ʒ</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>,<span class="wrap"> </span>-<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span><span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>,<span class="wrap"> </span>-<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ʃ/: 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> officially the <b>Republic of Indonesia</b>, (<a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a>: <i>Republik Indonesia</i>) is a nation in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Archipelago" title="Malay Archipelago">Malay Islands</a>. It has 18,108 <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a>. People live on about 6,000 of these islands. The most important islands of Indonesia are <a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Java</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">Bali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sulawesi" title="Sulawesi">Sulawesi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a>. The capital of Indonesia is <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, on Java. The president now is <a href="/wiki/Joko_Widodo" title="Joko Widodo">Joko Widodo</a>. Modern Indonesia began on the 17th of August 1945. At 10 o'clock on that Friday morning, Sukarno read Indonesia's Declaration of Independence. Indonesia's Independence Day is a national holiday. </p>
      <p>Indonesia is the fourth most populated country in the world with 238,452,952 people (2004 est.) Half of the <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> lives in Java, There are 111 people per km<sup>2</sup>. The land area is 1.904 million km<sup>2</sup>, or slightly smaller than <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a> of Indonesia is <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Bahasa Indonesia</a>, but a total of 737 languages are spoken in different parts of Indonesia. Most of these many languages are only spoken among remote tribal groups. Other languages widely spoken in Indonesia include <a href="/wiki/Javanese_language" title="Javanese language">Javanese</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Balinese_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Balinese language (not yet started)">Balinese</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sundanese_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sundanese language (not yet started)">Sundanese</a>. Indonesia's neighbors are <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a> which share land borders with Indonesia. Other nearby countries are <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> to the south, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> to the Northwest, and <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> to the Northeast. </p>
      <p>Indonesia has the most <a href="/wiki/Active_volcano" class="mw-redirect" title="Active volcano">active volcanoes</a> of any country in the world. It is also close to <a href="/wiki/Fault_(geology)" title="Fault (geology)">fault lines</a> so there are many <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunamis</a>. </p>
      <p>Most people in Indonesia follow <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, but Indonesia is not an Islamic country by law. Indonesia has the largest <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> population on earth. Other religions Indonesians follow include <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> (<a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>), <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre_World_War_II">Pre World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Pre World War II">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Pre World War II">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Indonesia has a written history as far back as the 7th century and a much longer <a href="/wiki/Oral_history" title="Oral history">oral history</a>. Before the colonial Dutch came in the 1596, much of what is now Indonesia was many different kingdoms. Often they were fighting each other. </p>
      <p>Indonesia was <a href="/wiki/Colonized" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonized">colonized</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> in the 17th century and renamed the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>. During this time, the Dutch captured thousands of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Malagasy_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Malagasy people (not yet started)">Malagasy people</a> from <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and forced them to work on <a href="/wiki/Pepper" class="mw-disambig" title="Pepper">pepper</a> <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a> in their colonies as <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The Dutch treated the islands like their property until <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. </p>
      <p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> drove out the Dutch and took control of Indonesia. After Japan surrendered in the war, Indonesia claimed its <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> on the 17th of August 1945. The proclamation was read by <a href="/wiki/Sukarno" title="Sukarno">Sukarno</a> in Jakarta. Sukarno later became Indonesia's first President. </p>
      <p>British troops came into Indonesia to restore peace and to rescue Europeans who had been prisoners of the Japanese. The British troops also had the job of shipping home 300,000 Japanese troops. The Indonesian Republicans fought the British troops, because it was expected that the British would give Indonesia back to the Dutch. The Indonesian Republicans killed many of the Japanese prisoners, before they could be sent home. They also began killing people from <a href="/wiki/Minority_group" title="Minority group">minority groups</a> who might be against the new Republic. Many European and Indonesian European people were killed. Many Chinese business people and other minority groups were killed or made homeless. In Java there were many thousands of homeless people. </p>
      <p>In 1946, the Dutch came back. When the British left in 1947, there were 55,000 Dutch soldiers in Indonesia. The Dutch action was called "Operatie Product" or "Politionele Acties". The Indonesian Republicans fought the Dutch until 1949. But the Indonesian Republicans were badly organised and often fought among themselves. As the Dutch forced the Republican soldiers out of different areas, they moved in more troops until there were 100,000 Dutch troops. The Dutch refused to obey the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> who said they should stop the fighting in Indonesia. The United States of America organised for meetings between Dutch and Indonesian leaders. The Dutch finally agreed to recognise Indonesia's independence in November 1949. </p>
      <p>Because of the fighting and the bad organisation, it took a long time for the country to become peaceful, and for the <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> to get better. Many Indonesian soldiers had died, between 45,000 and 100,000. Also, a very large number of <a href="/wiki/Civilian" title="Civilian">civilians</a>, Indonesians, Europeans and Chinese, had died; perhaps as many as 200,000. </p>

      <p>Indonesia has 34 provinces. Five of them have special status. Each province has its own <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislature</a> and <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">governor</a>. The provinces are divided into regencies (<i><a href="/wiki/Regencies_of_Indonesia" title="Regencies of Indonesia">kabupaten</a></i>) and cities (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cities_of_Indonesia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cities of Indonesia (not yet started)">kota</a></i>). These are further divided into districts (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Subdistricts_of_Indonesia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Subdistricts of Indonesia (not yet started)">kecamatan</a></i>), and again into village groupings (either <i>desa</i> or <i>kelurahan</i>). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People_and_culture">People and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: People and culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: People and culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are people of many different cultural groups living in Indonesia, has more than 700 ethnic groups. It is affected by <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indians</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese people</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malays" title="Malays">Malays</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a>.
