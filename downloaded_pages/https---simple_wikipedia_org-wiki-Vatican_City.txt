      <p><b>Vatican City</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ən/: 'on' in 'button'">ən</span></span><span class="wrap"> </span><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">ɪ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span> <span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:En-us-Vatican_City.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20"></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/32/En-us-Vatican_City.ogg" class="internal" title="En-us-Vatican City.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span></span>; officially <b>Vatican City State</b>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i lang="it">Stato della Città del Vaticano</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[6]</a></sup> is an independent <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a> and the smallest <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the world by <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_area" title="List of countries by area">size</a>, at 0.44&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">km²</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[7]</a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/Territory" title="Territory">territory</a> is completely surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> and it is only one of three countries in the world that are <a href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">enclaves</a> of another country (the others being <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a>, also in Italy, and <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a> in southern Africa). Also, it is the only country in the world that is an enclave of a <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a>, as all of the land around it is part of <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, the capital of Italy. </p>
      <p>The Vatican City is the <a href="/wiki/Headquarters" title="Headquarters">headquarters</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> and its government, the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> which is, religiously speaking, the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop of Rome">Bishop of Rome</a> and head of the Roman Catholic Church. The current Pope, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">cardinal</a> Jorge Mario Bergoglio, was elected on 13 March 2013. </p>
      <p>Established on 11 February 1929 with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lateran_Agreement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lateran Agreement (not yet started)">Lateran Agreement</a> (<i>Patti Lateranensi</i>) signed by <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_XI" title="Pope Pius XI">Pope Pius XI</a>, the Vatican City is also important for its <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a>. The Vatican's masterpieces are very well known in the world: <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Square" title="St. Peter's Square">St. Peter's Square</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Basilica" title="St. Peter's Basilica">St. Peter's Basilica</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sistine_Chapel" title="Sistine Chapel">Sistine Chapel</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_Museums&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vatican Museums (not yet started)">Vatican Museums</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_Palace" title="Apostolic Palace">Apostolic Palace</a>, where the Pope lives. There are also hundreds of other <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculptures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Picture" title="Picture">pictures</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Pope used to rule the Papal States, which included most of Italy. Catholic popes had generally tried to stop Italy from becoming one country because they feared they would lose their control of at least one of the Papal States.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.">source?</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1861 Italy was unified under the King of Savoy, but <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latium" class="mw-redirect" title="Latium">Latium</a> remained unconquered. On September 20, 1870 Italian troops invaded. Rome became capital of the new kingdom. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> claimed he was a prisoner of the Italian state and <a href="/wiki/Excommunication" title="Excommunication">excommunicated</a> all the people who helped invade the Papal state. This stopped Catholics from taking part in public life under Catholic government. </p>
      <p>In 1929 <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a>, decided to sign an agreement with the Holy See, called the <a href="/wiki/Lateran_Treaty" title="Lateran Treaty">Lateran Treaty</a>, which created the Vatican State. Another treaty gave the Vatican money each year to compensate for the lost territories. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_Government">Politics and Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics and Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics and Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The government <a href="/wiki/Hierarchy" title="Hierarchy">structure</a> is theocracy with the <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pontifical leader</a> being the highest authority. The pope is elected by the College of Cardinals which can lead the Roman Catholic Church and the city-state itself. The Pope also holds the title of "Bishop of Rome". </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: People">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: People">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Vatican City has a population of 453 people, and 246 of them are citizens. Also, there are 372 Vatican citizens who live in other countries.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="***REMOVED***">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Language">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Language">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Vatican does not have a law declaring an official language. <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> is the main language used. The <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>'s official language is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The religion of the city is the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a>. </p>
