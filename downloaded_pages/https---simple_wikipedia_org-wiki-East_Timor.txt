      <p>The <b>Democratic Republic of East Timor</b> or <b>Timor Leste</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. It is on the eastern side of the <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a>. It also includes the smaller islands of <a href="/wiki/Atauro" title="Atauro">Atauro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaco_Island" title="Jaco Island">Jaco Island</a>, and a small area, named <a href="/wiki/Oecusse" title="Oecusse">Oecusse</a>, inside the west side of Timor. Oecusse is an <a href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclave</a>, which means it is separated from the main part of the country by part of another country. That other country is <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. </p>
      <p>The capital and largest city, <a href="/wiki/Dili" title="Dili">Dili</a>, is home to one hundred and fifty thousand people. The next largest city is <a href="/wiki/Baucau,_Timor_Leste" class="mw-redirect" title="Baucau, Timor Leste">Baucau.</a> (see <a href="/wiki/List_of_settlements_in_East_Timor" title="List of settlements in East Timor">List of settlements in East Timor</a>). </p>
      <p>East Timor gets its name from the <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a> word for "east", <i>timur</i>. </p>
      <p>It is a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations" title="Association of Southeast Asian Nations">Association of Southeast Asian Nations</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries (not yet started)">Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Timor&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Timor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>For a long time, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> controlled the East Timor and called it the Colony of Portuguese Timor. In 1975, the Portuguese army left, and East Timor was invaded (taken over) by the Indonesian army in 1975. The invasion was very violent. The army stayed there until 1999, when they gave up control of the territory with the help of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>. </p>
      <p>At the time, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> government said it did not know Indonesia was going to invade East Timor. But secret documents, released from 2002 to 2005, show that <a href="/wiki/Henry_Kissinger" title="Henry Kissinger">Henry Kissinger</a>, the Secretary of State of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, did know, and supported the invasion. </p>
      <p>When it got its <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> on 20 May 2002, it became the first country to become independent in the twenty-first century (since the year 2001). In 2006, when <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> became independent, East Timor was no longer the newest one. </p>
      <p>Since then, East Timor has not been given much attention in the news. Recently, the <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> music artist <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a> made a <a href="/wiki/Song" title="Song">song</a> named "Timor", which talks about the country. The attention is because there has been fighting in East Timor between gangs (groups of criminals with guns), security forces (the country's army). <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, a large nearby country, sent troops in to make <a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">peace</a>. </p>
      <p>East Timor is a small country located between Australia and Indonesia, 3,000 years ago, East Timor was a mountainous island composed of migrant indigenous people from New Guinea, Australia and Melanesia. </p>
      <p>There were some migrants from Austronesia searching for a new life on this island. Some of the people arrived from South <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and North <a href="/wiki/Indochina" title="Indochina">Indochina</a> looking for trade because East Timor had resources which could be exported, like sandalwood, honey, slaves and wax. </p>
      <p>During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> occupied the region, encountered strong resistance to their attempts to force the population to grow foods for their troops and export. At the time 30% of the population died. </p>
      <p>On 25 April 1974, the Portuguese Armed Forces headed by General António de Spínola organized a Military Coup against <a href="/wiki/Salazar" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar">Salazar</a>’s regime called the Revolution of Carnations. After this revolution, Portugal decided to give freedom to the colonized countries. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Timor&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Timor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>East Timor is the largest of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Sunda_Islands" title="Lesser Sunda Islands">Lesser Sunda Islands</a>. To the north of the island are the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ombai_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ombai Strait (not yet started)">Ombai Strait</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetar_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wetar Strait (not yet started)">Wetar Strait</a>, and the greater <a href="/w/index.php?title=Banda_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Banda Sea (not yet started)">Banda Sea</a>. To the south the <a href="/wiki/Timor_Sea" title="Timor Sea">Timor Sea</a> separates the island from <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. </p>
      <p>The country has many mountains. Its highest point is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tatamailau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tatamailau (not yet started)">Tatamailau</a> (also known as Mount Ramelau) at 2,963 metres (9,721&nbsp;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-gb_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gb-6">[6]</a></sup> The climate is <a href="/wiki/Tropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical">tropical</a> and generally hot and humid. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nino_Konis_Santana_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nino Konis Santana National Park (not yet started)">Nino Konis Santana National Park</a> is on the eastern tip of the island. The park includes <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">coral reefs</a>, coastal area, and mountains.<sup id="cite_ref-conservation_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conservation-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
