      <p><b>Ecuador</b> is a small <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. It used to be part of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a>, because <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> was interested in finding <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> in Ecuador. Before Spain, Ecuador was part of the <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca Empire</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ecuador" title="President of Ecuador">President</a> of Ecuador is <a href="/wiki/Len%C3%ADn_Moreno" title="Len√≠n Moreno">Len√≠n Moreno</a> since 2017. </p>
      <p>Ecuador produces <a href="/wiki/Bananas" class="mw-redirect" title="Bananas">bananas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cocoa" title="Cocoa">cocoa</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Ecuador is located along the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FFAE_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FFAE-7">[7]</a></sup> There are <a href="/wiki/Jungle" title="Jungle">jungles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> mountains. Ecuador has many active <a href="/wiki/Volcanoes" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcanoes">volcanoes</a> and also one of the highest densities of volcanoes in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> there are beaches. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of the country is <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a>. This city is <a href="/wiki/Altitude" title="Altitude">high up</a> in the Andes, which makes it hard for some new visitors to <a href="/wiki/Breathe" class="mw-redirect" title="Breathe">breathe</a>. The biggest city is <a href="/wiki/Guayaquil" title="Guayaquil">Guayaquil</a> on the coast. <a href="/wiki/Cuenca,_Ecuador" title="Cuenca, Ecuador">Cuenca</a> is famous for its old buildings and <a href="/wiki/Colonial" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonial">colonial</a> <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> borders Ecuador to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> borders it to the north. The <a href="/wiki/Gal%C3%A1pagos_Islands" title="Gal√°pagos Islands">Gal√°pagos Islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> are also part of Ecuador and are famous for lots of animals. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> travelled to the Gal√°pagos Islands. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In 2011, 14,440,000 people lived in Ecuador.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The ethnic makeup of the country is: 65% Mestizo, 25% Amerindian, 7% white, and 3% black. Some 27% of the people live below the national poverty line. </p>
      <p>The official language is <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>. Many other <a href="/wiki/Indigenous" class="mw-redirect" title="Indigenous">indigenous</a> languages are spoken. Most of the people in Ecuador are <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholics" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholics">Roman Catholics</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> although Ecuador has religious freedom and people can follow any religion they choose. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_Center_of_Quito">Historic Center of Quito</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Historic Center of Quito">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Historic Center of Quito">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Historic Center of Quito, Ecuador is one of the largest, least-altered and best-preserved historic centers in the Americas. This center was, together with the historic centre of Krak√≥w in Poland, the first to be declared World Heritage Site by UNESCO on 18 September 1978. The Historic Centre of Quito is located in the center south of the capital on an area of 320&nbsp;hectares (790&nbsp;acres), and is considered one of the most important historic areas in Latin America.There are about 130 monumental buildings (which hosts a variety of pictorial art and sculpture, mostly of religious inspired in a multi-faceted range of schools and styles) and 5,000 properties registered in the municipal inventory of heritage properties. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Church_of_San_Fransisco">Church of San Fransisco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Church of San Fransisco">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Church of San Fransisco">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <i>Iglesia y Monasterio de San Francisco</i> (English: Church and Monastery of St. Francis), colloquially known as El San Francisco, is a colonial-styled church and monastery located in Quito, Ecuador. Construction of the building began a few weeks after the founding of the city in 1534 and ended in 1604. The founder of the church was Franciscan missionary Jodoco Ricke. </p>
      <p>The building's construction began around 1550, sixteen years after Quito was founded by Spanish conquistadors, and was finished in approximately 1680. The building was officially inaugurated in 1605. It is not known who designed the original plans for the complex, though the most-accepted theory is that they were sent from Spain, based on the topographical study of Ricke and Gosseal. It is also possible that architects came from Spain for the construction of the monastery, or that Ricke and Gosseal managed the entire construction. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Quito_School">The Quito School</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: The Quito School">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: The Quito School">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>The Quito School (<i>Escuela Quite√±a</i>) is an artistic tradition that developed in the territory of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Audience_of_Quito&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Royal Audience of Quito (not yet started)">Royal Audience of Quito</a>, from Pasto and Popay√°n in the north to Piura and Cajamarca in the south, during the colonial period (1542-1824). This artistic production was one of the most important activities in the economy of the Royal Audience of Quito. </p>
      <p>The major artists of the Quito School are the sculptors <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bernardo_de_Legarda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bernardo de Legarda (not yet started)">Bernardo de Legarda</a> , <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Chili&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manuel Chili (not yet started)">Manuel Chili</a> (Caspicara) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_Angel_Tejada_Zambrado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Miguel Angel Tejada Zambrado (not yet started)">Miguel Angel Tejada Zambrado</a> and the painters Fray <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Gosseal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pedro Gosseal (not yet started)">Pedro Gosseal</a>, Fray <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Bed%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pedro Bed√≥n (not yet started)">Pedro Bed√≥n</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicol%C3%A1s_Javier_Gor%C3%ADbar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nicol√°s Javier Gor√≠bar (not yet started)">Nicol√°s Javier Gor√≠bar</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hernando_de_la_Cruz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hernando de la Cruz (not yet started)">Hernando de la Cruz</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_de_Santiago&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Miguel de Santiago (not yet started)">Miguel de Santiago</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_de_Samaniego&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manuel de Samaniego (not yet started)">Manuel de Samaniego</a> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basilica_del_Voto_Nacional">Basilica del Voto Nacional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Basilica del Voto Nacional">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Basilica del Voto Nacional">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>The Basilica of the National Vow, a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">church</a> located in the historic center of <a href="/wiki/Quito,_Ecuador" class="mw-redirect" title="Quito, Ecuador">Quito, Ecuador</a>. It is sometimes also called the Catedral Consagraci√≥n de Jes√∫s or the Bas√≠lica de San Juan. It is the largest neo-Gothic basilica in the Americas. </p>
      <p>The basilica arose from the idea, proposed by father Julio Matovelle in 1883, of building a monument as a perpetual reminder of the consecration of Ecuador to the Sacred Heart, President Luis Cordero issued the decree on July 23, 1883, and it was carried out by president Jos√© Mar√≠a Pl√°cido Caama√±o on March 5, 1884. The congress, in accordance with the year's budget, designated 12,000 pesos for the construction - 1,000 pesos per month, beginning in 1884. By the decree of July 3, 1885, the fourth Quitense Provincial Council turned the construction of the basilica into a religious commitment in the name of the country. The basilica remains technically "unfinished." &amp; local legend says that when the Bas√≠lica is completed, the end of the world will come. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metropolitan_Cathedral">Metropolitan Cathedral</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Metropolitan Cathedral">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Change section: Metropolitan Cathedral">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Construction began in 1562, seventeen years after the diocese of Quito was created (1545) and located in the heart of the historic city and its status as the main church of the city, is one of the largest religious symbols of spiritual value for the Catholic community in the city. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Church_of_San_Agust.C3.ADn"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Church_of_San_Agust√≠n">Church of San Agust√≠n</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Church of San Agust√≠n">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecuador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Change section: Church of San Agust√≠n">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>It is one of the seven monumental churches of the 16th and 17th centuries whose main <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Portico (not yet started)">portico</a> was built on stone in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_Baroque_architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spanish Baroque architecture (not yet started)">Spanish Baroque-architecture</a> style. </p>
      <p>The church includes a small <a href="/w/index.php?title=Atrium_(architecture)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Atrium (architecture) (not yet started)">atrium</a> (decorated by a large stone cross), an inside yard with a large garden and a large session hall where the frayers held dissertations or "capitulations" of faith. The <a href="/wiki/Cloister" title="Cloister">cloister</a> and <a href="/wiki/Convent" class="mw-redirect" title="Convent">convent</a> have a separate entrance which leads to the garden. The bell tower reaches a high of twenty-two meters (seventy feet) and houses two bronze bells of the period. </p>

      <p>Ecuador is one of 17 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Megadiverse_countries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Megadiverse countries (not yet started)">megadiverse countries</a> in the world. In addition to the mainland, Ecuador owns the <a href="/wiki/Gal%C3%A1pagos_Islands" title="Gal√°pagos Islands">Gal√°pagos Islands</a>. This is what the country is best known for.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Ecuador has 1,600 bird <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> and 38 more <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> in the Gal√°pagos. In addition to over 16,000 species of plants, the country has 106 endemic reptiles, 138 endemic amphibians, and 6,000 species of butterfly. The Gal√°pagos Islands are famous as the place of birth of Darwin's <a href="/wiki/Theory_of_Evolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Theory of Evolution">Theory of Evolution</a>. They are a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> World Heritage Site.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Many different kinds of <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potatoes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corn" class="mw-redirect" title="Corn">corn</a> are grown in Ecuador. There are also large <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">banana</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Farms&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Farms (not yet started)">farms</a>. On the coast, many people eat <a href="/wiki/Seafood" title="Seafood">seafood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>. <b>Cuy</b> is a famous <a href="/wiki/Indigenous" class="mw-redirect" title="Indigenous">indigenous</a> food that means <a href="/wiki/Guinea_pig" title="Guinea pig">guinea pig</a>. </p>
