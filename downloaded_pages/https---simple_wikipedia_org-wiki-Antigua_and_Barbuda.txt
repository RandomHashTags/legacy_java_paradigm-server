      <p><b>Antigua and Barbuda</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Island_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Island nation">island nation</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on the boundary with the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a> are part of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>. In 2011, about 81,800 people lived there. The capital and largest port and city is <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="St. John's, Antigua and Barbuda">St. John's</a>, on Antigua. The country used to be a part of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>. The nation still belongs to the British Commonwealth. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_and_Barbuda&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_and_Barbuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> is the main part of the economy. It is more than half of the <a href="/wiki/Gross_Domestic_Product" class="mw-redirect" title="Gross Domestic Product">Gross Domestic Product</a> (GDP). Antigua is famous for its many luxury resorts. Investment banking and financial services are also important. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_and_Barbuda&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_and_Barbuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Royal Antigua and Barbuda Defence Force has 285 members. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_and_Barbuda&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Languages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_and_Barbuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Languages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>English is the official language, but many of the locals speak <a href="/wiki/Antiguan_Creole" class="mw-redirect" title="Antiguan Creole">Antiguan Creole</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbudan</a> accent is slightly different from the <a href="/wiki/Antiguan" class="mw-redirect" title="Antiguan">Antiguan</a>. </p>
