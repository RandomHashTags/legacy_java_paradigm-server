      <p><b>Hungary</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>. Its capital city is <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>. Hungary is slightly bigger than its western neighbour <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> and has about 10 million inhabitants. Other countries that border Hungary are <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>. Hungary's official language is the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian language</a>. It has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> (EU) since <a href="/wiki/2004" title="2004">2004</a>. In Hungarian, the country is called <i>Magyarország</i> (literally, "Hungarian country"), or <i>Magyar Köztársaság</i> (Hungarian Republic). This is named after the Magyar tribes who came to Hungary in the late <a href="/wiki/9th_century" title="9th century">9th century</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span id="Medieval_Hungary.2C_896-1526"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Medieval_Hungary,_896-1526">Medieval Hungary, 896-1526</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Medieval Hungary, 896-1526">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Medieval Hungary, 896-1526">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Arpadfeszty.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Arpadfeszty.jpg/250px-Arpadfeszty.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="174" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Arpadfeszty.jpg/375px-Arpadfeszty.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Arpadfeszty.jpg/500px-Arpadfeszty.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4386" data-file-height="3044"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Arpadfeszty.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>The arrival of the <a href="/wiki/Magyar" class="mw-redirect" title="Magyar">Magyars</a> (Hungarians) in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carpathian_Basin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carpathian Basin (not yet started)">Carpathian Basin</a>.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>The Magyars established Hungary in 896, after they had arrived there from their previous, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern European</a> territories. Prince <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81rp%C3%A1d&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Árpád (not yet started)">Árpád</a> was their leader at the time; he also established the first royal house of the country, the Árpád-house. In <a href="/wiki/1000" title="1000">1000</a>, after the first king, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Stephen" title="Saint Stephen">Saint Stephen</a> had been crowned, the country became a <a href="/wiki/Kingdom" title="Kingdom">Kingdom</a>. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/1241" title="1241">1241</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a> invaded the country, causing the Hungarian king Béla IV to flee and approximately 500,000 Hungarians were killed, along with with heavy damage. In <a href="/wiki/1301" title="1301">1301</a>, the Árpád-house died out. Later on, kings from various houses ruled over Hungary. The greatest of them is Matthias Corvinus, famous for taking Austrian terrirories like Vienna, etc. and protecting the country against <a href="/wiki/Ottoman" class="mw-redirect" title="Ottoman">Ottoman</a> aggression. However, some decades after he had died (<a href="/wiki/1490" title="1490">1490</a>), the Ottoman Sultan <a href="/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent" title="Suleiman the Magnificent">Suleiman the Magnificent</a> defeated the Hungarian king in the Battle of Mohács (<a href="/wiki/1526" title="1526">1526</a>). The Kingdom was cut up into three parts: the western and northern areas remained Hungary, the southern region fell under Ottoman rule, and the eastern part became an Ottoman vassal as the Principality of <a href="/wiki/Transylvania" title="Transylvania">Transylvania</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Between_1526-1867">Between 1526-1867</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Between 1526-1867">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Between 1526-1867">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Kingdom of Hungary became a <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> dependency, because an agreement made in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> in <a href="/wiki/1515" title="1515">1515</a> said that the Habsburg family would take over the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Jagiellon_dynasty" title="Jagiellon dynasty">Jagellion</a> family in <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemia</a> and Hungary if the line of kings should die out. In <a href="/wiki/1686" title="1686">1686</a>, the Ottomans were forced to leave, and the country was reunified. Many nationalities were living in the country at this time. Along with Hungarians, there were also the ancestors of modern <a href="/wiki/Slovaks" title="Slovaks">Slovaks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Romanians" class="mw-redirect" title="Romanians">Romanians</a>. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/1703" title="1703">1703</a>, Francis II Rákóczi, a Hungarian nobleman organized a revolution against the Habsburgs, as the Hungarians were not satisfied with them. However, his revolution failed in <a href="/wiki/1711" title="1711">1711</a>, and he had to go into <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">exile</a>. The next most important event is the "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungarian_Revolution_of_1848&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hungarian Revolution of 1848 (not yet started)">Hungarian Revolution of 1848</a>", when the inhabitants of the country mutinied again. Fighting ended in <a href="/wiki/1849" title="1849">1849</a>, with Habsburg success. The leaders of the revolution were executed. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Austria-Hungary.2C_1867-1918"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Austria-Hungary,_1867-1918">Austria-Hungary, 1867-1918</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Austria-Hungary, 1867-1918">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Austria-Hungary, 1867-1918">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The country tried to find the way towards consolidation: in <a href="/wiki/1867" title="1867">1867</a>, the two most important Habsburg territories, Austria and Hungary signed a treaty, and they established the <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austro-Hungarian</a> Monarchy. The new situation was feasible for both sides: the agreement brought massive economic growth. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Between_the_World_Wars.2C_1918-1946"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Between_the_World_Wars,_1918-1946">Between the World Wars, 1918-1946</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Between the World Wars, 1918-1946">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Between the World Wars, 1918-1946">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In 1918 after the defeat in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, the Kingdom was abolished, and a republic was established, as people had enough of war. This republic was short-lived, and soon the Communists seized power (<a href="/wiki/1919" title="1919">1919</a>). Their rule was irresponsible and many people, including the peasants and the <a href="/wiki/Intelligentsia" class="mw-redirect" title="Intelligentsia">intelligentsia</a>, were tortured. The communists had to abandon the country as <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> troops invaded. </p>
      <p>In late 1919, the Hungarian forces, led by Admiral Miklós Horthy, finally occupied the capital, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>. In response to the communist terror, they launched the "White Terror", in which they persecuted the communists and their supporters. The kingdom was re-established but there was no king. Horthy was elected as the <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a> of the country, as the assembly decided not to recall the Habsburgs. </p>
      <p>Hungary lost the war. According to the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Trianon" title="Treaty of Trianon">Treaty of Trianon</a> (<a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a>), Hungary lost two thirds of its territory. In the majority of these territories, non-Hungarians were the majority; however, many Hungarians also found themselves outside their country, in the nation state of <a href="/wiki/Romanians" class="mw-redirect" title="Romanians">Romanians</a> or <a href="/wiki/Slovaks" title="Slovaks">Slovaks</a>. Thus, the main political goal of the governments under Horthy was to revise this treaty, and at least, to <a href="/wiki/Irredentism" title="Irredentism">regain</a> the Hungarian-inhabited lands. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Communist_Hungary.2C_1946-1989"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Communist_Hungary,_1946-1989">Communist Hungary, 1946-1989</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Communist Hungary, 1946-1989">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Communist Hungary, 1946-1989">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>After the fall of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>, Soviet troops occupied all of the country. So Hungary gradually became a communist satellite state of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. After 1948, Communist leader <a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1ty%C3%A1s_R%C3%A1kosi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mátyás Rákosi (not yet started)">Mátyás Rákosi</a> established Stalinist rule in the country. He forced collectivization and a <a href="/wiki/Planned_economy" class="mw-redirect" title="Planned economy">planned economy</a>. This led to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=1956_Hungarian_Revolution&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1956 Hungarian Revolution (not yet started)">1956 Hungarian Revolution</a>. Hungary withdrew from the <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Pact" title="Warsaw Pact">Warsaw Pact</a>. But the Soviets sent in over 150,000 troops and 2,500 tanks.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Nearly a quarter of a million people left the country during the brief time that the borders were open in 1956. <a href="/w/index.php?title=J%C3%A1nos_K%C3%A1d%C3%A1r&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="János Kádár (not yet started)">János Kádár</a> became leader of the communist party. In 1991 Soviet military presence in Hungary ended, and the transition to a market economy began. </p>

      <p>Today, Hungary is a democratic republic. Elections are held every four years. </p>
      <p>The current president of the country is <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A1nos_%C3%81der" title="János Áder">János Áder</a>, and the prime minister is <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Orb%C3%A1n" title="Viktor Orbán">Viktor Orbán</a>. Orbán was Prime Minister from 1998 to 2002, and was elected again in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-voa_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voa-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Hungarians make up the largest part of the population but there are also several other ethnic communities. The largest of these are the <a href="/wiki/Roma_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Roma people">Roma</a> and Germans. Hungarian is the most widely used language in Hungary. Ethnic communities often use their own languages as well. The majority of Hungarians are religious, mainly Roman Catholic and <a href="/wiki/Calvinist" class="mw-redirect" title="Calvinist">Calvinist</a> (in Hungarian 'református' - 'reformed'). There are <a href="/wiki/Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Jews">Jews</a>, often in intellectual and artistic occupations, and some <a href="/wiki/Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>. <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Christianity" class="mw-redirect" title="Orthodox Christianity">Orthodox Christianity</a> is practised by ethnic minorities from eastern Europe. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Kekesteto1.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Kekesteto1.JPG/300px-Kekesteto1.JPG" decoding="async" width="300" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Kekesteto1.JPG/450px-Kekesteto1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Kekesteto1.JPG/600px-Kekesteto1.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200"></a>

         </div>The Kékestető

      <p>Hungary's highest point is <i>Kékestető</i> (1014&nbsp;m; in the northeastern part of Hungary). The River <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>, one of Europe's largest rivers, divides Hungary into a western and an eastern part. The region west of the Danube is called Transdanubia (<i>Dunántúl</i>). Transdanubia has a hilly landscape and many small villages and towns. The large flat area in the eastern part is the Great Plains (<i>Alföld</i>). Along the Slovakian border there are mountains. </p>
      <p>Lake <a href="/wiki/Balaton" title="Balaton">Balaton</a>, Central Europe's largest lake can be found in Hungary. This small country has several thermal spas too. Lake Hévíz is one of the world's largest thermal lakes. Hungary, however is a landlocked country (it has no coastline). </p>
      <p>Its biggest cities are <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Debrecen" title="Debrecen">Debrecen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miskolc" title="Miskolc">Miskolc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Szeged" title="Szeged">Szeged</a>, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9cs" title="Pécs">Pécs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gy%C5%91r" title="Győr">Győr</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ny%C3%ADregyh%C3%A1za&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nyíregyháza (not yet started)">Nyíregyháza</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Szolnok&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Szolnok (not yet started)">Szolnok</a>. Szolnok is found at the juncture of the Tisza River and the Zagyva River (stream). </p>
