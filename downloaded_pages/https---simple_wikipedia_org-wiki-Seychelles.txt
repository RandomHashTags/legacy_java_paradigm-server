      <p><b>Seychelles</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Seychelles" title="Victoria, Seychelles">Victoria</a>. The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a> are <a href="/wiki/Creole" class="mw-redirect" title="Creole">Creole</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> is made up of 115 <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a>. </p>
      <p>Seychelles and the islands to the south, <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, all had legal <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> and were part of the <a href="/wiki/Slave_trade" title="Slave trade">slave trade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Most of the people are descendants of freed slaves, who make up about 90% of the population. Many of them also are descended from slave owners who abused their ancestors.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> There are small minorities of immigrants from Europe, China and India. Most people are Roman <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholics</a>, about 90% of them. About 8% are <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestants</a>. </p>
      <p>Other nearby island countries and territories include <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rodrigues&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rodrigues (not yet started)">Rodrigues</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Agalega&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Agalega (not yet started)">Agalega</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a> to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Comoros" title="Comoros">Comoros</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mayotte" title="Mayotte">Mayotte</a> to the southwest. Seychelles has an estimated population of 86,525. It is the smallest population of any African state.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Seychelles is to the northeast of <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and about 1,600&nbsp;km (994&nbsp;mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>. The number of islands in the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> is often given as 115 but the Constitution of the Republic of Seychelles lists 155. </p>
      <p>According to the president of <a href="/wiki/Nauru" title="Nauru">Nauru</a>, the Seychelles has been ranked the ninth most endangered nation due to flooding from <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Some of the districts in Seychelles include: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anse_Boileau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anse Boileau (not yet started)">Anse Boileau</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Takamaka,_Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Takamaka, Seychelles (not yet started)">Takamaka</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cote_D%E2%80%99Or&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cote D’Or (not yet started)">Cote D’Or</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subdivisions">Subdivisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Subdivisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Subdivisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Seychelles is divided into twenty-five administrative regions. Eight of the districts make up the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> of Seychelles. They are called Greater <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Seychelles" title="Victoria, Seychelles">Victoria</a>. Another 14 districts are considered the rural part of the main island of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mah%C3%A9,_Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mahé, Seychelles (not yet started)">Mahé</a>. There are two districts on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Praslin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Praslin (not yet started)">Praslin</a> and one on <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Digue&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="La Digue (not yet started)">La Digue</a> which also include satellite islands. The rest of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Outer_Islands_(Seychelles)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Outer Islands (Seychelles) (not yet started)">Outer Islands</a> are not considered part of any district. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>During the plantation era, <a href="/wiki/Cinnamon" title="Cinnamon">cinnamon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanilla" title="Vanilla">vanilla</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a> were the main <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exports</a>. In the 1960s, about 33% of the working population worked at plantations, and 20% worked in the public or government sector. In 1971, with the opening of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Seychelles International Airport (not yet started)">Seychelles International Airport</a>, tourism became a serious industry. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Flora and fauna">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Flora and fauna">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Like many fragile island ecosystems, the Seychelles had loss of <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> during early human history. This included the disappearance of most of the <a href="/wiki/Giant_tortoise" title="Giant tortoise">giant tortoises</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granitic</a> islands. There was also the <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinction</a> of species such as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles_Chestnut-flanked_White-eye&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Seychelles Chestnut-flanked White-eye (not yet started)">chestnut flanked white eye</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Seychelles_Parakeet" class="mw-redirect" title="Seychelles Parakeet">Seychelles Parakeet</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles_Black_Terrapin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Seychelles Black Terrapin (not yet started)">Seychelles Black Terrapin</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Saltwater_crocodile" title="Saltwater crocodile">saltwater crocodile</a>. However, extinctions were far fewer than on islands such as <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>. This was partly due to a shorter period of human occupation being only since 1770. The Seychelles today is known for success stories in protecting its flora and fauna. The rare <a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychelles_Black_Parrot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Seychelles Black Parrot (not yet started)">Seychelles Black Parrot</a>, the national bird of the country, is now protected. </p>
      <p>The granitic islands of Seychelles are home to about 75 <a href="/wiki/Endemic" class="mw-redirect" title="Endemic">endemic</a> plant species. There are a further 25 or so species in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aldabra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aldabra (not yet started)">Aldabra</a> group. Particularly well-known is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Coco_de_Mer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Coco de Mer (not yet started)">Coco de Mer</a>, a species of palm that grows only on the islands of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Praslin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Praslin (not yet started)">Praslin</a> and neighbouring <a href="/w/index.php?title=Curieuse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Curieuse (not yet started)">Curieuse</a>. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jellyfish_tree&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jellyfish tree (not yet started)">jellyfish tree</a> is to be found in only a few locations on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mahe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mahe (not yet started)">Mahe</a>. This strange and ancient plant is in a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of its own (Medusagynaceae). Other unique plant species include the Wright's Gardenia <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rothmannia_annae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rothmannia annae (not yet started)">Rothmannia annae</a></i> found only on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aride_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aride Island (not yet started)">Aride</a> Island Special Reserve. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Freshwater_crab" title="Freshwater crab">freshwater crab</a> genus <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seychellum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Seychellum (not yet started)">Seychellum</a></i> is endemic to the granitic Seychelles. There are a further 26 species of <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crabs</a> and 5 species of <a href="/wiki/Hermit_crab" title="Hermit crab">hermit crabs</a> that live on the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-Haig_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Haig-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>

        </div>
       </div>

      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aldabra_Giant_Tortoise&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aldabra Giant Tortoise (not yet started)">Aldabra Giant Tortoise</a> now lives on many of the islands of the Seychelles. The Aldabra population is the largest in the world. These unique reptiles can be found even in <a href="/wiki/Captive" class="mw-redirect" title="Captive">captive</a> herds. </p>
      <p>There are several unique varieties of orchids on the Islands. </p>
      <p>The marine life around the islands, especially the more remote coral islands, can be spectacular. More than 1,000 species of fish have been recorded. Since the use of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Speargun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Speargun (not yet started)">spearguns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dynamite" title="Dynamite">dynamite</a> for fishing was banned in the 1960s, the wildlife is unafraid of <a href="/wiki/Snorkeling" title="Snorkeling">snorkelers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">divers</a>. <a href="/wiki/Coral_bleaching" title="Coral bleaching">Coral bleaching</a> in 1998 has damaged most reefs, but some reefs show healthy recovery. </p>
      <p>The main natural resources of the Seychelles are fish, <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cinnamon" title="Cinnamon">cinnamon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coconut" class="mw-redirect" title="Coconut">coconuts</a>, salt and iron. </p>
