      <p><b>Ireland</b> (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">Éire</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:IPA_for_Irish" class="extiw" title="en:WP:IPA for Irish">[ˈeːɾʲə]</a></span> <span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eire_pronunciation.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20"></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e0/Eire_pronunciation.ogg" class="internal" title="Eire pronunciation.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span>), also known as the <b>Republic of Ireland</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> country on the island of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. It is a member of the European Union. The country is sometimes called the <i>Republic of Ireland</i> in order to distinguish between the territory of the Irish state and island proper, but Ireland is its official name. It covers about five-sixths of the island of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. The other sixth is called <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, which is still part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. The republic's <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">Cork</a> is the second largest city in the country and the third largest on the island of Ireland. The population of Ireland is 4.6 million. <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is the main language spoken. The <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish language</a> is widely taught in all schools, and it is still spoken as a first language in some areas of the country, known as the <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht" title="Gaeltacht">Gaeltacht</a>. Irish is the first official language, followed by English. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Ireland&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Several hundred years ago, the English took over the country and made it part of the United Kingdom in 1801. Ireland left the United Kingdom in 1922 and has been an independent country since then. It was a <a href="/wiki/Neutral_country" class="mw-redirect" title="Neutral country">neutral country</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Ireland&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg/220px-Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="309" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg/330px-Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg/440px-Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1083" data-file-height="1522"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Michael_D._Higgins_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div><a href="/wiki/President_of_Ireland" title="President of Ireland">President of Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_D._Higgins" title="Michael D. Higgins">Michael D. Higgins</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The head of State, the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ireland" title="President of Ireland">President</a>, is elected for seven years by the adult <a href="/wiki/Citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Citizen">citizens</a>. The same president can only be elected twice (14 years). Most of the president's tasks are representing and ceremonial ones. The president has little power over the government. </p>
      <p>Ireland is a democracy and people vote for their representatives. The <a href="/wiki/Taoiseach" title="Taoiseach">Taoiseach</a> (equivalent to a prime minister) is selected by the parliament. The Taoiseach is usually the head of the biggest party. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas" title="Oireachtas">Oireachtas</a> (equivalent to the parliament) consists of the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann" title="Dáil Éireann">Dáil Éireann</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Lower_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Lower House">lower House</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Seanad" class="mw-redirect" title="Seanad">Seanad</a> (the 'upper House'). The system is similar to that of France or the United States, except that the President is not an <a href="/wiki/Executive" class="mw-disambig" title="Executive">executive</a>. </p>
      <p>The main political parties in Ireland are <a href="/wiki/Fine_Gael" title="Fine Gael">Fine Gael</a> 25%, <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a> 22%, <a href="/wiki/Fianna_Fail" class="mw-redirect" title="Fianna Fail">Fianna Fail</a> 18%, the Labour Party 7% and others, ref RED C poll 29 th June 2014. </p>
