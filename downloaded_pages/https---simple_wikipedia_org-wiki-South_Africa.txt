      <p>The <b>Republic of South Africa</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the southern <a href="/wiki/Region" title="Region">region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. About fifty-seven <a href="/wiki/Million" title="Million">million</a> people live there. South Africa is next to <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swaziland" class="mw-redirect" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a>. </p>
      <p>The biggest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> of South Africa is <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>. The country has three <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capitals</a> for different purposes. They are <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bloemfontein" title="Bloemfontein">Bloemfontein</a>. This is because the <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> is based in Pretoria, the <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> is in Cape Town and the Supreme Court is in Bloemfontein.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are 11 <a href="/wiki/National_language" title="National language">national languages</a>. They are <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="English Language">English</a>, Ndebele, Xhosa, <a href="/wiki/Zulu_language" title="Zulu language">Zulu</a>, Swati, Setswana, Sesotho, Sesotho sa Leboa, Venda and Tsonga. They are also known as National Lexicography Units (NLUs). Because of all the languages, the country has an <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/official" class="extiw" title="wikt:official">official</a> name in each language. </p>
      <p>One of South Africa's most well known people is <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a>. He was its <a href="/wiki/President_of_South_Africa" title="President of South Africa">president</a> from 1994 until 1999. He died in 2013 at the age of 95. The current president is <a href="/wiki/Cyril_Ramaphosa" title="Cyril Ramaphosa">Cyril Ramaphosa</a>. </p>
      <p>Many rare animals such as <a href="/wiki/Lions" class="mw-redirect" title="Lions">lions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheetahs" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheetahs">cheetahs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zebras" class="mw-redirect" title="Zebras">zebras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giraffes" class="mw-redirect" title="Giraffes">giraffes</a> live in South Africa. </p>
      <p>South Africa is one of the richest countries in Africa. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The first <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> people to come to <b>South Africa</b> were Portuguese explorers. In 1487, <a href="/wiki/Bartolomeu_Dias" title="Bartolomeu Dias">Bartolomeu Dias</a> found what he called the "Cape of Storms". The <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a> of <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> changed it to "<a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>". He called it this because the cape gave the Portuguese a new chance to find a sea route to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1652, <a href="/wiki/Jan_van_Riebeeck" title="Jan van Riebeeck">Jan van Riebeeck</a> <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colonized</a> the Cape. He started the camp for the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a>. This was so that they could give fresh food to ships on their way to the south of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. There were very few <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/native" class="extiw" title="wikt:native">native</a> people living in the Cape. Because of this, slaves were brought from <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> to work at the colony. In 1795, <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> took the Cape from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a>, to stop <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> from taking it. The British gave it back in 1803 but then <a href="/wiki/Annexation" title="Annexation">annexed</a> the Cape in 1807 when the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a> went bankrupt. </p>
      <p>Diamonds were found in South Africa in 1867. Gold was found in 1884. This made a great number of people to come to South Africa from Europe. They were hoping to make money. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/First_Boer_War" title="First Boer War">First Boer War</a> happened in 1880-1881. The war was between the British and the <a href="/wiki/Boers" class="mw-redirect" title="Boers">Boers</a> Republics. At that time, the British had the Cape. The Boer Republics were established when Dutch settlers moved north in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Great_Trek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Great Trek (not yet started)">Great Trek</a>. The British lost the war and came back 8 years later in 1899. They won this <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">Second Boer War</a> in 1902. The British had brought many more soldiers the second time. The Boers had no chance of winning. On 31 May 1910 the Union of South Africa was made from the Cape and Natal colonies. It was also made from <a href="/wiki/Free_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Free State">Orange Free State</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Transvaal" title="Transvaal">Transvaal</a>. These were two Boer Republics. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="floatleft">
       <a href="/wiki/File:South_Africa_sat.jpg" class="image" title="Satellite picture of South Africa"><img alt="Satellite picture of South Africa" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/South_Africa_sat.jpg/220px-South_Africa_sat.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="170" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/South_Africa_sat.jpg/330px-South_Africa_sat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/South_Africa_sat.jpg/440px-South_Africa_sat.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1974" data-file-height="1527"></a>
      </div> 
      <p>South Africa is found at the southernmost <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/region" class="extiw" title="wikt:region">region</a> of Africa, with a long coastline that reaches more than 2,500&nbsp;km (1,553&nbsp;mi) and along <a href="/wiki/Two" class="mw-redirect" title="Two">two</a> oceans (the South <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a>). At 1,219,912&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (471,011&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi),<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> South Africa is the 25th-largest country in the world and is almost the size of <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Njesuthi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Njesuthi (not yet started)">Njesuthi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Drakensberg" title="Drakensberg">Drakensberg</a> at 3,408&nbsp;m (11,181&nbsp;ft) is the highest part in South Africa. </p> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Drakensburgmountains.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Drakensburgmountains.jpg/220px-Drakensburgmountains.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="148" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Drakensburgmountains.jpg/330px-Drakensburgmountains.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Drakensburgmountains.jpg/440px-Drakensburgmountains.jpg 2x" data-file-width="450" data-file-height="302"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Drakensburgmountains.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>The Drakensberg mountains, the highest mountain range in South Africa
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>The back of South Africa is a big and flat. It is a very <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">populated</a> <a href="/wiki/Scrubland" title="Scrubland">scrubland</a>, the Karoo, which is drier towards the northwest along the Namib desert. The eastern coastline however, is well-watered, which makes a <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> kind of like the tropics. </p>
      <p>To the north of Johannesburg, the <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/altitude" class="extiw" title="wikt:altitude">altitude</a> drops beyond the <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/escarpment"><span style="color: #CC2200;" title="escarpment (Page does not exist on Wiktionary)">escarpment</span></a></span> of the Highveld. It then turns into the lower lying Bushveld, an area of mixed dry forest and a lot of <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">wildlife</a>. East of the Highveld, beyond the eastern escarpment, the Lowveld reaches towards the Indian Ocean. It has mostly high <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperatures</a>, and is also the location of subtropical agriculture. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>South Africa has a mostly <a href="/wiki/Temperate_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate climate">temperate climate</a>. It is surrounded by the Atlantic and Indian Oceans on three sides, and is located in the climatically milder <a href="/wiki/Southern_hemisphere" title="Southern hemisphere">southern hemisphere</a>. Average <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/elevation" class="extiw" title="wikt:elevation">elevation</a> is higher towards the north (towards the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>) and further inland. Because of this <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/vary" class="extiw" title="wikt:vary">varied</a> <a href="/wiki/Topography" title="Topography">topography</a> and oceanic influence, different parts of South Africa have different climates. </p>
      <p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> in the southern Namib in the farthest northwest and <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> climate in the east along the <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> border and the Indian ocean. From the east, the land quickly rises over mountains towards the back <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> known as the Highveld. Even though South Africa is thought as semi-arid, there is a difference in climate as well as topography. </p>
      <p>The southwest has a climate similar to that of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> with mild, moderately wet winters and hot, dry summers. This area also makes much of the wine in South Africa. This <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/region" class="extiw" title="wikt:region">region</a> is also mostly known for its <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">wind</a>, which blows almost all year. The wind can sometimes be severe when passing around the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> mostly gets bad for sailors, making many shipwrecks. Further east on the south coast, rainfall falls more evenly throughout the year, making a green landscape. This area is popularly known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Garden_Route&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Garden Route (not yet started)">Garden Route</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Orange_Free_State" title="Orange Free State">Orange Free State</a> is mostly flat, because it lies in the center of the high plateau. North of the Vaal River, the Highveld becomes better watered and does not get subtropical heat. Johannesburg, in the center of the Highveld, is at 1,740 <a href="/wiki/Meter" class="mw-redirect" title="Meter">m</a> (5,709 <a href="/wiki/Foot_(unit_of_length)" class="mw-redirect" title="Foot (unit of length)">ft</a>) and gets a yearly rainfall of 740 <a href="/wiki/Millimeter" class="mw-redirect" title="Millimeter">mm</a> (29.9 <a href="/wiki/Inch" title="Inch">in</a>). Winters in this region are cold, although <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">snow</a> is rare. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plants_and_wildlife">Plants and wildlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Plants and wildlife">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Plants and wildlife">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>South Africa is ranked sixth out of the world’s 17 megadiverse countries,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> with more than 20,000 different kinds of plants, or about 10% of all the known species of plants on Earth, making it very rich in plant <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>. </p>
      <p>The most common <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biome</a> in South Africa is the grassland, mostly on the Highveld. This is where <a href="/wiki/Poaceae" title="Poaceae">grasses</a>, low shrubs, and acacia trees, mostly camel-thorn and whitethorn are more common then plants. Plants become even more less common towards the northwest. This is because of low <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">rainfall</a>. There are many species of water-storing plants like aloes and euphorbias in the very hot and dry Namaqualand area. The grass and thorn <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> turns slowly into a bush savanna towards the north-east of the country, with more thick growth. There are many numbers of <a href="/wiki/Baobab" title="Baobab">baobab</a> trees in this area, near the northern end of Kruger National Park.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>South Africa's economy is divided. It is divided between <a href="/wiki/First_World" title="First World">First World</a> and <a href="/wiki/Third_World" title="Third World">Third World</a> standards. The developed part of the economy is similar to that of most nations with wealth (for example, <a href="/wiki/Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Britain">Britain</a> or <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>). The rest of the economy is closer to that of poor nations, such as the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. The main companies in South Africa are mining (mostly for gold and diamonds), <a href="/wiki/Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Car">car</a> making, and <a href="/wiki/Service_Sector" class="mw-redirect" title="Service Sector">service industries</a>, such as insurance. </p>
      <p>Unemployment is very high. Income inequality is about the same as <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. During 1995–2003, the number of older jobs went down. <a href="/wiki/Informal_economy" title="Informal economy">Informal jobs</a> went up. Overall unemployment got worse.<sup id="cite_ref-sach3_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sach3-13">[12]</a></sup> The <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/average" class="extiw" title="wikt:average">average</a> South African household income went down a lot between 1995 and 2000. As for racial inequality, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Statistics_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Statistics South Africa (not yet started)">Statistics South Africa</a> said that in 1995 the average white household earned four times as much as the average <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">black</a> household. In 2000 the average white family was earning six times more than the average black household.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup> The action policies have seen a rise in black economic wealth. There is a developing black middle class.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> Other problems are crime, corruption, and HIV/AIDS. South Africa suffers from mostly heavy overall <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/regulation" class="extiw" title="wikt:regulation">regulation</a> compared to developed countries. <sup id="cite_ref-assessment2008_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-assessment2008-17">[16]</a></sup> <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/Restrictive"><span style="color: #CC2200;" title="Restrictive (Page does not exist on Wiktionary)">Restrictive</span></a></span> labor rules have added to the unemployment weakness.<sup id="cite_ref-sach3_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sach3-13">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>South Africa is a nation of about 50&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Million" title="Million">million</a> people of diverse <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/origin" class="extiw" title="wikt:origin">origins</a>, cultures, <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a>, and religions. The last census was held in 2001 and the next will be in 2011. Statistics South Africa had five racial categories by which people could classify themselves, the last of which, "unspecified/other" drew "not needed" responses, and these results were not counted.<sup id="cite_ref-Census2001_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census2001-18">[17]</a></sup> The 2009 middle-year <a href="/wiki/Estimate" class="mw-redirect" title="Estimate">estimated</a> figures for the other categories were Black African at 79.3%, White at 9.1%, Colored at 9.0%, and <a href="/wiki/Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian">Asian</a> at 2.6%.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Even though the population of South Africa has grown in the past decade<sup id="cite_ref-Census2001_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census2001-18">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup> (mostly because of immigration), the country had an yearly population growth rate of −0.501% in 2008 (CIA est.), counting immigration. The CIA thinks that in 2009 South Africa's population started to grow again, at a rate of 0.281%. South Africa is home to an <a href="/wiki/Estimate" class="mw-redirect" title="Estimate">estimated</a> 5&nbsp;million <a href="/wiki/Illegal_immigrant" class="mw-redirect" title="Illegal immigrant">illegal immigrants</a>, counting about 3&nbsp;million Zimbabweans.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Series" title="Series">series</a> of anti-immigrant riots happened in South Africa beginning on 11 May 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-24">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Society_and_culture">Society and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Society and culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Society and culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>It may be suggested that there is no single South African culture because of its <a href="/wiki/Ethnic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic">ethnic</a> <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/diversity" class="extiw" title="wikt:diversity">diversity</a>. Today, the diversity in foods from many cultures is enjoyed by all and marketed to tourists who wish to taste South African food. Food is not the only thing, music and dance is there too. </p>
      <p>South African food is mostly meat-based and has <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/spawn" class="extiw" title="wikt:spawn">spawned</a> the South African social gathering known as a <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Braai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Braai (not yet started)">braai</a></i>, or <a href="/wiki/Barbecue" title="Barbecue">barbecue</a>. South Africa has also developed into a big wine maker. It has some of the best <a href="/wiki/Vineyard" title="Vineyard">vineyards</a> lying in <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valleys</a> around Stellenbosch, Franschoek, Paarl and Barrydale. </p>
      <p>South Africa is the only Western country of Africa. Most South African blacks still have poor lives. It is among these people, however, that cultural traditions live most strongly. This is because blacks have become <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">urbanised</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_civilization" title="Western civilization">Westernised</a>, much parts of traditional culture have fallen. Urban blacks normally speak English or Afrikaans in addition to their native <a href="/wiki/Tongue" title="Tongue">tongue</a>. There are smaller but still important groups of speakers of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khoisan_(language)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Khoisan (language) (not yet started)">Khoisan</a> languages who are not in the <a href="/wiki/Eleven" class="mw-redirect" title="Eleven">eleven</a> official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a>, but are one of the eight other officially recognized languages. </p>
      <p>Members of middle class, who are mostly white but whose ranks are growing numbers of black, colored and Indian people,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[27]</a></sup> have lifestyles similar in many ways to that of people found in <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>. Members of the middle class often study and work from foreign countries for greater exposure to the markets of the <a href="/wiki/World" title="World">world</a>. </p>
      <p>Approximately 9,800 Roma gypsies live in South Africa. </p>

      <p>According to the 2001 national <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>, Christians accounted for 79.7<a href="/wiki/Percent" class="mw-redirect" title="Percent">%</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a>. This has Zion Christian (11.1%), <a href="/wiki/Pentecostalism" title="Pentecostalism">Pentecostal</a> (Charismatic) (8.2%), <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> (7.1%), <a href="/wiki/Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> (6.8%), Dutch Reformed (6.7%), <a href="/wiki/Anglican" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> (3.8%); members of other Christian churches accounted for another 36% of the population. <a href="/wiki/Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> accounted for 1.5% of the population, <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> about 1.3%, and <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a> 0.2%. 15.1% had no religious affiliation, 2.3% were other and 1.4% were unspecified.<sup id="cite_ref-state.gov_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state.gov-29">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[29]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_at_the_Olympics" title="South Africa at the Olympics">South Africa at the Olympics</a>

      <p>South Africa's most liked <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sports</a> are <a href="/wiki/Soccer" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer">soccer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>. Other sports that are popular are <a href="/wiki/Swimming" title="Swimming">swimming</a>, athletics, <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> and netball. Soccer is the most liked among youth. There are other sports like <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skateboard" title="Skateboard">skateboarding</a> that are becoming more liked. </p> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:GraemeSmith.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/GraemeSmith.jpg/220px-GraemeSmith.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/GraemeSmith.jpg/330px-GraemeSmith.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/GraemeSmith.jpg/440px-GraemeSmith.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600"></a>

        </div>

      <p>Famous boxers from South Africa are Baby Jake Jacob Matlala, Vuyani Bungu, Welcome Ncita, Dingaan Thobela, Gerrie Coetzee and Brian Mitchell. There are football players who have played for major <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/foreign" class="extiw" title="wikt:foreign">foreign</a> clubs. Some of them are Lucas Radebe and Philemon Masinga (both were of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_A.F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds United A.F.C.">Leeds United</a>), Quinton Fortune (Atletico Madrid and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>), Benni McCarthy (Ajax Amsterdam, <a href="/wiki/F.C._Porto" title="F.C. Porto">F.C. Porto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>), Aaron Mokoena (Ajax Amsterdam, Blackburn Rovers and <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a>), Delron Buckley (<a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>) and Steven Pienaar (Ajax Amsterdam and <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>). South Africa made <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor racing's 1979 world champion <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>. Famous current cricket players are Herschelle Gibbs, Graeme Smith, Jacques Kallis, JP Duminy and more. Most of them also participate in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Premier_League" title="Indian Premier League">Indian Premier League</a>. </p>
      <p>South Africa has also made many world class rugby players. Some of them are Francois Pienaar, Joost van der Westhuizen, Danie Craven, Frik du Preez, Naas Botha, and Bryan Habana. South Africa hosted and won the 1995 Rugby World Cup at their first try. They won the 2007 Rugby World Cup in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. South Africa was only allowed to <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/participate" class="extiw" title="wikt:participate">participate</a> from 1995 since the end of <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">Apartheid</a>. It followed the 1995 Rugby World Cup by hosting the 1996 African Cup of Nations. It had the <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">national</a> team, 'Bafana Bafana,' going on to win the <a href="/wiki/Tournament" title="Tournament">tournament</a>. It also hosted the 2003 Cricket World Cup. and the 2007 World Twenty20 Championship. Both of them were a great success. </p>
      <p>In 2010, South Africa became the first African <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> to host the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>. The <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">national team</a> has competed in three World Cups. It bid for the right to stage the <a href="/wiki/2004_Olympic_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Olympic Games">2004 Olympic Games</a>, but finished third to <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>. South Africa is also well known for their <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_cricket_team" title="South Africa national cricket team">cricket team</a>. They are right now the world's number one in test match cricket. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The main schools span the first seven years of schooling. In the age of <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">Apartheid</a>, schools for blacks were subject to judgment through <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/inadequate"><span style="color: #CC2200;" title="inadequate (Page does not exist on Wiktionary)">inadequate</span></a></span> funding and so forth. Instruction can take place in <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a> as well. Public payment on education was at 5.4% of the 2002-05 GDP.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[30]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>South Africa's <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> is the most powerful in southern Africa. South Africa spends more on its military than any of its neighboring countries do. They have very advanced <a href="/wiki/Weapon" title="Weapon">weapons</a> as a result. South Africa used to have <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons</a>, but they were taken apart in 1993. </p>
