      <p><b>Liechtenstein</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>. It is the sixth <a href="/wiki/Small" class="mw-redirect" title="Small">smallest</a> country in the <a href="/wiki/World" title="World">world</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and, with <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>, one of only two doubly landlocked countries. This means that it is landlocked by a landlocked country. Liechtenstein is between <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. </p>
      <p>Liechtenstein was linked with <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> until the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, after which it became linked with <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a> union. </p>
      <p>The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a> in Liechtenstein is German. </p>
      <p>The capital of Liechtenstein is <a href="/wiki/Vaduz" title="Vaduz">Vaduz</a>, a very small town of 5000 people. Liechtenstein is famous for its many private banks. </p>
