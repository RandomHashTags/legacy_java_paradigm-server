      <p><b>The Republic of Uganda</b> is a landlocked <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and biggest city is <a href="/wiki/Kampala" title="Kampala">Kampala</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> is the Ugandan Shilling. The official <a href="/wiki/Languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Languages">languages</a> of Uganda are <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kiswahili" class="mw-redirect" title="Kiswahili">Swahili</a>. The most common <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> is <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>. The President of Uganda is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yoweri_Kaguta_Museveni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yoweri Kaguta Museveni (not yet started)">Yoweri Kaguta Museveni</a>. The country is to the East of Africa. The population of Uganda is 32 million people. The area of Uganda is about 236,040 <sup>km2</sup>. </p>
      <p>Uganda produces <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a>. </p>
      <p>The literacy rate of Uganda is 68%, which mean people in Uganda who are at least 15 years old know how to read and write.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Uganda is among countries thought to be very corrupt by Transparency International. It is rated at 2.4 on a scale from 0 (the most corrupt) to 10 (the most clean).<sup id="cite_ref-CPI_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPI-4">[4]</a></sup> Uganda is one of the poorest nations in the world. 37.7 percent of the people live on less than $1.25 a day.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Uganda became independent from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on 9 October 1962.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Uganda is almost completely in the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> basin. At the centre of the country is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Kyoga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake Kyoga (not yet started)">Lake Kyoga</a>. Although landlocked, Uganda has many large lakes. Besides Lake Victoria and Lake Kyoga, there are <a href="/wiki/Lake_Albert_(Africa)" title="Lake Albert (Africa)">Lake Albert</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Edward&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake Edward (not yet started)">Lake Edward</a> and the smaller <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_George_(Uganda)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake George (Uganda) (not yet started)">Lake George</a>. Most important cities are in the south, near Lake Victoria, including the capital <a href="/wiki/Kampala" title="Kampala">Kampala</a> and the nearby city of <a href="/wiki/Entebbe" title="Entebbe">Entebbe</a>. </p>
      <p>The climate is mostly <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equatorial</a>. But it is not the same everywhere because there are some changes in <a href="/wiki/Altitude" title="Altitude">altitude</a>. The difference in altitude changes the climate. Southern Uganda is wetter with rain generally throughout the year. At <a href="/wiki/Entebbe" title="Entebbe">Entebbe</a> on the northern shore of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a>, most rain falls from March to June and in the November/December period. Further to the north a dry season slowly emerges. At <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gulu (not yet started)">Gulu</a> about 120&nbsp;km from the South Sudanese border, November to February is much drier than the rest of the year. </p>

          </table> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_sport">Culture and sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture and sport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Culture and sport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Because there are so many communities, culture in Uganda is diverse. Many Asians (mostly from India) who had to leave during the regime of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Amin (not yet started)">Amin</a> have returned to Uganda.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p><p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Football</a> is the national sport in Uganda. Games with the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_national_football_team" title="Uganda national football team">Ugandan national football team</a> usually attract large crowds of Ugandans. The Ugandan Super League is the top division of Ugandan football. There are 16 clubs from across the country. It was made in 1968. Uganda's most famous footballers are <a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Obua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="David Obua (not yet started)">David Obua</a> of Scottish club Hearts and <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Sekagya" title="Ibrahim Sekagya">Ibrahim Sekagya</a>, who is the captain of the national team. Uganda's notable past greats of the game include <a href="/w/index.php?title=Denis_Obua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Denis Obua (not yet started)">Denis Obua</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Majid_Musisi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Majid Musisi (not yet started)">Majid Musisi</a>, Fimbo Mukasa and Paul Kasule. </p><p><a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a> has become more popular. Uganda won the <a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Cricket_League&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World Cricket League (not yet started)">World Cricket League</a> (WCL) Division 3 in 2007. </p><p>In 2007, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda_national_rugby_union_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Uganda national rugby union team (not yet started)">Uganda national rugby union team</a> were victorious in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Africa_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2007 Africa Cup (not yet started)">2007 Africa Cup</a>, beating <a href="/w/index.php?title=Madagascar_national_rugby_union_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Madagascar national rugby union team (not yet started)">Madagascar</a> in the final. </p><p>Hockey is the only Ugandan field sport to date to have qualified for and represented the country at the Olympics. This was at the <a href="/wiki/1972_Summer_Olympics" title="1972 Summer Olympics">1972 Summer Olympics</a>. Uganda won gold medals at the Olympics in athletics with legendary hurdler <a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Akii-Bua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="John Akii-Bua (not yet started)">John Akii-Bua</a> in 1972 and marathon winner at the London 2012 Olympics <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_Kiprotich&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stephen Kiprotich (not yet started)">Stephen Kiprotich</a>. </p><p>In July 2011 Kampala, Uganda qualified for the 2011 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_League_World_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Little League World Series (not yet started)">Little League World Series</a>. Due to <a href="/wiki/Visa" title="Visa">visa</a> trouble they were unable to attend the Series.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2012, Uganda qualified again for the Little League World Series. This time they were able to attend. </p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_pages">Related pages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Related pages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Uganda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Related pages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
