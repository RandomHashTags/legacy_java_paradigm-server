      <p>The <b>Solomon Islands</b> are a group of <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a> in the South Pacific. They are known for their beauty and for the many languages spoken there. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of the Solomon Islands is <a href="/wiki/Honiara" title="Honiara">Honiara</a>. </p>

      <p>In the 1890s the islands became a <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a> forces arrived in early 1942 and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> attacked them, starting the <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal_Campaign" title="Guadalcanal Campaign">Guadalcanal Campaign</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_unrest">Civil unrest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Civil unrest">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Civil unrest">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>There was civil untrest between 1998 and 2003, commonly called <i>the tensions</i> or <i>the ethnic tension</i>; it included fighting between the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Isatabu_Freedom_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Isatabu Freedom Movement (not yet started)">Isatabu Freedom Movement</a> (also known as the Guadalcanal Revolutionary Army) and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaita_Eagle_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Malaita Eagle Force (not yet started)">Malaita Eagle Force</a> (as well as the Marau Eagle Force). (Although much of the conflict was between <a href="/w/index.php?title=Guales&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Guales (not yet started)">Guales</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaitan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Malaitan (not yet started)">Malaitans</a>, two research papers say that the 'ethnic conflict' label is an oversimplification.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2006, riots broke out following the election of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Snyder_Rini&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Snyder Rini (not yet started)">Snyder Rini</a> as Prime Minister, destroying part of the capital's Chinatown and displacing more than 1,000 Chinese residents;<sup id="cite_ref-McKinnon2009_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McKinnon2009-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Regional_Assistance_Mission_to_Solomon_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Regional Assistance Mission to Solomon Islands (not yet started)">Regional Assistance Mission to Solomon Islands</a> (RAMSI), the 16-country Pacific Islands Forum initiative set up in 2003 with assistance from Australia, intervened, sending in additional police and army officers to bring the situation under control. A <a href="/wiki/Motion_of_no_confidence" title="Motion of no confidence">motion of no confidence</a> was passed against the Prime Minister. Following his resignation, a five-party Grand Coalition for Change Government was formed in May 2006, with Manasseh Sogavare as Prime Minister, quelling the riots and running the government. The army part of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Regional_Assistance_Mission_to_Solomon_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Regional Assistance Mission to Solomon Islands (not yet started)">RAMSI</a> was removed and rebuilding took shape.<sup id="cite_ref-Doing_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doing-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_change_of_Prime_Minister">2007 change of Prime Minister</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 2007 change of Prime Minister">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: 2007 change of Prime Minister">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 13 December 2007, Prime Minister <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manasseh_Sogavare&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manasseh Sogavare (not yet started)">Manasseh Sogavare</a> was toppled by a vote of no confidence in Parliament,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> following the <a href="/wiki/Defection" title="Defection">defection</a> of five ministers to the opposition. It was the first time a prime minister had lost office in this way in Solomon Islands. On 20 December, Parliament elected the opposition's candidate (and former Minister for Education) <a href="/w/index.php?title=Derek_Sikua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Derek Sikua (not yet started)">Derek Sikua</a> as Prime Minister, in a vote of 32 to 15.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earthquakes">Earthquakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Earthquakes">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Earthquakes">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 2 April 2007 at 07:39:56 local time (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B11" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC+11">UTC+11</a>) an earthquake with magnitude 8.1 occurred at <a href="/wiki/Hypocenter" title="Hypocenter">hypocenter</a> S8.453 E156.957, 349&nbsp;kilometres (217&nbsp;miles) northwest of the island's capital, <a href="/wiki/Honiara" title="Honiara">Honiara</a> and south-east of the capital of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Province_(Solomon_Islands)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Western Province (Solomon Islands) (not yet started)">Western Province</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gizo,_Solomon_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gizo, Solomon Islands (not yet started)">Gizo</a>, at a depth of 10&nbsp;km (6.2&nbsp;miles).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> More than 44 <a href="/wiki/Aftershock" title="Aftershock">aftershocks</a> with magnitude 5.0 or greater occurred up until 22:00:00 UTC, Wednesday, 4 April 2007. A <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> followed killing at least 52 people, destroying more than 900 homes and leaving thousands of people homeless.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Land upthrust extended the shoreline of one island, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ranongga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ranongga (not yet started)">Ranongga</a>, by up to 70&nbsp;metres (230&nbsp;ft) exposing many once pristine coral reefs.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On February 6, 2013, an earthquake with magnitude of 8.0 occurred at <a href="/wiki/Epicentre" class="mw-redirect" title="Epicentre">epicentre</a> S10.80 E165.11 in the Santa Cruz Islands followed by a tsunami up to 1.5&nbsp;metres. At least nine people were killed and many houses demolished. The main quake was preceded by a sequence of earthquakes with a magnitude of up to 6.0. </p>
