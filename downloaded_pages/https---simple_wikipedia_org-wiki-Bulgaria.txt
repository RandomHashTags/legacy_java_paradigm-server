      <p><b>Bulgaria</b> (officially called the <b>Republic of Bulgaria</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">southeastern Europe</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and the biggest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>. Their money is called the lev. The Bulgarian government is a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rumen_Radev" title="Rumen Radev">Rumen Radev</a> became President in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-president.bg_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-president.bg-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of Bulgaria is a little more than 7 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_factbook_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_factbook-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Bulgaria has its own language, called <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>. It is a type of <a href="/wiki/Slavic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavic language">Slavic language</a>. It is related to languages like <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>. Bulgaria borders <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Macedonia" title="North Macedonia">North Macedonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>. One of the national heroes of Bulgaria is <a href="/wiki/Vasil_Levski" title="Vasil Levski">Vasil Levski</a> who led the fight for independence in the late 1800s. Bulgaria is an ancient country, some places have had people living there for over 6,000 years. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bulgaria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In what is now Bulgaria, many different people and different <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">cultures</a> lived over time. This includes <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hamangia_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hamangia culture (not yet started)">Hamangia culture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vin%C4%8Da_culture" title="Vinča culture">Vinča culture</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eneolithic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eneolithic (not yet started)">eneolithic</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Varna_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Varna culture (not yet started)">Varna culture</a> (5th millennium BC) and the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ezero_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ezero culture (not yet started)">Ezero culture</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Thracians" title="Thracians">Thracians</a> lived in the area of modern Bulgaria. King <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tere&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tere (not yet started)">Tere</a> united the people in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Odrysian_kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Odrysian kingdom (not yet started)">Odrysian kingdom</a> around 500&nbsp;BC. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a> had influence over the people on the 4th century and later by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> during the time of <a href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesus Christ">Christ</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Avars" title="Eurasian Avars">Eurasian Avars</a>, South <a href="/wiki/Slavs" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavs">Slavs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a> settled all over the territory of modern Bulgaria during the 6th century. </p>
      <p>The first Bulgarian empire was established in 681&nbsp;AD. The second Bulgarian empire started about 1185&nbsp;AD, with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Turnovo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Turnovo (not yet started)">Turnovo</a> as the capital. The <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> took over in 1396, and ruled Bulgaria for about 500 years. The Ottomans had very strict rules and the Bulgarians suffered. The Bulgarians rebelled several times against the Turkish rulers. After the first Balkan war (1876), Bulgaria pulled away from <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>'s influence with the help of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> who were already fighting the Ottomans. But it was not until 1908 that the whole country of Bulgaria was united as modern Bulgaria. </p>
      <p>Bulgaria joined the side of Germany in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and lost. </p>
      <p>During the first year of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> Bulgaria said it was neutral and refused to join sides with <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. But the increased tension in the war made the leaders sign the <a href="/wiki/Tripartite_Pact" title="Tripartite Pact">Tripartite Pact</a> with Germany in March 1941. This let German forces go through Bulgaria to reach Greece. However, Bulgaria did not follow Hitler's wishes. Bulgaria's troops refused to participate in military coups and also refused to send <a href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jews</a> to <a href="/wiki/Concentration_camp" title="Concentration camp">concentration camps</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Holocaust" class="mw-redirect" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a>. This made Adolf Hitler very suspicious, and the king of Bulgaria, <a href="/wiki/Tsar" title="Tsar">Tsar</a> Boris III, died one day after talking to Hitler. </p>
      <p>In 1944 when it became clear that the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied Powers</a> would win the war, Bulgaria had to find another solution. Their leaders declared that they withdrew from the <a href="/wiki/Axis_countries" title="Axis countries">Axis</a> forces and they let the Soviets come in. But the Soviet Union didn't respect Bulgaria's wish to be neutral. They replaced the royal monarchy with <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">Communism</a> in 1947 before they withdrew from Bulgaria. For 40 years, under their leader Todor Zhivkov, Bulgaria was very close to the Soviet Union and followed their instructions. </p>
      <p>Shortly after the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a>, on November 10 1989, the Communist party gave up their rule and allowed the country to elect leaders of their own choice. But in a few years Bulgaria had serious money problems under the new <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialist government</a>. Since that time Bulgaria has recovered in many ways, and is much more stable. But it is still one of the poorest countries in Europe. Problems in the administration, a weak court system and organized crime are the biggest issues for Bulgaria.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_factbook_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_factbook-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_and_Social_Welfare">Economy and Social Welfare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bulgaria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy and Social Welfare">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Economy and Social Welfare">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Bulgaria's economy was dependent on the <a href="/w/index.php?title=COMECON&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="COMECON (not yet started)">COMECON</a> market. This was a group of communist countries (Soviet Union, Bulgaria, <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>) that agreed to work together. This group fell apart in 1989 with the fall of communism in Europe. While this was good for personal freedom, it was too much of a change for the businesses and jobs. Also Yugoslavia, their neighbor, fell into a <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a> as their country broke apart, and that did not help Bulgaria. Living standards fell by 40 percent as people lost their jobs and their savings were not worth much, and even some newborn babies were sent to orphanages when their parents could not take care of them. </p>
      <p>In 1994 Bulgaria had a short economic boom. But that slowed down in 1996 from bad finance deals and other banking problems. In 1997 there was high <a href="/wiki/Inflation" title="Inflation">inflation</a> (the money could not buy as much as it did before). Retired people had little income (their pension did not go up as fast as inflation did) and their savings were not worth very much. Many retired people were begging in the streets for food. But since then the economy has grown steadily. People from outside the country started putting money into businesses and houses, and Bulgarians learned how to make their own businesses. </p>
      <p>Today Bulgaria has Europe's lowest income and corporate tax, 10 percent for both individuals and businesses. This makes Bulgaria a very good place for business. Bulgaria also has skilled and educated workers. The average monthly salary in Bulgaria is still cheap at 400 euro or $541 USD.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The state no longer has free health care. This system has been replaced by an insurance plan that people pay for, and drug costs are based on income. Private health care is encouraged nowadays. Help for people who lose their jobs is no longer automatic, but some help is available, based on family status and length of unemployment.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The retirement age for men is 64 years and for women 61 years.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It will be 63 years and 9 months for woman and 65 years and 3 months for man in 2032. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provinces">Provinces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bulgaria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Provinces">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bulgaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Provinces">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Since 1999, Bulgaria has been divided into 28 <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a> or regions. Each province is named after its local capital. The provinces are divided into <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipalities</a>, there are 264 in total. </p>
