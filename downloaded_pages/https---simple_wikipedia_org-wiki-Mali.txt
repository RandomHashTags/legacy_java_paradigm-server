      <p><b>Mali</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> (a country entirely surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Land" title="Land">land</a>) in <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>. Mali is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> on the <a href="/wiki/North" title="North">north</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> on the <a href="/wiki/East" title="East">east</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> and the <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire" class="mw-redirect" title="CÃ´te d'Ivoire">CÃ´te d'Ivoire</a> on the <a href="/wiki/South" title="South">south</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> on the south-west, and <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> on the <a href="/wiki/West" title="West">west</a>. Physical features of Mali include the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> in the north, with the <a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9n%C3%A9gal_River" class="mw-redirect" title="SÃ©nÃ©gal River">SÃ©nÃ©gal River</a> in the southern part of the country. As of a July 2011 <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/estimate" class="extiw" title="wikt:estimate">estimate</a>, Mali has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of approximately 14,000,000 people. Mali has a total <a href="/wiki/Area" title="Area">area</a> of 1,220,190 square kilometres (471,120&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). Most of the people live in the southern part of the country, with Mali's <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and most populated <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> being <a href="/wiki/Bamako" title="Bamako">Bamako</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mali&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mali&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Mand%C3%A9" class="mw-redirect" title="MandÃ©">MandÃ©</a> people founded several <a href="/wiki/Kingdom" title="Kingdom">kingdoms</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahel</a>. This was a big area that included Mali. These kingdoms included the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_Empire" title="Ghana Empire">Ghana Empire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mali_Empire" title="Mali Empire">Mali Empire</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Songhai_Empire" title="Songhai Empire">Songhai Empire</a>. <a href="/wiki/Timbuktu" title="Timbuktu">Timbuktu</a> was an important city in these <a href="/wiki/Empire" title="Empire">empires</a> because a lot of <a href="/wiki/Trans-Saharan_trade" title="Trans-Saharan trade">trade across the Sahara Desert</a> went there. Timbuktu was also a good place for learning. The Songhai Empire became much smaller after a <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Moroccan</a> attack in 1591. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> invaded Mali in 1880. After that, France owned Mali. France gave Mali the names <i>French Sudan</i> and the <i>Sudanese Republic</i>. At some times it also included other nearby countries. In early 1959, Mali and Senegal united and they became the <a href="/wiki/Mali_Federation" title="Mali Federation">Mali Federation</a>. They became independent from France on June 20, 1960. Senegal left the Mali Federation a few months later. The <b>Republic of Mali</b>, with <a href="/wiki/Modibo_Ke%C3%AFta" title="Modibo KeÃ¯ta">Modibo KeÃ¯ta</a> as the first <a href="/wiki/Heads_of_state_of_Mali" class="mw-redirect" title="Heads of state of Mali">president</a>, left the <a href="/wiki/French_Community" title="French Community">French Community</a> on September 22, 1960. </p>
      <p>There was a <a href="/wiki/Coup" class="mw-redirect" title="Coup">coup</a> in Mali in 1968. Modibo KeÃ¯ta lost his job and was put in prison. Mali was then ruled by <a href="/wiki/Moussa_Traor%C3%A9" title="Moussa TraorÃ©">Moussa TraorÃ©</a> until 1991. He treated the country badly, and so there was another coup in 1991 after protests against the government, and a <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Mali" class="mw-redirect" title="Constitution of Mali">new constitution</a> was made. The leader of the country was then <a href="/wiki/Amadou_Toumani_Tour%C3%A9" title="Amadou Toumani TourÃ©">Amadou Toumani TourÃ©</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1992, <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Oumar_Konar%C3%A9" title="Alpha Oumar KonarÃ©">Alpha Oumar KonarÃ©</a> won Mali's first democratic <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">election</a>. President KonarÃ© won again in 1997 and he made big political and economic changes. In 2002, Amadou Toumani TourÃ© won the election and he started his second term as head of state. He was a retired general and was the military leader of the 1991 coup. Today, Mali is one of the least stable countries in Africa. </p>

      <p>Mali is the world's 24th-largest country. The area of the country is 1,240,000 <a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">kmÂ²</a> (478,734 <a href="/wiki/Square_mile" title="Square mile">miÂ²</a>). Mali and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> are about the same size. It is two times bigger than <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. </p>
      <p>Most of Mali is part of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a>. Mali is does not have any sea coast. It is landlocked. Mali does not have many mountains. Flat areas in the north are covered by <a href="/wiki/Sand" title="Sand">sand</a>. <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">Savanna</a> is around the <a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a> in the south. The climate is dry. The dry season is hot and dusty. There are many natural resources in Mali, including <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uranium" title="Uranium">uranium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phosphate" title="Phosphate">phosphates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaolinite" title="Kaolinite">kaolinite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salt" title="Salt">salt</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a>. </p>

      <p>Mali is one of the poorest countries in the <a href="/wiki/World" title="World">world</a>. 65% of its land area is <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> or something similar. There have been several long droughts over the last hundred years. Therefore, most economic activity is near the Niger River. </p>
      <p>About 10% of the people are <a href="/wiki/Nomad" class="mw-redirect" title="Nomad">nomads</a> and 80% of workers have jobs in <a href="/wiki/Farming" title="Farming">farming</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>. <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">Industry</a> is mainly to process farm products. Women also do <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">pottery</a> and the pots are bought and taken to <a href="/wiki/Market" title="Market">markets</a>. Many foreign <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourists</a> like the traditional methods which the women use to make the pots. </p>
      <p>Mali's main <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">export</a> is <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>, so if the price of cotton changes, Mali's economy is affected a lot. Mali also receives a lot of financial help from other countries. </p>
      <p>In 1997, the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a> (IMF) recommended a programme for changing the economy and the government followed this. Several international companies started exploring for larger amounts of gold in 1996-1998, and the Malian government thinks that Mali will become an important gold <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exporter</a> soon. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mali&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mali&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>About 90% of people from Mali follow <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni Islam</a>, but they do not always forget their traditional religions. <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Muslims</a> have their own schooling system. The number of Muslims from Mali who go to <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> is increasing and some study in <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arab</a> countries. About 1% of the people are <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christians</a>. When Mali was under French control, Christian teachers were sent to Islamic areas. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a> of Mali under French rule was <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, but now not many people outside towns can read or write this language. However, about 60% of the people use other languages well. Many people can read and write in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bamanakan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bamanakan (not yet started)">Bamanakan</a> (the most popular spoken language). This language has its own alphabet, called <a href="/w/index.php?title=N%27Ko&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="N'Ko (not yet started)">N'Ko</a>. Other people can read and write in <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>, after going to an Islamic school. One of the oldest universities in the world is Sankore University in <a href="/wiki/Timbuktu" title="Timbuktu">Timbuktu</a>. It began in the <a href="/wiki/1400s" title="1400s">1400s</a>. </p>
      <p>Famous musicians from Mali include <a href="/wiki/Kora_(instrument)" title="Kora (instrument)">kora</a> player <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toumani_Diabat%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Toumani DiabatÃ© (not yet started)">Toumani DiabatÃ©</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" class="mw-redirect" title="Guitarist">guitarist</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ali_Farka_Tour%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ali Farka TourÃ© (not yet started)">Ali Farka TourÃ©</a>, who has died. </p>
