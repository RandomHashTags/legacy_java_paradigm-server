      <p><b>Saint Lucia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. It is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, and it is north of the islands of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines" title="Saint Vincent and the Grenadines">Saint Vincent and the Grenadines</a> and south of <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>. As of 2010 there are about 174,000 people. </p>
      <p>The official language is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sltb-about_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sltb-about-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-usstate-bgnote_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usstate-bgnote-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lucian_Creole_French&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Saint Lucian Creole French (not yet started)">Saint Lucian Creole French</a> (Kwéyòl), which is a <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>-based Creole is spoken by 95% of the people. About 70% of the people are <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>. </p>
      <p>Two <a href="/wiki/Nobel_laureates" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobel laureates">Nobel laureates</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Lewis_(economist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arthur Lewis (economist) (not yet started)">Arthur Lewis</a>, an economist, and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Walcott" title="Derek Walcott">Derek Walcott</a>, a poet and playwright, have come from the island. </p>
      <p>Saint Lucia is a full and participating member of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Community" title="Caribbean Community">Caribbean Community (CARICOM)</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organisation_of_Eastern_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Organisation of Eastern Caribbean States (not yet started)">Organisation of Eastern Caribbean States</a> (OECS) and <a href="/wiki/La_Francophonie" class="mw-redirect" title="La Francophonie">La Francophonie</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lucia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:502px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Day248bdriveq.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Day248bdriveq.JPG/500px-Day248bdriveq.JPG" decoding="async" width="500" height="177" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Day248bdriveq.JPG/750px-Day248bdriveq.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Day248bdriveq.JPG/1000px-Day248bdriveq.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3441" data-file-height="1219"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Day248bdriveq.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>View of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Soufri%C3%A8re,_Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Soufrière, Saint Lucia (not yet started)">Soufrière</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanic</a> island of Saint Lucia is more mountainous than many other <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> islands. The highest point is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Gimie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Gimie (not yet started)">Mount Gimie</a>, at 950 metres (3,120&nbsp;ft) above sea level. Two other mountains, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pitons&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pitons (not yet started)">Pitons</a>, form the island's most famous landmark. They are between <a href="/w/index.php?title=Soufri%C3%A8re,_Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Soufrière, Saint Lucia (not yet started)">Soufrière</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Choiseul,_Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Choiseul, Saint Lucia (not yet started)">Choiseul</a> on the western side of the island. Saint Lucia is also one of the few islands in the world that has a drive-in volcano. It is also famous for clear seas and sandy beaches. </p>
      <p>The capital city of Saint Lucia is <a href="/wiki/Castries" title="Castries">Castries</a>. Over one third of all the people live in the capital. Major towns include <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gros_Islet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gros Islet (not yet started)">Gros Islet</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Soufri%C3%A8re,_Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Soufrière, Saint Lucia (not yet started)">Soufrière</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vieux_Fort&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vieux Fort (not yet started)">Vieux Fort</a>. </p>
      <p>The local <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> is <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical</a>. They have northeast <a href="/wiki/Trade_wind" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade wind">trade winds</a>. There is a dry season from December 1 to May 31, and a wet season from June 1 to November 30. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lucia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Sport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Windward_Islands_cricket_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Windward Islands cricket team (not yet started)">Windward Islands cricket team</a> includes players from Saint Lucia. They play in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Regional_Four_Day_Competition&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Regional Four Day Competition (not yet started)">West Indies regional tournament</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Sammy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Darren Sammy (not yet started)">Darren Sammy</a> is the first Saint Lucian to represent the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team" title="West Indies cricket team">West Indies</a>. He is the current captain.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lucia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Tourism">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Tourism">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Tourism is important to Saint Lucia's economy. There are more tourists during the dry season (January to April). Saint Lucia tends to be popular due to its tropical weather and scenery and its many beaches and resorts. </p>
      <p>Other tourist attractions include a drive-in <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sulphur_Springs,_Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sulphur Springs, Saint Lucia (not yet started)">Sulphur Springs</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Lucia_Botanical_Gardens&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="St Lucia Botanical Gardens (not yet started)">Botanical Gardens</a>, the Majestic twin Peaks "The Pitons", a world heritage site, the rain forests, and Pigeon Island National Park. </p>
      <p>Most tourists visit Saint Lucia as part of a cruise. Most of their time tends to be spent in <a href="/wiki/Castries" title="Castries">Castries</a>, although <a href="/w/index.php?title=Soufri%C3%A8re,_Saint_Lucia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Soufrière, Saint Lucia (not yet started)">Soufriere</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marigot_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marigot Bay (not yet started)">Marigot Bay</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gros_Islet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gros Islet (not yet started)">Gros Islet</a> are popular locations to visit. </p>
