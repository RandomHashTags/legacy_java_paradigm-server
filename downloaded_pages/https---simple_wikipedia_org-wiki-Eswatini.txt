      <p><b>Eswatini</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is officially the <b>Kingdom of Eswatini</b> (<i>Umbuso weSwatini</i>). Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Mbabane" title="Mbabane">Mbabane</a>. The country is named after the 19th-century king <a href="/wiki/Mswati_II" title="Mswati II">Mswati II</a>. Eswatini was <a href="/wiki/Colonized" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonized">colonized</a> by the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Boer" title="Boer">Boers</a> at the end of the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was formerly called <b>Swaziland</b> until April 2018. </p>
      <p>Eswatini is a small country. It is no more than 200 kilometres (120&nbsp;mi) north to south and 130 kilometres (81&nbsp;mi) east to west. It is completely surrounded by land. Eswatini does not touch the <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">sea</a>. The two countries that touch it are <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. </p>
      <p>On 19 April 2018, the King of Eswatini <a href="/wiki/Mswati_III" title="Mswati III">Mswati III</a> announced that the Kingdom of Swaziland had renamed itself the Kingdom of Eswatini.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eswatini&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Health">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eswatini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Health">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Eswatini is strongly affected by <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a> and <a href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>. The 2012 <a href="/wiki/CIA_World_Factbook" class="mw-redirect" title="CIA World Factbook">CIA World Factbook</a> showed Swaziland with the highest HIV infection rate in the world. <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">Life expectancy</a> is 50 years. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eswatini&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eswatini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Education in Eswatini is free at primary level, mainly 1st and 2nd grades. It is also free for orphaned and vulnerable children. Children are not required to attend.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1996, the primary school enrollment rate was 90.8%. Girls and boys both attended at the primary level.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1998, 80.5% of children reached grade five.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The University of Eswatini provides higher education. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eswatini&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eswatini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Most of Eswatini’s people are ethnically <a href="/w/index.php?title=Swazi_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Swazi people (not yet started)">Swazi</a>. There is also a small number of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zulus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zulus (not yet started)">Zulu</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=White_Africans&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="White Africans (not yet started)">White Africans</a>, mostly people of <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afrikaner" class="mw-redirect" title="Afrikaner">Afrikaner</a> descent. Swaziland also had <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> settlers and African refugees from <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>. </p>
      <p>82.70% of the people are <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a>, Christianity in Eswatini is sometimes mixed with traditional beliefs and practices. Some people think of the king as having a spiritual role. Eswatini also has a small <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Muslim minority</a>. </p>
      <p>Swazi have been <a href="/w/index.php?title=Subsistence_farmers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Subsistence farmers (not yet started)">subsistence farmers</a> and herders. They now mix such activities with work in the growing urban economy and in government. Some Swazi work in the mines in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. </p>
      <p><i><a href="/wiki/Swazi_language" title="Swazi language">SiSwati</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> are the official languages. SiSwati has 2.5&nbsp;million speakers and is taught in schools. It is also one of the official languages of South Africa. </p>
      <p>About 76,000&nbsp;people in the country speak <a href="/wiki/Zulu_language" title="Zulu language">Zulu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tsonga_language" title="Tsonga language">Tsonga</a> is spoken by about 19,000&nbsp;people in Swaziland. <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a> is also spoken by some residents of <a href="/wiki/Afrikaner" class="mw-redirect" title="Afrikaner">Afrikaner</a> descent. </p>
