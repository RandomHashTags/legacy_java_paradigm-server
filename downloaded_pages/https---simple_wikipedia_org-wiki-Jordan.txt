      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Jordan has borders with the countries of <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>. It also has a very short <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, and a longer coast along the <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a>. </p>
      <p>Most of Jordan is <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> and the country is very dry. The northwest of Jordan is its highest part, and its lowest part is the Dead Sea. This is the lowest place on the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> that is open to the sky. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Jordan is <b><a href="/wiki/Amman" title="Amman">Amman</a></b>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Islam_in_Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Islam in Jordan (not yet started)">Islam in Jordan</a>
      </div> 
      <p>People from Jordan are called <b>Jordanians</b>. Most of them speak <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> as their first language. The <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of Jordan is about 10.5 million people. </p>
      <p>Jordan is a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a> and has a <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a>. The King is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>, but he does not have all the power in the country. King <a href="/wiki/Abdullah_II_of_Jordan" title="Abdullah II of Jordan">Abdullah II</a> has been the King of Jordan since February 1999. Most Jordanians are <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>. There are around 200-400 000 <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christians</a>. </p>
      <p>Jordan's national meal is mansaf. The most popular sport is <a href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Football (soccer)">football</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Unlike some of the countries around it, Jordan has little <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>. Some of its <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> comes from <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> instead. Jordan is the largest producer of the mineral phosphate in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Jordan is a Member of the <a href="/wiki/OECD" title="OECD">OECD</a> since 2019 as the first Arab Country to do so. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> of Jordan is called the <b>Jordanian dinar</b>. </p>
