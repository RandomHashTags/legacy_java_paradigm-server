      <p><b>Belize</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. It used to be called <i>British Honduras</i>, but changed its name in 1973. Long before that it was part of the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Maya civilization">Mayan Empire</a>. Belize is the only <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> speaking country in Central America. </p>
      <p>Some people in Belize speak <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> or <a href="/wiki/Belizean_Kriol_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Belizean Kriol language">Kriol</a>, but <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is the official language and the most commonly spoken. This is because Belize is a former <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, while its neighbors were once colonies of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Many people speak two languages.<sup id="cite_ref-belize2011_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-belize2011-7">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-belize2000_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-belize2000-8">[7]</a></sup> Belize is a melting pot of cultures. </p> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Caracolfacup_o.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Caracolfacup_o.jpg/200px-Caracolfacup_o.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Caracolfacup_o.jpg/300px-Caracolfacup_o.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Caracolfacup_o.jpg/400px-Caracolfacup_o.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Caracolfacup_o.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>'Caana', a Mayan pyramid in Belize.

      <p>More than three hundred and thirty thousand (374,681 (2017))<sup id="cite_ref-cia_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-1">[1]</a></sup> people live in Belize. Kriols make up about 21% of the Belizean people. Three Maya groups now live in the country: the <a href="/wiki/Yucatec" class="mw-redirect" title="Yucatec">Yucatec</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mopan_people" title="Mopan people">Mopan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Q%27eqchi%27_people" title="Q'eqchi' people">Kekchi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> The Garinagu are a mix of African, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arawaks&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arawaks (not yet started)">Arawak</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carib_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carib people (not yet started)">Carib</a> ancestry. There are also <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">Mestizos</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Belmopan" title="Belmopan">Belmopan</a>, which is near the centre of the country, is its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a>. The first capital was Belize City. Other towns and cities include <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corozal_Town" title="Corozal Town">Corozal Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_Walk_Town" title="Orange Walk Town">Orange Walk Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punta_Gorda,_Belize" title="Punta Gorda, Belize">Punta Gorda</a>, Santa Elena/San Ignacio (known as the twin town) and San Pedro Town. </p>
      <p>Belize is on the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. There are about 450 <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a>. The largest island, <a href="/wiki/Ambergris_Caye" title="Ambergris Caye">Ambergris Caye</a>, is forty kilometres long. Many other islands are very small. In the water around the islands are <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">coral reefs</a>. </p>
      <p>The temperature along the coast is warm all year round. It typically ranges from 20&nbsp;°C to 32&nbsp;°C. Sometimes <a href="/wiki/Hurricane" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane">hurricanes</a> hit, causing great damage. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">

      <p>Belize is on the Caribbean coast of northern Central America. It shares a border on the north with the Mexican state of <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>, on the west with the Guatemalan department of <a href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_(department)" class="mw-redirect" title="Petén (department)">Petén</a>, and on the south with the Guatemalan department of <a href="/wiki/Izabal_Department" title="Izabal Department">Izabal</a>. To the east is the Caribbean Sea. The <a href="/wiki/Belize_Barrier_Reef" title="Belize Barrier Reef">Belize Barrier Reef</a> is along most of the 386 kilometres (240&nbsp;mi) of predominantly <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshy</a> coastline. Belize have the second largest living barrier reef in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> The area of the country totals 22,960 square kilometres (8,865&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). There are many lagoons along the coasts and in the northern part of the country. This makes the actual land area smaller at 21,400 square kilometres (8,263&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Rio_Hondo_(Belize)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Hondo (Belize)">Hondo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sarstoon_River" title="Sarstoon River">Sarstoon River</a> make the northern and southern border. </p>
      <p>The north of Belize is mostly flat, swampy coastal plains. In some places it is heavily forested. The south has the low <a href="/wiki/Mountain_range" title="Mountain range">mountain range</a> of the Maya Mountains. The highest point in Belize is <a href="/wiki/Doyle%27s_Delight" title="Doyle's Delight">Doyle's Delight</a> at 1,124&nbsp;m (3,688&nbsp;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> The Caribbean coast is lined with a <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">coral reef</a> and about 450 <a href="/wiki/Islets" class="mw-redirect" title="Islets">islets</a> and islands. The islands are locally called <a href="/wiki/Caye" class="mw-redirect" title="Caye">cayes</a> (pronounced "keys). Three of only four coral <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atolls</a> in the Western Hemisphere are off the coast of Belize. </p>
      <p>Over 60% of Belize's land surface is covered by forest.<sup id="cite_ref-servir.net_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-servir.net-12">[11]</a></sup> 20% is covered by cultivated land (agriculture) and human settlements.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> There are also important <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystems</a> across Belize's landscape.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Armed_forces">Armed forces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Armed forces">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Armed forces">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Belize Defence Force (BDF) is the military. It is responsible for protecting Belize. In 1997, the regular army had over 900 people, the reserve army 381, the air wing 45 and the maritime wing 36. This amounts to an overall strength of about 1400.<sup id="cite_ref-cavehill.uwi.edu_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cavehill.uwi.edu-16">[15]</a></sup> In 2005, the maritime wing became part of the Belizean Coast Guard.<sup id="cite_ref-ra_20071213_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ra_20071213-17">[16]</a></sup> In the same year, the government spent $1.2&nbsp;million on the military. This is 1.87% of the country's <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a> (GDP).<sup id="cite_ref-cia_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>After Belize became independent in 1981 the United Kingdom kept some military in the country to protect it from invasion by <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>. The main British force left in 1994. This was three years after Guatemala said Belize was independent. The United Kingdom still keeps some military people in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-cavehill.uwi.edu_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cavehill.uwi.edu-16">[15]</a></sup> In 2011 the base was only 10 soldiers due to British budget cuts. They hope to reopen the base later.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cuisine">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Cuisine">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Belizean cuisine is inspired by British, Mexican and Western Caribbean cooking. Since Belize is a meting pot of culture, it has adopted dishes from many different countries. The basic ingredients are rice and beans. These are often eaten with chicken, pork, veal, fish or vegetables. Coconut milk and fried <a href="/wiki/Plantain" title="Plantain">plantains</a> are added to the dishes to create a truly tropical taste. Exotic ingredients include <a href="/wiki/Armadillo" title="Armadillo">armadillo</a> meat, <a href="/wiki/Venison" title="Venison">venison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iguana" title="Iguana">iguana</a>, iguana egg, and fried <a href="/wiki/Paca" title="Paca">paca</a>. Conch soup is a traditional dish. It has a characteristic taste and thick consistency due to added <a href="/wiki/Okra" title="Okra">okra</a>, potatoes, yams, cassava flour and a touch of toasted <a href="/wiki/Habanero" class="mw-redirect" title="Habanero">habanero</a>. Belizean food is almost always served alongside white rice in coconut milk. </p>
      <p>The most common dishes that you will encounter in Belize are the following: </p>
      <p>Mestizo- Tamales, Relleno, Escabeche, Empanades and many other corn food. </p>
      <p>Creole- The famous Rice and Beans, sere, and other dishes with may include cassava and yam. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The major sports in Belize are <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>, basketball, volleyball and cycling. There are smaller followings of boat racing, <a href="/wiki/Track_%26_field" class="mw-redirect" title="Track &amp; field">track &amp; field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Softball" title="Softball">softball</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>. Fishing is also popular in areas of Belize. The <a href="/wiki/Cross_Country_Cycling_Classic" title="Cross Country Cycling Classic">Cross Country Cycling Classic</a> is one of the most important Belize sports events. This one-day sports event is meant for amateur cyclists but has also gained a worldwide popularity. </p>
      <p>On Easter day, citizens of <a href="/wiki/Dangriga" title="Dangriga">Dangriga</a> participate in a yearly fishing tournament. First, second, and third prize are awarded based on a scoring combination of size, <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, and number. The tournament is broadcast over local radio stations. Prize money is awarded to the winners. </p>
      <p>Belize's National Basketball Team is the only National Team to have major victories internationally. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Black_Orchid">Black Orchid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Black Orchid">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Black Orchid">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:EncycliaCochleata.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/EncycliaCochleata.jpg/170px-EncycliaCochleata.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="117" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/EncycliaCochleata.jpg/255px-EncycliaCochleata.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/EncycliaCochleata.jpg/340px-EncycliaCochleata.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1947" data-file-height="1344"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:EncycliaCochleata.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>A <a href="/wiki/Prosthechea_cochleata" title="Prosthechea cochleata">black orchid</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The black orchid (Encyclia Cochleatum) is the National Flower of Belize. This orchid grows on trees in damp areas, and flowers nearly all year round. Its clustered bulblike stems vary in size up to six inches long and carry two or three leaves. </p>
      <p>The black orchid flower has greenish-yellow petals and sepals with purple blotches near the base. The "lip" (one petal of special construction, which is the flower's showiest) is shaped like a valve of a clam shell (hence the name Encyclia Cochleatum) and is deep purple-brown, almost black, with conspicuous radiating purple veins. </p>
      <p><br> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mahogany_Tree">Mahogany Tree</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Mahogany Tree">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Mahogany Tree">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Squaring_mahogany.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Squaring_mahogany.jpg/220px-Squaring_mahogany.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="122" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Squaring_mahogany.jpg/330px-Squaring_mahogany.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Squaring_mahogany.jpg/440px-Squaring_mahogany.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2840" data-file-height="1572"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Squaring_mahogany.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Mahogany loggers, around 1930
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The National Tree of Belize is the Mahogany Tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Swietenia_macrophylla" class="mw-redirect" title="Swietenia macrophylla">Swietenia macrophylla</a></i>), one of the magnificent giants of the Belize rain forest. It rises straight and tall to over a hundred feet. In the early months of the year, when the leaves fall and new red-brown growth appears, the tree can be seen from a great distance. The tree puts out a many small whitish flowers. The flowers blossom into dark fruits, which are pear-shaped capsules about six inches long. The mahogany tree matures in 60 to 80 years. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Keel_Billed_Toucan">Keel Billed Toucan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Keel Billed Toucan">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Keel Billed Toucan">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>The Keel Billed Toucan (<i><a href="/wiki/Ramphastos_sulfuratus" class="mw-redirect" title="Ramphastos sulfuratus">Ramphastos sulfuratus</a></i>) is the National Bird of Belize.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup> It is noted for its great, canoe-shaped bill and its brightly coloured green, blue, red and orange feathers. There are toucans in open areas of the country with large trees. It is mostly black with bright yellow cheeks and chest, red under the tail and a distinctive white patch at the base of the tail. They make a monotonous frog-like croak. Toucans like fruits. They eat by cutting with the serrated edge of their bills. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tapir">Tapir</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Tapir">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Tapir">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Tapir or Mountain Cow (Tapirello Bairdii) is the largest land mammal of the American tropics. The tapir is a stoutly built animal with short legs, about the size of a donkey and weighs up to 600 pounds. Its general color is dusty brown with a white fringe around the eyes and lips, white tipped ears and occasional white patches of fur on the throat and chest. </p>
      <p>In spite of its local name, the tapir is not a cow. It is closely related to the horse and is also kin to the rhinoceros. The tapir is a vegetarian. It spends much of its time in water or mud shallows, and is a strong swimmer. </p>
      <p>The National Animal is protected under the law thus the hunting of the tapir is illegal. </p>
