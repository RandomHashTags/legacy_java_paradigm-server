Mexico is south of <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and other <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> states. <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a> are south of Mexico. Mexico is between the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. </p>
      <p>People living in Mexico or who are from there are called Mexicans. Most Mexican people speak <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>. There are also Mexicans who speak <a href="/wiki/Native_American" title="Native American">Native American</a> languages, like <a href="/wiki/Nahuatl" title="Nahuatl">Nahuatl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayan_languages" title="Mayan languages">Mayan</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zapotec_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zapotec language (not yet started)">Zapotec</a>. The capital of Mexico is <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Before the Europeans came, many Native American cultures existed in Mexico. The earliest was the <a href="/wiki/Olmec" title="Olmec">Olmec</a> culture in the south. The Olmecs are famous for the large stone heads they made. On the Yucatán <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> lived the <a href="/wiki/Mayans" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayans">Mayans</a>. The Mayans lived in <a href="/wiki/City_states" class="mw-redirect" title="City states">city states</a> ruled by kings. The Mayans were most powerful between <a href="/wiki/200" title="200">200</a> and <a href="/wiki/900" title="900">900</a> A.D. Another powerful empire belonged to <a href="/wiki/Teotihuacan" class="mw-redirect" title="Teotihuacan">Teotihuacan</a>. Teotihuacan was a very large city, one of the largest at that time. After Teotihuacan declined the <a href="/wiki/Toltecs" class="mw-redirect" title="Toltecs">Toltecs</a> became powerful. Things made by the Toltecs have been found from the southern parts of the U.S. all the way to <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>. A famous Toltec god is <a href="/wiki/Quetzalcoatl" title="Quetzalcoatl">Quetzalcoatl</a>. The Toltec culture declined too, and it was succeeded by the <a href="/wiki/Aztecs" title="Aztecs">Aztecs</a>. The Aztecs called their own <a href="/wiki/Empire" title="Empire">empire</a> Mexico. A famous Aztec king was <a href="/wiki/Moctezuma_II" title="Moctezuma II">Moctezuma II</a>. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/1519" title="1519">1519</a> the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Explorer" class="mw-redirect" title="Explorer">explorer</a> <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s" title="Hernán Cortés">Hernán Cortés</a> came to Mexico. The Aztecs thought he was the returned Quetzalcoatl, so they did not want to fight against him. Cortes allied himself with the enemies of the Aztecs. In <a href="/wiki/1521" title="1521">1521</a> they conquered the Aztec capital <a href="/wiki/Tenochtitlan" title="Tenochtitlan">Tenochtitlan</a>. The Aztec Empire became part of Spain. It was called <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/1810" title="1810">1810</a> the Mexican priest <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Hidalgo" title="Miguel Hidalgo">Miguel Hidalgo</a> started the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_war_of_independence" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican war of independence">Mexican war of independence</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1821" title="1821">1821</a> the Spanish finally retreated and Mexico became independent. The first leader of independent Mexico was <a href="/w/index.php?title=Agustin_de_Iturbide&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Agustin de Iturbide (not yet started)">Agustin de Iturbide</a>. He set up the <a href="/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire" title="First Mexican Empire">First Mexican Empire</a> and became emperor. But the Mexicans were not happy with him, and in <a href="/wiki/1823" title="1823">1823</a> the country became a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. </p>
      <p>A man who was very important in Mexico in the early <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a> was <a href="/wiki/Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_de_Santa_Anna" title="Antonio López de Santa Anna">Antonio López de Santa Anna</a>. He was the president of Mexico 11 times. When he became a <a href="/wiki/Dictator" title="Dictator">dictator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> declared independence (1836). The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Alamo" title="Battle of the Alamo">Battle of the Alamo</a> was part of this <a href="/wiki/Texas_Revolution" title="Texas Revolution">Texas Revolution</a>. Between 1846 and 1848 there was <a href="/wiki/Mexican-American_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican-American War">war between Mexico and the United States</a>. In this war Mexico lost its large northern areas, which became the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">southwestern United States</a>. After this war Santa Anna was <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">sent away</a> to <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. </p>
      <p>Between <a href="/wiki/1858" title="1858">1858</a> and <a href="/wiki/1861" title="1861">1861</a> there was war again, between <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservatives</a>. The liberal <a href="/wiki/Benito_Ju%C3%A1rez" title="Benito Juárez">Benito Juárez</a> won the war and became president afterwards. Juarez stayed president until <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> invaded Mexico and made <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_of_Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Maximilian of Habsburg">Maximilian of Habsburg</a> emperor of the Second Mexican Empire. But Maximilian was very unpopular. After <a href="/wiki/Cinco_de_Mayo" title="Cinco de Mayo">more war</a> he was <a href="/wiki/Death_penalty" title="Death penalty">executed</a> in <a href="/wiki/1867" title="1867">1867</a>, and Juarez became president again. </p>
      <p>Conservatives thought Juarez had too much power. In <a href="/wiki/1876" title="1876">1876</a> they ousted him, and made <a href="/w/index.php?title=Porfirio_D%C3%ADaz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Porfirio Díaz (not yet started)">Porfirio Díaz</a>, a general who had won a battle against the French, president. Porfirio Díaz made the country wealthier, but the poor people became poorer. Franciso I. Madero started the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Revolution" title="Mexican Revolution">Mexican Revolution</a> in <a href="/wiki/1910" title="1910">1910</a>. </p>
      <p>The next 10 years the country was in chaos. There were many presidents who ruled for a short time and all kinds of people fought against each other. Famous people from this period are <a href="/wiki/Emiliano_Zapata" title="Emiliano Zapata">Emiliano Zapata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pancho_Villa" title="Pancho Villa">Pancho Villa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Francisco_I._Madero" title="Francisco I. Madero">Francisco I. Madero</a>. When <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Obreg%C3%B3n" title="Álvaro Obregón">Álvaro Obregón</a> became president in <a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a> the fighting calmed down. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/1929" title="1929">1929</a> President <a href="/w/index.php?title=Plutarco_El%C3%ADas_Calles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plutarco Elías Calles (not yet started)">Plutarco Elías Calles</a> founded the National Mexican Party, PNM. The party was later renamed <a href="/wiki/Institutional_Revolutionary_Party" title="Institutional Revolutionary Party">Institutional Revolutionary Party</a>, PRI. The party would rule for a very long time. Most PRI presidents were not popular, it was said that they were only president to become richer themselves. An exception was president <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A1zaro_C%C3%A1rdenas" title="Lázaro Cárdenas">Lázaro Cárdenas</a>. He was president between <a href="/wiki/1934" title="1934">1934</a> and <a href="/wiki/1940" title="1940">1940</a>. </p>
      <p>After several decades more and more people became unhappy with the PRI. In 1968 security forces shot at protesters, this caused several hundred deaths and became known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tlatelolco_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tlatelolco massacre (not yet started)">Tlatelolco massacre</a>. Another uprising was in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> when <a href="/wiki/Zapatista_Army_of_National_Liberation" title="Zapatista Army of National Liberation">Zapatistas</a> rebelled in the province <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>. </p>
      <p>Mainly through ballot box <a href="/wiki/Fraud" title="Fraud">fraud</a> the PRI managed to stay into power until <a href="/wiki/2000" title="2000">2000</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Fox" title="Vicente Fox">Vicente Fox</a> of the National Action Party, PAN, was elected president. In total the PRI had governed Mexico for 71 years. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Mexico is a <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitutional</a> <a href="/wiki/Federal_republic" title="Federal republic">federal</a> <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> ruled by a president. The president is elected every 6 years. The current president is <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Manuel_L%C3%B3pez_Obrador" title="Andrés Manuel López Obrador">Andrés Manuel López Obrador</a>. Parliament has a <a href="/wiki/Senate" title="Senate">Senate</a> and House of Deputies. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Mexico is in the southern part of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. It is roughly shaped like a <a href="/wiki/Triangle" title="Triangle">triangle</a>. Mexico is more than 3000&nbsp;km (1,850 miles) long from northwest to southeast. Mexico is between two large seas: the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> in the West and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> in the East. Mexico has two large <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsulas</a>. <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_peninsula" class="mw-redirect" title="Baja California peninsula">Baja California</a> in the northwest, and <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_(state)" title="Yucatán (state)">Yucatán</a> in the southeast. In central and western Mexico are the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Madre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sierra Madre (not yet started)">Sierra Madre</a> mountains. In the Sierra Madre is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pico_de_Orizaba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pico de Orizaba (not yet started)">Pico de Orizaba</a>, the highest <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountain</a> of Mexico. In central Mexico there are also a few <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanoes</a> like the <a href="/wiki/Popocat%C3%A9petl" title="Popocatépetl">Popocatépetl</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Iztacc%C3%ADhuatl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Iztaccíhuatl (not yet started)">Iztaccíhuatl</a>. The Pico de Orizaba is also a volcano. In the north of Mexico are <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a>. In the south are tropical <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforests</a>. Some rivers in Mexico are the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande" title="Rio Grande">Río Bravo</a> (known in the US as the Rio Grande), the <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Balsas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Río Balsas (not yet started)">Río Balsas</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_P%C3%A1nuco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Río Pánuco (not yet started)">Río Pánuco</a>, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Yaqui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Río Yaqui (not yet started)">Río Yaqui</a>. Many animals are found in Mexico such as <a href="/wiki/Birds" class="mw-redirect" title="Birds">birds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguars" class="mw-redirect" title="Jaguars">jaguars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monkeys" class="mw-redirect" title="Monkeys">monkeys</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: People">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: People">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Mexicans&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="List of Mexicans (not yet started)">List of famous Mexican people</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Mexico is the most populous <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish-speaking</a> country in the world. It is also the second most populous country in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> (after <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>). 60% of Mexicans have Native American and European forefathers; these are called <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">mestizos</a>. Almost 30% of Mexicans are pure Native American and 10% are <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a>. Most Mexicans (90%) speak Spanish. 10% of the Mexicans speak a Native American language, like <a href="/wiki/Nahuatl" title="Nahuatl">Nahuatl</a>, the language of the Aztecs, <a href="/wiki/Maya_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Maya languages">Maya</a> or <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zapotec_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zapotec language (not yet started)">Zapotec</a>. Most people in Mexico are <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> (89%) and 6% are <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Other ethnic groups in Mexico are Arabs, Gypsies, Africans, Jews and Asians. </p>
