      <p><b>Tonga</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It has 169 <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a>. Only 36 islands have inhabitants. The most important island is <a href="/wiki/Tongatapu" title="Tongatapu">Tongatapu</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Tupou_V&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="George Tupou V (not yet started)">George Tupou V</a> is the <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a> of Tonga and the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> is Pohiva Tu'ionetoa. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Tonga is <a href="/wiki/Nuku%27alofa" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuku'alofa">Nuku'alofa</a>. </p>
      <p>The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a> of Tonga are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tongan_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tongan language (not yet started)">Tongan</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. Tonga had 102,321 inhabitants in 2000. It is a very hot country. Tonga became <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a> in 1970. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>About 3000 years before, the <a href="/wiki/Polynesians" class="mw-redirect" title="Polynesians">Polynesians</a> had settled in <a href="/wiki/Tongatapu" title="Tongatapu">Tongatapu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1845, Tāufaʻāhau united Tonga into a kingdom. He declared Tonga a constitutional monarchy. Between 1845 and 1893, <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> was stopped. Also, <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> was made up. </p>
      <p>In 1900, Tonga became a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Protected_state&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Protected state (not yet started)">protected state</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. Tonga maintained its sovereignty. It remained the only Pacific nation never to have given up its monarchical government. Foreign affairs was managed by the UK. Tonga was no longer a protected state and became independent in 1970. Tonga joined the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a> in 1970. It became a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> in September 1999. Tonga has never lost indigenous governance. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Tonga is a constitutional monarchy. Tonga provides for its citizens: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>free and mandatory education for all</li> 
       <li>secondary education with only nominal fees</li> 
       <li>and foreign-funded scholarships for post-secondary education</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Tongans have universal access to a national health care system. The Tongan constitution protects land ownership. Land cannot be sold to foreigners. While there is a land shortage on the urbanised main island of Tongatapu, there is farmland available in the outlying islands. The majority of the population engages in some form of subsistence production of food. Women and men have equal access to education and health care. They are fairly equal in employment. Women are discriminated against in land holding, electoral politics, and government ministries. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Tonga is in <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>. It is an archipelago in the South <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It has 177 islands. Thirty-six of them are inhabited. The islands are divided into three main groups – Vava'u, Ha'apai, and Tongatapu. The largest island is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tongatapuon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tongatapuon (not yet started)">Tongatapuon</a>. The capital city of <a href="/wiki/Nuku%CA%BBalofa" title="Nukuʻalofa">Nukuʻalofa</a> is on Tongatapuon. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The climate is tropical with a distinct warm period (December–April) and a cooler period (May–November). The average wettest period is around March. The average daily humidity is 80%. </p>
