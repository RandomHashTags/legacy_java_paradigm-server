      <p><b>Kenya</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in East <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, about halfway down, near the horn of Africa. It has the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to its east and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a> to its west. Kenya borders the <a href="/wiki/Jubaland" title="Jubaland">Jubaland</a> part of <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> (east), <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> (north), <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a> (north-west), <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> (west), and <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> (south). Kenya is about the size of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and almost as large as <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> (U.S.). </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> of Kenya is <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>, which is the 14th largest city in Africa (after Accra,Ghana) <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.listnbest.com/15-africas-largest-cities-top-metropolitan-areas/2/">[1]</a>. Some cities on the seaside are <a href="/wiki/Mombasa" title="Mombasa">Mombasa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malindi" title="Malindi">Malindi</a> on the Indian Ocean, Nyeri, Nanyuki, Naivasha, and Thika in the Kenyan Highlands, and Kisumu on <a href="/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a>. </p>
      <p>The first humans may have lived near the lakes of Kenya along the <a href="/wiki/Great_Rift_Valley" title="Great Rift Valley">Great Rift Valley</a>, which cuts Kenya from north to south. </p>
      <p>Kenya's coast is <a href="/wiki/Tropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical">tropical</a> and gets very hot. Inland, it is drier and cooler where the mountains rise up. The highest mountain in Kenya is Mt. Kenya, at 5,199 metres (17,057&nbsp;ft). <a href="/wiki/Mount_Kilimanjaro" title="Mount Kilimanjaro">Mount Kilimanjaro</a> crosses over the south border, with <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, but the highest part of Kilimanjaro is in Tanzania. </p>
      <p>Kenya is home to many different <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">indigenous peoples</a> with their own <a href="/wiki/Cultures" class="mw-redirect" title="Cultures">cultures</a>, <a href="/wiki/Languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Languages">languages</a>, and <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">histories</a>. There are at least 44 living languages and 1 extinct <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ethnologue.com/country/KE">[2]</a> language that is not spoken any more. English and Swahili are the official languages spoken in Kenya. Because of <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">colonialism</a> school-going Kenyans are required to learn English, and it is used in schools and <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">universities</a>. </p>
      <p>Kenya was <a href="/wiki/Colonization" title="Colonization">colonized</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Britain">British</a>, who began taking land from indigenous peoples to build <a href="/wiki/Ranch" title="Ranch">ranches</a>. They also <a href="/wiki/Discrimination" title="Discrimination">discriminated</a> against Kenyans in their own land.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Kenyans who were against this formed a group called the Kenya Land and Freedom Army, or Mau Mau that fought a war against Britain for <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a>. The British committed <a href="/wiki/War_crimes" class="mw-redirect" title="War crimes">war crimes</a> to stop the Mau Mau,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> but on December 12, 1963 they agreed to give Kenya independence. </p>
      <p>For many years after independence, a single <a href="/wiki/Party" title="Party">party</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenya_African_National_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kenya African National Union (not yet started)">Kenya African National Union</a> (KANU), ruled the country. General elections were held every 5 years. However, all candidates for election to office had to belong to the ruling party, KANU. The party used the police to <a href="/wiki/Harassment" title="Harassment">harass</a> and <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">torture</a> <a href="/wiki/Socialists" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialists">socialists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Communists" class="mw-redirect" title="Communists">communists</a> in Kenya, and worked closely with Britain and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> to keep them out of politics.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Uhuru_Kenyatta" title="Uhuru Kenyatta">Uhuru Kenyatta</a> is currently the president of Kenya. He is the first one to belong to a party other than KANU. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenya&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>All Kenyans of school-going age are required to attend Primary <a href="/wiki/School" title="School">School</a>. However, school fees and required uniforms often keep students away from school. The Kenyan school system consists of 8 years of primary school, standard 1 through 8, 4 years of high school (Form 1 to 4) and 4 years of university but plans are underway of changing the system to 2 years in pre-school, 6 years in primary school,3 years in junior high school,3 years in senior high school and 3 years in university (2-6-6-3) in 2018. At the end of primary school, all students sit for a standardized exam called Kenya Certificate of Primary <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">Education</a> (KCPE). The grades attained in this exam determine which high school the student will attend. In Form 4 (this is the last year in high school), students sit for another exam called Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE). The highest achieving students are granted admission into the 5 national <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">universities</a> (Nairobi, Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology, Kenyatta University, Egerton University and Moi University). Tertiary colleges, like Globovillee college, also feed the <a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">diploma</a> graduates to universities. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Land_and_animals">Land and animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenya&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Land and animals">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Land and animals">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Kenya is a country of grassland, but it is not rich,but it is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Productive&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Productive (not yet started)">productive</a> land especially in the highlands. This is a very dry grassland with poor soil.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-9">[9]</a></sup> Kenya also has very few mineral resources but their main mineral is soda ash. Three-fourths of the country is covered with plains. They are low in altitude along the coast, but get higher further inland, making a large plateau. The part east of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Turkana" title="Lake Turkana">Lake Turkana</a> is the only true desert, but the rest can be very close to desert. </p>
      <p>Savannas usually get between 4 and 16&nbsp;inches (100 to 400&nbsp;mm) of rain in a year. These lands, however, are called <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> because of the type of plants that live there and how they get their rain.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-9">[9]</a></sup> Savannas have a wet and dry season. During the wet season it can rain hard for long periods of time then not rain at all in the dry season. Savannas that have more rain often have many trees spaced out across their plains. These trees have deep roots or store water, like desert plants do, to live through the long, dry seasons without rain. Even drier savannas will have only grass, and that too only in a few clumps. The dry land is very bad for crops, but it is a wonderful place for all kinds of wild animals to gather and stay.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-9">[9]</a></sup> That is why Kenya has a lot of parks where the animals are kept, and protected from all the hunters. People/tourists come from all over the world to go on photo <a href="/wiki/Safari" class="mw-redirect" title="***REMOVED***">safaris</a> in Kenya's special <a href="/wiki/Wildlife" title="Wildlife">wildlife</a> parks. The people come to Kenya on safari to see animals such as the rhinoceros, giraffe, wildebeest, elephant, cheetah, antelope, and lion. These animals live on the savanna grasslands. </p>
      <p>The wild herbivores move as they eat, and they never stay in one spot because there is not enough grass for all of them. People also usually raise cattle on the savanna. These animals are kept in one place and often eat up all the grass there.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenya&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Since the independence of Kenya in 1963, Kenya had usually had a one-party government. In 1991, a section of the constitution was scrapped, that automatically made it a multi-party state. It is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> Commonwealth.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-9">[9]</a></sup> The people are, like the Congo, divided into many tribes that often fight. However, Kenya's government is trying to get the people to work together and has encouraged them to run businesses and <a href="/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">factories</a>. Kenya is a developing country, slowly growing more modern.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
