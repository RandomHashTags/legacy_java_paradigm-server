      <p><b>Niue</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the south <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>. Situated between Tonga, Samoa and the Cook Islands, it is commonly known as "Rock of <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a>". It has its own <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a>, but it is an <a href="/w/index.php?title=Associated_state&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Associated state (not yet started)">associated state</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. This means that Niue's head of state is New Zealand's <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereign</a> (queen) in right, and most <a href="/wiki/Diplomacy" title="Diplomacy">diplomatic</a> relations are conducted by the latter on Niue's <a href="/w/index.php?title=Behalf&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Behalf (not yet started)">behalf</a>. </p>
      <p>The territory is 2,400 kilometres northeast of New Zealand in a triangle between <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cook_Islands" title="Cook Islands">Cook Islands</a>. </p>
