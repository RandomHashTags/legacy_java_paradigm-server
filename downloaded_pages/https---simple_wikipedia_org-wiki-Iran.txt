      <p><b>Iran</b> officially the <b>Islamic Republic of Iran</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span lang="fa" dir="rtl">ايران</span>‎), historically known as <b>Persia</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> region. It shares <a href="/wiki/Border" title="Border">borders</a> with <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and biggest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a>. Iran is the eighteenth largest country in the world. It has more than 84.9 million people. Iran has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> since 1945. It is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_the_Petroleum_Exporting_Countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries">Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries</a> (OPEC).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It is an <a href="/wiki/Islamic_republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamic republic">Islamic republic</a>. </p>
      <p>In Iran, Persians, Azerbaijanis, Kurds, Mazandaranis, Gilaks, Lurs and Bakhtiaris make up the nations minority ethnic groups. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Iran" title="History of Iran">History of Iran</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In the past, Iran was called "Persia" by people outside of the <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a>. The people that lived there called the country "Iran". The official name was Persia, a region in Iran. The name Persia was used when dealing with other countries and in <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> papers. </p>
      <p>In 1935, <a href="/wiki/Reza_Sh%C4%81h_Pahlavi" title="Reza Shāh Pahlavi">Reza Shāh Pahlavi</a> was <a href="/wiki/Shah" title="Shah">Shah</a> of Iran. He officially asked foreigners to call the country "Iran". This was done to show that Iran belongs to all the non-Persian Iranians as well as to Persian Iranians. The name Iran means <i>land of the <a href="/wiki/Aryan" title="Aryan">Aryans</a></i>. It is used in the ancient book of the <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrian" class="mw-redirect" title="Zoroastrian">Zoroastrians</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Avesta_(religious_scripture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Avesta (religious scripture)">Avesta</a>. In the 19th and early 20th century, the name <i>Aryan</i> was used by <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a> to mean <i>all <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_people" title="Indo-European people">Indo-Europeans</a></i>. The "Aryan Race" was a term that <a href="/wiki/Adolph_Hitler" class="mw-redirect" title="Adolph Hitler">Hitler</a> used to describe his "Superior" or "perfect" race, but it first meant Iranians.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> "Aryan" means "noble" in Iranian languages. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Persian_Empire">Persian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Persian Empire">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Persian Empire">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Around 500 BC, the area that is now Iran was the center of the <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Achaemenid Empire</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek</a> city states fought against the Persian armies led by <a href="/wiki/Darius_the_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Darius the Great">Darius the Great</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xerxes" title="Xerxes">Xerxes</a>. Then <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a> took the country by fighting the Achaemenid dynasty of <a href="/wiki/Persia" class="mw-redirect" title="Persia">Persia</a>. He ruled until he died,then the Greek Seleucids ruled until they were defeated by the Parthian Empire which later fought the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>. </p>
      <p>After the Parthians, the Sassanian dynasty (224-651) took over. Other people took Persia by <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">fi\ghting</a>, like the <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arabs</a> (7th century), <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turks</a> (10th century) and <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a> (13th century). However, Iran has always had a different <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> and continued to survive. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_World_War_II">After World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: After World War II">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: After World War II">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Revolution" title="Islamic Revolution">Islamic Revolution</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">Central Intelligence Agency</a> (CIA) worked in Iran to create 1953 <a href="/wiki/Riot" title="Riot">riots</a> which led to the removal of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> Mosaddegh. The <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> then made the <a href="/wiki/Shah" title="Shah">Shah</a> <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Reza_Pahlavi" title="Mohammad Reza Pahlavi">Mohammad Reza Pahlavi</a> the most powerful person in Iran, again. The Shah left Iran in 1979 in the face of a revolt. The Iranian <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> was changed to an <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Republic" title="Islamic Republic">Islamic Republic</a> by <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Revolution" title="Islamic Revolution">Islamic Revolution</a>. Soon afterwards, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Students_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Iranian Students Movement (not yet started)">Iranian Students Movement</a> (Tahkim Vahdat), with the backing of the new <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a>, took over the <a href="/wiki/U.S." class="mw-redirect" title="U.S.">U.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/Embassy" title="Embassy">Embassy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a>. They held most of the <a href="/wiki/Diplomat" class="mw-redirect" title="Diplomat">diplomats</a> <a href="/wiki/Hostage" title="Hostage">hostage</a> for 444 days. </p>
      <p>Relations between the two countries have not been good since. For example, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> claims that Iran supports <a href="/wiki/Terrorist" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrorist">terrorist</a> <a href="/wiki/Group" title="Group">groups</a> against <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>. Iran does not see Israel as a country. Iran, along with most <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arab</a> countries, believes that Israel does not have the <a href="/wiki/Rights" title="Rights">right</a> to exist. However, Iran has collaborated with the West at times. These deals have been about energy or about fighting terrorism. </p>
      <p>Iran fought the <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War" title="Iran–Iraq War">Iran–Iraq War</a> in the 1980s. Many foreign countries supported Iraq. </p>
      <p>Now, the <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">West</a> is trying to prevent Iran from using <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_engineering" title="Nuclear engineering">nuclear technology</a>, even though Iran is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Non-Proliferation_Treaty" title="Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty">Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty</a>. The <a href="/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency" title="International Atomic Energy Agency">International Atomic Energy Agency</a> (IAEA) has reported many times that there is no evidence that Iran is developing <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapons</a>. However, it also says that it can not say for sure that Iran is not doing so in <a href="/wiki/Secret" class="mw-redirect" title="Secret">secret</a>. </p>
      <p>A December 2007 <a href="/wiki/CIA" class="mw-redirect" title="CIA">CIA</a> report on nuclear activity in Iran said that Iran's <a href="/wiki/Secret" class="mw-redirect" title="Secret">secret</a> program to get nuclear <a href="/wiki/Weapon" title="Weapon">weapons</a> <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a> was stopped in 2003. It said that Iran will probably not be able to build a nuclear <a href="/wiki/Weapon" title="Weapon">weapon</a> soon. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Iran has the <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural resource</a> of <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">oil</a>. It is a member of OPEC. Oil is one of its main exports. <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">Rice</a>, handicrafts, <a href="/wiki/Carpet" title="Carpet">carpets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crocus" title="Crocus">crocus</a> are important local products. Iran is the world's largest exporter and producer of <a href="/wiki/Caviar" title="Caviar">caviar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Iran is also one of the world's biggest exporters of pistachio nuts. </p>
      <p>Iran has factories that produce industrial products. Iran is also involved in the field of biomedical sciences. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Rial" class="mw-redirect" title="Rial">Rial</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Money" title="Money">money</a> used in Iran. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion_in_Iran">Religion in Iran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion in Iran">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Religion in Iran">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>About 90% of Iranian people are <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>. The <a href="/wiki/State_religion" title="State religion">state religion</a> is <a href="/wiki/Shia_Islam" title="Shia Islam">Shia Islam</a>. It has been the state religion since the <a href="/wiki/Safavid_dynasty" title="Safavid dynasty">Safavid dynasty</a> in the 16th century.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This is the religion of about 75% of Iranians.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-15">[15]</a></sup> They belong to the <a href="/wiki/Twelver" class="mw-redirect" title="Twelver">Twelver</a> branch. About 9% of Iranians Muslim belong to the <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni</a> branch of Islam. The 9% of Iranians who are not Muslim are <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá'í Faith">Bahá'ís</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mandaeism" title="Mandaeism">Mandeans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrian" class="mw-redirect" title="Zoroastrian">Zoroastrians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jews</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-15">[15]</a></sup> It is thought that there are between 300,000 and 350,000 Persian (Iranian) Jews. </p>
