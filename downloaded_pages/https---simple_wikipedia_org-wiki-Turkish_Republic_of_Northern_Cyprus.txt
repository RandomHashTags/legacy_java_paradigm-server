      <p><b>Northern Cyprus</b> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i lang="tr">Kuzey Kıbrıs</i>), officially the <b>Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus</b> (<b>TRNC</b>; <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i lang="tr">Kuzey Kıbrıs Türk Cumhuriyeti</i>, <i>KKTC</i>), is a <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> state<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in the northern half of the island of Cyprus. Three hundred thousand people live in its 3,335&nbsp;km². <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> is the only country which recognizes the territory as an independent republic despite the international sanctions and embargos in place. </p>
      <p>Cyprus was conquered and became part of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> from 1570–1914. Many Turks became <a href="/wiki/Settler" title="Settler">settlers</a>. As the Ottoman Empire sided against the British empire in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, the British annexed Cyprus. In 1923, by the Treaty of Lausanne Turkey gave up the island to the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> which in 1925 converted it to a <a href="/wiki/Crown_colony" title="Crown colony">crown colony</a>. The British administered it from 1878 until 1960. </p>
      <p>In 1974, in response to a growing conflict with Greece, the Turkish army invaded Cyprus. Many settlers from mainland Turkey came from Turkey into the northern part of the island. The move drew international condemnation and Northern Cyprus remains unrecognized by the United Nations. </p>
      <p>Since 1974, the United Nations has established a ceasefire line between Turkish Cypriots and Greek Cypriots. The ''Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus'' was formed in 1983 and <a href="/wiki/Rauf_Denkta%C5%9F" title="Rauf Denktaş">Rauf Denktash</a> became the first president. Negotiations for a unified island have been held several times without success. </p>
      <p>Northern Cyprus, despite its international pariah status, stands out amongst other "frozen conflict" zones as having a relatively stable and democratic form of government with high levels of political freedom and civic engagement in the political process. </p>
