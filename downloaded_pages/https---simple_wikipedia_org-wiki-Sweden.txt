      <p><b>Sweden</b> (<a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>: <i lang="sv">Sverige</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Nordic_countries" title="Nordic countries">Nordic country</a> in the part of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> called <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>. Its neighbors are <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. Sweden is also connected to <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> in the south by a bridge. It is a <a href="/wiki/Developed_country" title="Developed country">developed country</a>. It is famous for its <a href="/wiki/Welfare_state" title="Welfare state">welfare state</a>. People who live in Sweden are called Swedes. </p>
      <p>The population of Sweden is about 10 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-&quot;stats_sweden&quot;_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-&quot;stats_sweden&quot;-11">[10]</a></sup> Sweden's <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, which is also Sweden's largest city, with almost one million people. Other large cities are <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malmö">Malmö</a>. These cities are all in the southern half of the country, where it is not as cold as in the north. Swedes drink filmjölk, it is a traditional fermented milk product from Sweden. It is made by fermenting cow's milk. Glogg, is spiced mulled wine. It normally contains red wine, sugar, orange peel, and spices. Swedes eat yellow pea soup with pork (or pork sausages) along with mustard. </p>
      <p>Sweden is a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a> because it has a king, <a href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf_of_Sweden" class="mw-redirect" title="Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden">Carl XVI Gustaf</a>, but he does not have any real power. Sweden is a parliamentary state meaning that the <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> is <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">elected</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> which is appointed by the people. The country is <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratically</a> ruled by a government headed by an elected <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">prime minister</a>. <a href="/wiki/Stefan_L%C3%B6fven" title="Stefan Löfven">Stefan Löfven</a> was elected <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">Prime Minister</a> in September 2014. He took office in October 2014. </p>
      <p>Sweden has an official majority language, <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a> (the Swedish word for the language is <i>svenska</i>). Sweden has five official <a href="/w/index.php?title=Minority_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Minority language (not yet started)">minority languages</a>: <a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">Yiddish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sami_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Sami language">Sami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Me%C3%A4nkieli" class="mw-redirect" title="Meänkieli">Meänkieli</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Romani" class="mw-redirect" title="Romani">Romani</a>. </p>
      <p>Sweden became a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> (EU) on 1 January <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. Unlike most countries in the European Union, Sweden is not a member of the <a href="/wiki/Eurozone" title="Eurozone">Eurozone</a> and has not begun to use the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euro</a> as currency. This is because the people have voted against using the euro. The <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> remains the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_krona" title="Swedish krona">Swedish krona</a> (Swedish crown). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Sweden has been a country for a thousand years. In the Middle Ages Sweden had the same king as Denmark and Norway. In the early 16th century Sweden got its own king, Gustav Vasa. During the 17th century Sweden was a great power. Sweden had taken Estonia, Latvia, and Finland and parts of Norway, Germany, and Russia. In the 18th century Sweden became weaker and lost these places. In the early 19th century Sweden's king died without an heir and the Swedish parliament voted for Jean Baptiste Bernadotte as the new king. Bernadotte fought Denmark and made them allow Norway to enter a <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Sweden_and_Norway" title="Union of Sweden and Norway">personal union with Sweden</a>. </p>
      <p>This was Sweden's last war, and Sweden has not been at war for 200 years. In 1905, the Swedish-Norwegian personal union was dissolved. In many wars, including <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>, the country was neutral, meaning it did not take sides. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, it traded with both the <a href="/wiki/British" title="British">British</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">Germans</a> in order to protect its neutrality. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Region">Region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Region">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Region">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Sweden is divided into 21 counties. They are <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_County" title="Stockholm County">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uppsala_County" title="Uppsala County">Uppsala</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dermanland_County" title="Södermanland County">Södermanland</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%96sterg%C3%B6tland_County" title="Östergötland County">Östergötland</a>, <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B6nk%C3%B6ping_County" title="Jönköping County">Jönköping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kronoberg_County" title="Kronoberg County">Kronoberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalmar_County" title="Kalmar County">Kalmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gotland_County" title="Gotland County">Gotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blekinge_County" title="Blekinge County">Blekinge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sk%C3%A5ne_County" title="Skåne County">Skåne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halland_County" title="Halland County">Halland</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4stra_G%C3%B6taland_County" title="Västra Götaland County">Västra Götaland</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4rmland_County" title="Värmland County">Värmland</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%96rebro_County" title="Örebro County">Örebro</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4stmanland_County" title="Västmanland County">Västmanland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dalarna_County" title="Dalarna County">Dalarna</a>, <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4vleborg_County" title="Gävleborg County">Gävleborg</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sternorrland_County" title="Västernorrland County">Västernorrland</a>, <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A4mtland_County" title="Jämtland County">Jämtland</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4sterbotten_County" title="Västerbotten County">Västerbotten</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Norrbotten_County" title="Norrbotten County">Norrbotten</a>. </p>
      <p>Sweden has 25 historical provinces (<i>landskap</i>). They are found in three different regions: <a href="/wiki/Norrland" title="Norrland">Norrland</a> in the North, <a href="/wiki/Svealand" title="Svealand">Svealand</a> in the central region, and <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6taland" title="Götaland">Götaland</a> in the South. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Sweden has been Christian for a thousand years. Sweden is traditionally a Protestant country, but it is now one of the least religious countries in the world. <a href="/wiki/Statistical_survey" title="Statistical survey">Statistical surveys</a> say 46-85% of all people in Sweden are <a href="/wiki/Agnostic" class="mw-redirect" title="Agnostic">agnostics</a> or <a href="/wiki/Atheism" title="Atheism">atheists</a>. This means they do not believe in a <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">god</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> About 6.4 million people in Sweden, which is 67% of all the people, are members of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Sweden" title="Church of Sweden">Church of Sweden</a>, but only 2% of members go to church often.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Music">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Music">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular music">popular music</a>, <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roxette" title="Roxette">Roxette</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Cardigans" title="The Cardigans">The Cardigans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe_(band)" title="Europe (band)">Europe</a>, Entombed, <a href="/wiki/At_the_Gates" title="At the Gates">At the Gates</a>, <a href="/wiki/In_Flames" title="In Flames">In Flames</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dark_Tranquillity" title="Dark Tranquillity">Dark Tranquillity</a>, Hypocrisy, Grave, <a href="/wiki/Dissection_(band)" title="Dissection (band)">Dissection</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tove_Lo" title="Tove Lo">Tove Lo</a>, Laleh, Watain, and <a href="/wiki/Ace_of_Base" title="Ace of Base">Ace of Base</a> have had several hits throughout the years. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Sweden_at_the_Olympics" title="Sweden at the Olympics">Sweden at the Olympics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden national football team</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Sweden is a country with many talented athletes, such as soccer player <a href="/wiki/Zlatan_Ibrahimovi%C4%87" title="Zlatan Ibrahimović">Zlatan Ibrahimović</a>. Sweden (men and women's teams combined) has five bronze medals and two silver medals from the World Cup in football (soccer). The soccer league in Sweden is called <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a> (men's) and <a href="/wiki/Damallsvenskan" title="Damallsvenskan">Damallsvenskan</a> (women's). Sweden has also performed well in ice hockey. The men's ice hockey top division in Sweden is called <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Hockey_League" title="Swedish Hockey League">SHL</a> and the women's <a href="/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Women%27s_Hockey_League&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Swedish Women's Hockey League (not yet started)">SDHL</a>. Sweden has also had several successful table tennis players, including <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stellan_Bengtsson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stellan Bengtsson (not yet started)">Stellan Bengtsson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jan-Ove_Waldner" title="Jan-Ove Waldner">Jan-Ove Waldner</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing" title="Alpine skiing">alpine skiers</a> including <a href="/wiki/Ingemar_Stenmark" title="Ingemar Stenmark">Ingemar Stenmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pernilla_Wiberg" title="Pernilla Wiberg">Pernilla Wiberg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anja_P%C3%A4rson" title="Anja Pärson">Anja Pärson</a>. Other champions include biathlete <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_Forsberg" title="Magdalena Forsberg">Magdalena Forsberg</a> and tennis players <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Borg" title="Björn Borg">Björn Borg</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mats_Wilander&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mats Wilander (not yet started)">Mats Wilander</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Edberg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stefan Edberg (not yet started)">Stefan Edberg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Bj%C3%B6rkman" title="Jonas Björkman">Jonas Björkman</a>. Swimmer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sara_Sj%C3%B6str%C3%B6m&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sara Sjöström (not yet started)">Sara Sjöström</a> has several gold, silver and bronze medals from the Olympic Games and holds several world records. </p>
      <p>Sweden also succeeds in <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiing</a>, having won several medals in the Olympic games. </p>
