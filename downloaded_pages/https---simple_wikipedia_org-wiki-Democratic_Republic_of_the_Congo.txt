      <p>The <b>Democratic Republic of the Congo</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">République démocratique du Congo</i>), commonly referred to as <b>DR Congo</b>, <b>Congo-Kinshasa</b> or the <b>DRC</b>, is a country in <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">central Africa</a>. It was known as <a href="/wiki/Za%C3%AFre" class="mw-redirect" title="Zaïre">Zaïre</a> from 1971 to 1997. It is the second largest country in Africa by area and the eleventh largest in the world. With a population of over 71 million,<sup id="cite_ref-cia.gov_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia.gov-2">[2]</a></sup> the Democratic Republic of the Congo is the nineteenth most populous nation in the world, the fourth most populous nation in Africa, as well as the most populous Francophone (French-speaking) country. </p>
      <p>DRC borders the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a> to the north; <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a> in the east; <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> to the south; the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a>, the Angolan exclave of <a href="/wiki/Cabinda_Province" title="Cabinda Province">Cabinda</a>, and the Atlantic Ocean to the west. It is separated from <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> by <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tanganyika" title="Lake Tanganyika">Lake Tanganyika</a> in the east.<sup id="cite_ref-cia.gov_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia.gov-2">[2]</a></sup> The country has access to the ocean through a 40-kilometre (25&nbsp;mi) stretch of Atlantic coastline at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Muanda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Muanda (not yet started)">Muanda</a> and the roughly 9&nbsp;km wide mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a> which opens into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>. It has the second-highest total Christian population in Africa. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>When the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Congo" title="Belgian Congo">Belgian Congo</a> became independent, its leaders fought each other. The <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and later the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> helped destroy the groups who wanted <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> from the new country. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Second_Congo_War&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Second Congo War (not yet started)">Second Congo War</a>, beginning in 1998, devastated the country. It involved nine African nations and some twenty armed groups.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Despite the signing of peace accords in 2003, fighting continues in the east of the country. There, the prevalence of <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">rape</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Sexual_violence" title="Sexual violence">sexual violence</a> is described as the worst in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-8">[8]</a></sup> The war is the world's deadliest conflict since World War II, killing 5.4 million people since 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-The_deadliest_war_in_the_world_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_deadliest_war_in_the_world-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The vast majority died from conditions of <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diarrhea" title="Diarrhea">diarrhea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malnutrition" title="Malnutrition">malnutrition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Democratic Republic of the Congo was formerly, in chronological order, the <b><a href="/wiki/Congo_Free_State" title="Congo Free State">Congo Free State</a></b>, <b><a href="/wiki/Belgian_Congo" title="Belgian Congo">Belgian Congo</a></b>, <b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_the_Congo_(L%C3%A9opoldville)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Republic of the Congo (Léopoldville) (not yet started)">Congo-Léopoldville</a></b>, Congo-Kinshasa, and <b><a href="/wiki/Zaire" title="Zaire">Zaire</a></b> (<i>Zaïre</i> in French).<sup id="cite_ref-cia.gov_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia.gov-2">[2]</a></sup> Though it is in the Central African <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> subregion, the nation is also economically and regionally affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a> as a member of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_African_Development_Community&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Southern African Development Community (not yet started)">Southern African Development Community</a> (SADC). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The country is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, the South Atlantic Ocean, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Congo" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Congo">Republic of Congo</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> across <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tanganyika" title="Lake Tanganyika">Lake Tanganyika</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a>. </p>
      <p>The capital of the CAR is <a href="/wiki/Kinshasa" title="Kinshasa">Kinshasa</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Sites</a> in the Democratic Republic of Congo include <a href="/w/index.php?title=Virunga_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Virunga National Park (not yet started)">Virunga National Park</a> (1979), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Garamba_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Garamba National Park (not yet started)">Garamba National Park</a> (1980), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kahuzi-Biega_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kahuzi-Biega National Park (not yet started)">Kahuzi-Biega National Park</a> (1980), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salonga_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Salonga National Park (not yet started)">Salonga National Park</a> (1984) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Okapi_Wildlife_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Okapi Wildlife Reserve (not yet started)">Okapi Wildlife Reserve</a> (1996). </p>
