      <p><b>Cambodia</b> or <b>Kampuchea</b> (officially called <b>Kingdom of Cambodia</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in Southeast Asia. It is near <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. About 13 <a href="/wiki/Million" title="Million">million</a> people live in Cambodia. The people of Cambodia are called <i>Cambodians</i> or <i>Kampuchea</i>. <a href="/wiki/Khmer_language" title="Khmer language">Khmer</a> is the official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a>. The country has recently emerged from a long civil war and the rule of the <a href="/wiki/Khmer_Rouge" title="Khmer Rouge">Khmer Rouge</a>. It is part of <a href="/wiki/ASEAN" class="mw-redirect" title="ASEAN">ASEAN</a>, Association of South East Asian Nations. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The ancestors of Cambodia had an empire called Angkor centered in the northwest of present-day Cambodia. The Angkor civilization is the world's largest pre-industrial civilization. One of the buildings from Angkor is a <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a>/<a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a> temple called <a href="/wiki/Angkor_Wat" title="Angkor Wat">Angkor Wat</a> which is the world's largest religious structure. This empire was later destroyed due to ecological and environmental problems as well as failing infrastructure. <a href="/wiki/Theravada" title="Theravada">Theravada Buddhism</a> came to the country in the 13th century via monks from Sri Lanka. Since then, Buddhism has been the official religion. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Cambodia is set entirely in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropic_zone&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tropic zone (not yet started)">tropic zone</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mekong_River" title="Mekong River">Mekong River</a> runs through the middle of the country and is the most important source of water in the country. The country is the size of <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Cambodia, people play many sports. Some sports that are enjoyed that come from the West include <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soccer" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer">soccer</a>. Traditional Cambodian sports are buffalo racing, dragon boat racing and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bokator&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bokator (not yet started)">bokator</a> Khmer martial art also known as <a href="/wiki/Pradal_serey" title="Pradal serey">pradal serey</a>. Cambodia attended its first <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> in 1956 and participated in two more before warfare and civil strife interrupted its attendance. The country returned to regular participation with the 1996 Summer Games. Cambodia managed fourth in <a href="/wiki/Soccer" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer">soccer</a> in the 1972 Southeast Asian games. Cambodia hosted the GANEFO games in the 1960s. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The culture of Cambodia has been influenced by <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a>. Today most people in Cambodia practice Buddhism. A lot of their customs revolve around Buddhism. </p>
      <p>The food of Cambodia includes tropical <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Noodle" title="Noodle">noodles</a> and various <a href="/wiki/Soup" title="Soup">soups</a>. Cambodians like to eat a rice noodle soup called 'kah-tieu' in the morning. Cambodians are famous for a type of 'kah-tieu' called 'kah-tieu Phnom Penh' which has <a href="/wiki/Shrimp" title="Shrimp">shrimp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beef" title="Beef">beef</a> balls, fried garlic, pork broth and chicken. Cambodians also eat a red curry noodle soup with rice vermicelle noodles. <a href="/wiki/Curry" title="Curry">Curry</a> is also eaten with rice or French bread in Cambodia. Cambodian food is similar to <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnamese</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Thai_food&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Southern Thai food (not yet started)">Southern Thai food</a>. </p>
      <p>Cambodia also has a mystical <a href="/wiki/Tattoo" title="Tattoo">tattoo</a> called a yantra tattoo that is popular with soldiers. A yantra tattoo has ancient Khmer and Pali (An ancient Indian language) writing. A yantra tattoo is usually done by a religious person or monk. The tattoo artist guarantees that the person cannot receive any physical harm as long as they follow certain conditions. A person is supposed to not talk to anyone for three days and three nights. Another alternative is to follow the five Buddhist percepts. Movie star actress <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Jolie" title="Angelina Jolie">Angelina Jolie</a> is known to have a yantra tattoo. </p>
      <p>Cambodians celebrate the Cambodian New Year in April. It is based on Theravada Buddhism. The date depends on astrological signs but are usually are on April 13-15 or April 14-16. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag">Flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Flag">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Flag">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Cambodian flag includes a three-towered temple called Angkor Wat. It is the most famous monument in the country. Many tourists visit the temple. The Cambodian flag has three horizontal bands. There are two blue bands on the top and the bottom. There is a red band that is twice the height of each blue band. The red band represents the nation. The temple represents the structure of the universe.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Transportation">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Transportation">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>People in Cambodia use many different types of transportation. Transportation in Cambodia include: <a href="/wiki/Boat" title="Boat">boat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Car">car</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elephant" title="Elephant">elephant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Train" title="Train">train</a> and <a href="/wiki/Airplane" class="mw-redirect" title="Airplane">airplane</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Royal.Place.Phnom.Penh.Palais.Royal.Cambodge.001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Royal.Place.Phnom.Penh.Palais.Royal.Cambodge.001.jpg/220px-Royal.Place.Phnom.Penh.Palais.Royal.Cambodge.001.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Royal.Place.Phnom.Penh.Palais.Royal.Cambodge.001.jpg/330px-Royal.Place.Phnom.Penh.Palais.Royal.Cambodge.001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Royal.Place.Phnom.Penh.Palais.Royal.Cambodge.001.jpg/440px-Royal.Place.Phnom.Penh.Palais.Royal.Cambodge.001.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></a>

      <p>The Cambodian economy has been growing rapidly in recent years. Cambodia is set to build its first <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">skyscraper</a>, Gold Tower 42. Cambodia is also building a satellite city next to <a href="/wiki/Phnom_Penh" title="Phnom Penh">Phnom Penh</a> which is called Camko City. Camko City is being constructed by Korean companies to modernize Phnom Penh so as to make it appealing to foreign investors and businesses. Cambodia is also one of the most corrupted nations in the world and has been pressured by international communities to fix it. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_relations">Foreign relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Foreign relations">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Foreign relations">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Cambodia has foreign relations with most nations. It is part of <a href="/wiki/ASEAN" class="mw-redirect" title="ASEAN">ASEAN</a>. Cambodia has border issues with <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> over lost territories. Cambodia is one of a few nations with good relations with both <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Koreas</a>. <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> president Lee Myung Bak was an economic advisor to Cambodian prime minister Hun Sen and former Cambodian King Norodom Sihanouk was a good friend with former <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> leader Kim Il-sung. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_groups">Ethnic groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Ethnic groups">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Ethnic groups">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Khmer (Cambodians) account for the vast majority of the population. Ethnic minorities include Chinese, Vietnamese, <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> Cham-Malays, Laotians, and various native peoples of the rural highlands. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Land">Land</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Land">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Land">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Although much of Cambodia is heavily forested, the central lowland region is covered with <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> paddies, fields of dry crops such as corn (maize) and tobacco, tracts of tall grass and reeds, and thinly wooded areas. Savanna grassland occur in the plains, with the grasses reaching a height of 5&nbsp;feet (1.5&nbsp;metres). In the eastern highlands the high plateaus are covered with grasses and deciduous forests. Broad-leaved evergreen forests grow in the mountainous areas to the north, with trees 100&nbsp;feet (30&nbsp;metres) high emerging from thick undergrowths of vines, rattans, palms, bamboos, and assorted woody and herbaceous ground plants. In the southwestern highlands, open forests of pines are found at the higher elevations, while the rain-drenched seaward slopes are blanketed with virgin rainforests growing to heights of 150&nbsp;feet (45&nbsp;metres) or more. Vegetation along the coastal strip ranges from evergreen forests to nearly impenetrable mangroves. </p>
