      <p><b>Costa Rica</b> (officially called <b>Republic of Costa Rica</b>), is a country in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> to the southeast, the Pacific Ocean to the west, and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> to the east. The official language of Costa Rica is <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>. The official <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rican_col%C3%B3n" title="Costa Rican colón">colón</a>. It has enjoyed a long period of <a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">peace</a> since the 1863 <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a>. Because of its natural beauty and political stability, it is <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nicknamed</a> the "<a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> of <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>". Costa Rica has had army since December 1, 1863. Instead of spending money on the military, the government spends money on education and head 1<sup id="cite_ref-2011Census_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011Census-3">[3]</a></sup> people live in Costa Rica. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9,_Costa_Rica" title="San José, Costa Rica">San José</a>. The current President is <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Alvarado_Quesada" title="Carlos Alvarado Quesada">Carlos Alvarado Quesada</a>. He was elected in 2014. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Columbian_period">Pre-Columbian period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Pre-Columbian period">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Pre-Columbian period">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">indigenous people</a> of Costa Rica belong to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediate_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Intermediate Area (not yet started)">Intermediate Area</a>. This Intermediate Area is where the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mesoamerican&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mesoamerican (not yet started)">Mesoamerican</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Andean&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Andean (not yet started)">Andean</a> indigenous cultures mixed together. The country was influenced by various <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chibchan_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chibchan languages (not yet started)">Chibcha</a> speaking indigenous people. </p>
      <p>The impact of indigenous peoples' culture on modern Costa Rica has been small. The country did not have a strong native civilization to begin with. Most of the indigenous population was absorbed into the Spanish colony. There are still some small indigenous groups. The largest is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bribri_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bribri people (not yet started)">Bribri</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Boruca_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Boruca people (not yet started)">Boruca</a> tribes in south Costa Rica. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_colonization">Spanish colonization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Spanish colonization">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Spanish colonization">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> sailed to the eastern shores of Costa Rica during his last trip in 1502,.<sup id="cite_ref-embassy_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-embassy-7">[7]</a></sup> He reported large amounts of <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> <a href="/wiki/Jewelry" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewelry">jewelry</a> among the indigenous people.<sup id="cite_ref-lp_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lp-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>During the colonial period, Costa Rica was the most southern province of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captaincy_General_of_Guatemala&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Captaincy General of Guatemala (not yet started)">Captaincy General of Guatemala</a>. But it mostly ruled itself as a part of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a>. Costa Rica's distance from the capital in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, its legal prohibition to trade with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Viceroyalty_of_New_Granada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Viceroyalty of New Granada (not yet started)">Viceroyalty of New Granada</a>, and the lack of gold and silver made Costa Rica into a poor, little populated region of the Spanish Empire.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Costa Rica was called "the poorest and most miserable Spanish colony in all America" by a Spanish governor in 1719.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Costa Rica's poverty was also because there were not enough indigenous people available for <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a>. Most of the Costa Rican settlers had to work on their own land. There were no large <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hacienda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hacienda (not yet started)">haciendas</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a>). For all these reasons, Costa Rica was unappreciated and forgotten by the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Spain" title="Habsburg Spain">Spanish Crown</a>. It had to develop on its own. </p>
      <p>Costa Rica became a "rural democracy" with no oppressed <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">mestizo</a> or indigenous class. It was not long before Spanish settlers turned to the hills, where they found rich volcanic soil and a better climate.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence">Independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Independence">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Independence">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Costa Rica never fought for independence from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. On September 15, 1821, after the last Spanish defeat in the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">Mexican War of Independence</a> (1810–21), the authorities in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> declared the independence of all of Central America. That date is still celebrated as Independence Day in Costa Rica. </p>
      <p>Costa Rica joined the <a href="/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire" title="First Mexican Empire">First Mexican Empire</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Agust%C3%ADn_de_Iturbide&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Agustín de Iturbide (not yet started)">Agustín de Iturbide</a>. After its collapse in 1823, Costa Rica became a province of the new <a href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Central_America" title="Federal Republic of Central America">Federal Republic of Central America</a>. it existed from 1823 to 1839. It had a very loose authority over its provinces, especially the poor and remote Costa Rica. </p>
      <p>In 1824, the Costa Rican capital was moved to <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9,_Costa_Rica" title="San José, Costa Rica">San José</a>. This led to a brief battle with the old capital, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cartago,_Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cartago, Costa Rica (not yet started)">Cartago</a>. </p>
      <p>While civil wars were common in the Federal Republic of Central America, Costa Rica was largely peaceful. </p>
      <p>In 1838, Costa Rica formally proclaimed itself an independent country. The local population had little allegiance to the federal government in Guatemala. From colonial times to now, Costa Rica's reluctance to become politically tied with the rest of Central America has been a major obstacle to efforts for greater regional integration. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_growth">Economic growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economic growth">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Economic growth">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Coffee was first planted in Costa Rica in the early 19th century. It was first shipped to Europe in 1843. It became Costa Rica's first major export. Coffee would remain Costa Rica's biggest export until the 20th century. </p>
      <p>Most of the coffee exported was transported by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bullock_cart&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bullock cart (not yet started)">oxcart</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a> port of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puntarenas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Puntarenas (not yet started)">Puntarenas</a>. Since the main market for the coffee was in Europe, it soon became a high priority to develop a transportation route from the Central Plateau to the Atlantic Ocean. For this purpose, in the 1870s, the Costa Rican government asked U.S. businessman <a href="/w/index.php?title=Minor_C._Keith&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Minor C. Keith (not yet started)">Minor C. Keith</a> to build a railroad to the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> port of <a href="/wiki/Lim%C3%B3n" title="Limón">Limón</a>. Despite enormous difficulties with construction, disease, and financing, the railroad was completed in 1890. </p>
      <p>Most Afro-Costa Ricans come from <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaican</a> <a href="/wiki/Immigrants" class="mw-redirect" title="Immigrants">immigrants</a> who worked on the railway.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> United States convicts, Italians and Chinese immigrants also worked in the construction of the railroad. </p>
      <p>In exchange for making the railroad, Costa Rican gave Keith large amounts of land and a lease on the train route, which he used to produce bananas and export them to the United States. As a result, bananas became the largest export. The <a href="/wiki/United_Fruit_Company" title="United Fruit Company">United Fruit Company</a> began to hold a major role in the national economy. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 20th century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: 20th century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>Costa Rica has enjoyed greater peace and political stability than other Latin American nations. </p>
      <p>But in 1917–19, General <a href="/w/index.php?title=Federico_Tinoco_Granados&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Federico Tinoco Granados (not yet started)">Federico Tinoco Granados</a> ruled as a military <a href="/wiki/Dictator" title="Dictator">dictator</a> until he was overthrown. The unpopularity of Tinoco's government led to a considerable decline in the size, wealth, and political influence of the Costa Rican military. </p>
      <p>In 1948, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Figueres_Ferrer" title="José Figueres Ferrer">José Figueres Ferrer</a> led an armed uprising between the previous president <a href="/wiki/Rafael_%C3%81ngel_Calder%C3%B3n_Guardia" title="Rafael Ángel Calderón Guardia">Rafael Ángel Calderón Guardia</a> (he served as president between 1940 and 1944) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Otilio_Ulate_Blanco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Otilio Ulate Blanco (not yet started)">Otilio Ulate Blanco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> With more than 2,000 dead, the 44-day <a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rican_Civil_War&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Costa Rican Civil War (not yet started)">Costa Rican Civil War</a> was the bloodiest event in Costa Rica during the 20th century. </p>
      <p>The victorious rebels formed a government that <a href="/w/index.php?title=Military_of_Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Military of Costa Rica (not yet started)">abolished the military</a>. It oversaw the drafting of a new constitution by a democratically elected assembly.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Having enacted these reforms, the junta relinquished its power on November 8, 1949, to the new democratic government. After that, Figueres became a national hero, winning the country's first democratic election under the new constitution in 1953. Since then, Costa Rica has held 14 presidential elections, the latest in 2014. All of them have been widely regarded by the international community as peaceful and transparent. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Costa Rica is on the Central American <a href="/wiki/Isthmus" title="Isthmus">isthmus</a>. It is between latitudes 8<a href="/w/index.php?title=8th_parallel_north&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="8th parallel north (not yet started)">°</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=12th_parallel_north&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="12th parallel north (not yet started)">12°N</a>, and longitudes <a href="/w/index.php?title=82nd_meridian_west&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="82nd meridian west (not yet started)">82°</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=86th_meridian_west&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="86th meridian west (not yet started)">86°W</a>. It borders the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> to the east and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> to the west. It has a total of 1,290 kilometres (800&nbsp;mi) of coastline. Costa Rica also borders <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> to the north (309&nbsp;km or 192&nbsp;mi of border) and <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> to the southeast (639&nbsp;km or 397&nbsp;mi of border). In total, Costa Rica has 51,100 square kilometres (19,700&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) plus 589 square kilometres (227&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) of <a href="/wiki/Territorial_waters" title="Territorial waters">territorial waters</a>. </p>
      <p>The highest point in the country is <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Chirrip%C3%B3" title="Cerro Chirripó">Cerro Chirripó</a>, at 3,819 metres (12,530&nbsp;ft). The largest lake in Costa Rica is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Arenal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake Arenal (not yet started)">Lake Arenal</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Costa Rica is located between 8 and 12 degrees north of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>. The climate is <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical</a> all year. The country has many <a href="/w/index.php?title=Microclimate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Microclimate (not yet started)">microclimates</a> depending on <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rainfall" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a> of each region. </p>
      <p>Costa Rica's seasons are defined by how much rain falls. It does not have the <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">four seasons</a> other countries have. The year can be split into two seasons: the dry season (known as summer), and the rainy season (known as winter). The "summer" starts in December and ends in April. "Winter" starts in May and ends in November. During this time it rains constantly in some regions. </p>
      <p>The location receiving the most rain is the Caribbean. Annual rainfall is over 5,000&nbsp;mm (196.9&nbsp;in). Humidity is also higher here. The mean annual <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> on the coast is around 27&nbsp;°C (81&nbsp;°F). 20&nbsp;°C (68&nbsp;°F) in the main populated areas, and below 10&nbsp;°C (50&nbsp;°F) on the highest mountains.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plants_and_animals">Plants and animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Plants and animals">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_Rica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Plants and animals">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">

      <p>Costa Rica is home to many plants and animals. The country has only about 0.25% of the world's landmass, but it has 5% of the world's <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian07_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian07-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Honey99_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Honey99-18">[18]</a></sup> Almost 25% of Costa Rica's land area is in national parks and protected areas. <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> This is the largest percentage of protected areas in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-ODI1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODI1-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Costa Rica successfully reduced <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>. It had one of the worst deforestation rates in the world from 1973 to 1989. But it was almost zero by 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-ODI1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODI1-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Corcovado_National_Park" title="Corcovado National Park">Corcovado National Park</a> is internationally known by <a href="/wiki/Ecologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecologist">ecologists</a> for its biodiversity. Visitors can see many different types of animals. An example is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_cats&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Big cats (not yet started)">big cats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tapirs" class="mw-redirect" title="Tapirs">tapirs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Corcovado is the only park in Costa Rica where all four Costa Rican monkeys can be found.<sup id="cite_ref-watching_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-watching-26">[26]</a></sup> These include the <a href="/wiki/White-headed_capuchin" title="White-headed capuchin">white-headed capuchin</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mantled_howler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mantled howler (not yet started)">mantled howler</a>,the endangered <a href="/w/index.php?title=Geoffroy%27s_spider_monkey&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Geoffroy's spider monkey (not yet started)">Geoffroy's spider monkey</a><sup id="cite_ref-watching_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-watching-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_American_squirrel_monkey&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Central American squirrel monkey (not yet started)">Central American squirrel monkey</a>. The squirrel monkey is found only on the Pacific coast of Costa Rica and a small part of <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tortuguero_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tortuguero National Park (not yet started)">Tortuguero National Park</a> is home to spider, howler, and white-throat Capuchin monkeys. It is also the home of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Three-toed_sloth&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Three-toed sloth (not yet started)">three-toed sloth</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Two-toed_sloth&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Two-toed sloth (not yet started)">two-toed sloth</a>. It has 320 species of birds and many reptiles. There is an annual nesting of the endangered <a href="/wiki/Green_turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Green turtle">green turtle</a>, and is the most important nesting site for the species. Giant <a href="/wiki/Leatherback_turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Leatherback turtle">leatherback</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawksbill_turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Hawksbill turtle">hawksbill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Loggerhead_Sea_Turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Loggerhead Sea Turtle">loggerhead</a> turtles also nest here. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monteverde_Cloud_Forest_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve (not yet started)">Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve</a> is home to almost 2,000 plant species.<sup id="cite_ref-karttv_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-karttv-28">[28]</a></sup> Including many <a href="/wiki/Orchid" title="Orchid">orchids</a>. It is also home to more than 400 types of birds. There are more than 100 species of mammals here.<sup id="cite_ref-karttv_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-karttv-28">[28]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Costa Rica is a center of biological diversity for reptiles and <a href="/wiki/Amphibians" class="mw-redirect" title="Amphibians">amphibians</a>. They have the world's fastest running lizard, the spiny-tailed iguana (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ctenosaura_similis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ctenosaura similis (not yet started)">Ctenosaura similis</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-'Garland1984'_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-'Garland1984'-29">[29]</a></sup> </p>

      <p><b>Article 168</b><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> of the <b>Constitution of Costa Rica</b> says the political divisions are classified into 3 groups: <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a>, cantons, and districts. Costa Rica has seven <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a>. The provinces are divided into 81 cantons. The canton is directed by a <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a>. Mayors are chosen democratically every four years by the canton's people. There are no provincial legislatures. The cantons are further divided into 463 districts. </p>

      <p>Costa Rica entered the <a href="/wiki/Summer_Olympics" class="mw-redirect" title="Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a> for the first time in 1936 with the fencer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bernardo_de_la_Guardia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bernardo de la Guardia (not yet started)">Bernardo de la Guardia</a>. They entered the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" class="mw-redirect" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympics</a> for the first time in 1980 with the skier <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arturo_Kinch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arturo Kinch (not yet started)">Arturo Kinch</a>. All four of Costa Rica's Olympic medals were won by the sisters <a href="/wiki/Silvia_Poll" title="Silvia Poll">Silvia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Poll" title="Claudia Poll">Claudia Poll</a> in <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swimming (sport)">swimming</a>. Claudia won the country's only gold medal in <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Association football</a> is the most popular sport in Costa Rica. The <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">national team</a> has played in four <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> tournaments. They reached the quarter-finals for the first time in <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Its best performance in the regional <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="CONCACAF Gold Cup">CONCACAF Gold Cup</a> was runner-up in <a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2002 CONCACAF Gold Cup (not yet started)">2002</a>. </p>
