      <p><b>Syria</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, the west part of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It borders (from south to north) on <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. Its western part faces the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>. Its eastern and northern parts are <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountainous</a>. </p>
      <p>The current <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> is <a href="/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad" title="Bashar al-Assad">Bashar al-Assad</a>. Syria's national <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a>. The biggest city is <a href="/wiki/Aleppo" title="Aleppo">Aleppo</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Syrian_civil_war" title="Syrian civil war">Syrian civil war</a> began in 2011. </p>
      <p>The population of Syria is 74% <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Sunni</a>, 12% <a href="/wiki/Alawite" class="mw-redirect" title="Alawite">Alawi</a>, 10% <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian.</a> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Syria has a very long <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a>. It was a land of <a href="/wiki/Phoenician" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenician">Phoenicians</a>. Later it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">Achaemenid Empire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>, and then the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Roman_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Roman Empire">Eastern Roman Empire</a>. In those days people spoke the <a href="/wiki/Syriac_language" title="Syriac language">Syriac language</a>. The city <a href="/wiki/Antioch" title="Antioch">Antioch</a> was great and one of the important cities in <a href="/wiki/Christendom" title="Christendom">Christendom</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad Caliphate</a> control of Syria in the 7th century. In this Arab Empire people began to speak the <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic language</a>. Today most Syrian people believe in Islam but there are still Christians too. </p>
      <p>When <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> started, the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> ruled Syria and many other places. When it ended, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> controlled <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> and Syria. <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">Britain</a> had <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palestine" class="mw-disambig" title="Palestine">Palestine</a>. They drew a <a href="/wiki/Border" title="Border">border</a> between Iraq and Syria in 1920. France controlled Syria until 1946 when Syria became its own country.<sup id="cite_ref-WDL_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WDL-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Syria was part of the United Arab Republic with <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> in 1958-1961. Syria had some wars with <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and some territories like the <a href="/wiki/Golan_Plateau" class="mw-redirect" title="Golan Plateau">Golan Plateau</a> were occupied by Israel. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Syria is between latitudes 32° and 38° N, and longitudes 35° and 43° E. It is mostly arid <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a>. The area bordering the Mediterranean is fairly green. The <a href="/wiki/Euphrates" title="Euphrates">Euphrates</a>, Syria's most important river, crosses the country in the east. </p>
      <p>The climate in Syria is dry and hot. Winters are mild. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_government">Politics and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics and government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Politics and government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Syria is a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The old <a href="/w/index.php?title=Constitution_of_Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Constitution of Syria (not yet started)">Constitution of Syria</a> was started on 13 March 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-const_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-const-9">[9]</a></sup> It made Syria as a secular socialist state. <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> was the majority religion. A new constitution has been in place since 2012. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Branches_of_government">Branches of government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Branches of government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Branches of government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Executive_branch" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive branch">executive branch</a> is the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Syria" title="President of Syria">president</a>, two <a href="/wiki/Vice_president" title="Vice president">vice presidents</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">prime minister</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Ministers" class="mw-redirect" title="Council of Ministers">Council of Ministers</a>. The constitution says the president must be a Muslim.<sup id="cite_ref-const_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-const-9">[9]</a></sup> It does not make Islam the state religion. According to the 2012 constitution, the <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> is elected by Syrian people in a direct election. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=People%27s_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="People's Council (not yet started)">People's Council</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_branch" class="mw-redirect" title="Legislative branch">legislative branch</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_control">State control</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: State control">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: State control">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Nearly all of Syria’s radio and television outlets are state owned. The <a href="/wiki/Ba%27ath_Party" title="Ba'ath Party">Ba'ath Party</a> controls nearly all newspapers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_rights">Human rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Human rights">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Human rights">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Syria's human rights are among the worst in the world, according to human rights organizations such as <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Watch" title="Human Rights Watch">Human Rights Watch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The authorities arrest democracy and human rights activists, <a href="/wiki/Censorship" title="Censorship">censor</a> websites, detain bloggers, and have travel bans. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governorates">Governorates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Governorates">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Governorates">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Syria has fourteen Governorates, or <i>muhafazat</i>. The governorates are divided into sixty districts. The governorates are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Al-Hasakah_Governorate" title="Al-Hasakah Governorate">Al Hasakah</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Latakia_Governorate" title="Latakia Governorate">Al Ladhiqiyah</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Quneitra_Governorate" title="Quneitra Governorate">Al Qunaytirah</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Raqqa_Governorate" title="Raqqa Governorate">Ar Raqqah</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/As-Suwayda_Governorate" title="As-Suwayda Governorate">As Suwayda</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Daraa_Governorate" title="Daraa Governorate">Dara</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Deir_ez-Zor_Governorate" title="Deir ez-Zor Governorate">Dayr az Zawr</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Damascus_Governorate" title="Damascus Governorate">Dimashq</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Aleppo_Governorate" title="Aleppo Governorate">Halab</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Hama_Governorate" title="Hama Governorate">Hama</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Homs_Governorate" title="Homs Governorate">Homs</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Idlib_Governorate" title="Idlib Governorate">Idlib</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Rif_Dimashq_Governorate" title="Rif Dimashq Governorate">Rif Dimashq</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Tartus_Governorate" title="Tartus Governorate">Tartus</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Syria" title="President of Syria">President of Syria</a> is commander in chief of the Syrian armed forces. There are about 400,000 troops. Males must go in the military when they are age 18.<sup id="cite_ref-globalsecurity_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globalsecurity-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Syria is a middle-income country. The economy is based on agriculture, oil, industry, and tourism. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Transport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Transport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Syria has three international airports (Damascus, Aleppo and Lattakia). They are hubs for Syrian Air. Foreign airlines also fly to them.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Most Syrian cargo is carried by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chemins_de_Fer_Syriens&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chemins de Fer Syriens (not yet started)">Chemins de Fer Syriens</a>, the Syrian railway company. </p>

      <p>Education is free from ages 6 to 12. All children this age must attend school. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The most popular sports in Syria are <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swimming (sport)">swimming</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a>. <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a> was home to the fifth and seventh <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_Arab_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pan Arab Games (not yet started)">Pan Arab Games</a>. Many popular football teams are based in Damascus, Aleppo, Homs, and Latakia. </p>
