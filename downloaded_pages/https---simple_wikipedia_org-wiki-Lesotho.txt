      <p><b>Lesotho</b> is a small country in southern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> is about 1,800,000. The capital of Lesotho is called <a href="/wiki/Maseru" title="Maseru">Maseru</a>. </p>
      <p>Lesotho has the highest <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a> rate in Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesotho&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesotho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Lesotho has no <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">sea</a> nor on a lake. This type of country is called <i>landlocked</i>, meaning it is surrounded by land. All of Lesotho's <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> must either be flown in by <a href="/wiki/Aeroplane" class="mw-redirect" title="Aeroplane">aeroplane</a>, or brought in by land. Lesotho position is unusual: it is completely surrounded by the country of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> and has no borders with any other country. This type of country is called an <i><a href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">enclave</a></i>. Enclave countries are very rare, but Lesotho is one (there are only two other countries like this, <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a>). This makes Lesotho's relationship with South Africa very important to them both. </p>
      <p>Lesotho has many <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a> and is one of the most mountainous countries in the world. All of Lesotho is at least 1,400 m above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>. People sometimes travel there to <a href="/wiki/Ski" class="mw-redirect" title="Ski">ski</a> in <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a>. The many mountains in Lesotho mean that it rains there a lot. Lesotho uses some of its water to generate <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a> and sells some of the water to South Africa. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesotho&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesotho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Lesotho became a country in <a href="/wiki/1818" title="1818">1818</a>, but it was then called <b>Basutoland</b>. A man called Moshoeshoe brought several of the groups of people in the area together and formed a new country with him as its <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a>. This new country came under attack from its enemies and in <a href="/wiki/1868" title="1868">1868</a> Moshoeshoe asked <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> for help. Great Britain said it would help if Lesotho became part of the British Empire. </p>
      <p>Lesotho eventually gained its <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> on <a href="/wiki/4_October" class="mw-redirect" title="4 October">4 October</a> <a href="/wiki/1966" title="1966">1966</a>. Now it is a member of the British Commonwealth. King Letsie III is the ruler of Lesotho. </p>
