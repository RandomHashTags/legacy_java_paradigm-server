      <p><b>Nigeria</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 188,500,000,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> more than any other country in Africa. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Abuja" title="Abuja">Abuja</a>. Nigeria is known for being a large country, and one of the richest in Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>From the <a href="/wiki/1500s" title="1500s">1500s</a> to the <a href="/wiki/1800s" title="1800s">1800s</a>, many people from the land now called Nigeria (and other parts of West Africa) were taken away and turned into <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> by <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a>, and they were sent to work in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>. These slaves were bought and sold in the Americas by Europeans who lived there. Today, many people related to those slaves still live in America, though they are no longer slaves. They are called <a href="/wiki/African-Americans" class="mw-redirect" title="African-Americans">African-Americans</a>. </p>
      <p>From <a href="/wiki/1901" title="1901">1901</a> to 1960, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> ruled Nigeria. However, by 1960 the people wanted independence very much, and Britain finally let them have it. For some time after this, Nigeria was a <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship" title="Dictatorship">dictatorship</a>, where the leaders stayed in control even if the majority of people disliked them. At this time, Nigeria had a big war called the Biafro War with Christian <a href="/wiki/Igbo_people" title="Igbo people">Igbo people</a> who wanted their own country in the East. They did not want to be a part of Nigeria, which was ruled by The Muslim North and Mixed West. The war ended with a reunification of Nigeria. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/1999" title="1999">1999</a>, Nigeria became a democracy, where people choose their leaders. After that, <a href="/wiki/Olusegun_Obasanjo" title="Olusegun Obasanjo">Olusegun Obasanjo</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Yoruba" class="mw-redirect" title="Yoruba">Yoruba</a> <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> from the south, became President. In 2007, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Umaru_Yar%27Adua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Umaru Yar'Adua (not yet started)">Umaru Yar'Adua</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Hausa" class="mw-redirect" title="Hausa">Hausa</a> <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, was elected to be the next President. Yar'Adua died in May, 2009. <a href="/wiki/Goodluck_Jonathan" title="Goodluck Jonathan">Goodluck Jonathan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Vice_president" title="Vice president">vice president</a>, then became president. </p>
      <p>Goodluck Jonathan was re-elected as the president after wining the 2011 general elections under the political party, PDP. </p>
      <p>He served as the country's president until 2015. </p>
      <p>Since 2002, there have been a battles in the north of the country. The battles are between government troops and the <a href="/wiki/Islamism" title="Islamism">Islamist</a> group <a href="/wiki/Boko_Haram" title="Boko Haram">Boko Haram</a>. This group wants to have <a href="/wiki/Sharia_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Sharia law">Sharia law</a> for the country.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Nigeria has a total area of 923,768&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (356,669&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It is the world's 32nd-largest country. It shares a border with <a href="/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a> (773&nbsp;km), <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> (1497&nbsp;km), <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> (87&nbsp;km) and <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> (1690&nbsp;km). It has a coastline of at least 853&nbsp;km.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Anthem_Of_Nigeria">National Anthem Of Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: National Anthem Of Nigeria">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: National Anthem Of Nigeria">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Nigeria, there are two <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthems</a>. The first one is called "Nigeria We Hail Thee". The second and current national anthem is called "Arise O' Compatriots. The first one was written by Lilian Jean William and composed by Frances Berda. The second one was written by 5 people and was composed by the Police band. Arise O' Compatriots was first heard during Nigeria's independence. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Nigeria, there are almost equal numbers of Muslims and Christians. Most of the Christians live in the south, and most of the Muslims live in the north. Contrary to some beliefs, the Nigerian civil war was not only attributed to religious intolerance. The war which took place between 6 July 1967 – 15 January 1970, was a political conflict caused by the attempted secession of the southeastern provinces of Nigeria as the self-proclaimed Republic of Biafra. The conflict was the result of economic, ethnic, cultural and religious tensions among the various peoples of Nigeria. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_resources">Natural resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Natural resources">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Natural resources">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Nigeria produces a large amount of oil, and some fighting has been going on because many people want a share of the oil profits. This fighting has been happening in the area called the <a href="/wiki/Niger_Delta" title="Niger Delta">Niger Delta</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a> flows into the Atlantic Ocean. </p>
      <p>The largest city in Nigeria is <a href="/wiki/Lagos" title="Lagos">Lagos</a>. Nigeria has both grasslands and rainforests, and can get very hot, because it is close to the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>. Petroleum and agriculture make up the Nigerian economy. </p>

      <p>Nigerian cuisine, like West African cuisine in general, is known for its richness and variety. Many different spices, herbs and flavourings are used along with <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil" title="Palm oil">palm oil</a> or <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">groundnut</a> oil. These make deeply flavoured sauces and soups often made very hot with <a href="/wiki/Chili_pepper" title="Chili pepper">chili peppers</a>. Nigerian feasts are colourful and lavish. Good smelling market and roadside snacks cooked on barbecues or fried in oil are plentiful and varied.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigeria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Sport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Association football is Nigeria's national sport. The country has its own <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigerian_Premier_League&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nigerian Premier League (not yet started)">Premier League</a> of football. Nigeria's Men's <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nigerian_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nigerian national football team (not yet started)">national football team</a>, known as the Super Eagles, has made the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> five times. These were in <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998</a>, <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002</a>, <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010</a>, <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014</a>, and most recently in <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018</a>. They won the African Cup of Nations in <a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_African_Cup_of_Nations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1980 African Cup of Nations (not yet started)">1980</a>, <a href="/wiki/1994_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="1994 African Cup of Nations">1994</a> and <a href="/wiki/2013_African_Cup_of_Nations" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 African Cup of Nations">2013</a>. They also hosted the Junior World Cup. They won the gold medal for football in the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a>. </p>
      <p>Nigeria is also involved in other sports such as basketball, <a href="/wiki/Cricket_(sport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cricket (sport)">cricket</a>, sprints and track and field.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Boxing is also an important sport in Nigeria; <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dick Tiger (not yet started)">Dick Tiger</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Peter&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Samuel Peter (not yet started)">Samuel Peter</a> are both former World Champions. </p>
