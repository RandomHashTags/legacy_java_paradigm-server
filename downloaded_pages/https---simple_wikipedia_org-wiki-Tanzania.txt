      <p><b>Tanzania</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a>. The official languages are <a href="/wiki/Kiswahili" class="mw-redirect" title="Kiswahili">Kiswahili</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. It is next to Africa's biggest lake, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a>. </p>
      <p>In 2017, there were about 54,000,000 people living in Tanzania.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> They are divided into 120 <a href="/wiki/Tribe" title="Tribe">tribes</a>; none have more than 10% of the population. Tanzania is a multicultural society. There are many languages &amp; religions in Tanzania; the main ones being <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>. </p>
      <p>The president of the United Republic of Tanzania is John Magufuli. Tanzania is a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, UNIDO, the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement" title="Non-Aligned Movement">Non-Aligned Movement</a>, SADC, PTA, and the ADB. It is also signatory to the Lome Convention. </p>
      <p>Mainland Tanzania (formerly <a href="/wiki/Tanganyika" title="Tanganyika">Tanganyika</a>) received <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> in December 1961, while <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a> gained her independence in January 1964, through a <a href="/wiki/Revolution" title="Revolution">revolution</a>. The two countries joined together to form the United Republic of Tanzania in April 1964. Until July 1992, Tanzania was a one-party state. Following a Presidential Commission, the country has started a multi-party political system. It has held successful general elections in 1995 and 2000. " </p>
      <p>Tanzania is home to the <a href="/wiki/Serengeti" title="Serengeti">Serengeti</a>. Many animals such as <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheetah" title="Cheetah">cheetahs</a> live there. </p>
