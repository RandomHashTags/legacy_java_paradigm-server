      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkmenistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkmenistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Turkmenistan was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> since its creation, in 1922. At first, Turkmenistan was an <a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkmen_Autonomous_Soviet_Socialist_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Turkmen Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic (not yet started)">autonomous republic</a> within <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian SFSR</a>. In 1925 it was promoted to a Soviet republic. Turkmenistan is an independent country since 1991, after the breakup of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. <a href="/wiki/Saparmurat_Niyazov" title="Saparmurat Niyazov">Saparmurat Niyazov</a> became Turkmenistan’s first president in 1991. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkmenistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkmenistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Turkmenistan is one of the driest desert areas in the world. Some places have an average annual precipitation of only 12&nbsp;mm (0.47&nbsp;in). The highest temperature recorded in <a href="/wiki/Ashgabat" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashgabat">Ashgabat</a> is 48.0&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">°C</a> (118.4&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit" title="Fahrenheit">°F</a>). <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kerki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kerki (not yet started)">Kerki</a>, an extreme inland city located on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Amu_Darya" title="Amu Darya">Amu Darya</a> river, recorded 51.7&nbsp;°C (125.1&nbsp;°F) in July 1983, but this value is unofficial. 50.1C is the highest temperature recorded at Repetek Reserve, recognized as the highest temperature ever recorded in the whole former Soviet Union. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkmenistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkmenistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Turkmenistan is the world's 52nd-largest country. It is slightly smaller than <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and somewhat larger than the US state of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. Over 80% of the country is covered by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Karakum_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Karakum Desert (not yet started)">Karakum Desert</a>. </p>
      <p>The Turkmen shore along the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> is 1,768 kilometres (1,099&nbsp;mi) long. The <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> is entirely landlocked, with no access to the ocean. </p>
      <p>The major cities include <a href="/wiki/Ashgabat" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashgabat">Aşgabat</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%BCrkmenba%C5%9Fy,_Turkmenistan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Türkmenbaşy, Turkmenistan (not yet started)">Türkmenbaşy</a> (formerly Krasnovodsk) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Da%C5%9Foguz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Daşoguz (not yet started)">Daşoguz</a>. </p>
      <p>Turkmenistan has a humid climate. Annual rainfall is low, particularly in summer. Winters are dry and mild. The wettest region in Turkmenistan is Köýtendağ Range. Köýtendağ is situated in southeastern Turkmenistan on the borders with Uzbekistan. </p>
