      <p><b>South Ossetia</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ɒ/: 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/j/: 'y' in 'yes'">j</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>, <small>less commonly</small> <span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ɒ/: 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="/ʃ/: 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> officially the <b>Republic of South Ossetia – the State of Alania</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> or the <b>Tskhinvali Region</b>, is a <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> independent, <a href="/wiki/Border_dispute" title="Border dispute">disputed</a> place in <a href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">South Caucasus</a>. It declared independence from <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> in 1990. </p>
      <p>South Ossetia was a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a> (region) with some self-rule and controlled big parts of the region. When it declared its independence in 1990, Georgia tried to take back the region by force and it led to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=1991-1992_South_Ossetia_War&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1991-1992 South Ossetia War (not yet started)">1991-1992 South Ossetia War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Georgia tried to retake South Ossetia again in 2004 and in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-king_fivedaywar_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-king_fivedaywar-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2008, Ossetia's fighters were backed by <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> troops and they gained full control of the region, but its separation from Georgia has only been <a href="/wiki/Unrecognised_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Unrecognised state">recognized</a> by three<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.">source?</span></a></i>]</sup> other countries (<a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Abkhazia" title="Abkhazia">Abkhazia</a> (a similar place), and it is <a href="/wiki/De_jure" title="De jure">de jure</a> (officially) a part of the Georgian region (<a href="/wiki/Mkhare" title="Mkhare">mkhare</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Shida_Kartli" title="Shida Kartli">Shida Kartli</a>. </p>
      <p>The rebels were supported by Russian and Cossack forces but, Georgia itself refuses to recognize rebel South Ossetia as an independent state; the government calls it by the medieval name of Samachablo or, more recently, <a href="/wiki/Tskhinvali" title="Tskhinvali">Tskhinvali</a> region (after the republic's capital). </p>
      <p>South Ossetia relies heavily on Russian aid.<sup id="cite_ref-Economy_Army_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Economy_Army-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Sometimes, South Ossetia, <a href="/wiki/Transnistria" title="Transnistria">Transnistria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Artsakh" title="Republic of Artsakh">Artsakh</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Abkhazia" title="Abkhazia">Abkhazia</a> are named collectively as post-Soviet "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Frozen_conflict&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Frozen conflict (not yet started)">frozen conflict</a>" zones.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Ossetia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Ossetia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>South Ossetia is situated at central <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a>, a border between Asia and Europe. It occupies a part of <a href="/wiki/Greater_Caucasus" title="Greater Caucasus">Greater Caucasus</a> range and the foothills of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kartalin_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kartalin Valley (not yet started)">Kartalin Valley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-About_the_Republic_of_South_Ossetia_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About_the_Republic_of_South_Ossetia-12">[12]</a></sup> South Ossetia is a very mountainous region. </p>
