      <p><b>Burkina Faso</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in West <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It used to be called <a href="/wiki/Upper_Volta" title="Upper Volta">Upper Volta</a> and the name was changed to Burkina Faso in 1984. The country was once ruled by <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, but it has been independent since 1960. The capital is <a href="/wiki/Ouagadougou" title="Ouagadougou">Ouagadougou</a>. </p>
      <p>In 2005, about 13,228,000 people lived in the country. It is next to <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Togo" title="Togo">Togo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a> and <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire" class="mw-redirect" title="Côte d'Ivoire">Côte d'Ivoire</a>. It does not have any <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a> with an <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">sea</a>. People from Burkina Faso are called Burkinabé (pronounced burr-KEE-na-bay). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burkina_Faso&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burkina_Faso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>People have lived in the area of Burkina Faso for thousands of years. At first, they were <a href="/wiki/Hunter-gatherer" title="Hunter-gatherer">hunter-gatherers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a> animals and collecting <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruits</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vegetable" title="Vegetable">vegetables</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-8">[8]</a></sup> Later they became <a href="/wiki/Farmer" title="Farmer">farmers</a>. People called the <a href="/wiki/Mossi" title="Mossi">Mossi</a> arrived between the 11th and 13th centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-8">[8]</a></sup> They ruled the area until the end of the 19th century. In 1896 <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> beat the Mossi kingdom and became the <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonial</a> rulers of Burkina Faso. After <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, the country was called <a href="/wiki/Upper_Volta" title="Upper Volta">Upper Volta</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1960, Upper Volta became independent from France. The first <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> of the new country was <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Yam%C3%A9ogo" title="Maurice Yaméogo">Maurice Yaméogo</a>. After he became the president, Yaméogo banned other <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a>. For several years the people of Upper Volta were very unhappy with the government and in 1966 the <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> took over in a military <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup</a>. In 1983 the government was taken over again by military men called <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Sankara" title="Thomas Sankara">Thomas Sankara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Compaor%C3%A9" title="Blaise Compaoré">Blaise Compaoré</a>. Sankara became president. In 1984, he changed the name of the country to Burkina Faso. It means "land of honest people".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In December 1985, Burkina Faso went to war for five days with near-by country <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>. In 1987, there was another military coup and Sankara was <a href="/wiki/Assassination" title="Assassination">assassinated</a> (murdered). Blaise Compaoré became the president. </p>
      <p>On 28 October 2014 protesters began to march and demonstrate in Ouagadougou. Compaoré was ready to change the constitution and <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/extend" class="extiw" title="wikt:extend">extend</a>his 27-year rule. On 30 October 2014, some protesters set fire to the parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> They also took over the national television headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 31 October 2014, President Compaoré, resigned after 27 years in office.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2015 the country held their first election. Roch Marc Christian Kabore, a former Prime Minister, was elected as President. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burkina_Faso&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burkina_Faso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Burkina Faso is made up of two major types of countryside. The larger part of the country is covered by a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Peneplain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Peneplain (not yet started)">peneplain</a>. It has a gently undulating landscape with a few isolated hills. The southwest of the country forms a <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> <a href="/wiki/Massif" title="Massif">massif</a>. The highest peak, Ténakourou, is found at an elevation of 749 meters (2,457&nbsp;ft). The area is bordered by sheer cliffs up to 150 meters (492&nbsp;ft) high. The average altitude of Burkina Faso is 400 meters (1,312&nbsp;ft). The difference between the highest and lowest terrain is no greater than 600 meters (1,969&nbsp;ft). Burkina Faso is a mostly flat country. </p>
      <p>Burkina Faso has a tropical climate with two distinct seasons. In the rainy season, the country receives between 600 and 900 millimeters (23.6 and 35.4&nbsp;in) of rainfall. In the dry season, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Harmattan (not yet started)">harmattan</a> – a hot dry wind from the Sahara – blows. The rainy season lasts about four months, from May/June to September. It is shorter in the north of the country. </p>
      <p>Burkina Faso's natural resources include <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">manganese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marble" title="Marble">marble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phosphate" title="Phosphate">phosphates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pumice" title="Pumice">pumice</a>, salt and small deposits of gold. </p>
      <p>Burkina Faso's fauna and flora are protected in two national parks and several reserves. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burkina_Faso&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burkina_Faso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">Literature</a> in Burkina Faso is based on the <a href="/wiki/Oral_tradition" title="Oral tradition">oral tradition</a>, which remains important. Since the 1970s, literature has developed in Burkina Faso with many more writers being published.<sup id="cite_ref-JM_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JM-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There is also a large artist community, especially in <a href="/wiki/Ouagadougou" title="Ouagadougou">Ouagadougou</a>. Much of the crafts produced are for the growing tourist industry. </p>
      <p>The food of Burkina Faso, typical of west African cuisine, is based around <a href="/wiki/Staple_food" title="Staple food">staple foods</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sorghum" title="Sorghum">sorghum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Millet" title="Millet">millet</a>, rice, maize, <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">peanuts</a>, potatoes, <a href="/wiki/Bean" title="Bean">beans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yam_(vegetable)" class="mw-redirect" title="Yam (vegetable)">yams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Okra" title="Okra">okra</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ox_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ox-15">[15]</a></sup> The most common sources of protein are chicken, eggs and fresh water fish. </p>
