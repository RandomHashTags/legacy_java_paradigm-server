      <p><b>Guatemala</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. It is known for its mixed culture, a product of both <a href="/wiki/Mayan" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayan">Mayan</a> and Spanish cultures, from the <a href="/wiki/Colonial_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonial America">colonial period</a>. Guatemala is known for its ever-changing weather, a product of the mountain range which crosses the center of the country. Guatemala has many languages, a total of 23. Because of its natural beauty, it is a popular <a href="/wiki/Tourist" class="mw-redirect" title="Tourist">tourist</a> destination. </p>
      <p>The capital of Guatemala is <a href="/wiki/Guatemala_City" title="Guatemala City">Guatemala City</a>. The country suffers from extreme poverty, corruption, drug trafficking, and discrimination. </p>
      <p>Guatemala is the world's largest producer and exporter of <a href="/wiki/Cardamom" title="Cardamom">cardamom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>It is considered to be a third world country. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guatemala&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guatemala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>It is the country with the largest <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Neighbor" class="mw-redirect" title="Neighbor">neighbors</a> are the countries <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>. More than fourteen million people live in the 22 Guatemalan departments. Most of the population is made up of mestizo (mixed race) people, who are a combination of Native and European people. Mestizo people make up 40% of the population. Native Mayan people make up 49% of the total population, while 9% are direct descendants of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> people. Black people (including some others), make up 2% of population. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guatemala&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guatemala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Most people believe that Guatemala City was the first choice for the capital city, but the capital was moved several times. This was caused by natural disasters. The first city was destroyed when the <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> Volcan de Agua erupted, forcing the move to what today is called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Antigua_Guatemala&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Antigua Guatemala (not yet started)">Antigua Guatemala</a>. This city was destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Earthquakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Earthquakes">earthquakes</a>. Finally, the capital was moved to what we know as Guatemala City. </p>
