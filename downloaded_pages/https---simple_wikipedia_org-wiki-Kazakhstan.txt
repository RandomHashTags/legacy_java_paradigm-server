      <p><b>Kazakhstan</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the middle of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. Its official name is the <b>Republic of Kazakhstan</b>. Kazakhstan is the ninth biggest country in the world, and it is also the biggest <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> country in the world. Before <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union#Dissolution" title="Soviet Union">the end</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, it was called "Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic". The <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> of the country from 1991 through March 2019 was <a href="/wiki/Nursultan_Nazarbayev" title="Nursultan Nazarbayev">Nursultan Nazarbayev</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nur-Sultan" title="Nur-Sultan">Nur-Sultan</a> is the capital city of Kazakhstan. <a href="/wiki/Almaty" title="Almaty">Almaty</a> was the capital until 1998, when it moved to Nur-Sultan, which was called Astana at that time. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh language</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Native_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Native language">native language</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> has equal official status for all administrative and institutional purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> is the largest religion about 70% of the population are Muslims, with <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> practiced by 26%;<sup id="cite_ref-2009_Census_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009_Census-10">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> leases (rents) the land for the <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> (site of Russian <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">spacecraft</a> launches) from Kazakhstan. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Kazakhstan is a <a href="/wiki/Transcontinental_country" title="Transcontinental country">transcontinental country</a> mostly in Asia with a small western part across the <a href="/wiki/Ural_River" title="Ural River">Ural River</a> in Europe. It has borders with the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a> in the north and west, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a> in the southwest, and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> in the far east. The northern border is mostly with <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, so Russia has the longest border with Kazakhstan.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-11">[10]</a></sup> Basically, Kazakhstan runs from the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> in the west to the mainly <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> Chinese autonomous region of <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a>. </p>
      <p>Kazakhstan has no <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a> shoreline, but borders the Caspian Sea, which boats use to get to neighboring countries. The Caspian Sea is an <a href="/wiki/Endorheic_basin" title="Endorheic basin">Endorheic basin</a> without connections to any ocean. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_resources">Natural resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Natural resources">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Natural resources">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Kazakhstan has plenty of <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a>. It attracted over $40 billion in foreign investment since 1993 and accounts for some 57% of the nation's industrial output. According to some estimates,<sup id="cite_ref-Homestead_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Homestead-12">[11]</a></sup> Kazakhstan has the second largest <a href="/wiki/Uranium" title="Uranium">uranium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chromium" title="Chromium">chromium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lead" title="Lead">lead</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zinc" title="Zinc">zinc</a> reserves, the third largest <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">manganese</a> reserves, the fifth largest <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> reserves, and ranks in the top ten for <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>. It is also an exporter of <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamonds</a>. Kazakhstan has the 11th largest proven reserves of both petroleum and natural gas.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provinces">Provinces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Provinces">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Provinces">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Kazakhstan is divided into 14 provinces. The provinces are divided into districts. </p>
      <p>Almaty and Nur-Sultan cities have the status of State importance and are not in any province.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA-recommended_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-recommended-14">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Baikonur&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Baikonur (not yet started)">Baikonur</a> city has a special status because it is leased to Russia for Baikonur cosmodrome until 2050.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA-recommended_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-recommended-14">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Each province is headed by an Akim (provincial governor) appointed by the president. Municipal Akims are appointed by province Akims. The Government of Kazakhstan moved its capital from Almaty to Nur-Sultan on December 10, 1997. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kazakhstan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of Kazakhstan is 17,165,000. It takes the 62th place in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population" class="mw-redirect" title="List of countries by population">List of countries by population</a>. Average density is one of the lowest on earth with almost 6 people/km<sup>2 </sup> ( <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population_density" class="mw-redirect" title="List of countries by population density">List of countries by population density</a>). </p>
