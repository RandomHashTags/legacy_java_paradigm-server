      <p><b>Latvia</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">Northern Europe</a>. The capital is <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a>. It is one of the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltic States">Baltic States</a>, together with <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> in the north and <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> in the south. Latvia's neighbours to the east are the countries <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>. Latvia is split into four parts called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurzeme&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kurzeme (not yet started)">Kurzeme</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vidzeme&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vidzeme (not yet started)">Vidzeme</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zemgale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zemgale (not yet started)">Zemgale</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latgale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Latgale (not yet started)">Latgale</a>. </p>
      <p>People from Latvia are called <b>Latvians</b>. They speak the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian language</a>, which is a little like the <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian language</a>, though not close enough to be understood. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latvia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latvia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Latvia was settled by the Baltic tribes thousands of years ago. They mainly fished, hunted, and traded. </p>
      <p>German traders and crusaders came to Latvia at the end of the 12th century. Latvians lost control of their homeland. Over the next 800 years, Germans, Danes, Swedes, Poles, and Russians all invaded Latvia. Latvia finally became independent in 1918. </p>
      <p>The Soviet Union invaded Latvia during World War II and killed or took away many of its people to Siberia and other places far away from their homes. The Soviet Union then invaded again and occupied Latvia until 1991, when the Soviet Union fell apart and Latvia became an <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent country</a> again. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latvia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latvia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> on 13 December 2002, Latvia and nine other countries were invited to join the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. On 20 September 2003, Latvians held an election to vote on joining. Two thirds of Latvians voted to join, and on <a href="/wiki/1_May" class="mw-redirect" title="1 May">1 May</a> <a href="/wiki/2004" title="2004">2004</a> Latvia became a member of the EU. </p>
      <p>Latvia has been a NATO member since <a href="/wiki/29_March" class="mw-redirect" title="29 March">29 March</a>, <a href="/wiki/2004" title="2004">2004</a>. </p>
