      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>People have lived in Estonia since at least 9,000&nbsp;BC. The oldest known settlement in Estonia is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pulli_settlement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pulli settlement (not yet started)">Pulli settlement</a>, which was on the banks of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=P%C3%A4rnu_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pärnu River (not yet started)">Pärnu River</a>. </p>
      <p>Estonia became <a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Tartu_(Russian%E2%80%93Estonian)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Treaty of Tartu (Russian–Estonian) (not yet started)">independent from Russia</a> in 1920 after a brief <a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_War_of_Independence&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Estonian War of Independence (not yet started)">War of Independence</a> at the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> (1939–1945), Estonia was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Germany</a>. It then became the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic">Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic</a> and lost its independence. </p>
      <p>Estonia had its independence restored in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It became a member of the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Treaty_Organization" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic Treaty Organization">North Atlantic Treaty Organization</a> (NATO) on 29 March 2004. It joined the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> on 1 May 2004. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Estonia is bordered to the north by the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Finland" title="Gulf of Finland">Gulf of Finland</a> with <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> on the other side, to the west by the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> on the other side, to the south by <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> (343&nbsp;km), and to the east by <a href="/wiki/Lake_Peipus" title="Lake Peipus">Lake Peipus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> (338.6&nbsp;km).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Average elevation reaches only 50 metres (164&nbsp;ft). The country's highest point is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Suur_Munam%C3%A4gi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Suur Munamägi (not yet started)">Suur Munamägi</a> in the southeast at 318 metres (1,043&nbsp;ft). </p>
      <p>Estonia has many <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>, almost 50% of the land is covered with <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-timber_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timber-14">[14]</a></sup> The most common tree species are pine, spruce and birch.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Estonia has over 1,400 <a href="/wiki/Lakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Lakes">lakes</a> and over 2,000 <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a>. The longest rivers are <a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%B5handu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Võhandu (not yet started)">Võhandu</a> (162&nbsp;km or 101&nbsp;mi), <a href="/w/index.php?title=P%C3%A4rnu_(river)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pärnu (river) (not yet started)">Pärnu</a> (144&nbsp;km or 89&nbsp;mi), and <a href="/w/index.php?title=P%C3%B5ltsamaa_(river)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Põltsamaa (river) (not yet started)">Põltsamaa</a> (135&nbsp;km or 84&nbsp;mi). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biodiversity">Biodiversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Biodiversity">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Biodiversity">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Mammals in Estonia include the <a href="/wiki/Grey_wolf" class="mw-redirect" title="Grey wolf">grey wolf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_lynx" title="Eurasian lynx">lynx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brown_bear" title="Brown bear">brown bear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_fox" title="Red fox">red fox</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_badger" title="European badger">badger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wild_boar" title="Wild boar">wild boar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moose" title="Moose">moose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_deer" title="Red deer">red deer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roe_deer" title="Roe deer">roe deer</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_beaver" class="mw-redirect" title="European beaver">beaver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_otter" title="Eurasian otter">otter</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_seal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Grey seal (not yet started)">grey seal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ringed_seal" title="Ringed seal">ringed seal</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Critically_endangered_species" title="Critically endangered species">critically endangered</a> <a href="/wiki/European_mink" title="European mink">European mink</a> has been successfully reintroduced to the island of <a href="/wiki/Hiiumaa" title="Hiiumaa">Hiiumaa</a>. The rare <a href="/w/index.php?title=Siberian_flying_squirrel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Siberian flying squirrel (not yet started)">Siberian flying squirrel</a> is present in east Estonia.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Over 300 bird species have been found in Estonia. These include the <a href="/wiki/White-tailed_eagle" title="White-tailed eagle">white-tailed eagle</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_spotted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lesser spotted eagle (not yet started)">lesser spotted eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_eagle" title="Golden eagle">golden eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_capercaillie" title="Western capercaillie">western capercaillie</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Stork&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Black Stork (not yet started)">black</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=White_stork&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="White stork (not yet started)">white stork</a>, many species of <a href="/wiki/Owls" class="mw-redirect" title="Owls">owls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geese" class="mw-redirect" title="Geese">geese</a> and many others.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Barn_swallow" title="Barn swallow">Barn swallow</a> is the <a href="/wiki/National_bird" class="mw-redirect" title="National bird">national bird</a> of Estonia.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Protected_areas_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Protected areas of Estonia (not yet started)">Protected areas</a> cover 18% of Estonian land and 26% of its sea territory. There are 5 national parks, 159 nature reserves, and many other protection areas.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>According to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Speedtest.net&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Speedtest.net (not yet started)">speedtest.net</a> Estonia has one of the fastest Internet download speeds in the world with an average download speed of 27.12 Mbit/s.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Estonia won the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a> in 2001 with the song "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Everybody_(Eurovision_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Everybody (Eurovision song) (not yet started)">Everybody</a>" performed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tanel_Padar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tanel Padar (not yet started)">Tanel Padar</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Benton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dave Benton (not yet started)">Dave Benton</a>. In 2002, Estonia hosted the event. </p>
      <p>The Estonian <a href="/wiki/National_Day" title="National Day">National Day</a> is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Independence_Day_(Estonia)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Independence Day (Estonia) (not yet started)">Independence Day</a> celebrated on 24 February. This is the day the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_Declaration_of_Independence&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Estonian Declaration of Independence (not yet started)">Estonian Declaration of Independence</a> was issued. As of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Estonia&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, there are 12 public holidays and 12 national holidays celebrated each year. </p>
