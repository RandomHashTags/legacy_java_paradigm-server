      <p><b>Malaysia</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. It is a federation which has 13 states. It is divided into two parts by the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>. Its mainland is on the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a>. It faces the <a href="/wiki/Malacca" title="Malacca">Straits of Malacca</a> on its west coast and the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> on its east coast. The other part of this country, sometimes known as <a href="/wiki/East_Malaysia" title="East Malaysia">East Malaysia</a>, is on the northern part of the island of <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a> in the South China Sea. <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a> is its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a>. The federal capital was recently moved to <a href="/wiki/Putrajaya" title="Putrajaya">Putrajaya</a>, a newly <a href="/wiki/New_town" title="New town">created city</a> exclusively designed for administration. </p>
      <p>The country is&nbsp;multi-ethnic&nbsp;and&nbsp;multi-cultural, with most people being <a href="/wiki/Malays_(ethnic_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Malays (ethnic group)">Malay</a>, but there are also <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indians</a>. Its official language is <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a> written in the <a href="/wiki/Latin_alphabet" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin alphabet">Latin alphabet</a>. <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is a recognised language and also the official language in <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a> state along with Malay. <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> is also often used. There are over 130 other languages spoken in Malaysia, with 94 in Malaysian Borneo and 40 on the peninsula. <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> is the official religion, but non-Malay <a href="/wiki/Citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Citizen">citizens</a> have the freedom to practice other religions. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Between <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, Malaysia was an ancient trading place. When <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a> came to this area, <a href="/wiki/Malacca" title="Malacca">Malacca</a> became an important trade port. </p>
      <p>The states of Malaysia became a colony of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British Empire</a>, starting with Penang in 1786. The peninsular part became independent on 31 August 1957 as the Federation of Malaya. In September 1963, Malaya, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and the Borneo part joined together to become Malaysia. In 1965, Singapore was expelled from the federation and declared independence. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_economy">Politics and economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics and economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics and economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Malaysia has 13 <a href="/wiki/State" title="State">states</a>, which are Johor, Kedah, Kelantan, Melaka, Negeri Sembilan, Pahang, Perak, Perlis, Penang, Sabah, Sarawak, Selangor and Terengganu, and three <a href="/w/index.php?title=Federal_territories&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Federal territories (not yet started)">federal territories</a>, Kuala Lumpur, Putrajaya and Labuan. The number of points of the star on the flag represents the number of states Malaysia has, but it has 14 because Singapore was one of the states in Malaysia during its creation. The 14th point now represents the federal territories, called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilayah_Persekutuan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wilayah Persekutuan (not yet started)">Wilayah Persekutuan</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> of Malaysia is known as the <a href="/wiki/Yang_di-Pertuan_Agong" title="Yang di-Pertuan Agong">Yang di-Pertuan Agong</a>, otherwise called the "King of Malaysia". That title is currently held by Sultan <a href="/wiki/Abdullah_of_Pahang" title="Abdullah of Pahang">Abdullah of Pahang</a>. Malaysia's <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a>. Its current prime minister is <a href="/wiki/Mahathir_Mohamad" title="Mahathir Mohamad">Mahathir Mohamad</a>, upon being elected in May 2018 at age 92. It also has a <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Judiciary" title="Judiciary">court system</a>. It is a founding member of <a href="/wiki/ASEAN" class="mw-redirect" title="ASEAN">ASEAN</a>. Its economy is steadily growing and it is a relatively rich country in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Malaysia is a multi-ethnic, multi-cultural and multilingual society, consisting of 65% Malays and other indigenous tribes, 25% Chinese, 7% Indians. The Malays, which form the largest community, are all Muslims since one has to be Muslim to be legally Malay under Malaysian law. The Malays play a dominant role politically and are included in a grouping identified as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bumiputera&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bumiputera (not yet started)">bumiputera</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_drink">Food and drink</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Food and drink">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Food and drink">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Many cultures from within the country and from surrounding areas have influenced the cuisine. Much of the influence comes from the Malay, Chinese, Indian, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Javanese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatran</a> cultures,&nbsp;largely because the country was part of the ancient&nbsp;spice route. </p>
      <p>Food from one culture is sometimes also cooked using ways taken from another culture, for example,&nbsp;shrimp paste&nbsp;(belacan) and&nbsp;sambal&nbsp;are commonly used by Chinese restaurants for cooking. This means that although much of Malaysian food can be traced back to a certain culture, they have their own identity.&nbsp;Rice is popular in many dishes. Chili is commonly found in local cuisine, although this does not necessarily make them spicy. Examples are <a href="/wiki/Laksa" title="Laksa">laksa</a>, which is a spicy <a href="/wiki/Noodle" title="Noodle">noodle</a> dish common in Malaysia and teh tarik, which is a popular drink. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Malaysia's national sports centre is the Bukit Jalil Sports Complex. <a href="/wiki/Nicol_David" title="Nicol David">Nicol David</a> (squash) and Lee Chong Wei (badminton) are notable athletes from Malaysia. Malaysia won 12 gold medals in the Commonwealth Games 2010 in India, and broke their Commonwealth Games target. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Transport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malaysia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Transport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Malaysia's road network covers 98,721&nbsp;kilometres (61,342&nbsp;mi) and includes 1,821&nbsp;kilometres (1,132&nbsp;mi) of <a href="/wiki/Highway" title="Highway">expressways</a>. The longest highway of the country, the North-South Expressway, extends over 800&nbsp;kilometres (497&nbsp;mi) between the Thai border and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>. The road systems in <a href="/wiki/Sabah" title="Sabah">Sabah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a> are less developed and of lower quality in comparison to that of Peninsular Malaysia. Malaysia has 118 airports, of which 38 are paved. The official airline of Malaysia is <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines" title="Malaysia Airlines">Malaysia Airlines</a>, providing international and domestic air service alongside two other carriers. The railway system is state-run, and covers a total of 1,849&nbsp;kilometres (1,149&nbsp;mi). There are also Light Rail Transit in Kuala Lumpur. </p>
