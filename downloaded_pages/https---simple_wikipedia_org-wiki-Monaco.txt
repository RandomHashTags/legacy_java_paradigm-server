      <p><b>Monaco</b>, officially the <b>Principality of Monaco</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: Principauté de Monaco), is the second smallest <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the world after the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a>; approximately 39,000 persons live there. It is near south-eastern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>. <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> is the most common language spoken in Monaco. The Sovereign Prince of Monaco is <a href="/wiki/Prince_Albert_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Albert II">Prince Albert II</a>, whose family has ruled since 1297. The government and the prince share power. <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> is the main <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a>. People in Monaco pay no <a href="/wiki/Income_tax" class="mw-redirect" title="Income tax">income tax</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>, famous for its <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casino</a>, is in the north-east of the country. Monaco is famous for two car races: the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monte_Carlo_Rally&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Monte Carlo Rally (not yet started)">Monte Carlo Rally</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. </p>
      <p>A sovereign and independent state, the Principality of Monaco has borders on its land-ward side with several communes of the French Department of the <a href="/wiki/Alpes-Maritimes" title="Alpes-Maritimes">Alpes-Maritimes</a>; from west to east these are Cap d’Ail, La Turbie, Beausoleil and Roquebrune Cap Martin. Seawards, Monaco faces the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a>. </p>
      <p>The population of the Principality consists of 39,102 inhabitants (2019), 5,070 of whom are Monégasques, 12,047 French and 5,000 Italian (according to the last official census in 1990). </p>
      <p>Its surface area is 2.02 square kilometres, of which approximately 0.4 were recovered from the sea since 1980. </p>
      <p>It lies in a narrow coastal strip which sometimes rises straight upwards with its highest point at 163 metres. Its width varies between 1,050 metres and only 350 metres. Its coastline is 4,100 metre long. </p>
      <p>The Principality has only one <a href="/wiki/Commune" title="Commune">commune</a>, Monaco, whose limits are the same as those of the state. </p>
      <p>Monaco is made of four districts: Monaco-Ville (historic seat of the Principality, on the monolith where the Prince’s Palace stands), <a href="/wiki/Monte-Carlo" class="mw-redirect" title="Monte-Carlo">Monte-Carlo</a> (the district surrounding its Casino), La Condamine (around Port Hercules), and Fontvielle (the new industrial area built on ground reclaimed from the sea, which constitutes 0.22 square kilometres). </p>
