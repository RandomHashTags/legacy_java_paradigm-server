      <p>The <b>Central African Republic</b> (<b>CAR</b>) (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">République centrafricaine</i>, <small>pronounced&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[ʁepyblik sɑ̃tʁafʁikɛn]</a></span>, or <i>Centrafrique</i> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[sɑ̃tʀafʀik]</span>; <a href="/wiki/Sango" class="mw-redirect" title="Sango">Sango</a> <i>Ködörösêse</i> <i>tî Bêafrîka</i>), is a <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> country in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Central Africa</a>. It is mostly a <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> or high, flat piece of land about 941&nbsp;metres above the sea. The CAR covers a land area of about 620,000 square kilometres (240,000&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi), and has an estimated population of about 4.4 million as of 2008. <a href="/wiki/Bangui" title="Bangui">Bangui</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a>. </p>
      <p>There are thick forests in the south, and these are home to rare <a href="/wiki/Gorilla" title="Gorilla">gorillas</a>. This part of Africa also has <a href="/wiki/Pygmies" class="mw-redirect" title="Pygmies">Pygmies</a>. </p>
      <p>Other important towns are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bouar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bouar (not yet started)">Bouar</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zinga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zinga (not yet started)">Zinga</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ouadda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ouadda (not yet started)">Ouadda</a>. Its southern border is the <a href="/wiki/Ubangi_River" title="Ubangi River">Ubangi River</a>. There are 3.8 million people in the C.A.R. Most of them belong to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Banda_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Banda people (not yet started)">Banda</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Baya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Baya (not yet started)">Baya</a> tribes. The main language is <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> but some people speak the national language of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sango_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sango language (not yet started)">Sango</a>. </p>
      <p>The people mostly grow their own food, but sometimes droughts (long spells without any rain) make this difficult. The C.A.R. also has <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamond</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uranium" title="Uranium">uranium</a> mines. It sells these, as well as <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, and timber to other countries to make money. </p>
      <p>The currency used is the CFA Franc - 1 US Dollar is worth 590.3 CFA Francs<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>. </p>
      <p>The country was ruled by <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> until 1960 when it became independent. </p>
      <p>The country has not been very stable since then, with multiple <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">wars with itself</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Corruption" title="Corruption">corruption</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_African_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_African_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">

      <p>The Central African Republic is a <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> nation. It is bordered by the countries of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a>. </p>
      <p>Much of the Central African Republic is flat, or rolling plateau <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a>. It is typically about 500 metres (1,640&nbsp;ft) above sea level. Most of the northern half is in the <a href="/wiki/World_Wildlife_Fund" class="mw-redirect" title="World Wildlife Fund">World Wildlife Fund</a>'s East Sudanian savanna <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a>. In the northeast are the Fertit Hills. There are scattered hills in the southwest part of the country. To the northwest is the Yade Massif, a <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> plateau with an altitude of 1,143 feet (348&nbsp;m). </p>
      <p>At 622,941 square kilometres (240,519&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi), the Central African Republic is the world's 42nd-largest country. It is about the size of the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, and is somewhat smaller than the US state of <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. </p>
      <p>Much of the southern border is formed by tributaries of the <a href="/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a>. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mbomou_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mbomou River (not yet started)">Mbomou River</a> in the east merges with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uele_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Uele River (not yet started)">Uele River</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/Ubangi_River" title="Ubangi River">Ubangi River</a>. In the west, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sangha_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sangha River (not yet started)">Sangha River</a> flows through part of the country. The eastern border is along the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Nile_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Nile River">Nile River</a> <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">watershed</a>. </p>
      <p>Forest covers up to 8% of the land. The densest parts are in the south. The forest is highly diverse. It includes commercially important <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ayous&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ayous (not yet started)">Ayous</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sapele&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sapele (not yet started)">Sapelli</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sipo_(timber)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sipo (timber) (not yet started)">Sipo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The deforestation rate is 0.4% per year, and lumber <a href="/w/index.php?title=Poach&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Poach (not yet started)">poaching</a> is commonplace.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
