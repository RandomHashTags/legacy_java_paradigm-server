            <li value="1">Around one-third of the people (230,000) live in the capital, Georgetown.</li> 
           </ol>
          </div></td>

      <p>The <b>Co-operative Republic of Guyana</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. It is one of the most sparsely populated countries in the world (700,000 people live there inside its over 200,000&nbsp;km² of area). <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Guyana" title="Georgetown, Guyana">Georgetown</a> is the capital; like most of the country's settlements, it is on a flat coastal <a href="/wiki/Plain" title="Plain">plain</a> that goes from northwest to southeast. <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> claims the land west of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Essequibo_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Essequibo River (not yet started)">Essequibo River</a> as part of <a href="/wiki/Guayana_Esequiba" title="Guayana Esequiba">Guayana Esequiba</a>. </p>
      <p>Guyana's current <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> is <a href="/wiki/Irfaan_Ali" title="Irfaan Ali">Irfaan Ali</a>. The country gained <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on May 26, 1966 (before which it was called <b>British Guiana</b>). </p>
      <p>Guyana is member of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. It is <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_countries_by_suicide_rate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="List of countries by suicide rate (not yet started)">ranked first</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicides</a> per capita worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
