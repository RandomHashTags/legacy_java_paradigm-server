      <p><b>Malta</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. It is an island near the center of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Malta is <a href="/wiki/Valletta" title="Valletta">Valletta</a>. Around 400,000 people live in Malta and it is one of the most <a href="/wiki/Dense" class="mw-redirect" title="Dense">densely</a> <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">populated</a> countries in the world. Its many <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">cities</a> and <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">towns</a> grew close to each other over time. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="***REMOVED***">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Language">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Language">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a> is the national <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a> of Malta. Maltese and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> are the official languages in line with the constitution of the country. A majority of the people of Malta can speak three languages; Maltese, English and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Money">Money</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Money">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Money">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Malta had its own <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a>, called the <a href="/wiki/Maltese_Lira" class="mw-redirect" title="Maltese Lira">Maltese Lira</a> until 2008. The Maltese <i>Lira</i> was better known as the Maltese <i>Pound</i> because of the strong links to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. One Maltese Lira consisted of 100 <i>cents</i>. In 2008 Malta joined the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a> countries in adopting the Euro as the National Currency. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>People have lived in Malta since at least <a href="/w/index.php?title=5200_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="5200 BC (not yet started)">5200 BC</a>. The island has some key <a href="/wiki/Archeology" class="mw-redirect" title="Archeology">archaeological</a> sites dating from early times. </p>
      <p>Malta became well known as the base of the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights Hospitaller</a>. They moved to Malta after being forced to leave <a href="/wiki/Palestine" class="mw-disambig" title="Palestine">Palestine</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">Rhodes</a>, in Greece. In 1565 they survived a great attack by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>. The history of the knights at Malta continued the strong <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholic</a> beliefs in the country that were started by the Norman and Spanish rulers. </p>
      <p>In 1798, <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a> took control of Malta. The <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> rule was not popular and in <a href="/wiki/1800" title="1800">1800</a> Maltese rebels invited the British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> to come to the island. This led to 200 years as a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Independent" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> member. </p>
      <p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Malta was attacked this time by the Axis forces. The entire island was <a href="/wiki/Symbol" title="Symbol">symbolically</a> given the <a href="/wiki/George_Cross" title="George Cross">George Cross</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hero" title="Hero">heroism</a>. </p>
      <p>Malta became independent in <a href="/wiki/1964" title="1964">1964</a> and a republic in <a href="/wiki/1974" title="1974">1974</a>. In <a href="/wiki/2004" title="2004">2004</a> Malta joined the <a href="/wiki/EU" class="mw-redirect" title="EU">European Union</a> and in <a href="/wiki/2008" title="2008">2008</a> Malta joined the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a>. </p>
