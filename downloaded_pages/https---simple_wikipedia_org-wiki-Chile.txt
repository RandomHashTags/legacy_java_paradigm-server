      <p><b>Chile</b> (officially called <b>Republic of Chile</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> on the south-western side of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Most people there speak <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>. </p>
      <p>Chile, which claims a part of the <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctic continent</a>, is the longest country on earth. The <a href="/wiki/Atacama_Desert" title="Atacama Desert">Atacama Desert</a>, in the north of the country, is the driest place on earth. The average rainfall there is less than 0.05&nbsp;mm (0.0020&nbsp;in) per year. The center of Chile, with the two cities Santiago and Valparaíso, has a <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a> with an average temperatures of 28&nbsp;°C (82&nbsp;°F) in January and 11&nbsp;°C (52&nbsp;°F) in July. In the middle of Chile, the country is very good for growing things. </p>
      <p>There are about 16.9 million people living in Chile in 2009. About 10 million people live in the center of Chile around Valparaíso and <a href="/wiki/Santiago,_Chile" title="Santiago, Chile">Santiago</a>, on about 20% of the total land. </p>
      <p>Chile is a well-educated country. Only 2.7% are not able to read or write. Some believe that Chile has one of the best school systems in South America. </p>
      <p>About 95% of Chileans are people with a combination of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> descent, mostly <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a>, but also <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italians" class="mw-redirect" title="Italians">Italian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arab_people" title="Arab people">Arab people</a>. Around 2% of the population is <a href="/wiki/Native_American" title="Native American">Native American</a>, but most people have native ancestors. <a href="/wiki/Immigrants" class="mw-redirect" title="Immigrants">Immigrants</a> are 7% of the population. including Peruvians, Bolivians, Colombians, Haitians, Chinese and Europeans. The majority of people are <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> (62.8%), but many do not go to church. About 10% are <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a>, and there are some <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jews</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> as well. The country's official language is <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>. <a href="/wiki/Chili_pepper" title="Chili pepper">Chili peppers</a>, first cultivated by Native Americans from other Latin American countries and the United States, did not come from this country, although it has a similar name. </p>
      <p>Chile's currency is the Chilean <i>peso</i>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Chile borders <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> to the northeast, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> to the east, and the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a> in the far south. It is over 4,630 kilometres (2,880&nbsp;mi) north to south, but only 430 kilometres (270&nbsp;mi) at its widest point east to west. </p>
      <p>The northern <a href="/wiki/Atacama_Desert" title="Atacama Desert">Atacama Desert</a> has great mineral wealth, mostly <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nitrate" title="Nitrate">nitrates</a>. Chile is the largest producer of copper.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Andes_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Andes Mountains">Andes Mountains</a> are on the eastern border. </p>
      <p>Chile controls <a href="/wiki/Easter_Island" title="Easter Island">Easter Island</a> and Sala y Gómez Island, the easternmost islands of <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animals">Animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Animals">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Animals">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Only a few of the many distinctive South American animals are found in Chile. Among the larger mammals are the <a href="/wiki/Cougar" class="mw-redirect" title="Cougar">puma</a> or cougar, the llama-like <a href="/wiki/Guanaco" title="Guanaco">guanaco</a> and the fox-like chilla. In the forest region, several types of <a href="/wiki/Marsupial" title="Marsupial">marsupials</a> and a small deer known as the pudu are found. </p>
      <p>There are many species of small birds, but not most of the larger common Latin American types. Few freshwater fish are from Chile, but North American trout have been successfully introduced into the Andean lakes. The coast of Chile is close to the <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">Humboldt Current</a>, so ocean waters have many fish and other forms of marine life. This in turn supports a rich variety of waterfowl, including several penguins. There are many whales, and six species of seals in the area. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fungi">Fungi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Fungi">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Fungi">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Just over 3,000 species of <a href="/wiki/Fungi" class="mw-redirect" title="Fungi">fungi</a> are recorded in Chile.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This number is far from complete. The true total number of fungal species in Chile is likely to be far higher. The generally accepted estimate is that only about 7 percent of all fungi worldwide have so far been discovered.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plants">Plants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Plants">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Plants">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The northernmost coastal and central region is largely empty of vegetation. It is the most close to an absolute desert in the world. On the slopes of the Andes, besides the scattered tola desert brush, grasses are found. The central valley has several species of <a href="/wiki/Cactus" title="Cactus">cacti</a>, the hardy espinos (a kind of <a href="/wiki/Acacia" title="Acacia">acacia</a> tree), the Chilean pine, the southern <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beeches</a> and the copihue, a red bell-shaped flower that is Chile's national flower. </p>
      <p>In southern Chile, south of the <a href="/wiki/Biob%C3%ADo_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Biobío River">Biobío River</a> lots of rain has made dense forests of laurels, magnolias, and various species of conifers and beeches, which become smaller and more stunted to the south.<sup id="cite_ref-Bio&amp;Con_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio&amp;Con-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The cold temperatures and winds of the extreme south make it impossible for heavy forestation. <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">Grassland</a> is found in Atlantic Chile (in <a href="/wiki/Patagonia" title="Patagonia">Patagonia</a>). </p>
      <p>Much of the Chilean plant life is different from that of neighboring <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. This shows that the Andean barrier existed during the formation of Chile.<sup id="cite_ref-Bio&amp;Con_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio&amp;Con-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regions">Regions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Regions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Regions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">Literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Literature">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Literature">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Chileans call their country <i>país de poetas</i>-country of poets.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gabriela_Mistral" title="Gabriela Mistral">Gabriela Mistral</a> was the first <a href="/wiki/Latin_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin American">Latin American</a> to receive a Nobel Prize for Literature (1945). Chile's most famous poet, however, is <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Neruda" title="Pablo Neruda">Pablo Neruda</a>. He also received the Nobel Prize for Literature (1971). </p>
      <p>Among the list of other Chilean poets are Lily Garafulic, Vicente Huidobro, Pablo Simonetti, and Paulo Coloane. Isabel Allende is the best-selling Chilean novelist, with 51 millions of her novels sold worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Novelist José Donoso's novel <i>The Obscene Bird of the Night</i> is said by critic Harold Bloom to be one of the important works of 20th century Western literature. Another internationally recognized Chilean novelist is Roberto Bolaño. His translations into English have had an excellent reception from the critics.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_drink">Food and drink</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Food and drink">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Food and drink">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Chilean food shows the differences in the land across the country. There is an assortment of seafood, beef, fruits, and vegetables. Traditional recipes include asado, cazuela, empanadas, humitas, pastel de choclo, pastel de papas, curanto and sopaipillas.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Crudos is an example of the mixture of culinary additions from the various ethnic groups in Chile. Onions were brought by the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish colonists</a>, and the use of mayonnaise and yogurt was introduced by <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> immigrants, as was beer. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Chile's most popular sport is <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a>. Chile has been in eight FIFA World Cups which includes hosting the <a href="/wiki/1962_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1962 FIFA World Cup">1962 FIFA World Cup</a>. Other results by the national football team include four finals at the Copa América, one silver and two bronze medals at the <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games" title="Pan American Games">Pan American Games</a>, a bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> and two third places finishes in the FIFA under-17 and under-20 youth tournaments. The top league in the Chilean football league system is the Chilean Primera División. It was named by the IFFHS in 2011 as the ninth strongest national football league in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-iffhs_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iffhs-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">Tennis</a> is Chile's most successful sport. Its national team won the World Team Cup clay tournament twice (2003 &amp; 2004). They played the Davis Cup final against <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> in 1976. At the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> the country took gold and bronze in men's singles and gold in men's doubles. Marcelo Ríos became the first Latin American man to reach the number one spot in the ATP singles rankings in 1998. Anita Lizana won the <a href="/wiki/US_Open_(tennis)" title="US Open (tennis)">US Open</a> in 1937. She was the first woman from Latin America to win a grand slam tournament. Luis Ayala was twice a runner-up at the French Open and both Ríos, Nicolas Massu Friedt and Fernando González Ciuffardi reached the Australian Open men's singles finals. González also won a silver medal in singles at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in Beijing. </p>
      <p>At the Summer Olympic Games Chile has a total of two gold medals (tennis), seven silver medals (athletics, <a href="/wiki/Equestrian" class="mw-redirect" title="Equestrian">equestrian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a>, shooting and tennis) and four bronze medals (tennis, boxing and football). In 2012 Chile won its first Paralympic Games medal (gold in Athletics). </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Rodeo" title="Rodeo">Rodeo</a> is the country's <a href="/wiki/National_sport" title="National sport">national sport</a>. It is practiced in the more rural areas of the country. A sport similar to <a href="/wiki/Hockey" title="Hockey">hockey</a>. <a href="/wiki/Skiing" title="Skiing">Skiing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snowboarding" title="Snowboarding">snowboarding</a> are practiced at ski centers in the Central Andes. <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">Surfing</a> is popular at some coastal towns. <a href="/wiki/Polo" title="Polo">Polo</a> is professionally practiced in Chile. In 2008 Chile won top prize in the World Polo Championship. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">Basketball</a> is a popular sport. Chile earned a bronze medal in the first men's FIBA World Championship held in 1950. They won a second bronze medal when Chile hosted the 1959 FIBA World Championship. Chile hosted the first FIBA World Championship for Women in 1953 finishing the tournament with the silver medal. Other sports such as marathons and ultramarathons are also increasing in popularity. San Pedro de Atacama is host to the yearly "Atacama Crossing," a six-stage, 250-kilometer footrace which has about 150 competitors from 35 countries each year. The Dakar Rally off-road automobile race has been held in both Chile and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> since 2009. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_symbols">National symbols</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: National symbols">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: National symbols">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Colca-condor-c03.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Colca-condor-c03.jpg/220px-Colca-condor-c03.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Colca-condor-c03.jpg/330px-Colca-condor-c03.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Colca-condor-c03.jpg/440px-Colca-condor-c03.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3528" data-file-height="2324"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Colca-condor-c03.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>The <a href="/wiki/Andean_Condor" class="mw-redirect" title="Andean Condor">Andean Condor</a> is the national bird of Chile
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The national flower is the copihue (<i>Lapageria rosea</i>, Chilean bellflower), which grows in the woods of southern Chile. </p>
      <p>The coat of arms shows the two national animals: the <a href="/wiki/Condor" title="Condor">condor</a> (<i>Vultur gryphus</i>, a very large bird that lives in the mountains) and the huemul (<i>Hippocamelus bisulcus,</i> an endangered white tail deer). It also has the saying <i>Por la razón o la fuerza</i> (<i>By reason or by force</i>). </p>
      <p>The flag of Chile has two equal horizontal bands of white (top) and red. There is a blue square the same height as the white band. The square has a white five-pointed star in the center. The star is a guide to progress and honor. Blue is for the sky, white is for the snow-covered Andes, and red stands for the blood spilled to get independence. </p>
