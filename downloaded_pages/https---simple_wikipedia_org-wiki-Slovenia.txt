      <p><b>Slovenia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Europe" title="Southern Europe">Southern Europe</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> and largest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> of Slovenia is <a href="/wiki/Ljubljana" title="Ljubljana">Ljubljana</a>. Its major language is Slovenian. Its current population is about 2.0 million. Slovenia's leading exports are manufactured goods and <a href="/wiki/Aluminium" title="Aluminium">aluminium</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_republic" title="Parliamentary republic">parliamentary republic</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> It is a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup> The economy of Slovenia is small, open, and export-oriented. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovenia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A very long time ago, <a href="/wiki/Illyria" title="Illyria">Illyrian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Celt" class="mw-redirect" title="Celt">Celtics</a> tribes lived in Slovenia. In the 1st century BC, Slovenia was ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a>. In the sixth century AD, Slavs lived there. Slovenia was ruled by <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> from 1335 until 1918. In 1918 it became a part of <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> took parts of the country but in 1945 it became part of <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a> again. </p>
      <p>In June 1991, following a 10-day war, Slovenia became an <a href="/wiki/Independent" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent">independent</a> country. </p>
      <p>Currently it is considered the most advanced country from what is called "Ex-Yugoslavia."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2009)">source?</span></a></i>]</sup> It is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. </p>
