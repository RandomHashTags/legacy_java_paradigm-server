      <p><b>Lao People's Democratic Republic</b> or <b>Laos</b> (ລາວ), is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in southeast <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. The capital of Laos is <a href="/wiki/Vientiane" title="Vientiane">Vientiane</a>. </p>
      <p>Laos is <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> (it does not have a <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">sea</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a>). It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> (used to be known in English as "<a href="/wiki/Burma" class="mw-redirect" title="Burma">Burma</a>") and by <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> to the northwest, by <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> to the east, by <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a> to the south and by <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> to the west. The <a href="/wiki/Mekong" class="mw-redirect" title="Mekong">Mekong</a> river forms a large part of the western boundary with Thailand. Boats from Laos cannot get to the ocean using the Mekong because of rapids and waterfalls in the south of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a> is <a href="/wiki/Lao_language" title="Lao language">Lao</a>, a language belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Tai_languages" title="Tai languages">Tai language group</a>. 98% of Lao people believe in <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>. The main crop in Laos is <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laos&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Laos is the only landlocked country in Southeast Asia. The country is slightly smaller than <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>. The landscape is mostly rugged mountains. The highest point is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Phou_Bia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Phou Bia (not yet started)">Phou Bia</a> at 2,818 metres (9,245 feet). The Mekong River forms the border with Thailand. The mountains of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Annamite_Range&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Annamite Range (not yet started)">Annamite Range</a> form the eastern border with Vietnam. The climate is tropical. The rainy season is from May to November. The dry season is from December to April. Laos has three seasons (rainy, cold and hot). The capital and largest city is <a href="/wiki/Vientiane" title="Vientiane">Vientiane</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laos&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fa_Ngum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fa Ngum (not yet started)">Fa Ngum</a> created the first unified Lao Kingdom. </p>
      <p>Lao became a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> colony in 1893 and also became part of <a href="/wiki/French_Indochina" title="French Indochina">French Indochina</a>. In 1949 it became independent from France, as the Kingdom of Laos. Later there was a civil war, and in 1975 it became a One-party state under the leadership of the <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communist</a> party.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laos&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government and politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Government and politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Laos is one of the world's five remaining <a href="/wiki/Communist_state" title="Communist state">communist states</a>. The only legal political party is the Lao People's Revolutionary Party (LPRP). The head of state is President Choummaly Sayasone. He is the General Secretary of the Lao People's Revolutionary Party. </p>
      <p>Shortly after the end of the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, the North Vietnamese Army (NVA) joined forces with the Pathet Lao, started a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup</a> on December 2, 1975 to overthrow the royalist Lao government, and established a communist government that continues to run the country to this day. </p>
      <p>The current head of government is Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Thongloun_Sisoulith" title="Thongloun Sisoulith">Thongloun Sisoulith</a>, as of 2016. The current President is <a href="/wiki/Bounnhang_Vorachith" title="Bounnhang Vorachith">Bounnhang Vorachith</a>. Government policies are determined by the party. Important government decisions are checked by the Council of Ministers. </p>
