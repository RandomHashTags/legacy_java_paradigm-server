      <p><b>The Independent State of Samoa</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It became independent from <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> in 1962. It has two <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands, including Upolu and Savai'i</a>. The capital of Samoa is <a href="/wiki/Apia" title="Apia">Apia</a>. It is on the island of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upolu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Upolu (not yet started)">Upolu</a>. The head of the country is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Va%27aletoa_Sualauvi_II&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Va'aletoa Sualauvi II (not yet started)">Va'aletoa Sualauvi II</a>. </p>
      <p>The languages spoken in Samoa include <a href="/w/index.php?title=Samoan_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Samoan language (not yet started)">Samoan</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. </p>
      <p>The sport that is most popular in Samoa is <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">Rugby</a>, and many Samoan people play Rugby. </p>
      <p>Samoa was admitted to the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> on 18 September 1962.<sup id="cite_ref-UN_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p> A <a href="/wiki/Measles" title="Measles">measles</a> <a href="/wiki/2019_Samoa_measles_outbreak" title="2019 Samoa measles outbreak">outbreak began</a> in October 2019 and continued through December. As of December 28, there were 81 deaths out of 5667 cases.<sup id="cite_ref-PR43_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR43-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DeathToll_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DeathToll-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samoa&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samoa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Samoa is south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>. It is about halfway between <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hawai%E2%80%98i&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hawai‘i (not yet started)">Hawai‘i</a> and New Zealand in the <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesian</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. The main island of Upolu is home to nearly three-quarters of Samoa's people </p>
      <p>The two large islands of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upolu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Upolu (not yet started)">Upolu</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Savai%27i&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Savai'i (not yet started)">Savai'i</a> are 99% of the total land area. There are eight small islets. These are the three islets in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Apolima_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Apolima Strait (not yet started)">Apolima Strait</a>: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manono_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manono Island (not yet started)">Manono Island</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Apolima&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Apolima (not yet started)">Apolima</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nu%27ulopa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nu'ulopa (not yet started)">Nu'ulopa</a>. There are four <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleipata_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aleipata Islands (not yet started)">Aleipata Islands</a> off the eastern end of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upolu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Upolu (not yet started)">Upolu</a>. They are: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nu%27utele&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nu'utele (not yet started)">Nu'utele</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nu%27ulua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nu'ulua (not yet started)">Nu'ulua</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Namua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Namua (not yet started)">Namua</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fanuatapu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fanuatapu (not yet started)">Fanuatapu</a>. And there is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nu%27usafe%27e&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nu'usafe'e (not yet started)">Nu'usafe'e</a>. </p>
      <p>East of Samoa is a group of islands called <a href="/wiki/American_Samoa" title="American Samoa">American Samoa</a>. They are part of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, but they are similar to the country Samoa, because they also speak Samoan. </p>
      <p>Samoa used to be east of the <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">international date line</a>. In 2011, the line changed, so that Samoa would be to the west of the date line.<sup id="cite_ref-Stuff.co.nz_4974902_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stuff.co.nz_4974902-11">[11]</a></sup> This change took effect on the night of 29 December. Friday was skipped altogether and the following day was Saturday 31 December.<sup id="cite_ref-Samoans_set_to_time_travel_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Samoans_set_to_time_travel-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Samoa has 194,320 people. 92.6% are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Samoans&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Samoans (not yet started)">Samoans</a>, 7% <a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronesian&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Euronesian (not yet started)">Euronesians</a> (people of mixed, European and <a href="/wiki/Polynesians" class="mw-redirect" title="Polynesians">Polynesian</a> ancestors) and 0.4% are <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_ethnic_groups&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="European ethnic groups (not yet started)">Europeans</a>. </p>
      <p>Religion in Samoa includes the following: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_Congregational_Church_of_Samoa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Christian Congregational Church of Samoa (not yet started)">Christian Congregational Church of Samoa</a> 35.5%, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> 19.6%, <a href="/wiki/Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> 15%, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latter-day_Saint&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Latter-day Saint (not yet started)">Latter-day Saints</a> 12.7%, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Samoan_Assemblies_of_God&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Samoan Assemblies of God (not yet started)">Samoan Assemblies of God</a> 10.6%, <a href="/wiki/Seventh-day_Adventist_Church" title="Seventh-day Adventist Church">Seventh-day Adventist</a> 3.5%, Worship Centre 1.3%, unspecified 0.8%.There is also about 522 Jehovah's Witnessses in Samoa since 1931.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Samoa has one of seven <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_House_of_Worship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bahá'í House of Worship (not yet started)">Bahá'í Houses of Worship</a> in the world. It is in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiapapata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tiapapata (not yet started)">Tiapapata</a>. </p>
      <p>Many people say that Samoan people are related to <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori people</a> because lots of the language words are very similar to Maori language. </p>
      <p>As with other Polynesian cultures (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Hawaiian_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hawaiian culture (not yet started)">Hawai'ian</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahitians&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tahitians (not yet started)">Tahitian</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C4%81ori_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Māori culture (not yet started)">Māori</a>), Samoans have two gender specific and culturally important <a href="/wiki/Tattoo" title="Tattoo">tattoos</a>. For males, it is called the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pe%27a&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pe'a (not yet started)">Pe'a</a>, It is intricate and geometrical patterns <a href="/wiki/Tattoo" title="Tattoo">tattooed</a> on areas from the knees up towards the ribs. A male who has such a tatau is called a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Soga%27imiti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Soga'imiti (not yet started)">soga'imiti</a>. A Samoan girl or <i>teine</i> is given a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Malu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Malu (not yet started)">malu</a>. It covers the area from just below her knees to her upper thighs.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
