      <p><b>Pakistan</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">southern Asia</a>. It is next to <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. It is officially called the <b>Islamic Republic of Pakistan</b>. It has a long coastline along the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> in the south. Pakistan has the <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population" class="mw-redirect" title="List of countries by population">fifth largest population</a> (207.77 million) in the world. Pakistan has a total land area of 880,940&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (340,130&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) (including the Pakistani controlled territories of Azad Kashmir and Gilgit Baltistan). This makes Pakistan the 34th largest country in the world. Pakistan has the seventh largest army in the world. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> of Pakistan is <a href="/wiki/Islamabad" title="Islamabad">Islamabad</a>. Before 1960, it was <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a>, which is now the country's largest city. </p>
      <p>The name <b>Pākistān</b> means <i>Land of the Pure</i> in <a href="/wiki/Persian_and_Urdu" title="Persian and Urdu">Persian and Urdu</a>. </p>

title="-stan (not yet started)">Land of</a> (the) Spiritually Pure</i> in both <a href="/wiki/Urdu" title="Urdu">Urdu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> languages. Many <a href="/wiki/South-central_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="South-central Asia">South-central Asian</a> states and regions end with the element -<i>stan,</i> such as <i>Afghanistan,</i> <i>Pakistan,</i> <i>Balochistan,</i> <i>Kurdistan,</i> and <i>Turkistan.</i> This -<i>stan</i> is formed from the Iranian root *STA "to stand, stay," and means "place (where one stays), home, country". Iranian peoples have been the main inhabitants of the various land regions of the Ancient <a href="/wiki/Persian_Empire" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Persian Empire">Persian Empires</a> now owned by the states for over a thousand years. The names are compounds of -<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=-stan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="-stan (not yet started)">stan</a></i> and the name of the peoples living there. Pakistan is a bit different; its name was coined on 28 January 1933 as <i>Pakstan</i> by <a href="/wiki/Choudhary_Rahmat_Ali" title="Choudhary Rahmat Ali">Choudhary Rahmat Ali</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Movement" title="Pakistan Movement">Pakistan Movement</a> activist, who published it in his paper <i><a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Declaration" title="Pakistan Declaration">Now or Never</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-nowornever_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nowornever-14">[12]</a></sup> by using the suffix -<i>istan</i> from <i>Baloch</i>istan preceded by the first letters of <i>P</i>unjab, <i>A</i>fghania, <i>K</i>ashmir and <i>S</i>indh. The name is actually an <a href="/wiki/Acronym" title="Acronym">acronym</a> that stands for the "thirty million Muslim brethren who lived in Pakistan—by which we mean the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Islamic_Republic_of_India&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Islamic Republic of India (not yet started)">Five Northern units of India</a> viz: <a href="/wiki/Punjab_(Pakistan)" title="Punjab (Pakistan)"><b>P</b>unjab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">(<b>A</b>fghan Province)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kashmir" title="Kashmir"><b>K</b>ashmir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh"><b>S</b>ind</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Balochistan_(Pakistan)" title="Balochistan (Pakistan)">Baluchis<b>tan</b></a>".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[13]</a></sup> The letter <i>i</i> was incorporated to ease pronunciation and forms the linguistically correct and meaningful name.<sup id="cite_ref-davison_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davison-16">[14]</a></sup> Interestingly, a word almost the same in form, etymology, and meaning to the Iranian suffix -<i>stan</i> is found in Polish, which has a word <i>stan</i> meaning "state" (in the senses of both polity and condition). It can be found in the example of a Polish name for the "United States of America," <i>Stany Zjednoczone Ameryki</i> (literally "States United of America"). </p>
      <p><br> <b><big>National Anthem:</big></b> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Qaum%C4%AB_Tar%C4%81na" title="Qaumī Tarāna">lyrics</a> of the national anthem of Pakistan was written by <a href="/wiki/Ahmed_Ghulamali_Chagla" title="Ahmed Ghulamali Chagla">Ahmed Ghulamali Chagla</a> and was adopted as the country's national anthem on 13 August 1954. The music had actually been composed nearly three years before the lyrics were written to fit the tune. </p>
      <p><br> <b><big>Flag:</big></b> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Pakistan" title="Flag of Pakistan">flag</a> is a green field with a white crescent moon and five-rayed star at its centre, and a vertical white stripe at the hoist side. Though the green colour is mandated only as 'dark green', its official and most consistent representation is Pakistan green, which is shaded distinctively darker. </p>
      <p>The flag was designed by Syed Amir uddin Kedwaii and was based on the original flag of the Muslim League. It was adopted by the Constituent Assembly of Pakistan on August 11, 1947, just days before independence. The white colour on the flag represents the minorities and the green the Muslim majority. The crescent on the flag represents progress while the five-rayed star represents light and knowledge. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government and politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Government and politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Pakistan" title="Government of Pakistan">Government of Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Pakistan" title="Politics of Pakistan">Politics of Pakistan</a>
      </div> 
      <p><b>Pakistan</b> has a federal <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_system" title="Parliamentary system">parliamentary system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gop1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gop1-17">[15]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> is an indirectly-elected ceremonial <a href="/wiki/President_of_Pakistan" title="President of Pakistan">President</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Electoral_college" title="Electoral college">Electoral college</a> of the country, (composed of the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Pakistan" title="Senate of Pakistan">Senate</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Pakistan" title="National Assembly of Pakistan">National Assembly</a>, and the four Provincial Assemblies) chooses a leadership representing the President of Pakistan for a five-year term. The president is also the <a href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief" title="Commander-in-Chief">Commander in Chief</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pakistani_military" title="Pakistani military">Joint Armed Forces</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Pakistan" title="Prime Minister of Pakistan">Prime Minister</a>, who is also indirectly elected. </p>
      <p>The President's appointment and term are constitutionally independent of the Prime Minister’s term. The Prime Minister is usually the leader of the largest party in the National Assembly or of a coalition in the National Assembly. </p>
      <p>The Prime Minister is the head of government. Pakistan's legislature is made of a 100-member Senate (upper house) and a 342-member National Assembly (lower house). The Chief Justice of Pakistan is the chief judge who oversees the judicature's court system at all levels of command. </p>
      <p>On 17 August 2018 Ex-cricketer Imran Khan was elected as the New Prime Minister of Naya Pakistan. Because In 2018, <a href="/wiki/Imran_Khan" title="Imran Khan">Imran Khan</a> (the chairman of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Tehreek-e-Insaf" title="Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf">PTI</a>) Won the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Pakistan_general_election&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2018 Pakistan general election (not yet started)">2018 Pakistan general election</a> with 116 general seats and so became the current 22nd <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Pakistan" title="Prime Minister of Pakistan">Prime Minister of Pakistan</a> in election of <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Pakistan" title="National Assembly of Pakistan">National Assembly of Pakistan</a> for <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> by getting 176 votes against <a href="/wiki/Shehbaz_Sharif" title="Shehbaz Sharif">Shehbaz Sharif</a> (the chairman of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan_Muslim_League_(N)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pakistan Muslim League (N) (not yet started)">PMLN</a>) who got 96 votes.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[16]</a></sup> Pakistan is also headed by a "President", the Prime Minister Acts as the Leader of Pakistan. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Pakistan is officially a <a href="/wiki/Federal_republic" title="Federal republic">federal republic</a>, but during a long period in its history it changed to a democratic state and a military <a href="/wiki/Dictator" title="Dictator">dictatorship</a>. Military dictators include <a href="/wiki/Ayub_Khan" title="Ayub Khan">Ayub Khan</a> in the 1960s, General <a href="/wiki/General_Muhammad_Zia-ul-Haq" class="mw-redirect" title="General Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq">Zia-ul-Haq</a> in the 1980s. </p>
      <p>Pakistan's two largest <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a> are the <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Peoples_Party" title="Pakistan Peoples Party">Pakistan People's Party</a> and the government party <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan_Muslim_League_(N)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pakistan Muslim League (N) (not yet started)">Pakistan Muslim League (N)</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Tehreek-e-Insaf" title="Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf">Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf</a> has also gained popularity in the past years. </p>
      <p>On 27 December 2007, the leader of the Pakistan Peoples Party, <a href="/wiki/Benazir_Bhutto" title="Benazir Bhutto">Benazir Bhutto</a>, was <a href="/wiki/Assassinate" class="mw-redirect" title="Assassinate">assassinated</a>. The reason is yet to be determined. </p>

      <p>Pakistan is made up of four provinces, two territories and two special areas. Both special areas are in <a href="/wiki/Kashmir" title="Kashmir">Kashmir</a>. Each district is divided into several <a href="/wiki/Tehsil" title="Tehsil">tehsils</a> and each tehsil is divided into several union councils. There are around 596 tehsils and over 6,000 union councils in Pakistan. </p>

      </div> 
      <p>Pakistan has a semi-industrialized economy.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-siteresources.worldbank.org_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-siteresources.worldbank.org-23">[21]</a></sup> The growth poles of the Pakistani economy are situated along the <a href="/wiki/Indus_River" title="Indus River">Indus River</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-siteresources.worldbank.org_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-siteresources.worldbank.org-23">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[22]</a></sup> Diversified economies of Karachi and Punjab's urban centres, coexist with less developed areas in other parts of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-siteresources.worldbank.org_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-siteresources.worldbank.org-23">[21]</a></sup> Despite being a very poor country in 1947, Pakistan's economic growth rate has been better than the global average during the following four decades, but incautious policies led to a slowdown in the late 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-econ_1990s_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ_1990s-25">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Recently, large economic reforms have resulted in a stronger economic outlook and sped up growth especially in the manufacturing and financial services sectors.<sup id="cite_ref-econ_1990s_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ_1990s-25">[23]</a></sup> Since the 1990s, there has been great improvement in the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_exchange_market" title="Foreign exchange market">foreign exchange market</a> position and rapid growth in hard <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> reserves.<sup id="cite_ref-econ_1990s_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ_1990s-25">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The 2005 estimate of foreign debt was close to US$40&nbsp;billion. However, this decreased with help from the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a> and significant debt-relief from the United States. Pakistan's gross domestic product, as measured by <a href="/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity" title="Purchasing power parity">purchasing power parity</a>, is estimated to be $475&nbsp;billion<sup id="cite_ref-imfdata_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imfdata-26">[24]</a></sup> while its per capita income stands at $2,942.<sup id="cite_ref-imfdata_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imfdata-26">[24]</a></sup> The poverty rate in Pakistan is estimated to be between 23%<sup id="cite_ref-wbundp_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbundp-27">[25]</a></sup> and 28%.<sup id="cite_ref-ciapeople_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ciapeople-28">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Pakistan became <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_India" title="Partition of India">Independent</a> in 1947 from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> which was known as the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a>. The first organised people in Ancient Pakistan lived 9000 years ago. These people were the ones who made up the <a href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Indus Valley Civilization</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[27]</a></sup> which is one of the oldest civilizations on Earth. After that, the Vedic period came. This also included parts of north-western <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of India">Republic of India</a>. Until 1971, Pakistan also included an area in the <a href="/wiki/North-east_India" class="mw-redirect" title="North-east India">North-east India</a> region. This is now called <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>. It lost that area after a war with the Indian Army and the joint militant group of Indo-Bangladeshi alliance of <a href="/wiki/Mitro_Bahini" title="Mitro Bahini">Mitro Bahini</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a>. During recent times Pakistan has been in the centre of world politics. This is first because of its support to guerillas in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, following a Soviet <a href="/wiki/Invasion" title="Invasion">invasion</a> 1979, and later during the 1990s because of its cooperation with and support for the <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">Taliban</a> <a href="/wiki/Regime" title="Regime">regime</a> in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>. However, since 2000 Pakistan has supported the West in their war against fundamentalist <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorism</a>, including the removal of the Taliban regime in Afghanistan. </p>
      <p>Pakistan is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a>. However, after the war in East Pakistan the country was excluded (between 1972-1989). It was also a member between 1999 and 2007, it was excluded in 2007 for a time but again became a member in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[28]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Pakistan covers 880,940&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (340,130&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi),<sup id="cite_ref-area_from_factbook_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-area_from_factbook-31">[29]</a></sup> roughly similar to the combined land areas of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and the UK. Its eastern regions are located on the Indian plate and the western and northern regions on the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_plateau" title="Iranian plateau">Iranian plateau</a> and Eurasian landplate. Apart from the 1,046&nbsp;km (650&nbsp;mi) <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> coastline, Pakistan's <a href="/wiki/Borders_of_Pakistan" title="Borders of Pakistan">land borders</a> total 6,774&nbsp;km (4,209&nbsp;mi)—2,430&nbsp;km (1,510&nbsp;mi) with <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> to the northwest, 523&nbsp;km (325&nbsp;mi) with <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> to the northeast, 2,912&nbsp;km (1,809&nbsp;mi) with <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> to the south and east, and 909&nbsp;km (565&nbsp;mi) with <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> to the southwest.<sup id="cite_ref-ciafactbook_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ciafactbook-32">[30]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>The northern and western highlands of Pakistan contain the towering Karakoram and Pamir mountain ranges, which include some of the world's highest peaks, including <a href="/wiki/K2" title="K2">K2</a> 8,611&nbsp;m (28,251&nbsp;ft) and <a href="/wiki/Nanga_Parbat" title="Nanga Parbat">Nanga Parbat</a> 8,126&nbsp;m (26,660&nbsp;ft). The <a href="/wiki/Balochistan_(Pakistan)" title="Balochistan (Pakistan)">Balochistan</a> Plateau lies to the west, and the <a href="/wiki/Thar_Desert" title="Thar Desert">Thar Desert</a> and an expanse of alluvial plains, the Punjab and <a href="/wiki/Sindhustan" class="mw-redirect" title="Sindhustan">Sindh</a>, lie to the east. The 1,609&nbsp;km (1,000&nbsp;mi) <a href="/wiki/Indus_River" title="Indus River">Indus River</a> and its tributaries flow through the country from the disputed territory of <a href="/wiki/Occupied_Kashmir" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupied Kashmir">Occupied Kashmir</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[31]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Pakistan has four seasons: a cool, dry winter from December through February; a hot, dry spring from March through May; the summer rainy season, or southwest monsoon period, from June through September; and the retreating monsoon period of October and November. The beginning and length of these seasons vary somewhat according to location.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[32]</a></sup> Rainfall can change radically from year to year, and consecutive patterns of flooding and drought are also not uncommon.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[33]</a></sup> </p>

However a few <a href="/wiki/Minority_group" title="Minority group">minority groups</a> exist. Pakistan also has some <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sikh" class="mw-redirect" title="Sikh">Sikh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrians" class="mw-redirect" title="Zoroastrians">Zoroastrians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Animism" title="Animism">animist</a> minority groups in the <a href="/wiki/North" title="North">northern</a> parts of the country.</p>

      <p>After the split from <a href="/wiki/British_India" title="British India">British India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> had much less importance in the newly created state of Pakistan, but has played an important role in its culture and politics as well as the history of its regions. In fact, Pakistan has the 5th largest population of Hindus, after <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>. </p>
      <p>The word <i>Hindu</i> comes from the <a href="/wiki/Indus_River" title="Indus River">Sindhu</a> (Indus River) of Pakistan. The Sindhu is one of the holy rivers of Hinduism.       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poverty">Poverty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Poverty">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Poverty">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Poverty in Pakistan is a growing concern. Although the middle-class has grown in Pakistan, nearly one-quarter of the population is classified poor as of October 2006. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_at_the_Olympics" title="Pakistan at the Olympics">Pakistan at the Olympics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_national_field_hockey_team" title="Pakistan national field hockey team">Pakistan national field hockey team</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_national_football_team" title="Pakistan national football team">Pakistan national football team</a>

      <p>The national sport of Pakistan is <a href="/wiki/Field_hockey" title="Field hockey">field hockey</a>, although <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> is the most popular game across the country.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[35]</a></sup> The national cricket team has won the <a href="/wiki/Cricket_World_Cup" title="Cricket World Cup">Cricket World Cup</a> once (in 1992), were runners-up once (in 1999), and co-hosted the games twice (in 1987 and 1996). Pakistan were runners-up in the inaugural 2007 ICC World Twenty20 held in South Africa and were the champions at the 2009 ICC World <a href="/wiki/Twenty20" title="Twenty20">Twenty20</a> held in England. The team also won two Asia Cups in 2000 and 2012. Lately however, Pakistani cricket has suffered heavily due to teams refusing to tour Pakistan after militants attacked the touring <a href="/wiki/2009_attack_on_the_Sri_Lanka_national_cricket_team" title="2009 attack on the Sri Lanka national cricket team">Sri Lankan team in March 2009</a>, after which no international cricket was played until May 2015, when the Zimbabwean team agreed to tour. However, now, with increased security, the <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka_cricket_team" title="Sri Lanka cricket team">Sri Lankan cricket team</a> came for a tour as recently as 2019. </p>
      <p>In addition to sports like field hockey, cricket, squash rackets, football and others, Pakistanis are also very keen on <a href="/wiki/Equestrianism" title="Equestrianism">equestrianism</a> of various types,and equestrian sports such as <a href="/wiki/Polo" title="Polo">Polo</a> and the traditional <a href="/wiki/Tent_pegging" title="Tent pegging">Tent pegging</a> are played by many. Other traditional rural sports include two types of <a href="/wiki/Wrestling" title="Wrestling">Wrestling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kabbadi" class="mw-redirect" title="Kabbadi">Kabbadi</a> and a martial art called <a href="/wiki/Gatka" title="Gatka">Gatka</a>. </p>
