      <p><b>Nicaragua</b> is a country in Central America. It is officially called the <b>Republic of Nicaragua</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">República de Nicaragua</i>). It has a size of 129,494 square <a href="/wiki/Kilometre" title="Kilometre">kilometres</a>. It is the largest country in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. It borders <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> to the north and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> to the south. The capital of the country is <a href="/wiki/Managua" title="Managua">Managua</a>. Managua is the third-largest city in Central America. It is also the biggest city of Nicaragua. Almost a quarter of the population lives in the capital city. </p>
      <p>The population of Nicaragua is approximately 6&nbsp;million. The population is <a href="/wiki/Multicultural" class="mw-redirect" title="Multicultural">multicultural</a>. The population includes <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">indigenous</a> native tribes from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miskito&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Miskito (not yet started)">Miskito Coast</a>, Europeans, Africans, and Asians. The main language is Spanish. Some native tribes on the eastern coast speak their native languages. Some of these languages are Miskito, Sumo, and Rama. Some people speak English Creole. The mixture of cultures has created diversity in art and literature. Some famous Nicaraguan writers are <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Dar%C3%ADo" title="Rubén Darío">Rubén Darío</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Cardenal" title="Ernesto Cardenal">Ernesto Cardenal</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gioconda_Belli&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gioconda Belli (not yet started)">Gioconda Belli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The biological diversity, warm tropical climate, and active volcanoes make Nicaragua an increasingly popular <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourist_destination&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tourist destination (not yet started)">tourist destination</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
