      <p>The <b>State of Palestine</b>, also known simply as <b>Palestine</b>, is a <i><a href="/wiki/De_jure" title="De jure">de jure</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a> in the Middle East with <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> as its capital.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It is recognized by 141 <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a> members. Since 2012 it has non-member observer status in the United Nations. This amounts to a <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i>, or implicit, recognition of statehood.<sup id="cite_ref-UNStatehoodBid2012accepted_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNStatehoodBid2012accepted-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Charbonneau_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Charbonneau-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lederer_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lederer-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The State of Palestine claims the <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">West Bank</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gaza_Strip" title="Gaza Strip">Gaza Strip</a> as its territory. Its independence was declared on 15 November 1988. </p>
