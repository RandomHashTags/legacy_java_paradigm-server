      <p><b>Botswana</b> (officially called the <b>Republic of Botswana</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Botswana is <a href="/wiki/Gaborone" title="Gaborone">Gaborone</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_settlements_in_Botswana" title="List of settlements in Botswana">List of settlements in Botswana</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The countries that border Botswana are <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>. It is also possible to travel from Botswana directly into <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> by crossing the <a href="/wiki/Zambezi_River" title="Zambezi River">Zambezi River</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a>. </p>
      <p>Botswana is <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a>. All of Botswana's <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> must be brought in over land or by airplane. </p>
      <p>The two most important <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geographical features</a> in Botswana are the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Okavango_Delta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Okavango Delta (not yet started)">Okavango Delta</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kalahari_Desert" title="Kalahari Desert">Kalahari Desert</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Botswana is divided into 10 districts: </p>

       <li><a href="/wiki/Kweneng_District" title="Kweneng District">Kweneng District</a></li>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>People from Botswana are called <i>Batswana</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of Botswana is about 2.2 million. <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This is only 2.7 persons per km², a small number for a country of this size. This is because most of the country is <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> and very dry. </p>
      <p>Botswana has two official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a>: Setswana and English. Setswana is the national language (also called <i><a href="/wiki/Tswana_language" title="Tswana language">Tswana</a></i>). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Governance">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Governance">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Botswana is a <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>. The people vote for their political <a href="/wiki/Leader" title="Leader">leaders</a>. Those leaders vote for the president. In 2008 <a href="/wiki/Ian_Khama" title="Ian Khama">Ian Khama</a> became the fourth <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> of Botswana. </p>
      <p>President Khama won elections in 2009 and 2014. The current President of Botswana is Dr. Eric K. Mokgweetsi who took over from Dr. Ian Khama Seretse Khama in 2018. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> of Botswana has been growing very fast since it became an <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent country</a> in 1966. The people of Botswana have had the world's fastest growth in <a href="/wiki/Standard_of_living" title="Standard of living">standard of living</a> since that time. </p>
      <p>Botswana's economy comes mostly from <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a>. This includes <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamonds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metals</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">minerals</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Salt" title="Salt">salt</a>). Botswana is the highest producer of <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamonds</a> by value in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1999, Botswana produced over 30 million <a href="/wiki/Carat_(mass)" title="Carat (mass)">carats</a> (6,000 kg) of diamonds from the three Debswana mines. That is about 25% of worldwide production. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> is also important to the country. Many people come to Botswana each year to see its <a href="/wiki/Wildlife" title="Wildlife">wildlife</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> of Botswana is called the <i>pula</i> (which means <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">rain</a>). It is made up of 100 <i>thebe</i> (which means <a href="/wiki/Shield" title="Shield">shield</a>). In 2017, one US dollar was about 10.32 pulas.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: In popular culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: In popular culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <i>No. 1 Ladies' Detective Agency</i> books by Alexander McCall Smith are set in Botswana. The HBO television series based on the books was filmed in Botswana. </p>
