      <p><b>Barbados</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the Atlantic Ocean. The island has an <a href="/wiki/Area" title="Area">area</a> of about 430&nbsp;km². Its capital and largest city is <a href="/wiki/Bridgetown" title="Bridgetown">Bridgetown</a>. The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a> of Barbados is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. Barbados is a popular <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourist</a> destination. In 2008, the island had a population of about 284,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> About 80,000 live in or around Bridgetown.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The island is in the western area of the North Atlantic and 100 kilometres (62&nbsp;mi) east of the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It is about 168 kilometres (104&nbsp;mi) east of the islands of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines" title="Saint Vincent and the Grenadines">Saint Vincent and the Grenadines</a>, and 400 kilometres (250&nbsp;mi) north-east of <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>. Barbados is outside of the main Atlantic <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_belt" title="Hurricane belt">hurricane belt</a>. </p>
      <p>The island's only airport is the <a href="/wiki/Grantley_Adams_International_Airport" title="Grantley Adams International Airport">Grantley Adams International Airport</a>. </p>
      <p>Barbados produces <a href="/wiki/Rum" title="Rum">rum</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The earliest inhabitants of Barbados were <a href="/wiki/Native_American" title="Native American">Native Americans</a>. Barbados was first visited by the Spanish around the late 1400s to early 1500s. It first was on a Spanish map in 1511.<sup id="cite_ref-Spanish_Main_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spanish_Main-9">[9]</a></sup> The native Arawaks may have fled or been enslaved.<sup id="cite_ref-Spanish_Main_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spanish_Main-9">[9]</a></sup> The Portuguese visited in 1536. They brought wild hogs for a good supply of meat whenever the island was visited. The first English ship, the <i>Olive Blossom</i>, came in 1624. They said the island belonged to the British king <a href="/wiki/James_VI_and_I" class="mw-redirect" title="James VI and I">James I</a>. Two years later in 1627 the first permanent settlers arrived from England.<sup id="cite_ref-Commonwealth_History_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Commonwealth_History-10">[10]</a></sup> Slaves from <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> were sent to Barbados to work on sugar plantations. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government and politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Government and politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Barbados has been an independent country since 30 November 1966. <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_of_Barbados" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen of Barbados">Queen of Barbados</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The Queen is represented locally by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Governors-General_of_Barbados" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Governors-General of Barbados">Governor-General</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Barbados" title="Prime Minister of Barbados">Prime Minister</a> is the local <a href="/wiki/Head_of_the_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Head of the government">head of the government</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Barbados Defence Force has roughly 600 members. The Barbados Defence Force (BDF) is the name given to the combined armed forces of Barbados. The BDF was established August 15, 1979, and has responsibility for the territorial defence and internal security of the island. The headquarters for the Barbados Defence Force are in the St. Ann's Fort area at The Garrison, Saint Michael. There are three main components of the BDF: Force Headquarters — provided administrative and logistical support for the entire force The Barbados Regiment — this is the main land force component, and encompasses both regular and reserve units. Barbados Coast Guard — this is the maritime element, with responsibility for patrolling Barbados' territorial waters as well as drug interdiction and humanitarian and life-saving exercises. It too encompasses both regular and reserve units. Barbados Cadet Corp — Military youth organisation. Includes Infantry and Sea Cadets. This organisation was started in 1904. The first females joined the cadet corps in 1970s to 1980s. The Cadet Corps has a pledge and also a song. It started with 3 normal units but today has expanded to 22. These units are grouped into Zones. There are also a band, sea cadet and medical units along with a shooting programme. In 1981 a Cessna 402C entered service as part of the air wing. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_settlements_in_Barbados" title="List of settlements in Barbados">List of settlements in Barbados</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Barbados is the easternmost island in the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>. It is flat in comparison to its island neighbours to the west. The highest point of the nation is <a href="/wiki/Mount_Hillaby" title="Mount Hillaby">Mount Hillaby</a>. </p>
      <p>The country usually has two seasons. The "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wet season (not yet started)">wet season</a>" is from June to November. The "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Dry_season&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dry season (not yet started)">dry season</a>" is from December to May. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The population of Barbados is about 300,000 people, nearly 90% of that is predominantly of <a href="/wiki/African" class="mw-redirect" title="African">African</a> and Mixed descent, 6% are <a href="/wiki/Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian">Asian</a> and 4% Whites. There is also an <a href="/wiki/East_India" title="East India">East Indian</a> minority on the island and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> and other ethnic groups. Gypsies were expelled to Barbados during the colonial era. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Most Barbadians are Christians (95%). The most common Christian church is <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglicans</a> (40%). Other Christian churces in Barbados are the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>, Pentecostals (Evangelicals) <a href="/wiki/Jehovah%27s_Witness" class="mw-redirect" title="Jehovah's Witness">Jehovah's Witnesses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seventh-Day_Adventist" class="mw-redirect" title="Seventh-Day Adventist">Seventh-Day Adventist</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spiritual_Baptist&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spiritual Baptist (not yet started)">Spiritual Baptists</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> was the official state religion. It was ended by the Parliament of Barbados after independence.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Other religions include <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hindus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Muslims</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Baha%27i_Faith" class="mw-redirect" title="Baha'i Faith">Baha'i Faith</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Jews">Jews</a> and Wiccans. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The influence of the <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> on Barbados is more noticeable than on other islands in the West Indies. A good example of this is the island's national sport: <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>. Barbados has had several great cricketers, including <a href="/wiki/Garfield_Sobers" title="Garfield Sobers">Sir Garfield Sobers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Worrell" title="Frank Worrell">Sir Frank Worrell</a>. </p>
      <p>Citizens are officially called Barbadians. </p>
      <p>In the music business, <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a> (born Robyn Fenty) is one of Barbados' best-known <a href="/wiki/Grammy_awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy awards">Grammy winning</a> artists. </p>
      <p>In Barbados, the official language is English. But they also speak an English-African Creole language known as <a href="/wiki/Barbadian_English" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbadian English">Bajan</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a> is very popular on the island. Barbadians play on the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team" title="West Indies cricket team">West Indies cricket team</a>. The country hosted the final of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 Cricket World Cup</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Obadele_Thompson" title="Obadele Thompson">Obadele Thompson</a> is a world class <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sprint (running)">sprinter</a> from Barbados. <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Brathwaite" title="Ryan Brathwaite">Ryan Brathwaite</a>, a hurdler, reached the 2008 Olympic semi-finals in Beijing. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Polo" title="Polo">Polo</a> is very popular with the rich on the island. </p>
      <p>In golf, the <a href="/wiki/Barbados_Open" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbados Open">Barbados Open</a> is an annual stop on the <a href="/wiki/European_Seniors_Tour" title="European Seniors Tour">European Seniors Tour</a>. </p>
