      <p><b>Oman</b>, officially the <b>Sultanate of Oman</b>, is a country in the <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="Southwest Asia">southwestern part</a> of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, on the southeastern coast of the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>. It borders the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> to the northwest, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> to the west, and <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a> to the southwest. The coast is formed by the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> to the south and east, and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">Gulf of Oman</a> to the northeast. The capital is <a href="/wiki/Muscat" title="Muscat">Muscat</a>. </p>
      <p>The country is a <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">monarchy</a>, ruled by a <a href="/wiki/Sultan_of_Oman" title="Sultan of Oman">Sultan</a>, and the people are called Omanis. Right now, the sultan is <a href="/wiki/Haitham_bin_Tariq_Al_Said" class="mw-redirect" title="Haitham bin Tariq Al Said">Haitham bin Tariq Al Said</a>. For a long time, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> have supported the sultans of Oman. In the 1960s and 1970s, some Omanis started a <a href="/wiki/Revolution" title="Revolution">revolution</a> to overthrow the sultan and end <a href="/wiki/Oppression" title="Oppression">oppression</a>, but the sultan defeated them with the help of Great Britain.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oman&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Since 28 October 2011, Oman is divided into <b>Eleven Governorates</b> (<b>Muhafazah</b>):<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Ad Dakhiliyah Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Ad Dhahirah North Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Al Batinah North Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Al Batinah South Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Al Buraimi Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Al Wusta Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Ash Sharqiyah North Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Ash Sharqiyah South Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Dhofar Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Muscat (Masqat) Governorate</b></li> 
       <li><b>Musandam Governorate</b></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Below the governorates, Oman is divided into <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Oman" title="Provinces of Oman">provinces</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oman&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Oman has a hot climate and very little rainfall. Annual rainfall in Muscat averages 100&nbsp;mm (3.9&nbsp;in), falling mostly in January. <b>Dhofar</b> is subject to the southwest <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a>, and rainfall up to 640&nbsp;mm (25.2&nbsp;in) has been recorded in the rainy season from late June to October. While the mountain areas receive more rainfall, some parts of the coast, particularly near the island of <b>Masirah</b>, sometimes receive no rain at all within the course of a year. The climate generally is very hot, with temperatures reaching around 50&nbsp;°C (122.0&nbsp;°F) (peak) in the hot season, from May to September. </p>
