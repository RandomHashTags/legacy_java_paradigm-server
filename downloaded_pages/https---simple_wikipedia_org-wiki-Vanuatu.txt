      <p><b>Vanuatu</b> is a country in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a> of Vanuatu are <a href="/wiki/Bislama" title="Bislama">Bislama</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. Vanuatu has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of about 202,609 people (2004). The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Vanuatu is <a href="/wiki/Port_Vila" title="Port Vila">Port Vila</a>. The country was ruled as a <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> by <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> until it got its <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">freedom</a> in 1980. During the colonial time, it was named the "New Hebrides" or "Nouvelles Hebrides". </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanuatu&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanuatu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Vanuatu is an island <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>. It has about 82 small islands. The islands were made from <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">Volcanos</a>. 65 of the islands are inhabited. There is about 800 miles (1,300&nbsp;km) north to south distance between the outermost islands. Two of the islands (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthew_and_Hunter_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Matthew and Hunter Islands (not yet started)">Matthew and Hunter</a>) are also claimed by <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Fourteen of Vanuatu's islands have surface areas of more than 100 square kilometres (39&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). </p>
      <p>The highest point in Vanuatu is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Tabwemasana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Tabwemasana (not yet started)">Mount Tabwemasana</a>, at 1,879 metres (6,165&nbsp;ft), on the island of Espiritu Santo. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanuatu&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Flora and fauna">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanuatu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Flora and fauna">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Even though it has tropical forests, Vanuatu has a small number of plant and animal <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>. There are no large mammals. The 19 species of native reptiles include the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ramphotyphlops_braminus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ramphotyphlops braminus (not yet started)">flowerpot snake</a>, found only on Efate. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fiji_Banded_Iguana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fiji Banded Iguana (not yet started)">Fiji Banded Iguana</a> (<i>Brachylophus fasciatus</i>) was introduced as a <a href="/wiki/Feral" title="Feral">feral</a> animal in the 1960s.<sup id="cite_ref-Sprackland_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sprackland-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lp_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lp-8">[8]</a></sup> There are 11 species of bats and 61 species of land and water birds. </p>
      <p>The region is rich in sea life. There are more than 4,000 species of marine <a href="/wiki/Molluscs" class="mw-redirect" title="Molluscs">molluscs</a>. Coneshell and stonefish carry poison that will kill humans. The giant East African land snail arrived only in the 1970s but already has spread from the Port-Vila region to Luganville. </p>
      <p>There are three or possibly four adult <a href="/wiki/Saltwater_crocodile" title="Saltwater crocodile">saltwater crocodiles</a> living in Vanuatu's <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangroves</a> and no current breeding population.<sup id="cite_ref-lp_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lp-8">[8]</a></sup> It is said the crocodiles reach the northern part of the islands after <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclones</a>. This is because of the island chain's closeness to the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a>, where crocodiles are very common.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanuatu&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cuisine">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanuatu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Cuisine">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The national dish of Vanuatu is the lap lap.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
