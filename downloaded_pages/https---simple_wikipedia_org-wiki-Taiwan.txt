      <p><b>Taiwan</b>, officially the <b>Republic of China</b> (<b>ROC</b>; Chinese: 中華民國; pinyin: Zhōnghuá Mínguó), is a region and country in East Asia. It is the nationalist government of China since its settlement in 1949. It is called the <b><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> (ROC)</b> (also called <i>Taiwan</i>) which is a special region comprising the island of Taiwan and nearby islands (<a href="/wiki/Pescadores" class="mw-redirect" title="Pescadores">Pescadores</a> islands and parts of <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>). The ROC government led by Chinese Nationalist (<a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> abbreviated as KMT) moved to Taiwan after the Communist army took over the capital of Beijing. Currently, the ROC government governs <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>, the capital of Taiwan. Taiwan is southeast of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>'s mainland, south of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and north of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. </p>
      <p>Taiwan has also been called <b>Formosa</b>, a Portuguese name which means "beautiful" in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>. </p>
      <p>The largest cities in Taiwan are the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>, and the port city of <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung" class="mw-redirect" title="Kaohsiung">Kaohsiung</a>. </p>
      <p>Most people living in Taiwan (sometimes called Taiwanese) are <a href="/wiki/Han" class="mw-redirect" title="Han">Han</a>. Taiwan has three large Han groups. They speak different <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">dialects</a> of Chinese and their ancestors came from different places: the Southern Fujianese (from China's <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian Province</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Hakka_people" title="Hakka people">Hakka</a> (from China), and Mainlanders (from <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">Mainland China</a> after 1948). </p>
      <p>There are also <a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwanese_Aborigines&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Taiwanese Aborigines (not yet started)">Taiwanese Aborigines</a> who have lived in Taiwan before the Han came to live there. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li>In 1517, A discovery ship of <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> saw this island and names it "Ilha Formosa", or "Beautiful Island" in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <ul>
       <li>17th century: From 1624 until 1661 <a href="/wiki/Dutch_(people)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch (people)">Dutch</a> colonize the southern part of Formosa and established a colonial administration in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort Zeelandia">Fort Zeelandia</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> colonize the north and established a colonial administration in Fuerte Santo Domingo or <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Santo_Domingo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fort Santo Domingo (not yet started)">Fort Santo Domingo</a>. The Dutch eventually defeated the <a href="/wiki/Spaniard" class="mw-redirect" title="Spaniard">Spaniards</a> and took full control of Formosa. A Chinese general named <a href="/wiki/Koxinga" title="Koxinga">Koxinga</a>, defeated the Dutch at the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Fort_Zeelandia" title="Siege of Fort Zeelandia">Siege of Fort Zeelandia</a>.</li> 
       <li>1860: Taiwan becomes a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_port&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Treaty port (not yet started)">treaty port</a> following the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Tientsin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Treaty of Tientsin (not yet started)">Treaty of Tientsin</a>, opening the island to contact with the world.</li> 
       <li>1874: <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> invaded southern Taiwan, seemingly to "punish" the aborigines there for the murder of ship-wrecked Okinawan fishermen in 1871, but actually to establish a colony. Japanese forces withdraw later in the year after the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meiji&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Meiji (not yet started)">Meiji</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Qing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Qing (not yet started)">Qing</a> empires nearly went to war.</li> 
       <li>1884-1885: Taiwan is blockaded by French navy during the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sino-Franco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sino-Franco (not yet started)">Sino-Franco</a> War.</li> 
       <li>1895: Qing China lost the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a> and gave Taiwan to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> permanently.</li> 
       <li>1945: Japan lost in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> to U.S. and gave up Taiwan permanently.</li> 
       <li>1946: U.S. accepts millions refugees and soldiers from China to Taiwan and U.S. President installs <a href="/wiki/The_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="The Republic of China">The Republic of China</a> to govern Taiwan and to fight China and <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communism</a>.</li> 
       <li>1947: Taiwanese widely protest <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">governmental</a> <a href="/wiki/Corruption" title="Corruption">corruption</a> under the Nationalists. <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek" title="Chiang Kai-shek">Chiang Kai-shek</a> sends in the army to restore order, killing tens of thousands. Some Taiwanese began the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_independence_movement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Taiwan independence movement (not yet started)">Taiwan independence movement</a>.</li> 
       <li>1949: 
        <ul>
         <li><a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> (KMT) party and the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> (CCP).</li> 
         <li>The Nationalists (KMT) lose the war, and escape to the island of Taiwan. They set up <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> as the temporary capital of Republic of China (ROC).</li> 
         <li>The Communist Party of China (CCP) establishes Beijing as the capital of The <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> (PRC).</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>1951: Japan signs the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_San_Francisco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Treaty of San Francisco (not yet started)">Treaty of San Francisco</a> (1951) with U.S.</li> 
       <li>1979: The KMT government jails many <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> activists who opposed it (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaohsiung_Incident&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kaohsiung Incident (not yet started)">Kaohsiung Incident</a>).</li> 
       <li>1986: The <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Progressive_Party" title="Democratic Progressive Party">Democratic Progressive Party</a> (DPP) is formed; it is the first party to form in the ROC other than the KMT. It remains illegal for the first year, but the KMT government does not try to ban it.</li> 
       <li>1987: The KMT government lifts the <a href="/wiki/Martial_law" title="Martial law">martial law</a> after almost 40 years.</li> 
       <li>1988: <a href="/wiki/Lee_Teng-hui" title="Lee Teng-hui">Lee Teng-hui</a> (KMT) became <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="President of the Republic of China">president</a> after <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Ching-kuo" title="Chiang Ching-kuo">Chiang Ching-kuo</a> is dead.</li> 
       <li>1995: Lee Teng-hui (KMT) became the first president elected by people.</li> 
       <li>2000: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Shui-bian&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Li Shui-bian (not yet started)">Li Shui-bian</a> (DPP) became president.</li> 
       <li>2000: <a href="/wiki/Chen_Shui-bian" title="Chen Shui-bian">Chen Shui-bian</a> (DPP) became president.</li> 
       <li>2004: Chen Shui-bian is re-elected after a controversial assassination attempt, in which many KMT-supporters believed, was staged by Chen. However, after unwillingness to cooperate about his medical records the investigation was inconclusive.</li> 
       <li>2008: Former president Chen Sui-bian and his wife are arrested for corruption and money laundering.</li> 
       <li>2008: <a href="/wiki/Ma_Ying-jeou" title="Ma Ying-jeou">Ma Ying-jeou</a> (KMT) was elected as the president of the Republic of China and thus creating a change of political parties for the second time.</li> 
       <li>2009: Kaohsiung hosts the 2009 World Games.</li> 
       <li>2016: <a href="/wiki/Tsai_Ing-wen" title="Tsai Ing-wen">Tsai Ing-wen</a> (DPP) was elected as the first female president of Taiwan.</li> 
       <li>2019: <a href="/wiki/Same_sex_marriage" class="mw-redirect" title="Same sex marriage">same sex marriage</a> was legalized<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[11]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status_of_Taiwan">Status of Taiwan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Status of Taiwan">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Status of Taiwan">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are two <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> governments in the world: The <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> (PRC) and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> (ROC). Today, in reality, the PRC government controls mainland China, and the ROC government governs Taiwan. The ROC government governed most of China mainland from 1911 to 1949, before losing control of China mainland to the PRC. </p>
      <p>Today, people who live in Taiwan have different ideas. Although many Taiwanese think there is no freedom in China, there are still some Taiwanese who want to be united again with China. The majority of the people in Taiwan want to keep everything like it is now. </p>
      <p>Today most countries of the world recognize the People's Republic of China as China. Even though Taiwan is not recognized by the UN as a sovereign nation,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[12]</a></sup> most countries still have close economic and cultural relations with Taiwan. So, both sides are not making any big changes from the political <a href="/w/index.php?title=Status_quo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Status quo (not yet started)">status quo</a>. This policy was expressed in a 1992 <a href="/wiki/Consensus" title="Consensus">Consensus</a> among some leaders of both sides. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/March" title="March">March</a> 2004, China's government passed a law called the Anti-<a href="/wiki/Secession" title="Secession">Secession</a> Law. The law requires the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> to <a href="/wiki/Invade" class="mw-redirect" title="Invade">invade</a> Taiwan immediately if they declare <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[13]</a></sup> The law shows China's <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/concern" class="extiw" title="wikt:concern">concern</a> over a growing move towards independence by the government of Taiwan.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[14]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg/170px-Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg/255px-Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg/340px-Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1600"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Taiwan_NASA_Terra_MODIS_23791.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Taiwan is mostly mountainous in the east, with gently sloping plains in the west. The <a href="/wiki/Penghu_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Penghu Islands">Penghu Islands</a> are west of the main island.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The island of Taiwan is about 180 kilometers off the southeastern coast of China. It is across the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Strait" title="Taiwan Strait">Taiwan Strait</a>. It has an area of 35,883&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (13,855&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-taiwan-popstat_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taiwan-popstat-8">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a> is to the north, the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea" title="Philippine Sea">Philippine Sea</a> to the east, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Luzon_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Luzon Strait (not yet started)">Luzon Strait</a> directly to the south and the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> to the southwest.<sup id="cite_ref-yb:geography_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yb:geography-18">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Taiwan's highest point is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yu_Shan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yu Shan (not yet started)">Yu Shan</a> (Jade Mountain). It is 3,952 meters high (12,966&nbsp;ft). There are five other peaks over 3,500 meters. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Penghu_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Penghu Islands">Penghu Islands</a> are 50&nbsp;km (31.1&nbsp;mi) west of the main island. They have an area of 126.9&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (49.0&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). More distant islands controlled by the Republic of China are the <a href="/wiki/Kinmen" title="Kinmen">Kinmen</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wuchiu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wuchiu (not yet started)">Wuchiu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matsu_Islands" title="Matsu Islands">Matsu Islands</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>. They have a total area of 180.5&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (69.7&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pratas_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pratas Islands (not yet started)">Pratas Islands</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiping_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Taiping Island (not yet started)">Taiping Island</a> in the South China Sea have a total area of 2.9&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (1.1&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). They have no permanent inhabitants.<sup id="cite_ref-taiwan-popstat_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taiwan-popstat-8">[6]</a></sup> </p>
