      <p>The <b>Czech Republic</b> (<a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>: <i lang="cs">Česká republika</i>, <small>pronounced&nbsp;</small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:IPA_for_Czech_and_Slovak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wikipedia:IPA for Czech and Slovak (not yet started)">[ˈtʃɛskaː ˈrɛpuˌblɪka]</a></span><small>&nbsp; <span class="nowrap">(<a href="/wiki/Media:Cs-Ceska_Republika.oga" title="Media:Cs-Ceska Republika.oga"><img alt="Speaker Icon.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" decoding="async" width="13" height="13" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="500"></a> <a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e9/Cs-Ceska_Republika.oga" class="internal" title="Cs-Ceska Republika.oga">listen</a>)</span></small>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>. As of 2 May 2016 the official short name of the country is <b>Czechia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>: <i lang="cs">Česko</i>). The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and the biggest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> is the Czech <a href="/wiki/Crown" title="Crown">Crown</a> (<i>koruna česká</i> - CZK). €1 is about 25 CZK. The <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> of Czechia is <a href="/wiki/Milo%C5%A1_Zeman" title="Miloš Zeman">Miloš Zeman</a>. Czechia's <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> is about 10.5 million. </p>
      <p>The local <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech language</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Slavic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavic language">Slavic language</a> related to languages like <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>. </p>
      <p>Czechia <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">has no sea</a>; its neighbour countries are <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Czech_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Czech_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Its history dates from the 9th century AD, for a long time it was one of the most powerful countries in Central Europe. Later on it was the biggest, most populated and richest country of the <a href="/wiki/First_Reich" class="mw-redirect" title="First Reich">First Reich</a>, where many Emperors started their career. Under the conditions of the Treaty of Vienna 1515 parts of Czechia, then in the Kingdoms of <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, came under <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg</a> rule after the death of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagiellonian_dynasty&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jagiellonian dynasty (not yet started)">Louis the Jagiellon</a> in 1526. They stayed a part of the Habsburg dynasty rule until 1918. </p>
      <p>The area of the today's Czechia was a part of <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> (current area of Czechia and <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovak republic</a>) from 1918 to 1992. Czechoslovakia became <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a> in 1918 from <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Austro-Hungarian Empire">Austro-Hungarian Empire</a>. The first Czechoslovakian president was <a href="/wiki/Tomas_Garrigue_Masaryk" class="mw-redirect" title="Tomas Garrigue Masaryk">Tomáš Garrigue Masaryk</a>. In February 1948 the <a href="/wiki/Communist" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist">Communist</a> party took over the country and for the next 41 years Czechoslovakia was a <a href="/wiki/Socialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist">Socialist</a> state with a rule of one (Communist) party. In 1968 there was a reformation movement (<a href="/wiki/Prague_Spring" title="Prague Spring">Prague Spring</a>) within the Communist party, but reforms were stopped by the invasion of <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Pact" title="Warsaw Pact">Warsaw pact</a> armies. In November 1989 Czechoslovakia returned to democracy through the peaceful "<a href="/wiki/Velvet_Revolution" title="Velvet Revolution">Velvet Revolution</a>".&nbsp; Czechoslovakia peacefully split into two countries (Czechia and Slovakia) in 1993. Czechia has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> since May 1 2004, and a member of <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> since March 12, 1999. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Czech_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Czech_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The highest point in the country is <a href="/wiki/Sn%C4%9B%C5%BEka" title="Sněžka">Sněžka</a> at 1,602&nbsp;m (5,256&nbsp;ft). </p>
      <p>There are four <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national parks</a> in Czechia. The oldest is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Krkono%C5%A1e_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Krkonoše National Park (not yet started)">Krkonoše National Park</a>. The others are <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0umava_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Šumava National Park (not yet started)">Šumava National Park</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Podyj%C3%AD_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Podyjí National Park (not yet started)">Podyjí National Park</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bohemian_Switzerland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bohemian Switzerland (not yet started)">Bohemian Switzerland</a>. </p>

      <p>Czechia has one of the least religious populations in the world. According to the 2011 census, 34.2% of the population stated they had no religion, 10.3% were <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>, 0.8% were <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> (0.5% <a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelical_Church_of_Czech_Brethren&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Evangelical Church of Czech Brethren (not yet started)">Czech Brethren</a> and 0.4% <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hussite_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hussite Church (not yet started)">Hussite</a>), and 9.4% followed other forms of religion both denominational or not (of which 863 people answered they are <a href="/wiki/Neopaganism" class="mw-redirect" title="Neopaganism">Pagan</a>). 45.2% of the population did not answer the question about religion. </p>
