      <p>The <b>Cook Islands</b> are a group of <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a> in the southern <a href="/wiki/Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific">Pacific</a>. They form an independent state, but have strong ties with <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. The 15 small islands have a total land surface of 240 square kilometers. About 18.000 people live on the islands, most of them from <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>. The largest island, Rarotonga, also holds the territory's capital, <a href="/wiki/Avarua" title="Avarua">Avarua</a>. </p>
      <p>The nation also has a distinctive <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesian</a> language known as Cook Island Maori, which is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Maori_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Maori language">Maori language</a> of New Zealand and to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tahitian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tahitian language (not yet started)">Tahitian language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
