      <p><b>Jamaica</b> is an island country in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>; it is part of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a>. The island, 10,990 square kilometres (4,240&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) in area, lies about 140 kilometres (87&nbsp;mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, and 191 kilometres (119&nbsp;mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>. Its capital city is <a href="/wiki/Kingston" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingston">Kingston</a>; other towns include Montego Bay, St. Ann's Bay and Spanish Town. The island is divided into three counties – Cornwall, Middlesex and Surrey – which are subdivided into 14 parishes: Kingston, St. Andrew, St. Catherine, Clarendon, Manchester, St. Elizabeth, Westmoreland, Hanover, St. James, Trelawny, St. Ann, St. Mary, Portland and St. Thomas. </p>
      <p>Jamaica is the third-largest island country in the Greater Antilles. Its <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Taíno">Taíno</a> name is <i>Xaymaca</i>, meaning "Land of Springs".<sup id="cite_ref-taino_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taino-8">[8]</a></sup> Jamaica is part of the West Indies. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Jamaica">History of Jamaica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History of Jamaica">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History of Jamaica">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Taíno">Taino</a> indigenous people, originating in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, settled on the island between 4000 and 1000&nbsp;BC.<sup id="cite_ref-primearticles1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primearticles1-9">[9]</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> arrived in 1494, there were more than 200 villages ruled by <i>caciques</i> (chiefs of villages). The south coast of Jamaica was the most populated, especially around the area now known as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Harbour&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Old Harbour (not yet started)">Old Harbour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-primearticles1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primearticles1-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Christopher Columbus, during his second voyage to the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, claimed Jamaica for Spain after landing there on 5 May 1494 and his probable landing point was Dry Harbour, now called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery_Bay,_Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Discovery Bay, Jamaica (not yet started)">Discovery Bay</a>. There is some debate as to whether he landed in St. Ann's Bay or in Discovery Bay. <a href="/wiki/Saint_Ann_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Saint Ann Parish, Jamaica">St. Ann's Bay</a> was named "Saint Gloria" by Columbus, as the first sighting of the land.<sup id="cite_ref-JNHT_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JNHT-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1503, during his fourth voyage, Columbus had to spend one year on the northern coast of the island, and he named the island <i>Isla de Santiago</i> (by <a href="/wiki/James_(son_of_Zebedee)" title="James (son of Zebedee)">Sant Iago Apostol</a>, in Spanish <i>Santiago</i>). </p>
      <p>In 1509, the new Governor of the Hispaniola, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Columbus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Diego Columbus (not yet started)">Diego Columbus</a>, sent <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Esquivel" title="Juan de Esquivel">Juan de Esquivel</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">Conquistador</a> born in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, with 70 men to Jamaica to complete the conquest of that island. They first lived in the St. Ann's Bay area and soon Esquivel founded a town, <i>Sevilla La Nueva</i> (in English, "The New Seville") on the north coast, one mile to the west of St. Ann's Bay.<sup id="cite_ref-jis2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jis2-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Sevilla was abandoned around 1524 because it was deemed unhealthy.<sup id="cite_ref-JNHT_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JNHT-10">[10]</a></sup> The capital was moved to <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Town" title="Spanish Town">Spanish Town</a>, then called <i>Saint Jago de la Vega</i>, in the south around 1534.<sup id="cite_ref-Spanish_town_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spanish_town-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_rule">British rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: British rule">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: British rule">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In 1654 <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a> decided to break the Spanish control of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" class="mw-redirect" title="West Indies">West Indies</a> and he sent a <a href="/wiki/Naval_fleet" title="Naval fleet">fleet</a> in an <a href="/wiki/Expedition" title="Expedition">expedition</a>) led by <a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Penn_(Admiral)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="William Penn (Admiral) (not yet started)">William Penn</a> and General <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Venables&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Robert Venables (not yet started)">Robert Venables</a>. The fleet arrived to the <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Santo Domingo</a> island on 13 April 1655 but the British lost in two battles on 17 and 25 April and they decided to move to Jamaica.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On 10 May 1655, Penn and Venables led a successful attack on Jamaica. The Spanish surrendered to the English, freed their slaves and then fled to Cuba. It was this set of freed slaves and their descendants living in the Jamaican mountains who became known as the <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jis2_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jis2-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence">Independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Independence">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Independence">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>After a long period of direct British colonial rule, Jamaica gained a degree of local political control in the late 1930s, and held its first election under full universal adult suffrage in 1944. Jamaica joined nine other U.K. territories in the West Indies Federation in 1958 but withdrew after Jamaican voters rejected membership in 1961. Jamaica got its independence in 1962, remaining a member of the Commonwealth.<sup id="cite_ref-eoearth_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eoearth-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Jamaica is a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_system" title="Parliamentary system">parliamentary democracy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>, with Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a> serving as the monarch.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> However, as Elizabeth II is shared as <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> of fifteen other countries (the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realm</a>) in addition to the UK<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and resides mostly in the United Kingdom, she is thus often represented as Queen of Jamaica in Jamaica and abroad by the <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor-General</a> of Jamaica.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The governor-general is nominated by the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> of Jamaica and appointed by the monarch. All the members of the Cabinet are appointed by the governor-general on the advice of the prime minister. The monarch and the governor-general serve largely <a href="/wiki/Ceremony" title="Ceremony">ceremonial</a> roles. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">Parliament</a> of Jamaica is <a href="/wiki/Bicameral" title="Bicameral">bicameral</a>. This means that it consists of two Houses, the <a href="/wiki/Senate" title="Senate">Senate</a>, also called the <a href="/wiki/Upper_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Upper House">Upper House</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives" title="House of Representatives">House of Representatives</a>, also known as the <a href="/wiki/Lower_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Lower House">Lower House</a>. The members of the House (known as <a href="/wiki/Members_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Members of Parliament">Members of Parliament</a> or <i>MPs</i>) are <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">elected</a> by the people of Jamaica. The leader of the <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> with most members in the House of Representatives is appointed by the governor-general to be the prime minister. <a href="/wiki/Senator" class="mw-redirect" title="Senator">Senators</a> are nominated jointly by the prime minister and the parliamentary leader of the opposition and are then appointed by the governor-general.<sup id="cite_ref-jis_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jis-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Tarch 2016. Prime Minister Holness acts as <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> of Jamaica. </p>
      <p>Jamaica has traditionally had a system of two <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">parties</a>, with power often alternating between the <a href="/w/index.php?title=People%27s_National_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="People's National Party (not yet started)">People's National Party</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica_Labour_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jamaica Labour Party (not yet started)">Jamaica Labour Party</a> (JLP). The party with current administrative and legislative power is Jamaica Labour Party, with a two-thirds Parliamentary majority as of 2016. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The people of Jamaica is 62.5% <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> (10.8% <a href="/wiki/Seventh-day_Adventist_Church" title="Seventh-day Adventist Church">Seventh-day Adventist Church</a>, 9.5% <a href="/wiki/Pentecostalism" title="Pentecostalism">Pentecostal</a>, 8.3 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Other_Church_of_God&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Other Church of God (not yet started)">Other Church of God</a>, 7.2% <a href="/wiki/Baptist" title="Baptist">Baptist</a>, 6.3% <a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Testament_Church_of_God&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="New Testament Church of God (not yet started)">New Testament Church of God</a>, 4.8% <a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_of_God_in_Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Church of God in Jamaica (not yet started)">Church of God in Jamaica</a>, 4.3% <a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_of_God_of_Prophecy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Church of God of Prophecy (not yet started)">Church of God of Prophecy</a>, 3.6% <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican Church">Anglicans</a>, 7.7% other Christian), 2.6% <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Catholics</a>, 14.2% other or unspecified, 20.9% none.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-22">[22]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Jamaica is between <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitudes</a> 17° 42"N and 18° 31"N and <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitudes</a> 78° 22"W and 76° 11", that is between the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">Tropic of Cancer</a>. It has an area of 10,990 square kilometres (4,240&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-cia_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-22">[22]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Jamaica is the third-largest island country in the Greater Antilles, after Cuba and the Hispaniola (Haiti and the Dominican Republic); it is larger than <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The island is 235 kilometres (146&nbsp;mi) from <a href="/wiki/East" title="East">east</a> to <a href="/wiki/West" title="West">west</a>; the width, from north to south, varies between 35 kilometres (22&nbsp;mi) to 82 kilometres (51&nbsp;mi). The country is composed mainly of the mainland, but near the coast there are a few isolated small islands.<sup id="cite_ref-USGEO_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGEO-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <b>Blue Mountains</b> are the longest <a href="/wiki/Mountain_range" title="Mountain range">mountain range</a> in Jamaica. They include the island's highest point, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Mountain_Peak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Blue Mountain Peak (not yet started)">Blue Mountain Peak</a>, at 2,256 metres (7,402&nbsp;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-USGEO_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGEO-23">[23]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivers">Rivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Rivers">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Rivers">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <b>Rio Minho</b> is the longest river in Jamaica at 92.8 kilometres (57.7&nbsp;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-JNHT2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JNHT2-24">[24]</a></sup> It rises close to the island's geographic centre, flows generally south-southwest and reaches the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlisle_Bay_(Jamaica)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carlisle Bay (Jamaica) (not yet started)">Carlisle Bay</a> in the central south coast, to the west of the island's southernmost point, <a href="/wiki/Portland_Point" title="Portland Point">Portland Point</a>. </p>
      <p>The <b>Black River</b> is one of the longest rivers in Jamaica. At a length of 53.4&nbsp;km (33.2&nbsp;mi), it was believed to be the longest until it was discovered that the Rio Minho was longer. It was originally called Rio Caobana.<sup id="cite_ref-JNHT2_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JNHT2-24">[24]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Famous_people">Famous people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Famous people">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Famous people">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The island is known for the <a href="/wiki/Rastafarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Rastafarian">Rastafarian</a> movement, as well as <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Bob_Marley" title="Bob Marley">Bob Marley</a>. The popular sprinter <a href="/wiki/Usain_Bolt" title="Usain Bolt">Usain Bolt</a> is also an asset from the country. Usain Bolt won gold in the 100 and 200 races in Rio 2016. England goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hart" title="Joe Hart">Joe Hart</a> is also born in Jamaica. </p>
