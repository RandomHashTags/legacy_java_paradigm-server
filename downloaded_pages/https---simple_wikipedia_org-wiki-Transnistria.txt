      <p><b>Transnistria</b>, <b>Transdniestria</b>, or <b>Pridnestrovie</b>, officially the <b>Pridnestrovian Moldavian Republic</b> (<b>PMR</b>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Приднестровская Молдавская Республика</span>, <small>romanized:&nbsp;</small><i lang="ru-Latn" title="Russian-language romanization">Pridnestrovskaya Moldavskaya Respublika</i>), is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_states_with_limited_recognition" title="List of states with limited recognition">partially-recognized state</a> in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a>. </p>
      <p>The country is recognized by three other partially or unrecognized states, <a href="/wiki/Abkhazia" title="Abkhazia">Abkhazia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Nagorno-Karabakh Republic">Nargorno-Karabakh Republic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Ossetia" title="South Ossetia">South Ossetia</a>. The country declared <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> from <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a> on 2 September 1990. With the help of the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> army, it defeated the Moldovan army in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=War_of_Transnistria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="War of Transnistria (not yet started)">War of Transnistria</a>. </p>
      <p>There has been peace since 1992, but the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> calls Transnistria a "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Frozen_conflict&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Frozen conflict (not yet started)">frozen conflict</a>" region. </p>
      <p>There is argument about whether it is really a country or not. The region continues to boast independence, and acts independently over its territory with the help of peacekeeping forces from foreign countries. </p>
      <p>In a vote in 17 September 2006, 97% voted to be free from Moldova. This vote has not been accepted by Moldova, which calls the territory the "Territory of the Left Bank of the <a href="/wiki/Dniester" title="Dniester">Dniester</a>". </p>
      <p>Transnistria is the only independent state to still use the <a href="/wiki/Hammer_and_sickle" title="Hammer and sickle">hammer and sickle</a> on its flag.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
