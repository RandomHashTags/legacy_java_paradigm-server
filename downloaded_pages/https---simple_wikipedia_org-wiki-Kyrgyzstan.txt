      <p><b>Kyrgyzstan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Kyrgyz_language" title="Kyrgyz language">Kyrgyz</a>: Кыргызстан), formally the <b>Kyrgyz Republic</b>, and sometimes known as <b>Kirghizia</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. The country is <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> (has no <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a>) and mountainous. It has borders with <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Bishkek" title="Bishkek">Bishkek</a>. It was a <a href="/wiki/Socialist_republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist republic">socialist republic</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, but became independent in 1991. The country was peaceful in the 1990s. Its <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a>, <a href="/wiki/Askar_Akayev" title="Askar Akayev">Askar Akayev</a> showed an <a href="/wiki/Autocracy" title="Autocracy">autocratic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Totalitarianism" title="Totalitarianism">authoritarian</a> character. </p>
      <p>In 2005, there was an unexpected <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyrgyz_revolution_of_2005&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kyrgyz revolution of 2005 (not yet started)">revolution</a> after the elections of <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> (the legislature) in March. President Akayev resigned on April 4 of that year. Opposition leaders formed a coalition (a group from more than one party), and a new <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> was formed, led by President <a href="/wiki/Kurmanbek_Bakiyev" title="Kurmanbek Bakiyev">Kurmanbek Bakiyev</a> and Prime Minister <a href="/w/index.php?title=Feliks_Kulov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Feliks Kulov (not yet started)">Feliks Kulov</a>. </p>
      <p>At the moment, different political groups are fighting for power in the republic. Three of the 75 elected members of Parliament have been <a href="/wiki/Murder" title="Murder">murdered</a>. Kyrgyzstan’s main religion is Islam. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyrgyzstan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyrgyzstan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Kyrgyzstan is a landlocked country in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. It is farther from the ocean than any other country in the world. It borders <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. Mountains cover over 80% of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The land area of Kyrgyzstan is just a little bigger than the state of <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Issyk-Kul_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Issyk-Kul Lake (not yet started)">Issyk-Kul Lake</a> is the largest lake in Kyrgyzstan. It is the largest mountain lake in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and the second largest mountain lake in the world after <a href="/wiki/Lake_Titicaca" title="Lake Titicaca">Titicaca</a>. Peak Jengish Chokusu, at 7,439 m (24,406 ft), is the highest point. The highest peaks are in the Kakshaal-Too range. </p>
      <p>Bishkek, the capital city, is in the north. It is largest city in Kyrgyzstan. The second largest is the ancient town of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Osh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Osh (not yet started)">Osh</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyrgyzstan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyrgyzstan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Kyrgyzstan was the second poorest country in the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> next to Tajikistan. Now it is the second poorest country in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">Agriculture</a> is an important part of the economy in Kyrgyzstan. Much farming is still being done by hand and by horse. Main crops include <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">wheat</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sugar_beets&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sugar beets (not yet started)">sugar beets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potatoes" class="mw-redirect" title="Potatoes">potatoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vegetables" class="mw-redirect" title="Vegetables">vegetables</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a>. </p>
      <p>Kyrgyzstan is rich in mineral resources. It has small <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> reserves. Among its mineral reserves are solid amount of <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uranium" title="Uranium">uranium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antimony" title="Antimony">antimony</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metals</a> but not <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a>. <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">Metallurgy</a> is an important industry. </p>
      <p>Imports include petroleum and natural gas, ferrous metals, <a href="/wiki/Chemicals" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemicals">chemicals</a>, most <a href="/wiki/Machine" title="Machine">machinery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a> products, <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a> and construction materials. Its trade partners are <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. </p>
