      <p><b>North Macedonia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian</a>: Северна Македонија - Severna Makedonija) officially the <b>Republic of North Macedonia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian</a>: <i>Република Северна Македонија</i> - <i>Republika Severna Makedonija</i> (<a href="/wiki/Albanian_language" title="Albanian language">Albanian</a>: Republika e Maqedonisë së Veriut <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>) is a country located on the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkan peninsula</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Southeastern Europe">Southeastern Europe</a>. North Macedonia borders <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> to the east. The country's <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Macedonian_denar" title="Macedonian denar">Macedonian denar</a> (MKD). </p>
      <p>The capital and largest city is <a href="/wiki/Skopje" title="Skopje">Skopje</a>, with more than 500,000 residents. It has many smaller cities. Some important ones are <a href="/wiki/Bitola" title="Bitola">Bitola</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Prilep&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Prilep (not yet started)">Prilep</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tetovo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tetovo (not yet started)">Tetovo</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kumanovo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kumanovo (not yet started)">Kumanovo</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohrid&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ohrid (not yet started)">Ohrid</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Veles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Veles (not yet started)">Veles</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stip&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stip (not yet started)">Stip</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Strumica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Strumica (not yet started)">Strumica</a>. </p>
      <p>North Macedonia is often called <i>a land of lakes and mountains</i>. There are more than 50 large lakes and sixteen mountains higher than 2000 meters above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>. </p>
      <p>North Macedonia is a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Organization" title="World Trade Organization">World Trade Organization</a> (WTO). It joined <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> on 27 March 2020. Since December 2005, it is a candidate for joining the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The language spoken by the majority of the population is <a href="/wiki/Macedonian" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Macedonian">Macedonian</a>. Albanian is also spoken by the Albanian minority (25%) living in the country. North Macedonia has two official languages, Macedonian and Albanian (since 2019). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>What is now North Macedonia used to be a state called Socialist Republic of Macedonia in the southeastern part of the country of <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. When that country broke up in 1991, North Macedonia became independent. </p>
      <p>In past centuries the territory which today is the Republic of North Macedonia was ruled by many different states and empires. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earliest_Residents">Earliest Residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Earliest Residents">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Earliest Residents">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>People have been living in North Macedonia for over 1000 years. The first people that lived in Macedonia were the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> people. They lived in Macedonia from 7000 to 3500 BCE. From 1000 to 1 BCE, Dacians, Thracians, Illyrians, Celts, and Greeks inhabited Northern Macedonia. <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="Alexander_the_Great.27s_Empire"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Alexander_the_Great's_Empire">Alexander the Great's Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Alexander the Great's Empire">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Alexander the Great's Empire">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Until the Romans invaded Macedonia, Macedonia was just hundreds of small, independent, <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-states</a>. One example of a city state is <a href="/wiki/Illyria" title="Illyria">Illyria</a>. They sometimes merged together, but not often. One city-state that grew over time was the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(ancient_kingdom)" title="Macedonia (ancient kingdom)">Macedon.</a> The kingdom of Macedon is best known for <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great.</a> He invaded and controlled the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> (excluding <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a>),<a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a> state of India.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, when Alexander died in 323 BCE, at the age of 33, he lost his vast empire. The empire was divided into 5 countries, Lysimachia (Macedon), Cassander (Northern Greece), the Antigonid Empire (Turkey, Syria, Lebanon, Israel, Southern Greece), the Ptolemaic Empire (Egypt), and the Seleucid Empire (Iraq, Iran, Afghanistan).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Rule">Roman Rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Roman Rule">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Roman Rule">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Towards the end of the 3rd century BCE, the Romans invaded the <a href="/wiki/Balkan_Peninsula" class="mw-redirect" title="Balkan Peninsula">Balkan peninsula</a>. Illyria was taken over in 9 CE. The North and East of Macedonia were taken over by the Roman Empire in the year 29 CE. They became the Roman province of Moesia. Starting in the 3rd century CE, the borders of Macedonia were being attacked by the Goths, Huns, Bulgars, Avars, and others. In 395 AD, the Roman Empire split in two. They were the Western Roman Empire and the Eastern Roman Empire (<a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Rule"><a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> Rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Byzantine Rule">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Byzantine Rule">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Although, Macedonia was part of the Byzantine Empire, there was little Byzantine influence. In the mid-6th century, <a href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">Slavic</a> tribes started to settle in Macedonia. From the 7th century to the 13th century, Byzantine Macedonia was governed by local princes and kings, allied with the Byzantine Empire. In the 9th century, the Byzantine Empire brought <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> to Macedonia. The people who brought Christianity to Macedonia were saints Cyril and Methodius. Their goal was to bring Christianity and the <a href="/wiki/Cyrillic_alphabet" title="Cyrillic alphabet">Cyrillic alphabet</a> to Slavs in Europe. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ottoman_Rule"><a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> Rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Ottoman Rule">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Ottoman Rule">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Ottoman Empire was originally a small city-state in Turkey. The city-state grew, and it invaded <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Adrianople</a> in 1354. From there, it expanded and took over Turkey. <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, the capital of the Byzantine Empire, did not get invaded by the Ottomans until 1453. After the Battle of the Maritsa River, the Ottomans conquered southern Serbia and Macedonia. Macedonia was under Ottoman Rule until 1913.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yugoslav_Rule"><a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslav</a> Rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Yugoslav Rule">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Yugoslav Rule">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>After the Ottoman Empire dissolved, Macedonia became a part of the newly formed country Yugoslavia. From 1914 to 1941, Yugoslavia was a monarchy. During WW2, the Axis Powers took over Yugoslavia. Macedonia was taken over by Bulgaria. The Axis powers left Yugoslavia after WW2.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> After WW2, Yugoslavia became a communist state. <a href="/wiki/Josip_Broz_Tito" title="Josip Broz Tito">Josip Broz Tito</a> was the leader of Yugoslavia from 1944 to 1980. In 8 September 1991, Macedonia became an independent state.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Present_Day">Present Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Present Day">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Present Day">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>During the <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_Wars" title="Yugoslav Wars">Yugoslav Wars</a>, Macedonia was mostly peaceful. However in 2001, fighting broke out between ethnic Albanians and Macedonians. The fighting ended with the Ohrid Agreement. </p>
      <p>Greece and the Republic of North Macedonia were arguing over the name Macedonia. The United Nations calls the Republic of North Macedonia, "<b>the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia</b>" <b>(FYROM)</b> (Macedonian: <i>Поранешна Југословенска Република Македонија - ПЈРМ</i> - <i>Poranešna Jugoslovenska Republika Makedonija -PJRM</i>). The north and northeast part of Greece has been called Macedonia for ages and officially since 1912, just like <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>, the southeast county of England, has been called Kent for a long time. 'FYROM' is also used by NATO and many other international organisations. But, many countries now call the country 'Republic of Macedonia'. The United Kingdom, for example, uses Republic of Macedonia in the diplomatic list.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On 17 June 2018, North Macedonia and Greece agreed to the Prespa agreement<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> which would see the country change its name to the <b>Republic of North Macedonia</b>. The government started completing the constitutional change needed to change the country's name, which was completed on 12 February 2019. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Macedonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Macedonia is a democratic country with a <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a>. </p>
