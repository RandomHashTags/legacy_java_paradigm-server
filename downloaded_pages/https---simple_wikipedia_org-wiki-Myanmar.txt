      <p><b>Myanmar</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. Its full name is the <b>Republic of the Union of Myanmar</b>. It is also sometimes called <b>Burma</b>. Myanmar is the largest country in Southeast Asia that is not an island. It is also part of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. </p>
      <p>It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">China</a> on the north, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> on the east, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> on the southeast, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> on the west, and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of India">India</a> on the northwest, with the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Sea" title="Andaman Sea">Andaman Sea</a> to the south, and the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> to the southwest. There are over 2,000 kilometres (1,200&nbsp;mi) of coastline. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myanmar&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myanmar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The country was ruled by a <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> <a href="/wiki/Junta" title="Junta">junta</a> led by <a href="/wiki/General_officer" title="General officer">General</a> <a href="/wiki/Ne_Win" title="Ne Win">Ne Win</a> from 1962 to 1988. Its political system today stays under the tight control of its military government. In 1991, Senior General <a href="/wiki/Than_Shwe" title="Than Shwe">Than Shwe</a> began ruling the country. In 2011 <a href="/wiki/Thein_Sein" title="Thein Sein">Thein Sein</a> was elected as a first president of the civilian government. In 2016 <a href="/wiki/Htin_Kyaw" title="Htin Kyaw">Htin Kyaw</a> became the second elected civilian leader.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Aung_San_Suu_Kyi" title="Aung San Suu Kyi">Aung San Suu Kyi</a>, who is prevented from becoming President by the <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> of Myanmar, will act as an advisor to Kyaw.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In March 2018, <a href="/wiki/Win_Myint_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Win Myint (politician)">Win Myint</a> became the country's tenth and current President. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_and_history_of_the_name">Origin and history of the name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myanmar&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Origin and history of the name">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myanmar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Origin and history of the name">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In 1989, the military junta officially changed the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> version of its name from <i>Burma</i> to <i>Myanmar</i>. It also made a new name in English for places in the country, such as its former <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a>, from <i>Rangoon</i> to <a href="/wiki/Yangon" title="Yangon">Yangon</a>. The official name of the country in the <a href="/wiki/Burmese_language" title="Burmese language">Burmese language</a>, <i>Myanmar</i> did not change, however. The renaming was controversial, seen by some as <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">linguistically</a> bad. Accepting the name change in the English-speaking world has been slow, with many people still using the name <i>Burma</i> to refer to the country. Major news organizations like the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> still call it Burma. Some question the military junta's authority to "officially" change the name in English in the first place. <a href="/wiki/Aung_San_Suu_Kyi" title="Aung San Suu Kyi">Aung San Suu Kyi</a>, however, calls the country Myanmar now. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myanmar&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myanmar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Myanmar had a strong kingdom in ancient times, but the nation was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> in the 1800s. It was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a> in the 1940s. Myanmar became independent in 1948 as the Union of Burma, and had a democratic government at first. However, in 1962, a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> brought the military into power, where it has been ever since. The founder of modern Myanmar, <a href="/wiki/Aung_San" title="Aung San">Aung San</a> was assassinated months before independence. His daughter <a href="/wiki/Aung_San_Suu_Kyi" title="Aung San Suu Kyi">Aung San Suu Kyi</a> was placed under house arrest many times for leading the democracy movement. </p>
      <p>In 1991, the military junta agreed to democratic elections, which were won by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_for_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National League for Democracy (not yet started)">National League for Democracy</a>, and should have made Aung San Suu Kyi the Prime Minister. However, the dictatorship ignored the results of the elections and continued ruling. In <a href="/wiki/November" title="November">November</a> 2005, the military <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> stated that the national capital would be moved from <a href="/wiki/Yangon" title="Yangon">Yangon</a> to a location near <a href="/wiki/Pyinmana" title="Pyinmana">Pyinmana</a>, which was renamed <a href="/wiki/Naypyidaw" title="Naypyidaw">Naypyidaw</a> in <a href="/wiki/March" title="March">March</a> 2006. </p>
      <p>Since independence in 1948 and the assassination of Aung San, Burma has had <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil wars</a> between its governments and minority <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_group" title="Ethnic group">ethnic groups</a> like the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kachin_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kachin people (not yet started)">Kachin</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Karen_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Karen people (not yet started)">Karen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shan_people" title="Shan people">Shan</a> and others. These conflicts are known as the <a href="/wiki/Internal_conflict_in_Burma" class="mw-redirect" title="Internal conflict in Burma">Internal conflict in Burma</a>. </p>

      <p>Today, there are 14 sections. 7 are called <a href="/wiki/State" title="State">states</a> and the other 7 are called <a href="/wiki/Division" class="mw-disambig" title="Division">divisions</a>. </p>
      <p>The divisions are split into <a href="/wiki/Township" class="mw-redirect" title="Township">townships</a>. The townships are divided into <a href="/wiki/Village" title="Village">villages</a> and wards. </p>
