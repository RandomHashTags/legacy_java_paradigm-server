      <p><b>Lithuania</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">northern</a> <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> on the eastern shore of the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a>. Lithuania is a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>, and several other organizations. </p>
      <p>About 3,000,000 people live in the country. The official language is <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a> which is spoken by more than 82% of the people. <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and largest city. </p>
      <p>The colors of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Lithuania" title="Flag of Lithuania">Lithuanian flag</a> are yellow (at the top), for the sun, green (in the middle), for the fields, and red (at the bottom), for the blood of Lithuanians fighting for its independence. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Lithuania began to turn into a country in the <a href="/wiki/7th_century" title="7th century">7th</a>–<a href="/wiki/9th_century" title="9th century">9th</a> <a href="/wiki/Centuries" class="mw-redirect" title="Centuries">centuries</a> from <a href="/wiki/Baltic_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltic countries">Baltic nations group</a>. The Balts, the <a href="/wiki/Ancestor" title="Ancestor">ancestors</a> of Lithuanians and <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvians</a>, came to area between <a href="/wiki/Nemunas" class="mw-redirect" title="Nemunas">Nemunas</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Daugava&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Daugava (not yet started)">Daugava</a> <a href="/wiki/Rivers" class="mw-redirect" title="Rivers">rivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a>, from a supposed original homeland of the <a href="/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language" title="Proto-Indo-European language">Proto-Indo-European languages</a>. Many <a href="/wiki/Scientist" title="Scientist">scientists</a> think they came there sometime in the <a href="/wiki/3rd_millennium_BC" title="3rd millennium BC">3rd millennium BC</a>. </p>
      <p>The traditional date of the beginning of the country is 1236 when the Lithuanians won the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_%C5%A0iauliai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Battle of Šiauliai (not yet started)">Battle of Šiauliai</a> (Battle of Sun). </p>
      <p>Lithuania (at that time - The <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duchy of Lithuania">Grand Duchy of Lithuania</a>) made a Treaty with <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> in 1569. The country was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> in 1795, ending the <a href="/wiki/Polish-Lithuanian_Commonwealth" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth</a>. It got back its <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> on February 16, 1918. It was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> on June 16, 1940, by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> from 1941-1944 and again the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> from 1944-1990. Lithuania re-declared its independence on March 11, 1990. Currently Lithuania is an <a href="/wiki/Independent" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent">independent</a>, semi-<a href="/wiki/President" title="President">presidential</a>, <a href="/wiki/Democratic" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic">democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Lithuania has been a member of NATO and the European Union since 2004. </p>
      <p>Lithuania is a semi presidential republic, that restored its independence and democracy in 1990. Since then, very important reforms were made and Lithuania is now declared as a democratic state that grants the human rights. </p>
      <p>The Constitution that was adopted in 1992 declares that the leader of the country is the President, who must be elected to take office. The elections are held every 5 years. If the President breaks their oath, they can be forced to resign by the Parliament. The President also represents Lithuania abroad and is the commander-in-chief. </p>
      <p>The legislative power of Lithuania is called the Seimas, or Parliament. There are 141 members of Seimas who are elected for 4-year-terms. Seimas passes the laws that must be executed by the government, that is formed in Seimas and it must be accepted by the President. The Prime Minister is set and fired by the President. </p>
      <p>The justice is under the power of the courts. The supreme court in Lithuania is the Constitutional Court. </p>

      <p>The county <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">governor</a> rules the county. He or she must be appointed by the central government. Municipalities are governed by the Municipal Councils that are elected for 4 year terms. The head of a municipality is the <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a>. The elderates are governed by the elders. The elders are appointed by the municipal councils. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Lithuania is a country in northern Europe. Its neighbours are Poland in the southwest, Russia (Kaliningrad) in west, Latvia in north and Belarus in the east. Lithuania borders the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> and 99 kilometres of its <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a> belong to Lithuania. The highest hill is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Auk%C5%A1tojas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aukštojas (not yet started)">Aukštojas</a> (294 metres high), the largest lake is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dr%C5%ABk%C5%A1iai_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Drūkšiai Lake (not yet started)">Drūkšiai Lake</a>. 31% of the land is suitable for farms. </p>
      <p>Lithuania is divided into 5 cultural regions according to their past and traditions: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Aukštaitija</li> 
       <li>Dzūkija</li> 
       <li>Mažoji Lietuva (Lithuania Minor)</li> 
       <li>Suvalkija</li> 
       <li>Žemaitija (Samogitia)</li>
      </ul>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Lithuania has a fast growing economy. It grew up to 7% in the first quarter of 2008. </p>
      <p>GDP per capita, based on purchasing power parity is estimated to be $19,730 in 2008. The nominal GDP per capita is estimated to be $14,213 at the same year. According to these numbers, Lithuanian per capita GDP reaches only 61% of EU average. However, it is impressive that only in 8 years, since 2000 it grew up from 30% of EU average. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Emigration" title="Emigration">Emigration</a> still creates a problem. According to the official data, emigration in 2006 was 30% lower than previous year, with 3,483 people leaving in four months. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>About 80% of people in Lithuania are Lithuanians. There are large national minorities: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>The Poles, (6.3%), mainly live in Vilnius County, which was taken over by Poland in 1920.</li> 
       <li>The Russians, (5.1%), mainly live in Vilnius County and Utena County, as workers at the Ignalina <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuclear plant">nuclear plant</a>.</li> 
       <li>The Belorussians, (1.1%), most of them live in Vilnius County.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Lithuanian is spoken by 82% of the people and it is the only official language. Polish is used mostly in Vilnius County where Polish politicians are elected to represent the Polish minority. The documents and street names must be in Lithuanian. </p>
      <p>The biggest cities are <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a>, 542,287 people, <a href="/wiki/Kaunas" title="Kaunas">Kaunas</a>, 358,107 people, and <a href="/wiki/Klaip%C4%97da" title="Klaipėda">Klaipėda</a>, 185,899 people. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg/220px-Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg/330px-Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg/440px-Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Vilnius_University_in_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Vilnius University Great Yard in 2006
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The nursery schools and the kindergartens are the first-level education forms. However, they are not compulsory. The children start attending the primary school at age 7, where educational programs last for 4 years; then they must start attending secondary school (5th to 10th grades). After finishing 8th or 10th grade, the student can continue learning at the high school or choose courses at the vocational college. The students who finish the high school can join colleges and universities. Higher education is free for the students whose annual median of grades is 8 or higher. The others have to pay 500 Litass per semester at least. </p>
      <p>The higher education schools are universities and colleges. The main universities are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_university&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vilnius university (not yet started)">Vilnius University</a> (the oldest university in northeastern Europe, founded in 1579);</li> 
       <li>University of Vytautas the Great, in <a href="/wiki/Kaunas" title="Kaunas">Kaunas</a>;</li> 
       <li>The University of Technology of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gediminas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gediminas (not yet started)">Gediminas</a>, in Vilnius;</li> 
       <li>The Klaipėda University.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_and_Television">Radio and Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Radio and Television">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Radio and Television">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Lithuanians can choose from many <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> stations. The first radio station was started in 1926 in Kaunas. The first television station started in 1957. The main radio stations are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Public broadcaster: LRT Radijas (talk station), LRT Klasika (classical music), LRT Opus (alternative music)</li> 
       <li>M-1 (TOP 40)</li> 
       <li>Lietus (Lithuanian pop)</li> 
       <li>Radiocentras (TOP 40)</li> 
       <li>Power Hit Radio (dance)</li> 
       <li>ZIP FM (TOP 40)</li> 
       <li>M-1 Plius (adult contemporary)</li> 
       <li>Žinių Radijas (talk station)</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The most popular TV stations are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Public broadcaster: LRT Televizija (main programme), LRT Plius (culture, sports, movies)</li> 
       <li>TV3 (national)</li> 
       <li>LNK (national)</li> 
       <li>BTV (national)</li> 
       <li>Lrytas TV (national)</li> 
       <li>TV1 (women-oriented)</li> 
       <li>TV8 (women-oriented)</li> 
       <li>TV6 (men-oriented)</li> 
       <li>Info TV (news)</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Printed_Media_and_Internet">Printed Media and Internet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Printed Media and Internet">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lithuania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Printed Media and Internet">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The oldest legal <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspaper</a> in Lithuania is the Polish Kurier Wilenski. It was first published in the 18th century and is now only popular with the Polish community. The biggest selling newspapers are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Lietuvos Rytas (national)</li> 
       <li>Verslo žinios (business)</li> 
       <li>Kauno diena (regional)</li> 
       <li>Vakaro žinios (tabloid)</li> 
       <li>Vakarų ekspresas (regional)</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Internet news portals are very popular in Lithuania. They have the latest information and also let people make comments. The most popular Internet sites with news and information are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Delfi.lt</li> 
       <li>15min.lt</li> 
       <li>lrytas.lt</li> 
       <li>tv3.lt</li> 
       <li>lrt.lt</li>
      </ul>
