      <p><b>El Salvador</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">República de El Salvador</i>) is the smallest and most densely populated country in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. The country's capital and largest city is <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador" title="San Salvador">San Salvador</a>. Other important cities are: <a href="/wiki/Santa_Ana_Department" title="Santa Ana Department">Santa Ana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sonsonate_Department" title="Sonsonate Department">Sonsonate</a>. El Salvador borders the Pacific Ocean on the south, and the countries of <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> to the west and <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> to the north and east. </p>
      <p>In 2010 El Salvador ranked in the top 10 Latin American countries in terms of <a href="/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a>. It was in the top 3 in Central America (behind <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>). Also, tropical forests and overall forests have expanded by nearly 20% from the year 1992 to 2010, making it one of the few countries experiencing <a href="/w/index.php?title=Reforestation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Reforestation (not yet started)">reforestation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In El Salvador there are only two seasons. The dry season starts in mid-October and it lasts until mid May. During the dry season, it hardly ever rains. The wet season starts in mid-May and ends in mid-October. In this season it rains every day. The dry season is called summer and the rainy season is called winter. </p>
      <p>Most of the population is mestizo, a mixture of European and American people. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Salvador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Salvador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The civilization of El Salvador began around 1500 B.C., leaving an evidence that would be the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tazumal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tazumal (not yet started)">Tazumal</a> Ruins and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chalchuapa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chalchuapa (not yet started)">Chalchuapa</a> Ruins. The first habitants were Pocomanes, Lencas and Pipiles; they stayed in the middle and east zone of El Salvador until about the eleventh century. The Spaniard <a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9s_Ni%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Andrés Niño (not yet started)">Andrés Niño</a> led an expedition from Central America and arrived in Meanguera Island in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_of_Fonseca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gulf of Fonseca (not yet started)">Gulf of Fonseca</a> on May 31, 1522. That was the first Salvadoran territory visited by Spaniard men. </p>
      <p>In June of 1524, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_de_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pedro de Alvarado (not yet started)">Pedro de Alvarado</a> began a war of conquest against Cuzcatlán (Land of beautiful things) that was populated by native tribes of the country. During 17 days of bloody battles many natives and Spaniard men died, including the native <a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlacatl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Atlacatl (not yet started)">Atlacatl</a>. Pedro de Alvarado, deterred and injured, left the battle and set off to <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, leaving his brother, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzalo_de_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gonzalo de Alvarado (not yet started)">Gonzalo de Alvarado</a>, to continue the conquest of Cuzcatlan. After this, their cousin, Diego de Alvardo established the San Salvador Ville on April 1525 in a site named La Bermuda, near Suchitoto City. In 1546, <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Carlos I</a> from Spain named San Salvador a city. </p>
      <p>During the following years, the country grew under Spaniard control. In 1810 a feeling of freedom formed between the countries of Central America. On the morning of November 5th, 1811, the priest <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Mat%C3%ADas_Delgado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="José Matías Delgado (not yet started)">José Matías Delgado</a> rang the bells of La Merced Church in San Salvador, calling for an uprising. After many internal conflicts, the Declaration of Independence of Central América was signed in Guatemala on September 15th, 1821. That day is the Independence day all around Central America. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Salvador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Salvador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>El Salvador has a total area of 21,041&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (8,124&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). As the smallest country in continental America, El Salvador is sometimes called <i>Pulgarcito de America</i> (the "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Tom_Thumb&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tom Thumb (not yet started)">Tom Thumb</a> of the Americas"). The highest point in El Salvador is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_El_Pital&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cerro El Pital (not yet started)">Cerro El Pital</a>, at 2,730 metres (8,957&nbsp;ft), on the border with <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>. </p>
      <p>El Salvador has a long history of <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_earthquakes_in_El_Salvador&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="List of earthquakes in El Salvador (not yet started)">destructive earthquakes</a> and volcanic eruptions. The capital San Salvador was destroyed in 1756 and 1854, and it suffered heavy damage in the 1919, 1982, and 1986 tremors. El Salvador has over twenty volcanoes. Two of them, <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Miguel_(volcano)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Miguel (volcano) (not yet started)">San Miguel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Izalco_(volcano)" title="Izalco (volcano)">Izalco</a>, have been active in recent years. </p>
      <p>El Salvador has over 300 rivers. The most important is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lempa_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lempa River (not yet started)">Rio Lempa</a>. Important lakes are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Ilopango&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake Ilopango (not yet started)">Lake Ilopango</a> (70&nbsp;km²) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Coatepeque&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake Coatepeque (not yet started)">Lake Coatepeque</a> (26&nbsp;km²). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_Fauna">Flora and Fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Salvador&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Flora and Fauna">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Salvador&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Flora and Fauna">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are eight species of sea turtles in the world. Of these six nest on the coasts of Central America, and four make their home on the Salvadoran coast: the <a href="/wiki/Leatherback_turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Leatherback turtle">leatherback turtle</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hawksbill_turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Hawksbill turtle">hawksbill</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Galapagos_green_turtle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Galapagos green turtle (not yet started)">Galapagos green turtle</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Olive_ridley_sea_turtle" title="Olive ridley sea turtle">olive ridley sea turtle</a>. </p>
      <p>It is estimated that there are 500 species of birds, 1,000 species of butterflies, 400 species of orchids, 800 species of trees, and 800 species of marine fish in El Salvador. </p>
      <p>There are 4 <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national parks</a> in El Salvador: <a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Imposible_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="El Imposible National Park (not yet started)">El Imposible</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Los_Volcanes_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Los Volcanes National Park (not yet started)">Los Volcanes</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Montecristo_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Montecristo National Park (not yet started)">Montecristo</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Diego_y_San_Felipe_Las_Barras_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Diego y San Felipe Las Barras National Park (not yet started)">San Diego y San Felipe Las Barras</a>. </p>
