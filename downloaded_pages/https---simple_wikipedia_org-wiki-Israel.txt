      <p>The <b>State of Israel</b> is a country in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> on the eastern side of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>. Israel became an <a href="/wiki/Independent" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent">independent</a> country in 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[21]</a></sup> Israel is the only Jewish <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Jews">Jews</a> all over the world think of Israel as their <a href="/wiki/Spiritual" class="mw-redirect" title="Spiritual">spiritual</a> home. Israel's <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> was 8.1 million people in 2013 and 6.04 million are <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>. Almost all the other <a href="/wiki/Citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Citizen">citizens</a> of Israel are <a href="/wiki/Arab_Israelis" title="Arab Israelis">Arab</a> (1.6 million) and include <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Druze" title="Druze">Druze</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Samaritan" class="mw-redirect" title="Samaritan">Samaritans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[24]</a></sup> Israel's largest city is <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>. Israel's capital city is Jerusalem (limited recognition). Most countries keep <a href="/wiki/Embassy" title="Embassy">embassies</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>. </p>
      <p>Israel is a small country, but it has <a href="/wiki/Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Mountains">mountains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deserts" class="mw-redirect" title="Deserts">deserts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shore" title="Shore">shores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valleys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plain" title="Plain">plains</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> is hot and rainless in the summers with high humidity in the coastal plain and lower elevations, and cool and rainy in the winters, rarely going below freezing temperatures. </p>
      <p>Israel has few <a href="/wiki/Natural_resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural resources">natural resources</a> and <a href="/wiki/Import" title="Import">imports</a> more <a href="/wiki/Goods" class="mw-redirect" title="Goods">goods</a> than it <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exports</a>. It has a relatively high <a href="/wiki/Standard_of_living" title="Standard of living">standard of living</a> and <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a>. Almost all of its people <a href="/wiki/Literacy" title="Literacy">can read and write</a>. </p>
      <p>According to the Democratic Index, Israel is the only democratic republic in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>. According to Freedom House, both Israel and <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> are the only full democracies in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Middle_East" title="Greater Middle East">Greater Middle East</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Democratic_Index_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Democratic_Index-28">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[27]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a>'s <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a> goes back thousands of years, to <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">ancient</a> times. Two world <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>, began here. It is the place where the Jewish <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> and religion first grew. Jews and Christians call it the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Land" title="Holy Land">Holy Land</a>, because it is the place of many <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/event" class="extiw" title="wikt:event">events</a> <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/describe" class="extiw" title="wikt:describe">described</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>, and because some <a href="/wiki/Judaism#Mitzvot_(Commandments)" title="Judaism">commandments</a> of Jewish law can be accomplished only on its soil.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[28]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Premodern">Premodern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Premodern">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Premodern">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>From the time of the first Jewish patriarch <a href="/wiki/Abraham" title="Abraham">Abraham</a> four thousand years ago, the land now called Israel were populated by <a href="/wiki/Canaanite" title="Canaanite">Canaanites</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Semitic_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Semitic people">Semitic peoples</a>. Around 1400 <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">BCE</a>, another Semitic people, called the Hebrews, settled in <a href="/wiki/Canaan" title="Canaan">Canaan</a> under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Moses" title="Moses">Moses</a> and Joshua. They were named the “Children of Israel” or “<a href="/wiki/Israelite" class="mw-redirect" title="Israelite">Israelites</a>”: which were divided into 12 <a href="/wiki/Tribes_of_Israel" title="Tribes of Israel">tribes</a>. A few centuries later, the Hebrews made <a href="/wiki/Saul" title="Saul">Saul</a>, as their leader. The next king, <a href="/wiki/David" title="David">David</a>, began the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom" title="Kingdom">Kingdom</a> of Israel in about 1000 BCE and made the city of Jerusalem his capital. His son, <a href="/wiki/Solomon" title="Solomon">Solomon</a>, built the <a href="/wiki/Temple_in_Jerusalem" title="Temple in Jerusalem">first Temple</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Worship" title="Worship">worship</a> of their <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a>. Solomon died in about 928 BCE. His kingdom broke into two countries. The northern country kept the name Israel. The southern country, called <a href="/wiki/Judah" title="Judah">Judah</a>, kept Jerusalem as its capital. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Assyrian" class="mw-redirect" title="Assyrian">Assyrians</a> conquered the Kingdom of Israel in 732 BCE and the <a href="/wiki/Babylon" title="Babylon">Babylonians</a> conquered the Kingdom of Judah in 586 BCE and destroyed <a href="/wiki/Solomon" title="Solomon">Solomon</a>'s Temple. Many Jews returned from Babylonia and built a country again and rebuilt the Temple. First the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Empire" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Persian Empire">Persians</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greeks</a> and then the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a> ruled the Land of Israel. </p>
      <p>The Jews fought against the Romans but the Romans defeated them. In 70 <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">CE</a>, the Romans destroyed Jerusalem and the Second Jewish Temple there. Again, in 135 CE, the Romans defeated the Jews and killed or took many of them to other places. The number of Jews living in Israel became much smaller. Many were forced to live in other countries. This <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/spread" class="extiw" title="wikt:spread">spreading</a> of Jewish <a href="/wiki/Community" title="Community">communities</a> outside of Israel is called the <a href="/wiki/Diaspora" title="Diaspora">Diaspora</a>. </p>
      <p>Many of the Jews who <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/remain" class="extiw" title="wikt:remain">remained</a> moved to the <a href="/wiki/Galilee" title="Galilee">Galilee</a>. Jewish teachers wrote important Jewish books, called the <a href="/wiki/Mishnah" class="mw-redirect" title="Mishnah">Mishnah</a> and part of the <a href="/wiki/Talmud" title="Talmud">Talmud</a> there, in the 2nd to 4th centuries CE. </p>
      <p>The Romans began to call this region by the word that became <a href="/wiki/Palestine" class="mw-disambig" title="Palestine">Palestine</a> in English. The Roman and then the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> empires ruled until 635 CE, when Arabs conquered the region. Different Arab rulers, and for a while <a href="/wiki/Crusades" title="Crusades">Crusaders</a>, ruled the land. In 1516, the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> conquered the land and ruled the region until the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern">Modern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Modern">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Modern">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Since the Diaspora, there have been many attempts to make a new homeland for the Jewish people. In the 1880s, this wish for a Jewish nation in Israel became a movement called <a href="/wiki/Zionism" title="Zionism">Zionism</a>. Jews from all over the world began to come to the area and settled in desert zones, then governed by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Turkish</a> and later by the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Governments</a>. </p>
      <p>On 14 May 1948, <a href="/wiki/British_Mandate_of_Palestine" title="British Mandate of Palestine">British control over Palestine</a> ended. The Jewish inhabitants (under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/David_Ben-Gurion" title="David Ben-Gurion">David Ben-Gurion</a>) declared independence for the new Jewish state. Immediately following Israel's <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_independence" title="Declaration of independence">declaration of independence</a>, the armies of several nearby countries – including <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> – attacked the new country.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[29]</a></sup> Since the 1980s, Israel's main military opponents have been Islamist groups, such as <a href="/wiki/Hezbollah" title="Hezbollah">Hezbollah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[30]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>The countries of <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> are to the north of Israel; <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> is on the east; and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> is to the southwest. Israel also controls the <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">West Bank</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_River" title="Jordan River">Jordan River</a>. </p>
      <p>Israel has a long <a href="/wiki/Coastline" class="mw-redirect" title="Coastline">coastline</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>. In the south, the town of <a href="/wiki/Eilat" title="Eilat">Eilat</a> is on the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Aqaba" title="Gulf of Aqaba">Gulf of Aqaba</a>, which is part of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>. </p>
      <p>The Galilee is a fertile and mountainous region in the north. There is a flat plain called the Coastal Plain to the west, near the coast of the Mediterranean Sea. The Negev Desert is a barren area of flat plains, mountains, and <a href="/wiki/Crater" title="Crater">craters</a> in the south. There is a range of mountains in the center that runs from the north to south. </p>
      <p>On the eastern side, there is a low area called a depression. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hula_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hula Valley (not yet started)">Hula Valley</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Galilee" title="Sea of Galilee">Sea of Galilee</a> are in this low area in the north. The <a href="/wiki/Jordan_River" title="Jordan River">Jordan River</a> runs from the Sea of Galilee to the <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a>. The land next to the Dead Sea is the lowest in the world. It is -426&nbsp;meters below <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ADE_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADE-34">[31]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a> is normally hot and dry in the summer and mild to cool in the winter. Rain falls mostly in the winter (between the months of November and April). There is more rain in the north than in the south, and hardly any rain in the desert. Snow falls in higher elevations. Israel built a very big <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> system to bring water from the north to the dry areas in the south so that <a href="/wiki/Crop" title="Crop">crops</a> can grow there also.<sup id="cite_ref-ADE_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADE-34">[31]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> is the biggest city in Israel. <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haifa" title="Haifa">Haifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beersheba" class="mw-redirect" title="Beersheba">Beersheba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rishon_LeZion" title="Rishon LeZion">Rishon LeZion</a> are also large cities. Israel says that its <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is Jerusalem. Most countries do not <a href="/wiki/Recognition#Politics" title="Recognition">recognize</a> that. They treat Tel Aviv as the capital.<sup id="cite_ref-ADE_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADE-34">[31]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_government">National government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: National government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: National government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Israel is a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliamentary democracy">parliamentary democracy</a>. All Israeli <a href="/wiki/Citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Citizen">citizens</a> who are 18 years or older may <a href="/wiki/Vote" class="mw-redirect" title="Vote">vote</a>. The Israeli <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> is called the Knesset. The Knesset has 120 <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/member" class="extiw" title="wikt:member">members</a>. Each member is <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">elected</a> for no more than four years at a time. The Knesset makes <a href="/wiki/Laws" class="mw-redirect" title="Laws">laws</a>, helps decide national <a href="/wiki/Policy" class="mw-disambig" title="Policy">policy</a>, and approves <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">budgets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">taxes</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/voter" class="extiw" title="wikt:voter">Voters</a> do not vote for individual <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/candidate" class="extiw" title="wikt:candidate">candidates</a> in Knesset <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">elections</a>. Instead, they vote for a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">party</a>. This party makes a list with all its candidates. The list may have only one candidate or as many as 120 candidates. In an election, the <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/percentage" class="extiw" title="wikt:percentage">percentage</a> of the vote that each list wins decides how many <a href="/wiki/Representative" class="mw-disambig" title="Representative">representatives</a>, or <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/seat" class="extiw" title="wikt:seat">seats</a>, the party gets in the Knesset. For example, if a party list gets 33 percent of the vote, it gets 40 Knesset seats. </p>
      <p>Israel has no <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/write" class="extiw" title="wikt:write">written</a> <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a>. Instead, the Knesset made "<a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/basic" class="extiw" title="wikt:basic">Basic</a> <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">Laws</a>". The Basic Laws say how the <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> must work and give <a href="/wiki/Civil_rights" title="Civil rights">civil rights</a> to the citizens. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Israel" title="Prime Minister of Israel">Prime Minister</a> is the head of Israel's government. He or she is usually the <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/leader" class="extiw" title="wikt:leader">leader</a> of the party that has the most seats in the Knesset. The prime minister must keep the support of a majority of Knesset members to stay in office. He or she <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/appoint" class="extiw" title="wikt:appoint">appoints</a> <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">ministers</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet" title="Cabinet">cabinet</a>. The Knesset approves appointments to the Cabinet. The ministers are <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/responsible" class="extiw" title="wikt:responsible">responsible</a> for subjects such as <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defense_(military)" title="Defense (military)">defense</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Social_welfare" class="mw-redirect" title="Social welfare">social welfare</a>. The prime minister is the head of the cabinet and decides the <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/topic" class="extiw" title="wikt:topic">topics</a> of cabinet meetings and makes the final decisions. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Netanyahu" title="Benjamin Netanyahu">Benjamin Netanyahu</a> has been the Prime Minister since March 2009. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">President</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>. The Knesset elects the president for seven years. Most of the president's duties are <a href="/wiki/Ceremony" title="Ceremony">ceremonial</a>: The president signs laws and <a href="/wiki/Treaty" title="Treaty">treaties</a> approved by the Knesset, appoints <a href="/wiki/Judge" title="Judge">judges</a>, and members of some public organizations. He or she also accepts the <a href="/wiki/Document" title="Document">documents</a> from <a href="/wiki/Ambassador" title="Ambassador">ambassadors</a> and foreign <a href="/wiki/Diplomat" class="mw-redirect" title="Diplomat">diplomats</a> bring when they are <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/appoint" class="extiw" title="wikt:appoint">appointed</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Reuven_Rivlin" title="Reuven Rivlin">Reuven Rivlin</a> has been the President since July 2014. </p>

      <p>Israel has many <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a>, with a large <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/variety" class="extiw" title="wikt:variety">variety</a> of <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/opinion" class="extiw" title="wikt:opinion">opinions</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2020_Israeli_legislative_election" title="2020 Israeli legislative election">elections of 2020</a>, 20 parties won seats in the <a href="/wiki/Knesset" title="Knesset">Knesset</a>. </p>
      <p>The parties belong to three main groups. The biggest groups are the <a href="/wiki/Zionism" title="Zionism">Zionist</a> parties. These include the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_liberalism" title="Conservative liberalism">conservative liberals</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/HaLikud" class="mw-redirect" title="HaLikud">HaLikud</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Social_democracy" title="Social democracy">social democrats</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/HaAvoda" title="HaAvoda">HaAvoda</a> (Labor Party); and the <a href="/wiki/Religious_Zionists" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious Zionists">religious Zionists</a>. There are also smaller religious <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox Jewish</a> parties, special-interest parties, and Israeli Arab parties. </p>
      <p>A single party usually does not win enough seats in the Knesset by itself to have a <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/majority" class="extiw" title="wikt:majority">majority</a>, so one of the bigger parties asks for support from the other parties, including the religious parties, to form a <a href="/wiki/Coalition" title="Coalition">coalition</a> government. This gives these parties a lot of power although they are small. </p>
      <p>The Likud supports <a href="/wiki/Free_market" title="Free market">free market</a> <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/policy" class="extiw" title="wikt:policy">policies</a> and <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/limit" class="extiw" title="wikt:limit">limited</a> government <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/involve" class="extiw" title="wikt:involve">involvement</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a>. Likud believes strongly in protecting Israel's <a href="/wiki/Security" title="Security">security</a>. It wants to give less away in the peace <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/process" class="extiw" title="wikt:process">process</a> for a <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/negotiate" class="extiw" title="wikt:negotiate">negotiated</a> agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Palestinian" class="mw-redirect" title="Palestinian">Palestinians</a> and the Arab states. </p>
      <p>The Labor Party supports government <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/control" class="extiw" title="wikt:control">control</a> of the economy, but also believes in a limited amount of <a href="/wiki/Free_market" title="Free market">free enterprise</a>. The party says it will give more away for an agreement with the Palestinians and the Arab states. </p>
      <p>Current <a href="/wiki/Knesset" title="Knesset">Knesset</a> is the 23rd Knesset, sworn in on March 16, 2020. Current government is the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Netanyahu_Government" title="Fifth Netanyahu Government">Fifth Netanyahu Government</a>, installed on May 17, 2020. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>At independence, Israel was a <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poor</a> country with little <a href="/wiki/Agricultural" class="mw-redirect" title="Agricultural">agricultural</a> or <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industrial</a> production. But Israel's <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> has grown tremendously since 1948. The nation now enjoys a relatively high standard of living, despite having few <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural resources</a> and a limited water supply. </p>
      <p>Many immigrants came to Israel in the years immediately after independence. Many of these immigrants were skilled laborers and <a href="/wiki/Professional" title="Professional">professionals</a> who greatly aided the nation's economic development. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_industries">Service industries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Service industries">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Service industries">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Many of Israel's <a href="/wiki/Service_sector" title="Service sector">service industry</a> workers are employed by the government or by businesses owned by the government. Government workers provide many of the <a href="/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a> that are needed by Israel's large immigrant <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a>, such as housing, <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, and job training. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Tourism">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Tourism">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> is one of the country's important sources of <a href="/wiki/Revenue" title="Revenue">income</a>. Tourists visit many <a href="/wiki/Archaeological_site" title="Archaeological site">archaeological</a>, historical, and religious sites; <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museums</a>; <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserves</a>; and beach resorts in Israel.<sup id="cite_ref-Tourism_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tourism-36">[33]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Tourists support many of Israel's service industries, especially <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurants</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotels</a>. Over 2.7 million foreign tourists visited Israel in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-2009Travelpulse_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009Travelpulse-37">[34]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufacturing">Manufacturing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Manufacturing">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Manufacturing">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Israeli <a href="/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">factories</a> produce such <a href="/wiki/Good_(economics)" title="Good (economics)">goods</a> as <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">chemical</a> products, <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronic equipment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">fertilizer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastics</a>, processed foods, scientific and optical instruments, <a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">textiles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Clothing" title="Clothing">clothing</a>. The cutting of <a href="/wiki/Import" title="Import">imported</a> <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamonds</a> is a major industry. Government-owned plants <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacture</a> equipment used by Israel's large armed forces. Israel is the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exporter</a> of <a href="/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicle" title="Unmanned aerial vehicle">drones</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[35]</a></sup> Tel Aviv and Haifa are Israel's major manufacturing centers. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Agriculture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Agriculture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>Agriculture formerly employed a much larger percentage of Israel's workforce. But much of the work once performed by people is now performed by machines. Important agricultural products include <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruits</a>; <a href="/wiki/Egg_(food)" title="Egg (food)">eggs</a>; <a href="/wiki/Grain" title="Grain">grain</a>; <a href="/wiki/Poultry" title="Poultry">poultry</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Vegetable" title="Vegetable">vegetables</a>. </p>
      <p>The government develops, helps finance, and controls agricultural activity, including <a href="/wiki/Fishing_industry" title="Fishing industry">fishing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a>. Israel produces most of the food it needs to feed its people, except for grain. Agricultural exports provide enough income to pay for any necessary food imports. Most Israeli farmers use modern agricultural methods. Water drawn from the Sea of Galilee irrigates large amounts of land in Israel. </p>
      <p>Most Israeli <a href="/wiki/Farm" title="Farm">farms</a> are organized as moshavim or <a href="/wiki/Kibbutz" title="Kibbutz">kibbutzim</a>. Israel also has some private farms. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining">Mining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Mining">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Mining">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Dead Sea, the world's saltiest body of water, is Israel's leading source of <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">minerals</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bromine" title="Bromine">Bromine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magnesium" title="Magnesium">magnesium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potash" title="Potash">potash</a> and <a href="/wiki/Table_salt" title="Table salt">table salt</a> are extracted from the sea. Potash, used mainly in fertilizers, is the most important mineral. </p>
      <p>In the Negev Desert, there are <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mines</a> for <a href="/wiki/Phosphate" title="Phosphate">phosphates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gypsum" title="Gypsum">gypsum</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy">Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Energy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Change section: Energy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:247px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Elifaz-Solar-Field.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Elifaz-Solar-Field.jpg/245px-Elifaz-Solar-Field.jpg" decoding="async" width="245" height="264" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Elifaz-Solar-Field.jpg/368px-Elifaz-Solar-Field.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Elifaz-Solar-Field.jpg/490px-Elifaz-Solar-Field.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="861"></a>

        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>Israel has few <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> sources. It has no <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> deposits or <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydroelectric power</a> resources, and only small amounts of <a href="/wiki/Crude_oil" class="mw-redirect" title="Crude oil">crude oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>. As a result, Israel depends on imported crude oil for <a href="/wiki/Gasoline" title="Gasoline">gasoline</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diesel_fuel" title="Diesel fuel">diesel</a> for transportation, and coal producing electricity for its energy needs. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">Solar energy</a> – energy from the sun – is used widely to heat water for houses. Israel is developing other ways to use solar energy to power houses and factories. </p>
      <p>In 2008, Israel began investing in building <a href="/wiki/Electric_vehicle" title="Electric vehicle">electric cars</a> and the stations to charge them. There may also be large natural gas fields in the Mediterranean Sea that Israel could develop. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_trade">International trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: International trade">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Change section: International trade">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>For 2006, Israeli exports grew by 11% to just over $29 billion; the hi-tech sector accounted for $14 billion, a 20% increase from the previous year. </p>
      <p>Because it has few natural resources, Israel imports more goods than it exports. The country's main imports include chemicals, <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> equipment, grain, <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a>, military equipment, <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_product" title="Petroleum product">petroleum products</a>, rough diamonds, and textiles. </p>
      <p>Israel's main exports are chemical products, citrus fruits, clothing, electronic equipment, fertilizers, polished diamonds, military equipment, and processed foods. The nation's main trading partners include the <a href="/wiki/Benelux" title="Benelux">Benelux countries</a> (<a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>); <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>; <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>; <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>; the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Transportation">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Change section: Transportation">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>Israel has a well-developed <a href="/wiki/Transportation" class="mw-redirect" title="Transportation">transportation</a> system. Most middle-class Israeli families either own a car or have one provided by their employer. Paved roads reach almost all parts of the country. <a href="/wiki/Public_transportation" class="mw-redirect" title="Public transportation">Public transportation</a> both in and between cities is provided primarily by <a href="/wiki/Bus" title="Bus">bus</a>. </p>
      <p>Ben-Gurion Airport is Israel's main international <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a>. It is near Tel Aviv. There are smaller airports are located at Atarot, near Jerusalem, and at Eilat. El Al, Israel's international <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airline</a>, flies regularly to the United States, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and parts of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. Israel has three major deepwater <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">ports</a>: Haifa, Ashdod, and Eilat. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communications">Communications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Communications">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Change section: Communications">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Israel's <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a> system is one of the best in the Middle East. Israel has about 30 daily <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspapers</a>, about half of which are in <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>. The rest are in <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">Yiddish</a>, or one of several foreign <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a>. The Israel Broadcasting Authority, a public corporation set up by the government, runs the <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> and nonmilitary <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio station">radio stations</a>. </p>
