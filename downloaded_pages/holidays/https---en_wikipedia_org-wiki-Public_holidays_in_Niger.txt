      <p>The government and people of <b><a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a></b> observe twelve official <b>public holidays</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> These include international commemorations, the commemoration of important dates in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Niger" title="History of Niger">history of Niger</a>, and religious holidays. Both <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> holidays are observed as official public holidays. While the former colonial power, France, instituted Christian observances, the vast majority of <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Niger" title="Islam in Niger">Nigeriens are Muslim</a>. </p>
      <p>Because <a href="/wiki/Muslim_holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim holidays">Muslim holiday</a> are dependent on the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_calendar" title="Islamic calendar">Islamic calendar</a>, and—like Christian <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>—move from year to year. Some Muslim holidays are also dependent upon astronomical sightings (e.g.: the observance of <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/moonrise" class="extiw" title="wikt:moonrise">moonrise</a> for <a href="/wiki/Ramadan" title="Ramadan">Ramadan</a>). National commemorations and secular international holidays are fixed on the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, the standard civil calendar used in Niger as most of the world. </p>
      <p>Nigeriens celebrate a number of other holidays, festivals, and commemorations which are not public holidays. Some are yearly civic commemorations, some religious, ethnic, or regional festivals which may be widely celebrated only by specific groups or in specific areas. Other yearly events, such as cultural festivals, markets, or sporting events may be much anticipated events without being legal holidays. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_holidays">Official holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Niger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Official holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Below are the twelve official public holidays recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Niger" title="Government of Niger">Government of Niger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Businesses, schools, and public services are closed on these days. They are often dates of public festivals, political speeches, and large gatherings. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_festivals">Other festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Niger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Nigeriens celebrate a number of holidays and festivals. Many are regional, recognized partially or only locally by government, or are traditions of specific ethnic groups. They include:<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/Cure_sal%C3%A9e" class="mw-redirect" title="Cure salée">Cure salée</a>&nbsp;: annual gathering of <a href="/wiki/Tuareg_people" title="Tuareg people">Tuareg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fula_people" title="Fula people">Fulani</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> nomadic clans at <a href="/wiki/Ingall" class="mw-redirect" title="Ingall">Ingall</a> (August or September);</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A9rewol" title="Guérewol">Guérewol</a>&nbsp;: The "<a href="/wiki/Wodaabe" title="Wodaabe">Bororo</a>" Fulani matchmaking festival at the end of the rainy season (August or September). Famous for the traditional makeup and dancing of young Fulani men eager to woo a spouse, Guérewol take place both in conjunction with the <a href="/wiki/Cure_sal%C3%A9e" class="mw-redirect" title="Cure salée">Cure salée</a>, as well as at other traditional gather spots of nomadic people in northern Niger;</li> 
       <li>"<i>National Day of Nigerien Women</i>" -- "Journée nationale de la femme nigérienne" (13 May). Commemorates a 1992 march by women in Niamey during the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Conference_(Niger)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Conference (Niger) (page does not exist)">National Conference</a> period, demanding greater involvement of women in national institutions. Made a "National Commemoration" 25 November 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>"Festival national de la jeunesse, des sports et de la culture": Government sponsored national youth sport and cultural competition (annual);</li> 
       <li>National championship of <a href="/wiki/Lutte_Traditionnelle" title="Lutte Traditionnelle">Lutte Traditionnelle</a> (traditional wrestling). Contestants progress from <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Niger" title="Regions of Niger">Regional</a> championships to team completion which awards a prestigious trophy—a ceremonial <a href="/wiki/Sabre" title="Sabre">sabre</a>—to one region;</li> 
       <li>"Foires agro-sylvo pastorales": annual Agropastoral governance and cultural forum, Niamey;</li> 
       <li>"Prix Dan Gourmou"&nbsp;: Annual music competition and festival, Niamey;</li> 
       <li>"FIMA", "Festival International de la Mode Africaine" (International Festival of African Fashion) every two years in Niamey;</li> 
       <li>"Rencontres Théâtrales du Niger": National theater festival&nbsp;;</li> 
       <li>"Festival international de conte"/ " Gatan - Gatan ": Festival of traditional story telling;</li> 
       <li>"Salon international de l'artisanat pour la femme" (SAFEM): Women Artisans festival, Niamey;</li> 
       <li>"<i>Festival de l'Aïr</i>" (27–29 December): Music and arts festival at <a href="/wiki/If%C3%A9rouane" class="mw-redirect" title="Iférouane">Iférouane</a>;</li> 
       <li>" Hotoungo " at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gangui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gangui (page does not exist)">Gangui</a>, a local traditional farmers gathering and festival;</li> 
       <li>" Bianou " festival at <a href="/wiki/Agadez" title="Agadez">Agadez</a>;</li> 
       <li>" Wassan Kara " festival at <a href="/wiki/Zinder" title="Zinder">Zinder</a>;</li> 
       <li>The " Guetna " at <a href="/wiki/Tassara" title="Tassara">Tassara</a>, Annual festival among the nomadic <a href="/wiki/Diffa_Arabs" title="Diffa Arabs">Diffa Arabs</a>.;</li> 
       <li>Annual Anza animist religious festival at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Massalata_-_Konni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Massalata - Konni (page does not exist)">Massalata - Konni</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Dogondutchi" class="mw-redirect" title="Dogondutchi">Dogondutchi</a>;</li> 
       <li>Traditional fishermen's festival at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Karay-Kopto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Karay-Kopto (page does not exist)">Karay-Kopto</a> on the River Niger;</li> 
       <li>" Gossi ", a Soninké animist festival at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Karma-Songha%C3%AF&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Karma-Songhaï (page does not exist)">Karma-Songhaï</a> on the River Niger;</li> 
       <li>"Fête de la girafe" (15 September), <a href="/wiki/Kour%C3%A9,_Niger" title="Kouré, Niger">Kouré</a>, near Niamey, celebrating the <a href="/wiki/West_African_Giraffe" class="mw-redirect" title="West African Giraffe">West African Giraffe</a> native to the area;</li> 
       <li>le " Sharow " Puel festival;</li> 
       <li>Traditional boxing tournaments ("Faka") each year after harvest in December or January, <a href="/wiki/Zinder_Region" title="Zinder Region">Zinder Region</a>;</li> 
       <li>" Mani Hori ": festival of traditional <a href="/wiki/Songhai_people" title="Songhai people">Songhai</a> women's crafts, each year after harvest in December or January.</li>
      </ul>
