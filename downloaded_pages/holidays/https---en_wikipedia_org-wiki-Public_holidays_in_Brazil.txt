   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Brazil</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub" class="noprint">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="contentSub2"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>

      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, public holidays may be legislated at the federal, statewide and municipal levels. Most holidays are observed nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Apart from the yearly official holidays (listed below),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Brazil" title="Constitution of Brazil">Constitution of Brazil</a> also establishes that <a href="/wiki/Election_Day_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Election Day (politics)">election days</a> are to be considered national holidays as well. <a href="/wiki/General_election" title="General election">General elections</a> are biennially held on the first Sunday of October in the first round, and on the last Sunday of October in the second round. Numerous religious and ethnic holidays are also celebrated in Brazil. </p>

      <ul>
       <li>Note: The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Brazil" title="Constitution of Brazil">Constitution</a> establishes that <a href="/wiki/Election_day" title="Election day">electoral days</a> are to be considered national holidays in Brazil. <a href="/wiki/General_election" title="General election">General elections</a> are held on the first Sunday of October, in the first round, and last Sunday of October, in the second round, of election years (every two years).</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Unofficial_holidays">Unofficial holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Unofficial holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Fat_Tuesday" class="mw-redirect" title="Fat Tuesday">Fat Tuesday</a></b>, the last day of <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a>. In some places, it's an official municipal holiday, but even where it isn't, many businesses close.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Ash_Wednesday" title="Ash Wednesday">Ash Wednesday</a></b> is a half-holiday: many businesses will stay closed until noon.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a></b>, the Friday preceding <a href="/wiki/Easter_Sunday" class="mw-redirect" title="Easter Sunday">Easter Sunday</a>, is a municipal holiday in many places.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dia_das_M%C3%A3es&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dia das Mães (page does not exist)">Dia das Mães</a></b>, the Brazilian observation of <a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother's Day">Mother's Day</a>, is celebrated every second Sunday in May.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a></b> is celebrated on the Thursday, 60 days after Easter Sunday.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Dia_dos_Namorados" title="Dia dos Namorados">Dia dos Namorados</a></b>, the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> equivalent of <a href="/wiki/St._Valentine%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Valentine's Day">St. Valentine's Day</a>, is observed on June 12. On this day, boyfriends and girlfriends, husbands and wives, exchange gifts, cards, or a flower bouquet. It is celebrated on the eve of <a href="/wiki/St._Anthony_of_Padua" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Anthony of Padua">St. Anthony of Padua</a>'s day, because, in Brazil, he is considered the patron of those who wants a loving companion.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Festa_Junina" title="Festa Junina">Festas Juninas</a></b>, the Catholic feasts of <a href="/wiki/St._Anthony_of_Padua" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Anthony of Padua">Santo Antônio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_John_the_Baptist" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint John the Baptist">São João</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">São Pedro</a> are celebrated June 13, 24, and 29 respectively in many Brazilian cities, especially in the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Region,_Brazil" title="Northeast Region, Brazil">Northeast region</a>.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dia_dos_Pais&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dia dos Pais (page does not exist)">Dia dos Pais</a></b>, the Brazilian observation of <a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father's Day">Father's Day</a>, is celebrated every second Sunday in August.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dia_das_Crian%C3%A7as&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dia das Crianças (page does not exist)">Dia das Crianças</a></b>, the Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Day" title="Children's Day">Children's Day</a>, is celebrated on October 12, coinciding with the feast of Our Lady of Aparecida.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_holidays">Regional holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Regional holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable plainlinks">
       This list is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Lists#Incomplete_lists" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Lists">incomplete</a>; you can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Brazil&amp;action=edit">adding missing items</a> with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">reliable sources</a>.
      </div> 
      <p>Federal law gives each state the right to create one state holiday, and each municipality to create up to four municipal holidays. Some of the more notable ones are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>January 12: Founding of Belém, City of <a href="/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m" title="Belém">Belém</a> only.</li> 
       <li>January 20: <a href="/wiki/Saint_Sebastian" title="Saint Sebastian">Saint Sebastian</a> (São Sebastião), City of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> only.</li> 
       <li>January 22: Founding of the first city in Brazil, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Vicente,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Vicente, São Paulo">São Vicente</a>. City of São Vicente only.</li> 
       <li>January 25: Founding of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> (Aniversário de São Paulo), City of São Paulo only.</li> 
       <li>January 26: Founding of <a href="/wiki/Santos,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Santos, São Paulo">Santos</a> (Aniversario de Santos), City of Santos only.</li> 
       <li>April 21: Founding of <a href="/wiki/Brasilia" class="mw-redirect" title="Brasilia">Brasilia</a> (Aniversário de Brasília), coinciding with <a href="/wiki/Tiradentes" title="Tiradentes">Tiradentes</a>, Federal District only.</li> 
       <li>April 23: <a href="/wiki/Saint_George" title="Saint George">Saint George</a> (São Jorge), City of Rio de Janeiro only. In 2007, governor <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9rgio_Cabral" class="mw-redirect" title="Sérgio Cabral">Sérgio Cabral</a> expanded this holiday to all the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Rio_de_Janeiro" class="mw-redirect" title="State of Rio de Janeiro">State of Rio de Janeiro</a>.</li> 
       <li>July 2: Bahia Independence Day, State of <a href="/wiki/Bahia" title="Bahia">Bahia</a> only.</li> 
       <li>July 8: Sergipe Political Emancipation Day, State of <a href="/wiki/Sergipe" title="Sergipe">Sergipe</a> only</li> 
       <li>July 9: <a href="/wiki/Constitutionalist_Revolution" title="Constitutionalist Revolution">Constitutionalist Revolution</a> (Revolução Constitucionalista), <a href="/wiki/State_of_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" class="mw-redirect" title="State of São Paulo">State of São Paulo</a> only.</li> 
       <li>August 15: Our Lady of the Good Voyage (Nossa Senhora da Boa Viagem), City of <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a> only.</li> 
       <li>August 26: Emancipation of the city of Campo Grande, City of <a href="/wiki/Campo_Grande" title="Campo Grande">Campo Grande</a> only.</li> 
       <li>September 8: Our Lady of the Light in the Pine (Nossa Senhora da Luz dos Pinhais), City of <a href="/wiki/Curitiba" title="Curitiba">Curitiba</a> only.</li> 
       <li>September 20: <a href="/wiki/War_of_Tatters" class="mw-redirect" title="War of Tatters">Farroupilha's Revolution</a> (Revolução Farroupilha), State of <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Rio Grande do Sul</a> only.</li> 
       <li>October 11: Creation of the State of Mato Grosso do Sul, separated from <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso" title="Mato Grosso">Mato Grosso</a> in 1977, State of <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso_do_Sul" title="Mato Grosso do Sul">Mato Grosso do Sul</a> only.</li> 
       <li>October 3: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Urua%C3%A7%C3%BA&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Uruaçú (page does not exist)">Uruaçú</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cunha%C3%BA&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cunhaú (page does not exist)">Cunhaú</a> Martyrs Day, State of <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Norte" title="Rio Grande do Norte">Rio Grande do Norte</a> only.</li> 
       <li>October 24: Founding of <a href="/wiki/Goi%C3%A2nia" title="Goiânia">Goiânia</a>, City of Goiânia only.</li> 
       <li>November 4: Founding of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Carlos" title="São Carlos">São Carlos</a>, City of São Carlos only.</li> 
       <li>November 20: <a href="/wiki/Zumbi" title="Zumbi">Zumbi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Palmares_(quilombo)" title="Palmares (quilombo)">Palmares</a>, Black Awareness Day (Zumbi dos Palmares, Dia da Consciência Negra), cities of São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro only.</li> 
       <li>November 21: Our Lady of Apresentação (Nossa Senhora da Apresentação) Day, City of <a href="/wiki/Natal,_Rio_Grande_do_Norte" title="Natal, Rio Grande do Norte">Natal</a> only.</li> 
       <li>November 30: <a href="/wiki/Evangelicalism" title="Evangelicalism">Evangelical</a> Day (Dia do Evangélico), Federal District only.</li> 
       <li>December 8: Our Lady of Conceição (Dia de Nossa Senhora da Conceição), State of <a href="/wiki/Pernambuco" title="Pernambuco">Pernambuco</a>, City of <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a>, City of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dourados,_Mato_Grosso_do_Sul&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dourados, Mato Grosso do Sul (page does not exist)">Dourados</a>, and City of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Maria,_Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Santa Maria, Rio Grande do Sul">Santa Maria</a> only.</li> 
       <li>December 19: Emancipation of the Province of Paraná, State of <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_(state)" title="Paraná (state)">Paraná</a> only.</li>
      </ul>
