      <p><b>Public holidays in India</b>, also known as <i>statutory holidays</i>, consist of a variety of cultural, nationalistic, and religious <a href="/wiki/Holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Holidays">holidays</a> that are legislated in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">union</a> or <a href="/wiki/State_governments_of_India" title="State governments of India">state</a> levels. While many of these holidays are honoured and acknowledged nationwide, State legislation varies in regard to which are officially recognized. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, being a culturally diverse society, celebrates many holidays and festivals, but there are only three national holidays: <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(India)" title="Republic Day (India)">Republic Day</a> (26 January), <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(India)" title="Independence Day (India)">Independence Day</a> (15 August) and <a href="/wiki/Gandhi_Jayanti" title="Gandhi Jayanti">Gandhi Jayanti</a> (2 October).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>States have local festivals depending on prevalent religious and linguistic demographics. Popular Hindu festivals of <a href="/wiki/Makar_Sankranti" title="Makar Sankranti">Makar Sankranti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pongal_(festival)" title="Pongal (festival)">Pongal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maha_Shivratri" class="mw-redirect" title="Maha Shivratri">Maha Shivratri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Onam" title="Onam">Onam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janmashtami" class="mw-redirect" title="Janmashtami">Janmashtami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saraswati_Puja" class="mw-redirect" title="Saraswati Puja">Saraswati Puja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diwali" title="Diwali">Diwali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ganesh_Chaturthi" title="Ganesh Chaturthi">Ganesh Chaturthi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raksha_Bandhan" title="Raksha Bandhan">Raksha Bandhan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holi" title="Holi">Holi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Durga_Puja" title="Durga Puja">Durga Puja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Navratri" class="mw-redirect" title="Navratri">Dussehra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diwali" title="Diwali">Diwali</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jain" class="mw-redirect" title="Jain">Jain</a> festivals are <a href="/wiki/Mahavir_Janma_Kalyanak" title="Mahavir Janma Kalyanak">Mahavir Janma Kalyanak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paryushan" class="mw-redirect" title="Paryushan">Paryushan</a>; <a href="/wiki/Sikh" class="mw-redirect" title="Sikh">Sikh</a> festivals like <a href="/wiki/Guru_Nanak_Jayanti" class="mw-redirect" title="Guru Nanak Jayanti">Guru Nanak Jayanti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vaisakhi" title="Vaisakhi">Vaisakhi</a>; Muslim festivals of <a href="/wiki/Eid_ul-Fitr" class="mw-redirect" title="Eid ul-Fitr">Eid ul-Fitr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eid_ul-Adha" class="mw-redirect" title="Eid ul-Adha">Eid ul-Adha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mawlid" title="Mawlid">Mawlid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muharram" title="Muharram">Muharram</a>;       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays_with_religious_significance">Holidays with religious significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Holidays with religious significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In India, people from various religions coexist together. Religious and cultural holidays are characterized by a diversity of religious beliefs and practices. <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">People from different religions celebrate their festivals. It is not that a particular festival is celebrated by the people of only a particular religion but, people from other religions also play a great role in the celebration of the festival of other religions.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>

      <h3><span id="Parsee_.28Zoroastrian.29_holidays"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Parsee_(Zoroastrian)_holidays">Parsee (Zoroastrian) holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Parsee (Zoroastrian) holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Shahenshahi" class="mw-redirect" title="Shahenshahi">Shahenshahi</a></i> and <i>Kadmi</i> variants of the <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrian_calendar" title="Zoroastrian calendar">Zoroastrian calendar</a> do not intercalate leap years and hence the day of the Gregorian calendar year on which these days are celebrated shifts ahead with time. The third variant of the Zoroastrian calendar, known as either <i>Fasli</i> (in India) or <i>Bastani</i> (in Iran), intercalcates according to Gregorian calendar rules and thus remains synchronous with the seasons. The Parsis in India use a <a href="/wiki/Shahenshahi" class="mw-redirect" title="Shahenshahi">Shahenshahi</a> calendar, unlike the <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrians</a> who use a <a href="/wiki/Kadmi" class="mw-redirect" title="Kadmi">Kadmi</a> calendar. The North American and European Parsis have adapted their own version of the <a href="/wiki/Fasli" class="mw-redirect" title="Fasli">Fasli</a> calendar. <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">This is, however, is looked down upon by many of the Parsis, who continue to use the Shahenshai calendar.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Dubious claim that requires a reliable source (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> These differences cause changes in the dates of the holidays. For example, the Zoroastrian New Year, <a href="/wiki/Nowruz" title="Nowruz">Nowruz</a>, falls in the spring for the Iranians but in the summer for the Parsis. </p>

      <p>Central and State governments in India issue annually a list of holidays to be observed in the respective government offices during the year.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> The list is divided into two parts: </p>
      <ul>
       <li>Gazetted holidays (Annexure I)</li> 
       <li>Restricted holidays (Annexure II)</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>In addition, local administrations also issue a list of holidays, known as local holidays, which are observed at the district level. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_government">Central government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Central government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Personnel,_Public_Grievances_and_Pensions" title="Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions">Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions</a> (Department of Personnel and Training) on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Government of India</a> issues a list of holidays to be observed in central government offices during the year. The list is divided in two parts i.e. Annexure I &amp; Annexure </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Annexure_I">Annexure I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Annexure I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>Annexure I, also known as Gazetted holidays, consists of a list of holidays that are mandatory once decided.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> This list consists of two parts: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Paragraph 2</li> 
       <li>Paragraph 3.1</li>
      </ul> 
      <h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Paragraph_2">Paragraph 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Paragraph 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5> 
      <p>It consists of holidays that are observed compulsorily across India.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> These holidays are: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Republic Day</li> 
       <li>Independence Day</li> 
       <li>Gandhi Jayanti</li> 
       <li>Mahavir Janma Kalyanak</li> 
       <li>Buddha Purnima</li> 
       <li>Christmas Day</li> 
       <li>Dussehra</li> 
       <li>Diwali (Deepavali)</li> 
       <li>Good Friday</li> 
       <li>Guru Nanak's Birthday</li> 
       <li>Eid ul-Fitr</li> 
       <li>Eid al-Adha (Bakrid)</li> 
       <li>Muharram</li> 
       <li>Prophet Muhammad's Birthday (Id-e-Milad)</li>
      </ol> 
      <p>so all the holidays are there </p> 
      <h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Paragraph_3.1">Paragraph 3.1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Paragraph 3.1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5> 
      <p>In addition to the 14 compulsory holidays mentioned in paragraph 2, three holidays are chosen from the list below by the Central Government Employees Welfare Coordination Committee in the respective state capitals (if necessary, in consultation with Coordination Committees at other places in the State). The final list is applied uniformly across all Central Government offices within each state. They are notified after seeking the prior approval of the ministry, and no changes can be made thereafter. No change is permissible in regard to festivals and dates.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>An additional day for Dasara</li> 
       <li>Holi</li> 
       <li>Janamashtami (Vaishanvi)/Krishnashtami</li> 
       <li>Rama Navami</li> 
       <li>Maha Sivarathri</li> 
       <li>Ganesh Chaturthi/Vinayak Chaturthi</li> 
       <li>Makar Sankranti/Sankranthi</li> 
       <li>Onam</li> 
       <li>Sri Panchami/Basanta Panchami</li> 
       <li>Vishu/Vaisakhi/Vaisakhadi/Bhag Bihu/Mashadi/Ugadi/Chaitra Sakladi/Cheti Chand/Gudhi Pada 1st Navratra/Nauraj</li>
      </ol> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Annexure_II">Annexure II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Annexure II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>Annexure II also known as Restricted holidays, consists of a list of holidays which are optional. Each employee is allowed to choose any two holidays from the list of Restricted Holidays. The Coordination Committees at the State Capitals draw up a separate list of Restricted Holidays, keeping in mind the occasions of local importance, but the nine occasions left over, after choosing the three variable holidays in paragraph 3.1, are included in the list of restricted holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_government_organisations">Central government organisations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Central government organisations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Central Government Organisations, which include industrial, commercial and trading establishments, observe up to 16 holidays per year, including three national holidays, viz. Republic Day, Independence Day and Mahatma Gandhi's birthday, as compulsory holidays. The remaining holidays/occasions may be determined by such establishments/organisations themselves, subject to paragraph 3.2.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Union_territory_administrations">Union territory administrations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Union territory administrations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Union territory administrations decide the list of holidays based on Ministry of Home Affairs letter No.14046/27 /83- GP-I dated 15 February 1984, by which they observe a total of 16 holidays, including the three National Holidays, viz. Republic Day, Independence Day and Mahatma Gandhi's birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indian_missions_abroad">Indian missions abroad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_India&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Indian missions abroad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In respect of Indian missions abroad, the number of holidays is determined in accordance with the instructions contained in the Department of Personnel and Training's O.M. No.12/5/2002-JCA dated 17 December 2002. They have the option to select 11 (eleven) holidays of their own only after including the three National Holidays and Diwali, Milad-Un-Nabi or Id-E-Milad, Mahavir Janma Kalyanak, Idu'l Fitr, Dussehra (Vijaya Dasami), Guru Nanak's Birthday, Christmas Day as compulsory holidays falling on weekdays.<sup id="cite_ref-GOI_10-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOI-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
