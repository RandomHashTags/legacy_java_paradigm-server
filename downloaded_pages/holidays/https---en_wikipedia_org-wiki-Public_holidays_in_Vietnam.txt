      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_holidays">Public holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Public holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>Public holidays in Vietnam</b> are days when workers get the day off work. Prior to 2007, Vietnamese workers observed 8 days of public holiday a year, among the lowest in the region. On 28 March 2007 the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Vietnam" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Vietnam">government</a> added the traditional holiday commemorating the mythical <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B9ng_V%C6%B0%C6%A1ng" class="mw-redirect" title="Hùng Vương">Hùng kings</a> to its list of public holidays,<sup id="cite_ref-vietbao_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vietbao-1">[1]</a></sup> increasing the number of days to 10. As in most other nations, if a holiday falls during the weekend, it is observed on the following Monday. </p>
