      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fixed_public_holidays">Fixed public holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fixed public holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Article_J_of_the_Constitution_of_Hungary_on_national_holidays">Article J of the Constitution of Hungary on national holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Article J of the Constitution of Hungary on national holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>(1) The national holidays of Hungary shall be: </p>
      <p>a) the 15th day of March, in memory of the 1848–49 Revolution and War of Independence, </p>
      <p>b) the 20th day of August, in memory of the state's founding and its founder King Saint Stephen, </p>
      <p>c) the 23rd day of October, in memory of the 1956 Revolution and War of Independence. </p>
      <p>(2) The official state holiday shall be the 20th day of August. </p>
      <p>Source:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

He was canonized on 20 August 1083, and 20 August is his <a href="/wiki/Feast_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Feast day">feast day</a> in Hungary. <p>Celebrated with a half-hour <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a> display on the bank of the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> in the evening, watched from both river banks, the Buda hills, and the rooftops of Pest and Buda. Under the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_People%27s_Republic" title="Hungarian People's Republic">Hungarian People's Republic</a>, 20 August was celebrated as the founding of the socialist republic.<sup id="cite_ref-HPR_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPR-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> </td>

         <td>On this day, Hungarian children polish their boots and put them in the window. <i>Mikulás</i> comes in the night and fills them with chocolates and/or small presents. If they were bad, they might get sticks instead. </td>

        <tr> 
         <td>31 December</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/New_Year" title="New Year">New Year</a>'s Eve</td> 
         <td><i>Szilveszter</i> </td> 
         <td>Young people go partying until morning. Streets are noisy with paper trumpets, hoots and the pop of champagne corks; people often wear masks and throw <a href="/wiki/Petards" class="mw-redirect" title="Petards">petards</a>. Those who stay home usually watch comedies made for the occasion; at midnight they drink champagne and wish each other good luck for the new year (<i>BÚÉK</i>). All Hungarian radio &amp; television channels broadcast the <a href="/wiki/Isten,_%C3%A1ldd_meg_a_magyart" class="mw-redirect" title="Isten, áldd meg a magyart">national anthem</a> at midnight, and then the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Hungary" title="President of Hungary">President</a>'s speech. Firework displays are common. The next day streets are empty, and people sleep late (or sleep themselves sober). Lentils are eaten, symbolising coins for good luck. </td>
        </tr>

Traditions include <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masquerade_ball" title="Masquerade ball">masquerading</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parade" title="Parade">parades</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dancing</a>. </td>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_events">Special events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Special events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Hungary's most notable annual events include the <a href="/wiki/Budapest_Spring_Festival" title="Budapest Spring Festival">Budapest Spring Festival</a> (mid-march to mid-April), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hortob%C3%A1gy_Equestrian_Days&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hortobágy Equestrian Days (page does not exist)">Hortobágy Equestrian Days</a> (late June), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sopron_Early_Music_Days&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sopron Early Music Days (page does not exist)">Sopron Early Music Days</a> (late June), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Festival_in_Budapest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Festival in Budapest (page does not exist)">Festival in Budapest</a> (late June), <a href="/wiki/Miskolc_Opera_Festival" title="Miskolc Opera Festival">Miskolc Opera Festival</a> (late June), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miskolc_Kal%C3%A1lka_International_Folk_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Miskolc Kalálka International Folk Festival (page does not exist)">Miskolc Kalálka International Folk Festival</a> (July), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gy%C5%91r_Summer_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Győr Summer Festival (page does not exist)">Győr Summer Festival</a> (late June), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gy%C5%91r_Summer_Cultural_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Győr Summer Cultural Festival (page does not exist)">Győr Summer Cultural Festival</a> (late June to late July), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pannon_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pannon Festival (page does not exist)">Pannon Festival</a> in Pécs (July and August), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Szentendre_Summer_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Szentendre Summer Festival (page does not exist)">Szentendre Summer Festival</a> (July), <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C5%91szeg_Street_Theatre_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kőszeg Street Theatre Festival (page does not exist)">Kőszeg Street Theatre Festival</a> (late July), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Savaria_International_Dance_Competition&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Savaria International Dance Competition (page does not exist)">Savaria International Dance Competition</a> in Szombathely (July), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debrecen_Jazz_Days&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Debrecen Jazz Days (page does not exist)">Debrecen Jazz Days</a> (July), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Szeged_Open_Air_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Szeged Open Air Festival (page does not exist)">Szeged Open Air Festival</a> (mid-July to August), <a href="/wiki/Di%C3%A1ksziget" class="mw-redirect" title="Diáksziget">Diáksziget</a> (shorter: <i>"Sziget"</i> or "Sziget Festival", Student Island or Pepsi Island) north of Budapest (August), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eger_Wine_Harvest_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eger Wine Harvest Festival (page does not exist)">Eger Wine Harvest Festival</a> (September), and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Budapest_Autumn_Arts_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Budapest Autumn Arts Festival (page does not exist)">Budapest Autumn Arts Festival</a> (mid-September to mid-October). </p>
      <p>St Stephen's Day (August 20) is celebrated with sporting events, parades and fireworks nationwide. On the same day there is a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Floral Festival (page does not exist)">Floral Festival</a> in Debrecen and a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bridge_Fair&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bridge Fair (page does not exist)">Bridge Fair</a> in nearby Hortobágy. Formula 1 car races are held in early August at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> near <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, 18&nbsp;km northeast of Budapest.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
