      <p>There are 11 official public holidays in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Alsace" title="Alsace">Alsace</a> region and the <a href="/wiki/Moselle_(department)" title="Moselle (department)">Moselle</a> department observe 2 additional days.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Unlike most countries, these holidays do not shift when they fall during a week-end,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which means that the average number of observed public holidays <i>falling on weekdays</i> is 8.7 and ranges from 7 to 10.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Most <a href="/wiki/Asian_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian countries">Asian countries</a> and all <a href="/wiki/North_American" class="mw-redirect" title="North American">North American</a> countries observe between 2 and 10 more public holidays per year <i>on weekdays</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_France&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>See <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/F%C3%AAtes_et_jours_f%C3%A9ri%C3%A9s_en_France#Tableau_récapitulatif" class="extiw" title="fr:Fêtes et jours fériés en France">Fêtes et jours fériés en France</a> (Wikipedia page in French), to have all the dates (French Overseas Departments (DOM) added). </p>
      <p><i>Note:</i> French law dictates that work should stop, but be paid, only for the <i>Fête du Travail</i> (May Day, 1 May),<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> except in industries where it is infeasible to stop working.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The rest of the public holidays are listed in statute law,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but law does not dictate that work should stop; however a leave from work may be granted by the employer or by <i>convention collective</i> (agreement between employers' and employees' unions). </p>
      <p>In 2005, French prime minister <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Raffarin" title="Jean-Pierre Raffarin">Jean-Pierre Raffarin</a> removed Pentecost (Whit) Monday's status as a public holiday. This decision was eventually overruled by French courts in 2008.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Today, companies are free to consider Whit Monday as a working day; the question then that should be asked here is, what is it that was">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Employers are free to decide whether to make Whit Monday a day off or not.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
