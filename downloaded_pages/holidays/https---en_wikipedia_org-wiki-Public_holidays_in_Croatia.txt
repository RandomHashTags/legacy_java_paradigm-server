      <p>* - In 2020 there was a change in holidays: June 25 (Statehood Day) and October 8 (Independence Day) were demoted from public holidays to memorial days, and May 30 (National Day) and November 18 (Remembrance Day) were promoted from memorial days to public holidays. </p>
      <p>Note: Citizens of the Republic of Croatia who celebrate different religious holidays have the right not to work on those dates. Christians who celebrate Christmas, Easter and Easter Monday per the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, Muslims on the days of <a href="/wiki/Ramadan_Bayram" class="mw-redirect" title="Ramadan Bayram">Ramadan Bayram</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kurban_Bayram" class="mw-redirect" title="Kurban Bayram">Kurban Bayram</a>, and Jews on the days of <a href="/wiki/Rosh_Hashanah" title="Rosh Hashanah">Rosh Hashanah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yom_Kippur" title="Yom Kippur">Yom Kippur</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Unofficial_holidays">Unofficial holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Croatia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Unofficial holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li>Popular carnival celebrations are held in most cities and towns in the country on <a href="/wiki/Shrove_Tuesday" title="Shrove Tuesday">Shrove Tuesday</a> (<i>Pokladni utorak</i>), when customarily businesses in <a href="/wiki/Public_sector" title="Public sector">public sector</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hospitality_industry" title="Hospitality industry">hospitality industry</a> cease work for the day earlier than usual, but the day is not officially designated a public holiday.</li> 
       <li>Some cities also celebrate <i>de facto</i> public holidays on their patron saints' feast days. For example, in <a href="/wiki/Split,_Croatia" title="Split, Croatia">Split</a>, the day of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Domnius" title="Saint Domnius">Saint Domnius</a> (<i>Sveti Duje</i>) is celebrated on May 7, while <a href="/wiki/Dubrovnik" title="Dubrovnik">Dubrovnik</a> marks the day of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Blaise" title="Saint Blaise">Saint Blaise</a> (<i>Sveti Vlaho</i>) on February 3; business usually cease work earlier than usual on these days.</li> 
       <li>Even though Christmas Eve (December 24), New Year's Eve (December 31), and Good Friday are not public holidays, businesses customarily close earlier (as early as 12pm).</li>
      </ul>
