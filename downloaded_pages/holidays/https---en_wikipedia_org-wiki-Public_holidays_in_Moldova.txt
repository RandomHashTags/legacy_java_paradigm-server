   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Moldova</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

      <p><b>Public holidays in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Moldova" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Moldova">Republic of Moldova</a></b> refer to the celebrated non-working days established by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Moldova" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Moldova">Government of Moldova</a> and valid for the whole territory of the country. Autonomous territorial units <a href="/wiki/Gagauzia" title="Gagauzia">Gagauzia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transnistria" title="Transnistria">Transnistria</a>, as well cities, communes and cantonal authorities also establish local holidays, which are, however, not non-working days. There are 14 nationally celebrated holidays in the modern Moldova. Most holidays celebrated in the Republic of Moldova recognize events or people from <a href="/wiki/History_of_Moldova" title="History of Moldova">Moldovan history</a>. Most retail businesses close on New Year's and Independence Day, but remain open on all other holidays. Private businesses often observe only the big holidays such as <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a>, Easter Monday, <a href="/wiki/Victory_Day_(May_9)" class="mw-redirect" title="Victory Day (May 9)">Victory Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Republic_of_Moldova)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence Day (Republic of Moldova)">Independence Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Limba_noastr%C4%83_(public_holiday)" title="Limba noastră (public holiday)">Language Day</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Christmas_and_holiday_season" title="Christmas and holiday season"><i>holiday season</i></a> in the winter traditionally ran between <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a> until <a href="/wiki/Old_New_Year" title="Old New Year">Old new Year's Day</a>. As of 2009, the holiday season now officially begins with Western Christmas on December 25, now a legal holiday in the Republic of Moldova. The holiday seasons gets underway much earlier with the official lighting of the Chisinau town Christmas tree at the end of November or very beginning of December when other than Christmas, some locals celebrate <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">Winter solstice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hanukkah" title="Hanukkah">Hanukkah</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kwanzaa" title="Kwanzaa">Kwanzaa</a>. The <i>Summer holiday season</i> traditionally (though unofficially) starts in May with celebrations of anniversary of most important localities (<a href="/wiki/B%C4%83l%C5%A3i" class="mw-redirect" title="Bălţi">Bălţi</a> - 21 May) and culminates in the end of August with the celebrations of Independence Day and Limba Noastra. </p>
