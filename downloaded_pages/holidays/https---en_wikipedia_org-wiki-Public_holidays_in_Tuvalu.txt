         <td>16 February</td>

         <td>Bogin te Ieka (Day of the Flood)</td> 
         <td>Commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">Tsunami</a> that struck the island on that day in 1882.<sup id="cite_ref-TH10_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TH10-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RC04_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RC04-5">[5]</a></sup> </td>

         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>23 April</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Funafuti" title="Funafuti">Funafuti</a></td> 
         <td>Funafuti Bomb Day</td> 
         <td>Commemorates the day during the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a> (World War II) when 680 people took refuge in the concrete walled, <a href="/wiki/Pandanus" title="Pandanus">pandanus</a>-thatched church from a Japanese bombing raid. Corporal B. F. Ladd, an American soldier, persuaded them to get into dugouts, as a bomb struck the building shortly after.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td><i><a href="/wiki/Moveable_feast" title="Moveable feast">moveable</a></i> in May</td>

         <td>Te Aso o te Setema</td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>21 October</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Funafuti" title="Funafuti">Funafuti</a></td> 
         <td>Cyclone Day</td>

         <td><a href="/wiki/Vaitupu" title="Vaitupu">Vaitupu</a></td> 
         <td>Te Aso Fiafia (Happy Day)</td> 
         <td>Commemorates 25 November 1887 which was the date on which the final instalment of a debt of $13,000 was repaid to H. M. Ruge and Company.<sup id="cite_ref-TH11_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TH11-9">[9]</a></sup> </td>
