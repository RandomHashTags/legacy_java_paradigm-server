   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Iran</h1>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> uses three official calendar systems, including the <a href="/wiki/Solar_Hijri_calendar" title="Solar Hijri calendar">Solar Hijri calendar</a> as the main and national calendar, the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> for international events and Christian holidays, and the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_calendar" title="Islamic calendar">Islamic calendar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lunar_calendar" title="Lunar calendar">Lunar calendar</a>) for Islamic holidays. </p>
      <p>The surfeit of public holidays has been a subject of concern for almost 30 years. A substantial number of unofficial holidays are added each year to the national holidays that further aggravate the situation.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Iran has the most public holidays in the world with around 25 holidays. The holidays in Iran may differ regarding the Arabic Calendar.<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://publicholidays.me/iran/">[1]</a><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.qppstudio.net/publicholidays2016/iran.htm">[2]</a> </p>
