      <p> <b><a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">National holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a></b> are classified into state holidays and other holidays and commemorative days, including religious holidays. Nine of them are non-working days.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <table class="wikitable">

         <td><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a></td> 
         <td>Новы год (Novy Hod)</td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>7 January</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Orthodox">Orthodox Christmas</a></td> 
         <td>Каляды праваслаўныя (Kalady pravasłaŭnyja)</td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>8 March</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/International_Women%27s_Day" title="International Women's Day">International Women's Day</a></td> 
         <td>Мiжнародны жаночы дзень (Mižnarodny žanočy dzień)</td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td><i><a href="/wiki/Moveable_feast" title="Moveable feast">moveable</a></i></td>

         <td>Радунiца (Radunica)</td> 
         <td>9 days after Orthodox Easter </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>1 May</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/International_Labour_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="International Labour Day">International Labour Day</a></td> 
         <td>Дзень працы (Dzień Pracy)</td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>9 May</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Victory_Day_(Eastern_Europe)" class="mw-redirect" title="Victory Day (Eastern Europe)">Victory Day</a></td> 
         <td>Дзень Перамогi (Dzień Pieramohi)</td> 
         <td>Marks the victory against Germany in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>3 July</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Belarus)" title="Independence Day (Belarus)">Independence Day</a></td> 
         <td>Дзень Незалежнасцi (Dzień Niezaležnasci)</td> 
         <td>Adopted in 1996, in commemoration of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a>'s 1944 liberation of <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Minsk_Offensive" title="Minsk Offensive">Minsk Offensive</a> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>7 November</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/October_Revolution_Day" title="October Revolution Day">October Revolution Day</a></td> 
         <td>Дзень Кастрычніцкай рэвалюцыі (Dzień Kastryčnickaj revalucyi)</td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>25 December</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Catholic Christmas</a></td> 
         <td>Каляды каталiцкiя (Kalady katalickija)</td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr>

         <td>In solemn remembrance of all the victims of the Second World War, and in commemoration of the heroic last stand of <a href="/wiki/Brest_Fortress" title="Brest Fortress">Brest Fortress</a> in 1941, on the very country and city (<a href="/wiki/Brest,_Belarus" title="Brest, Belarus">Brest</a>) in which the Eastern Front began. </td>
