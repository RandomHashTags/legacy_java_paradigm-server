      <p>Other traditional, international and professional holidays, as well as religious holidays, can also be celebrated in Armenia. National minorities can also celebrate their national holidays. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reshuffling_by_government">Reshuffling by government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reshuffling by government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Government is allowed to swap working and non-working days. It usually makes use of this e.g. declaring <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a> (calculated according to <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church" title="Armenian Apostolic Church">Armenian Apostolic Church</a>, and in general not always coinciding with Orthodox or Western Easter day calculations) non-working, while one of proximate Saturdays becomes a working days instead. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_memorable_days">Other memorable days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other memorable days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>On the fourth Sunday before 15 August (Dormition/Assumption), a special celebration called <i><a href="/wiki/Vardavar" title="Vardavar">Vardavar</a></i> (Վարդավար) takes place, where anyone is free to soak others with water from spray guns, hoses or even buckets. </p>
