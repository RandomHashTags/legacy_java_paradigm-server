   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Colombia</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub" class="noprint">

      <p>Colombia has 19 holidays (12 Catholic holidays and 7 Civic holidays), plus Palm and Easter Sunday. The city of <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">Barranquilla</a> has 2 extra holidays celebrating Monday and Tuesday of <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a>. </p>
      <p>The following are <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Public holidays">public holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Año Nuevo / (<a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a>) (January 1)</li> 
       <li>Día de los Reyes Magos / (<a href="/wiki/Epiphany_(Christian)" class="mw-redirect" title="Epiphany (Christian)">Epiphany</a>) (January 6<sup>[1]</sup>)</li> 
       <li>Monday of Carnival (48 days before <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter Sunday</a> or 2 days before <a href="/wiki/Ash_Wednesday" title="Ash Wednesday">Ash Wednesday</a>. Valid only for the city of <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">Barranquilla</a>.)</li> 
       <li>Tuesday of Carnival (47 days before Easter Sunday or the day before Ash Wednesday. Valid only for the city of Barranquilla.)</li> 
       <li>Día de San José / (<a href="/wiki/Saint_Joseph%27s_Day" title="Saint Joseph's Day">Saint Joseph's Day</a>) (March 19<sup>[1]</sup>)</li> 
       <li>Jueves Santo / (<a href="/wiki/Maundy_Thursday" title="Maundy Thursday">Maundy Thursday</a>) and Viernes Santo (<a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a>) (the Thursday and Friday before Easter Sunday, variable dates in March or April)</li> 
       <li>Primero de Mayo / (<a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a>) (May 1)</li> 
       <li>Ascensión del señor / (<a href="/wiki/Ascension_of_Jesus" title="Ascension of Jesus">Ascension of Jesus</a>) (39 days after <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter Sunday</a><sup id="cite_ref-note_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a> (60 days after <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter Sunday</a><sup id="cite_ref-note_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li>Sagrado Corazón /( <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Heart" title="Sacred Heart">Sacred Heart</a>) (68 days after <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter Sunday</a><sup id="cite_ref-note_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li>San Pedro y San Pablo / <a href="/wiki/Feast_of_Saints_Peter_and_Paul" title="Feast of Saints Peter and Paul">Saint Peter and Saint Paul</a>) (June 29<sup id="cite_ref-note_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li>Independencia de Cali / <a href="/wiki/Cali" title="Cali">Cali</a> Declaration of Independence (July 3)</li> 
       <li>Declaración de la Independencia de Colombia / <a href="/wiki/Colombian_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Colombian Declaration of Independence">Declaration of Independence</a> (July 20)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Boyac%C3%A1" title="Battle of Boyacá">Battle of Boyacá</a> (August 7)</li> 
       <li>La Asunción / (<a href="/wiki/Assumption_of_Mary" title="Assumption of Mary">Assumption of Mary</a>) (August 15<sup id="cite_ref-note_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li>Día de la Raza / (<a href="/wiki/Columbus_Day" title="Columbus Day">Columbus Day</a>) (October 12<sup id="cite_ref-note_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li>Día de los Santos / <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%E2%80%99_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="All Saints’ Day">All Saints’ Day</a> (November 1<sup id="cite_ref-note_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li>Independencia de Cartagena / Independence of <a href="/wiki/Cartagena,_Colombia" title="Cartagena, Colombia">Cartagena</a> (November 11<sup id="cite_ref-note_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-note-1">[1]</a></sup>)</li> 
       <li>La Inmaculada Concepción (<a href="/wiki/Immaculate_Conception" title="Immaculate Conception">Immaculate Conception</a>) (December 8)</li> 
       <li>Navidad (<a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>) (December 25)</li>
      </ul>
