      <p><a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> has numerous public holidays, including national memorial, religious and secular holidays of Bengali origin. The Bengali traditional calendar, known as <a href="/wiki/Banggabda" class="mw-redirect" title="Banggabda">Banggabda</a> is the national and official calendar in Bangladesh. The holidays are celebrated according to Bengali, Islamic or Gregorian calendars for religious and civil purposes, respectively. Religious festivals like Eid are celebrated according to the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_calendar" title="Islamic calendar">Islamic calendar</a> whereas other national holidays are celebrated according to the Bengali and <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. While the Islamic calendar is based on the movement of the moon, it loses synchronization with the seasons, through seasonal drift. Therefore, some public holidays are subject to change every year based on the lunar calendar. </p>
      <p>There are fifteen public holidays in Bangladesh. Muslims and non-Muslims have four religious holidays each in addition to the secular seven national holidays. For the <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>, four major <a href="/wiki/Islamic_holidays" title="Islamic holidays">Islamic holidays</a>: <a href="/wiki/Muharram" title="Muharram">Muharram</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mawlid" title="Mawlid">Mawlid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eid_ul-Fitr" class="mw-redirect" title="Eid ul-Fitr">Eid ul-Fitr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eid_ul-Adha" class="mw-redirect" title="Eid ul-Adha">Eid ul-Adha</a> are observed. For the <a href="/wiki/Hindus" title="Hindus">Hindus</a>: <a href="/wiki/Krishna_Janmashtami" title="Krishna Janmashtami">Krishna Janmashtami</a> and <a href="/wiki/Durga_Puja" title="Durga Puja">Durga Puja</a> are celebrated. As for the <a href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhists</a>: <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vesak" title="Vesak">Vesak</a> (one day each) are celebrated. </p>
