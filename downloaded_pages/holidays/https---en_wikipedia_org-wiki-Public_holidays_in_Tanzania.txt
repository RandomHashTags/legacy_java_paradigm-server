      <p><b>Public holidays in Tanzania</b> are in accordance with the Public Holidays Ordinance (Amended) Act,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> 1966 and are observed throughout the nation. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Tanzania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Public Holidays Ordinance (Amended) Act, 1966 lists twelve public holidays in its schedule. At present, Tanzania has a total of seventeen public holidays:<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> eight <a href="/wiki/Religious_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious holiday">religious holidays</a>, three <a href="/wiki/National_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="National Day">national holidays</a>, two commemorating the death anniversaries of the inaugural leaders of its constituent states and the remaining four of other national importance. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Tanzania" title="President of Tanzania">President of Tanzania</a> may declare additional holidays at his or her discretion, for example, during the general election day. The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Zanzibar" title="President of Zanzibar">President of Zanzibar</a> may do the same within the semi-autonomous islands of <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A recent example of this was on 4 November 2015 where President Kikwete announced a national holiday for the next day to celebrate Magufuli winning the presidential election.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
