   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Spain</h1>

      <p><b>Public holidays celebrated in Spain</b> include a mix of religious (<a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>), national and regional observances. Each municipality is allowed to have a maximum of 14 <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holidays</a> per year; a maximum of nine of these are chosen by the national government and at least two are chosen locally. </p>
      <p>If one of the "national holidays" happens to fall on a Sunday the regional governments — the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous communities of Spain</a> — can choose an alternate holiday or they can allow local authorities to choose. In practice, except for holidays falling on a Sunday, the regional governments can choose up to three holidays per year; or they can choose fewer to allow for more options at the local level. </p>
      <p>A <a href="/wiki/Puente_(holiday)" title="Puente (holiday)">puente</a> (bridge) is sometimes made between weekends and holidays that fall on Tuesday or Thursday. The puente will then create a long weekend. </p>
      <p>Since 2010, <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">Melilla</a>, both <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_cities_of_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous cities of Spain">autonomous cities of Spain</a>, have declared the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim holiday">Muslim holiday</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Adha" title="Eid al-Adha">Eid al-Adha</a> or Feast of the Sacrifice, as an official public holiday. It was the first time a non-Christian <a href="/wiki/Religious_festival" title="Religious festival">religious festival</a> has been officially celebrated in Spain since the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
