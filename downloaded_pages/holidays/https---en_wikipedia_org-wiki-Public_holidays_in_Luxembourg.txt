      <p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="National Day">National Holiday</a> celebrates the birthday of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Grand Duke</a> and used to be movable to coincide with the actual birthday of monarch. To avoid cold weather on the January 5 birthday of <a href="/wiki/Jean,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Jean, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Grand Duke Jean</a> (reigned 1964-2000), the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duke%27s_Official_Birthday" title="Grand Duke's Official Birthday">Grand Duke's Official Birthday</a> celebration was pinned since 1962 to Jean's <a href="/wiki/Name_day" title="Name day">name day</a> on June 23, and was maintained under the reign of his successor <a href="/wiki/Henri,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Henri</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Unofficial_holidays">Unofficial holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Unofficial holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a> (Friday before Easter Sunday) is a bank holiday, meaning that it is only a public holiday for bank employees. </p>
      <p>Local complementary unofficial holidays may exist, like the <a href="/wiki/Kermesse_(festival)" title="Kermesse (festival)">Kermesse Day</a> (known as <i>Kiermes</i>) that always fall on a Monday in the city of <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_City" title="Luxembourg City">Luxembourg</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival Day</a> also happening in the capital city. </p>
