      <p><a href="/wiki/Public_holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Public holidays">Public holidays</a> are celebrated by the entire population of Egypt. <a href="/wiki/Holiday" title="Holiday">Holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> have many classifications. Some holidays are religious and others are secular, while some can be fixed holidays on the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">calendar</a> while others are movable. There are four <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamic</a> holidays and two <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> holidays. The <a href="/wiki/National_day" title="National day">national day</a> of Egypt is celebrated on July, 23<sup id="cite_ref-MFA_country_profile_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MFA_country_profile-1">[1]</a></sup> which coincides with the annual celebration of the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_revolution_of_1952" title="Egyptian revolution of 1952">Egyptian revolution of 1952</a> when the modern republic of Egypt was declared, ending the period of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Egypt" title="Kingdom of Egypt">Kingdom of Egypt</a>. </p>
      <p>Government offices and ministries in Egypt rest on <a href="/wiki/Friday" title="Friday">Fridays</a> of each week. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Bank" title="Bank">banks</a> and many institutes have non-working days on <a href="/wiki/Saturday" title="Saturday">Saturdays</a> too which is an official resting-day<sup id="cite_ref-MFA_country_profile_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MFA_country_profile-1">[1]</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Sunday" title="Sunday">Sundays</a> which is not official but commonly used as a resting-day by non-governmental institutes and shops with <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> religious observance. Some <a href="/wiki/Barber" title="Barber">barbershops</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hairdressers" class="mw-redirect" title="Hairdressers">hairdressers</a> close their shops on <a href="/wiki/Monday" title="Monday">Mondays</a> instead of Friday, Saturday and Sunday when they keep their shops open. </p>

         <td>January 25 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Egyptian_revolution_of_2011" title="Egyptian revolution of 2011">Revolution Day 2011</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><br><a href="/wiki/National_Police_Day_(Egypt)" title="National Police Day (Egypt)">National Police Day</a> </td> 
         <td>عيد ثورة 25 يناير<br>عيد الشرطة </td> 
         <td>Celebrates the day of the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_revolution_of_2011" title="Egyptian revolution of 2011">Egyptian revolution of 2011</a>, protesting the 30-year rule of <a href="/wiki/Hosni_Mubarak" title="Hosni Mubarak">Hosni Mubarak</a>.<br>Celebrates the anniversary of Police officers resistance against the British Army in 1952 during the final months of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Egypt" title="Kingdom of Egypt">colonial era</a>. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>April 25 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Sinai_Liberation_Day" title="Sinai Liberation Day">Sinai Liberation Day</a> </td> 
         <td>عيد تحرير سيناء </td> 
         <td>Celebrates the final withdrawal of all <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> military forces from the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> in 1982. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr>

         <td><a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a> </td> 
         <td>عيد العمال </td> 
         <td><br> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>June 30 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/June_2013_Egyptian_protests" title="June 2013 Egyptian protests">30 June Day</a> <sup id="cite_ref-al-ahram-30-june_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-al-ahram-30-june-3">[3]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>عيد ثورة 30 يونيو </td> 
         <td>Observes the <a href="/wiki/June_2013_Egyptian_protests" title="June 2013 Egyptian protests">June 2013 Egyptian protests</a>, which saw President <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Morsi" title="Mohamed Morsi">Mohamed Morsi</a> <a href="/wiki/2013_Egyptian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="2013 Egyptian coup d'état">deposed by the military</a> a few days later. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>July 23 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Revolution_Day_(Egypt)" title="Revolution Day (Egypt)">Revolution Day</a> </td> 
         <td>عيد ثورة 23 يوليو </td> 
         <td>Celebrates the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_Revolution_of_1952" class="mw-redirect" title="Egyptian Revolution of 1952">Egyptian Revolution of 1952</a> which led to the declaration of the modern republic of Egypt. This is considered the <a href="/wiki/National_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="National Day">National Day</a> of Egypt.<sup id="cite_ref-MFA_country_profile_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MFA_country_profile-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Official_Holidays_Egypt_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Official_Holidays_Egypt-4">[4]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>October 6 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Day" title="Armed Forces Day">Armed Forces Day</a> </td> 
         <td>عيد القوات المسلحة </td> 
         <td>Celebrates Egypt's initial military success in the <a href="/wiki/October_War" class="mw-redirect" title="October War">October War</a> which led to the liberation of the land of <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai</a> from occupation back to Egyptian sovereignty. </td>
        </tr>
       </tbody>
      </table>
