   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Eritrea</h1>

      <p>There are approximately sixteen nationally recognized <b>public holidays in <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a></b>, a country in the <a href="/wiki/Horn_of_Africa" title="Horn of Africa">Horn of Africa</a>. The primary <a href="/wiki/National_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="National Day">national holiday</a> is <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence Day">Independence Day</a> (24/05), Martyrs Day (20/06) and Revolution Day (September 1st - 01/09). Other commemorative public holidays include Fenkil Day -- commemorating the Battle of Massawa (February 10 - 10/02) and Afabet Day -- commemorating the Battle of Afabet (20/03). Additional holidays follow the calendar of the <a href="/wiki/Eritrean_Orthodox_Tewahedo_Church" title="Eritrean Orthodox Tewahedo Church">Eritrean Orthodox Tewahedo Church</a> and the two holy Eids (Eid Aladaha and Eid Al-Fitir) <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> holidays are observed as public holidays in Eritrea. </p>
