      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays">Holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Nicaragua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

         <td><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a><sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>Many Nicaraguans celebrate New Year's Day with their families, although it is usual to celebrate it with your friends in a bar or in a party too. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>February 1 </td> 
         <td>Air Force Day </td> 
         <td>Held on 1 February in honor of the nation’s air force. (Celebrated only governmentally) </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>March or April </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Maundy_Thursday" title="Maundy Thursday">Holy Thursday</a><sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>The Last Supper of Jesus, national holiday </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>March of April </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a><sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>The <a href="/wiki/Crucifixion_of_Jesus" title="Crucifixion of Jesus">Crucifixion of Jesus</a>, national holiday </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>First Sunday after the first full moon after 21 March </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a><sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>Resurrection of Jesus, national holiday </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>May 1 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a><sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>Celebrated nationally on the first of May. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>May 27 </td> 
         <td>Army Day </td> 
         <td>Held on May 27 in honor of Nicaragua's army. (Celebrated only governmentally) </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>July 19 </td> 
         <td>Liberation Day/<a href="/wiki/FSLN" class="mw-redirect" title="FSLN">FSLN</a> <a href="/wiki/Revolution_Day" title="Revolution Day">Revolution Day</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>Celebrated on 19 July on a national level. It marks the day that the National Liberation Army defeated the <a href="/wiki/Somoza" class="mw-redirect" title="Somoza">Somoza</a> dictatorship in the <a href="/wiki/Nicaraguan_Revolution" title="Nicaraguan Revolution">Nicaraguan Revolution</a>. In recent years it is mostly a political celebration for members of the <a href="/wiki/Sandinista_National_Liberation_Front" title="Sandinista National Liberation Front">FSLN</a>. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>July 25 </td> 
         <td><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fiesta_de_Santiago&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fiesta de Santiago (page does not exist)">Fiesta de Santiago</a> </td> 
         <td>Celebrated on July 25 in Boaco, Somoto and Managua. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>July 26 </td> 
         <td>Fiesta de <a href="/wiki/Saint_Anne" title="Saint Anne">Santa Ana</a> </td> 
         <td>Celebrated on July 26 in Nandaime, Niquinohomo, Moyogalpa and Ometepe. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>August 1 and 10 </td> 
         <td><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fiesta_de_Santo_Domingo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fiesta de Santo Domingo (page does not exist)">Fiesta de Santo Domingo</a> </td> 
         <td>Managuans celebrate <a href="/wiki/Saint_Dominic" title="Saint Dominic">Santo Domingo de Guzmán</a> (Patron saint) </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>August 27 </td> 
         <td><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crab_Soup_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Crab Soup Day (page does not exist)">Crab Soup Day</a> </td> 
         <td>Residents of the Corn Islands celebrate <a href="/w/index.php?title=Abolition_of_slavery_of_the_Corn_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Abolition of slavery of the Corn Islands (page does not exist)">Abolition of slavery of the Corn Islands</a> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>September 14 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Jacinto_(1856)" title="Battle of San Jacinto (1856)">Battle of San Jacinto</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </td> 
         <td>Celebrated on the national level. It is held on the anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Jacinto_(1856)" title="Battle of San Jacinto (1856)">Battle of San Jacinto (1856)</a>. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>September 15 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence Day">Independence Day</a> </td> 
         <td>A national holiday held on September 15 to celebrate <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Independence_of_Central_America" title="Act of Independence of Central America">Act of Independence of Central America</a> from Spain in 1821. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>September 30 </td> 
         <td>Fiesta de San Jerónimo </td> 
         <td>Catholics of the cities of <a href="/wiki/Masaya" title="Masaya">Masaya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bluefields" title="Bluefields">Bluefields</a> celebrate <a href="/wiki/Saint_Jerome" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Jerome">Saint Jerome</a>. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>October 12 </td> 
         <td>Indigenous Resistance Day </td> 
         <td>Formerly Columbus Day; Highlights the struggle of native peoples against European colonialism.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>November 2 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/All_Souls%27_Day" title="All Souls' Day">All Souls' Day</a><br><a href="/wiki/Day_of_the_Dead" title="Day of the Dead">Day of the Dead</a> </td> 
         <td>Nicaraguans commemorate their deceased relatives by going to the cemeteries to leave flowers and pray to the graves of their relatives. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>December 7/8 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Immaculate_Conception" title="Immaculate Conception">Immaculate Conception<br>(La Griteria Immaculate)</a> </td> 
         <td>In the night of December 7, Catholics of Nicaragua celebrate La Gritería, a holiday dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Immaculate_Conception" title="Immaculate Conception">Immaculate Conception</a>, the patroness of the country, and on December 8 they usually go to <a href="/wiki/Mass_in_the_Catholic_Church" title="Mass in the Catholic Church">Mass</a>. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>December 24 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">Christmas Eve</a> </td> 
         <td>Nicaraguans usually reunite with their family and wait until it is Christmas. You can hear fireworks through all the night. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>December 25 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> </td> 
         <td>Celebrated internationally. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>December 31 </td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a> </td> 
         <td>At 12am Nicaraguans celebrate with fireworks (Spanish: <a href="/w/index.php?title=P%C3%B3lvoras&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pólvoras (page does not exist)">pólvoras</a>, cohetes). </td>
        </tr>
