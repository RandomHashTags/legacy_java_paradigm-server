      <p> In <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, a <b>public holiday</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">feriado</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Calendar_date" title="Calendar date">calendar date</a>, legally recognised and defined in the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Code" class="mw-redirect" title="Labour Code">Labour Code</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Concordat_of_2004" title="Concordat of 2004">Concordat of 2004</a>, on which most businesses and non-essential services are closed. On some of these dates, public commemorative festivities are traditionally held. </p>
      <p>Public holidays in Portugal are a mixture of select religious (<a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a>) observances and days that have national historical or cultural significance. These dates have changed over time: currently, there are 13 mandatory holidays and one optional (<a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a>) that has to be specifically designated as a day off work (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">tolerância de ponto</i>) each year by government decree. Specific dates may alternatively be observed only at <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Regions_of_Portugal" title="Autonomous Regions of Portugal">region</a> or <a href="/wiki/List_of_municipalities_of_Portugal" title="List of municipalities of Portugal">municipal</a> level. </p>

      <h2><span id="Revoked_holidays_in_2013.E2.80.932015"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Revoked_holidays_in_2013–2015">Revoked holidays in 2013–2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Portugal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Revoked holidays in 2013–2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In 2012, the Coalition government of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Passos_Coelho" title="Pedro Passos Coelho">Pedro Passos Coelho</a> controversially revoked four holidays – two civilian holidays (Republic Day and Restoration of Independence) and two religious ones (Corpus Christi and All Saints Day). The move was effective from 2013 onwards and was presented as a measure to increase productivity, in the context of the <a href="/wiki/Economic_Adjustment_Programme_for_Portugal" title="Economic Adjustment Programme for Portugal">2011–2014 Troika bailout to Portugal</a> (even though that measure was never requested by the Memorandum of Understanding). Public debate and re-evaluation were scheduled in 2018 for the religious holidays. </p>
      <p>The four holidays were eventually restored by the government of <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Costa" title="António Costa">António Costa</a>, in January 2016. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_holidays">Local holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Portugal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Local holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
