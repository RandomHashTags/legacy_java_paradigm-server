      <p>The following are the <b>national <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holidays</a></b> and other observances of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. </p>
      <p>Though holidays of many faiths are respected, public holidays usually include most Catholic based holidays. Historic holidays include the celebration of the May Revolution (25 May), Independence Day (9 July), <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Argentina" title="Flag of Argentina">National Flag</a> Day (20 June) and the death of <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_San_Mart%C3%ADn" title="José de San Martín">José de San Martín</a> (17 August). </p>
      <p>The extended family gathers on Christmas Eve at around 9 p.m. for dinner, music, and often dancing. Candies are served just before midnight, when the fireworks begin. They also open gifts from Papá Noel (Father Christmas or "Santa Claus"). <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a> is also marked with fireworks. Other widely observed holidays include <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">Labor Day</a> (1 May) and Veterans Day (formerly <a href="/wiki/Malvinas_Day" title="Malvinas Day">Malvinas Day</a>, 2 April). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_holidays_in_2019">Public holidays in 2019</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Public holidays in 2019">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The "movable holidays" whose dates coincide with Tuesdays and Wednesdays will be moved to the previous Monday. Those that coincide with Thursday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday will be moved to the following Monday.       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bicentennial_holidays">Bicentennial holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bicentennial holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>As part of the celebration of the Independence Day Bicentennial, the following extraordinary and one-time holidays were arranged: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>February 27, 2012: Bicentenary of the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Argentina" title="Flag of Argentina">Flag of Argentina</a> and the first pledge to it.</li> 
       <li>September 24, 2012: Bicentenary of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Battle of Tucumán">Battle of Tucumán</a>.</li> 
       <li>January 31, 2013: Bicentenary of the inaugural session of the <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_the_Year_XIII" title="Assembly of the Year XIII">Assembly of the Year XIII</a>.</li> 
       <li>February 20, 2013: Bicentenary of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Salta" title="Battle of Salta">Battle of Salta</a>.</li> 
       <li>July 9, 2016: Bicentennial of the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Argentine Declaration of Independence">Argentine Declaration of Independence</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_observances">Other observances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other observances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Teacher%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Teacher's Day">Teacher's Day</a> (<i>Día del Maestro</i>) on September 11, commemorating the death of <a href="/wiki/Domingo_Faustino_Sarmiento" title="Domingo Faustino Sarmiento">D. F. Sarmiento</a>; only observed by primary school students.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Spring_Day_(Argentina)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spring Day (Argentina)">Student's Day</a>/<a href="/wiki/Spring_Day_(Argentina)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spring Day (Argentina)">Spring Day</a> (<i>Día del Estudiante</i>/<i>Día de la Primavera</i>) on September 21; only observed by high-school and university students.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/D%C3%ADa_del_Amigo" class="mw-redirect" title="Día del Amigo">Friend's day</a> (<i>Día del Amigo</i>) on July 20; unofficial.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Mothers%27_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Mothers' Day">Mothers' Day</a> (Third Sunday of October).</li> 
       <li>The Argentinan Animals' Day (April 29) predates <a href="/wiki/World_Animal_Day" title="World Animal Day">World Animal Day</a> (October 4) by 17 years. It was first held in 1908 on the initiative of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ignacio_Lucas_Albarrac%C3%ADn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ignacio Lucas Albarracín (page does not exist)">Ignacio Lucas Albarracín</a>, director of the <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires_Eco-Park_(formerly_Zoo)" title="Buenos Aires Eco-Park (formerly Zoo)">Zoological Garden</a> and president of the Animal Protection Association of <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>. Initially the day was observed on April 2, it was moved after Albarricín died on April 29, 1926 to coincide with this day.<sup id="cite_ref-arge_29de_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arge_29de-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Fathers%27_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Fathers' Day">Fathers' Day</a> (Third Sunday of June).</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Day" title="Children's Day">Children's Day</a> (Third Sunday of August).</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Navy_Day" title="Navy Day">Navy Day</a> on May 17, for the victory at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Buceo" title="Battle of Buceo">Battle of Buceo</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Lorenzo" title="Battle of San Lorenzo">Battle of San Lorenzo</a> anniversary on February 3</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Loyalty_Day_(Argentina)" title="Loyalty Day (Argentina)">Loyalty Day</a> and Television Day on October 17</li>
      </ul>
