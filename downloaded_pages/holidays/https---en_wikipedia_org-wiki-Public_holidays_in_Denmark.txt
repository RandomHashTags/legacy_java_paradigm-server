Elderly, middle class and right-of-centre people often regard this day more important than May 1. With few exceptions, all shops stay closed on <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Grundlovsdag</i></i> by law.<sup id="cite_ref-Lukkelov_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lukkelov-1">[1]</a></sup> </td>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_special_days">Other special days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Other special days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Some of these days derive from politics, and some from Roman Catholic traditions that predate the current national church. Some are simply the Scandinavian tradition of starting the celebrations of a special day on the evening before the actual day. </p> 
      <table class="wikitable"> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <th>Date</th> 
         <th>English Name</th> 
         <th>Danish Name</th> 
         <th>Notes </th>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>5 February</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Mary,_Crown_Princess_of_Denmark" title="Mary, Crown Princess of Denmark">Crown Princess Mary's birthday</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Kronprinsesse Marys fødselsdag</i></i></td> 
         <td>This is not celebrated a lot, yet, but one day she may become the Queen (Consort) of Denmark.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>6 February</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Princess_Marie_of_Denmark" title="Princess Marie of Denmark">Princess Marie's birthday</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Prinsesse Maries fødselsdag</i></i></td> 
         <td>Outside of the royal house there is no particular tradition for celebrating on this day.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>Seven weeks before <a href="/wiki/Easter_Sunday" class="mw-redirect" title="Easter Sunday">Easter Sunday</a></td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Fastelavn" title="Fastelavn">Fastelavn</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Fastelavn</i></i></td> 
         <td>Diminished version of the catholic <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a>. On the following day, <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Fastelavnsmandag</i></i>, children go to school dressed up in costume and go door-to-door for candy and sweets. A popular baked good associated with the day is <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Fastelavnsbolle</i></i> (lit. Fastelavns bun), a round sweet roll usually covered with icing and filled with cream.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>1 April</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/April_Fools%27_Day" title="April Fools' Day">April Fools' Day</a></td> 
         <td><i>1. April</i></td> 
         <td>The day is marked by the commission of hoaxes and other practical jokes of varying sophistication on friends, enemies and neighbors, or sending them on fools' errands, the aim of which is to embarrass the gullible.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>9 April</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/German_invasion_of_Denmark_(1940)" title="German invasion of Denmark (1940)">German invasion of Denmark</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Danmarks besættelse</i></i></td> 
         <td>Historic date. This was the day when Germany invaded Denmark in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Flags on flagpoles must be at half mast until 12:00, to indicate the mourning, after that it goes to full mast to indicate that Denmark is a free country today.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>16 April</td> 
         <td>Birthday of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Margrethe_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Margrethe II">Queen Margrethe II</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Dronningens fødselsdag</i></i></td> 
         <td>The Queen is usually celebrated in some places, and she will appear either on her balcony at <a href="/wiki/Amalienborg_Palace" class="mw-redirect" title="Amalienborg Palace">Amalienborg Palace</a> or outside of <a href="/wiki/Marselisborg_Palace" title="Marselisborg Palace">Marselisborg Palace</a> depending on where she spends her birthday.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>1 May</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/International_Workers%27_Day" title="International Workers' Day">International Workers' Day</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Arbejdernes kampdag / 1. maj</i></i> </td> 
         <td>Many people attend political meetings in the morning arranged by the labour unions or the labour parties, afterwards demonstrations are held all over the country, going from the place of the meeting and joining each other along the way to a joint meeting place, often a park. The demonstrations can differ in size from a few hundred to ten-thousands depending on the city and organization.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <p>The day is also known as <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Arbejdernes kamp og festdag</i></i> (Workers day of struggle and celebration) referring to the celebration of the past victories of the workers movement, especially the 8-hours working day. Many, both families and young people, meet at the sites of the political meetings holding picnics and drink beer and other alcoholic beverages. <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>'s <i lang="da" title="Danish-language text"><a href="/wiki/F%C3%A6lledparken" title="Fælledparken">Fælledparken</a></i> is well known for its annual May 1 celebrations, gathering an average of a hundred thousand people at the meeting of the central Labour Union "<a href="/wiki/LO,_The_Danish_Confederation_of_Trade_Unions" class="mw-redirect" title="LO, The Danish Confederation of Trade Unions">LO</a>". This is a full holiday for blue collar workers, but not for white collar workers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>5 May</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/German_surrender_at_L%C3%BCneburg_Heath" title="German surrender at Lüneburg Heath">Liberation day</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Danmarks befrielse</i></i> </td> 
         <td>Historic date. This was the day that the German forces surrendered in Denmark under <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. However, the island of Bornholm was not liberated on this date - instead, the occupation continued until the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> liberated the island. Afterwards the <a href="/wiki/USSR" class="mw-redirect" title="USSR">USSR</a> held control of the island for a time, before it were rejoined with the rest of Denmark. The day is marked by public memorial ceremonies for fallen members of the Danish resistance, and by demonstrations of the left-wing, both in memory of the communist resistance fighters and also carrying slogans of peace and solidarity linking the struggle in the past with new ones today.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>Second Sunday of May</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother's Day">Mother's Day</a></td> 
         <td><i>Mors dag</i></td> 
         <td> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>26 May</td> 
         <td>Birthday of <a href="/wiki/Frederik,_Crown_Prince_of_Denmark" title="Frederik, Crown Prince of Denmark">Crown Prince Frederik</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Kronprins Frederiks fødselsdag</i></i></td> 
         <td>This is not celebrated a lot, yet, but one day he will become the King of Denmark.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>

         <td><i>Fars dag</i></td> 
         <td>Coincides with Constitution Day. </td>
        </tr>

         <td>7 June</td> 
         <td>Birthday of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Joachim_of_Denmark" title="Prince Joachim of Denmark">Prince Joachim</a></td>

         <td> </td>

         <td>15 June</td> 
         <td>Day of Valdemar and Reunion day</td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text"><a href="https://da.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valdemarsdag" class="extiw" title="da:Valdemarsdag">Valdemarsdag</a> og Genforeningsdag</i></i></td> 
         <td>Celebration of <a href="/wiki/Valdemar_II_of_Denmark" title="Valdemar II of Denmark">Valdemar II of Denmark</a>'s victory in a battle in <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> in 1219, at which Denmark's national flag <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Denmark" title="Flag of Denmark">Dannebrog</a> fell from the skies. It is also the date on which Danes celebrate that <i lang="da" title="Danish-language text"><a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8nderjylland" class="mw-redirect" title="Sønderjylland">Sønderjylland</a></i> in 1920 was reunited with the rest of Denmark after a referendum, thus held in high regard in that part of the country. Next to <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Grundlovsdag</i></i>, an equivalence to an actual national day, but is less widely known and celebrated today than before.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr>

         <td>23 June</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Midsummer" title="Midsummer">Saint John's Eve</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text"><a href="https://da.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sankthans" class="extiw" title="da:Sankthans">Sankt Hans aften</a></i></i></td> 
         <td>Pre-Christianity celebration day, celebrating summer solstice on June 24. <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Sankt Hans</i></i> (<i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Johannes</i></i>) is the Danish name of St. John the Baptist. The day is celebrated with a bonfire on the evening before (see <a href="/wiki/Midsummer#Denmark" title="Midsummer">Denmark section under Midsummer</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>

         <td>31 October</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Allehelgensaften</i></i></td> 
         <td>According to superstition, the 31 October is the night when witches, ghosts and dark forces are set loose to disgrace the saints celebrated on the following <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Allehelgensdag</i></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>

        <tr> 
         <td>10 November</td> 
         <td>The eve before <a href="/wiki/St._Martin%27s_Day#Denmark" title="St. Martin's Day">Saint Martin's day</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Mortensaften</i></i> </td> 
         <td>11 November is a Catholic day. <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Sankt Morten</i></i> is the Danish name of Saint Martin of Tours. According to legend, Martin was forced to become a bishop by his parishioners and tried to hide in a barn. However, the noise of the geese gave him away. For this reason, but probably in reality because of the goose slaughtering season, it is tradition to eat a goose dinner, although over time duck has become a more practical dish on this occasion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>13 December</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucy%27s_Day" title="Saint Lucy's Day">Saint Lucy's Day</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Luciadag</i></i></td> 
         <td>Catholic day that was located on winter solstice before the European calendar reform. Revived in Sweden in 1928, and in Denmark from the 1940s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </td>

        <tr> 
         <td>24 December</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">Christmas Eve</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Juleaften</i></i></td> 
         <td>The celebration of Christmas in Denmark starts in the evening, traditionally with a Christmas tree, exchanging presents and having dinner with the family. With few exceptions, all shops stay closed by law on <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Juleaftensdag</i></i>, the day of <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Juleaften</i></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lukkelov_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lukkelov-1">[1]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>31 December</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a></td> 
         <td><i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Nytårsaften</i></i></td> 
         <td>Mostly celebrated with friends or family, a homemade gourmet dinner and often liberal amounts of alcohol. The Queen traditionally holds a televised speech at 6pm. Midnight is celebrated with <a href="/wiki/Champagne_(wine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Champagne (wine)">Champagne</a>, <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text"><a href="/wiki/Kransekage" class="mw-redirect" title="Kransekage">kransekage</a></i></i> (an <a href="/wiki/Almond" title="Almond">almond</a> cake consisting of piled rings) and private displays of <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a>. With few exceptions, all shops stay closed by law from 3pm on <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Nytårsaftensdag</i></i>, the day of <i><i lang="da" title="Danish-language text">Nytårsaften</i></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lukkelov_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lukkelov-1">[1]</a></sup> </td>
