      <p>Public offices and most private businesses in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> operate six days a week and only close on <a href="/wiki/Saturday" title="Saturday">Saturdays</a>. <a href="/wiki/International_organizations" class="mw-redirect" title="International organizations">International organizations</a> have their own rules, and normally close on <a href="/wiki/Saturday" title="Saturday">Saturdays</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunday" title="Sunday">Sundays</a>. Government holidays for the upcoming <a href="/wiki/Calendar_year" title="Calendar year">calendar year</a> are published in Nepal Gazette. Nepal celebrates a number of religious and non-religious holidays. On most of these holidays, most government offices and private institutions are closed, although is not mandatory for privately owned businesses to close and international organizations may operate their own calendar. </p>
      <p>Some of these events are region, religion or gender specific. For example, a certain holiday in Nepal can only be for women. </p>
      <p>The longest consecutive public holiday in Nepal is during <a href="/wiki/Dasain" class="mw-redirect" title="Dasain">Vijaya Dashami</a>. On this festival, holidays fall consecutively i.e. from Fulpati to Duwadashi for six days. Ghatasthapana and Kojagrat Purnima holidays are part of this festival but are separate from the six-day holiday. These festival holidays do not fall on the same calendar date every year, as they are celebrated on the basis of Lunar dates also known as <a href="/wiki/Tithi" title="Tithi">tithi</a>. Holidays such as Loktantra Diwas (Democracy Day) and Republic day are celebrated on the basis of both the <a href="/wiki/Vikram_Samvat" title="Vikram Samvat">Bikram Sambat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> dates. </p>
