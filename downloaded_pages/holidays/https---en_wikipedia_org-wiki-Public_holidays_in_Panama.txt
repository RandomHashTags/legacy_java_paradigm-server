      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays">Holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Panama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Panama's national public holidays are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>January 1 <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a></li> 
       <li>January 9 <a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Panama)" title="Martyrs' Day (Panama)">Martyrs' Day</a>, anniversary of the riots over sovereignty in the <a href="/wiki/Canal_Zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Canal Zone">Canal Zone</a> in 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><i><a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnaval</a></i> Tuesday: The Tuesday before Ash Wednesday</li> 
       <li><i>Viernes Santo</i> - <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a></li> 
       <li>May 1 <a href="/wiki/International_Workers%27_Day" title="International Workers' Day">International Workers' Day</a> - Labour Day - May Day</li> 
       <li>July 1 (every 5 years) presidential inauguration</li> 
       <li>November 3 Separation Day (from Colombia, 1903).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>November 4 <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Panama" title="Flag of Panama">Flag Day</a> -Observed only by Public Agencies and all schools. Not a holiday for the private sector.</li> 
       <li>November 5 <a href="/wiki/Col%C3%B3n,_Panama" title="Colón, Panama">Colón Day</a>, celebrates the day in 1903 when citizens of Colón stopped the advance of the Colombian army on <a href="/wiki/Panama_City" title="Panama City">Panama City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>November 10 "Primer Grito de Independencia de la Villa de los Santos" celebrating <a href="/wiki/The_Gesture_of_Rufina_Alfaro" class="mw-redirect" title="The Gesture of Rufina Alfaro">The Gesture of Rufina Alfaro</a> and the uprising in the <a href="/wiki/Villa_de_los_Santos" class="mw-redirect" title="Villa de los Santos">Villa de los Santos</a> against Spain.</li> 
       <li>November 28 <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence Day">Independence Day</a> (from Spain, 1821)</li> 
       <li>December 8 <a href="/wiki/Mothers%27_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Mothers' Day">Mothers' Day</a> &amp; Feast of the <a href="/wiki/Immaculate_Conception" title="Immaculate Conception">Immaculate Conception</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>December 25 <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a></li>
      </ul>
