      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Holiday" title="Holiday">Holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a></b> are regulated by the Non-working Days Act of 18 January 1951 (Ustawa z dnia 18 stycznia 1951 o dniach wolnych od pracy; Journal of Laws 1951 No. 4, Item 28). The Act, as amended in 2010, currently defines thirteen <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holidays</a>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="May_Holidays">May Holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Poland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: May Holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Under <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="People's Republic of Poland">communist rule</a>, the 1st of May was celebrated as Labour Day with government-endorsed parades, concerts and similar events. The holiday carried over to present day Poland, albeit with a neutral "State Holiday" name. In addition, the 3rd of May was created as Constitution Day. The May holidays (1st, 2nd and 3 May) are called "Majówka" in Polish, a pun made from the May month name (it can be translated as <i>May-day picnic</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_and_state_holidays">National and state holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Poland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National and state holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The following are <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">national and state holidays</a> in Poland, although they are normally working days unless declared a <b>public holiday</b>: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>February 19 - Polish Science Day, established in 2020</li> 
       <li>March 1 - National "<a href="/wiki/Cursed_soldiers" title="Cursed soldiers">cursed soldiers</a>" Remembrance Day, <i>Narodowy Dzień Pamięci "Żołnierzy Wyklętych"</i>, established in 2011</li> 
       <li>March 24 - National Day for the Remembrance of Poles Rescuing Jews under <a href="/wiki/German-occupied_Poland" title="German-occupied Poland">Nazi German Occupation</a>, established in 2018</li> 
       <li>April 14 - Baptism of Poland Day, established in 2019</li> 
       <li><b>May 1</b> - State Holiday, informally called <a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a>, <i>Święto Państwowe</i>, <b>public holiday</b>, established in 1950</li> 
       <li><b>May 3</b> - <a href="/wiki/May_3rd_Constitution_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="May 3rd Constitution Day">May 3rd Constitution Day</a>, <i>Święto Narodowe Trzeciego Maja</i>, <b>public holiday</b>, established originally in 1919, disestablished in 1946, then established again in 1990</li> 
       <li>May 8 - <a href="/wiki/Victory_Day" title="Victory Day">Victory Day</a>, <i>Narodowy Dzień Zwycięstwa</i>, established in 2015</li> 
       <li>July 12 - Day of Struggle and Martyrdom of the Polish Villages, established in 2017</li> 
       <li>August 1 - National <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Uprising" title="Warsaw Uprising">Warsaw Uprising</a> Remembrance Day, <i>Narodowy Dzień Pamięci Powstania Warszawskiego</i>, established in 2009</li> 
       <li>August 31 - Day of Solidarity and Freedom, <i>Dzień Solidarności i Wolności</i>, set on the anniversary of <a href="/wiki/August_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="August Agreement">August Agreement</a> from 1980, established in 2005</li> 
       <li>October 19 - National Day of Remembrance for Steadfast Clergy, established in 2018</li> 
       <li><b>November 11</b> - <a href="/wiki/National_Independence_Day_(Poland)" title="National Independence Day (Poland)">National Independence Day</a>, <i>Narodowe Święto Niepodległości</i>, <b>public holiday</b>, established originally in 1937, disestablished in 1945, then established again in 1989</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_national_and_state_holidays">Former national and state holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Poland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Former national and state holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li>May 9 - <a href="/wiki/Victory_Day_(9_May)" title="Victory Day (9 May)">Victory Day (9 May)</a>, established in 1945, moved to 8 May in 2015 (public holiday for the last time in 2014)</li> 
       <li>July 22 - <a href="/wiki/National_Day_of_the_Rebirth_of_Poland" title="National Day of the Rebirth of Poland">National Day of the Rebirth of Poland</a> (<i>Narodowe Święto Odrodzenia Polski</i>), set on the anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/PKWN_Manifesto" title="PKWN Manifesto">PKWN Manifesto</a>'s signing, established in 1945, disestablished on 28 April 1990 (celebrated for the last time in 1989)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>November 7 - <a href="/wiki/October_Revolution_Day" title="October Revolution Day">Great October Socialist Revolution Day</a> (<i>Wielka Październikowa Rewolucja Socjalistyczna</i>), established in 1945, disestablished in 1990 (The Russian minority and Polish communist loyalists continues to celebrate this holiday)</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_holidays">Other holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Poland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays_declared_by_statute">Holidays declared by statute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Poland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Holidays declared by statute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The following are <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">holidays declared by statute</a> in Poland. These holidays are declared in <a href="/wiki/Statute" title="Statute">statute</a> and as such they form a part of law in Poland. However, these holidays are not granted the distinction of national or state holidays. These are normally working days, unless coinciding with a <b>public holiday</b>. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>January 27 – Public Employment Services Worker's Day, <i>Dzień Pracownika Publicznych Służb Zatrudnienia</i>, established in 2010</li> 
       <li>February 8 – Prison Service Day, <i>Święto Służby Więziennej</i>, established in 2010</li> 
       <li>February 22 – Crime Victims Day, <i>Dzień Ofiar Przestępstw</i>, coinciding with <i>European Victims Day</i>, established in 2003</li> 
       <li>May 2 – <a href="/wiki/Flag_Day" title="Flag Day">Flag Day</a>, <i>Dzień Flagi Rzeczypospolitej Polskiej</i>, established in 2004</li> 
       <li>May 2 – Polish Diaspora Day, <i>Dzień Polonii i Polaków za Granicą</i>, established in 2002</li> 
       <li>May 4 – Firefighters Day, <i>Dzień Strażaka</i>, coinciding with <a href="/wiki/International_Firefighters%27_Day" title="International Firefighters' Day">International Firefighters' Day</a>, established in 2002</li> 
       <li>May 16 – <a href="/wiki/Border_Guard_(Poland)" title="Border Guard (Poland)">Border Guard's</a> Day, <i>Święto Straży Granicznej</i>, established in 1991 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a>)</li> 
       <li>May 29 – Veterans of Overseas Military Activities Day, <i>Dzień Weterana Działań poza Granicami Państwa</i>, coinciding with <a href="/wiki/International_Day_of_United_Nations_Peacekeepers" title="International Day of United Nations Peacekeepers">International Day of United Nations Peacekeepers</a>, established in 2011 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a> or <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a>)</li>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays_declared_by_parliamentary_resolution">Holidays declared by parliamentary resolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Poland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Holidays declared by parliamentary resolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The following are <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">holidays declared by parliamentary resolution</a> in Poland. These holidays are declared by a resolution of <a href="/wiki/Sejm" title="Sejm">Sejm</a>. These holidays do not form a part of law in Poland, and consequently are not granted the distinction of national or state holidays. These are normally working days. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>March 23 – Polish-Hungarian Friendship Day, <i>Dzień Przyjaźni Polsko-Węgierskiej</i>, established in 2007 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a> or <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a>)</li> 
       <li>March 24 – National Life Day, <i>Narodowy Dzień Życia</i>, established in 2004 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a> or <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a>)</li> 
       <li>April 13 – <a href="/wiki/Katyn_Massacre" class="mw-redirect" title="Katyn Massacre">Katyn Memorial Day</a>, <i>Dzień Pamięci Ofiar Zbrodni Katyńskiej</i>, set on the anniversary of the discovery of mass graves in Katyn, established in 2007 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a> or <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a>)</li> 
       <li>April 18 – Coma Patients' Day, <i>Dzień Pacjenta w Śpiączce</i>, established in 2012 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a> or <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a>)</li> 
       <li>April 28 – Day for Safety and Health at Work, <i>Dzień Bezpieczeństwa i Ochrony Zdrowia w Pracy</i>, coincides with <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Memorial_Day" title="Workers' Memorial Day">Workers' Memorial Day</a>, established in 2003</li> 
       <li>May 27 – <a href="/wiki/Local_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Local Government">Local Government's</a> Day, <i>Dzień Samorządu Terytorialnego</i>, set on the anniversary of the first local government elections in Poland, established in 2000 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a> or <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a>)</li> 
       <li>May 30 – Foster Care Day, <i>Dzień Rodzicielstwa Zastępczego</i>, established in 2006 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a> or <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a>)</li> 
       <li>June 1 – Day Without Alcohol, <i>Dzień bez Alkoholu</i>, established in 2006 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a> or <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a>)</li> 
       <li>June 4 – Day of Freedom and Citizens' Rights, <i>Dzień Wolności i Praw Obywatelskich</i>, set on the anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Polish_legislative_election,_1989" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish legislative election, 1989">4th of June 1989 Polish elections</a>, established in 2013 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a> or <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a>)</li> 
       <li>June 14 – National Day of Remembrance of Nazi Concentration Camps Victims, <i>Narodowy Dzień Pamięci Ofiar Nazistowskich Obozów Koncentracyjnych</i>, set on the anniversary of the arrival of the first mass transport of political prisoners to <a href="/wiki/Auschwitz_concentration_camp" title="Auschwitz concentration camp">Auschwitz concentration camp</a>, established in 2006 (this may coincide with <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a>)</li> 
       <li>June 28 – National Day of Remembrance of <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84_1956_protests" class="mw-redirect" title="Poznań 1956 protests">Poznań 1956 protests</a>, <i>Narodowy Dzień Pamięci Poznańskiego Czerwca 1956</i>, set on the anniversary of the outbreak of <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84_1956_protests" class="mw-redirect" title="Poznań 1956 protests">Poznań 1956 protests</a>, established in 2006</li> 
       <li>July 11 - National Day of Remembrance of Victims of <a href="/wiki/Massacres_of_Poles_in_Volhynia_and_Eastern_Galicia" title="Massacres of Poles in Volhynia and Eastern Galicia">the Genocide Committed by Ukrainian Nationalists on Citizens of the Second Polish Republic</a>, <i>Narodowy Dzień Pamięci Ofiar Ludobójstwa dokonanego przez ukraińskich nacjonalistów na obywatelach II Rzeczypospolitej Polskiej</i>, set on the anniversary of the "bloody sunday" in <a href="/wiki/Volhynia" title="Volhynia">Volhynia</a>, the culmination of the massacres of Poles in Volhynia, established in 2016</li> 
       <li>August 2 – <a href="/wiki/Genocide_Remembrance_Day_of_the_Roma_and_Sinti" class="mw-redirect" title="Genocide Remembrance Day of the Roma and Sinti">Genocide Remembrance Day of the Roma and Sinti</a>, <i>Dzień Pamięci o Zagładzie Romów i Sinti</i>, set on the anniversary of the Roma and Sinti exterminaton at <a href="/wiki/Auschwitz_concentration_camp" title="Auschwitz concentration camp">Auschwitz concentration camp</a>, established in 2011</li> 
       <li>September 17 – <a href="/wiki/Sybirak" title="Sybirak">Sybirak's</a> Day, <i>Dzień Sybiraka</i>, set on the anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Soviet_invasion_of_Poland" title="Soviet invasion of Poland">Soviet invasion of Poland</a>, established in 1998</li> 
       <li>September 27 – <a href="/wiki/Polish_Underground_State" title="Polish Underground State">Polish Underground State's</a> Day, <i>Dzień Podziemnego Państwa Polskiego</i>, set on the anniversary of the formation of <a href="/wiki/Service_for_Poland%27s_Victory" title="Service for Poland's Victory">Service for Poland's Victory</a>, established in 2013</li> 
       <li>October 4 – <a href="/wiki/World_Animal_Day" title="World Animal Day">Animals' Day</a>, <i>Dzień Zwierząt</i>, coincides with <a href="/wiki/World_Animal_Day" title="World Animal Day">World Animal Day</a>, established in 2006</li> 
       <li>December 13 – <a href="/wiki/Martial_law_in_Poland" title="Martial law in Poland">Martial Law</a> Victims Remembrance Day, <i>Dzień Pamięci Ofiar Stanu Wojennego</i>, set on the anniversary of declaration of <a href="/wiki/Martial_law_in_Poland" title="Martial law in Poland">Martial law in Poland</a>, established in 2002</li>
      </ul>
