      <p><a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a> observes several public <a href="/wiki/Holiday" title="Holiday">holidays</a>. The two weekend days are Friday and Saturday. Annual public holidays include: </p> 
      <table class="wikitable sortable"> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <th>Date</th> 
         <th>English name</th> 
         <th>Local (<a href="/wiki/Arabic_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic Language">Arabic</a>) name</th> 
         <th>Description </th>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>Second Tuesday in February</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/National_Sports_Day" title="National Sports Day">National Sports Day</a></td> 
         <td>اليوم الوطني للرياضة</td> 
         <td>A <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holiday</a>. Started in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>18 December</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Qatar_National_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Qatar National Day">Qatar National Day</a></td> 
         <td>اليوم الوطني لقطر</td> 
         <td>National Day of Qatar.<sup id="cite_ref-q_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-q-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ame_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ame-3">[3]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>1st, 2nd, 3rd <i><a href="/wiki/Shawwal" title="Shawwal">Shawwal</a></i></td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr" title="Eid al-Fitr">Eid al-Fitr</a></td> 
         <td>عيد الفطر</td> 
         <td>Commemorates end of <a href="/wiki/Ramadan" title="Ramadan">Ramadan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-q_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-q-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ame_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ame-3">[3]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td>10th, 11th, 12th <i><a href="/wiki/Dhu_al-Hijjah" title="Dhu al-Hijjah">Zulhijjah</a></i></td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Adha" title="Eid al-Adha">Eid al-Adha</a></td> 
         <td>عيد الأضحى</td> 
         <td>Commemorates <a href="/wiki/Abraham" title="Abraham">Ibrahim</a>'s willingness to sacrifice his son. Also known as the <i>Big Feast</i> (celebrated from the 10th to 13th).<sup id="cite_ref-q_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-q-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ame_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ame-3">[3]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr>
