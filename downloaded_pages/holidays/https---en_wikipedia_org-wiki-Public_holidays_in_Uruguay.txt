      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Moveable_holidays">Moveable holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Uruguay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Moveable holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>According to Uruguayan Law 16,805 with modifications of Law 17,414, <i>the holidays declared by law, subject to the commemoration of them, follow the following scheme</i> (whose commemoration as “moveable holidays”):<sup id="cite_ref-ley-16805_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ley-16805-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ley-17414_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ley-17414-3">[3]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>If coincide on Saturday, Sunday or Monday will be observed in those days.</li> 
       <li>If occur on Tuesday or Wednesday, will be observed on Monday immediately preceding.</li> 
       <li>If occur on Thursday or Friday shall be observed on the Monday immediately following</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>This will not occur with <i>Carnival and Tourism Week, and corresponding to January 1 and 6, May 1, June 19, July 18, August 25, November 2 and December 25, which will continue watching on the day of the week that may occur, whatever the same</i>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence_Day">Independence Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Uruguay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Independence Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Uruguay_of_1830" title="Constitution of Uruguay of 1830">Constitution of Uruguay of 1830</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Historian <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonardo_Borges_(historian)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leonardo Borges (historian) (page does not exist)">Leonardo Borges</a> argues that Independence Day, celebrated on August 25, does not reflect the actual birth of the nation. When the independence of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian Empire">Brazilian Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_the_R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="United Provinces of the Río de la Plata">United Provinces of the Río de la Plata</a> was signed on August 25, 1825, Uruguay remained part of Argentina, the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Oriental" title="Banda Oriental">Banda Oriental</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-independence_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independence-4">[4]</a></sup> Borges says that "[I]t seems like nonsense" to establish August 25 as Independence Day. Ana Ribeiro, Undersecretary of Education and Culture, argues that all such dates are arbitrary, and points out that <a href="/wiki/Bastille_Day" title="Bastille Day">Bastille Day</a> does not celebrate the deternining date of the French Revolution.<sup id="cite_ref-independence_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independence-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Montevideo_(1828)" title="Treaty of Montevideo (1828)">Treaty of Montevideo</a>, signed on August 27, 1828, and ratified on October 4 of the same year, after the <a href="/wiki/Cisplatine_War" title="Cisplatine War">Cisplatine War</a>, granted independence to Banda Oriental, which became Uruguay. </p>
