      <p>There are eleven <b>public holidays in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a></b>. Each major local race and religion have two holidays each in addition to the secular holidays of <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labour_Day_(Singapore)" title="Labour Day (Singapore)">Labour Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Day_(Singapore)" title="National Day (Singapore)">National Day</a>. For the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_New_Year" title="Chinese New Year">Chinese New Year</a> (two days) and the <a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a> holiday <a href="/wiki/Vesak" title="Vesak">Vesak Day</a> (one day) are also observed. For the <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deepavali" class="mw-redirect" title="Deepavali">Deepavali</a> (one day) is celebrated. For the <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>, the two <a href="/wiki/Islamic_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamic holiday">Islamic holidays</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hari_Raya_Puasa" class="mw-redirect" title="Hari Raya Puasa">Hari Raya Aidil Fitri</a> (one day on 1 <a href="/wiki/Shawwal" title="Shawwal">Syawal</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Hari_Raya_Haji" class="mw-redirect" title="Hari Raya Haji">Hari Raya Aidil Adha</a> (one day on 10 <a href="/wiki/Dhu_al-Hijjah" title="Dhu al-Hijjah">Zulhijah</a>) are celebrated. As for the <a href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christians</a>, the two <a href="/wiki/Christian_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian holiday">Christian holidays</a> of <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Christmas Day">Christmas Day</a> (25 December) are celebrated. These public holidays have been recognised since <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>'s 1966 Holidays Act.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_public_holidays_in_Singapore">List of public holidays in Singapore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: List of public holidays in Singapore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are generally 11 public holidays a year. However, due to the differences between the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_calendar" title="Islamic calendar">Islamic calendar</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hari_Raya_Puasa" class="mw-redirect" title="Hari Raya Puasa">Hari Raya Aidil Fitri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hari_Raya_Haji" class="mw-redirect" title="Hari Raya Haji">Hari Raya Aidil Adha</a> are respectively celebrated twice in a Gregorian calendar year every 32 or 33 years. This occurred in 1968 and 2000 for Hari Raya Puasa as well as 1974 and 2006 for Hari Raya Haji, giving Singaporeans 12 public holidays in those years.<sup id="cite_ref-ST_20091026_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ST_20091026-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>If any public holiday falls on a Sunday in Singapore, the following day is declared to be a public holiday if it is not itself already a public holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Thus, since National Day (9 August) in 2015 landed on a Sunday, two additional holidays were given (on August 7 and August 10). The president is empowered to declare any day to be observed as a public holiday in addition to or substitution for any day specified in the schedule to the Holidays Act, and may also declare any day in a year to be observed as an additional public holiday when in that year two public holidays fall on the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <br> If any public holiday falls on a Saturday in Singapore, the following Monday is declared as a school holiday for students in primary &amp; secondary schools, only if that Monday is not a public holiday itself. </p>

      <p>Polling Day (for a general election or a presidential election) is a public holiday. </p>
      <p>Under Section 35 of the Parliamentary Elections Act and Section 17 of the Presidential Elections Act, Polling Day (for a general election or a presidential election) is a public holiday. By-elections are not covered under the act. As per public holidays falling on a Saturday, under the Employment Act, employees not required to work on that day are entitled to one day off in lieu or be given one day's pay.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span id="Workers.27_rights"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Workers'_rights">Workers' rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Singapore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Workers' rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>It is legal for employers to agree to give their employees other holidays in substitution for one or more public holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> No act or thing relating to any <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Singapore" title="Government of Singapore">government</a> department or public authority, any judicial proceeding, any transaction, instrument or any other act or thing is rendered invalid where it is done or executed on a Sunday or public holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Under the Employment Act,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> an employee who is required to work on a public holiday is entitled to an extra day's salary at the basic rate of pay, in addition to the gross rate of pay for that holiday. </p>
      <p>The days observed as general <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> are declared in the schedule to the Holidays Act.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Manpower_(Singapore)" title="Ministry of Manpower (Singapore)">Ministry of Manpower</a>, which issues a yearly list of the dates on which public holidays fall, the holidays were "chosen and agreed upon after close consultation with different community and religious leaders in Singapore".<sup id="cite_ref-ST_20091026_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ST_20091026-2">[2]</a></sup> Other factors taken into account were the impact on business costs and statutory leave provided for under the Employment Act.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Thus, some religious holidays such as <a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mawlid" title="Mawlid">Mawlid</a> (the birthday of the Prophet <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a>), <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thaipusam" title="Thaipusam">Thaipusam</a> were removed from the list of public holidays to improve business competitiveness.<sup id="cite_ref-ST_20091026_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ST_20091026-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
