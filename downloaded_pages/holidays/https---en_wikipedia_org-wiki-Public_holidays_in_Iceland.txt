   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Iceland</h1>

      <p><b>Public holidays</b> in <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> are established by the act of the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Icelandic parliament">Icelandic parliament</a><i>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> The public holidays are the religious holidays of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Iceland" title="Church of Iceland">Church of Iceland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/First_day_of_summer_(Iceland)" title="First day of summer (Iceland)">first day of summer</a>, <a href="/wiki/May_Day" title="May Day">May Day</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_National_Day" title="Icelandic National Day">Icelandic National Day</a>. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">Christmas Eve</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a> are holidays from 1PM.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are also twelve official <a href="/wiki/Flag_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Flag day">flag days</a> in Iceland, some of which are not public holidays. On a flag day all government buildings fly the flag. Although citizens are not obliged to do it, most people do, if they have a flagpole. </p>
