      <p><a href="/wiki/Public_holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Public holidays">Public holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> are largely controlled by government sector employers who are given paid time off. The government holiday schedule mainly benefits employees of government and government regulated businesses. At the discretion of the employer, other non-federal holidays such as <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">Christmas Eve</a> are common additions to the list of paid holidays. </p>
      <p>Major <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> holidays such as <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a> are officially observed, leave is permitted for other religious holidays as well. For example, some school children and employees take days off for <a href="/wiki/Muslim_holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim holidays">Muslim holidays</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Hindu_festivals" title="List of Hindu festivals">Hindu holidays</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox</a> observances according to the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. While not normally taken off work, <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine's Day">Valentine's Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother's Day">Mother's Day</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father's Day">Father's Day</a> are traditionally observed by the <a href="/wiki/Tswana_people" title="Tswana people">Batswana</a>. <span data-ve-clipboard-key="0.5989953683402354-3"> </span> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_holidays">Public holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Public holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A public holiday (also known as "stats" or "general" or "statutory" holiday) in Botswana is legislated through the parliament of Botswana.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Most workers, public and private, are entitled to take the day off with regular pay. However, some employers may require employees to work on such a holiday, but the employee must either receive a day off in lieu of the holiday or must be paid at a premium rate – usually 1½ (known as "time and a half") or twice (known as "double time") the regular pay for their time worked that day, in addition to the holiday pay.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays_with_religious_and_cultural_significance">Holidays with religious and cultural significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Holidays with religious and cultural significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The religious and cultural holidays in Botswana are characterized by a diversity of religious beliefs and practices. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Christian_holidays">Christian holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Botswana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Christian holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Some private businesses and certain other institutions are closed on Good Friday. The financial market and stock market is closed on Good Friday. Most retail stores remain open although some might close early. Public schools and most universities are closed on Good Friday, either as a holiday of its own, or part of spring break. The postal service operates, and banks regulated by the federal government do not close for Good Friday. </p>
      <p>Many companies, including banks, malls, shopping centers and most private retail stores that normally open on Sundays are closed on <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>. </p>
