      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Ghana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ol>
       <li>Introduced by the Ministry of Food and Agriculture, National Farmers' Day was organized as a day’s activity for the nation to honor its hard working <a href="/wiki/Farmer" title="Farmer">farmers</a>, with certificates and prizes, who excelled in their contributions to improve the <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> sector. In 1988, the first Friday of every December was designated by the <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> as <i>Farmers' Day</i> and is celebrated as a statutory Public Holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>With the exception of Farmers' Day which is always on a Friday, when these dates fall at the weekend, the following Monday tends to be declared a holiday.</li> 
       <li>Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Day is celebrated in commemoration of the birthday of <a href="/wiki/Kwame_Nkrumah" title="Kwame Nkrumah">Dr. Kwame Nkrumah</a>, the first President of Ghana.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>The Constitution Day is a newly designated Public Holiday, which will be observed starting on 7 January 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
      </ol> 
      <p>It is not mandatory that all Ghanaian institutions/organizations recognize public holidays. Institutions/organizations that are exempt include; The Ghana National Fire Service, food vendors, some category of hotels, health service providers such as hospitals, dealers in pharmaceutical products etc. </p>
