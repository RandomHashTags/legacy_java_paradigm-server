      <p>There are currently seven official <b><a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland China</a></b>. Each year's holidays are announced about three weeks before the start of the year by the General Office of the <a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="State Council of the People's Republic of China">State Council</a>. A notable feature of mainland Chinese holidays is that weekends are usually swapped with the weekdays next to the actual holiday to create a longer holiday period. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Festivals in China have been around since the Qin Dynasty around 221-206 BC. During the more prosperous <a href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a> from AD 618–907, festivals involved less sacrifice and mystery to more entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[4]</a></sup> Culminating to the modern era Between the 1920s until around the 1970s, the Chinese began observing two sets of holidays, which were the traditional and what became "official", celebrating the accomplishments of the communist regime.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[5]</a></sup> There was then a major reform in 2008, abolishing the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a> <a href="/wiki/Golden_Week_(China)" title="Golden Week (China)">Golden Week</a> and adding three traditional Chinese holidays (<a href="/wiki/Qingming_Festival" title="Qingming Festival">Qingming Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duanwu_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Duanwu Festival">Duanwu Festival</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mid-Autumn_Festival" title="Mid-Autumn Festival">Mid-Autumn Festival</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[6]</a></sup> From at least 2000 until this reform, the Spring Festival public holiday began on New Year's Day itself. From 2008 to 2013 it was shifted back by one day to begin on Chinese New Year's Eve. In 2014, New Year's Eve became a working day again, which provoked hostile discussion by <a href="/wiki/Netizens" class="mw-redirect" title="Netizens">netizens</a> and academics.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj2013_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj2013-23">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-reuters2013_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters2013-24">[8]</a></sup> However, since 2015, Chinese New Year's Eve is usually swapped with nearby weekends so that people need not work on Chinese New Year's Eve. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Holidays in China are complicated and are one of the least predictable among developed nations. In all these holidays, if the holiday lands on a weekend, the days will be reimbursed after the weekend. </p>
      <p>The Chinese New Year and National Day holidays are three days long. The week-long holidays on May (Labor) Day and National Day began in 2000, as a measure to increase and encourage holiday spending. The resulting seven-day or eight-day (if Mid-Autumn Festival is near National Day) holidays are called "<a href="/wiki/Golden_Week_(China)" title="Golden Week (China)">Golden Weeks</a>" (<span lang="zh-Hans"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%BB%84%E9%87%91" class="extiw" title="wikt:黄金">黄金</a><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%91%A8" class="extiw" title="wikt:周">周</a></span>), and have become peak seasons for travel and tourism. In 2008, the Labor Day holiday was shortened to three days to reduce travel rushes to just twice a year, and instead, three traditional Chinese holidays were added. </p>
      <p>Generally, if there is a three-day or four-day (if Mid-Autumn Festival is near National Day) holiday, the government will declare it to be a seven-day or eight-day holiday. However, citizens are required to work during a nearby weekend. Businesses and schools would then treat the affected Saturdays and Sundays as the weekdays that the weekend has been swapped with. Schedules are released late in the year prior and might change during the year. </p>

      <p>Shift the Saturdays and Sundays nearby to make a 7-day holiday. People may need to work for 6 or 7 continuous days before or after the holiday. </p> 
      <h4><span id="National_Day_.28not_near_Mid-Autumn_Festival.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Day_(not_near_Mid-Autumn_Festival)">National Day (not near Mid-Autumn Festival)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: National Day (not near Mid-Autumn Festival)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>Shift the Saturdays and Sundays nearby to make a 7-day holiday. The holiday is from 1 to 7 October. People may need to work for 6 or 7 continuous days before or after the holiday. </p> 
      <h4><span id="New_Year.2C_Tomb-Sweeping_Day.2C_Labour_Day.2C_Dragon_Boat_Festival_and_Mid-Autumn_Festival_.28not_near_National_Day.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Year,_Tomb-Sweeping_Day,_Labour_Day,_Dragon_Boat_Festival_and_Mid-Autumn_Festival_(not_near_National_Day)">New Year, Tomb-Sweeping Day, Labour Day, Dragon Boat Festival and Mid-Autumn Festival (not near National Day)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: New Year, Tomb-Sweeping Day, Labour Day, Dragon Boat Festival and Mid-Autumn Festival (not near National Day)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <ul>
       <li>Wednesday: No weekend shifting. The holiday is only 1 day long. This is to prevent people from working for 7 continuous days since 2014. Sometimes shift the Sundays nearby to make a 4-day holiday. People may need to work for 6 continuous days after the holiday.</li> 
       <li>Tuesday or Thursday: Shift the Saturdays and Sundays nearby to make a 3-day holiday. People may need to work for 6 continuous days before or after the holiday.</li> 
       <li>Saturday or Sunday: The public holiday is transferred to Monday.</li>
      </ul>
