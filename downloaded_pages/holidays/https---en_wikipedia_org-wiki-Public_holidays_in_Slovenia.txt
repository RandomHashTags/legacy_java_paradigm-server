   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Public holidays in Slovenia</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub" class="noprint">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>

    <div id="contentSub2"></div>

      <p> There are two kinds of <b>public holidays in Slovenia</b> – <a href="/wiki/State_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="State holiday">state holidays</a> and work-free days. State holidays are those celebrated by the state. These include <a href="/wiki/Official_functions" class="mw-redirect" title="Official functions">official functions</a> and flying the <a href="/wiki/National_flag_of_Slovenia" class="mw-redirect" title="National flag of Slovenia">national flag</a>. The latter are actually <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Religious_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious holiday">religious holidays</a>, which are equivalent to any Sunday: companies and schools are closed, but there is no official celebration. </p>
      <p>9 of 14 state holiday days are work-free, and there are additional 6 work-free days in Slovenia.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Two of them always fall on Sunday, thus, there are effectively at most 13 work-free days in Slovenia. </p>
      <p>State holidays that are work-free are shown in pale green, while work-free days that are not state holidays (coinciding with Catholic religious holidays) are shown in blue. </p>

      <p>In addition to these, several other holidays are traditionally and popularly celebrated by the people of Slovenia, although not being work-free. The best known are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a> (<i>pust</i>, date varies),</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/International_Women%27s_Day" title="International Women's Day">International Women's Day</a>, 8 March</li> 
       <li>St. George's Day (<i>jurjevanje</i>, the welcoming of spring; 23 April),</li> 
       <li>St. Martin's Day (<i>martinovanje</i>, changing of <a href="/wiki/Must" title="Must">must</a> into wine; 11 November)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Saint_Nicholas_Day" title="Saint Nicholas Day">Saint Nicholas Day</a> (<i>miklavž</i>, when children receive presents; 6 December)</li> 
       <li>Insurrection Day (<i>dan vstaje</i>, 22 July, work-free until 1991)</li>
      </ul>
