      <p>The following is a list of <b>Public holidays in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a></b>, which includes <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> <a href="/wiki/Holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Holidays">holidays</a> and secular holidays. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_holidays">Public holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Public holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a>, 1 January (public holiday)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Ash_Wednesday" title="Ash Wednesday">Ash Wednesday</a>, Between 4 February and 10 March (public holiday)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine's Day">Valentine's Day</a>, 14 February (observance)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a>, Friday of <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week" title="Holy Week">Holy Week</a>, late March or early April(public holiday)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>, Between 21 March and 25 April (observance). <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a> begins.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a>, Monday after Easter(public holiday)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother's Day">Mother's Day</a>, Second Sunday of May (observance)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a>, 23 May (public holiday) People participate in community improvement projects.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Father%27s_Day" title="Father's Day">Father's Day</a>, Third Sunday of May(observance)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Abolitionism in the United Kingdom">Emancipation</a> Day, 1 August (public holiday). Honors the 311,000 slaves freed in 1840.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Jamaica)" title="Independence Day (Jamaica)">Independence Day</a>, 6 August (public holiday). Independence from the British Empire in 1962.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Heroes%27_Day" title="Heroes' Day">National Heroes Day</a>, Third Monday of October (public holiday). Honors <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Bustamante" title="Alexander Bustamante">Alexander Bustamante</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nanny_of_the_Maroons" title="Nanny of the Maroons">Nanny of the Maroons</a>, and five other heroes.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Christmas_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Christmas Day">Christmas Day</a>, 25 December (religious and public holiday)<sup id="cite_ref-holidays_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holidays-1">[1]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a>, 26 December (public holiday)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
      </ul>
