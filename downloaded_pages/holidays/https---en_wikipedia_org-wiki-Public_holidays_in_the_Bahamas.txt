      <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">holidays</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a></b> include the following:<sup id="cite_ref-bahamas_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bahamas-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the Bahamas, holidays that fall on a Saturday or Sunday are typically celebrated on the following Monday. Events that fall on a Tuesday typically are celebrated on the previous Monday. Holidays that fall on Wednesdays or Thursdays (with the exception of Independence Day, Christmas Day, and Boxing Day) are celebrated the following Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-bahamas_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bahamas-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_holidays">Public holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_Bahamas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Public holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <table class="wikitable sortable plainrowheaders"> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <th style="width:15%;">Date </th> 
         <th style="width:20%;">English name </th> 
         <th>Remarks </th>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>January 1</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a></td> 
         <td>First day of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, celebrated with <a href="/wiki/Junkanoo" title="Junkanoo">Junkanoo</a> parades in most islands.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a> sees many beach parties throughout the Bahamas.<sup id="cite_ref-christmas_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-christmas-3">[3]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>January 10</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Majority_Rule" class="mw-redirect" title="Majority Rule">Majority Rule</a> Day</td> 
         <td>Commemorates the day the Bahamian government gained majority rule for the first time, on this day in 1967. It is usually listed with the emancipation of slavery in 1836 and independence from <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> in 1973 as the most important events in the history of the Bahamas.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><br><br>Became an official public holiday in 2014. Celebrated with dancing in streets and feasts of traditional <a href="/wiki/Bahamian_cuisine" title="Bahamian cuisine">Bahamian cuisine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>Friday before Easter Sunday</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a></td> 
         <td>Marks the end of the <a href="/wiki/Lenten" class="mw-redirect" title="Lenten">Lenten</a> season and the beginning of a 4-day holiday weekend. Most Bahamians attend church services and serve fish as the main meal for the day.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>Monday after Easter Sunday</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Easter_Monday" title="Easter Monday">Easter Monday</a></td> 
         <td>The last day of the long Easter holiday weekend. Marks the beginning of the beach <a href="/wiki/Picnic" title="Picnic">picnicking</a> season. Cookouts and <a href="/wiki/Regatta" title="Regatta">regattas</a> are also common.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>Seventh Monday after Easter</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Whit_Monday" title="Whit Monday">Whit Monday</a></td> 
         <td>Marks the end of the Easter cycle, 50 days after Easter Sunday. It is the day after the <a href="/wiki/Feast_of_Pentecost" class="mw-redirect" title="Feast of Pentecost">Feast of Pentecost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>First Friday in June</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Randol_Fawkes" title="Randol Fawkes">Randol Fawkes</a> — <a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">Labour Day</a></td> 
         <td>Established by Fawkes in 1961. The day's festivities start with a large parade in downtown <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a>, commencing around 10:00&nbsp;a.m. The parade is led by bands and Junkanooers, who provide music for the spectators and marchers. The majority of marchers come from <a href="/wiki/Labour_unions" class="mw-redirect" title="Labour unions">labour unions</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_the_Bahamas" title="List of political parties in the Bahamas">political parties</a>. The parade ends at the Southern Recreation Grounds, where leaders of the unions and political parties give speeches.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>July 10</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence Day">Independence Day</a></td> 
         <td>Marks the day Bahamas became a fully independent nation, officially splitting from Great Britain in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> A week-long celebration is held leading up to the day of, when a mix of carnival and Junkanoo parades, <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a>, and speeches of freedom and independence are commonplace throughout the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>First Monday in August</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Emancipation_Day" title="Emancipation Day">Emancipation Day</a>/August Monday</td> 
         <td>Celebrates the emancipation of slaves in 1834. Junkanoo, <a href="/wiki/Beaching_(nautical)" title="Beaching (nautical)">beaching</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sailing" title="Sailing">sailing</a>, and regattas take place throughout the country. Old slave villages such as Gambier and Fox Hill hold their own celebrations.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>Second Monday in October</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/National_Heroes%27_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="National Heroes' Day">National Heroes' Day</a> (formerly <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Day" title="Discovery Day">Discovery Day</a> or <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Day" title="Columbus Day">Columbus Day</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>)</td> 
         <td>Became National Heroes' Day in 2013 and is dedicated to honoring <a href="/wiki/Order_of_National_Hero_(Bahamas)" title="Order of National Hero (Bahamas)">national heroes of the Bahamas</a>. A ceremony is held with a speech from the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_Bahamas" title="Prime Minister of the Bahamas">Prime Minister</a>, and national heroes are celebrated with a week full of activities. Many people take this day to have family gatherings and picnics.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>December 25</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Christmas_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Christmas Day">Christmas Day</a></td> 
         <td>Christmas season in the Bahamas sees many carnivals and festivals throughout the country. Customs have been adopted from other countries, as well, such as gift-giving, feasting, and sending <a href="/wiki/Christmas_card" title="Christmas card">Christmas cards</a>. The holiday season starts with a <a href="/wiki/Christmas_tree" title="Christmas tree">Christmas tree</a> lighting ceremony in Nassau's Pompey Square.<sup id="cite_ref-christmas_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-christmas-3">[3]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr valign="top"> 
         <td>December 26</td> 
         <td><a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a></td> 
         <td>The name comes from the pre-emancipation practice of plantation owners sending boxes to slaves. Usually the wooden boxes were high-quality shipped from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The day is marked with Junkanoo festivals throughout the nation, including the Christmas Junkanoo Festival in Nassau.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </td>
        </tr>
