      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_days">Religious days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religious days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Only the holidays of <a href="/wiki/Ramadan" title="Ramadan">Ramadan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Qurban" title="Qurban">Qurban</a> remain as non-working religious days in Azerbaijan as the country is highly <a href="/wiki/Secular" class="mw-redirect" title="Secular">secular</a> and irreligious.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The religious population of the country, mainly in <a href="/wiki/Nardaran" title="Nardaran">Nardaran</a> and a number of other villages and regions celebrate the <a href="/wiki/Day_of_Ashura" class="mw-redirect" title="Day of Ashura">Day of Ashura</a>, a Shia mourning day in the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_calendar" title="Islamic calendar">Islamic calendar</a>. Religious minorities of the country – mainly <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Christians" class="mw-redirect" title="Orthodox Christians">Orthodox Christians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> - also celebrate notable religious days of their faith.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Despite the fact that the holiday <a href="/wiki/Novruz" class="mw-redirect" title="Novruz">Novruz</a> takes its roots from the religion of <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrianism</a>, almost all Azerbaijanis celebrates it as a holiday of spring. </p>
