      <p><b>Austria</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Österreich</i>; officially called <b>Republic of Austria</b>), is a country in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>. Around Austria there are the countries of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">Liechtenstein</a>. Currently, the chancellor is <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Kurz" title="Sebastian Kurz">Sebastian Kurz</a>. The previous chancellor was <a href="/wiki/Brigitte_Bierlein" title="Brigitte Bierlein">Brigitte Bierlein</a> (2019). Austria has been a <a href="/wiki/State" title="State">member-state</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> since 1955 the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> since 1995 and <a href="/wiki/OPEC" title="OPEC">OPEC</a> since 2019. </p>
      <p>The people in Austria speak <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, a few also speak <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenian_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Slovenian language">Slovenian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>. The capital of Austria is <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> (Wien). </p>
      <p>Austria is more than a thousand years old. Its history can be followed to the <a href="/wiki/Ninth_century" class="mw-redirect" title="Ninth century">ninth century</a>. At that time the first people moved to the land now known as Austria. The name "Ostarrichi" is first written in an official document from <a href="/wiki/996" title="996">996</a>. Since then this word has developed into the Modern German word <i>Österreich,</i> which literally means "East Empire." </p>

      <p>Austria is a <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Neutrality_(international_relations)" title="Neutrality (international relations)">neutral</a> state, that means it does not take part in wars with other countries. It has been in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> since 1955 and in the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> since 1995. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_times">Ancient times</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Ancient times">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Ancient times">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>There has been human settlement in the area that is now Austria for a long time. The first settlers go back to the Paleolithic age. That was the time of the <a href="/wiki/Neanderthal" title="Neanderthal">Neanderthals</a>. They left works of art such as the <a href="/wiki/Venus_of_Willendorf" title="Venus of Willendorf">Venus of Willendorf</a>. In the Neolithic age people were living there to dig for <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">mineral</a> resources, especially <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a>. <a href="/wiki/%C3%96tzi_the_Iceman" title="Ötzi the Iceman">Ötzi</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Mummy" class="mw-redirect" title="Mummy">mummy</a> found in a <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glacier</a> between Austria and Italy, is from that time. In the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> people built bigger settlements and fortresses, especially where there were <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">mineral</a> resources. <a href="/wiki/Salt_mine" title="Salt mine">Salt mining</a> began near <a href="/wiki/Hallstatt" title="Hallstatt">Hallstatt</a>. At that time, <a href="/wiki/Celt" class="mw-redirect" title="Celt">Celts</a> began to form the first states. </p>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Roman_cities_and_their_modern_names">The Roman cities and their modern names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: The Roman cities and their modern names">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: The Roman cities and their modern names">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>The Romans came 15 B.C. to Austria and made the Celtic Regnum Noricum to a province. Modern Austria was part of three provinces, Raetia, Noricum and Pannonia. The border in the north was the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_times">Modern times</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Modern times">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Modern times">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Austria was the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austrian Empire</a> from about 800 to 1867 and was ruled by The House of <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> for most of that period. Between 1867 and 1918 it was a part of <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a>. Then it became a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The <i>First Republic</i> was from 1918 to 1938. From 1938 to 1945 Austria was part of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>. The <i>Second Republic</i> was started in 1945. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Austria is a largely <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountainous</a> country since it is partially in the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>. The high mountainous Alps in the west of Austria flatten somewhat into low lands and plains in the east of the country where the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> flows. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_and_Arts">Music and Arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Music and Arts">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Music and Arts">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Many famous composers were Austrians or born in Austria. There are <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haydn" class="mw-redirect" title="Haydn">Joseph Haydn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franz_Schubert" title="Franz Schubert">Franz Schubert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anton_Bruckner" title="Anton Bruckner">Anton Bruckner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johann_Strauss_I" title="Johann Strauss I">Johann Strauss, Sr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johann_Strauss_II" title="Johann Strauss II">Johann Strauss, Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Mahler" title="Gustav Mahler">Gustav Mahler</a>. In modern times there were <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schoenberg" title="Arnold Schoenberg">Arnold Schoenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anton_Webern" title="Anton Webern">Anton Webern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alban_Berg" title="Alban Berg">Alban Berg</a>, who belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Viennese_School" title="Second Viennese School">Second Viennese School</a>. </p>
      <p>Austria has many <a href="/wiki/Artist" title="Artist">artists</a>, there are <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Klimt" title="Gustav Klimt">Gustav Klimt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oskar_Kokoschka" title="Oskar Kokoschka">Oskar Kokoschka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egon_Schiele" title="Egon Schiele">Egon Schiele</a> or <a href="/wiki/Friedensreich_Hundertwasser" title="Friedensreich Hundertwasser">Friedensreich Hundertwasser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inge_Morath" title="Inge Morath">Inge Morath</a> or <a href="/wiki/Otto_Wagner" title="Otto Wagner">Otto Wagner</a> and scienc. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food">Food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Food">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Food">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Famous Austrian dishes are <a href="/wiki/Wiener_Schnitzel" class="mw-redirect" title="Wiener Schnitzel">Wiener Schnitzel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apfelstrudel" class="mw-redirect" title="Apfelstrudel">Apfelstrudel</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Schweinsbraten&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Schweinsbraten (not yet started)">Schweinsbraten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaiserschmarren" class="mw-redirect" title="Kaiserschmarren">Kaiserschmarren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kn%C3%B6del" title="Knödel">Knödel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sachertorte" title="Sachertorte">Sachertorte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tafelspitz" title="Tafelspitz">Tafelspitz</a>. But you can also find a lot of local dishes like <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C3%A4rntner_Reindling&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kärntner Reindling (not yet started)">Kärntner Reindling</a> (a kind of cake), <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C3%A4rntner_Nudeln&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kärntner Nudeln (not yet started)">Kärntner Nudeln</a> (also called "Kärntner Kasnudeln", you may write it "...nudln" too), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiroler_Kn%C3%B6dl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tiroler Knödl (not yet started)">Tiroler Knödl</a> (may be written "...knödel"; ), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiroler_Schlipfkrapfen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tiroler Schlipfkrapfen (not yet started)">Tiroler Schlipfkrapfen</a> (another kind of "Kärntner Nudeln"), <a href="/wiki/Salzburger_Nockerl" title="Salzburger Nockerl">Salzburger Nockerl</a> (also may be written ..."Nockerln"), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Steirisches_Wurzelfleisch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Steirisches Wurzelfleisch (not yet started)">Steirisches Wurzelfleisch</a> (..."Wurzlfleisch") or <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sterz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sterz (not yet started)">Sterz</a> ("Steirischer Sterz"). </p>
