      <p><b>San Marino</b> is one of the smallest <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">countries</a> in the world. It is found in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and is fully surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> (this is called an <i><a href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">enclave</a></i>, and only the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a> and San Marino are like this). Fewer than 30,000 people live there. Its total area is 61&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>. Its capital is the <a href="/wiki/City_of_San_Marino" class="mw-redirect" title="City of San Marino">City of San Marino</a>. </p>
      <p>San Marino is the world's oldest <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> that still exists. It was started on <a href="/wiki/3_September" class="mw-redirect" title="3 September">3 September</a> in A.D. <a href="/wiki/301" title="301">301</a> by a skilled builder called Saint Marinus. Its written <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> was <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/adopt" class="extiw" title="wikt:adopt">adopted</a> on October 8, 1600. The very small nation was recognized by <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon</a>'s France in 1797, and by the other European nations at the 1815 <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a>. </p>
      <p>Even though it is an independent country, it depends very much on Italy. Since the 19th century, when Italy was unified, San Marino has been fully surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. </p>
      <p>The biggest <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a> in San Marino is <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>. Selling <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamp" title="Postage stamp">postage stamps</a> is an important source of income, too. San Marino is not a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, but the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euro</a> is used in San Marino. </p>
      <p>People in San Marino speak the <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian language</a>. Most people in San Marino believe in <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a>. </p>

      <p>San Marino is covered by the <a href="/wiki/Apennine_Mountains" title="Apennine Mountains">Apennine</a> mountain range, and it has a rugged terrain. The highest point in the country is <a href="/wiki/Monte_Titano" title="Monte Titano">Monte Titano</a>. There are no bodies of water of any significant size. San Marino has no natural level ground. It is hilly terrain. </p>
      <p>San Marino is the third smallest country in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, Only <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> are smaller. </p>
      <p>Two rivers flow through San Marino. There is no major water transport, and no major port or harbour. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Marino&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cuisine">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Marino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Cuisine">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The cuisine of San Marino is strongly similar to <a href="/wiki/Italian_cuisine" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian cuisine">Italian</a>, especially that of the <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">Marche</a> regions. It has a number of its own unique dishes and products. Its best known is probably the <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Torta_Tre_Monti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Torta Tre Monti (not yet started)">Torta Tre Monti</a></i> ("Cake of the Three Mountains" or "Cake of the Three Towers"), a wafer layered cake covered in chocolate. It shows <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Three_Towers_of_San_Marino&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Three Towers of San Marino (not yet started)">The Three Towers of San Marino</a>. The country also has a small wine industry. </p>
