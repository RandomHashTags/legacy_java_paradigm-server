      <p><b>Peru</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">Lima</a>. The ruins of <a href="/wiki/Machu_Picchu" title="Machu Picchu">Machu Picchu</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> mountains, and the source of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon River</a> are all found in Peru. </p>
      <p>Peru is bordered to the north by <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, to the east by <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, to the south by <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, and to the southeast by <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>. Peru is a representative democratic republic divided into 25 regions and over 33 million people live in it. </p>
      <p>Peruvian territory was home to the Norte Chico civilization, one of the oldest in the world, and to the Inca Empire, the largest state in Pre-Columbian America. The Spanish Empire conquered the region in the 16th century and established a Viceroyalty, which included most of its South American colonies. After achieving independence in 1821. </p>
      <p>Peru suffered a terrible <a href="/wiki/Guerrilla_warfare" title="Guerrilla warfare">guerrilla war</a> in the 1980s. The communist (Maoist) <a href="/wiki/Sendero_Luminoso" title="Sendero Luminoso">Shining Path</a> tried to take over the country. But after the leader of the group was captured in 1992, Shining Path was not a threat anymore. During the 1990s, it was ruled by President <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Fujimori" title="Alberto Fujimori">Alberto Fujimori</a>. During this time, the economy of Peru got better, and it became easier to start a company or operate a business. After Fujimori, <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Toledo" title="Alejandro Toledo">Alejandro Toledo</a> was elected President, and then Alan Garcia, who was President from 1985 to 1990, was elected again in 2006. <a href="/wiki/Ollanta_Humala" title="Ollanta Humala">Ollanta Humala</a> was elected President in 2011 and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Pablo_Kuczynski" title="Pedro Pablo Kuczynski">Pedro Pablo Kuczynski</a> was elected President in 2016. In March 2018, Kuczynski resigned following political scandals and Vice President <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_Vizcarra" title="Martín Vizcarra">Martín Vizcarra</a> was sworn-in as the next president. </p>
      <p>Peru's most important <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exports</a>, products that it sells to other countries, are fish, gold and other metals, oil, coffee, sugar, and cotton. Also, the food in Peru is very diverse, including typical dishes like Ceviche and Broiled Chicken. </p>
      <p>Tourists from other countries like to come to Peru because of the history and also to enjoy nature. Many people come to climb mountains in the Cordillera Blanca in the <a href="/wiki/Ancash_Region" title="Ancash Region">Ancash Region</a>, and many people visit Peru's long Pacific coast or the Amazon jungle. Cuzco and Macchu Picchu are just two of the places where many buildings built by the Incas are still standing after hundreds of years, and these are some of the most visited places. The Incas were not the only tribe in Peru who left buildings and artifacts, but they were the most powerful. </p>
      <p> Peru is divided into 25 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Regions_of_Peru_regions&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Regions of Peru regions (not yet started)">regions of Peru regions</a>. Lima is the capital and other main regions are Cuzco, Arequipa and Lambayeque. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peru&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Peru was the home of the <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca Empire</a>. The Incas were a well-organized Indian civilization that began the city of Cuzco (now called <a href="/wiki/Cusco" title="Cusco">Cusco</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-12">[9]</a></sup> Beginning in the 1400s, they defeated many nearby tribes and built an empire in the Andes. The Inca forced the people to work for the <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a> for a certain number of days every year. They used this "work tax" to build roads and terraces on the sides of the mountains to grow crops, and huge cities with rich <a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">palaces</a> for the rulers and their queens. Records were kept on quipa, knotted ropes, since the Incas never invented writing. These could be quickly sent anywhere in the empire by a series of <a href="/wiki/Relay" title="Relay">relay</a> runners set up along the roads. Heavier loads were sent by <a href="/wiki/Llama" title="Llama">llamas</a>, the pack animals of the Andes.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-12">[9]</a></sup> The Incas were rich in <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a> which could be found in the mountains. The Spanish wanted that treasure when they discovered the nation in the 1500s. Francisco Pizarro, a Spanish man, kidnapped and killed the Inca ruler in 1532, even after his people paid a huge amount of treasure for his release. The Incas fought the Spanish for many years, but the last Inca king was killed in 1572. Peru was a Spanish colony until 1821. <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> is still the main language of the people, although many also speak Quechua, the Inca language.<sup id="cite_ref-lifepac_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifepac-12">[9]</a></sup> </p>
