      <p><b>Cameroon</b> (officially called the <b>Republic of Cameroon</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Yaound%C3%A9" title="Yaoundé">Yaoundé</a>. The largest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> in Cameroon is <a href="/wiki/Douala" title="Douala">Douala</a>. The population of Cameroon is about 20 million. Cameroon's president is <a href="/wiki/Paul_Biya" title="Paul Biya">Paul Biya</a>. They speak nearly 250 languages in Cameroon. French and English are the official languages. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameroon&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameroon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>At 475,442 square kilometres (183,569&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi), Cameroon is the world's 53rd-largest country.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It is about the size of <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The country is in <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central</a> and West Africa on the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bight_of_Bonny&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bight of Bonny (not yet started)">Bight of Bonny</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> and the Atlantic Ocean. The country's neighbours are <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> to the west; <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> to the northeast; the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> to the east; and <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> to the south. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regions">Regions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameroon&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Regions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameroon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Regions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

On 12 November 2008, the President changed the divisions from provinces to regions.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Each region is headed by a presidentially appointed governor. </p>

      <p>The official languages of Cameroon are <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. It is part of both the British <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Francophonie" class="mw-redirect" title="La Francophonie">La Francophonie</a>. Most people speak French. </p>
