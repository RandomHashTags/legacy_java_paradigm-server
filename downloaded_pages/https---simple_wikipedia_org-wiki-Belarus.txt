      <p><b>Belarus</b> (officially called <b>Republic of Belarus</b>) is a country in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-unee_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unee-8">[8]</a></sup> About nine million people live there. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>. It was part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> until 1991. The president of Belarus has been <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a> since 1994. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>. Over forty percent of its 207,600 square kilometres (80,200&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) is forested.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The State is a member of the <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">CIS</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Collective_Security_Treaty_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Collective Security Treaty Organization (not yet started)">Collective Security Treaty Organization</a>, the Eurasian Economic Community, the Union State of Russia and Belarus (from 2 April 1997), as well as a member of other international organizations. </p>
      <p>Until the 20th century, the lands of modern-day Belarus belonged to several countries. These included the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Polotsk" title="Principality of Polotsk">Principality of Polotsk</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duchy of Lithuania">Grand Duchy of Lithuania</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Revolution_(1917)" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian Revolution (1917)">Russian Revolution</a>, Belarus became part of the Soviet Union. It was renamed the <a href="/wiki/Byelorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic">Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic</a> (BSSR). The borders of Belarus took their modern shape in 1939. Some lands of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Polish_Republic" title="Second Polish Republic">Second Polish Republic</a> were added into it after the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_invasion_of_Poland" title="Soviet invasion of Poland">Soviet invasion of Poland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-uni1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uni1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-uni2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uni2-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-uni3_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uni3-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-uni4_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uni4-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-uni5_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uni5-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-uni6_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uni6-15">[15]</a></sup> The nation and its territory were <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/devastate" class="extiw" title="wikt:devastate">devastated</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War&nbsp;II</a>. Belarus lost about a third of its population and more than half of its economic resources.<sup id="cite_ref-axell_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-axell-16">[16]</a></sup> In 1945 the Belorussian SSR became a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, along with the Soviet Union and the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_SSR" class="mw-redirect" title="Ukrainian SSR">Ukrainian SSR</a>. </p>
      <p>The parliament of the republic declared the <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a> of Belarus on <span class="nowrap">27 July</span> 1990. During the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">dissolution of the Soviet Union</a>, Belarus became independent on <span class="nowrap">25 August</span> 1991. </p>
      <p>Over 70% of Belarus's population of 9.49&nbsp;million live in the urban areas.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> More than 80% of the population are ethnic Belarusians. Most of the rest are Russians, <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Poles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainians</a>. The country has two official languages: <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>. The main religion in the country is <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church" title="Russian Orthodox Church">Russian Orthodox Christianity</a>. The second most popular, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a>, has a much smaller following. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prior_to_First_World_War">Prior to First World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Prior to First World War">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Prior to First World War">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Both <b><a href="/wiki/Homo_erectus" title="Homo erectus">Homo erectus</a></b> and <a href="/wiki/Neanderthal" title="Neanderthal">Neanderthal</a> remains have been found in the region. From 5,000 to 2,000 BCE, <a href="/wiki/Bandkeramik" class="mw-redirect" title="Bandkeramik">Bandkeramik</a> cultures lived here. <a href="/wiki/Cimmerians" title="Cimmerians">Cimmerians</a> were in the area by 1,000 BCE. By 500 BCE, Slavs moved in. The <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Avars" title="Eurasian Avars">Avars</a> came through around 400–600 CE. They were unable to move the Slavs.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The region that is now Belarus was first settled by <a href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">Slavic</a> tribes in the 6th century. They came into contact with the <a href="/wiki/Varangians" title="Varangians">Varangians</a>, who were bands of <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavian</a> warriors and traders.<sup id="cite_ref-rambaud_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rambaud-19">[19]</a></sup> They formed <a href="/wiki/Kievan_Rus%27" title="Kievan Rus'">Kievan Rus'</a> in 862. </p>
      <p>When Kievan Rus' ruler <a href="/wiki/Yaroslav_I_the_Wise" class="mw-redirect" title="Yaroslav I the Wise">Yaroslav I the Wise</a> died, the state split.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Later some were added into the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duchy of Lithuania">Grand Duchy of Lithuania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Lithuania made a union with <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. The union ended in 1795.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The land of Belarus went to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The land stayed with Russia until going to the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Since_initial_independence">Since initial independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Since initial independence">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Since initial independence">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Belarus said they were free from Germany on <span class="nowrap">25 March</span> 1918. They formed the <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_People%27s_Republic" title="Belarusian People's Republic">Belarusian People's Republic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-birgerson_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birgerson-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Then the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Soviet_War" title="Polish–Soviet War">Polish–Soviet War</a> started. A part of Belarus under Russian rule became the <a href="/wiki/Belorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Belorussian Soviet Socialist Republic">Belorussian Soviet Socialist Republic</a> in 1919. Then it added to the <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian%E2%80%93Belorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Lithuanian–Belorussian Soviet Socialist Republic">Lithuanian–Belorussian Soviet Socialist Republic</a>. Other lands were divided between Poland and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> after the war ended in 1921. The Belorussian SSR became a founding member of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics in 1922.<sup id="cite_ref-birgerson_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birgerson-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The western part of modern Belarus stayed part of Poland.<sup id="cite_ref-ocu1_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ocu1-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ocu2_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ocu2-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ocu3_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ocu3-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1939, <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> and the Soviet Union invaded Poland. This was the beginning of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Parts of Poland were added to the <a href="/wiki/Belorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Belorussian Soviet Socialist Republic">Belorussian Soviet Socialist Republic</a>. They are now <a href="/wiki/West_Belarus" title="West Belarus">West Belarus</a>. </p>
      <p>Nazi Germany invaded the Soviet Union in 1941. BSSR was the hardest-hit Soviet republic in World War II. During that time, Germany destroyed 209 out of 290 cities in the republic, 85% of the republic's industry, and more than one million buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-axell_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-axell-16">[16]</a></sup> Casualties were between two and three million.<sup id="cite_ref-axell_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-axell-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-warpop_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warpop-31">[31]</a></sup> The population of Belarus did not come back to its pre-war level until 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-warpop_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warpop-31">[31]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a> wanted Belorussian SSR to be more Russian. Russians were sent from other parts of the Soviet Union to be in the government. The use of the <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian language</a> was limited. After Stalin's death in 1953, <a href="/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev" title="Nikita Khrushchev">Nikita Khrushchev</a> continued the plan. </p>
      <p>In 1986, the Belorussian SSR had <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fallout" title="Nuclear fallout">nuclear fallout</a> from the explosion at the <a href="/wiki/Chernobyl_disaster" title="Chernobyl disaster">Chernobyl</a> power plant in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic">Ukrainian SSR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gorby_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorby-32">[32]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Belarus said it was free on <span class="nowrap">27 July</span> 1990. With the support of the Communist Party, the country's name was changed to the Republic of Belarus on <span class="nowrap">25 August</span> 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-byind_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-byind-33">[33]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Belarus is <a href="/wiki/Landlocked_country" title="Landlocked country">landlocked</a> and mostly flat. It has a lot of <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshy</a> land.<sup id="cite_ref-ciageo_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ciageo-34">[34]</a></sup> Many streams and 11,000 lakes are found in Belarus.<sup id="cite_ref-ciageo_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ciageo-34">[34]</a></sup> Three major rivers run through the country: the <a href="/wiki/Neman_River" title="Neman River">Neman</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pripyat_River" title="Pripyat River">Pripyat</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Dnieper_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Dnieper River">Dnieper</a>. </p>
      <p>The highest point is <a href="/wiki/Dzyarzhynskaya_Hara" title="Dzyarzhynskaya Hara">Dzyarzhynskaya Hara</a> at 345 metres (1,132&nbsp;ft). Belarus has a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hemiboreal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hemiboreal (not yet started)">hemiboreal</a> <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">humid continental climate</a> (<i>Dfb</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Koeppen_climate_classification" class="mw-redirect" title="Koeppen climate classification">Koeppen climate classification</a>). </p>
      <p>Natural resources include <a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">peat</a> deposits, small amounts of oil and natural gas, <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a>, dolomite (<a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a>), <a href="/wiki/Marl" title="Marl">marl</a>, chalk, sand, gravel, and clay.<sup id="cite_ref-ciageo_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ciageo-34">[34]</a></sup> About 70% of the radiation from neighboring Ukraine's 1986 <a href="/wiki/Chernobyl_disaster" title="Chernobyl disaster">Chernobyl nuclear disaster</a> entered Belarusian territory. The farmland continues to be affected by radiation fallout.<sup id="cite_ref-Chernobyl_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chernobyl-35">[35]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Belarus is a <a href="/wiki/Presidential_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidential system">presidential</a> republic. It is governed by a <a href="/wiki/President_of_Belarus" title="President of Belarus">president</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Belarus" title="National Assembly of Belarus">National Assembly</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_rights">Human rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Human rights">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Human rights">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Lukashenko has described himself as having an "authoritarian ruling style".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Western countries have described Belarus under Lukashenko as a dictatorship.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> has stopped Belarus from membership since 1997 for undemocratic voting. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_Belarus" title="Armed Forces of Belarus">Armed Forces of Belarus</a> have three branches: the Army, the Air Force, and the <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_Ministry_of_Defense" class="mw-redirect" title="Belarusian Ministry of Defense">Ministry of Defense</a> joint staff. Lieutenant General Yuri Zhadobin heads the Ministry of Defense.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Alexander Lukashenko (as president) is Commander-in-Chief.<sup id="cite_ref-s4_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s4-39">[39]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Most of the Belarusian economy is <a href="/wiki/Planned_economy" class="mw-redirect" title="Planned economy">state-controlled</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stategov_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stategov-41">[41]</a></sup> It has been described as "Soviet-style."<sup id="cite_ref-AJE1_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AJE1-42">[42]</a></sup> The country relies on Russia for some imports, including petroleum.<sup id="cite_ref-natotrade_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-natotrade-43">[43]</a></sup> As of 1994, Belarus's main exports included heavy machinery (especially tractors), agricultural products, and energy products.<sup id="cite_ref-byexports_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-byexports-44">[44]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>According to 2009 census, the population is 9,503,807.<sup id="cite_ref-2009census_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009census-2">[2]</a></sup> Ethnic <a href="/wiki/Belarusians" title="Belarusians">Belarusians</a> are 83.7% of Belarus' total population.<sup id="cite_ref-2009census_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009census-2">[2]</a></sup> The next largest ethnic groups are: <a href="/wiki/Russians" class="mw-redirect" title="Russians">Russians</a> (8.3%), <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Poles</a> (3.1%), and <a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainians</a> (1.7%).<sup id="cite_ref-2009census_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009census-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>, the nation's capital and largest city, is home to 1,836,808 residents as of 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-2009census_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009census-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gomel" title="Gomel">Gomel</a>, with 481,000 people, is the second-largest city and is the capital of the Homiel Voblast. Other large cities are <a href="/wiki/Mogilev" class="mw-redirect" title="Mogilev">Mogilev</a> (365,100), <a href="/wiki/Vitebsk" title="Vitebsk">Vitebsk</a> (342,400), <a href="/wiki/Hrodna" class="mw-redirect" title="Hrodna">Hrodna</a> (314,800) and <a href="/wiki/Brest,_Belarus" title="Brest, Belarus">Brest</a> (298,300).<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> For other places in Belarus see <a href="/wiki/List_of_settlements_in_Belarus" title="List of settlements in Belarus">List of settlements in Belarus</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">Literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Literature">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Literature">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Belarusian literature began with 11th- to 13th-century religious scripture. By the 16th century, <a href="/wiki/Polotsk" title="Polotsk">Polotsk</a> resident <a href="/wiki/Francysk_Skaryna" title="Francysk Skaryna">Francysk Skaryna</a> translated the Bible into Belarusian. The modern era of Belarusian literature began in the late 19th century. One important writer was <a href="/wiki/Yanka_Kupala" title="Yanka Kupala">Yanka Kupala</a>. Several poets and authors went into exile after the Nazi occupation of Belarus. They would not return until the 1960s.<sup id="cite_ref-byeb_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-byeb-46">[46]</a></sup> The last major revival of Belarusian literature was in the 1960s with novels published by <a href="/wiki/Vasil_Byka%C5%AD" title="Vasil Bykaŭ">Vasil Bykaŭ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uladzimir_Karatkievich" title="Uladzimir Karatkievich">Uladzimir Karatkievich</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Music">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Music">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the 19th century, Polish composer <a href="/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_Moniuszko" title="Stanisław Moniuszko">Stanisław Moniuszko</a> made operas and chamber music pieces while living in Minsk. At the end of the 19th century, major Belarusian cities formed their own opera and ballet companies. </p>
      <p>The National Academic Theatre of Ballet, in Minsk, was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Prix_Benois_de_la_Danse" title="Prix Benois de la Danse">Benois de la Dance Prize</a> in 1996 as the top ballet company in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-clasmus_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clasmus-47">[47]</a></sup> Rock music has become more popular in recent years, though the Belarusian government has tried to limit the amount of foreign music aired on the radio. Since 2004, Belarus has been sending artists to the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dress">Dress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Dress">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Change section: Dress">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The traditional Belarusian dress is from the <a href="/wiki/Kievan_Rus%27" title="Kievan Rus'">Kievan Rus'</a> period. Due to the cool climate, clothes were made to keep body heat and were usually made from <a href="/wiki/Flax" title="Flax">flax</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cuisine">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Change section: Cuisine">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Belarusian cuisine is mainly vegetables, meat (especially pork), and breads. Foods are usually either slowly cooked or stewed. A typical Belarusian eats a light breakfast and two hearty meals, with dinner being the largest meal of the day. Wheat and rye breads are eaten in Belarus. Rye is more plentiful because conditions are too harsh for growing wheat. To show <a href="/wiki/Hospitality" title="Hospitality">hospitality</a>, a host will give an offering of <a href="/wiki/Bread_and_salt" title="Bread and salt">bread and salt</a> when greeting a guest or visitor.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Popular drinks in Belarus include Russian wheat vodka and <i><a href="/wiki/Kvass" title="Kvass">kvass</a></i>, Kvass is a drink made from fermented malted brown bread or rye flour. <i>Kvass</i> may also be added with sliced vegetables to create a cold soup called <i><a href="/wiki/Okroshka" title="Okroshka">okroshka</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Heritage_Sites">World Heritage Sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: World Heritage Sites">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Change section: World Heritage Sites">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Belarus has four <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Sites</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Mir_Castle_Complex" title="Mir Castle Complex">Mir Castle Complex</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nesvizh_Castle" title="Nesvizh Castle">Nesvizh Castle</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bia%C5%82owie%C5%BCa_Forest" title="Białowieża Forest">Belovezhskaya Pushcha</a> (shared with <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/Struve_Geodetic_Arc" title="Struve Geodetic Arc">Struve Geodetic Arc</a> (shared with nine other countries).<sup id="cite_ref-UNSECO_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNSECO-51">[51]</a></sup> </p>
