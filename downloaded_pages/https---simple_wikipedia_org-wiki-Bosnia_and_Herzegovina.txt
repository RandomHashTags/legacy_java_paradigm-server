      <p><b>Bosnia~Herzegovina</b> or <b>Bosnia-Herzegovina</b> (locally: <i>Bosna i Hercegovina/Босна и Херцеговина</i>, most commonly abbreviated as <i>BiH/БиХ</i>) is a country in <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Southeastern Europe">Southeastern Europe</a>, with an area of 51,197 <a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">km<sup>2</sup></a> and 4,600,000 people. Countries around Bosnia and Herzegovina are <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a>. The capital is <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>, and other important cities are <a href="/wiki/Banja_Luka" title="Banja Luka">Banja Luka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mostar" title="Mostar">Mostar</a>, Zenica and <a href="/wiki/Tuzla" title="Tuzla">Tuzla</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The first state in Bosnia and Herzegovina was in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>, it was a very important province in the Balkans and the capital, Sarajevo, had 100,000 people. In 1878, it became a province of <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a> when the Empire took over Bosnia and Herzegovina in 1908. In 1914 the <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Franz_Ferdinand_of_Austria" title="Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria">Archduke Ferdinand</a> and his wife, Sophie, were assassinated in Sarajevo, leading to <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. From 1918 until 1992, the country was a part of the former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. After a 3-year long war, Bosnia and Herzegovina proclaimed <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> as a country consisting mostly of Serbs, Croats and Bosnian Muslims. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The country is divided into two entities: <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republika_Srpska" title="Republika Srpska">Republika Srpska</a>. These are then divided into 10 cantons. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Bosnia and Herzegovina has produced many athletes. Many of them were famous in the Yugoslav national teams before Bosnia and Herzegovina's independence. </p>
      <p>The most important international <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sporting event</a> in the history of Bosnia and Herzegovina was the hosting of the <a href="/wiki/1984_Winter_Olympics" title="1984 Winter Olympics">14th Winter Olympics</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=RK_Borac_Banja_Luka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="RK Borac Banja Luka (not yet started)">Borac</a> <a href="/wiki/Team_handball" class="mw-redirect" title="Team handball">handball</a> club has won seven <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yugoslav_Handball_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yugoslav Handball Championship (not yet started)">Yugoslav Handball Championships</a>, as well as the European Championship Cup in 1976 and the International Handball Federation Cup in 1991. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=KK_Bosna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="KK Bosna (not yet started)">Bosna</a> basketball club from Sarajevo were European Champions in 1979. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yugoslavia_national_basketball_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yugoslavia national basketball team (not yet started)">Yugoslav national basketball team</a> medaled in every world championship from 1963 through 1990. The team included Bosnian players such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dra%C5%BEen_Dalipagi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dražen Dalipagić (not yet started)">Dražen Dalipagić</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirza_Deliba%C5%A1i%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mirza Delibašić (not yet started)">Mirza Delibašić</a>. Bosnia and Herzegovina regularly qualifies for the <a href="/w/index.php?title=EuroBasket&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="EuroBasket (not yet started)">European Championship in Basketball</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jedinstvo_Aida&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jedinstvo Aida (not yet started)">Jedinstvo Aida</a> <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_basketball" title="Women's basketball">women's basketball</a> club, based in Tuzla, has won the 1989 European Championships in Florence. </p>
      <p>Bosnia has many world-class basketball players, notably <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirza_Teletovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mirza Teletović (not yet started)">Mirza Teletović</a>, the first Bosnian in the <a href="/wiki/NBA" class="mw-redirect" title="NBA">NBA</a>. Among others are, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Elmedin_Kikanovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Elmedin Kikanović (not yet started)">Elmedin Kikanović</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nihad_%C4%90edovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nihad Đedović (not yet started)">Nihad Đedović</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ognjen_Kuzmi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ognjen Kuzmić (not yet started)">Ognjen Kuzmić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jusuf_Nurki%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jusuf Nurkić (not yet started)">Jusuf Nurkić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ned%C5%BEad_Sinanovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nedžad Sinanović (not yet started)">Nedžad Sinanović</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nemanja_Mitrovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nemanja Mitrović (not yet started)">Nemanja Mitrović</a>. </p>
      <p>The Tuzla-Sinalco <a href="/wiki/Karate" title="Karate">karate</a> club from Tuzla has won the most Yugoslav championships, as well as four European Championships and one World Championship. </p>
      <p>The Bosnian <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> team has been <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yugoslav_Chess_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yugoslav Chess Championship (not yet started)">Champion of Yugoslavia</a> seven times. In addition the club ŠK Bosna Sarajevo won four Chess Club Cup&nbsp;: 1994 in <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>, 1999 in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugojno&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bugojno (not yet started)">Bugojno</a>, 2000 in <a href="/wiki/Neum" title="Neum">Neum</a>, and 2001 in Kallithea Elassonos. Chess grandmaster <a href="/w/index.php?title=Borki_Predojevi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Borki Predojević (not yet started)">Borki Predojević</a> has also won European Championships: Under-12 years <a href="/wiki/Litochoro" title="Litochoro">Litochoro</a> (Greece) in 1999, and Under-14 years <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kallithea_Elassonos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kallithea Elassonos (not yet started)">Kallithea Elassonos</a> (Greece) in 2001, and in 2003 won World Championship Under-16 years <a href="/w/index.php?title=Halkidiki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Halkidiki (not yet started)">Halkidiki</a> (Greece). </p>
      <p>Middle-weight <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxer</a> <a href="/wiki/Marijan_Bene%C5%A1" title="Marijan Beneš">Marijan Beneš</a> has won several Championships of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Yugoslav Championships and the European Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-nezavisne_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nezavisne-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1978 he won the World Title against <a href="/w/index.php?title=Elisha_Obed&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Elisha Obed (not yet started)">Elisha Obed</a> from the Bahamas. Another middle-weight boxer, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anton_Josipovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anton Josipović (not yet started)">Anton Josipović</a> won the Olympic Gold in Los Angeles, 1984. He also won Yugoslav Championship in 1982, the Championship of the Balkans in 1983, and the Belgrade Trophy in 1985. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Association football</a> is the most popular sport in Bosnia and Herzegovina. It dates from 1903, but its popularity grew significantly after <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. At the local level, <a href="/wiki/FK_Sarajevo" title="FK Sarajevo">FK Sarajevo</a> (1967 and 1984), <a href="/wiki/FK_%C5%BDeljezni%C4%8Dar_Sarajevo" class="mw-redirect" title="FK Željezničar Sarajevo">Željezničar</a> (1972) have both won the Yugoslav Championship. The former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia_national_football_team" title="Yugoslavia national football team">Yugoslav national football team</a> has included a number of Bosnian players, such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Josip_Katalinski&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Josip Katalinski (not yet started)">Josip Katalinski</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bla%C5%BE_Sli%C5%A1kovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Blaž Slišković (not yet started)">Blaž Slišković</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Du%C5%A1an_Bajevi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dušan Bajević (not yet started)">Dušan Bajević</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Enver_Mari%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Enver Marić (not yet started)">Enver Marić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mehmed_Ba%C5%BEdarevi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mehmed Baždarević (not yet started)">Mehmed Baždarević</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ivica_Osim" title="Ivica Osim">Ivica Osim</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Safet_Su%C5%A1i%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Safet Sušić (not yet started)">Safet Sušić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vahidin_Musemi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vahidin Musemić (not yet started)">Vahidin Musemić</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirsad_Fazlagi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mirsad Fazlagić (not yet started)">Mirsad Fazlagić</a>. </p>
      <p>Today, the team of Bosnia and Herzegovina has modern footballers like <a href="/wiki/Edin_D%C5%BEeko" title="Edin Džeko">Edin Džeko</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvjezdan_Misimovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zvjezdan Misimović (not yet started)">Zvjezdan Misimović</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedad_Ibi%C5%A1evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vedad Ibišević (not yet started)">Vedad Ibišević</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Emir_Spahi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Emir Spahić (not yet started)">Emir Spahić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Asmir_Begovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Asmir Begović (not yet started)">Asmir Begović</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miralem_Pjani%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Miralem Pjanić (not yet started)">Miralem Pjanić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sejad_Salihovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sejad Salihović (not yet started)">Sejad Salihović</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Senad_Luli%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Senad Lulić (not yet started)">Senad Lulić</a> and others. The independent <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team</a> has not qualified for a European or World Championship but has played twice in the play-off stages. </p>
      <p>Bosnian national teams have struggled to draft the best national players. Many players born in Bosnia and Herzegovina choose to play for other countries because of their <a href="/wiki/Ethnic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic">ethnic</a> identification. For example, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nikica_Jelavi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nikica Jelavić (not yet started)">Nikica Jelavić</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedran_%C4%86orluka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vedran Ćorluka (not yet started)">Vedran Ćorluka</a> were both born in Bosnia and Herzegovina but play for Croatia. Other internationally famous players from Bosnia and Herzegovina, who have made similar choices, are: <a href="/wiki/Dejan_Lovren" title="Dejan Lovren">Dejan Lovren</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mladen_Petri%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mladen Petrić (not yet started)">Mladen Petrić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mario_Stani%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mario Stanić (not yet started)">Mario Stanić</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Neven_Suboti%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Neven Subotić (not yet started)">Neven Subotić</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zlatan_Ibrahimovi%C4%87" title="Zlatan Ibrahimović">Zlatan Ibrahimović</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marko_Marin_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marko Marin (footballer) (not yet started)">Marko Marin</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Boris_%C5%BDivkovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Boris Živković (not yet started)">Boris Živković</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zlatko_Junuzovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zlatko Junuzović (not yet started)">Zlatko Junuzović</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savo_Milo%C5%A1evi%C4%87" title="Savo Milošević">Savo Milošević</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zdravko_Kuzmanovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zdravko Kuzmanović (not yet started)">Zdravko Kuzmanović</a>. </p>
      <p>Bosnia and Herzegovina was the world champion of <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Paralympics" title="2004 Summer Paralympics">2004 Summer Paralympics</a> and <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Paralympics" title="2012 Summer Paralympics">2012 Summer Paralympics</a>. Many among those on the team lost their legs in the Bosnian War. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cuisine">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Cuisine">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Bosnian cuisine uses many spices, in moderate quantities. Most dishes are light, as they are cooked in lots of water. The sauces are fully natural, with little more than the natural juices of the vegetables in the dish. Typical ingredients include tomatoes, potatoes, onions, <a href="/wiki/Garlic" title="Garlic">garlic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capsicum" title="Capsicum">peppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cucumber" title="Cucumber">cucumbers</a>, carrots, <a href="/wiki/Cabbage" title="Cabbage">cabbage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mushroom" title="Mushroom">mushrooms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spinach" title="Spinach">spinach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zucchini" title="Zucchini">zucchini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bean" title="Bean">dried beans</a>, fresh beans, <a href="/wiki/Plum" title="Plum">plums</a>, milk, <a href="/wiki/Paprika" title="Paprika">paprika</a> and cream called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Smetana_(dairy_product)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Smetana (dairy product) (not yet started)">Pavlaka</a>. Bosnian cuisine is balanced between <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Western culture">Western</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_world" title="Eastern world">Eastern</a> influences. As a result of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkish_cuisine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Turkish cuisine (not yet started)">Ottoman</a> administration for almost 500 years, Bosnian food is closely related to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkish_cuisine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Turkish cuisine (not yet started)">Turkish</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Greek_cuisine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Greek cuisine (not yet started)">Greek</a>, and other former <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mediterranean_cuisine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mediterranean cuisine (not yet started)">Mediterranean</a> cuisines. However, because of years of Austrian rule, there are many influences from Central Europe. Typical meat dishes include mostly beef and <a href="/wiki/Lamb_and_mutton" class="mw-redirect" title="Lamb and mutton">lamb</a>. Some local specialties are <a href="/wiki/%C4%86evap%C4%8Di%C4%87i" title="Ćevapčići">ćevapi</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Burek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Burek (not yet started)">burek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolma" title="Dolma">dolma</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sarma_(food)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sarma (food) (not yet started)">sarma</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pilaf&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pilaf (not yet started)">pilaf</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Goulash&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Goulash (not yet started)">goulash</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ajvar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ajvar (not yet started)">ajvar</a> and a whole range of Eastern sweets. Local wines come from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Herzegovina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Herzegovina (not yet started)">Herzegovina</a> where the climate is suitable for growing grapes. Herzegovinian loza (similar to Italian <a href="/wiki/Grappa" title="Grappa">Grappa</a> but less sweet) is very popular. Plum or apple alcohol beverages are made in the north. In the south, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Distillery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Distillery (not yet started)">distilleries</a> used to produce vast quantities of <a href="/wiki/Brandy" title="Brandy">brandy</a>. Brandy is the base of most <a href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" class="mw-redirect" title="Alcoholic beverage">alcoholic drinks</a>. </p>
      <p>Coffeehouses, where Bosnian coffee is served in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cezve&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cezve (not yet started)">džezva</a> with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Turkish_Delight&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Turkish Delight (not yet started)">rahat lokum</a> and sugar cubes, are in many places in Sarajevo and every city in the country. Coffee drinking is a favorite Bosnian pastime and part of the culture. Bosnians are believed to be some of the heaviest coffee drinkers in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
