      <p>The <b>Democratic Republic of São Tomé and Príncipe</b> is the second-smallest African country. It is made up of two islands: São Tomé and Príncipe. </p>
      <p>They are about 140&nbsp;km apart and about 250 and 225&nbsp;km, respectively, off of the northwestern coast of <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>. </p>
      <p>The islands were a Portuguese colony, but have been <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a> since July 1975. <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> is the official language. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Portuguese arrived in São Tomé Island on December 21, 1471, and Príncipe Island on January 17, 1492. As these were days dedicated to saints, both islands had saints’ names. S. Antão Island, the former name of Príncipe Island, changed in 1500 to honor the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_of_Portugal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Prince of Portugal (not yet started)">Prince of Portugal</a>. </p>
      <p>The official historical version says that the country was uninhabited before the arrival of the Portuguese sailors. </p>
      <p>The first successful Portuguese settlement in the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> was in 1493. The volcanic soil of the islands proved to be good for sugar crops. Hard work was needed to grow and harvest the sugar crops. Slaves were brought from Portugal to do the work. Because other countries began producing more sugar, the islands were not able to make money from it. Instead it became a stopping place for the <a href="/wiki/Slave_trade" title="Slave trade">slave trade</a>. </p>
      <p>In the 19th century, crops of <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cocoa" title="Cocoa">cocoa</a> were grown. These became successful. By 1908 the country was the largest producer of cocoa. It is the most important crop for the country. </p>
      <p>On February 3, 1953, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Batep%C3%A1_massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Batepá massacre (not yet started)">Batepá massacre</a> occurred. In the massacre hundreds of local people were killed by Portuguese settlers. The Portuguese wanted workers for the crops. The locals said they were being used as slaves. The governor told the military to take any locals who did not want to work. </p>
      <p>In the late 1950s a group wanted independence. They were called the MLSTP (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Movement_for_the_Liberation_of_S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe/Social_Democratic_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Movement for the Liberation of São Tomé and Príncipe/Social Democratic Party (not yet started)">Movement for the Liberation of São Tomé and Príncipe</a>). In 1974 they succeeded in removing <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Caetano" title="Marcelo Caetano">Marcelo Caetano</a> as their leader. On 12 July 1975 São Tomé and Príncipe became an independent country. The first president was <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Pinto_da_Costa" title="Manuel Pinto da Costa">Manuel Pinto da Costa</a>. He was the MLSTP`s General Secretary. </p>
      <p>Until 1990 the MLSTP was the only <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a>. They changed the <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> to allow other political parties. The new <a href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Convergence_Party_%E2%80%93_Reflection_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Democratic Convergence Party – Reflection Group (not yet started)"> Party of Democratic Convergence</a> (PCD) won the most seats in the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe)" title="National Assembly (São Tomé and Príncipe)">National Assembly</a>. <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Trovoada" title="Miguel Trovoada">Miguel Trovoada</a> was elected president. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The president is elected for a five-year term and can be re-elected. The prime minister is elected for a four-year term. The thirteen members of the cabinet (the group of ministers) are chosen by them. All adult citizens can vote in the elections. They vote by <a href="/wiki/Secret_ballot" title="Secret ballot">secret ballot</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provinces">Provinces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Provinces">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Provinces">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>São Tomé and Príncipe is divided into 2 <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a>: <a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="Príncipe">Príncipe</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="São Tomé Island (not yet started)">São Tomé</a>. </p>
      <p>The provinces are further divided into seven <a href="/wiki/District" title="District">districts</a>, six on São Tomé and one on Príncipe (with Príncipe having self-government since April 29, 1995). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_Climate">Geography and Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography and Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Geography and Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>São Tomé and Príncipe is formed by two volcanic islands - the biggest of which is S. Tomé - and several islets. It is in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>, off the western equatorial coast of Africa. Both islands belong to the Cameroon volcanic mountain line. </p>
      <p>The archipelago is 1,001km2 in size. The equator line passes through the Rolas’s Islet which is in the south of S. Tomé Island. The tallest peak is the Peak of S. Tomé at 2,024m tall. </p>
      <p>The climate is tropical, hot and humid, with an annual average temperature of 27 C. There are two main seasons. Gravana is the driest season. It has little or no rainfall and the temperatures are lower. Gravana lasts from June to August. The rainy season is from October to May. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Since the archipelago´s discovery, its economy has been based on agriculture and fishing. Cocoa is 95% of the country’s <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exports</a>. Other exports are copper, palm oil and coffee. Another economic activity is tourism. </p>
      <p>After independence, the country’s economy was nationalized, it was then privatised in the early 1990s. Now the government budget is mainly based on foreign assistance from donors such as the UN Development Programme, the World Bank, and country donors. </p>
      <p>The main <a href="/wiki/Import" title="Import">importers</a> of the archipelago's production are Portugal with 51%, France with 14%, Angola with 11%, and Japan with 10%. </p>
      <p>In 2001, petroleum was found in the waters claimed by the archipelago and <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>According to the 2010 census, about 163,000 people lived in São Tomé and Príncipe. 52,000 lived in the capital and less than seven thousand lived on Príncipe Island. </p>
      <p>São Tomé is the country’s capital and main city. </p>
      <p>The people of S. Tomé and Príncipe are divided into the following ethnic groups: mixed-blood, descendents of Portuguese settlers and African slaves; Forros, the biggest ethnic group and descendents of freed slaves; Angolares, descendents of Angolan slaves; Tonga, mixed blood of Forros and hired workers from Angola, Mozambique and Cape Verde; and Cape Verdean descendents. </p>
      <p>The country’s official language is Portuguese. Other languages spoken are Forro, a <a href="/wiki/Creole" class="mw-redirect" title="Creole">creole</a> spoken by 80% of S. Tomé Island`s people; Angolar, spoken mainly on the southeast of S. Tomé Island; Lung`ié, spoken in Príncipe Island; and Creole from Cape Verde. </p>
      <p>Almost everyone is Christian: <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>, Evangelical <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seventh-Day_Adventist" class="mw-redirect" title="Seventh-Day Adventist">Seventh-Day Adventist</a>. </p>

      <p>The country’s culture is the product of Portuguese and African cultural influence. Typical dances on the islands are Ússua, Socopé, Dêxa, and Puita. Tchiloli, Danço Congo and Auto dos Floripes are public theatrical performances, examples of the Portuguese cultural legacy. </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alda_Neves_da_Gra%C3%A7a_do_Esp%C3%ADrito_Santo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alda Neves da Graça do Espírito Santo (not yet started)">Alda Neves da Graça do Espírito Santo</a> was a famous poet from São Tomé. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Children are required to attend school for four years. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Lyceum_(S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Lyceum (São Tomé and Príncipe) (not yet started)">National Lyceum (São Tomé and Príncipe)</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="University of São Tomé and Príncipe (not yet started)">University of São Tomé and Príncipe</a> are the two colleges. </p>
