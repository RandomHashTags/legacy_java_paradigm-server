      <p><b>Fiji</b> is a small <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It has 322 <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a>. The most important islands are <a href="/wiki/Vanua_Levu" title="Vanua Levu">Vanua Levu</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Viti_Levu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Viti Levu (not yet started)">Viti Levu</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> of Fiji is <a href="/wiki/Suva" title="Suva">Suva</a>, and the city with the most people is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nadi (not yet started)">Nadi</a>. There are about 844,330 people in Fiji. The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a> of Fiji are <a href="/wiki/Fijian_language" title="Fijian language">Fijian</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hindustani" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindustani">Hindustani</a> (a mix of <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Urdu" title="Urdu">Urdu</a>). Fiji is part of the Pacific Rim. About 313000 speak <a href="/wiki/Fiji_Hindi" title="Fiji Hindi">Fiji Hindi</a>. </p>
      <p>In the 17th and 18th centuries, the Dutch and the British explored Fiji.<sup id="cite_ref-Fiji:_History_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fiji:_History-12">[12]</a></sup> Fiji was a British colony up until 1970; British occupation lasted almost a century.<sup id="cite_ref-Tourism_Fiji_:_A_Social,_Political_and_Environmental_Case_Study_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tourism_Fiji_:_A_Social,_Political_and_Environmental_Case_Study-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Fiji has many forest, mineral, and fish resources. </p>
