            <li value="2">English is not an official language, but it is widely spoken by the majority of the population and it is commonly used in government, legislation and commerce alongside Nauruan. Due to Nauru's history and relationship with Australia, <a href="/wiki/Australian_English" title="Australian English">Australian English</a> is the dominant variety.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-state_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state-4">[4]</a></sup> And is <i>de facto</i> official.</li>

states">sovereign</a> <a href="/wiki/Island_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Island nation">island nation</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesian</a> <a Nauru is the world's smallest island nation, covering just 21 square kilometres (8&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi), the smallest independent <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>, and the only republican state in the world without an official <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a>.       <p>Nauru is a <a href="/wiki/Phosphate_rock" class="mw-redirect" title="Phosphate rock">phosphate rock</a> <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a>, and its primary economic activity since 1907 has been the export of phosphate mined from the island.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauruan_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nauruan language (not yet started)">Nauruan</a> are the official languages of Nauru.       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauru&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Nauru&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Geography of Nauru (not yet started)">Geography of Nauru</a>

      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">

      <p>Nauru is a 21 square kilometres (8&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-3">[3]</a></sup> oval-shaped island in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, 42 kilometres (26&nbsp;mi) south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>. The island is surrounded by a coral reef, which is exposed at low tide and dotted with pinnacles.<sup id="cite_ref-state_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state-4">[4]</a></sup> The presence of the reef has prevented the establishment of a <a href="/wiki/Seaport" class="mw-redirect" title="Seaport">seaport</a>, although <a href="/w/index.php?title=Canals_in_Nauru&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Canals in Nauru (not yet started)">channels</a> in the reef allow small boats access to the island.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A fertile coastal strip 150 to 300 metres (490 to 980&nbsp;ft) wide lies inland from the beach.<sup id="cite_ref-state_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Coral cliffs surround Nauru's central plateau. The highest point of the plateau, called the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Command_Ridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Command Ridge (not yet started)">Command Ridge</a>, is 71 metres (233&nbsp;ft) above sea level.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The only fertile areas on Nauru are on the narrow coastal belt, where <a href="/wiki/Coconut_palm" title="Coconut palm">coconut palms</a> flourish. The land surrounding <a href="/w/index.php?title=Buada_Lagoon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Buada Lagoon (not yet started)">Buada Lagoon</a> supports bananas, <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapples</a>, vegetables, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pandanus_tectorius&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pandanus tectorius (not yet started)">pandanus trees</a>, and indigenous hardwoods such as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Calophyllum_inophyllum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Calophyllum inophyllum (not yet started)">tomano tree</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-state_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Nauru was one of three great <a href="/wiki/Guano" title="Guano">phosphate rock</a> islands in the Pacific Ocean (the others were <a href="/w/index.php?title=Banaba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Banaba (not yet started)">Banaba</a> (Ocean Island) in <a href="/wiki/Kiribati" title="Kiribati">Kiribati</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Makatea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Makatea (not yet started)">Makatea</a> in <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>). The phosphate reserves on Nauru are now almost entirely depleted. Phosphate mining in the central plateau has left a barren terrain of jagged limestone pinnacles up to 15 metres (49&nbsp;ft) high. Mining has stripped and devastated about 80&nbsp;per cent of Nauru's land area, and has also affected the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zone</a>; 40&nbsp;per cent of marine life is estimated to have been killed by silt and phosphate runoff.<sup id="cite_ref-state_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UNCCC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNCCC-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are only about 60&nbsp;recorded <a href="/wiki/Vascular_plant" title="Vascular plant">vascular plant</a> species native to the island, none of which are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Endemic_(ecology)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Endemic (ecology) (not yet started)">endemic</a>. Coconut farming, mining, and introduced species have caused serious disturbance to the native vegetation.<sup id="cite_ref-UNCCD_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNCCD-9">[9]</a></sup> There are no native land <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>, but there are native insects, land crabs, and birds, including the endemic <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauru_Reed_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nauru Reed Warbler (not yet started)">Nauru Reed Warbler</a>. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Polynesian_rat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Polynesian rat (not yet started)">Polynesian rat</a>, cats, dogs, pigs, and chickens have been introduced to Nauru from ships.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are limited natural fresh water resources on Nauru. Rooftop <a href="/wiki/Rainwater_tank" title="Rainwater tank">storage tanks</a> collect rainwater, but the islanders are mostly dependent on three <a href="/wiki/Desalination" title="Desalination">desalination plants</a> housed at Nauru's Utilities Agency. Nauru's climate is hot and very humid year-round because of its proximity to the equator and the ocean. Nauru is hit by <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> rains between November and February, but does not typically experience cyclones. Annual rainfall is highly variable and is influenced by the <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o-Southern_Oscillation" class="mw-redirect" title="El Niño-Southern Oscillation">El Niño-Southern Oscillation</a>, with several significant recorded droughts.<sup id="cite_ref-UNCCD_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNCCD-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The temperature on Nauru ranges between 26&nbsp;°C (79&nbsp;°F) and 35&nbsp;°C (95&nbsp;°F) during the day and between 22&nbsp;°C (72&nbsp;°F) and 34&nbsp;°C (93&nbsp;°F) at night.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>As an island, Nauru is vulnerable to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sea_level_rise&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sea level rise (not yet started)">sea level change</a>. Nauru is the seventh most <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> threatened nation due to flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> At least 80&nbsp;per cent of the land of Nauru is well elevated, but this area will be uninhabitable until the phosphate mining rehabilitation programme is implemented.<sup id="cite_ref-UNCCC_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNCCC-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauru&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

       See also: <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_settlements_in_Nauru&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="List of settlements in Nauru (not yet started)">List of settlements in Nauru</a>

      <p>Nauru is divided into fourteen administrative districts which are grouped into eight electoral constituencies.<sup id="cite_ref-state_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
