      <p><a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> is divided into 19 <b>regions</b> (<a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>: <i lang="fi">maakunta</i>; <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>: <i lang="sv">landskap</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[a]</a></sup> The regions are governed by regional councils, which serve as forums of cooperation for the <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Finland" title="Municipalities of Finland">municipalities</a> of a region. The main tasks of the regions are regional planning and development of enterprise and education. In addition, the public health services are usually organized on the basis of regions. Currently, the only region where a popular election is held for the council is Kainuu. Other regional councils are elected by <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Finland" title="Municipalities of Finland">municipal councils</a>, each municipality sending representatives in proportion to its population. </p>

      <h2><span id=".C3.85land"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Åland">Åland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regions_of_Finland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Åland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>One region, <a href="/wiki/%C3%85land" class="mw-redirect" title="Åland">Åland</a> has a special status and has a much higher degree of autonomy than the others, with its own <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_%C3%85land" title="Parliament of Åland">Parliament</a> and local laws, due to its <a href="/wiki/History_of_%C3%85land" class="mw-redirect" title="History of Åland">unique history</a> and the fact that the overwhelming majority of its people are <a href="/wiki/Finland_Swedes" class="mw-redirect" title="Finland Swedes">Finland Swedes</a>. The sole language of Åland is <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>/<a href="/wiki/Finland_Swedish" title="Finland Swedish">Finland Swedish</a>, unlike the rest of the country where <a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a> share official status. It has its own elected <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_%C3%85land" class="mw-redirect" title="Premier of Åland">head of government</a> who carries the title of Premier and heads the <a href="/wiki/Lantrad" class="mw-redirect" title="Lantrad">Lantrad</a>, the regional executive. Most powers that would be exercise by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Finland" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Finland">Government of Finland</a> on the mainland are instead exercised by Åland-specific authorities which execute independent policy in most areas. The Åland islanders elect a single representative to the national <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Finland" title="Parliament of Finland">legislature</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Finland" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Finland">Government of Finland</a> appoints a <a href="/wiki/Provincial_Governors_of_Finland" title="Provincial Governors of Finland">Governor</a> to represent the national government on Åland. Åland is a <a href="/wiki/Demilitarised_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Demilitarised zone">demilitarised zone</a> and Åland islanders are exempt from conscription. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Representation_of_the_state">Representation of the state</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regions_of_Finland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Representation of the state">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In addition to inter-municipal cooperation, which is the responsibility of regional councils, there are 15 <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Economic_Development,_Transport_and_the_Environment" title="Centre for Economic Development, Transport and the Environment">Centres for Economic Development, Transport and the Environment</a> (Finnish: <i>elinkeino-, liikenne- ja ympäristökeskus</i>, abbreviated <i>ely-keskus</i>), which is responsible for the local administration of labour, agriculture, fisheries, forestry and entrepreneurial affairs. They are each responsible for one or more of regions of Finland, and include offices of the Ministries of Employment and the Economy, Transport and Communications and Environment. The <a href="/wiki/Finnish_Defence_Forces" title="Finnish Defence Forces">Finnish Defence Forces</a> regional offices are responsible for the regional defence preparations and for the administration of conscription within the region. </p>
