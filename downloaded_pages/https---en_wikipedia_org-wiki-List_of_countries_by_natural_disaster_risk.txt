   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of countries by natural disaster risk</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub" class="noprint">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="contentSub2"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a> 
    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>This is a <b>list of countries by natural disaster risk</b>, as measured in the <i>World Risk Index</i>, calculated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_University" title="United Nations University">United Nations University</a> Institute for Environment and Human Security (UNU-EHS) and featured in the 2016 World Risk Report (WRR 2016) published by the Alliance Development Works/BÃ¼ndnis Entwicklung Hilft (BEH). </p>
      <p>The report systematically considers a country's vulnerability and its exposure to <a href="/wiki/Natural_hazard" title="Natural hazard">natural hazards</a> to determine a ranking of countries around the world based on their <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">natural disaster</a> risk. The WRI developed by UNU-EHS and BEH the main feature of the WRR, determines the risk of becoming a victim of a disaster as a result of vulnerability and natural hazards such as <a href="/wiki/Earthquakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Earthquakes">earthquakes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_eruptions" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcanic eruptions">volcanic eruptions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Storms" class="mw-redirect" title="Storms">storms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Floods" class="mw-redirect" title="Floods">floods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Droughts" class="mw-redirect" title="Droughts">droughts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sea_level_rise" title="Sea level rise">sea level rise</a> for 173 countries worldwide. The WRI is based on 28 indicators and research data which are globally freely available and results in a global risk ranking and maps which allow for comparison between countries. Risk is at its highest where a high level of exposure to natural hazards coincides with very vulnerable societies.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
