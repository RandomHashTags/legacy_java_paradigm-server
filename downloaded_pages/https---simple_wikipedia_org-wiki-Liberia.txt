      <p>The <b>Republic of Liberia</b> is a small country on the coast of West <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It has common borders with <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast</a>. The country has a size of about 520 kilometres (320&nbsp;mi) by 270 kilometres (170&nbsp;mi). As of 2020, there are about 3,300,000 people in Liberia. The capital city of Liberia is <a href="/wiki/Monrovia" title="Monrovia">Monrovia</a>. For ships, Liberia is a <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_convenience" title="Flag of convenience">flag of convenience</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Liberia is a country in Southwest Africa, on the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. Except for the coast, most of Liberia are low <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a>, with an altitude of 300 metres (980&nbsp;ft) to 500 metres (1,600&nbsp;ft) above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>. The coastal region is full of <a href="/wiki/Swamp" title="Swamp">swamps</a>, and reaches 30 kilometres (19&nbsp;mi) to 50 kilometres (31&nbsp;mi) inland. After that, there's a <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> at an altitude of about 400 metres (1,300&nbsp;ft). About 60% of the country is covered with <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a>. To the north, there are higher mountains. There are nine big <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubber_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Rubber tree">rubber trees</a>, which are important for the <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a>. There are <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a> swamps near the coast. In 2019, estimates are that close to 5 million people live in Liberia. About half of them live in the capital, Monrovia. There are 15 administrative divisions, called counties. The main environmental issues in Liberia are that endangered species are hunted and eaten. This kind of meat is called bushmeat. Some of the poached animals are also sold to neighbouring countries. A big part of Liberia is rainforest. like with other rainforest contries, <a href="/wiki/Slash-and-burn" title="Slash-and-burn">Slash-and-burn</a> argicculture is a problem. Illegal <a href="/wiki/Logging" title="Logging">logging</a> also is. In Monrovia, there is a large <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Liberia" title="History of Liberia">History of Liberia</a>
      </div> 
      <p>White Americans made the American Colonization Society and bought the land in 1822. They didn't want <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black people</a> who were not <a href="/wiki/Slavery_in_the_United_States" title="Slavery in the United States">slaves</a> to be in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>. So they took some of them to Liberia. At the start of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> (in the 1860s) about 12.000 freed slaves lived there. In the 1990s and in the early 2000s, two <a href="/wiki/Liberia_civil_wars" title="Liberia civil wars">civil wars</a> badly affected the country. </p>
