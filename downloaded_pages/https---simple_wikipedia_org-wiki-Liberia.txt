      <p>The <b>Republic of Liberia</b> is a small country on the coast of West <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It was founded in the early <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a> by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Colonization_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="American Colonization Society (not yet started)">American Colonization Society</a> because they didn't want <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black people</a> who were not <a href="/wiki/Slavery_in_the_United_States" title="Slavery in the United States">slaves</a> to be in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The name of the country means "Land of the Free" in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>, and gained its <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> on <a href="/wiki/July_26" title="July 26">July 26</a>, <a href="/wiki/1847" title="1847">1847</a>. Many of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Free_people_of_color&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Free people of color (not yet started)">free people of color</a> who were sent to Liberia were <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionaries</a>, and they were very cruel to the <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">Indigenous peoples</a> who were already living there.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its official language today is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> because of this history. </p>
      <p>In the 1990s and in the early 2000s, two <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil wars</a> badly affected the country. There are about 3,300,000 people in Liberia. The capital city of Liberia is <a href="/wiki/Monrovia" title="Monrovia">Monrovia</a>. </p>
      <p>The country's <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> is similar to that of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In the fall <a href="/wiki/2017" title="2017">2017</a> elections, <a href="/wiki/George_Weah" title="George Weah">George Weah</a> became the twenty-fifth <a href="/wiki/President_of_Liberia" title="President of Liberia">President of Liberia</a>. He was inaugurated on <a href="/wiki/January_22" title="January 22">January 22</a>, <a href="/wiki/2018" title="2018">2018</a>. The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Liberia" title="President of Liberia">President</a> before Weah was <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Johnson_Sirleaf" title="Ellen Johnson Sirleaf">Ellen Johnson Sirleaf</a>, the first African female president. </p>
      <p>For ships, Liberia is a <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_convenience" title="Flag of convenience">flag of convenience</a>. </p>
