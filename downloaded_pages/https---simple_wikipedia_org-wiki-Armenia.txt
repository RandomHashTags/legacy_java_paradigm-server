      <p><b>Armenia</b> <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> is officially the <b>Republic of Armenia</b>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in the <a href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">South Caucasus</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. It is in Eastern Europe on the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Highlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Armenian Highlands">Armenian Highlands</a>, </p>
      <p>Armenia is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> to the west and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> to the north. The <i>de facto</i> independent <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Artsakh" title="Republic of Artsakh">Republic of Artsakh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> is to the east, and <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and Azerbaijan's <a href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclave</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Republic" title="Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic">Nakhchivan</a> to the south.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Armenia is a multi-party, democratic <a href="/wiki/Nation_state" title="Nation state">nation-state</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>It has an ancient cultural heritage. <a href="/wiki/Urartu" title="Urartu">Urartu</a> was established in 860 <a href="/wiki/BC" class="mw-redirect" title="BC">BC</a> and by the 6th century BC it was replaced by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Satrapy_of_Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Satrapy of Armenia (not yet started)">Satrapy of Armenia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Kingdom of Armenia</a> reached its height under <a href="/wiki/Tigranes_the_Great" title="Tigranes the Great">Tigranes the Great</a> in the 1st century BC and became the first state in the world to adopt <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> as its <a href="/wiki/State_religion" title="State religion">official religion</a> in the late 3rd or early 4th century AD.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The official date of state adoption of Christianity is 301.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_invasion">Foreign invasion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Foreign invasion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Foreign invasion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Between the <a href="/wiki/16th_century" title="16th century">16th century</a> and <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, the traditional Armenian homeland composed of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Armenia" title="Eastern Armenia">Eastern Armenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Armenia" title="Western Armenia">Western Armenia</a> came under the rule of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Persian_Empire" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Persian Empire">Iranian</a> empires, repeatedly ruled by either of the two over the centuries. By the 19th century, Eastern Armenia had been conquered by the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, while most of the western parts of the traditional Armenian homeland remained under Ottoman rule. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 20th century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: 20th century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, Armenians living in their ancestral lands in the Ottoman Empire were systematically exterminated in the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1918" title="1918">1918</a>, following the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Revolution" title="Russian Revolution">Russian Revolution</a>, all non-Russian countries declared their independence after the Russian Empire ceased to exist, leading to the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/First_Republic_of_Armenia" title="First Republic of Armenia">First Republic of Armenia</a>. By <a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a>, the state was incorporated into the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Transcaucasian_Socialist_Federative_Soviet_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Transcaucasian Socialist Federative Soviet Republic (not yet started)">Transcaucasian Socialist Federative Soviet Republic</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/1922" title="1922">1922</a> became a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1936" title="1936">1936</a>, the Transcaucasian state was dissolved, transforming its constituent states, including the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic">Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic</a>, into full <a href="/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Republics of the Soviet Union">Union republics</a>. The modern Republic of Armenia became independent in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">dissolution of the Soviet Union</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 21st century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: 21st century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Armenia is divided into ten provinces, with the city of <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> having special administrative status as the country's capital. The chief executive in each of the ten provinces is the <i>marzpet</i> (<i>marz</i> governor), appointed by the government of Armenia. In Yerevan, the chief executive is the mayor, appointed by the president. </p>
      <p>As of 2007<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Armenia includes 915 communities, of which 49 are considered urban and 866 are considered rural. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Republic of Armenia recognises the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church" title="Armenian Apostolic Church">Armenian Apostolic Church</a>, the world's oldest national church, as the country's primary religious establishment.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The unique <a href="/wiki/Armenian_alphabet" title="Armenian alphabet">Armenian alphabet</a> was invented by <a href="/wiki/Mesrop_Mashtots" class="mw-redirect" title="Mesrop Mashtots">Mesrop Mashtots</a> in 405 AD. </p>
      <p>Armenia is a member of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Economic_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eurasian Economic Union (not yet started)">Eurasian Economic Union</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Collective_Security_Treaty_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Collective Security Treaty Organization (not yet started)">Collective Security Treaty Organization</a>. Armenia supports the de facto independent <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Artsakh" title="Republic of Artsakh">Republic of Artsakh</a>, which was proclaimed in 1991. </p>
