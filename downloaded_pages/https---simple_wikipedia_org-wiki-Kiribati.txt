      <p><b>Kiribati</b> (pronounced "KiriBASS") is an island <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> located on the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It has 33 <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atolls</a>, groups of tiny <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a>. The country is near the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/South_Tarawa" title="South Tarawa">South Tarawa</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Tarawa" title="Tarawa">Tarawa</a> atoll. </p>
      <p>Kiribati used to be called the <b>Gilbert Islands</b>. In 1892, the Gilbert Islands became a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a> with the Ellice Islands (nowadays known as <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</a>), together called <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_and_Ellice_Islands" title="Gilbert and Ellice Islands">Gilbert and Ellice Islands</a>. Kiribati became an <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> in 1979. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> is an important <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a>. </p>
      <p>Kiribati signed a "Treaty of Friendship and Territorial Sovereignty" (also called the "Treaty of Tarawa") with the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in 1979. The USA gave up its claims to the islands of Canton, Enderbury, Hull, Birnie, Gardner, Phoenix, Sydney, McKean, Christmas, Caroline, Starbuck, Malden, Flint, and Vostok in that treaty. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.trussel.com/kir/treaty.htm">[1]</a> </p>
      <p>The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a> of Kiribati are <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gilbertese_language" title="Gilbertese language">Gilbertese</a>. </p>
