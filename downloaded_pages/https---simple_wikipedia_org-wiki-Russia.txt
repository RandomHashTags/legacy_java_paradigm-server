      <p><b>Russia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Россия</span>), officially called the <b>Russian Federation</b> (Russian: <span lang="ru">Российская Федерация</span>)<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[12]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> that is in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> and in <a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="North Asia (not yet started)">North Asia</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_area" title="List of countries by area">largest country in the world</a> by <a href="/wiki/Land" title="Land">land</a> area. About 146.7 million people live in Russia according to the 2019 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> of Russia is <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, and the official language is <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>. </p>
      <p>Russia shares land borders with <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> (both via <a href="/wiki/Kaliningrad_Oblast" title="Kaliningrad Oblast">Kaliningrad Oblast</a>), <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. It also has borders over water with <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a>, and the United States by the <a href="/wiki/Bering_Strait" title="Bering Strait">Bering Strait</a>. </p>
      <p>Russia is a very large and diverse country. From 1922 to 1991, it used to be the main part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. It was a country based on <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">Communism</a>, but today its government is a <a href="/wiki/Federal" class="mw-disambig" title="Federal">federal</a> semi-presidential <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. It has elements of <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>. The <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">President</a> is chosen by direct <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">election</a>, but challenging candidates do not have access to the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">mass media</a>, and so have almost no chance of winning.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-16">[13]</a></sup> Its current President is <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>. The President rules the country, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Russian Parliament (not yet started)">Russian Parliament</a> plays a secondary role. </p>
      <p>Russia has 85 regional parliaments, and the presidents of these areas are very rarely ever displaced in an election. As a typical example, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirsan_Ilyumzhinov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kirsan Ilyumzhinov (not yet started)">Kirsan Ilyumzhinov</a> was President of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Kalmykia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Republic of Kalmykia (not yet started)">Republic of Kalmykia</a> in the Russian Federation from 1993 to 2010. Such a long tenure is not unusual in Russia, and suggests that the electoral system is heavily biased to keep incumbents in power.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[14]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Size_and_resources">Size and resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Size and resources">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Size and resources">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>At 17,075,400 square kilometres (6,592,800&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi), Russia is the <a href="/wiki/Largest_country_in_the_world" class="mw-redirect" title="Largest country in the world">largest country in the world</a>, covering more than one-eighth of the Earth's inhabited land area. Russia is also the world's eighth <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population" class="mw-redirect" title="List of countries by population">most populous nation</a> with 143&nbsp;million people as of 2012. Russia produces a lot of energy made from <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Extending from eastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> across the whole of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">northern Asia</a>, Russia spans eleven <a href="/wiki/Time_zones" class="mw-redirect" title="Time zones">time zones</a> and has a wide range of <a href="/wiki/Environments" class="mw-redirect" title="Environments">environments</a> and landforms. Russia has the world's largest reserves of mineral and energy resources,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[16]</a></sup> and is the largest producer of <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-IEA-Oil_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IEA-Oil-20">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cia-gas_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-gas-21">[18]</a></sup> Russia has the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a> reserves,<sup id="cite_ref-fao.org_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fao.org-22">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FAO_2010_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAO_2010-23">[20]</a></sup> and its <a href="/wiki/Lakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Lakes">lakes</a> contain about one-quarter of the world's <a href="/wiki/Fresh_water" title="Fresh water">fresh water</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-loc_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-loc-24">[21]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Constitution">Constitution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Constitution">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Constitution">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Russia is a federal semi-presidential republic. It has a president and a parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[22]</a></sup> Russia consists of 85 federal subjects (territorial units). All subjects of the <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federation</a> shall be equal. All entities are subject to the uniform federal law. Subjects of the federation have two representatives in the parliament. Subjects of the Russian Federation do not have a right to <a href="/wiki/Secession" title="Secession">secession</a> from it. Important issues are decided by the Federation President; lesser powers are given to the member republics. </p>
      <p>At the end of the twentieth century, Russia experienced many political changes. Some people fought to leave from the federation. </p>
      <p>Elections are held at all levels. According Steve White, the present government made it clear that they had no plans of making a "second edition" of the American or British political system, but rather a system that was closer to Russia's own traditions.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[23]</a></sup> Richard Sakwa wrote that the Russian government is considered legitimate by the majority of the Russian people. It seeks to deliver a set of public goods without trying to fit to extra-democratic logic to achieve them. Whether the system is becoming less <a href="/wiki/Autocratic" class="mw-redirect" title="Autocratic">autocratic</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dictator" title="Dictator">dictatorial</a>) is debatable.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[24]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>The United Russia is the ruling party, which supports the government. The other parties in the Duma (Russian parliament) do not criticize the government strongly, for fear of losing their places in the Duma. Many opposition parties, such as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=People%27s_Freedom_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="People's Freedom Party (not yet started)">People's Freedom Party</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Other_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Other Russia (not yet started)">Other Russia</a>, have been unable to register due to the strict rules. In the 2000s the government led a war in <a href="/wiki/Chechnya" title="Chechnya">Chechnya</a>, and in the process, <a href="/wiki/Civil_liberties" title="Civil liberties">civil liberties</a> and independent media were restricted. Corruption is widespread and human rights, especially in the <a href="/wiki/North_Caucasus" title="North Caucasus">North Caucasus</a>, are frequently violated. In 2008 Putin's government was in a <a href="/wiki/2008_South_Ossetia_war" title="2008 South Ossetia war">war with Georgia</a> in a dispute over a region with many ethnic Russians. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg/170px-Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="228" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg/255px-Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg/340px-Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg 2x" data-file-width="894" data-file-height="1200"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Peter_der-Grosse_1838.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>

      <p>The roots of Russia's history began when the <a href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">East Slavs</a> formed a group in Europe between the 3rd and 8th centuries AD.<sup id="cite_ref-britannica_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-britannica-28">[25]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Viking" class="mw-redirect" title="Viking">Vikings</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Descendant" class="mw-disambig" title="Descendant">descendants</a> founded the first East Slavic state of <a href="/wiki/Kievan_Rus%27" title="Kievan Rus'">Kievan Rus'</a> in the 9th century. They adopted <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> in 988.<sup id="cite_ref-Curtis_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Curtis-29">[26]</a></sup> This form of Christianity influenced Russian culture greatly.<sup id="cite_ref-Curtis_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Curtis-29">[26]</a></sup> Kievan Rus' eventually broke up and the lands were divided into many small <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudal states</a>. The most powerful successor state to Kievan Rus' was the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Duchy_of_Moscow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Grand Duchy of Moscow (not yet started)">Grand Duchy of Moscow</a>. This area served as the main force in later Russian unification and the fight against the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Horde" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Horde">Golden Horde</a> from Asia. Moscow slowly gained control of the regions around it and took over the cultural and political life of Kievan Rus'. </p>
      <p>In the 18th century, the nation had expanded through conquest, <a href="/wiki/Annexation" title="Annexation">annexation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Exploration" title="Exploration">exploration</a> to become the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, the third largest empire in history. It stretched from the <a href="/wiki/Polish-Lithuanian_Commonwealth" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth">Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth</a> eastward to the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>. The empire was ruled by an <a href="/wiki/Emperor" title="Emperor">emperor</a> called the <a href="/wiki/Tsar" title="Tsar">Tsar</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Peter the Great">Peter the Great</a> ruled Russia from 1689 until 1725. Peter moved the capital from <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> to a new city named <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>. He made Russian society more modern in many ways. The government began building ships for the Russian navy. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a> started in 1904 and ended in 1905 with Japan winning the war. The Russian defeat was one of the reasons for later <a href="/wiki/Revolution" title="Revolution">revolutions</a>. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/October_Revolution" title="October Revolution">October 1917</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bolshevik" title="Bolshevik">Bolsheviks</a> (later called "Communists"), influenced by the ideas of <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marx" title="Karl Marx">Karl Marx</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Vladimir Lenin</a>, took over the country and murdered the <a href="/wiki/Tsar" title="Tsar">Tsar</a> and other people who stood against them. Once they took power, the Bolsheviks, under Vladimir Lenin and <a href="/wiki/Leon_Trotsky" title="Leon Trotsky">Leon Trotsky</a>, created the first <a href="/wiki/Marxism" title="Marxism">Marxist</a> <a href="/wiki/Communist" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist">Communist</a> State. </p>
      <p>From the 1920s to the 1950s, <a href="/wiki/Josef_Stalin" class="mw-redirect" title="Josef Stalin">Josef Stalin</a> ruled as an absolute dictator of <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Russia" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet Russia">Soviet Russia</a>, and destroyed anything and anyone that was against his rule, including taking the property of farmers and shopkeepers. Many millions of people starved and died in the resulting <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">famines</a>. Stalin also removed, or "purged", all military personnel who were not loyal to him, and many were killed or sent to prison camps, or <a href="/wiki/Gulag" title="Gulag">gulags</a>, for many years. Even in the gulags, many prisoners died. </p>
      <p>Soviet Russia and <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> agreed not to attack each other in 1939. In June 1941, Germany broke the agreement and attacked in <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a>. The attack was part of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The war lasted in Europe until May 1945, and Russia lost more than 20 million people during that time. In spite of this large loss, Russia was one of the winners of the war and became a world <a href="/wiki/Superpower" title="Superpower">superpower</a>. </p>
      <p>From 1922 to 1991, Russia was the largest part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, or the <i>Union of Soviet Socialist Republics</i> (USSR). People sometimes used the name "Russia" for the whole Soviet Union, or sometimes "Soviet Russia". Russia was only one of 15 <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Socialist_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet Socialist Republic">Soviet Socialist Republics</a>. The republic was in fact named the "Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic" (RSFSR). </p>
      <p>The Soviet Union fell apart in the early 1990s. Russia took over the place of the USSR in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> (UN). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_present_Russian_Federation">History of present Russian Federation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History of present Russian Federation">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: History of present Russian Federation">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">

      <p><a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">Boris Yeltsin</a> was made the President of Russia in June 1991, in the first direct presidential election in Russian history. Wide-ranging reforms took place, including <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Free_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="Free trade">free trade</a> laws.<sup id="cite_ref-OECD_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OECD-30">[27]</a></sup> Radical changes "(<a href="/wiki/Shock_therapy" class="mw-redirect" title="Shock therapy">shock therapy</a>) were recommended by the United States and <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[28]</a></sup> A major <a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Economic crisis (not yet started)">economic crisis</a> followed. There was 50% decline in <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP</a> and industrial output between 1990–95.<sup id="cite_ref-OECD_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OECD-30">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[29]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The privatization largely shifted control of enterprises from state agencies to individuals with inside connections in the government system. Many of the newly rich business people took billions in cash and assets outside of the country .<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[30]</a></sup> The depression of state and economy led to the collapse of social services. Millions went into poverty, from 1.5% level of poverty in the late Soviet era, to 39–49% by mid-1993.<sup id="cite_ref-worldbank_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldbank-34">[31]</a></sup> The 1990s saw extreme corruption and lawlessness, rise of criminal gangs and violent crime.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[32]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The 1990s had many armed conflicts in the <a href="/wiki/North_Caucasus" title="North Caucasus">North Caucasus</a>. There were both local ethnic battles and <a href="/wiki/Separatist" class="mw-redirect" title="Separatist">separatist</a> <a href="/wiki/Islamist" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamist">Islamist</a> insurrections. Since the <a href="/wiki/Chechnya" title="Chechnya">Chechen</a> separatists declared independence in the early 1990s, a Chechen War was fought between the rebel groups and the Russian military. <a href="/wiki/Terrorist" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrorist">Terrorist</a> attacks against <a href="/wiki/Civilian" title="Civilian">civilians</a> caused hundreds of deaths. The most notable of these were the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moscow_theater_hostage_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Moscow theater hostage crisis (not yet started)">Moscow theater hostage crisis</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Beslan_school_siege&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Beslan school siege (not yet started)">Beslan school siege</a>. </p>
      <p>Russia took responsibility for settling the USSR's external debts, even though its population made up just half of the population of the USSR at the time of its dissolution.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[33]</a></sup> High budget <a href="/wiki/Economic_deficit" title="Economic deficit">deficits</a> caused the <a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Russian_financial_crisis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1998 Russian financial crisis (not yet started)">1998 Russian financial crisis</a><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[34]</a></sup> and resulted in further GDP decline.<sup id="cite_ref-OECD_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OECD-30">[27]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On 31 December 1999 President Yeltsin <a href="/wiki/Resignation" title="Resignation">resigned</a>, or quit being the president. The job of president was given to the recently <a href="/wiki/Appointment" class="mw-disambig" title="Appointment">appointed</a> Prime Minister, <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>. Putin then won the 2000 presidential election. Putin stopped the Chechen rebellion quickly, but violence still occurs in the Northern Caucasus at times. </p>
      <p>High oil prices and initially weak <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> followed by increasing <a href="/wiki/Demand" title="Demand">domestic demand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Consumption_(economics)" title="Consumption (economics)">consumption</a> and <a href="/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">investments</a> has helped the economy grow for nine straight years. This improved the standard of living and increasing Russia's influence on the world stage. While many reforms made during the Putin presidency have been criticized by Western nations as un-democratic,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[35]</a></sup> Putin's leadership led to <a href="/wiki/Stability" title="Stability">stability</a>, and progress. This won him widespread popularity in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[36]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On 2 March 2008, <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Medvedev" title="Dmitry Medvedev">Dmitry Medvedev</a> was elected President of Russia, whilst Putin became <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Russia" title="Prime Minister of Russia">Prime Minister</a>. Putin returned to the presidency following the 2012 presidential elections, and Medvedev was appointed Prime Minister. </p>

      <p>Russia has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 142 million citizens. Most people (73.7%) live in cities. The population decreased by 5 million people since the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. The current population growth is close to zero, and the population went down by 0.085% in 2008. </p>
      <p>Russia's area is about 17 million square kilometers (6.5 million sq. mi.). It is the largest country in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[37]</a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> is about 8.3 people per square kilometer (21.5 per sq. mi.). This is among the lowest country densities in the world. The population is most dense in the European part of the country, centering around <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>. <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> has a very low density. </p>

      <p>World-renowned composers of the 20th century included <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Scriabin" title="Alexander Scriabin">Alexander Scriabin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Igor_Stravinsky" title="Igor Stravinsky">Igor Stravinsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Rachmaninoff" title="Sergei Rachmaninoff">Sergei Rachmaninoff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Prokofiev" title="Sergei Prokofiev">Sergei Prokofiev</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dmitri_Shostakovich" title="Dmitri Shostakovich">Dmitri Shostakovich</a>. Dance companies at the <a href="/wiki/Mariinsky_Theatre" title="Mariinsky Theatre">Mariinsky Theatre</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bolshoi_Theatre" title="Bolshoi Theatre">Bolshoi Ballet</a> produced many famous <a href="/wiki/Dancer" class="mw-redirect" title="Dancer">dancers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[38]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Russians have contributed many famous works of literature.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[39]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pushkin" title="Alexander Pushkin">Alexander Pushkin</a> is considered a founder of modern Russian literature. He was a poet from the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[40]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Other famous poets and writers of the 19th century were <a href="/wiki/Anton_Chekhov" title="Anton Chekhov">Anton Chekhov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Lermontov" title="Mikhail Lermontov">Mikhail Lermontov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leo_Tolstoy" title="Leo Tolstoy">Leo Tolstoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Gogol" title="Nikolai Gogol">Nikolai Gogol</a> (he was born in what is now <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, but during his lifetime Ukraine was a part of Russia), <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Turgenev" title="Ivan Turgenev">Ivan Turgenev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Dostoyevsky" title="Fyodor Dostoyevsky">Fyodor Dostoyevsky</a>. Tolstoy and Dostoyevsky are considered by many people to be two of the greatest novelists ever.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[42]</a></sup> Three Russians won the Nobel Prize for Literature in the 20th century: <a href="/wiki/Boris_Pasternak" title="Boris Pasternak">Boris Pasternak</a> (1958), <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Sholokhov" title="Mikhail Sholokhov">Mikhail Sholokhov</a> (1965) and <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Solzhenitsyn" title="Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn">Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn</a> (1980). <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Bulgakov" title="Mikhail Bulgakov">Mikhail Bulgakov</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Master_and_Margarita" title="The Master and Margarita">The Master and Margarita</a></i> was also a <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">novel</a> of the highest quality. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Soccer" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer">Soccer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> are among the most popular sports. Boxing, gymnastics, weightlifting, and <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> are also popular sports. Track suits are popular clothing items for many Russians. Sports people to gain world fame include former tennis world number one <a href="/wiki/Maria_Sharapova" title="Maria Sharapova">Maria Sharapova</a>, who has won three Grand Slam titles, and was the world's highest paid female athlete in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[43]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Since the <a href="/wiki/1952_Summer_Olympics" title="1952 Summer Olympics">1952 Olympic Games</a>, Soviet and later Russian athletes are in the three in gold medals collected at the Summer Olympics. The <a href="/wiki/1980_Summer_Olympics" title="1980 Summer Olympics">1980 Summer Olympic Games</a> were held in Moscow while the <a href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympics" title="2014 Winter Olympics">2014 Winter Olympics</a> were hosted in <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a>. </p>
      <p>For the <a href="/wiki/2018_Winter_Olympics" title="2018 Winter Olympics">2018 Winter Olympics</a> which were held in <a href="/wiki/Pyeongchang" title="Pyeongchang">Pyeongchang</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, a lot of athletes could not compete because the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> found out they had been <a href="/wiki/Doping_in_sport" title="Doping in sport">doping</a>. Those who were not caught doping were able to play in the 2018 Olympics under the title of "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Olympic_Athletes_from_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Olympic Athletes from Russia (not yet started)">Olympic Athletes from Russia</a>", and they took home two gold medals, including one in ice hockey.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[45]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Chess">Chess</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Chess">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Chess">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">Chess</a> is the main intellectual sport in Russia. In the 20th century there were nine Russian <a href="/wiki/List_of_World_Chess_Champions" title="List of World Chess Champions">World Chess Champions</a>, more than all other nations combined. </p>
