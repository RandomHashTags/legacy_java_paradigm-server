      <p>The <b>Republic of Namibia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> coast. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> to the east, and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to the south. It gained <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> from South Africa in <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>. Before that it was called <b>South West Africa</b>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Windhoek" title="Windhoek">Windhoek</a>. </p>
      <p>Before <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> Namibia was a <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German</a> colony. <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> is still widely spoken in the country, although <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is the official language. </p>
      <p>Namibia has a population of 2.1 million people. </p>
      <p>The name of the country is from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Namib_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Namib Desert (not yet started)">Namib Desert</a>. This is said to be the oldest desert in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The dry lands of Namibia were lived in since early times by <a href="/wiki/Bushmen" class="mw-redirect" title="Bushmen">Bushmen</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Damara_(people)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Damara (people) (not yet started)">Damara</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nama_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nama people (not yet started)">Nama</a>. About the 14th century AD, <a href="/wiki/Bantu_people" title="Bantu people">Bantu</a> came to the area from central Africa. From the late 18th century onwards, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Orlam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Orlam (not yet started)">Orlam</a> clans from the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape Colony</a> crossed the <a href="/wiki/Orange_River" title="Orange River">Orange River</a>. They moved into the area that today is southern Namibia.<sup id="cite_ref-KDA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KDA-6">[6]</a></sup> The nomadic Nama tribes were largely peaceful. The missionaries with the Orlams were well received by them,<sup id="cite_ref-Dierks_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dierks-7">[7]</a></sup> the right to use waterholes and grazing was given. On their way further north, the Orlams met clans of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Herero_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Herero people (not yet started)">Herero</a> tribe. They were not as friendly. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nama-Herero_War&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nama-Herero War (not yet started)">Nama-Herero War</a> started in 1880. They did not stop until <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Imperial Germany</a> sent troops. </p>
      <p>The first Europeans to explore the region were the Portuguese navigators <a href="/w/index.php?title=Diogo_C%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Diogo Cão (not yet started)">Diogo Cão</a> in 1485 and <a href="/wiki/Bartolomeu_Dias" title="Bartolomeu Dias">Bartolomeu Dias</a> in 1486. Like most of <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a>, Namibia was not largely explored by Europeans until the 19th century. At this time traders and settlers arrived, mostly from Germany and Sweden. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="German_rule">German rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: German rule">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: German rule">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Namibia became a German colony in 1884. This was to stop the British. The country was called <a href="/wiki/German_South-West_Africa" title="German South-West Africa">German South-West Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> From 1904 to 1907, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Herero_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Herero people (not yet started)">Herero</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Namaqua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Namaqua (not yet started)">Namaqua</a> took up arms against the Germans. In the following <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herero_and_Namaqua_Genocide" class="extiw" title="en:Herero and Namaqua Genocide">Herero and Namaqua genocide</a>, 10,000 Nama (half the population) and about 65,000 Hereros (about 80% of the population) were killed. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_African_rule">South African rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: South African rule">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: South African rule">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> began to rule the land in 1915. They defeated the German force during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. It was a <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mandate_territory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mandate territory (not yet started)">mandate territory</a> from 1919. In 1946 the League was replaced by the United Nations. South Africa would not give up their rule of the land. Many people thought the land should be independent from South Africa. In 1971 South Africa was told their hold on the country was illegal.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> They still did not leave. </p>
      <p>The country officially became independent on 21 March 1990. <a href="/wiki/Sam_Nujoma" title="Sam Nujoma">Sam Nujoma</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Namibia" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Presidents of Namibia">President of Namibia</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Tourism">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Tourism">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Equus_burchelli_4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Equus_burchelli_4.jpg/220px-Equus_burchelli_4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Equus_burchelli_4.jpg/330px-Equus_burchelli_4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Equus_burchelli_4.jpg/440px-Equus_burchelli_4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2742" data-file-height="2056"></a>

         </div>An example of Namibian wildlife, the <a href="/wiki/Plains_Zebra" class="mw-redirect" title="Plains Zebra">Plains Zebra</a>, one focus of tourism

      <p>Tourism is a major contributor (14.5%) to Namibia's economy. It creates tens of thousands of jobs (18.2% of all employment). There are over a million tourists per year.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The country is among the main tourist places in Africa. It is known for <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecotourism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ecotourism (not yet started)">ecotourism</a> which features Namibia's extensive wildlife.<sup id="cite_ref-goodshape_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-goodshape-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are many lodges and reserves for tourists. Sport Hunting is also a large, and growing part of the Namibian economy. It was 14% of total tourism in the year 2000. Namibia has numerous species wanted by international sport hunters.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In addition, extreme sports such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sandboarding&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sandboarding (not yet started)">sandboarding</a> and 4x4ing have become popular. Many cities have companies that provide tours. The most visited places include the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caprivi_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caprivi Strip (not yet started)">Caprivi Strip</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fish_River_Canyon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fish River Canyon (not yet started)">Fish River Canyon</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sossusvlei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sossusvlei (not yet started)">Sossusvlei</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Skeleton_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Skeleton Coast (not yet started)">Skeleton Coast</a> Park, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sesriem&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sesriem (not yet started)">Sesriem</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Etosha_Pan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Etosha Pan (not yet started)">Etosha Pan</a> and the coastal towns of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Swakopmund&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Swakopmund (not yet started)">Swakopmund</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Walvis_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Walvis Bay (not yet started)">Walvis Bay</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BCderitz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lüderitz (not yet started)">Lüderitz</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Namibia has required free education for 10 years between the ages of 6 and 16. Grades 1–7 are primary level, grades 8–12 secondary. </p>
      <p>Most schools in Namibia are state-run. There are a few private schools. There are four teacher training colleges, three colleges of agriculture, a police training college, a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Polytechnic_of_Namibia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Polytechnic of Namibia (not yet started)">Polytechnic</a> at university level, and a <a href="/wiki/University_of_Namibia" title="University of Namibia">National University</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Sport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The most popular sport in Namibia is <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Namibia_national_football_team" title="Namibia national football team">Namibia national football team</a> qualified for the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Africa_Cup_of_Nations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2008 Africa Cup of Nations (not yet started)">2008 Africa Cup of Nations</a>. They have yet to qualify for any <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cups</a>. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibia_national_rugby_union_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Namibia national rugby union team (not yet started)">Namibian rugby team</a> has been in four separate World Cups. Namibia were participants in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Rugby_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1999 Rugby World Cup (not yet started)">1999</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Rugby_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2003 Rugby World Cup (not yet started)">2003</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Rugby_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2007 Rugby World Cup (not yet started)">2007</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Rugby_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2011 Rugby World Cup (not yet started)">2011</a> <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup" title="Rugby World Cup">Rugby World Cups</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a> is also popular. The national team played in the <a href="/wiki/2003_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2003 Cricket World Cup">2003 Cricket World Cup</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inline_Hockey&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Inline Hockey (not yet started)">Inline Hockey</a> was first played in 1995. It has become more and more popular in the last years. The Women's Inline Hockey National Team were in the 2008 <a href="/w/index.php?title=FIRS&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="FIRS (not yet started)">FIRS</a> World Championships. Namibia is the home for one of the toughest footraces in the world, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Namibian_ultra_marathon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Namibian ultra marathon (not yet started)">Namibian ultra marathon</a>. </p>
      <p>The most famous athlete from Namibia is certainly <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Fredericks" title="Frankie Fredericks">Frankie Fredericks</a>, sprinter (100 and 200 m). He won four Olympic silver medals (1992, 1996). He also has medals from several World Athletics Championships. He is also known for <a href="/wiki/Humanitarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Humanitarian">humanitarian</a> activities in Namibia and further. </p>
