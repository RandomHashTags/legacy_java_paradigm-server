      <p><b>Djibouti</b> (officially called the <b>Republic of Djibouti</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> on the <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">eastern</a> coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is also called <a href="/wiki/Djibouti_(city)" title="Djibouti (city)">Djibouti</a>. </p>
      <p>Djibouti gained its independence from France on June 27, 1977. The country was created out of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=French_Somaliland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="French Somaliland (not yet started)">French Somaliland</a> (later called the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> Territory of the Afars and Issas), which was created in the 1800s as a result of French <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonialism</a> in Africa. </p>
      <p>In 2020, about 920,000 people lived there. It is one of the least populous countries in Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Two ethnic groups, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Somalis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Somalis (not yet started)">Somali</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Afar_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Afar people (not yet started)">Afar</a> people, account for most of the people living in the country. </p>
      <p>Djibouti joined the United Nations on September 20, 1977.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intergovernmental_Authority_on_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Intergovernmental Authority on Development (not yet started)">Intergovernmental Authority on Development</a> (IGAD). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The history of Djibouti goes back thousands of years when it was part of the Sabean Empire (<a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>) to a time when Djiboutians traded hides and skins for the perfumes and spices of ancient <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. Through close contacts with the Arabian peninsula for more than 1,000 years, the Somali and Afar tribes in this region became among the first on the African continent to accept <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>. Djibouti is a Muslim country which regularly takes part in Islamic as well as Arab meetings. </p>
      <p>The country is close to a narrow part of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> so it is considered an important area from a military viewpoint. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Djibouti is near to the <a href="/wiki/Horn_of_Africa" title="Horn of Africa">Horn of Africa</a>. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a> in the north, <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> in the west and south, and Awdal of <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> in the southeast. The remainder of the border is formed by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden" title="Gulf of Aden">Gulf of Aden</a> at the east. </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Great_Rift_Valley" title="Great Rift Valley">Great Rift Valley</a> the rift between the <a href="/wiki/African_Plate" class="mw-redirect" title="African Plate">African Plate</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Somali_Plate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Somali Plate (not yet started)">Somali Plate</a> meet the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arabian_Plate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arabian Plate (not yet started)">Arabian Plate</a>. This makes a geologic tripoint<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> that is the lowest place in Africa. </p>
      <p>Djibouti has eight mountain ranges with peaks of over 1,000 m (3,281&nbsp;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-geo_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-geo-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mousa_Ali&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mousa Ali (not yet started)">Mousa Ali</a> range is the country's highest mountain range. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Bara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Grand Bara (not yet started)">Grand Bara</a> desert covers parts of southern Djibouti. </p>
      <p>Most species of wildlife are found in the northern part of the country, in the ecosystem of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Day_Forest_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Day Forest National Park (not yet started)">Day Forest National Park</a>. This forest area is the main habitat of the endangered and <a href="/wiki/Endemic" class="mw-redirect" title="Endemic">endemic</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti_francolin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Djibouti francolin (not yet started)">Djibouti francolin</a> (a bird). </p>

      <p>The Republic of Djibouti names Islam as the only <a href="/wiki/State_religion" title="State religion">state religion</a>. The Constitution of 1992 provides for the equality of citizens of all faiths as well as the freedom to practise any religion. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Association football</a> is the most popular sport in Djibouti. The country became a member of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-FIFA_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA-10">[10]</a></sup> They have only played in qualifying rounds for both the <a href="/wiki/African_Cup_of_Nations" class="mw-redirect" title="African Cup of Nations">African Cup of Nations</a> and <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Transportation">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Djibouti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Transportation">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The country is linked to <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> by way of the <a href="/wiki/Addis_Ababa%E2%80%93Djibouti_Railway" title="Addis Ababa–Djibouti Railway">Addis Ababa–Djibouti Railway</a>. </p>
