      <p><b>Luxembourg</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>; its official name is the <b>Grand Duchy of Luxembourg</b> (<a href="/wiki/Luxembourgish_language" title="Luxembourgish language">Luxembourgish</a>: <i lang="lb">Groussherzogtum Lëtzebuerg</i>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Grand-Duché de Luxembourg</i>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Großherzogtum Luxemburg</i>). It is a small <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Area" title="Area">land area</a>. It was one of the first countries in the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. It is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Benelux" title="Benelux">Benelux</a>. The countries next to Luxembourg are <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. In 2015, its <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> was 569,700, making it one of Europe's most <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">densely populated</a> countries. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Timeline">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Timeline">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li>963 - Luxembourg was founded. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigefroid&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sigefroid (not yet started)">Sigefroid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">Count</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ardennes" class="mw-disambig" title="Ardennes">Ardennes</a>, came to own the ruins of an old <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> <a href="/wiki/Fort" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort">fort</a> called "Castellum Lucilinburhuc" ("Little Castle") from the <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monks</a> of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Maximin%27s_Abbey,_Trier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="St. Maximin's Abbey, Trier (not yet started)">Abbey of St. Maximin in Trier</a>.</li> 
       <li>1354 - The emperor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Charles IV (not yet started)">Charles IV</a> made Luxembourg a <a href="/wiki/Duchy" title="Duchy">duchy</a>.</li> 
       <li>1437 - There were no people in the ruling family alive. The castle was given to the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburgs</a>.</li> 
       <li>1443 - Luxembourg was captured by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philip_of_Burgundy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Philip of Burgundy (not yet started)">Philip of Burgundy</a>.</li> 
       <li>1482 - <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_of_Burgundy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mary of Burgundy (not yet started)">Mary of Burgundy</a> died. Luxembourg was ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburgs</a> again.</li> 
       <li>1795 - After the <a href="/wiki/French_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="French revolution">French revolution</a> Luxembourg was taken over by <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It became a <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9partement" class="mw-redirect" title="Département">département</a> of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</li> 
       <li>1815 - Luxembourg became a <a href="/wiki/Grand_duchy" title="Grand duchy">grand duchy</a>. It was ruled by the <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="King of the Netherlands">king of the Netherlands</a>.</li> 
       <li>1815-1866 - Luxembourg became a member of the <a href="/wiki/German_Confederation" title="German Confederation">German Confederation</a>.</li> 
       <li>1890 - After the death of <a href="/wiki/William_III" class="mw-redirect" title="William III">William III</a>, Luxembourg passed to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=House_of_Nassau-Weilburg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="House of Nassau-Weilburg (not yet started)">House of Nassau-Weilburg</a>.</li> 
       <li>1914-1918 - Luxembourg was conquered by <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.</li> 
       <li>1940 - The Germans returned during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and in 1942 Luxembourg became part of the <a href="/wiki/Third_Reich" class="mw-redirect" title="Third Reich">Third Reich</a>.</li> 
       <li>1944 - Luxembourg became independent in <a href="/wiki/September" title="September">September</a> because the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> arrived.</li> 
       <li>1945 - Luxembourg joined the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a></li> 
       <li>1957 - Luxembourg became one of the first six members of the <a href="/wiki/European_Economic_Community" title="European Economic Community">European Economic Community</a>. That is now called the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.</li> 
       <li>2020 - Luxembourg became the first country in the world to make all public transport free to use.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Luxembourg is a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliamentary democracy">parliamentary democracy</a> lead by a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarch" class="mw-redirect" title="Constitutional monarch">constitutional monarch</a>. Under the <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> of 1868, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Executive_power&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Executive power (not yet started)">executive power</a> is in the hands of the <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet" title="Cabinet">cabinet</a>, which consists of several other <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">ministers</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span id="Districts.2C_cantons.2C_and_communes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Districts,_cantons,_and_communes">Districts, cantons, and communes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Districts, cantons, and communes">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Districts, cantons, and communes">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Luxembourg is divided into 3 <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Luxembourg" title="Districts of Luxembourg">districts</a>, which are further divided into 12 <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_Luxembourg" title="Cantons of Luxembourg">cantons</a> and then 116 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Communes_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Communes of Luxembourg (not yet started)">communes</a>. Twelve of the communes have <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Luxembourg" title="List of cities in Luxembourg">city status</a>, of which the city of <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Luxembourg (city)">Luxembourg</a> is the largest. </p>
