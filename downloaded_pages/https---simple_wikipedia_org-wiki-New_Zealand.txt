      <p><b>New Zealand</b> (known as <b><i lang="mi" title="Māori language text">Aotearoa</i></b> in the <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_language" title="Māori language">Māori language</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/Island_country" title="Island country">island country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a> in the south-western part of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It is made up of two large <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a> (the <a href="/wiki/North_Island" title="North Island">North Island</a> and the <a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a>) and many smaller islands. These islands are located to the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. New Zealand was one of the last places in the world that humans discovered, because it is a long way away from most of the world. During its time without humans, New Zealand was a good place for an unusual range of plants and animals to develop. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> of the country is <a href="/wiki/Wellington" title="Wellington">Wellington</a>, and the largest city is <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a>. Both of these cities are on the North Island. The largest city on the South Island is <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official languages</a> are <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_language" title="Māori language">Māori</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sign_language" title="Sign language">New Zealand sign language</a>. English has never been granted official language status in the NZ laws, but it is used as a default official language due to its wide usage. </p>
      <p>In 2010, a newspaper in NZ said that New Zealand was the 8th happiest country in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[11]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Name">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Name">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_language" title="Māori language">Māori language</a>, the country is called Aotearoa, which means "Land of the Long White Cloud". <i>Ao</i> means “cloud”, <i>tea</i> means “white”, and <i>roa</i> means “long”. The name "New Zealand" comes from “Zeeland” (which translates to "Sealand") in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>, after the islands were seen by Dutch explorer <a href="/wiki/Abel_Tasman" title="Abel Tasman">Abel Tasman</a>. <a href="/wiki/Zeeland" title="Zeeland">Zeeland</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">province</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government and politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Government and politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>New Zealand is a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a> and parliamentary <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> is <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Queen Elizabeth II</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> is <a href="/wiki/Jacinda_Ardern" title="Jacinda Ardern">Jacinda Ardern</a>, leader of the Labour Party. </p>
      <p>New Zealand has made itself a <i>Nuclear Free Zone</i>: They do not use <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear power</a> and do not allow <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapons</a> or nuclear powered vessels in their territory. </p>
      <p>Elections are held every 3 years.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: People">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: People">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>As of the 2018 census, 4,699,755 people lived in New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[13]</a></sup> Most of them have <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> <a href="/wiki/Ancestor" title="Ancestor">ancestors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-race_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race-21">[14]</a></sup> The indigenous people of New Zealand, the <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori</a>, have a population of 775,836, or 16.5%. </p>
      <p>The people of New Zealand often call themselves Kiwis. This name is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Kiwi" title="Kiwi">kiwi</a>, a flightless native <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> that is considered the country's national bird. </p>
      <p>The North Island is smaller than the South Island, but most people (more than 3 million) live there. This might be because the North Island is usually warmer, and has more big cities. </p>
      <p>About half of the people in New Zealand are religious. As of the 2018 census, 48.2% had no religion. The main religion in New Zealand is <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>. Just over 2 million New Zealanders are Christian.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[15]</a></sup> The second most common religion is Hinduism, with 2.6%. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cities">Cities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cities">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Cities">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Most New Zealanders live in urban areas, with the other 27% living in small towns or farming communities. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wellington" title="Wellington">Wellington</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of New Zealand. <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a> is the largest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a>, with around 1 million people. Other cities in the North Island include <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_New_Zealand" title="Hamilton, New Zealand">Hamilton</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tauranga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tauranga (not yet started)">Tauranga</a>, Napier/Hastings, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Plymouth" class="extiw" title="en:New Plymouth">New Plymouth</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whanganui" class="extiw" title="en:Whanganui">Whanganui</a>, and Palmerston North. South Island cities include <a href="/wiki/Christchurch,_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Christchurch, New Zealand">Christchurch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dunedin" title="Dunedin">Dunedin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelson,_New_Zealand" title="Nelson, New Zealand">Nelson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Invercargill" title="Invercargill">Invercargill</a> and Timaru. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geological_origin">Geological origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geological origin">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Geological origin">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>New Zealand sits at the boundary between the Australian and Pacific <a href="/wiki/Plate_tectonics" title="Plate tectonics">continental plates</a>. When these two plates bumped into each other ('collided'), the Pacific plate moved under the Australian plate, north of the South Island. As a result, there has been lots of <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanic</a> activity. South of the South Island, the Australian plate moves under the Pacific plate instead. The plates slide past each other in the South Island, where they have pushed up the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Alps" title="Southern Alps">Southern Alps</a>. Because of these two colliding plates, New Zealand often has <a href="/wiki/Earthquakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Earthquakes">earthquakes</a>. One of the best known is the <a href="/wiki/2011_Christchurch_earthquake_(February)" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Christchurch earthquake (February)">2010-2011 group of earthquakes</a> that hit the Christchurch area. </p>
      <p>The diagram shows that the North Island is on the Australian plate, but most of the South Island is on the Pacific plate. New Zealand is still geologically active, and will remain so as long as the islands are sitting above the plate boundary.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_geology">Ancient geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Ancient geology">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Ancient geology">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The oldest rocks in New Zealand date from the <a href="/wiki/Cambrian" title="Cambrian">Cambrian</a> period, 512 million years ago. New Zealand became part of the global <a href="/wiki/Supercontinent" title="Supercontinent">supercontinent</a> <a href="/wiki/Pangaea" title="Pangaea">Pangaea</a>. When that split (End-<a href="/wiki/Triassic" title="Triassic">Triassic</a>/early <a href="/wiki/Jurassic" title="Jurassic">Jurassic</a>), New Zealand became part of the southern supercontinent <a href="/wiki/Gondwana" title="Gondwana">Gondwana</a>. Finally Gondwana broke up, leaving the continents rather as they are today. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_history">Natural history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Natural history">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Zealand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Natural history">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>New Zealand was isolated from the rest of the world for a long time. It split from Australia 83 million years ago. Because of this, there are many <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plants</a> and <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">animals</a> that only live in New Zealand. Before humans came to New Zealand, there were no <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>, except three species of <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bat</a> and marine mammals such as <a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">seals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolphin" title="Dolphin">dolphins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Whale" title="Whale">whales</a>. Instead, New Zealand has many different kinds of bird. New Zealand has several species of flightless bird, including the <a href="/wiki/Kiwi" title="Kiwi">kiwi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kakapo" title="Kakapo">Kakapo</a>. For the lizards, especially important is the <a href="/wiki/Tuatara" title="Tuatara">Tuatara</a>, the only living member of a whole <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">order</a> of reptiles. </p>
      <p>New Zealand used to have more rare species, but some, mostly birds, were hunted to <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinction</a> early in its history. The giant <a href="/wiki/Moa" title="Moa">Moa</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dinornis" title="Dinornis">Dinornis</a></i>, is a famous example. The group of birds which the moa belongs to had been in existence since the <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> period. Humans arrived in New Zealand about a thousand years ago, when many moa were living, especially on the South Island. <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">Archaeological</a> sites with evidence of moa hunting are all over New Zealand. The moa became <a href="/wiki/Extinct" class="mw-redirect" title="Extinct">extinct</a> about five hundred years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[19]</a></sup> Extinctions closer to today have been caused by <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> change and introduced <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> like <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">rats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dogs</a>, cats, and possums, which kill the native birds and lizards, or eat their eggs. </p>

       <li>Sir <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Hillary" title="Edmund Hillary">Edmund Hillary</a>, the first man to climb to the top of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Everest" title="Mount Everest">Mount Everest</a>, was from New Zealand.</li> 
       <li>New Zealand has become more <a href="/wiki/Popularity" title="Popularity">popular</a> since New Zealander <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a> made the <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_trilogy" class="mw-redirect" title="The Lord of the Rings film trilogy"><i>The Lord of the Rings</i> movies</a> there.</li> 
       <li>Many farmers in New Zealand breed <a href="/wiki/Sheep" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheep">sheep</a>. There are many more sheep than people in New Zealand.</li> 
       <li>Many New Zealanders are interested in <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sports</a>. Their national sport is usually considered to be <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a> (in winter). They have impressive <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" title="Sportsperson">athletes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rowing" title="Rowing">rowing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cycling" class="mw-redirect" title="Cycling">cycling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shotput" class="mw-redirect" title="Shotput">shotput</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discus" class="mw-redirect" title="Discus">discus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_man" class="mw-redirect" title="Iron man">iron man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Triathlon" title="Triathlon">triathlon</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yachting&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yachting (not yet started)">yachting</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Climbing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Climbing (not yet started)">climbing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netball" title="Netball">netball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Softball" title="Softball">softball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_surfing" class="mw-redirect" title="Wind surfing">wind surfing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gliding" title="Gliding">gliding</a>, and more.</li> 
       <li>New Zealanders enjoy <a href="/w/index.php?title=Outdoor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Outdoor (not yet started)">outdoor</a> pursuits. The country has a "clean, green" <a href="/wiki/Image" class="mw-redirect" title="Image">image</a>, but per-person emissions are 7th worst out of 41 industrialized nations as of 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[20]</a></sup> Really bad air and water pollution is fairly rare, though occasional outbreaks in the past 20 years have caused problems for livestock and the surrounding waters. Most major rivers (60% estimated as of 2017) are not safe to swim in due to farming pollution and run-off.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[21]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>New Zealand, like many other small nations, has a strong local media. New Zealand has a wide variety of musicians, television, and radio celebrities, and stand-up comedians. Although most are simply "world-famous in New Zealand", many others have successfully made it on the international scene. Such people and groups include <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a> (actor), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kiri_Te_Kanawa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kiri Te Kanawa (not yet started)">Kiri Te Kanawa</a> (singer), the <a href="/wiki/Flight_of_the_Conchords" title="Flight of the Conchords">Flight of the Conchords</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lorde" title="Lorde">Lorde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crowded_House" title="Crowded House">Crowded House</a> (bands/singers), <a href="/wiki/Lee_Tamahori" title="Lee Tamahori">Lee Tamahori</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taika_Waititi" title="Taika Waititi">Taika Waititi,</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a> (movie directors).</li> 
       <li>The NZ Army mostly does peacekeeping and natural disaster support. New Zealand sent army engineers to Iraq to do rebuilding during the <a href="/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq" title="2003 invasion of Iraq">2003 invasion of Iraq</a>.</li> 
       <li>The middle of New Zealand's North Island has an almost-desert. State Highway 1, the main north-south road, goes through that desert.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[22]</a></sup> This section of the road in the desert - the Desert Road - can often be closed in winter. It is called a desert because it has bare areas and unusual plants that normally grow on mountains.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[23]</a></sup></li>
      </ul>
