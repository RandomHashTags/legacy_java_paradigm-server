      <p><b>Suriname</b>, or <b>Surinam</b>, <b>Dutch Guiana</b>, <b>Dutch Guyana</b> or <b>Sranang</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. The official name of the country is <b>Republiek Suriname</b> (this is <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> and translates to <i><a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">Republic</a> of Suriname</i>). It used to be known as <i>Nederlands Guyana</i>, <i>Netherlands Guiana</i> or <i>Dutch Guiana</i>. It is between <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>. <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a>, in which resides almost half of the country's population. About half a million people live in the country. Suriname became independent from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> in <a href="/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a>. Before that, it was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>. </p>

      <p>There are border disputes with <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> all to the south of the country. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a> of the country is centered on the <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> and processing of <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>. This makes up 15 percent of the <a href="/wiki/Gross_Domestic_Product" class="mw-redirect" title="Gross Domestic Product">Gross Domestic Product</a> and more than 70% of the <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">exports</a>. Other exports are <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crude_oil" class="mw-redirect" title="Crude oil">oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>. About 25% of the working people are employed in <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>. The main trade partners are the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and countries in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>. </p>
      <p>Dutch, French, Spanish and English explorers discovered the area in the early 16th century. A century later, sugar <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantation</a> colonies were established by the Dutch and English along the many rivers in the fertile Guyana plains. After the second <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Dutch_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Dutch War">Anglo-Dutch War</a> England traded any claims to the territory for <a href="/wiki/New_Amsterdam" title="New Amsterdam">New Amsterdam</a> which became New York. </p>
