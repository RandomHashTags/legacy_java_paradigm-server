      <p><b>Saint Vincent and the Grenadines</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. It is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organisation_of_Eastern_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Organisation of Eastern Caribbean States (not yet started)">Organisation of Eastern Caribbean States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Community" title="Caribbean Community">CARICOM</a>. </p>
      <p>The country speaks <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> as its official language. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a>, and its main <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Kingstown_(Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingstown (Saint Vincent and the Grenadines)">Kingstown</a>. </p>
      <p>Its <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_birds" title="List of national birds">national bird</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_Amazon" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Vincent Amazon">Saint Vincent Amazon</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> bird of the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_(island)" title="Saint Vincent (island)">Saint Vincent</a> island.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Saint Vincent and the Grenadines is a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_system" title="Parliamentary system">parliamentary democracy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a> with <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a> as head of state. The Queen does not live in the islands but she is represented in the country by the <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor-General</a>, currently Sir <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Ballantyne" title="Frederick Ballantyne">Frederick Ballantyne</a>. </p>
      <p>Control of the government rests with the <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">elected</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">prime minister</a> and his or her <a href="/wiki/Cabinet" title="Cabinet">cabinet</a>. The current Prime Minister is <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Gonsalves" title="Ralph Gonsalves">Ralph Gonsalves</a>. </p>
      <p>The country has no formal armed forces, although the police has a Special Service Unit that has a supporting role on the island. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">Parliament</a> is <a href="/wiki/Unicameral" class="mw-redirect" title="Unicameral">unicameral</a> is formed only by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=House_of_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="House of Assembly (not yet started)">House of Assembly</a> with 21 seats: 15 <a href="/wiki/Representative_democracy" class="mw-redirect" title="Representative democracy">representatives</a> elected by popular vote to serve five-year terms and 6 appointed <a href="/wiki/Senator" class="mw-redirect" title="Senator">senators</a>. The last elections were held on 13 December 2010 and the next will be held in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The island now known as Saint Vincent was originally named <i>Youloumain</i><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> by the native <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caribe_people&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caribe people (not yet started)">Caribs</a>. The Caribs inhabited Saint Vincent before the Europeans arrived, and it is possible still to find Carib <a href="/wiki/Artifact_(archaeology)" title="Artifact (archaeology)">artifacts</a> in the island. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> explored in 1498 the main island on Saint Vincent's Day.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCTL_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCTL-5">[5]</a></sup> The Caribs did not allowed Europeans to settle in Saint Vincent until 1719 when some <a href="/wiki/French_people" class="mw-redirect" title="French people">French people</a> came from <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a> and began to grow <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indigo" title="Indigo">indigo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a> with the help of <a href="/wiki/African" class="mw-redirect" title="African">African</a> <a href="/wiki/Slave" class="mw-redirect" title="Slave">slaves</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1763 by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1763)" title="Treaty of Paris (1763)">Treaty of Paris</a>, France gave control of Saint Vincent to Britain; France took the island again in 1779, but the British then regained Saint Vincent under the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1783)" title="Treaty of Paris (1783)">Treaty of Versailles (1783)</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1834 <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> was abolished and people from the <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Madeira" class="mw-redirect" title="Madeira">Madeira</a> and from <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> came to work as <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> workers.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCTL_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCTL-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1871 the group of islands became part of the Windward Islands Colony and in 1956 a member of the Federation of the Windward Islands. In 1958 Saint Vincent joined the Federation of the West Indies and in 1969 it got full internal self-government. Finally in 1979 it became an Independent Sovereign State within the Commonwealt.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCTL_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCTL-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The estimated population in 2003 was 103,220. The population density is 265 persons per square <a href="/wiki/Kilometre" title="Kilometre">kilometre</a>. </p>
      <p>The ethnic composition was 66% <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">Black</a>, 19% <a href="/wiki/Mulatto" title="Mulatto">Mixed</a>, 6.0% East Indian, 4.0% <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">European</a> (mainly <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a>), 2.0% <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carib&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carib (not yet started)">Carib Amerindian</a>, 3.0% others.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="***REMOVED***">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Language">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Language">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The official language of the islands is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> but most people use the Vicentian <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">Creole</a> English.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The people of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines was 75% <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> (47% <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican Church">Anglicans</a>, 28% <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodists</a>), 13% Catholics, 12% other (<a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hindu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seventh-day_Adventist_Church" title="Seventh-day Adventist Church">Seventh-Day Adventists</a>, other Protestant).<sup id="cite_ref-cia_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Saint Vincent and the Grenadines is a group of islands of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. It lies to the west of <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a> and north of <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>. </p>
      <p>There are 32 islands and <a href="/wiki/Cay" title="Cay">cays</a>, including the main island of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_(island)" title="Saint Vincent (island)">Saint Vincent</a> (344 square kilometres (133&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi)) and the northern two-thirds of the Grenadines (45 square kilometres (17&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi)), which are a chain of small islands stretching south from Saint Vincent to Grenada. The largest and most populated Grenadines islands are Bequia, Mustique, Canouan and Union Island.<sup id="cite_ref-gov_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gov-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The main island of <b>Saint Vincent</b>, at 
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r7263774"><span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;params=13_15_N_61_12_W_region:ID_type:isle&amp;title=Saint+Vincent"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">13°15′N</span> <span class="longitude">61°12′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="vcard"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">13.250°N 61.200°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">13.250; -61.200</span></span><span style="display:none">﻿ (<span class="fn org">Saint Vincent</span>)</span></span></span></a></span>, is 18 kilometres (11&nbsp;mi) long and 11 kilometres (6.8&nbsp;mi) wide, and has an area of 344 square kilometres (133&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi), or about 88% of the total country area, 19 times that of the country's second largest island <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bequia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bequia (not yet started)">Bequia</a>. It is dominated by the highest mountain in the country and an active <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Soufri%C3%A8re_(volcano)" title="La Soufrière (volcano)">La Soufrière</a> (1,234 metres (4,049&nbsp;ft)), which erupted violently in 1812 and 1902. The most recent eruption was on April 13, 1979. The island has many mountains and <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>. The island is tropical humid, with an average of between 18 and 31&nbsp;°C depending on the altitude. </p>
      <p>The Saint Vincent Passage is between the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a> and Saint Vincent islands, and the Bequia Channel is between the Grenadines and Saint Vincent islands. </p>

The islands north of the Martinique Channel belong to Saint Vincent and the Grenadines and the islands south of the channel belong to Grenada. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Administratively, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines is divided into six <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parishes</a>. Five parishes are on Saint Vincent, while the sixth is made up of the Grenadine islands. <a href="/wiki/Kingstown" title="Kingstown">Kingstown</a> is located in the Parish of Saint George and is the capital city and central administrative centre of the country. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>5 parishes on Saint Vincent: 
        <ul>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Tropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical">Tropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a> climate is a type of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climate</a> in which there is no dry season - all months have mean <a href="/wiki/Precipitation" title="Precipitation">precipitation</a> values of at least 60 millimetres (2.4&nbsp;in). Tropical rainforest climates have no pronounced summer or winter; it is typically hot and wet throughout the year and <a href="/wiki/Rainfall" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> is both heavy and frequent.<sup id="cite_ref-Weatherbase_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weatherbase-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> subtype for this climate is <b>Af</b> (Tropical Rainforest Climate).<sup id="cite_ref-Weatherbase_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weatherbase-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The average yearly temperature is 27&nbsp;°C (81&nbsp;°F). The coolest months are between November and February. During the rainy season, May through October, rain is frequent in the mountains of St. Vincent with the annual average rainfall being 380 centimetres (150&nbsp;in) away from the coast and 200 centimetres (79&nbsp;in) on the coast.<sup id="cite_ref-gov_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gov-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
