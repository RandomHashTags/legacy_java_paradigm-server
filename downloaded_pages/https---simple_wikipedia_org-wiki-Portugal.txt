      <p><b>Portugal</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Europe" title="Southern Europe">Southern Europe</a> on the Iberian Peninsula. Portugal is the westernmost country of Europe and part of the Mediterranean. It is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to the west and south and by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> to the north and east. It has been a member of the European Union since 1986. It was under a dictatorship between 1926 and 1974. It has since prospered but was hit hard by the 2007-2008 recession. </p>
      <p>It has a football team and won the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2017" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2017">Eurovision Song Contest 2017</a> meaning it hosts the event for the first time ever in 2018. </p>
      <p>It once had an empire and was a powerful maritime nation from 1500s‚Äì1800s, the 10th-largest empire with a maximum land area of 10.4 million km¬≤ which included <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, Angola, Mozambique, Guinea-Bissau, East Timoa, S√£o Tom√© and Pr√≠ncipe, Portuguese Ceylon (<a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>) and even Macau in China (until 2002). </p>
      <p>Three groups of <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> are also part of Portugal: the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> (<i>A√ßores</i>), <a href="/wiki/Madeira_Islands" title="Madeira Islands">Madeira</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Savage_Isles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Savage Isles (not yet started)">Savage Isles</a> (<i>Ilhas Selvagens</i>). The Savage Isles are a small group of uninhabited islands, administered by Madeira. Portugal claims that <a href="/wiki/Olivenza" title="Olivenza">Oliven√ßa</a> is also part of its territory, but it is controlled by Spain. </p>
      <p>It's a popular holiday destination but, is sometimes overlooked in favour of larger countries like France, Spain and Italy. The Algarve region in the south and capital Lisbon are the most popular. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portugal&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Portugal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Portugal became its own <a href="/wiki/Kingdom" title="Kingdom">kingdom</a> in <a href="/wiki/1139" title="1139">1139</a> but was not officially recognized until <a href="/wiki/1143" title="1143">1143</a>. Portugal has had links with England since the 1100s via a treaty. The border with <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> has been almost the same since the <a href="/wiki/13th_century" title="13th century">13th century</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">Fishing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> with other countries are important here. </p>
      <p>Portugal was important in world <a href="/wiki/Exploration" title="Exploration">exploration</a> for two reasons. <a href="/wiki/Henry_the_Navigator" title="Henry the Navigator">Henry the Navigator</a>, a prince from Portugal, was very interested in exploration. <a href="/wiki/Invention" title="Invention">Inventions</a> in <a href="/wiki/Navigation" title="Navigation">navigation</a> led to a bigger knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a>. </p>
      <p>This world exploration began the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese Empire</a>. Portugal was a <a href="/wiki/World_power" class="mw-redirect" title="World power">world power</a> during the <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">15th</a> and <a href="/wiki/16th_century" title="16th century">16th centuries</a>. It tried to colonize Canada in the 16th century. However, it lost a lot of money soon after this. Portugal and Spain were in the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Union" title="Iberian Union">Iberian Union</a> from 1580 to 1640. The city of <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> was destroyed in an <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> in <a href="/wiki/1755" title="1755">1755</a>. The country was <a href="/wiki/Occupation" class="mw-disambig" title="Occupation">occupied</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>. It lost its largest <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1822" title="1822">1822</a>. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/1910" title="1910">1910</a>, Portugal became a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">Republic</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> group took control of the country from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portuguese_1st_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Portuguese 1st Republic (not yet started)">Portuguese 1st Republic</a>. This began a time of <a href="/wiki/Rule" title="Rule">rule</a> by authoritarian <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">governments</a>. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> they were neutral but friendly to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>. In 1974 a peaceful <a href="/wiki/Left-wing" class="mw-redirect" title="Left-wing">left-wing</a> army <a href="/wiki/Coup" class="mw-redirect" title="Coup">coup</a>, called the <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnation_Revolution&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carnation Revolution (not yet started)">Carnation Revolution</a></i>, happened. The coup changed how the country was run. </p>
      <p>The next year, Portugal allowed its colonies in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> to be on their own: <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="S√£o Tom√© and Pr√≠ncipe">S√£o Tom√© and Pr√≠ncipe</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> declared itself independent from Portugal on 28 November 1975, and was invaded and occupied by <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> forces nine days later. </p>
      <p>Portugal went into the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> in <a href="/wiki/1986" title="1986">1986</a>. Another <a href="/wiki/Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian">Asian</a> colony, <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, became part of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> </p>
      <p>The main language of Portugal is <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>. </p>
      <p>Some famous Portuguese people are <a href="/wiki/Afonso_I_of_Portugal" title="Afonso I of Portugal">D. Afonso Henriques</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_the_Navigator" title="Henry the Navigator">Henry the Navigator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bartolomeu_Dias" title="Bartolomeu Dias">Bartolomeu Dias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_%C3%81lvares_Cabral" title="Pedro √Ålvares Cabral">Pedro √Ålvares Cabral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_de_Cam%C3%B5es" title="Lu√≠s de Cam√µes">Lu√≠s de Cam√µes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Pessoa" title="Fernando Pessoa">Fernando Pessoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A1lia_Rodrigues" title="Am√°lia Rodrigues">Am√°lia Rodrigues</a>, √Ålvaro Siza Vieira, Eduardo Souto de Moura. </p>
      <p>Lu√≠s de Cam√µes wrote the national poem of Portugal. It is called <i>Os Lus√≠adas</i> and was written in 1572.<sup id="cite_ref-WDL_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WDL-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
