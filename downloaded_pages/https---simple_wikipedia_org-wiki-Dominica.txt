      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>.
      </div> 
      <p><b>The Commonwealth of Dominica</b> is an island nation in the Caribbean Sea. It is an English-speaking country<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>. It is between the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>-speaking territories of <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>. The island is 751&nbsp;km² (290&nbsp;mi²) in area. <a href="/wiki/Roseau" title="Roseau">Roseau</a> is its capital. Portsmouth is another main town. Almost 70,000 people live on the island. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> of the island is the <a href="/wiki/East_Caribbean_dollar" title="East Caribbean dollar">East Caribbean dollar</a>. </p>
      <p>The island's name comes from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sunday" title="Sunday">Sunday</a>, <i>Dies Dominica</i>. It is not the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>-speaking <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a>. </p>
      <p>The country is <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nicknamed</a> the <i>Nature Island of the Caribbean</i>. </p>
      <p>Dominica is most famously depicted in <a href="/wiki/Jean_Rhys" title="Jean Rhys">Jean Rhys</a>' classic <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/prequel" class="extiw" title="wikt:prequel">prequel</a> to <i><a href="/wiki/Jane_Eyre" title="Jane Eyre">Jane Eyre</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wide_Sargasso_Sea" title="Wide Sargasso Sea">Wide Sargasso Sea</a></i>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The indigenous Carib people lived in Dominca before it was found by Europeans. They called it <i>Wai‘tu kubuli</i>, which means "Tall is her body." </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> came to Dominica in 1493. In 1763, it became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> from which its <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> was received on 3 November 1978. </p>
      <p>In August 1979, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_David" title="Hurricane David">Hurricane David</a>, wiped out its entire <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a>. On 21 November 2004, the island was hit by its most destructive <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> in history; Portsmouth and the northern area were deeply affected. </p>
      <p>On 18 September 2018, Hurricane Maria, the most powerful storm to hit the island, severely damaged its infrastructure and cut off all its communications to the outside world. The Assessment Capacities Project estimates that the hurricane has caused $1.37 billion in losses across the island, which is equal to 226 percent of its 2016 GDP. As of April 12, there are 65 fatalities confirmed across the island, including 34 who are missing and presumed to be dead.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1980, <a href="/wiki/Dame" class="mw-redirect" title="Dame">Dame</a> <a href="/wiki/Eugenia_Charles" title="Eugenia Charles">Eugenia Charles</a> became its prime minister, the first female government leader in the West Indies. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Dominica is an <a href="/wiki/Island_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Island nation">island nation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. It is the northernmost of the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a>. </p>
      <p>Dominica is largely covered by <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a>. It is home to the world's second-largest <a href="/wiki/Hot_spring" title="Hot spring">hot spring</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Boiling_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Boiling Lake (not yet started)">Boiling Lake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bl_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bl-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morne_Trois_Pitons_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Morne Trois Pitons National Park (not yet started)">Morne Trois Pitons National Park</a> is a tropical forest with scenic volcanic features. It was recognised as a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> on 4 April 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a> is a popular sport on the island. Dominica competes in <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">test cricket</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team" title="West Indies cricket team">West Indies cricket team</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Netball" title="Netball">Netball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> are gaining popularity as well. </p>
