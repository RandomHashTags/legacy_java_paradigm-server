      <p><b>Sierra Leone</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a>. The official language is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. </p>
      <p>The country was first made as a place where freed slaves could live. From <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> until <a href="/wiki/2000" title="2000">2000</a>, there was a civil war in Sierra Leone between rebels and the government. The war is now over. Sierra Leone is known for its <a href="/wiki/Blood_diamond" title="Blood diamond">blood diamonds</a>. These were mined and sold during the <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a>. This was in order to buy the weapons for the civil war.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are 71,740 square kilometres of land in Sierra; In terms of land area it is similar in size to <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. </p>
      <p>Sierra Leone has relied on <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamonds</a>, for its economic base. The country is among the largest producers of <a href="/wiki/Titanium" title="Titanium">titanium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, and a major producer of <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>. The country has one of the world's largest deposits of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rutile&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rutile (not yet started)">rutile</a>. Sierra Leone is also home to the third largest natural harbour in the world. Shipping from all over the globe goes to Freetown's famous <a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_Elizabeth_II_Quay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Queen Elizabeth II Quay (not yet started)">Queen Elizabeth II Quay</a>. Despite this natural wealth, 70% of its people live in <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Sierra Leone is a mostly <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> country.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The country has 2,090 known <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of higher plants, 147 <a href="/wiki/Mammals" class="mw-redirect" title="Mammals">mammals</a>, 626 <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, 67 <a href="/wiki/Reptiles" class="mw-redirect" title="Reptiles">reptiles</a>, 35 <a href="/wiki/Amphibians" class="mw-redirect" title="Amphibians">amphibians</a>, and 99 <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> species. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leone&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography and climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The country is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> to the north and northeast, <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a> to the south and southeast, and the Atlantic Ocean to the west.<sup id="cite_ref-LeVertP7_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeVertP7-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Bintumani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Bintumani (not yet started)">Mount Bintumani</a> reaches 1,948&nbsp;m (6,391&nbsp;ft). It is the highest point in the country. </p>
      <p>The <b>climate</b> is <b>hot, tropical and humid all year round, with two seasons.</b> The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainy_season&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rainy season (not yet started)">rainy season</a> is from May to November, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dry_season&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dry season (not yet started)">dry season</a> is from December to May. </p>
      <p>March &amp; April are the hottest months and daytime temperatures are around <b>38&nbsp;°C</b> (100&nbsp;°F) - <b>41&nbsp;°C</b> (106&nbsp;°F) with a solid 82% humidity. Night time temperatures do not vary a lot and may fluctuate by <b>2&nbsp;°C - 4&nbsp;°C</b>. </p>
      <p>December and January are prefably the best times to visit Sierra Leone as temperatures and humidity are much lower and reasonable due to the hammattan breeze from the sahara desert, although afternoon temperatures can still climb up to <b>40&nbsp;°C</b> and above, the hammattan breeze stops it from getting too humid, even though it will still be hot, humidity would be can be lowered down to 39% meaning you'll sweat less during this time than you would when the climate goes back to its original state, so therefore the heat would be more endurable especially around coastal areas. </p>
      <p>When the hammattan breeze has lowered the heat and the humidity to its base, the highest average daily temperature in December and January will only reach a maximum of <b>31&nbsp;°C</b> (88&nbsp;°F) and night time temperatures will be <b>24&nbsp;°C</b> (75&nbsp;°F), although it can seldomly drop down to <b>21&nbsp;°C</b> (70&nbsp;°F) at night, and sometimes in the morning which is the lowest the temperature can get. During this time, humidity is 70% &amp; it is pleasantly warm and enjoyable. </p>
      <p>The rainy season from <b>May-November</b>, average temperatures are <b>27&nbsp;°C</b> (81&nbsp;°F) - <b>29&nbsp;°C</b> (84&nbsp;°F) with humidity 95% - 100%. Some days when it doesn't rain, temperatures can climb up to <b>32&nbsp;°C</b> (90&nbsp;°F) and humidity can be moderate around 75%. Rainfall can be torrential, so if you're travelling to Sierra Leone around this time of the year, it is advisable to bring water proof clothing as it can rain for a whole week without interruption from sunlight. </p>

      <p>Education in Sierra Leone is legally required for all children for six years. A shortage of schools and teachers has made this impossible.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-8">[8]</a></sup> Two thirds of the adult population of the country are not able to read.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leone_Civil_War&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sierra Leone Civil War (not yet started)">Sierra Leone Civil War</a> caused the destruction of 1,270 primary schools. In 2001, 67% of all school-age children were out of school.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-8">[8]</a></sup> This has been better since the end of the civil war. </p>
      <p>The country has three <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">universities</a>: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fourah_Bay_College&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fourah Bay College (not yet started)">Fourah Bay College</a>, University of Makeni and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Njala_University&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Njala University (not yet started)">Njala University</a>. Teacher training colleges and religious seminaries are found in many parts of the country. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_customs">Food and customs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leone&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Food and customs">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Food and customs">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">Rice</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Staple_food" title="Staple food">staple food</a> of Sierra Leone. It is eaten at nearly every meal daily. The rice is prepared in many ways, and topped with different sauces made from some of Sierra Leone's favorite toppings. These include <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potato</a> leaves, <a href="/wiki/Cassava" class="mw-redirect" title="Cassava">cassava</a> leaves, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Corchorus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Corchorus (not yet started)">crain crain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Okra" title="Okra">okra</a> soup, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fried_fish&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fried fish (not yet started)">fried fish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">groundnut</a> stew.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Along the street of towns and cities one can find snacks such as fresh <a href="/wiki/Mangoes" class="mw-redirect" title="Mangoes">mangoes</a>, oranges, pineapple, fried <a href="/w/index.php?title=Plantains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plantains (not yet started)">plantains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ginger_beer" title="Ginger beer">ginger beer</a>, fried potato, fried <a href="/wiki/Cassava" class="mw-redirect" title="Cassava">cassava</a> with pepper sauce; small bags of popcorn or peanuts, bread, roasted corn, or skewers of grilled meat or shrimp. </p>
      <p>Poyo is a popular Sierra Leonean drink. It is a sweet, lightly fermented <a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_wine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Palm wine (not yet started)">palm wine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Poyo bars are areas of lively informal talk about politics, <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>, entertainment and other issues. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leone&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Football</a> is by far the most popular sport in Sierra Leone. Children, youth and adult are often seen playing <a href="/w/index.php?title=Street_football&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Street football (not yet started)">street football</a> across Sierra Leone. </p>
      <p>The Sierra Leone national football team, popularly known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leone_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leone Stars (not yet started)">Leone Stars</a>, represents the country in international competitions. It has never qualified for the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>. They were in the <a href="/wiki/1994_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="1994 African Cup of Nations">1994</a> and <a href="/wiki/1996_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="1996 African Cup of Nations">1996 African Cup of Nations</a>. Many of the national team footballers are <a href="/wiki/Celebrities" class="mw-redirect" title="Celebrities">celebrities</a> across Sierra Leone. They are often well known by most of the country's general population. Some well known Sierra Leonean international footballers include <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Kallon" title="Mohamed Kallon">Mohamed Kallon</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Bangura_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mohamed Bangura (footballer) (not yet started)">Mohamed Bangura</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rodney_Strasser&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rodney Strasser (not yet started)">Rodney Strasser</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ibrahim_Teteh_Bangura&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ibrahim Teteh Bangura (not yet started)">Ibrahim Teteh Bangura</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Bangura&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Al Bangura (not yet started)">Alhassan Bangura</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheriff_Suma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sheriff Suma (not yet started)">Sheriff Suma</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kamara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mohamed Kamara (not yet started)">Mohamed Kamara</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Umaru_Bangura&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Umaru Bangura (not yet started)">Umaru Bangura</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kei_Kamara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kei Kamara (not yet started)">Kei Kamara</a>. </p>
