      <p>The <b>Dominican Republic</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Rep√∫blica Dominicana</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>; the other country on the island is <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a>, and largest <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>. The national language is <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>. </p>
      <p>The country was part of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_empire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spanish empire (not yet started)">Spanish empire</a> until the late 18th century. In the 19th century France, Spain and Haiti controlled it at various times, and later it was independent. It was occupied by the United States from 1916 to 1924. The Dominican Republic is a <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">presidential</a> <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The country has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climate</a> but modified by <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevation</a> and the <i>trade winds</i> (<a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">winds</a> that come from the northeast, from the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The island of Hispaniola was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> on December 5th, 1492,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> but the first time that he saw part of the present Dominican Republic was on January 4th, 1493 when he saw a <a href="/wiki/Headlands_and_bays" title="Headlands and bays">headland</a> that he named <i>Monte Cristi</i> ("Mountain of Christ").<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> That mountain is called now <i>El Morro</i> and is near the city of <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando_de_Monte_Cristi" title="San Fernando de Monte Cristi">Monte Cristi</a>. </p>
      <p>From Monte Cristi, Columbus went east along the north coast of the island and on 6 January, after visiting the <a href="/wiki/Saman%C3%A1_Bay" title="Saman√° Bay">Saman√° Bay</a>, he went back to <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. In his second trip to <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">America</a>, he founded the first European city in the continent, <i>La Isabela</i>, near the present city of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Plata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Puerto Plata (not yet started)">Puerto Plata</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Later, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartholomew_Columbus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bartholomew Columbus (not yet started)">Bartholomew Columbus</a> founded the city of <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>, the oldest permanent European city in the Americas. From here, many <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spaniards</a> went to conquer other islands (<a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>). Because Cuba was closer to the continent, many people moved there from Hispaniola, and then to the continent. Because of that, the population of the island grew very slowly. By the Treaty of Ryswick in 1697, Spain gave the western third of the island to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and kept the eastern part, and so the island had two different colonies: the French <i>Saint-Domingue</i> and the Spanish <i>Santo Domingo</i>. In 1795, France got the whole island but they could only control the eastern part because <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> became independent very soon. In 1809, the French government gave back the old colony of "Santo Domingo" to Spain. </p>
      <p>On 1 December 1821, the Spanish lieutenant governor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_C%C3%A1ceres&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jos√© N√∫√±ez de C√°ceres (not yet started)">Jos√© N√∫√±ez de C√°ceres</a> declared the <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> from Spain. The new country had the name <i>Estado Independiente del Hait√≠ Espa√±ol</i> ("Independent State of Spanish Haiti"). But on 9 February 1822, the Haitian army occupied the country and stayed for 22 years. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Pablo_Duarte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Juan Pablo Duarte (not yet started)">Juan Pablo Duarte</a> founded a secret society, <i>La Trinitaria</i>, to fight for the Dominican independence. The Haitian occupation ended on 27 February 1844, when the inhabitants of the eastern part of the Hispaniola made a new country named <i>Rep√∫blica Dominicana</i> ("Dominican Republic"). From 1861 to 1865, the country was again a Spanish colony. On 16 August 1863 began the <i>Restoration War</i> when the Dominicans fought to be free again. That war ended in 1865 when the Spanish left and the Dominican Republic was again an independent country.<sup id="cite_ref-Dominican_Republic_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dominican_Republic-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The country was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> from 1916 to 1924. In 1930, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rafael_Leonidas_Trujillo_Molina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rafael Leonidas Trujillo Molina (not yet started)">Rafael Trujillo</a> became the president of the country through a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'√©tat">coup d'√©tat</a>. Trujillo was a cruel <a href="/wiki/Dictator" title="Dictator">dictator</a>, killing thousands of people, among them many Haitians. Trujillo was killed in 1961. After the death of Trujillo, Juan Bosch was elected in 1962 and became, in 1963, the first elected president since 1930. But was Bosch was in power for only seven months. In 1965, there was a civil war between those that wanted Bosch back on power and those that were opposed to him. Then the country was invaded again by the United States. </p>
      <p>Since the end of the civil war, the presidents of the Dominican Republic have been:<sup id="cite_ref-Dominican_Republic_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dominican_Republic-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="Government of the Dominican Republic">Government of the Dominican Republic</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The Dominican Republic is a <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">presidential</a> <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The government is divided in three branches: the Executive, the <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">Legislative</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Judiciary" title="Judiciary">Judiciary</a>. The Executive branch is made up of the <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">President</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Vice_President" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice President">Vice President</a> and the Ministers who are called <i>Secretaries of State</i>. The President is chief of state and head of government and is elected every 4 years. He nominates the cabinet. The current president is Danilo Medina S√°nchez. </p>
      <p>The Legislative branch makes the laws and is made up of the Congress, which is in <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>, the capital of the Dominican Republic. The Congress is divided into two groups: the Senate, with 32 members (one for every province and one for the National District), and the Chamber of Deputies with 178 members.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Judicial branch is made up of the <a href="/wiki/Court" title="Court">courts</a> of the country, including the Supreme Court of Justice.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Dominican Republic is a <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitutional</a> <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> ruled by a <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a>. The president is elected every 4 years. The current president is Danilo Medina S√°nchez, of the PLD. </p>
      <p>There are 3 important <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a> in the Dominican Republic: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>PRD</b>: the Dominican Revolutionary Party (Spanish: <i>Partido Revolucionario Dominicano</i>). The PRD is a somewhat <a href="/wiki/Socialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist">socialist</a> party. The party was founded in 1939 in <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. It was then established in the Dominican Republic in 1961.</li> 
       <li><b>PRSC</b>: the Social Christian Reformist Party (Spanish: <i>Partido Reformista Social Cristiano</i>). It is a <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservative</a> party founded in 1964 by Joaqu√≠n Balager, who was President of the Republic from 1966-78 and 1986-96.</li> 
       <li><b>PLD</b>: the Dominican Liberation Party (Spanish: <i>Partido de la Liberaci√≥n Dominicana</i>) was somewhat <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialist</a> when it was founded in 1973; currently it's a <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a> party.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_land">The land</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: The land">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: The land">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="Geography of the Dominican Republic">Geography of the Dominican Republic</a>
      </div> 
      <p>There are several mountain chains in the Dominican Republic. The four main chains, from North to South, are: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li><i><a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Septentrional" title="Cordillera Septentrional">Cordillera Septentrional</a></i> (in English, "Northern mountain range"), close to the Atlantic Ocean.</li> 
       <li><i>Cordillera Central</i> (in English, "Central mountain range") that continues into northern Haiti where it is called <i>Massif du Nord</i>. The highest mountains of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">West Indies</a> are in this chain; <i>Pico Duarte</i>, with 3,087 m, is the highest.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The main rivers of the Hispaniola have their sources in this mountain range.</li> 
       <li><i>Sierra de Neiba</i>.</li> 
       <li><i>Sierra de Bahoruco</i>, known in Hait√≠ as <i>Massif de la Selle</i>.</li>
      </ol> 
      <p>Between those mountains, there are several important valleys, such as: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>The Cibao Valley (Dominican Republic) is the largest and most important valley of the country. This long valley stretches from North Haiti to Saman√° Bay, south of the Cordillera Septentrional.</li> 
       <li>The San Juan Valley and <a href="/wiki/Plain" title="Plain">Plain</a> of Azua are big valleys south of the Cordillera Central.</li> 
       <li>The <i>Hoya de Enriquillo</i> or Neiba Valley is a very arid valley south of Sierra de Neiba.</li> 
       <li><i>Llano Costero del Caribe</i> (in English, "Caribbean Coastal Plain") is in the southeast of the country. It is a large <a href="/wiki/Prairie" title="Prairie">prairie</a> east of <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>. There are very important <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a> plantations in this plain.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>There are other smaller valleys in the mountains: Constanza, Jarabacoa, Bonao, Villa Altagracia.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The four most important rivers of the Dominican Republic are <a href="/wiki/River_Yaque_del_Norte" class="mw-redirect" title="River Yaque del Norte">Yaque del Norte</a>, Yuna, Yaque del Sur and <a href="/wiki/River_Ozama" class="mw-redirect" title="River Ozama">Ozama</a>. There are many lakes; the largest is the <i><a href="/wiki/Lake_Enriquillo" title="Lake Enriquillo">Lake Enriquillo</a></i>, in the <i>Hoya de Enriquillo</i>. </p>
      <p>The country has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climate</a> but modified by <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevation</a> and the <i>trade winds</i> (<a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">winds</a> that come from the northeast, from the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>). At sea level, the <a href="/wiki/Average" title="Average">average</a> temperature is 25¬∞C, with small changes from one season to another. In the highest mountains, the temperature in <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a> can be as low as 0¬∞C.<sup id="cite_ref-Climate_caribbean_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Climate_caribbean-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are two wet seasons: April-June and September-November. The most dry period is from December to March. <a href="/wiki/Rainfall" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainfall">Rainfall</a> varies greatly; eastern regions, like the Saman√° Peninsula, get an average of over 2,000&nbsp;mm in a year, but less than 500&nbsp;mm fall in the southwest (Hoya de Enriquillo).<sup id="cite_ref-Climate_caribbean_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Climate_caribbean-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>From June to November, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane">hurricanes</a> are frequent and can do much damage in the island. </p>

      <p>The Dominican Republic has a total population, estimated for July 2009, of 9,650,054 inhabitants, for a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 236.30 inhabitants per km¬≤. </p>
      <p>About 64% of Dominicans live in cities and towns and 87% of people that are 15 years old or more can read and write.<sup id="cite_ref-censo_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-censo-25">[25]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The two largest cities are <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a> (the capital city) with 1,817,754 inhabitants, and Santiago, in the Cibao Valley and with 908,250 inhabitants.<sup id="cite_ref-censo_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-censo-25">[25]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The ethnic composition of Dominicans is around 45% Mulatto, 40% Black, and 15% White </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Mulatto Dominicans: They are mainly descendants of Southern European colonist and West African slaves.</li> 
       <li>Black Dominicans: They are descendants of West Africans that were brought over as slaves to work mostly on sugar cane plantations. Most of the African ancestry of Dominicans can be traced back to the West African countries of Ghana, Cameroon, and Angola.</li> 
       <li>White Dominicans: They are mainly descendants of Spaniards, Portuguese, and French colonist. Most of the European ancestry of Dominicans comes from the Canary Islands and Southern Spain, while many others descend from Portuguese, Galicians, Asturians, and French people.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Dominican Republic has a mixed economy based mainly on <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, services (including <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Finance" title="Finance">finance</a>), <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> and money sent from the many Dominicans that live in other countries (<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>). Agricultural production (mainly sugarcane, with smaller amounts of coffee, cacao, and tobacco) was the main economic activity but now is in third place after tourism and <a href="/wiki/Manufacture" class="mw-redirect" title="Manufacture">manufacturing</a> in <i>zonas francas</i> ("<a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">free zones</a>" where the industries do not pay <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">taxes</a> and all the production is sent to other countries).<sup id="cite_ref-cia_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">Mining</a> is also important, mainly ferronickel (<a href="/wiki/Nickel" title="Nickel">nickel</a> with <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>. </p>
      <p>The Dominican Republic suffers from <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, with 83.3% of the population living below the poverty line in 2012. The wealth distribution is uneven: the richest 10% gets nearly 40% of national income.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-20">[20]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The culture of the Dominican Republic, like in other Caribbean countries, is a mix of <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta√≠no">Ta√≠no</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> (mainly <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a>) cultures. </p>
      <p>There are not many Ta√≠no traditions in the modern Dominican culture; many places keep their Ta√≠no names: Dajab√≥n, B√°nica, Haina, Yaque, Saman√°, etc. Also many objects, plants and animals have a Ta√≠no origin and their names have been included in other languages; for example: <i>canoa</i> (<a href="/wiki/Canoe" title="Canoe">canoe</a>, a small <a href="/wiki/Boat" title="Boat">boat</a>), <i>hamaca</i> (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Hammock&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hammock (not yet started)">hammock</a>, a simple bed), <i>ma√≠z</i> (<a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">maize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corn" class="mw-redirect" title="Corn">corn</a>), <i>yuca</i> (<a href="/wiki/Cassava" class="mw-redirect" title="Cassava">cassava</a>, that comes from the Ta√≠no word <i>casabe</i>, a kind of cassava bread eaten in the Caribbean), and <i>batata</i> (<a href="/wiki/Sweet_potato" title="Sweet potato">sweet potato</a>). </p>
      <p>That mix of different traditions created a culture that is known as <a href="/wiki/Creole" class="mw-redirect" title="Creole">Creole</a> (in Spanish: <i>Criolla</i>), common to all countries in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> and some parts of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Languages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Languages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the Dominican Republic, only Spanish is spoken, however; there are 3 major languages that are also spoken such as <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Creole_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Haitian Creole language">Haitian Creole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samana_English" class="mw-redirect" title="Samana English">Samana English</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West African</a> <a href="/wiki/Yoruba" class="mw-redirect" title="Yoruba">Yoruba</a> language known as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucumi_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lucumi language (not yet started)">Lucumi</a> spoken by few. There is a local dialect or patois which is spoken by all Dominicans - see <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Spanish_Creole" class="mw-redirect" title="Dominican Spanish Creole">Dominican Spanish Creole</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religions">Religions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Religions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The official religion is <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a> but there is freedom of religion. <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> groups are important, representing about 15% of the total population.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-20">[20]</a></sup> Each year major festivities called <i>fiestas patronales</i> are held. They are <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Catholic</a> festivities in honor of the Patron saints of the towns and villages; it is a Spanish tradition to associate every town with a Catholic saint. The festivities includes church services, street parades, fire works, dance contests and other activities. Dominican Voodoo or Santeria is also practiced in large numbers as well as Dominican Palo, Dominican Kongo religion, and others. </p>
      <p>African culture has the strongest influence in Dominican culture, especially in language, religion, food, and music. </p>
