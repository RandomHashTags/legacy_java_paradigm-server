      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Name">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Name">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> <a href="/wiki/Explorer" class="mw-redirect" title="Explorer">explorer</a> Dinis Dias (or Denis Fernadez) discovered in 1445 a <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> along the coast of <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> that he named <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cap-Vert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cap-Vert (not yet started)">Cap-Vert</a> (Dias named it <i>Cabo Verde</i>, "verde" being <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> for "green", a reference to the <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a> in the area). Dias did not discover the <i>Cape Verde Islands</i>, but rather the actual <a href="/wiki/Headlands_and_bays" title="Headlands and bays">cape</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>This peninsula is the westernmost point of the <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continent</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">

      <p>The first <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a> to arrive in Cape Verde were the Portugueses Diogo Gomes and Antonio da Noli in 1460. The islands were uninhabited, and the first settlement was founded in 1462 on the island of Santiago (the main Island) which was divided into two “capitanias”, Alcatrazes and Ribeira Grande. The first one failed and the main activity in Ribeira Grande was the exploitation of cotton farms. Ribeira Grande served also as a slave trade post and as post of slave Christianization before they were sent to the New World. </p>
      <p>The city suffered several pirate raids and for this reason in 1712, after a French attack, the authorities were forced to move the capital to Praia, where it is located until now. Cape Verde had the status of Portuguese colony until 1951 when Portugal changed its status to Overseas Province and in 1961 Portugal gave full citizenship to all Cape Verdeans. </p>
      <p>Cape Verde has been independent of Portugal since 5th July 1975. The fight for independence was led by the African Party for the Independence of Guinea and Cape Verde (PAIGC) and headed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Amilcar_Cabral&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Amilcar Cabral (not yet started)">Amilcar Cabral</a>, the national hero of both countries, Cape Verde and <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>. </p>

      <p>The Cape Verde archipelago is in the Atlantic Ocean, approximately 570&nbsp;km (350&nbsp;mi) off the coast of West Africa, near <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Gambia" title="The Gambia">Gambia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>, and is a part, together with the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madeira" class="mw-redirect" title="Madeira">Madeira</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Savage_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Savage Islands (not yet started)">Savage Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Macaronesia" title="Macaronesia">Macaronesia</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecological</a> <a href="/wiki/Region" title="Region">region</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cvcom_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cvcom-11">[11]</a></sup> It lies between the <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitudes</a> 14°N and 18°N, and the <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitudes</a> 22°W and 26°W. </p>
      <p>The country is an <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> with a total area of 4,033&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (1,557&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). It is formed by ten islands (nine inhabited) and several very small islands (<a href="/wiki/Islet" title="Islet">islets</a>) divided into two groups, arranged according to the prevailing wind direction: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>To the north, the <i><b>Ilhas de Barlavento</b></i> (<a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/windward" class="extiw" title="wikt:windward">Windward</a> islands), from west to east: <a href="/wiki/Santo_Ant%C3%A3o,_Cape_Verde" title="Santo Antão, Cape Verde">Santo Antão</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Vicente,_Cape_Verde" title="São Vicente, Cape Verde">São Vicente</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Luzia,_Cape_Verde" title="Santa Luzia, Cape Verde">Santa Luzia</a> (without inhabitants), <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Nicolau,_Cape_Verde" title="São Nicolau, Cape Verde">São Nicolau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sal,_Cape_Verde" title="Sal, Cape Verde">Ilha do Sal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boa_Vista,_Cape_Verde" title="Boa Vista, Cape Verde">Boa Vista</a>; and the islets of Branco and Raso, situated between Santa Luzia and São Nicolau, the islet of Pássaros, opposite the town of Mindelo on the island of São Vicente, and the islets Rabo de Junco, on the coast of the Sal island and the islets Sal Rei and Baluarte, on the coast of the island of Boa Vista.</li> 
       <li>To the south <i><b>Ilhas de Sotavento</b></i> (<a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/leeward" class="extiw" title="wikt:leeward">Leeward</a> islands), from east to west: <a href="/wiki/Maio,_Cape_Verde" title="Maio, Cape Verde">Maio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santiago,_Cape_Verde" title="Santiago, Cape Verde">Santiago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fogo,_Cape_Verde" title="Fogo, Cape Verde">Fogo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brava,_Cape_Verde" title="Brava, Cape Verde">Brava</a>; and the islet of Santa Maria, in front of the city of Praia, on Santiago island, the islets Grande, Rombo, Baixo, Cima, do Rei, Luís Carneiro and Sapado, situated about 8&nbsp;km from the island of Brava, and the islet Areia along the coast of the same island.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The largest islands are, to the southeast, Santiago, where lies <a href="/wiki/Praia" title="Praia">Praia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> and largest city of the country, and the island of Santo Antão, in the extreme northwest. Praia is also the main population centre of the archipelago, followed by Mindelo on the island of São Vicente. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The sun shines 350 days a year and temperatures range between 21 and 29&nbsp;°C (70 and 84&nbsp;°F). The <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/breeze" class="extiw" title="wikt:breeze">breeze</a> (a gentle to moderate <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">wind</a>) blows constantly from the ocean at a relatively low average <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a> of 40% to 60%. </p>
      <p>The Cape Verde Islands only have two <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">seasons</a>: The <i>Tempo das Brisas</i> ("Time of the winds") from October to mid-July and the <i>Tempo das chuvas</i> ("Rainy season") from August to September, when there may be heavy <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Rainfall" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Cool" class="mw-disambig" title="Cool">coolest</a> months are January and February (average temperature of 21&nbsp;°C), where temperatures can drop down to 16&nbsp;°C; the warmest is the month of September (up to 36&nbsp;°C) with an average temperature of 27&nbsp;°C. </p>
      <p>Otherwise, the islands of Barlavento (Santo Antão, São Vicente, São Nicolao, Boa Vista, Sal, Santa Luzia, Branco and Razo) are always somewhat cooler than those of the Sotavento (Maio, Santiago, Fogo and Brava), where summers can be quite hot.<sup id="cite_ref-cvcom_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cvcom-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife">Wildlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Wildlife">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Wildlife">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Cape Verde's isolation has resulted in the islands having a number of <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reptile" title="Reptile">reptiles</a>, many of which are <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> by human development. Endemic birds include <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander%27s_Swift&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alexander's Swift (not yet started)">Alexander's Swift</a> (<i>Apus alexandri</i>), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bourne%27s_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bourne's Heron (not yet started)">Bourne's Heron</a> (<i>Ardea purpurea bournei</i>), the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Raso_Lark&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Raso Lark (not yet started)">Raso Lark</a> (<i>Alauda razae</i>), the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cape Verde Warbler (not yet started)">Cape Verde Warbler</a> (<i>Acrocephalus brevipennis</i>), and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Iago_Sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Iago Sparrow (not yet started)">Iago Sparrow</a> (<i>Passer iagoensis</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The islands are also an important breeding area for seabirds including the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde_Shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cape Verde Shearwater (not yet started)">Cape Verde Shearwater</a>. Reptiles include the Cape Verde Giant Gecko (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tarentola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tarentola (not yet started)">Tarentola</a> gigas</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Cape Verde is a <a href="/wiki/Representative_democracy" class="mw-redirect" title="Representative democracy">representative</a> <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliamentary</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> —adopted in 1980 and revised in 1992, 1995 and 1999— defines the basic principles of its government. The <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and is elected for a 5-year term; the <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">Prime Minister</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a>. The Prime Minister is nominated by the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly" title="National Assembly">National Assembly</a> and appointed by the President.<sup id="cite_ref-constitution_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-constitution-16">[16]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The economic resources of Cape Verde are largely dependent on agriculture and fishing. Agriculture frequently suffers the effects of droughts. The most important crops are coffee, bananas, sugar cane, tropical fruits, corn, beans, sweet potato, and cassava. The industrial sector is incipient but is based on the production of “aguardente” (spirits from sugar cane), clothing and footwear, paints and varnishes, tourism, fishing and canned fish, and salt extraction. Banana, canned fish, frozen fish, lobsters, salt, and clothes are the main exports. The national currency is the Cape Verdean escudo. Remittances from emigration are another important source of resources for the State of Cape Verde. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>In Cape Verde, the annual rate of population growth and mortality are low, compared to average rates of other middle-income countries. The average life expectancy is 66 years and 71 years respectively for men and women. The resident population in the country is estimated at 500,000 inhabitants. There are an estimated additional one million Cape Verdeans living abroad, mainly in the United States, Western Europe, and Africa. Cape Verde has a young population with an average age of 23 years. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Cape Verdean culture is a unique mixture of European and African elements. Corn is the staple food of Cape Verde. The national or traditional dish is <i>cachupa</i>, which is a stew of hominy (dried <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">maize</a> kernals), beans, and whatever meat or vegetables may be available. Other common foods include rice, beans, fish, potatoes and <a href="/wiki/Manioc" title="Manioc">manioc</a>. A traditional breakfast is a steamed cornbread, eaten with honey and milk or coffee. Grogue, or <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a> <a href="/wiki/Alcoholic_drink" title="Alcoholic drink">liquor</a>, is manufactured on the islands and is a popular drink, particularly among the men. Cape Verdean music incorporates Portuguese, Caribbean, and African influences. Popular genres include morna, funaná, batuque, coladeira, and cola san jon. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Health">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Health">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Cape Verde, other than private clinics, the government guarantees a public health system which comprises several healthcare centers and three central hospitals (Hospital Agostinho Neto, in Praia, Hospital Baptista de Sousa, in São Vicente, and Hospital Regional de Santiago Norte, in Assomada). The cost of public health is supported by the government, but users must pay a fee which varies in accordance with the capacity of the user to afford it. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Verde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>After independence, the different governments of Cape Verde invested massively in education and illiteracy has been reduced drastically. Today almost one hundred percent of school-age children attend school. Attendance to primary schooling, which comprises 6 years, is compulsory and free of any charge. Education is guaranteed by a network of public schools that span from nursery school to university. There are also several private schools in all levels of education. </p>
