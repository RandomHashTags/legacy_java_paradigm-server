      <p>Iraq presently consists of 19 <a href="/wiki/Governorate" title="Governorate">governorates</a> (<span lang="ar" dir="rtl" title="Arabic language text">محافظة</span> <i><a href="/wiki/Muhafazah" title="Muhafazah">muḥāfażah</a></i> in Arabic, <i>parêzga</i> in Kurdish), also known as "provinces". As per the Iraqi constitution, three or more governorates can join to form an autonomous region.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Baghdad and Basra are the oldest standing administrative regions of Iraq while In 2014 the decision was made to create the <a href="/wiki/Halabja_Governorate" title="Halabja Governorate">Halabja Governorate</a> out of the Halabja District of the <a href="/wiki/Sulaymaniyah_Governorate" title="Sulaymaniyah Governorate">Sulaymaniyah Governorate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On 21 January 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Ministers_(Iraq)" title="Council of Ministers (Iraq)">Council of Ministers</a> of the Government of Iraq approved in principle proposals to create more governorates.<sup id="cite_ref-cab_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cab-3">[3]</a></sup> The Council announced that two new governorates <a href="/wiki/Tal_Afar" title="Tal Afar">Tal Afar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tuz_Khurmatu" title="Tuz Khurmatu">Tuz Khurmatu</a> would be formed from the current <a href="/wiki/Nineveh_Governorate" title="Nineveh Governorate">Nineveh Governorate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saladin_Governorate" title="Saladin Governorate">Saladin Governorate</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It was also announced that the city of <a href="/wiki/Fallujah" title="Fallujah">Fallujah</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Al_Anbar_Governorate" title="Al Anbar Governorate">Al Anbar Governorate</a> would become a separate governorate,<sup id="cite_ref-cab_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cab-3">[3]</a></sup> which was announced in response to a <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni Islamist</a> uprising in the city. </p>
