      <p><b>The Gambia</b> is officially called the <b>Republic of the Gambia</b>. This country is also known as <b>Gambia</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is a country in <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>. It is surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>. It is the smallest country on mainland Africa. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Banjul" title="Banjul">Banjul</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The largest cities are <a href="/wiki/Serekunda" title="Serekunda">Serekunda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brikama" title="Brikama">Brikama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The geography of Gambia is unusual. It is a long, thin country. Except for its coastline it is completely surrounded by the country of <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>. The River Gambia flows from Senegal through its centre and into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Gambia has a tropical climate. A hot and rainy season normally lasts from June until November.<sup id="cite_ref-hayward_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hayward-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_government">Politics and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics and government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Politics and government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Gambia gained <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gambia_Independence_Act_1964&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gambia Independence Act 1964 (not yet started)">independence</a> from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on 18 February 1965. It was ruled by <a href="/wiki/Dawda_Jawara" title="Dawda Jawara">Dawda Jawara</a> and his <a href="/w/index.php?title=People%27s_Progressive_Party_(Gambia)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="People's Progressive Party (Gambia) (not yet started)">People's Progressive Party</a> (PPP) from 1965 to 1994. There was a military take-over in 1994. In 1996, <a href="/wiki/Yahya_Jammeh" title="Yahya Jammeh">Yahya Jammeh</a> became President. Then in 2016, <a href="/wiki/Adama_Barrow" title="Adama Barrow">Adama Barrow</a> was <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gambian_presidential_election,_2016&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gambian presidential election, 2016 (not yet started)">elected</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Administrative divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Gambia is divided into eight local government areas. They are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Banjul" title="Banjul">Banjul</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Kanifing_(Gambia)" title="Kanifing (Gambia)">Kanifing</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Brikama_(Gambia)" title="Brikama (Gambia)">Brikama</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Mansa_Konko" title="Mansa Konko">Mansa Konko</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Kerewan_(Gambia)" title="Kerewan (Gambia)">Kerewan</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Kuntaur_(Gambia)" title="Kuntaur (Gambia)">Kuntaur</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Janjanbureh_(Gambia)" title="Janjanbureh (Gambia)">Janjanbureh</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Basse_(Gambia)" title="Basse (Gambia)">Basse</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>As of 2013, these are then divided into 43 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_the_Gambia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Districts of the Gambia (not yet started)">districts</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gambia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of The Gambia is about 1.7 million. People from The Gambia are called <b>Gambians</b>. A number of people from different <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">cultures</a> and backgrounds live in The Gambia. Some of the largest groups are called <i>Mandinka</i>, <i>Fula</i>, <i>Wolof</i> and <i>Jolo</i>, in that order. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is the official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">language</a>, but people speak other languages as well. </p>
      <p>Most Gambians follow the <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> of <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>. </p>
      <p>The American writer <a href="/wiki/Alex_Haley" title="Alex Haley">Alex Haley</a>, who wrote the book <i>Roots</i>, found that his family came from The Gambia in the 1760s. </p>
