      <p><b>Colombia</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/…ô/: 'a' in 'about'">…ô</span><span title="/Àà/: primary stress follows">Àà</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ å/: 'u' in 'cut'"> å</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/./: syllable break">.</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/…ô/: 'a' in 'about'">…ô</span></span>/</a></span> <span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:En-us-Colombia.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20"></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/09/En-us-Colombia.ogg" class="internal" title="En-us-Colombia.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span></span>; <small>Spanish:&nbsp;</small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Wikipedia:IPA for Spanish">[koÀàlom.bja]</a></span>), officially the <b>Republic of Colombia</b>, is a tropical <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equatorial</a> country in northern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. It is in the northwest part of the continent, and it has an area of 1,141,748&nbsp;km¬≤ (440,839&nbsp;sq mi). </p>
      <p>It is the only South American country with coasts on both oceans (<a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a>). </p>
      <p>The countries that have borders on the continent with Colombia are: <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> (east), <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> (southeast), <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> (south), <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> (southwest) and <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> (west). The countries that have borders with Colombia on the sea are: <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. </p>
      <p>42,888,592 people live in Colombia.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It is the third most populated country of Latin America after <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Colombia is <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogot√°">Bogot√°</a>. The second city is <a href="/wiki/Medell%C3%ADn" title="Medell√≠n">Medell√≠n</a>, a large industrial center. The third largest city is <a href="/wiki/Cali" title="Cali">Cali</a>, home of many multinational companies. The most important city of the country on the Caribbean coast is <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">Barranquilla</a>. It was also the first national port. <a href="/wiki/Cartagena_de_Indias" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartagena de Indias">Cartagena de Indias</a> has a great Spanish wall from the 17th Century and an old town with 500-year-old buildings. After these cities comes <a href="/wiki/Bucaramanga" title="Bucaramanga">Bucaramanga</a>, a large city near the Venezuelan border, and the center of the textile industry in Colombia. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meaning">Meaning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Meaning">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Meaning">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The word "<i>Colombia</i>" is named after <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>, the explorer who began the European colonization of <a href="/wiki/The_Americas" class="mw-redirect" title="The Americas">the Americas</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1819, Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador and Panama declared <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and formed a country called "<i><a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">Gran Colombia</a></i>". Venezuela and Ecuador separated in 1830. Panama continued to be part of Colombia until 1903, at which point it proclaimed independence. The official language is <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, but there are many dialects spoken by minorities. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Before_the_Spanish">Before the Spanish</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Before the Spanish">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Before the Spanish">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The land where Colombia now stands has been home to people for more than 20,000 years. The first people had many groups, cultures, and languages. There are many tools, caves, and things of those peoples. In Colombia, the peoples were similar to those of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, because scientists say that the first people came to <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">America</a> from <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> or South East <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> more than 50,000 years ago. The peoples of Colombia were called Arawak, Caribe, and Chibcha. But there were many other groups. The most famous were the Muiscas. legend of El Dorado comes from the Muiscas. The king of the Muiscas used to bathe in Lake Guatavita and he used a lot of gold for his bath. The Spanish heard about this, and the people told them that there was a great golden city somewhere. They started to look for El Dorado. The International Airport of Bogot√° is called El Dorado International Airport, because the legend was born in Colombia. The descendants of the first peoples of Colombia live integrated with others. Some groups live in the forest, but many live in the countryside and in the cities. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_colony">The colony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: The colony">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: The colony">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Spanish, led by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>, first came to <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">the Americas</a> in 1492. Columbus saw the coasts of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, Colombia and <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> and he gave them his own name. But <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alonso_de_Ojeda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alonso de Ojeda (not yet started)">Alonso de Ojeda</a> was the first colonist to explore the area of the Gulf of Dari√©n. After that, the Spanish founded the first European cities on the American continent: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Santa_Marta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Santa Marta (not yet started)">Santa Marta</a> in 1525 and <a href="/wiki/Cartagena_de_Indias" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartagena de Indias">Cartagena de Indias</a> in 1533. It was from Colombia that the Spanish conquered the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Incas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Incas (not yet started)">Incas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. </p>
      <p>Spain would <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">enslave</a> the <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">indigenous peoples</a> who were already living there, but they also used the <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a> they built at Cartagena to bring many <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black</a> slaves from <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> into the area. The Spanish captured many, many people from Africa and forced them to work on <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ranch" title="Ranch">ranches</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mines</a>, and as personal servants.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>While this was happening, they founded the city of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Popay%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Popay√°n (not yet started)">Popay√°n</a> in 1536 and they conquered the <i>Confederation of the Muiscas</i>. In Bacat√°, the capital of the Muiscas, the Spanish founded the city of <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogot√°">Santa Fe</a> in 1538. In 1550 the Spanish declared the territory as a colony and its capital was <i>Santa Fe de Bogot√°</i>, but in the first decades it depended on <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. Soon after, it was declared a Viceroyalty. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence">Independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Independence">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Independence">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On November 11, 1810, the city of <a href="/wiki/Cartagena_de_Indias" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartagena de Indias">Cartagena de Indias</a> declared its independence from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. The whole region would fight a war to become independent, led by people like <a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Nari%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Antonio Nari√±o (not yet started)">Antonio Nari√±o</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Sim√≥n Bol√≠var">Sim√≥n Bol√≠var</a>. Nari√±o was one of the most important leaders in the war for the independence of Colombia. He was a politician, journalist and soldier. Bolivar was another important leader, and he was a president and a colonel. Bolivar had promised <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandre_P%C3%A9tion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alexandre P√©tion (not yet started)">Alexandre P√©tion</a>, the president of <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>, that he would <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism" title="Abolitionism">abolish</a> slavery during his independence war. But he only agreed because he was afraid the slaves would revolt against the slaveowners, <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Revolution" title="Haitian Revolution">like they did in Haiti</a>, and only freed slaves if they agreed to fight in his army.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He never freed all the slaves, and slavery was not abolished in Colombia until 1851.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The geography of Colombia has six main natural regions. Each has its own unique characteristics. The <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes mountain</a> region shares borders with <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Coast">Pacific Coast</a> region shares borders with <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean coastal</a> region shares borders with Venezuela and Panama. The <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Los_Llanos,_Venezuela&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Los Llanos, Venezuela (not yet started)">Llanos</a></i> (plains) shares a border with Venezuela. The <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Rainforest" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon Rainforest">Amazon Rainforest</a> region shares a border with Venezuela, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and Ecuador. The insular region includes all the islands of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Geography_of_Colombia_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geography_of_Colombia-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The main rivers of Colombia are <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_River" title="Magdalena River">Magdalena</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cauca_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cauca River (not yet started)">Cauca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guaviare_River" title="Guaviare River">Guaviare</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Atrato_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Atrato River (not yet started)">Atrato</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meta_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Meta River (not yet started)">Meta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Putumayo_River" title="Putumayo River">Putumayo</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caquet%C3%A1_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caquet√° River (not yet started)">Caquet√°</a>. Colombia has four main <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">drainage systems</a>: the Pacific drain, the Caribbean drain, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Orinoco_Basin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Orinoco Basin (not yet started)">Orinoco Basin</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Basin" title="Amazon Basin">Amazon Basin</a>. </p>
      <p>Most of the population lives in the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> region. But the plains make over half the land. Only about 6% of the people live in the plains. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Nevada_de_Santa_Marta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta (not yet started)">Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta</a> mountain range includes the country's tallest peaks (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pico_Crist%C3%B3bal_Col%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pico Crist√≥bal Col√≥n (not yet started)">Pico Crist√≥bal Col√≥n</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pico_Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pico Sim√≥n Bol√≠var (not yet started)">Pico Sim√≥n Bol√≠var</a>). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>Colombia's temperature changes with the altitude, and the temperature is different by region. The regions around the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> are colder than the coast, which is lower. </p>
      <p>Because of the many altitudes, Colombia has many different <a href="/wiki/Fruits" class="mw-redirect" title="Fruits">fruits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vegetables" class="mw-redirect" title="Vegetables">vegetables</a> and kinds of animals. It has many valleys, beaches, plains, mountains and forests like the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_rainforest" title="Amazon rainforest">Amazon rainforest</a>. </p>
      <p>The top of some of the <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a> in Colombia are so cold that people can <a href="/wiki/Ski" class="mw-redirect" title="Ski">ski</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>. The lower mountains have warmer climates. The sea shores are cooled by ocean breezes. At sea level near the equator it is hot. </p>
      <p>Rivers are important as waterways in the land for <a href="/wiki/Shipping" class="mw-redirect" title="Shipping">shipping</a> things. One of the most important rivers in Colombia is the Magdalena-Cauca. The Magdelena River divides the eastern and center mountains. It makes a <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/fertile" class="extiw" title="wikt:fertile">fertile</a> <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valley</a>. The Cauca River divides the center mountain range and the western ridge, making another great valley. The Cauca is really a part of the Magdalena. However, the two do not meet until a few miles before the Magdalena goes into the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biodiversity">Biodiversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Biodiversity">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Biodiversity">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Colombia is a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Megadiverse_countries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Megadiverse countries (not yet started)">megadiverse country</a>. This means the country is home to a <a href="/wiki/Majority" title="Majority">majority</a> of Earth's <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>. There are also a high number of <a href="/wiki/Endemic" class="mw-redirect" title="Endemic">endemic</a> species. </p>
      <p>Colombia has 1,900 species of bird, more than any other country.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> There are about 2,000 species of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marine_fish&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marine fish (not yet started)">marine fish</a>. It is the second most diverse country in <a href="/wiki/Freshwater_fish" class="mw-redirect" title="Freshwater fish">freshwater fish</a>. As for plants, the country has between 40,000 and 45,000 species. Colombia is second in the number of <a href="/wiki/Amphibian" title="Amphibian">amphibian</a> species and is the third most diverse country in <a href="/wiki/Reptile" title="Reptile">reptiles</a>. There are about 2,900 species of <a href="/wiki/Mollusk" class="mw-redirect" title="Mollusk">mollusks</a> and about 300,000 species of <a href="/wiki/Invertebrates" class="mw-redirect" title="Invertebrates">invertebrates</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In May 2006, <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">President</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Uribe" title="√Ålvaro Uribe">√Ålvaro Uribe</a> was re-elected. His term lasted until 2010. Uribe was the first President in Colombia to win a second election in over 100 years. </p>
      <p>In August 2010, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Santos" title="Juan Manuel Santos">Juan Manuel Santos</a> became Colombia's president. Santos won the election on June 20 by a landslide. He promised to "preside over a government of national unity that will bring social prosperity for all Colombians"<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Eight years later, conservative Senator <a href="/wiki/Iv%C3%A1n_Duque_M%C3%A1rquez" title="Iv√°n Duque M√°rquez">Iv√°n Duque M√°rquez</a> was elected President and soon became the nation's latest president. He won the election by 2 million votes against his rival liberal Senator <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gustavo_Petro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gustavo Petro (not yet started)">Gustavo Petro</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Departments">Departments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Departments">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Departments">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Colombia is divided into 32 departments and one capital district. The capital district is treated as a department. Departments are divided into <a href="/w/index.php?title=Municipalities_of_Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Municipalities of Colombia (not yet started)">municipalities</a>. Municipalities are divided into <i>corregimientos</i>. Each department has a local government with a governor and assembly directly elected to four-year terms. Each municipality is headed by a mayor and council. Each <i>corregimiento</i> by an elected <i>corregidor</i>, or local leader. </p>

      <p>The population of Colombia was approximately 47 million as of 2016. The ethnic groups of the population are:<sup id="cite_ref-cia_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>48% Mestizo</li> 
       <li>23% Mulatto</li> 
       <li>20% White</li> 
       <li>6% Black</li> 
       <li>2% Amerindian</li> 
       <li>1% Zambo</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Up to 49.6% of the population is living below the poverty line. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Security">Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Security">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Change section: Security">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are several guerilla groups in Colombia, the <a href="/wiki/FARC" title="FARC">FARC</a> being the most powerful, a guerilla group responsible for kidnappings, murders, attacks, and drug-dealing. Drug dealing is a problem the government has been trying to fight. This creates violence in the cities. With Operation: Jaque, <a href="/wiki/Ingrid_Betancourt" title="Ingrid Betancourt">Ingrid Betancourt</a>, three Americans, and 11 soldiers were rescued from the FARC. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Transport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Change section: Transport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Most parts of Colombia are modern and the country has many roads. All major Colombian cities have airports and there are many ports on both oceans (<a href="/wiki/Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a>). Because Colombia is a huge country and there are many mountains, airports are very important in the country. There are some regions like the Amazon Forest and Dariend, where the most important way of transportation is by the rivers. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Roads</b>: The Pan American Highway in South America begins in Colombia. You can go as far as Tierra de Fuego in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> using that road. Most of the Colombian cities are connected by road in Colombia. The most important roads are the Bogot√°-Medell√≠n Highway, the Bogot√°-Cali Highway, the Bogot√°-Cucuta Highway, the Bogot√°-Barranquilla Highway and many others connecting big cities like Medell√≠n, Cali, Barranquilla and Cartagena de Indias with other important centers. You can go by road from Bogot√° to <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>. The major cities have bus stations.</li> 
       <li><b>Airports</b>: They are very important in Colombia and all the main cities have their own airports. The El Dorado International Airport in Bogot√° is the most important. The Palmaseca International Airport in Cali is the second most important. Other important airports are the ones of Medell√≠n, Barranquilla, San Andr√©s and Cartagena de Indias. The Colombian airports have international connections with most of the South American capitals, North America and Europe. The main Colombian airline is <a href="/wiki/Avianca" title="Avianca">Avianca</a> and it is the second oldest world airline and one of the most important in Latin America.</li> 
       <li><b>Ports</b>: Colombia has coasts on both oceans - Atlantic and Pacific - and it is one of the first world water reserve because it has many rivers and lakes. The longest rivers are the Amazon, Magdalena, Cauca, and the Atrato. The main ports on the Atlantic coast are Barranquilla, which was the first Colombian port, Cartagena de Indias and Santa Marta. There are other ones like Rioacha and Turbo. The ports on the Pacific coast are Buenaventura and Tumaco. The port of the San Andres Island is also one of the most important for the country in the Caribbean and Antillean Sea. The city of Leticia is a very important river port on the Amazon river.</li> 
       <li><b>Train</b>: The National Train was very important to Colombia during the 20th century, but it was forgotten when roads and airports were developed. Today the railways in Colombia are used more for tour visits, and they are not modern.</li> 
       <li><b>Transportation in the cities</b>: The major Colombian cities have a very good system of transportation. There are public buses that connect the suburbs to the center of the cities. There are many taxi companies in Colombia. There are also very modern urban transports in the main cities like Transmilenio in Bogot√°, the Metro in Medell√≠n, and similar proposals in Cali, Barranquilla, Cartagena de Indias and many others that are being developed.</li>
      </ul>

There are three important cities: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pereira&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pereira (not yet started)">Pereira</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manizales&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manizales (not yet started)">Manizales</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenia_(Colombia)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Armenia (Colombia) (not yet started)">Armenia</a>.</li>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Science">Science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Science">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Change section: Science">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Colombia has made many advances in medicine. Many foreigners come to the country to get health services, such as organ transplants or plastic surgery. Doctor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Elkin_Patarroyo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manuel Elkin Patarroyo (not yet started)">Manuel Elkin Patarroyo</a> of Colombia discovered a medicine against <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a>. He donated his discovery to be used in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>. He did not want to make business of it, instead he wanted to use it to help people. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resources">Resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Resources">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Change section: Resources">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Colombia is known for its <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Emerald" title="Emerald">emeralds</a>. It is also the biggest supplier of <a href="/wiki/Plantain" title="Plantain">plantains</a> to the United States. There are a few armed conflicts in Colombia. Most rebel groups finance their operations with the trade of <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug" class="mw-redirect" title="Illegal drug">illegal drugs</a> (mostly <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a>). </p>
