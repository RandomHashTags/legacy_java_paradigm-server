      <p><b>Samsung Pay</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Mobile_payment" title="Mobile payment">mobile payment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_wallet" title="Digital wallet">digital wallet</a> service by <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">Samsung Electronics</a> that lets users make payments using compatible phones and other Samsung-produced devices. The service supports <a href="/wiki/Contactless_payment" title="Contactless payment">contactless payments</a> using <a href="/wiki/Near-field_communications" class="mw-redirect" title="Near-field communications">near-field communications</a>, but also supports <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_stripe" class="mw-redirect" title="Magnetic stripe">magnetic stripe</a>–only <a href="/wiki/Payment_terminal" title="Payment terminal">payment terminals</a> by incorporating <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_secure_transmission" title="Magnetic secure transmission">magnetic secure transmission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cnet-faq_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnet-faq-1">[1]</a></sup> In countries like India it also supports bill payments.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The service was launched in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> on August 20, 2015 and in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on September 28 of the same year. In 2016, at the International <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Electronics_Show" title="Consumer Electronics Show">Consumer Electronics Show</a> (CES 2016), the telecommunications company announced that Samsung Pay would be coming soon to a variety of banks in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-digitaltrends.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digitaltrends.com-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service">Service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Pay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Samsung Pay was developed from the intellectual property of LoopPay, a <a href="/wiki/Crowdfunding" title="Crowdfunding">crowdfunded</a> <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startup company</a> that Samsung acquired in February 2015. The service supports both <a href="/wiki/Near-field_communications" class="mw-redirect" title="Near-field communications">NFC</a>-based mobile payment systems (which are prioritized when support is detected),<sup id="cite_ref-pocketlint-pay_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pocketlint-pay-4">[4]</a></sup> as well as those that only support <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_stripe" class="mw-redirect" title="Magnetic stripe">magnetic stripes</a>. This is accomplished via a technology known as <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_secure_transmission" title="Magnetic secure transmission">magnetic secure transmission</a> (MST), which emulates the swipe of a permanent magnet strip past a reader by generating the near-field magnetic waveform directly. LoopPay's developers stated that because of this design, the technology would work with "nearly 90%" of all <a href="/wiki/Point-of-sale" class="mw-redirect" title="Point-of-sale">point-of-sale</a> units in the United States (which excludes terminals that require the card to be physically inserted into a slot in order to function).<sup id="cite_ref-cnet-faq_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnet-faq-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On phones, the Samsung Pay menu is launched by swiping from the bottom of the screen. Different <a href="/wiki/Credit_card" title="Credit card">credit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Debit_card" title="Debit card">debit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loyalty_card" class="mw-redirect" title="Loyalty card">loyalty cards</a> can be loaded into the app, and selected by swiping between them on-screen.<sup id="cite_ref-pocketlint-pay_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pocketlint-pay-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In South Korea, Samsung Pay can be used for <a href="/wiki/Online_payment" class="mw-redirect" title="Online payment">online payments</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and to withdraw money on selected banks' <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" title="Automated teller machine">ATMs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">Mainland China</a>, Samsung Pay supports In-app payments, <a href="/wiki/QR_code_payment" title="QR code payment">QR code payments</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alipay" title="Alipay">Alipay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/WeChat_Pay" title="WeChat Pay">WeChat Pay</a>) and public transportation cards of Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and other cities. </p>
      <p>In Hong Kong, Samsung Pay can be linked with <a href="/wiki/Octopus_card" title="Octopus card">Octopus cards</a>, called Smart Octopus, to make electronic payments with stored value service for payments in online or offline systems.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In India, Samsung Pay supports the government's <a href="/wiki/Unified_Payments_Interface" title="Unified Payments Interface">UPI</a> / <a href="/wiki/BharatQR" title="BharatQR">BharatQR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It also supports bill payments via the <a href="/wiki/Bharat_Bill_Payment_System" title="Bharat Bill Payment System">Bharat Bill Payment System</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In May of 2020, Samsung Pay unveiled Samsung Money by SoFi, a mobile-first money management experience that makes available a cash management account and accompanying <a href="/wiki/Mastercard" title="Mastercard">Mastercard</a> debit card via the Samsung Pay app, in partnership with fintech company <a href="/wiki/SoFi" title="SoFi">SoFi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Security">Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Pay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Samsung Pay's security measures are based on <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Knox" title="Samsung Knox">Samsung Knox</a> and <a href="/wiki/ARM_TrustZone" class="mw-redirect" title="ARM TrustZone">ARM TrustZone</a> technologies; credit card information is stored in a secure <a href="/wiki/Tokenization_(data_security)" title="Tokenization (data security)">token</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pocketlint-pay_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pocketlint-pay-4">[4]</a></sup> Payments must be authenticated using a fingerprint scan or passcode.<sup id="cite_ref-recode-s6unveil_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-recode-s6unveil-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-engadget-looppay_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-engadget-looppay-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In August 2016, security researcher Salvador Mendoza disclosed a potential flaw with Samsung Pay, arguing that its security tokens were not sufficiently randomized and could become predictable. He also designed a handheld device that could be used to <a href="/wiki/Skimmer_(device)" class="mw-redirect" title="Skimmer (device)">skim</a> magnetic secure transmission tokens, and another which could spoof magnetic stripes on actual card readers using the token. Samsung responded to the report, stating that "If at any time there is a potential vulnerability, we will act promptly to investigate and resolve the issue".<sup id="cite_ref-zdnet-flaw_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zdnet-flaw-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Samsung Pay will not work with devices whose Knox warranty bit is tripped. </p>

      <p>In May 2016, it was reported that Samsung was developing a spin-off of the service known as Samsung Pay Mini. This service will be used for online payments only, and is also being targeted as a multi-platform service.<sup id="cite_ref-gsma-mini_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gsma-mini-45">[45]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In January 2017, Samsung has confirmed that Samsung Pay Mini will not only work on its Galaxy devices but on other Android phones as well, as long as they are running <a href="/wiki/Android_Lollipop" title="Android Lollipop">Android Lollipop</a> or above and have a screen resolution of 1280 × 720 pixels or higher.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In June 2017, Samsung launched Samsung Pay Mini and currently available on Galaxy J7 Max/On Max (in India)<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Availability is limited not just on the basis of where the payment card is issued, but also on the basis of the phone's region (CSC) code. Thus a phone made for an unsupported region can never use Samsung Pay even if it physically resides in a supported region and has a local SIM card. The rather unrelated error "Connection error. Unable to connect to Samsung Pay temporarily. Try again later." is how Samsung Pay reports this problem. </p>
      <p>In June 2020, Samsung announced a partnership between Samsung Pay, <a href="/wiki/Curve_(payment_card)" title="Curve (payment card)">Curve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mastercard" title="Mastercard">Mastercard</a> for the launch of Samsung Pay Card in the UK and more EE countries where <a href="/wiki/Curve_(payment_card)" title="Curve (payment card)">Curve</a> has customers later in 2020. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison_with_instant_payment">Comparison with instant payment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samsung_Pay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Comparison with instant payment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Digital_wallet" title="Digital wallet">digital wallet</a>-based payment systems like <a href="/wiki/PayPal" title="PayPal">PayPal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Pay" title="Apple Pay">Apple Pay</a>, <a href="/wiki/AliPay" class="mw-redirect" title="AliPay">AliPay</a>, <a href="/wiki/WeChat_Pay" title="WeChat Pay">WeChat Pay</a>, etc. users receive immediate notification of the transaction, but funds are transferred at best in the next business day. The settlement time depends on the payment method chosen by the customer, while for <a href="/wiki/Instant_payment" title="Instant payment">instant payment</a> systems, the funds are transferred within seconds or minutes. </p>
