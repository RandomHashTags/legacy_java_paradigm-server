      <p class="mw-empty-elt"> </p>
      <p>This list concerns <b>blood type distribution</b> between countries and regions. <a href="/wiki/Blood_type" title="Blood type">Blood type</a> (also called a <i>blood group</i>) is a classification of <a href="/wiki/Blood" title="Blood">blood</a>, based on the presence and absence of <a href="/wiki/Antibodies" class="mw-redirect" title="Antibodies">antibodies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heredity" title="Heredity">inherited</a> <a href="/wiki/Antigen" title="Antigen">antigenic</a> substances on the surface of <a href="/wiki/Red_blood_cell" title="Red blood cell">red blood cells</a> (RBCs). These antigens may be <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">proteins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbohydrate" title="Carbohydrate">carbohydrates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glycoprotein" title="Glycoprotein">glycoproteins</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Glycolipid" title="Glycolipid">glycolipids</a>, depending on the blood group system. </p>

      <p>Blood group B has its highest frequency in <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>, where it ranks first as the largest share of the earth's population. In <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> the share of the population is high, especially in Thailand and Indonesia, secondarily in <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Asia" title="North Asia">Northern Asia</a> and neighboring <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>, and its incidence diminishes both towards the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_world" title="Eastern world">east</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">west</a>, falling to single-digit percentages in <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-usarmy1971s_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usarmy1971s-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-brittanica2002s_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brittanica2002s-50">[50]</a></sup> It is believed to have been entirely absent from Native American and Australian Aboriginal populations prior to the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Europe" title="Ethnic groups in Europe">Europeans</a> in those areas.<sup id="cite_ref-brittanica2002s_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brittanica2002s-50">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ember1973_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ember1973-51">[51]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Blood group A is associated with high frequencies in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, especially in <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>, although its highest frequencies occur in some Australian Aboriginal populations and the <a href="/wiki/Blackfoot_Confederacy" title="Blackfoot Confederacy">Blackfoot Indians</a> of <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, USA.<sup id="cite_ref-dean2005j_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dean2005j-52">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-biotec2005d_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biotec2005d-53">[53]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Maps_of_allele_ABO_among_native_populations">Maps of allele ABO among native populations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blood_type_distribution_by_country&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Maps of allele ABO among native populations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
      <p> In the <a href="/wiki/ABO_blood_group_system" title="ABO blood group system">ABO blood group system</a>, there are three alleles: <i>i</i>, <i>I<sup>A</sup></i>, and <i>I<sup>B</sup></i>. As both <i>I<sup>A</sup></i> and <i>I<sup>B</sup></i> are dominant over <i>i</i>, only <i>ii</i> people have type O blood. Individuals with <i>I<sup>A</sup>I<sup>A</sup></i> or <i>I<sup>A</sup>i</i> have type A blood, and individuals with <i>I<sup>B</sup>I<sup>B</sup></i> or <i>I<sup>B</sup>i</i> have type B. Those with <i>I<sup>A</sup>I<sup>B</sup></i> have type AB.</p>
