      <p><b>Montenegro </b>(meaning<b> Black Mountain</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Southeastern Europe">Southeastern Europe</a>. It is on the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a>, between <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a> (to the South) and <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> to the North. Inland (to the East and South-East) it also has a common border with <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>. </p>
      <p>The country came to exist when its people decided to split from the country <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a> on May 21, 2006. For most of the 20th century it was part of <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. It had also been independent before <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and existed as a <a href="/wiki/Kingdom" title="Kingdom">kingdom</a>. Its leader is <a href="/wiki/Filip_Vujanovi%C4%87" title="Filip Vujanović">Filip Vujanović</a>. </p>
      <p>The national song is called <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oj,_svijetla_majska_zoro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Oj, svijetla majska zoro (not yet started)">Oj, svijetla majska zoro</a></i><br><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montenegrin_language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Montenegrin language (not yet started)">Montenegrin Cyrillic</a>: Ој, свијетла мајска зоро<br><i>"Oh, Bright Dawn of May."</i> </p>
      <p>The size of the <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GDP" class="mw-redirect" title="GDP">GDP</a> is $4.114 billion and it has a population of 625,000 people. The traditional old capital of Montenegro is <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cetinje&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cetinje (not yet started)">Cetinje</a></i>, but <a href="/wiki/Podgorica" title="Podgorica">Podgorica</a> is the new one. The country adopted the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Montenegro_and_the_euro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Montenegro and the euro (not yet started)">unilaterally</a> as Montenegro does not have its own currency, but the adoption has raised the national debt to 57 percent of GDP in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-index-2011_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-index-2011-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
