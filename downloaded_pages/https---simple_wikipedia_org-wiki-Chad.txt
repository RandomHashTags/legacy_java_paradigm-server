      <p><b>Chad</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Tchad</i>; <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl"><big>تشاد</big></span>‎, officially called the <b>Republic of Chad</b>), is a <a href="/wiki/Landlocked_country" title="Landlocked country">landlocked country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central Africa</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/N%27Djamena" title="N'Djamena">N'Djamena</a>. </p>
      <p>It was a French <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> until 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-2">[2]</a></sup> It suffers from poverty, illness, drought, and armed conflicts. In 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of Chad was 11,525,000.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> are the official languages. <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> are the most widely practiced religions. </p>
      <p>Chad has one of the highest levels of <a href="/wiki/Hunger" title="Hunger">hunger</a> in the world. <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">Food</a> insecurity is common in the country. 80% live in total <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> on less than a dollar a day, and 90% are unemployed in Chad. Chad has a low <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a>. Life expectancy is 54 years old in Chad. </p>
      <p>Chad is home to 134 kinds of mammals, 141 bird species, 52 kinds of reptiles, 30 amphibians and 130 kinds of fish. Many animals such as <a href="/wiki/Elephants" class="mw-redirect" title="Elephants">elephants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hippopotamuses" class="mw-redirect" title="Hippopotamuses">hippopotamuses</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhinoceroses&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rhinoceroses (not yet started)">rhinoceroses</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Warthogs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Warthogs (not yet started)">warthogs</a>, giraffes, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Antelopes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Antelopes (not yet started)">antelopes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lions" class="mw-redirect" title="Lions">lions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leopards" class="mw-redirect" title="Leopards">leopards</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cheetahs" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheetahs">cheetahs</a> live in Chad. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Chari_River.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Chari_River.jpg/240px-Chari_River.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Chari_River.jpg/360px-Chari_River.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/52/Chari_River.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="321"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Chari_River.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>View of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chari_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chari River (not yet started)">Chari River</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>Chad is the world's 21st-largest country. It is slightly smaller than <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and slightly larger than <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> To the north is <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, to the south is the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>, to the east is <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, and to the west are <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>. </p>
      <p>The country's capital is 1,060 kilometres (660&nbsp;mi) from the nearest seaport <a href="/wiki/Douala" title="Douala">Douala</a>, Cameroon.<sup id="cite_ref-EB_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UNHCHR_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNHCHR-8">[8]</a></sup> Due to this distance from the sea and the country's largely <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> climate, Chad is sometimes called the "Dead Heart of Africa".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Lake_Chad" title="Lake Chad">Lake Chad</a> is the largest <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetland</a> in Chad and the second largest in Africa. Lake Chad was 330,000 square kilometres (130,000&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) 7000 years ago. Now it is 17,806 square kilometres (6,875&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). Chad's highest peak is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Emi_Koussi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Emi Koussi (not yet started)">Emi Koussi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>. </p>
      <p>The region's tall grasses and large amount of marshes make it good for birds, reptiles, and large mammals. Chad's major rivers are the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chari_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chari River (not yet started)">Chari</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Logone_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Logone River (not yet started)">Logone</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Languages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Languages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Chad's official languages are <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, but over 100 languages and <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">dialects</a> are spoken. Due to the important role of Arab traders and merchants, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chadian_Arabic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chadian Arabic (not yet started)">Chadian Arabic</a> has become a <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">lingua franca</a>, a language for all to use. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Attending <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary school</a> is required in Chad, though only about 50% of children attend school.<sup id="cite_ref-unicef_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unicef-10">[10]</a></sup> Adult <a href="/wiki/Literacy" title="Literacy">literacy</a> is only 35%.<sup id="cite_ref-unicef_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unicef-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_N%27Djamena&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="University of N'Djamena (not yet started)">University of N'Djamena</a> provides higher education. </p>
