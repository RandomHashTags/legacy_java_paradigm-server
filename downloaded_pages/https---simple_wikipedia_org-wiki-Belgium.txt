      <p><b>Belgium</b>, officially the <b>Kingdom of Belgium</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federal</a> <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">state</a> in Western Europe. Belgium has an area of 30,528 square kilometres (11,787&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). Around 11 million people live in Belgium. It is a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and is home to its headquarters. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> of Belgium is <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, where the European Union, <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> and other famous organisations are based. </p>
      <p>There are three regions in Belgium: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> is the name of the northern half of Belgium, just south of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. Most of the people in this region, called the <i>Flemish people</i>, speak <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a> is the name of the southern half of Belgium, just north of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Here, most of the people, the <i>Walloons</i>, speak <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. There is a small part of Wallonia next to the border with <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> where the people speak <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>.</li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/Brussels-Capital_Region" title="Brussels-Capital Region">Brussels-Capital Region</a>, where the capital of Brussels is found, is in the middle of the country, but surrounded by Flanders <a href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">on all sides</a>. It used to be Dutch-speaking, but today French is mostly spoken, with some Dutch.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The population is about 60% Dutch-speaking, 39% French-speaking, and 1% German-speaking (the so-called <i>Deutschbelgier</i>). To look after all these groups, Belgium has a complicated system of <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a>. </p>

      <p>The name 'Belgium' comes from <i><a href="/wiki/Gallia_Belgica" title="Gallia Belgica">Gallia Belgica</a></i>. This was a <a href="/wiki/Roman_province" title="Roman province">Roman province</a> in the northernmost part of <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>. Before Roman invasion in 100&nbsp;BC, the <i><a href="/wiki/Belgae" title="Belgae">Belgae</a></i>, a mix of <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germanic_peoples" title="Germanic peoples">Germanic peoples</a>, lived there.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The Germanic <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> tribes during the 5th century brought the area under the rule of the <a href="/wiki/Merovingian" class="mw-redirect" title="Merovingian">Merovingian</a> kings. A slow shift of power during the 8th century led the kingdom of the Franks to change into the <a href="/wiki/Carolingian_Empire" title="Carolingian Empire">Carolingian Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CookP3_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CookP3-8">[8]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Verdun" title="Treaty of Verdun">Treaty of Verdun</a> in 843 divided the region into <a href="/wiki/Middle_Francia" title="Middle Francia">Middle</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Francia" title="West Francia">West Francia</a>. They were <a href="/wiki/Vassal" title="Vassal">vassals</a> either of the <a href="/wiki/King_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="King of France">King of France</a> or of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CookP3_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CookP3-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Burgundian_and_Habsburg_acquisitions_in_the_Low_Countries" title="Timeline of Burgundian and Habsburg acquisitions in the Low Countries">Many of these fiefdoms</a> were united in the <a href="/wiki/Burgundian_Netherlands" title="Burgundian Netherlands">Burgundian Netherlands</a> of the 14th and 15th centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years' War">Eighty Years' War</a> (1568–1648) divided the Low Countries into the northern <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">United Provinces</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Netherlands" title="Southern Netherlands">Southern Netherlands</a>. Southern Netherlands were ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Spain" title="Habsburg Spain">Spanish</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburgs</a>. This made up most of modern Belgium. </p>
      <p>After the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars:_Campaigns_of_1794" class="mw-redirect" title="French Revolutionary Wars: Campaigns of 1794">campaigns of 1794 in the French Revolutionary Wars</a>, the Low Countries were added into the <a href="/wiki/French_First_Republic" title="French First Republic">French First Republic</a>. This ended Austrian rule in the area. Adding back the Low Countries formed the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="United Kingdom of the Netherlands">United Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>. This happened at the end of the <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">First French Empire</a> in 1815. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Revolution" title="Belgian Revolution">Belgian Revolution</a> was in 1830.<sup id="cite_ref-Dobbelaere-VoyE_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dobbelaere-VoyE-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Leopold_I_of_Belgium" title="Leopold I of Belgium">Leopold&nbsp;I</a> became king on <span class="nowrap">July 21</span> 1831. This is now celebrated as Belgium's <a href="/wiki/National_Day" title="National Day">National Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Conference" title="Berlin Conference">Berlin Conference</a> of 1885 gave control of the <a href="/wiki/Congo_Free_State" title="Congo Free State">Congo Free State</a> to <a href="/wiki/Leopold_II_of_Belgium" title="Leopold II of Belgium">King Leopold&nbsp;II</a>. This was for him personally and not for the country of Belgium. Millions of Congolese people were hurt or killed, mostly to make <a href="/wiki/Rubber" title="Rubber">rubber</a>, and Leopold became very wealthy. In 1908 the Belgian state took control of the colony after there was a <a href="/wiki/Scandal" title="Scandal">scandal</a> about the deaths. It was then called the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Congo" title="Belgian Congo">Belgian Congo</a>, but the indigenous peoples continued to be killed and face violence from the colonial government.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Germany invaded Belgium in 1914. This was part of World War&nbsp;I. The opening months of the war were very bad in Belgium. During the war Belgium took over the of <a href="/wiki/Ruanda-Urundi" title="Ruanda-Urundi">Ruanda-Urundi</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a>). After the First World War, the <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Prussia" title="Districts of Prussia">Prussian districts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eupen-Malmedy" title="Eupen-Malmedy">Eupen and Malmedy</a> were added into Belgium in 1925. <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Belgium" title="Battle of Belgium">The country was again invaded by Germany in 1940</a> and under German control until 1944. After World War&nbsp;II, the people made king <a href="/wiki/Leopold_III_of_Belgium" title="Leopold III of Belgium">Leopold&nbsp;III</a> leave his throne in 1951. This is because they thought he helped the Germans. </p>
      <p>In 1960 the Belgian Congo stopped being under Belgian rule.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Two years later Ruanda-Urundi also became free. Belgium joined <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> as a founding member. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government and politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Government and politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Since 1993, Belgium is a <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federal</a> state, divided into three regions and three communities. </p>
      <p>Regions: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Brussels-Capital_Region" title="Brussels-Capital Region">Brussels-Capital Region</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Flemish_Region" title="Flemish Region">Flemish Region</a> (or Flanders)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Walloon_Region" class="mw-redirect" title="Walloon Region">Walloon Region</a> (or Wallonia)</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Communities: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flemish_Community&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Flemish Community (not yet started)">Flemish Community</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/w/index.php?title=French_Community_of_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="French Community of Belgium (not yet started)">French Community of Belgium</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/German-speaking_Community_of_Belgium" title="German-speaking Community of Belgium">German-speaking Community of Belgium</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>It has a system of <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> known as a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>, meaning that it has a <a href="/wiki/Monarch" title="Monarch">monarch</a>, but that the monarch does not rule the country, and that a government is elected <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratically</a>. </p>
      <p>Belgium has had its own monarchy since 1831. King <a href="/wiki/Albert_II_of_Belgium" title="Albert II of Belgium">Albert II</a> left the throne on July 21, 2013 and the current king is <a href="/wiki/Philippe_of_Belgium" title="Philippe of Belgium">Philippe</a>. </p>
      <p>In Belgium, the government is elected. Between mid-2010 and late 2011, after no clear result in the <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">election</a>, Belgium had no official government, until <a href="/wiki/Elio_Di_Rupo" title="Elio Di Rupo">Elio Di Rupo</a> became <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a>. <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a> both also have their own regional governments, and there is a notable independence movement in Flanders. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Michel" title="Charles Michel">Charles Michel</a> is currently the Prime Minister. </p> 
      <div style="clear:both;"></div> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Belgium" title="List of cities in Belgium">List of cities in Belgium</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Belgium is next to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. Its total area is 33,990 <a href="/wiki/Square_kilometers" class="mw-redirect" title="Square kilometers">square kilometers</a>. The land area alone is 30,528&nbsp;km². Belgium has three main geographical regions. The coastal plain is in the north-west. The central plateau are part of the Anglo-Belgian Basin. The <a href="/wiki/Ardennes_(forest)" title="Ardennes (forest)">Ardennes</a> uplands are in the south-east. The Paris Basin reaches a small fourth area at Belgium's southernmost tip, Belgian Lorraine. </p>
      <p>The coastal plain is mostly <a href="/wiki/Sand_dune" class="mw-redirect" title="Sand dune">sand dunes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polder" title="Polder">polders</a>. Further inland is a smooth, slowly rising landscape. There are fertile <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valleys</a>. The hills have many forests. The <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateaus</a> of the Ardennes are more rough and rocky. They have caves and small, narrow valleys. Signal de Botrange is the country's highest point at 694 metres (2,277&nbsp;ft). </p>

The third Region, <a href="/wiki/Brussels_Capital_Region" class="mw-redirect" title="Brussels Capital Region">Brussels</a> is not part of any province. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Belgian Armed Forces have about 46,000 active troops. In 2009 the yearly defence budget was $6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> There are four parts: Belgian Land Component, or the Army; Belgian Air Component, or the Air Force; Belgian Naval Component, or the Navy; Belgian Medical Component. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_technology">Science and technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Science and technology">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Science and technology">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">

      <p>Adding to science and technology has happened throughout the country's history. <a href="/wiki/Cartography" title="Cartography">cartographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Gerardus_Mercator" title="Gerardus Mercator">Gerardus Mercator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anatomy" title="Anatomy">anatomist</a> <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Vesalius" title="Andreas Vesalius">Andreas Vesalius</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Herbalist&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Herbalist (not yet started)">herbalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Rembert_Dodoens" title="Rembert Dodoens">Rembert Dodoens</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematician</a> <a href="/wiki/Simon_Stevin" title="Simon Stevin">Simon Stevin</a> are among the most influential scientists.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Chemist <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Solvay" title="Ernest Solvay">Ernest Solvay</a><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and engineer <a href="/wiki/Zenobe_Gramme" title="Zenobe Gramme">Zenobe Gramme</a><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> gave their names to the <a href="/wiki/Solvay_process" title="Solvay process">Solvay process</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gramme_dynamo" class="mw-redirect" title="Gramme dynamo">Gramme dynamo</a> in the 1860s. <a href="/wiki/Bakelite" title="Bakelite">Bakelite</a> was formed in 1907–1909 by <a href="/wiki/Leo_Baekeland" title="Leo Baekeland">Leo Baekeland</a>. A major addition to science was also due to a Belgian, <a href="/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre" title="Georges Lemaître">Georges Lemaître</a>. He is the one who made the <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang" title="Big Bang">Big Bang</a> theory of the start of the universe in 1927.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Three <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physiology_or_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine">Nobel Prizes in Physiology or Medicine</a> were awarded to Belgians: <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bordet" title="Jules Bordet">Jules Bordet</a> in 1919, <a href="/wiki/Corneille_Heymans" title="Corneille Heymans">Corneille Heymans</a> in 1938 and <a href="/wiki/Albert_Claude" title="Albert Claude">Albert Claude</a> together with <a href="/wiki/Christian_De_Duve" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian De Duve">Christian De Duve</a> in 1974. <a href="/wiki/Ilya_Prigogine" title="Ilya Prigogine">Ilya Prigogine</a> was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Chemistry" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobel Prize in Chemistry">Nobel Prize in Chemistry</a> in 1977.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Two Belgian mathematicians have been awarded the <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a>: <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Deligne" title="Pierre Deligne">Pierre Deligne</a> in 1978 and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Bourgain" title="Jean Bourgain">Jean Bourgain</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In February 2014, Belgium became the first country in the world to legalize <a href="/wiki/Euthanasia" title="Euthanasia">euthanasia</a> without any age limits.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>There have been many additions to painting and architecture. Several examples of major architectural places in Belgium belong to <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>'s <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_List" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage List">World Heritage List</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In the 15th century the religious paintings of <a href="/wiki/Jan_van_Eyck" title="Jan van Eyck">Jan van Eyck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rogier_van_der_Weyden" title="Rogier van der Weyden">Rogier van der Weyden</a> were important. The 16th century had more styles such as <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Brueghel_the_Elder" title="Pieter Brueghel the Elder">Peter Breughel</a>'s landscape paintings and <a href="/wiki/Lambert_Lombard" title="Lambert Lombard">Lambert Lombard</a>'s showing of the antique.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The style of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Paul_Rubens" title="Peter Paul Rubens">Peter Paul Rubens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_van_Dyck" title="Anthony van Dyck">Anthony van Dyck</a> was strong in the early 17th century in the Southern Netherlands.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>During the 19th and 20th centuries many original <a href="/wiki/Romanticism" title="Romanticism">romantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Expressionism" title="Expressionism">expressionist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">surrealist</a> Belgian painters started. These include <a href="/wiki/James_Ensor" title="James Ensor">James Ensor</a> and other artists in the <a href="/wiki/Les_XX" title="Les XX">Les XX</a> group, <a href="/wiki/Constant_Permeke" title="Constant Permeke">Constant Permeke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Delvaux" title="Paul Delvaux">Paul Delvaux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Magritte" title="René Magritte">René Magritte</a>. The sculptor <a href="/wiki/Panamarenko" title="Panamarenko">Panamarenko</a> is still a remarkable figure in contemporary art.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The artist <a href="/wiki/Jan_Fabre" title="Jan Fabre">Jan Fabre</a> and the painter <a href="/wiki/Luc_Tuymans" title="Luc Tuymans">Luc Tuymans</a> are other internationally known figures in contemporary art. </p>
      <p>Belgian contributions to architecture were also in the 19th and 20th centuries. <a href="/wiki/Victor_Horta" title="Victor Horta">Victor Horta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_van_de_Velde" title="Henry van de Velde">Henry van de Velde</a> were major starters of the <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> style.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the 19th and 20th centuries, there were major violinists, such as <a href="/wiki/Henri_Vieuxtemps" title="Henri Vieuxtemps">Henri Vieuxtemps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Ysa%C3%BFe" title="Eugène Ysaÿe">Eugène Ysaÿe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Grumiaux" title="Arthur Grumiaux">Arthur Grumiaux</a>. <a href="/wiki/Adolphe_Sax" title="Adolphe Sax">Adolphe Sax</a> invented the <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophone</a> in 1846. The composer <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Franck" title="César Franck">César Franck</a> was born in Liège in 1822. Newer music in Belgium is also famous. Jazz musician <a href="/wiki/Toots_Thielemans" title="Toots Thielemans">Toots Thielemans</a> and singer <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Brel" title="Jacques Brel">Jacques Brel</a> have made global fame. In rock/pop music, <a href="/wiki/Telex_(band)" title="Telex (band)">Telex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Front_242" title="Front 242">Front 242</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%27s_Choice" title="K's Choice">K's Choice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hooverphonic" title="Hooverphonic">Hooverphonic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zap_Mama" title="Zap Mama">Zap Mama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soulwax" title="Soulwax">Soulwax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deus_(band)" title="Deus (band)">dEUS</a> are well known. In the heavy metal scene, bands like <a href="/wiki/Machiavel" title="Machiavel">Machiavel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_Zero_(band)" title="Channel Zero (band)">Channel Zero</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enthroned" title="Enthroned">Enthroned</a> have a worldwide fan-base.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Belgium has several well-known authors, including the poet <a href="/wiki/Emile_Verhaeren" title="Emile Verhaeren">Emile Verhaeren</a> and novelists <a href="/wiki/Hendrik_Conscience" title="Hendrik Conscience">Hendrik Conscience</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georges_Simenon" title="Georges Simenon">Georges Simenon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzanne_Lilar" title="Suzanne Lilar">Suzanne Lilar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9lie_Nothomb" title="Amélie Nothomb">Amélie Nothomb</a>. The poet and playwright <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Maeterlinck" title="Maurice Maeterlinck">Maurice Maeterlinck</a> won the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_literature" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobel Prize in literature">Nobel Prize in literature</a> in 1911. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Tintin" title="The Adventures of Tintin">The Adventures of Tintin</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Herg%C3%A9" title="Hergé">Hergé</a> is the best known of <a href="/wiki/Franco-Belgian_comics" title="Franco-Belgian comics">Franco-Belgian comics</a>. Many other major authors, including <a href="/wiki/Peyo" title="Peyo">Peyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Franquin" title="André Franquin">André Franquin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_P._Jacobs" title="Edgar P. Jacobs">Edgar P. Jacobs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willy_Vandersteen" title="Willy Vandersteen">Willy Vandersteen</a> brought the Belgian cartoon strip industry a worldwide fame.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Belgian cinema has brought a number of mainly Flemish novels to life on-screen. Belgian directors include <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Delvaux" title="André Delvaux">André Delvaux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stijn_Coninx" title="Stijn Coninx">Stijn Coninx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luc_Dardenne" class="mw-redirect" title="Luc Dardenne">Luc</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Dardenne" class="mw-redirect" title="Jean-Pierre Dardenne">Jean-Pierre Dardenne</a>. Well-known actors include <a href="/wiki/Jan_Decleir" title="Jan Decleir">Jan Decleir</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marie_Gillain" title="Marie Gillain">Marie Gillain</a>. Successful films include <i><a href="/wiki/Man_Bites_Dog_(film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Man Bites Dog (film)">Man Bites Dog</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Alzheimer_Affair" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alzheimer Affair">The Alzheimer Affair</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cuisine">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Cuisine">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Brussels_waffle.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Brussels_waffle.jpg/170px-Brussels_waffle.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Brussels_waffle.jpg/255px-Brussels_waffle.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Brussels_waffle.jpg/340px-Brussels_waffle.jpg 2x" data-file-width="853" data-file-height="1280"></a>

        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>Belgium is famous for beer, chocolate, <a href="/wiki/Belgian_waffle" class="mw-redirect" title="Belgian waffle">waffles</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_fries" title="French fries">french fries</a>. French fries were first made in Belgium. The national dishes are "steak and fries with salad", and "<a href="/wiki/Moules-frites" title="Moules-frites">mussels with fries</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Other local fast food dishes include a <a href="/wiki/Mitraillette" title="Mitraillette">Mitraillette</a>. Brands of Belgian chocolate and <a href="/wiki/Pralines" class="mw-redirect" title="Pralines">pralines</a>, like <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Or_(brand)" title="Côte d'Or (brand)">Côte d'Or</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guylian" title="Guylian">Guylian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chocolatier_Neuhaus" class="mw-redirect" title="Chocolatier Neuhaus">Neuhaus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leonidas_(chocolate_maker)" title="Leonidas (chocolate maker)">Leonidas</a>, Corné and Galler are famous.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Belgium makes over 1100 varieties of beer.<sup id="cite_ref-BierbijbelNieuwsblad_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BierbijbelNieuwsblad-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BierbijbelKW_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BierbijbelKW-41">[41]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Trappist_beer" title="Trappist beer">Trappist beer</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Westvleteren_Brewery" title="Westvleteren Brewery">Abbey of Westvleteren</a> has repeatedly been rated the world's best beer.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The biggest brewer in the world by volume is <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Leuven" title="Leuven">Leuven</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg/220px-Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="197" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg/330px-Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg/440px-Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="715"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Kim_Clijsters_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Kim Clijsters was <a href="/wiki/WTA_Awards" title="WTA Awards">WTA Player of the Year</a> in 2005 and 2010
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>Since the 1970s, sports clubs are organised separately by each language community.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Association football is one of the most popular sports in both parts of Belgium, together with cycling, tennis, swimming and judo.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> With five victories in the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> and many other cycling records, Belgian <a href="/wiki/Eddy_Merckx" title="Eddy Merckx">Eddy Merckx</a> is said to be one of the greatest cyclists of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marie_Pfaff" title="Jean-Marie Pfaff">Jean-Marie Pfaff</a>, a former Belgian goalkeeper, is said to be one of the greatest in the history of football (soccer).<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Belgium and The Netherlands hosted the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Football_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="UEFA European Football Championship">UEFA European Football Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">2000</a>. Belgium hosted the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1972" title="UEFA Euro 1972">1972 European Football Championships</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Kim_Clijsters" title="Kim Clijsters">Kim Clijsters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justine_Henin" title="Justine Henin">Justine Henin</a> both were <a href="/wiki/WTA_Awards" title="WTA Awards">Player of the Year</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Tennis_Association" title="Women's Tennis Association">Women's Tennis Association</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Spa-Francorchamps" title="Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> motor-racing circuit hosts the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One World Championship">Formula One World Championship</a> <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>. The Belgian driver, <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a>, won eight Grands Prix and six <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a>. Belgium also has a strong reputation in <a href="/wiki/Motocross" title="Motocross">motocross</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Sporting events held each year in Belgium include the <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Van_Damme" title="Memorial Van Damme">Memorial Van Damme</a> athletics competition, the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> Formula One, and a number of <a href="/wiki/Classic_cycle_races" class="mw-redirect" title="Classic cycle races">classic cycle races</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Tour_of_Flanders" title="Tour of Flanders">Tour of Flanders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge%E2%80%93Bastogne%E2%80%93Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège–Bastogne–Liège">Liège–Bastogne–Liège</a>. The <a href="/wiki/1920_Summer_Olympics" title="1920 Summer Olympics">1920 Summer Olympics</a> were held in Antwerp. </p>
