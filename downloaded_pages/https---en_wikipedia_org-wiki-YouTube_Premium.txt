      <p><b>YouTube Premium</b> (formerly <b>YouTube Red</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Subscription_business_model" title="Subscription business model">subscription service</a> offered by the video platform <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. The service provides ad-free access to content across the service, as well as access to premium <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_YouTube_Originals" class="mw-redirect" title="List of YouTube Originals">YouTube Originals</a></i> programming produced in collaboration with the site's creators, downloading videos and background playback of videos on mobile devices, and access to the <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Music" title="YouTube Music">YouTube Music</a> music streaming service.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The service was originally launched in November 2014 as <b>Music Key</b>, offering only advertisement-free streaming of music videos from participating <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">labels</a> on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> and <a href="/wiki/Google_Play_Music" title="Google Play Music">Google Play Music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Music_Key_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Music_Key-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Variety_Music_Key_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Music_Key-8">[8]</a></sup> The service was then revised and relaunched as YouTube Red on October 31, 2015, expanding its scope to offer advertisement-free access to all YouTube videos, as opposed to just music.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> announced the rebranding of the service as YouTube Premium on May 17, 2018, alongside the return of a separate, <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Music" title="YouTube Music">YouTube Music</a> subscription service.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Later in the year, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> was planning to make some of the original content associated with the service available on an ad-supported basis. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YouTube_Premium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:YouTube_Red_Logo.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/YouTube_Red_Logo.svg/220px-YouTube_Red_Logo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="37" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/YouTube_Red_Logo.svg/330px-YouTube_Red_Logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/YouTube_Red_Logo.svg/440px-YouTube_Red_Logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="286" data-file-height="48"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:YouTube_Red_Logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>YouTube Red's logo between 2017 and 2018.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The service was first unveiled in November 2014 as Music Key, serving as a collaboration between <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> and <a href="/wiki/Google_Play_Music" title="Google Play Music">Google Play Music</a>, and meant to succeed the former's own subscription service. Music Key offered ad-free playback of music videos from participating <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">labels</a> hosted on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, as well as background and offline playback of music videos on mobile devices from within the YouTube app. The service also included access to Google Play Music All Access, which provides ad-free audio streaming of a library of music.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Alongside Music Key, Google also introduced tighter integration between Play Music and YouTube's apps, including the sharing of music recommendations, and access to YouTube's music videos from within the Play Music app.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Music_Key_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Music_Key-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Variety_Music_Key_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Music_Key-8">[8]</a></sup> Music Key was not YouTube's first foray into premium content, having launched film rentals in 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and premium, subscription-based channels in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>During its invite-only beta, Music Key faced mixed reception due to the limited scope of the offering; YouTube's chief business officer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kyncl" title="Robert Kyncl">Robert Kyncl</a> explained that his daughter was confused over why videos of songs from <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_(2013_film)" title="Frozen (2013 film)">Frozen</a></i> were not "music" in the scope of the service, and thus not advertisement-free.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup> These concerns and others led to a revamping of the Music Key concept to create YouTube Red; unlike Music Key, YouTube Red was designed to provide ad-free streaming to all videos, rather than just music content. This shift required YouTube to seek permission from its content creators and rights holders to allow their content to be part of the ad-free service; under the new contract terms, partners would receive a share of the total revenue from YouTube Red subscriptions, as determined by how much their content is viewed by subscribers.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> also sought to compete against sites such as <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Video" title="Prime Video">Prime Video</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a> by offering original content (YouTube Originals) as part of the subscription service, leveraging prominent YouTube personalities in combination with professional producers. Robert Kyncl acknowledged that many of YouTube's prominent personalities had built their followings and created content while operating on a "shoestring budget", but he maintained that "in order to scale up, it takes a different kind of enterprise, a different kind of skill set" such as story-telling and "showrunning".<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Variety_Red_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Red-15">[15]</a></sup> Prominent YouTube personality <a href="/wiki/PewDiePie" title="PewDiePie">PewDiePie</a>, who was involved in one of the planned originals for the service,<sup id="cite_ref-Variety_Red_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Red-15">[15]</a></sup> explained that the service was meant to mitigate profit loss from the use of <a href="/wiki/Ad_blocking" title="Ad blocking">ad blocking</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>YouTube Red was officially unveiled on October 21, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup> On May 18, 2016, YouTube Red and <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Music" title="YouTube Music">YouTube Music</a> launched in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, the first countries to gain access to the service outside the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On August 3, 2016, YouTube Red support was added to the <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Kids" title="YouTube Kids">YouTube Kids</a> app.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Later that month the service launched in Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On December 6, 2016, YouTube Red expanded to <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_YouTube_Premium">As YouTube Premium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YouTube_Premium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: As YouTube Premium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On May 17, 2018, <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> announced the upcoming rebranding of the service as <b>YouTube Premium</b>, which officially took effect on June 18. The rebranding came alongside the re-launch of <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Music" title="YouTube Music">YouTube Music</a>, with a separate subscription service focused solely on music (that, as before, is bundled with the larger YouTube Premium service, and also offered to <a href="/wiki/Google_Play_Music" title="Google Play Music">Google Play Music</a> subscribers). <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> also announced that the price of the service would increase from US$9.99 to US$11.99 per month for new subscribers; the existing pricing, as well as bundling of YouTube Premium benefits with <a href="/wiki/Google_Play_Music" title="Google Play Music">Google Play Music</a> subscriptions, is grandfathered for those who subscribed prior to the rebranding. Alongside the rebranding, the services also expanded into Canada, and 11 European countries (Austria, Finland, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Norway, Russia, Spain, Sweden and the United Kingdom), with more expansion to come in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> By July 2019, the services were available in at least 60 countries and territories with subscription price difference.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YouTube_Premium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A YouTube Premium subscription allows users to watch videos on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> without advertisements across the website and its <a href="/wiki/Mobile_app" title="Mobile app">mobile apps</a>, including the dedicated <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Music" title="YouTube Music">YouTube Music</a>, <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Gaming" class="mw-redirect" title="YouTube Gaming">YouTube Gaming</a>, and <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Kids" title="YouTube Kids">YouTube Kids</a> apps. Through the apps, users can also save videos to their device for offline viewing, play their audio in the background, and in <a href="/wiki/Picture-in-picture" title="Picture-in-picture">picture-in-picture</a> mode on <a href="/wiki/Android_Oreo" title="Android Oreo">Android Oreo</a> and newer.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Music_Key_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Music_Key-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Variety_Music_Key_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Music_Key-8">[8]</a></sup> YouTube Premium also offers original content that is exclusive to subscribers, which is created and published by YouTube's largest creators.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The service also offers ad-free music streaming through the YouTube Music Premium and <a href="/wiki/Google_Play_Music" title="Google Play Music">Google Play Music</a> services.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YouTube_Premium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_YouTube_Premium_original_programming" title="List of YouTube Premium original programming">List of YouTube Premium original programming</a>
      </div> 
      <p>YouTube Premium offers original films and series produced in collaboration with professional studios and <a href="/wiki/List_of_YouTubers" title="List of YouTubers">YouTube personalities</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup> under the banner <b>YouTube Originals</b>. For multi-episode series, the first episode of a YouTube Originals series is available free.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Verge_Red_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Verge_Red-9">[9]</a></sup> In selected countries where the service is not yet available, individual episodes can also be purchased through YouTube or <a href="/wiki/Google_Play_Movies_%26_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Play Movies &amp; TV">Google Play Movies &amp; TV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Access to YouTube Originals is also included in YouTube's separate <a href="/wiki/Streaming_television" title="Streaming television">streaming television</a> service <a href="/wiki/YouTube_TV" title="YouTube TV">YouTube TV</a>, but a YouTube Premium subscription is still required for the service's other benefits.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In November 2018, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> was planning to offer some of its premium shows available for free on an ad-supported basis by 2020. The Premium subscription would still cover ad-free access, timed exclusivity windows for original content, and content that is not made freely-available.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Variety_Free_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Free-32">[32]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YouTube_Premium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Licensing_terms_for_channels">Licensing terms for channels<span class="anchor" id="Licensing_terms_and_content_blocking"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YouTube_Premium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Licensing terms for channels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In May 2014, prior to the official unveiling of the Music Key service, the <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">independent music</a> trade organization Worldwide Independent Network alleged that YouTube was using non-negotiable contracts with independent labels that were "undervalued" in comparison to other streaming services, and stated that YouTube threatened to block a label's videos from public access if they did not agree to the new terms. In a statement to the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i> in June 2014, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kyncl" title="Robert Kyncl">Robert Kyncl</a> confirmed that these measures were "to ensure that all content on the platform is governed by its new contractual terms". Stating that 90% of labels had reached deals, he went on to say that "while we wish that we had [a] 100% success rate, we understand that is not likely an achievable goal and therefore it is our responsibility to our users and the industry to launch the enhanced music experience".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i> later reported that YouTube had reached an aggregate deal with <a href="/wiki/Merlin_Network" title="Merlin Network">Merlin Network</a>—a trade group representing over 20,000 independent labels, for their inclusion in the service. However, YouTube itself has not confirmed the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-Variety_Music_Key_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Music_Key-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Following the unveiling of YouTube Red, it was stated that these same contractual requirements would now apply to all YouTube Partner Program members; partners who do not accept the new terms and revenue sharing agreements related to the YouTube Red service will have their videos blocked entirely in regions where YouTube Red is available.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The YouTube channels of <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> were a notable party affected by the change; a representative of ESPN's parent, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>, stated that conflicts with third-party rightsholders in regards to sports footage contained in ESPN's YouTube videos prevented them from being offered under the new terms. A limited number of older videos remain on ESPN's main channel.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Similarly, a large amount of content licensed by <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> record labels became unavailable in regions where YouTube Red is available. It was believed that the ability to download videos for offline viewing in YouTube Red was a subject of hesitation for <a href="/wiki/Japanese_media" class="mw-redirect" title="Japanese media">Japanese media</a> companies due to the need to monitor when, where, and how content is being used in accordance with <a href="/wiki/Copyright_law_of_Japan" title="Copyright law of Japan">Japanese copyright laws</a>, hence their content was blocked under the new requirements.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-cdlovingjapan_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-cdlovingjapan-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nw-japanblock_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nw-japanblock-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rn24-japanesemusic_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rn24-japanesemusic-41">[41]</a></sup> </p>
