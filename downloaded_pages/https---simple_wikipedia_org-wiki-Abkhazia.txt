      <p><b>Abkhazia</b> <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> is a partially recognised independent country on the eastern coast of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>. </p>
      <p>The country fought a war with Georgia for its independence in 1991, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgian%E2%80%93Abkhaz_conflict&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Georgian–Abkhaz conflict (not yet started)">Georgian–Abkhaz conflict</a>. Since its <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_independence" title="Declaration of independence">declaration of independence</a> from <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> in 1991, it has been ruled by the partly-recognized <b>Republic of Abkhazia</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Georgia believes Abkhazia is part of its territory and has listed the province, in its official subdivisions, as an <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_republic" title="Autonomous republic">autonomous republic</a> On 28 August 2008, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Parliament of Georgia (not yet started)">Parliament of Georgia</a> passed a resolution declaring Abkhazia a "Russian-occupied territory".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Republic of Abkhazia, with <a href="/wiki/Sukhumi" title="Sukhumi">Sukhumi</a> as its capital, is a <a href="/wiki/Client_state" title="Client state">client state</a> of Russia. It is formally recognised by <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and by <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, and the <i>de facto</i> independent republics of <a href="/wiki/South_Ossetia" title="South Ossetia">South Ossetia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transnistria" title="Transnistria">Transnistria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Абхазия,_Южная_Осетия_и_Приднестровье_признали_независимость_друг_друга_и_призвали_всех_к_этому_же_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Абхазия,_Южная_Осетия_и_Приднестровье_признали_независимость_друг_друга_и_призвали_всех_к_этому_же-13">[13]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/OSCE" class="mw-redirect" title="OSCE">OSCE</a>, and <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> recognise Abkhazia as an integral part of the territory of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-civil.ge_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-civil.ge-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The secessionist movement of the Abkhaz minority led to the Georgian–Abkhaz conflict. The War in Abkhazia resulted in a Georgian military defeat and the mass exodus and <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_cleansing" title="Ethnic cleansing">ethnic cleansing</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Georgians" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgians">Georgian</a> population from Abkhazia. </p>
      <p>There was a 1994 <a href="/wiki/Ceasefire" title="Ceasefire">ceasefire</a> agreement. There is a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">UN</a>-monitored and Russian-dominated <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">CIS</a> peacekeeping operation. The sovereignty dispute has not been resolved. The dispute is a source of conflict between Georgia and Russia. </p>
