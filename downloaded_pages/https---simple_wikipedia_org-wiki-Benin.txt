      <p><b>Benin</b> (officially called the <b>Republic of Benin</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The capital of Benin is <a href="/wiki/Porto-Novo" title="Porto-Novo">Porto-Novo</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> is based in <a href="/wiki/Cotonou" title="Cotonou">Cotonou</a>, the country's largest city. Most people live on the small southern coastline on the <a href="/wiki/Bight_of_Benin" title="Bight of Benin">Bight of Benin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Because Benin was <a href="/wiki/Colonization" title="Colonization">colonized</a> by France and is still close to it, the official language of Benin is <a href="/wiki/French_people" class="mw-redirect" title="French people">French</a>. Languages such as <a href="/wiki/Fon_language" title="Fon language">Fon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoruba_language" title="Yoruba language">Yoruba</a> are commonly spoken. The largest religious group in Benin is <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a>. This is followed closely by <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vodun" class="mw-redirect" title="Vodun">Vodun</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Protestants" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestants">Protestantism</a>. </p>
      <p>Benin is a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation" title="Organisation of Islamic Cooperation">Organisation of Islamic Cooperation</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_Peace_and_Cooperation_Zone" title="South Atlantic Peace and Cooperation Zone">South Atlantic Peace and Cooperation Zone</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Francophonie" class="mw-redirect" title="La Francophonie">La Francophonie</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Sahel-Saharan_States" title="Community of Sahel-Saharan States">Community of Sahel-Saharan States</a>, the <a href="/wiki/African_Petroleum_Producers_Association" title="African Petroleum Producers Association">African Petroleum Producers Association</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Niger_Basin_Authority" title="Niger Basin Authority">Niger Basin Authority</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>From the 17th to the 19th century, Benin was ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Dahomey" class="mw-redirect" title="Dahomey">Kingdom of Dahomey</a>. It was called the <a href="/wiki/Slave_Coast" title="Slave Coast">Slave Coast</a> by <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">white people</a> from as early as the 17th century, because of the large number of slaves shipped from Porto-Novo, Cotonou, and other <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">ports</a> to European <a href="/wiki/Colonies" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonies">colonies</a> in <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">the Americas</a>. </p>
      <p>After slavery was made illegal, France fought a war against Dahomey and took over the kingdom. They renamed it <a href="/wiki/French_Dahomey" title="French Dahomey">French Dahomey</a>. In 1960, Dahomey gained official independence from France.<sup id="cite_ref-Ibp_Usa_p85_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ibp_Usa_p85-11">[11]</a></sup> From 1972 to 1990 a <a href="/wiki/Marxism%E2%80%93Leninism" title="Marxism–Leninism">Marxist–Leninist</a> <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">state</a> called the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Benin" title="People's Republic of Benin">People's Republic of Benin</a> existed. The current Republic of Benin was formed in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-Ibp_Usa_p85_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ibp_Usa_p85-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Benin is a narrow country in west Africa. The country measures about 325&nbsp;km (202&nbsp;mi) at its widest point. It is between the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">Tropic of Cancer</a>. Benin is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Togo" title="Togo">Togo</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> to the east, and the <a href="/wiki/Bight_of_Benin" title="Bight of Benin">Bight of Benin</a> to the south. </p>
      <p>The highest point is <a href="/wiki/Mont_Sokbaro" title="Mont Sokbaro">Mont Sokbaro</a> at 658&nbsp;m (2,159&nbsp;ft). </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/W_National_Park" title="W National Park">Reserve du W du Niger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pendjari_National_Park" title="Pendjari National Park">Pendjari National Park</a> attract tourists who want to see elephants, lions, antelopes, hippos, and monkeys.<sup id="cite_ref-bn_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bn-12">[12]</a></sup> Historically Benin has been a home for the <a href="/wiki/Endangered" class="mw-redirect" title="Endangered">endangered</a> <a href="/wiki/Painted_Hunting_Dog" class="mw-redirect" title="Painted Hunting Dog">Painted Hunting Dog</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lycaon_pictus" class="mw-redirect" title="Lycaon pictus">Lycaon pictus</a></i>;<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Benin's climate is hot and humid. Yearly rainfall in the coastal area averages 1300&nbsp;mm or about 51&nbsp;inches. Benin has two rainy and two dry seasons per year. The main rainy season is from April to late July, with a shorter less strong rainy period from late September to November. The main dry season is from December to April, with a short cooler dry season from late July to early September. In <a href="/wiki/Cotonou" title="Cotonou">Cotonou</a>, the average maximum temperature is 31&nbsp;°C (87.8&nbsp;°F); the minimum is 24&nbsp;°C (75.2&nbsp;°F). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts">Arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Arts">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Arts">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Beninese literature had a strong oral tradition long before French became the main language.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Felix_Couchoro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Felix Couchoro (not yet started)">Felix Couchoro</a> wrote the first Beninese novel, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Esclave_(Beninese_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="L'Esclave (Beninese novel) (not yet started)">L'Esclave</a></i> in 1929. </p>
      <p>Singer <a href="/wiki/Ang%C3%A9lique_Kidjo" title="Angélique Kidjo">Angélique Kidjo</a> and actor <a href="/wiki/Djimon_Hounsou" title="Djimon Hounsou">Djimon Hounsou</a> were both born in Cotonou, Benin. Composer <a href="/wiki/Wally_Badarou" title="Wally Badarou">Wally Badarou</a> and singer <a href="/wiki/Gnonnas_Pedro" title="Gnonnas Pedro">Gnonnas Pedro</a> are also from Benin. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the 2010 census, 27.2% of the population of Benin were <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a>, 24.4% were <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, 17.3% practiced <a href="/wiki/Vodun" class="mw-redirect" title="Vodun">Vodun</a>, 6% other traditional local religious groups, 1.9% other religious groups, and 6.5% have no religious affiliation.<sup id="cite_ref-report_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-15">[15]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Literacy" title="Literacy">literacy</a> rate in Benin is one of the lowest in the world. In 2002 it was said to be 34.7%.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> At one time the education system was not free.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-17">[17]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Cuisine">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Cuisine">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Beninese cuisine is known in Africa for its exotic ingredients and flavorful dishes. Beninese cuisine has lots of fresh meals with a variety of sauces. In southern Benin cuisine, the most common ingredient is <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">corn</a>. It is often used to prepare <a href="/wiki/Dough" title="Dough">dough</a> which is mainly eaten with <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">peanut</a>- or <a href="/wiki/Tomato" title="Tomato">tomato</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Sauce" title="Sauce">sauces</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">Fish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chicken" title="Chicken">chicken</a> are the most common meats used in southern Beninese cuisine however <a href="/wiki/Beef" title="Beef">Beef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goat" class="mw-redirect" title="Goat">goat</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bush_rat" title="Bush rat">bush rat</a> are also eaten. The main food in northern Benin is <a href="/wiki/Yam_(vegetable)" class="mw-redirect" title="Yam (vegetable)">yams</a>. The northern provinces use beef and <a href="/wiki/Pork" title="Pork">pork</a> meat which is fried in palm or peanut oil or cooked in sauces. <a href="/wiki/Cheese" title="Cheese">Cheese</a> is used in some dishes. <a href="/wiki/Couscous" title="Couscous">Couscous</a>, rice, and <a href="/wiki/Bean" title="Bean">beans</a> are commonly eaten, along with fruits such as <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">mangoes</a>, oranges, avocados, bananas, kiwi fruit, and pineapples.<sup id="cite_ref-Benin_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Benin-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Meat is usually expensive. Meals are usually light on meat and generous on <a href="/wiki/Vegetable_fat" class="mw-redirect" title="Vegetable fat">vegetable fat</a>. <a href="/wiki/Smoked_fish" title="Smoked fish">Smoked fish</a> is commonly prepared in Benin. Many people have outdoor mud stoves for cooking.<sup id="cite_ref-Benin_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Benin-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
