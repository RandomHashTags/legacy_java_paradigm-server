      <p><a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> is divided into 81 provinces (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i lang="tr">il</i>). Each province is divided into a number of different <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Turkey" class="mw-redirect" title="Districts of Turkey">districts</a> (<i lang="tr" title="Turkish language text">ilçe</i>). Each provincial government is seated in the central district (<i lang="tr" title="Turkish language text">merkez ilçe</i>). The central district usually bears the name of the province (e.g. the city of <a href="/wiki/Van,_Turkey" title="Van, Turkey">Van</a> is the central district of <a href="/wiki/Van_Province" title="Van Province">Van Province</a>). There are only three exceptions<sup id="cite_ref-oldProv_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oldProv-1">[1]</a></sup> to this naming scheme: </p> 
      <ul>
