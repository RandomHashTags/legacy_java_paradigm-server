      <p>The <b>Republic of Zambia</b> is a country in southern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It shares its borders with the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> to the north-east, <a href="/wiki/Malawi" title="Malawi">Malawi</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> to the west. It was called Northern Rhodesia and it is currently named after the <a href="/wiki/Zambezi_River" title="Zambezi River">Zambezi River</a>. Zambia is a undiscovered tourist hub home to one of the seven natural wonders of the world. The might Victoria falls. Zambia is a melting pot of culture and diversity yet to be appreciated with at least 72 spoken languages. </p>
      <p>The capital of Zambia is <a href="/wiki/Lusaka" title="Lusaka">Lusaka</a>, which is also the largest city in the country. <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Lungu" title="Edgar Lungu">Edgar Lungu</a> is the current president. Its <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/motto" class="extiw" title="wikt:motto">motto</a> is <i>One Zambia, One Nation</i> and its <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> is <i><a href="/wiki/Stand_and_Sing_of_Zambia,_Proud_and_Free" title="Stand and Sing of Zambia, Proud and Free">Stand and Sing of Zambia, Proud and Free</a></i>. Its official language is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zambia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zambia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Zambia originated from <a href="/wiki/Northern_Rhodesia" title="Northern Rhodesia">Northern Rhodesia</a> which was a colony of Great Britain. In 1964 Zambia became an independent country. The first president was <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Kaunda" title="Kenneth Kaunda">Kenneth Kaunda</a>, who ruled Zambia for 27 years with his party <a href="/wiki/United_National_Independence_Party" title="United National Independence Party">UNIP</a>. Zambia was a <a href="/w/index.php?title=One_party_democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="One party democracy (not yet started)">one party democracy</a>. UNIP was the only legal party and all other parties were banned. </p>
      <p>After protests, democratic elections were held in 1991. Kenneth Kaunda lost the elections and gave away his power in an orderly manner to his successor <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Chiluba" title="Frederick Chiluba">Frederick Chiluba</a>, a former union leader. </p>
      <p>Zambia is now a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Multi-party_democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Multi-party democracy (not yet started)">multi-party democracy</a>. It has had three democratic elections since 1991. The latest presidential election was in 2016, which was won by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edger_lungu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Edger lungu (not yet started)">edger lungu</a> against hakahende hichilema. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zambia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zambia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In the 1960s, Zambia was making a lot of money because of the <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> deposits that were mined in <a href="/wiki/Copperbelt" title="Copperbelt">Copperbelt</a> province. When copper became cheaper in the 1970s, the economy got worse because people in Zambia were not making as much money from selling copper. </p>
      <p>Today, Zambia is a poor country. It does not have many industries. Copper is still its main export. Commercial farming in Zambia is starting to make more money. </p>
