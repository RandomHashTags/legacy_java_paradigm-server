      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Human_immunodeficiency_virus" class="mw-redirect" title="Human immunodeficiency virus">human immunodeficiency virus</a> (HIV), which causes <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>, varies in <a href="/wiki/Prevalence" title="Prevalence">prevalence</a> from nation to nation. Listed here are the prevalence rates among adults in various countries, based on data from various sources, largely the <a href="/wiki/CIA_World_Factbook" class="mw-redirect" title="CIA World Factbook">CIA World Factbook</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CIAHIVprevalence_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIAHIVprevalence-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CIAHIVquantity_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIAHIVquantity-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CIAHIVdeaths_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIAHIVdeaths-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>As of 2018, 37.9&nbsp;million people are estimated infected with HIV globally.<sup id="cite_ref-UNAIDS_DATA_2019_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNAIDS_DATA_2019-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The HIV pandemic is most severe in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a>. Over 10% of all people infected with HIV/AIDS reside within the region. Adult HIV prevalence exceeds 20% in <a href="/wiki/Eswatini" title="Eswatini">Eswatini (Swaziland)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a>, while an additional six countries report adult HIV prevalence of at least 10%. Outside Africa, the highest prevalence rate is found in the <a href="/wiki/Bahamas" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> (3.3%).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In absolute numbers, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> (7.1 million), followed by <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> (3.2 million), and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> (2.1 million) had the highest HIV/AIDS number of cases by the end of 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> While South Africa's large population of HIV-positive people is attributable to its high disease prevalence (18.9%, one of the highest in the world), Nigeria's is lower at 2.9%, with India's prevalence rate at 0.3%.<sup id="cite_ref-CIAHIVprevalence_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIAHIVprevalence-1">[1]</a></sup> However, countries such as Nigeria with high HIV rates above 1% are classified as having Generalized HIV Epidemics (GHEs) by UNAIDS, while India's prevalence is well below this threshold, with a prevalence lower than the US's and about the same as France.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On the other end of the spectrum, <a href="/wiki/Svalbard" title="Svalbard">Svalbard</a> is reported as having no cases of HIV/AIDS. </p>
