      <p><b>The Republic of Yemen</b> or <b>Yemen</b> is a nation in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>. It is the old North Yemen and South Yemen, in the south of the Arabian Peninsula. It borders <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a> on the north and the east. To the south is the <a href="/wiki/Guardafui_Channel" title="Guardafui Channel">Guardafui Channel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden" title="Gulf of Aden">Gulf of Aden</a>, and to the west is the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>. Yemen owns the island of <a href="/wiki/Socotra" title="Socotra">Socotra</a> (217 miles) to the south off the coast of East <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. A person or thing from Yemen is called <i>Yemeni</i>. Yemen's <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Sana%27a" title="Sana'a">Sana'a</a>. </p>
      <p>Yemen is the origin land of all Arabs in the Middle East. In ancient times, Yemen was an important center of trade and power. Many powerful kingdoms were in Yemen, including the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sabaeans&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sabaeans (not yet started)">Sabaeans</a>. Yemen was important in the trade of <a href="/wiki/Spice" title="Spice">spices</a> as well. It was known to the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">ancient Romans</a> as <i>Arabia Felix</i> ("Happy Arabia" in <a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a>). They called it Happy Arabia because the area was so beautiful and powerful. </p>
      <p>In the 700s, Yemenis were among the first to join the new religion <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>. Since then, Yemenis have been staunch Muslims who were in the forefront of all Islam conquests. Men whose ancestors came from Yemen ruled <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a> (Muslim Spain) for centuries. The <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> took control of North Yemen, and the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> took South Yemen, in the early 20th century. The two parts were united in 1990 and later fought civil wars. The latest one started in 2015. Some famous people including former President <a href="/wiki/Ali_Abdullah_Saleh" title="Ali Abdullah Saleh">Ali Abdullah Saleh</a> were killed, and thousands died of hunger. </p>
      <p>Today, Yemen has over 20 million people. Most of them speak <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Yemen-map.png" class="image"><img alt="Map of Yemen" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Yemen-map.png/180px-Yemen-map.png" decoding="async" width="180" height="192" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Yemen-map.png/270px-Yemen-map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c2/Yemen-map.png 2x" data-file-width="322" data-file-height="343"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Yemen-map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Map of Yemen
        </div>

      </div> 
      <p>Yemen is in <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a>. It is on the southern half of the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>. A number of <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> islands, including the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hanish_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hanish Islands (not yet started)">Hanish Islands</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamaran&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kamaran (not yet started)">Kamaran</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Perim (not yet started)">Perim</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Socotra" title="Socotra">Socotra</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a>, belong to Yemen. Many of the islands are <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanic</a>. For example, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jabal_al-Tair_island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jabal al-Tair island (not yet started)">Jabal al-Tair</a> had a volcanic eruption in 2007 and before that in 1883. The highest point in Yemen is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jabal_an_Nabi_Shu%27ayb&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jabal an Nabi Shu'ayb (not yet started)">Jabal an Nabi Shu'ayb</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Yemen is one of the poorest and least developed countries in the <a href="/wiki/Arab_World" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab World">Arab World</a>. There is a 35% <a href="/wiki/Unemployment" title="Unemployment">unemployment</a> rate, which means only 35 out of every 100 people in the labor force don't have a job. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Government">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Government">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>As a result of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2011%E2%80%932012_Yemeni_uprising&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2011–2012 Yemeni uprising (not yet started)">2011–2012 Yemeni uprising</a>, the constitution of Yemen was rewritten. New elections were to be held in 2014. Some areas are controlled by armed militant groups. On 23 January 2015, the President, Prime Minister and Cabinet resigned. War continued, with <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> supporting different sides. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governorates">Governorates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Governorates">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Governorates">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>The governorates are divided into 333 districts. The districts are divided into 2,210 sub-districts, and then into 38,284 villages (as of 2001). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Yemen has about 24 million people according to June 2011 estimates. 46% of the people are under 15 years old. Yemenis are mainly of <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arab</a> origin.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-7">[7]</a></sup> Arabic is the official language. </p>
      <p>Religion in Yemen is mostly of two main <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamic</a> religious groups; 53% of the Muslim population is Sunni<sup id="cite_ref-UNHRC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNHRC-8">[8]</a></sup> and 45% is Shiite according to the UNHCR.<sup id="cite_ref-UNHRC_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNHRC-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>According to 2009 estimates, life expectancy in Yemen is 63.27 years.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sport">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Sport">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Yemen_at_the_Olympics" title="Yemen at the Olympics">Yemen at the Olympics</a>
      </div> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Football</a> is the most popular sport in Yemen. The <a href="/wiki/Yemen_national_football_team" title="Yemen national football team">Yemen national football team</a> competes in the <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">AFC</a> leagues. The country also hosts many football clubs that compete in the national or international leagues. </p>
      <p>Yemen's mountains have many opportunities for outdoor sports, such as <a href="/wiki/Rock_climbing" title="Rock climbing">rock climbing</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hill_climbing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hill climbing (not yet started)">hill climbing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skiing" title="Skiing">skiing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hiking</a>. The coast of Yemen and <a href="/wiki/Socotra" title="Socotra">Socotra</a> island have opportunities for water sports, such as <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sailing" title="Sailing">sailing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swimming (sport)">swimming</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diving</a>. Socotra island is one of the best surfing places in the world. </p>
      <p>Yemen's biggest sports event was hosting the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Gulf_Cup_of_Nations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2010 Gulf Cup of Nations (not yet started)">2010 Gulf Cup of Nations</a>. </p>
