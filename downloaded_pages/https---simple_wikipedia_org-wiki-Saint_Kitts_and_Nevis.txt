      <p>The <b>Federation of Saint Kitts and Nevis</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation" title="Help:Pronunciation">/seɪnt ˌkɪts ænd ˈniːvɪs/</a></span>, also known as the <b>Federation of Saint Christopher and Nevis</b>),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Federal" class="mw-disambig" title="Federal">federal</a> nation made up of two small <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. It is the smallest independent country in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Basseterre" title="Basseterre">Basseterre</a>. It is on the main island of St Kitts, also called Saint Christopher. The island of Nevis has some rights of self-government and two seats in the federal parliament. </p>
      <p>St Kitts and Nevis is about one-third of the way from <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> to <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>. </p>
      <p>The headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Caribbean_Central_Bank" title="Eastern Caribbean Central Bank">Eastern Caribbean Central Bank</a> is in St Kitts. That bank controls the <a href="/wiki/East_Caribbean_dollar" title="East Caribbean dollar">East Caribbean dollar</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_Politics">History and Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History and Politics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History and Politics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The two islands were part of the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a> Colony from 1871 to 1956, and of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_Federation" title="West Indies Federation">West Indies Federation</a> from 1958 to 1962. </p>
      <p>The Islands, plus <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>, got limited self-government in 1967. Anguilla objected and the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police" title="Metropolitan Police">Metropolitan Police</a> were sent there in 1968 to stop rioting. Until 19 December 1980 the official name of St Kitts and Nevis was Saint Christopher, Nevis and Anguilla.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>St Kitts and Nevis became an independent <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realm</a> in 1983, Anguilla has stayed a <a href="/wiki/British_overseas_territories" title="British overseas territories">British overseas territory</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1970, a movement to make Nevis separate from St Kitts started. </p>
      <p>In 1983, the constitution set up a 15-member National Assembly. The Assembly is unicameral, that is, it only has one chamber. 3 members of the Assembly are appointed by the Governor General, on the advice of the Prime Minister and the Leader of the Opposition. The other members are elected. If the Speaker decides to stand for re-election he is automatically elected, without a contest. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nevis">Nevis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Nevis">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Nevis">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Nevis is responsible for its own internal affairs. There is an eight-member Nevis Island Assembly, and an Island administration headed by the premier. </p>

      <p>The country has two main islands, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Kitts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Saint Kitts (not yet started)">Saint Kitts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nevis" class="mw-redirect" title="Nevis">Nevis</a>. The highest peak, at 1,156 metres, is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Liamuiga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Liamuiga (not yet started)">Mount Liamuiga</a>. There is also a smaller island named Booby Island. No one lives on Booby Island. </p>
      <p>The islands are of <a href="/wiki/Volcanic" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcanic">volcanic</a> origin. There are large central peaks covered in <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical rainforest">tropical rainforest</a>. People do not live on the steeper slopes leading to these peaks. Most of the people on both islands live closer to the sea. There are many <a href="/wiki/Rivers" class="mw-redirect" title="Rivers">rivers</a> coming from the mountains of both islands. These give fresh water to the people. St. Kitts also has one small <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lake</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Cricket is common in Saint Kitts and Nevis. Top players go to the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team" title="West Indies cricket team">West Indies cricket team</a>. The late <a href="/w/index.php?title=Runako_Morton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Runako Morton (not yet started)">Runako Morton</a> was from Nevis. Saint Kitts and Nevis is the smallest country on Earth to ever host a World Cup event. It was one of the host venues of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 Cricket World Cup</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Kitts_and_Nevis_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="St. Kitts and Nevis national football team (not yet started)">St. Kitts and Nevis national football team</a>, also known as the "Sugar Boyz", has had some international success. They went to the semifinal round of qualification for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> in the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF" class="mw-redirect" title="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</a> region. Led by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Glence_Glasgow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Glence Glasgow (not yet started)">Glence Glasgow</a> they defeated <a href="/wiki/U.S._Virgin_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Virgin Islands">U.S. Virgin Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>. They then lost to Mexico, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines" title="Saint Vincent and the Grenadines">Saint Vincent and the Grenadines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>. </p>
      <p>The St. Kitts and Nevis Billiard Federation, SKNBF, is the governing body for <a href="/wiki/Cue_sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Cue sport">cue sports</a> across the two islands. </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Collins&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kim Collins (not yet started)">Kim Collins</a> is the country's best known <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" class="mw-redirect" title="Track and field">track and field</a> athlete. He has won gold medals in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=100_metres&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="100 metres (not yet started)">100 metres</a> at both the <a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Championships_in_Athletics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World Championships in Athletics (not yet started)">World Championships in Athletics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/2000_Sydney_Olympics" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Sydney Olympics">2000 Sydney Olympics</a> he was the country's first athlete to reach an Olympic final. He and three other athletes represented St. Kitts and Nevis at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>. The four by one hundred metre relay team won a bronze medal in the 2011 world championships. </p>
      <p>American writer and former <a href="/wiki/Figure_skater" class="mw-redirect" title="Figure skater">figure skater</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Triathlete&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Triathlete (not yet started)">triathlete</a> Kathryn Bertine was given <a href="/wiki/Dual_citizenship" class="mw-redirect" title="Dual citizenship">dual citizenship</a>. She tried to qualify for the 2008 Summer Olympics representing St. Kitts and Nevis in women's <a href="/wiki/Road_bicycle_racing" title="Road bicycle racing">cycling</a>. She did not make enough points to go the Olympics.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>St. Kitts and Nevis had two athletes ride in the time trial at the 2010 <a href="/w/index.php?title=UCI_Road_World_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="UCI Road World Championships (not yet started)">UCI Road World Championships</a>. Athletes included Reginald Douglas and James Weekes.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
