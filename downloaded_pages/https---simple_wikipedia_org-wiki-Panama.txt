      <p><b>Panama </b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. Its official name is the <b>Republic of Panama</b>, and it is most famous for the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a>. Countries bordering Panama are <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> in the Northwest and <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> in the Southeast. Panama is between the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. Most of the people in Panama are Roman Catholic (about 80%). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Harbors">Harbors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panama&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Harbors">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Harbors">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/coastline"><span style="color: #CC2200;" title="coastline (Page does not exist on Wiktionary)">coastline</span></a></span> has many good natural harbors. However, Cristóbal, at the Caribbean <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/terminus"><span style="color: #CC2200;" title="terminus (Page does not exist on Wiktionary)">terminus</span></a></span> of the <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/canal"><span style="color: #CC2200;" title="canal (Page does not exist on Wiktionary)">canal</span></a></span>, had the only important port <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/facilities" class="extiw" title="wikt:facilities">facilities</a> in the late <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980s</a>. The many islands of the Archipiélago de Bocas del Toro, near the <a href="/wiki/Beaches" class="mw-redirect" title="Beaches">beaches</a> of <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>, made an <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/extensive"><span style="color: #CC2200;" title="extensive (Page does not exist on Wiktionary)">extensive</span></a></span> natural roadstead and shield for the port of Almirante. The over 350 San Blas Islands, near <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, are spread out for more than 160&nbsp;km along the sheltered Caribbean coastline. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panama&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The population of Panama is about 3,405,813 in May 2010. The ethnic makeup of Panama is: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>50.1%: Multiracial (European, African, Native American, Others).</li> 
       <li>22.0%: Black <a href="/wiki/African" class="mw-redirect" title="African">African</a> (Bantu-Congo <a href="/wiki/African" class="mw-redirect" title="African">42%</a>, Akan-Igbo <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">20%</a>, Mandinga-Wolof <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">20%</a>, Yoruba <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">10%</a>, Others <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">8%</a>).</li> 
       <li>6.7%: <a href="/wiki/Native_American" title="Native American">Native American</a> (Embera, Wounaan, Ngobe bugle, Kuna, Naso, Bribri).</li> 
       <li>8.6%: White <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> (Spanish-Canarian <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">74%</a>, German <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">13%</a>, Italian and Others <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">13%</a>).</li> 
       <li>5.5%: <a href="/wiki/Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian">Asian</a> (Chinese <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">93%</a>, Korean and Japanese <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">7%</a>).</li> 
       <li>7.1%: Other.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The information there **Other** is likely including Arabs, East Indians, and some Russian. </p>
      <p>For ships, Panama is a <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_convenience" title="Flag of convenience">flag of convenience</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panama&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Panama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In 2011 Panama had an unemployment rate of 2.7%.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cia-4">[4]</a></sup> Panama also had a food surplus in August 2008. Panama was ranked at number 60 on the <a href="/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a> in 2008. </p>
      <p>Panama's economy has experienced an economic boom in recent years. Real gross domestic product (GDP) growth was over 10.4% from 2006–2008. The Panamanian economy has been one of the fastest growing and best managed in Latin America. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Business_Chronicle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Latin Business Chronicle (not yet started)">Latin Business Chronicle</a> has predicted that Panama will be the fastest growing economy in Latin America in the five-year period of 2010–14.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Panama is impacted by the global financial crisis. It threatens to damage the social gains made in the past few years. </p>
      <p>The expansion project of the Panama Canal and the free trade agreement with the United States is expected to boost and extend the economy. </p>
