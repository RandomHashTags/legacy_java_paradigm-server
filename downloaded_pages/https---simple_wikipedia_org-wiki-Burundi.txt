      <p><b>Burundi</b> (officially called the <b>Republic of Burundi</b>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The capital of Burundi is <a href="/wiki/Gitega" title="Gitega">Gitega</a>. The official <a href="/wiki/Language" title="***REMOVED***">languages</a> of Burundi are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirundi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kirundi (not yet started)">Kirundi</a> <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. There are about eight and a half million people in Burundi. Burundi is one of the poorest countries in the world. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burundi&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burundi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>One of the smallest countries in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, Burundi is <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a>. It is bordered by Rwanda to the north, Tanzania to the east and south and the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the west. It has an equatorial climate. Burundi is a part of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Albertine_Rift&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Albertine Rift (not yet started)">Albertine Rift</a>, the western extension of the <a href="/wiki/East_African_Rift" class="mw-redirect" title="East African Rift">East African Rift</a>. </p>
      <p>The country lies on a rolling <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> in the center of Africa. The average elevation of the central plateau is 5,600 feet (1,707&nbsp;m), with lower elevations at the borders. The highest peak, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Heha&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Heha (not yet started)">Mount Heha</a> at 8,810 feet (2,685&nbsp;m),<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> is southeast of Bujumbura. The source of the <a href="/wiki/Nile_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Nile River">Nile River</a> is in Burundi province. It is linked from Lake Victoria to its headwaters by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruvyironza_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ruvyironza River (not yet started)">Ruvyironza River</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a> is also an important water source. It serves as a fork to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kagera_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kagera River (not yet started)">Kagera River</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup> Another major lake is <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tanganyika" title="Lake Tanganyika">Lake Tanganyika</a> in Burundi's southwestern corner.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Burundi's lands are mostly <a href="/wiki/Agricultural" class="mw-redirect" title="Agricultural">agricultural</a> or <a href="/wiki/Pasture" title="Pasture">pasture</a>. Settlement by rural populations has led to <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soil_erosion" title="Soil erosion">soil erosion</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are two <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national parks</a>: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kibira_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kibira National Park (not yet started)">Kibira National Park</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruvubu_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ruvubu National Park (not yet started)">Ruvubu National Park</a>. Both were formed in 1982 to keep wildlife populations.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup> </p>
