      </table> 
      <p>The <b>Republic of Trinidad and Tobago</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> in the south <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. It is 11 <a href="/wiki/Kilometre" title="Kilometre">km</a> (7 <a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">miles</a>) away from <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The country has two bigger islands, <b><a href="/wiki/Trinidad" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a></b> and <b><a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">Tobago</a></b>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Islands_of_Trinidad_and_Tobago&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Islands of Trinidad and Tobago (not yet started)">many smaller islands</a>. The capital is <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Spain" title="Port of Spain">Port-of-Spain</a>. The country has about 1,262,366 people in it. </p>
      <p>Trinidad and Tobago received <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> from the United Kingdom on August 31, 1962. Before that, it was ruled by the U.K. Right now, the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> (person who is in charge of the country) is Ms Paula-Mae Weekes. The <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> (person who is in charge of the government) are <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Prime Minister of Trinidad and Tobago">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Keith_Rowley" title="Keith Rowley">Keith Rowley</a> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="President of Trinidad and Tobago">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Paula-Mae_Weekes" title="Paula-Mae Weekes">Paula-Mae Weekes</a>. </p>
      <p>Trinidad and Tobago is very diverse. It has people of many different races and religions. There are people who are children of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabia">Arabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indians</a>, and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> who moved to the islands a long time ago. The biggest religions in the country are <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a>. There are also <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> and African religions in the country. </p>
      <p>The country has some <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> beneath the ground and underwater in the ocean. Natural gas is a liquid that makes machines work and heats houses. Also, many people called tourists visit the islands. They take pictures and learn about the islands. </p>
      <p>The people in the country are famous for their <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>. They also play <a href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> and cricket. </p>

      <p>The population of Trinidad and Tobago is 1.17 million as of (2002). The ethnic groups of the population are: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>40.3%: <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">East Indian</a></li> 
       <li>39.5%: <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">West African</a> descent</li> 
       <li>18.4%: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mixed&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mixed (not yet started)">Mixed Race</a></li> 
       <li>1.2%: <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> and Other</li> 
       <li>0.6%: <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> descent</li>
      </ul>
