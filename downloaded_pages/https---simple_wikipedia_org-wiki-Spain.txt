Spain has borders with <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a> and it holds territories in Africa that border <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>. In Spain's northeast side are the <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees_mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Pyrenees mountains">Pyrenees mountains</a>. </p>
      <p>The people of Spain are called Spaniards. They speak <a href="/wiki/Castilian_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Castilian language">Castilian</a> or <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> (in Spanish, "<span lang="es" dir="ltr">Castellano</span>", from <i>Castilla</i>, or "<span lang="es" dir="ltr">Español</span>" <a class="external text" href="https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish">[<span lang="es" dir="ltr">es</span>pa'ɲol]</a>. They speak other languages in some parts of the country. They are <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leonese_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="Leonese Language">Leonese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">Aragonese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aranese" class="mw-redirect" title="Aranese">Aranese</a> <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">Occitan</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> of about 56% of the population in Spain is <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholic</a>. </p>
      <p>Since 1975, Spain has had a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>. The King of Spain is <a href="/wiki/Felipe_VI_of_Spain" title="Felipe VI of Spain">Felipe VI</a>; he only does what the <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> allows him to. The parliament is called "Las Cortes Generales," and has two bodies: "El Congreso" (The Congress) and "El Senado" (The Senate) and it is chosen by the Spanish people by <a href="/wiki/Vote" class="mw-redirect" title="Vote">voting</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">Prime minister</a> is <a href="/wiki/Pedro_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Pedro Sánchez">Pedro Sánchez</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> and the king's <a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">palace</a> are in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Spain. </p>
      <p>Spain has more than five hundred thousand <a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">square kilometres</a> of land. It is smaller than France, but it is bigger than <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Almost fifty million people live in Spain. Spain is divided into 17 <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous communities</a> (this means that they can decide upon some affairs themselves). Each community has its own government. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Spain">History of Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History of Spain">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History of Spain">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Early history">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Early history">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>People have lived in Spain since the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a>. Later, the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> controlled Spain for about five hundred years; then as the Roman Empire broke up, groups of <a href="/wiki/Germanic_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Germanic people">Germanic people</a> including <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a> moved in and took control. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Moorish_occupation">Moorish occupation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Moorish occupation">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Moorish occupation">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In 711, the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyads</a> took over, and later groups from <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, called the <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moors</a>. At first the Moors ruled most of Spain but the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">reconquista</a> slowly forced them out over seven centuries. They called the land <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a>. They were <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Muslim_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim Spain">Muslim Spain</a> was the farthest western point of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Islamic_civilization&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Islamic civilization (not yet started)">Islamic civilization</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Caliphate" title="Caliphate">Caliphate</a> of Córdoba fell apart in the early <a href="/wiki/11th_century" title="11th century">11th century</a> and Muslim rulers sometimes fought each other when they were not fighting the Christians. Muslim Spain was focused on <a href="/wiki/Learning" title="Learning">learning</a>. The greatest <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">library</a> system outside <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a> was also there. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Kingdom_of_Le.C3.B3n"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Kingdom_of_León">Kingdom of León</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Kingdom of León">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Kingdom of León">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">Kingdom of León</a>, the most important in the early Spanish Middle Ages, was started in 910. This Kingdom developed the first democratic parliament (Cortes de Llión) in Europe in 1188. After 1301, León had the same King as the Kingdom of Castile in <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a>. The various kingdoms remained independent territories until 1833, when Spain was divided into regions and provinces. </p>
      <p>In 1492, the Christians took the last part of Spain that still belonged to the Moors, <a href="/wiki/Granada" title="Granada">Granada</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Boabdil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Boabdil (not yet started)">Boabdil</a>, the last Moorish King of <a href="/wiki/Granada" title="Granada">Granada</a>, surrendered to King <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II of Aragon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" class="mw-redirect" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella I of Castile</a> on 2 January 1492. Ferdinand and Isabella then ruled all of Spain. </p>
      <p>Before this, there were a number of Christian countries in what is now called Spain. Two of these countries, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a>, came together when Ferdinand II of Aragon married the queen <a href="/wiki/Isabella_of_Castile" title="Isabella of Castile">Isabella of Castile</a>. The King ruled as much as the Queen. </p>

      <p>In the same year, 1492, they sent <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> to sail across the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. Columbus found the islands of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. </p>
      <p>When other Europeans explored, like <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s" title="Hernán Cortés">Hernán Cortés</a> and <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pizarro" title="Francisco Pizarro">Francisco Pizarro</a>, they found out that there were two <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continents</a> there - <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Spanish <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">conquistador</a>es took over very large parts of those two continents. This <a href="/wiki/Empire" title="Empire">empire</a> did not make Spain a rich country, for most of the money had to be spent in wars in Italy and elsewhere. Some of these wars were fought against other European countries who were trying to take over parts of the Americas. </p>
      <p>Meanwhile, at home, the Muslim manuscripts had been either burnt or taken to other countries. Jews had also been expelled from Spain. Some Jews remained but they had to become <a href="/wiki/Christians" class="mw-redirect" title="Christians">Christians</a>. Among the few old things kept and respected in Spain were in <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>: <a href="/wiki/Harmony" title="Harmony">harmony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stringed_instrument" class="mw-redirect" title="Stringed instrument">stringed instruments</a>. The buildings that had been built by the Moors were kept, and many Muslim religious buildings (<a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosques</a>) were turned into churches. Some Jewish religious buildings were also turned into churches. Many Arab words became part of the Spanish Language. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="16th_and_17th_century">16th and 17th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 16th and 17th century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: 16th and 17th century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The grandson of Ferdinand and Isabella was Charles. When his grandfather died he inherited Castille and Aragon. He also inherited many territories at the death of his other grandfather, Maximilian I of Austria. Charles received from Maximilian the Austria state and the territories of Burgundy. He was named Charles I in Spain, but he was elected as the emperor of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>, and was called <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor</a>. This made the empire bigger than ever. However, it was not a single country, but a <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> of many independent countries with a single King. At first many Spaniards did not want Charles as their king, so they fought against him. However, he won. </p>
      <p>Charles did not like the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Protestant Reformation</a>, and fought against it. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="18th_century">18th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 18th century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: 18th century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the 18th century some of the parts of that large empire became their own countries, or were taken over by new countries, such as the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 19th century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: 19th century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Spain (and other European countries) was invaded by <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a> of France. <a href="/wiki/Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Britain">Britain</a> sent troops to defend the peninsula, since it was so weak. Most of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> became independent in the following decades. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 20th century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: 20th century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>There was not much peace in Spain during the first part of the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>. Some Spaniards tried to set up a government chosen by the people (a <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>), and they made <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alfonso_XIII_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alfonso XIII of Spain (not yet started)">Alfonso XIII</a> leave the country. However, in 1936, two different groups of Spaniards went to war over whether the government should be a democracy, in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a> (although those on the side of the Republic were largely socialist or anarchist), or take orders from one person. In 1939, those who wanted democracy were defeated, and a nationalist <a href="/wiki/Dictator" title="Dictator">dictator</a> named <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a> took over the government. </p>
      <p>Francisco Franco died on 20 November 1975. He had decided that Spain should have a monarchy again, and he chose Juan Carlos, the grandson of Juan of Bourbon who had been forced to leave the country, to be king and <a href="/wiki/Adolfo_Su%C3%A1rez" title="Adolfo Suárez">Adolfo Suárez</a> to become its first <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Spain" title="Prime Minister of Spain">Prime Minister</a>. But the king and Suárez did not rule as a dictator; instead, they chose to set up a democracy. </p>
      <p>On 23 February 1981 a group of people who had supported the now dead General Franco tried to take control of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic">democratic</a> Spanish Parliament by force, they entered the building and fired guns in the air. It was seen live on Spanish television and there was widespread fear that this might be the start of another civil war. However, Juan Carlos I, quickly appeared on television and broadcast to the nation that they should remain calm. The persons responsible for the attempt to take over the country were arrested. </p>
      <p>Now Spain is a modern democratic country, and does business with many countries around the world. It is the eighth largest economy in the world and is an important part of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: 21st century">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: 21st century">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 2 June 2014, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Carlos I">Juan Carlos I</a> announced that he would abdicate in favour of his son, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_VI_of_Spain" title="Felipe VI of Spain">Felipe VI</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[11]</a></sup> The date of abdication and handover to Felipe occurred on 19 June 2014. He and his wife kept their titles.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-19">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Religion">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Religion">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Ancient religions in Spain were mostly <a href="/wiki/Paganism" title="Paganism">pagan</a>. Today, however, at least 68 percent of Spain is <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[13]</a></sup> Spanish <a href="/wiki/Mysticism" title="Mysticism">mystic</a> <a href="/wiki/Teresa_of_%C3%81vila" title="Teresa of Ávila">Teresa of Ávila</a> is an important figure within <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" title="Catholicism">Catholicism</a>. 27 percent of Spaniards are <a href="/wiki/Irreligious_theism" title="Irreligious theism">irreligious</a>. 2 percent are from other religions, this include <a href="/wiki/Baha%27i" class="mw-redirect" title="Baha'i">Baha'i</a> <a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jain" class="mw-redirect" title="Jain">Jain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>,<a href="/wiki/Unitarian_Universalism" title="Unitarian Universalism">Unitarian Universalism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrianism</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_of_Spain">Geography of Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography of Spain">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Geography of Spain">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>The middle of Spain is a high, dry, flat land called La Meseta. In La Meseta it can be very hot in the <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a> and cold or very cold in the <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a>. Spain also has many <a href="/wiki/Mountain_range" title="Mountain range">mountain ranges</a>. The Mount <a href="/wiki/Teide" title="Teide">Teide</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>), the highest mountain of Spain and the islands of the Atlantic (it is the third largest volcano in the world from its base). In the north there is a range of mountains called <i>Los Picos de Europa</i> (The European Peaks). Here it is very cold in winter with a lot of snow but with gentle warm summers. </p>
      <p>In the south-east of the country is a range of mountains called <i><a href="/wiki/Sierra_Nevada_(Spain)" title="Sierra Nevada (Spain)">La Sierra Nevada</a></i> (The Snowy Mountains). This range of mountains contains the highest mountain in mainland Spain, Mulacen, at 2952&nbsp;metres. La Sierra Nevada is very popular in winter for <a href="/wiki/Winter_sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Winter sports">winter sports</a>, especially skiing. Snow remains on its peaks throughout the year. The south coast, has a warm and <a href="/wiki/Temperate_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate climate">temperate climate</a>, not very hot or very cold. Since Spain is in the south of Europe, it is very sunny. Many people from <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">Northern Europe</a> take their vacations in Spain, enjoying its <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beaches</a> and cities. </p>
      <p>Spain has a border with <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> in the west and borders with <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a> in the North. In the south, it borders <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>, a British territory. The Spanish territories of <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">Melilla</a> are in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and border onto <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>. </p>

         <li id="cite_note-a-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><a href="#cite_ref-a_2-0">↑</a></span> <span class="reference-text">The Spanish Constitution does not establish any official name for Spain, even though the terms <i>España</i> (Spain), <i>Estado español</i> (Spanish State) and <i>Nación española</i> (Spanish Nation) are used throughout the document. Nonetheless, the Spanish Ministry of Foreign Affairs established in an ordinance published in 1984 that the denominations <i>España</i> (Spain) and <i>Reino de España</i> (Kingdom of Spain) are equally valid to designate Spain in international treaties. The latter term is widely used by the government in national and international affairs of all kinds, including foreign treaties as well as national official documents, and is therefore recognised as the official name by many international organisations.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></span> </li>

In each of these, Spain's official name (Spanish: <i lang="es">Reino de España</i>, pronounced: <small></small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Wikipedia:IPA for Spanish">[ˈreino ð(e) esˈpaɲa]</a></span>) is as follows:
