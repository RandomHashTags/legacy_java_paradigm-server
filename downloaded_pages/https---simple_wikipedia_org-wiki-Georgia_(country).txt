      <p><b>Georgia</b> is a country in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Eastern Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Geographic_Encyclopaedia_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geographic_Encyclopaedia-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Other_Europes_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Other_Europes-11">[11]</a></sup> It is on the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>. During 1991-1995 its full name was the <b>Republic of Georgia</b>. Since 1995 it is Georgia as written in the Constitution. Before, it was part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, but now it is an <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The capital city is <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a>. Its population is almost 4 million. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgia_(country)&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgia_(country)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Georgians are an ancient people. Their capital Tbilisi was founded around AD 400, after the end of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> . Western Georgia was part of the Roman Empire before then. The Arabs captured it in 635&nbsp;AD. The culture continued and they flourished through trade. In the 900s Arabs influence diminished in <a href="/wiki/Caucasia" class="mw-redirect" title="Caucasia">Caucasia</a> significantly. In 1008 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kingdom_of_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kingdom of Georgia (not yet started)">Kingdom of Georgia</a> was formed. It was the major country in the region until the <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a> invaded in 1223. Georgia was part of the Mongolian empire for a century on and off until 1334, when King Giorgi V took over. In the 1400s Georgia dissolved into several principalities. In the 1500s the Persians invaded Eastern Georgia four times from 1541-1544. In 1555 the Kings of Kartli ruled through the will of the <a href="/wiki/Safavid_dynasty" title="Safavid dynasty">Persian</a> Shahs. </p>
      <p>In 1783 The treaty of Georgievsk was signed between <a href="/wiki/Catherine_the_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Catherine the Great">Catherine the Great</a> of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and King Heraclius II, giving Russia the power to protect Georgia. Then, in 1798 the Persians burned Tbilisi to the ground. </p>
      <p>From 1811 to 1918 Georgia was under the Tsar of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. Their culture survived intact. From 1918 to 1921 Georgia was independent, and then was part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1991 Georgia declared their independence from the Soviet Union. Newly established <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Georgia">Republic of Georgia</a> saw bloody <a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgian_Civil_War&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Georgian Civil War (not yet started)">Civil War</a> resulting in downfall of first-ever president of Georgia <a href="/wiki/Zviad_Gamsakhurdia" title="Zviad Gamsakhurdia">Zviad Gamsakhurdia</a>. Georgia was also involved in <a href="/w/index.php?title=War_in_Abkhazia_(1992%E2%80%931993)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="War in Abkhazia (1992–1993) (not yet started)">War in Abkhazia</a>. There was a rough patch between 1994 and 1995 when the economy was poor, although Georgia saw significant improvements in last few years. Now Georgia is applying to <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. </p>
      <p>In 2008 Georgia was involved in the <a href="/wiki/2008_South_Ossetia_war" title="2008 South Ossetia war">2008 South Ossetia war</a>. </p>

      <p>Georgia is next to the countries of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>. It also has a <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coast</a> on the Black Sea. It is at the edge of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. </p>
      <p>Georgia has many <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a>. Its highest point is 5,193&nbsp;m above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>. The mountains running through Georgia are called the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains" title="Caucasus Mountains">Caucasus Mountains</a>. </p>
      <p>The highest mountain in Georgia is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mount_Shkhara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mount Shkhara (not yet started)">Mount Shkhara</a> at 5,193&nbsp;m. The coastline of Georgia is 310&nbsp;km long. Georgia has about 25,000 rivers. The largest river is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtkvari&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mtkvari (not yet started)">Mtkvari</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgia_(country)&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgia_(country)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of Georgia is about 4 million. About 1.2 million of these live in Tbilisi. People from Georgia are called <b>Georgians</b>. Most of them speak <b>Georgian</b> as their first language, though some people also speak <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> or other languages. </p>
      <p>Georgians have their own unique three alphabets. They were invented by King Pharnavaz I of Iberia. </p>
      <p>The most famous person to have come from Georgia is <a href="/wiki/Josef_Stalin" class="mw-redirect" title="Josef Stalin">Josef Stalin</a>. </p>
      <p>Most Georgians are <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christians</a>, but there are some <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jews</a> as well. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> of Georgia is called the <i>lari</i>. </p> 
      <h2><span id="Joseph_Stalin.27s_Death"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Joseph_Stalin's_Death">Joseph Stalin's Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgia_(country)&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Joseph Stalin's Death">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgia_(country)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Joseph Stalin's Death">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>KGB chief Lavrentij Beria feared Stalin's next purge and like his predecessor KGB/NKVD chief Yezhov, he would be next, so he had him poisoned. It’s known that on the night of February the 28 1953, Stalin drank "fruit juice" (diluted Georgian wine). Poison, in the form of the poisonous, tasteless blood thinner warfarin (rat poison) was slipped in Stalin’s drink and caused stomach and brain hemorrhaging. Doctors were not called entire day on purpose to make sure the poison works and Stalin could not be saved. His death was recorded at 9:50 p.m. ironically exactly to the minute of signing Katyn execution orders of Polish POWs in 1940. Stalin was laid to rest in the Lenin Mausuleum but was reburied during night without any fanfare on October 31 1956. Upon Stalin’s death, Beria (also responsible for Katyn Massacre), with his network of spies and contacts, seemed poised to take over. But he fatally underestimated his opponent Khrushchev who eventually took over and executed Beria and 6 of his accomplices on December 23 1953. </p>
