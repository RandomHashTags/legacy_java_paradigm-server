      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Bolivia used to be a colony of Spain. The silver mines in Bolivia made most of Spain's money, and Spain used Bolivians as slaves to work in the mines. After many wars, <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Simón Bolívar">Simón Bolívar</a> helped Bolivia to be an independent country. </p>

      <p>Bolivia is 424,135 <a href="/wiki/Square_miles" class="mw-redirect" title="Square miles">mi²</a> (1,098,581 <a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">km²</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup> This means that Bolivia is the world's 28th-largest country (after <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>). It is the same sort of size as <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>. </p>
      <p>Bolivia is a landlocked nation, which means every border of Bolivia is a border with another country, and so it does not have a sea. It used to own some of the Pacific coast, but it lost it in 1879 in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Pacific" title="War of the Pacific">War of the Pacific</a>. The west of Bolivia is on the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> mountain range. The highest mountain in Bolivia is called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nevado_de_Sajama&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nevado de Sajama (not yet started)">Nevado de Sajama</a> and it is near the city of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oruro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Oruro (not yet started)">Oruro</a>. Although this part of the country is very high with lots of mountains, there are also parts of Bolivia which are very flat, and parts of the country which are very near sea level. There is also a bit of Bolivia covered by the Amazon rainforest, and a big lake which is the highest lake in the world. This lake is called <a href="/wiki/Lake_Titicaca" title="Lake Titicaca">Lake Titicaca</a>. </p>
      <p>The major cities are <a href="/wiki/La_Paz" title="La Paz">La Paz</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Alto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="El Alto (not yet started)">El Alto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz,_Bolivia" title="Santa Cruz, Bolivia">Santa Cruz de la Sierra</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cochabamba,_Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cochabamba, Bolivia (not yet started)">Cochabamba</a>. For other places in Bolivia see <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Bolivia" title="List of cities in Bolivia">List of cities in Bolivia</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

       <li>10%: <a href="/wiki/Caucasian" class="mw-disambig" title="Caucasian">White</a>.</li> 
       <li>2%: <a href="/wiki/Black" title="Black">Black</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Economy">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Economy">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Bolivia suffers from <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>. Over 65% of the population lives below the poverty line.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Divisions">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Divisions">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Bolivian culture has many <a href="/wiki/Inca" class="mw-redirect" title="Inca">Inca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aymara_people" title="Aymara people">Aymara</a> and other native influences in religion, music and clothing. There is a big festival in Oruro, which is called "El <a href="/wiki/Carnaval" title="Carnaval">carnaval</a> de Oruro". People in Bolivia like playing <a href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Football (soccer)">football</a>, and football, which is often played in the street. Zoos are also very popular, but they do not have much money. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_symbols">National symbols</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: National symbols">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: National symbols">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Cantuta" title="Cantuta">Cantuta</a> (often spelled kantuta or qantuta) (Cantua buxifolia or Fuchsia buxifolia) is a flower found in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yungas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yungas (not yet started)">Yungas</a>, and is the national flower of Bolivia along with the <i>patujú</i> (Heliconia rostrata) found in the tropical regions of Bolivia. </p>
