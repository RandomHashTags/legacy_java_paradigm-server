      <p><b>Brazil</b> (officially called <b>Federative Republic of Brazil</b>; how to say: <small>IPA:&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" class="mw-redirect" title="Help:IPA">[bɾɐˈziw]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">world</a>'s fifth largest country. The country has about 209 million people. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> of Brazil is <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">Brasília</a>. Brazil was named after <a href="/wiki/Brazilwood" title="Brazilwood">brazilwood</a>, which is a tree that once grew very well along the Brazilian coast.<sup id="cite_ref-Fausto1999_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fausto1999-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>The first people to come to Brazil came around 9,000&nbsp;B.C.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> That group of <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">indigenous people</a> is often called the <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South American</a> <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">Indians</a> and probably came from <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. They practiced <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foraging" title="Foraging">foraging</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Farming" title="Farming">farming</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Britannica-11">[11]</a></sup> Over thousands of years, many different indigenous peoples were living there. </p>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Pedro_%C3%81lvares_Cabral" title="Pedro Álvares Cabral">Pedro Álvares Cabral</a> was the first <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> to see Brazil. He saw it in 1500. He was from <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> and the Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Kingdom" title="Kingdom">kingdom</a> claimed Brazil. Soon, Portugal <a href="/wiki/Colonization" title="Colonization">colonized</a> Brazil and created colonies all along the coastline. They began to import <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black</a> <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> from <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and force them to work.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Because of the violence of the slave masters, many of these slaves would <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">run away</a> into the forest and create their own communities called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Quilombos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Quilombos (not yet started)">quilombos</a>. </p>
      <p>In the late 1500s and early 1600s, the Dutch and the French tried to take land in Brazil. Dutch, French, and Portuguese started moving inland further than the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas" title="Treaty of Tordesillas">Treaty of Tordesillas</a> said they could. This caused some fights with the Spaniards (people from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>) and indigenous peoples in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Britannica-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1822, Brazil claimed to be its own country and not a part of <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> anymore. Soon there was <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a>. Meanwhile, the quilombos survived and Brazil was bringing in more slaves than any other country in the Americas, even though many countries were beginning to legally <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism" title="Abolitionism">abolish</a> slavery. This led to an increase in slave revolts, especially in the 1860s and 1880s, which forced the government to change the system in order to keep the country stable.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Slavery was legally abolished in 1888. </p>
      <p>In 1889, there was a military <a href="/wiki/Coup" class="mw-redirect" title="Coup">coup</a>, and Pedro II had to leave the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Britannica-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1889, Brazil became a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The only people who could vote were people who owned land. There were some uprisings in the 1920s because some people thought the government was unfairly helping coffee growers. Brazil joined the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allies</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Britannica-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>During the 1960s, the <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> leader <a href="/w/index.php?title=Castelo_Branco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Castelo Branco (not yet started)">Castelo Branco</a> overthrew the government and created a <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship" title="Dictatorship">dictatorship</a> that was supported by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14">[14]</a></sup> It was very anti-<a href="/wiki/Communist" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist">communist</a> and they <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">imprisoned</a>, <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">tortured</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Killing" title="Killing">killed</a> many people on <a href="/wiki/Left-wing" class="mw-redirect" title="Left-wing">the left</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14">[14]</a></sup> Since then, the country has become more <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a>, but some people feel that there are still big problems in <a href="/wiki/Health" title="Health">health</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crime" title="Crime">crime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> and <a href="/wiki/Discrimination" title="Discrimination">social inequality</a>. </p>
      <p>In August 2016, then-<a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> <a href="/wiki/Dilma_Rousseff" title="Dilma Rousseff">Dilma Rousseff</a> was removed from office because of <a href="/wiki/Impeachment" title="Impeachment">impeachment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Languages">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Languages">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Most people in Brazil speak <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>. Brazil is the only country in South America that speaks Portuguese. </p>
      <p>Some people in Brazil speak <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> dialects. That came from German immigrants. 2% of Brazilians speak German as their <a href="/wiki/First_language" title="First language">first language</a>. <a href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">Yiddish</a> is spoken by the elders of the <a href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jewish</a> community. </p>
      <p>Other people in Brazil speak their ancestors' languages like <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>. <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> or "Portunhol", a mix of Portuguese and Castilian (Spanish) is spoken at some of the borders. Indigenous languages as <a href="/wiki/Guarani_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Guarani language">Guarani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aymara_language" title="Aymara language">Aymará</a> are the first languages of a small number of Brazilians. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Brazil has the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Rainforest" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon Rainforest">Amazon Rainforest</a>. It makes up 40% of the country's land area. Brazil also has other types of land, including a type of <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> called <i>cerrado</i>, and a dry plant region named <i>caatinga</i>. </p>
      <p>The most important cities are <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">Brasília</a> (the capital), <a href="/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m_(Brazil)" class="mw-redirect" title="Belém (Brazil)">Belém</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Curitiba" title="Curitiba">Curitiba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florian%C3%B3polis" title="Florianópolis">Florianópolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fortaleza_(Brazil)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fortaleza (Brazil)">Fortaleza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goi%C3%A2nia" title="Goiânia">Goiânia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manaus" title="Manaus">Manaus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porto_Alegre" title="Porto Alegre">Porto Alegre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Recife" title="Recife">Recife</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvador,_Bahia" title="Salvador, Bahia">Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> (the biggest city) and <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_(Brazil)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vitória (Brazil)">Vitória</a>. Other cities are at <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_cities_in_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="List of largest cities in Brazil">List of largest cities in Brazil</a>. </p>
      <p>Brazil is divided into <a href="/wiki/Political_subdivisions_of_Brazil" title="Political subdivisions of Brazil">26 states</a> plus the Federal District in five regions (north, south, northeast, southeast and centre-west): </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>North</b>: Acre, Amazonas, Rondônia, Roraima, Pará, Amapá, Tocantins</li> 
       <li><b>Northeast</b>: Maranhão, Pernambuco, Ceará, Piauí, Rio Grande do Norte, Paraíba, Alagoas, Sergipe, Bahia</li> 
       <li><b>Centre-West</b>: Goiás, Mato grosso, Mato Grosso do Sul, Distrito Federal/ Federal District</li> 
       <li><b>Southeast</b>: São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Espírito Santo, Minas Gerais</li> 
       <li><b>South</b>: Paraná, Santa Catarina and Rio Grande do Sul</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The country is the fifth largest in the world by area. It is known for its many <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforests</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jungle" title="Jungle">jungles</a>. It is next to every country in South America except <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>. The name Brazil comes from a tree named brazilwood. </p> 
      <h2><span id="People.2Fculture"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="People/culture">People/culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: People/culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: People/culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Brazil is the largest country in South America and fifth largest in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Its people are called Brazilians or Brasileiros (In Portuguese). The people include citizens of Portuguese or other European descent who mainly live in the South and Southeast, Africans, Native Americans, Arabs, Gypsies and people of Mixed ancestry. Brazil also has the largest Japanese community outside <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cnn_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-17">[17]</a></sup> Other East Asians follow the Japanese group.The Amazon River flows through Brazil, it is the 2nd longest river in the world (after the Nile).The current President of Brazil is Jair Messias Bolsonaro. Two major <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sporting</a> events were held in Brazil recently: the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>. </p>
