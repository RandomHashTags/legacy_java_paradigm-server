      <p class="mw-empty-elt"> </p>
      <p><b>Republic Day</b> is the name of a <a href="/wiki/Holiday" title="Holiday">holiday</a> in several countries to commemorate the day when they became <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republics</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="January">January</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1_January_in_Slovak_republic">1 January in Slovak republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1 January in Slovak republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The day of creation of Slovak republic. A national holiday since 1993. Officially called <i>The day of establishment of Slovak republic</i>. </p> 
      <h3><span id="9_January_in_Republika_Srpska_.28Bosnia_and_Herzegovina.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="9_January_in_Republika_Srpska_(Bosnia_and_Herzegovina)">9 January in Republika Srpska (Bosnia and Herzegovina)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 9 January in Republika Srpska (Bosnia and Herzegovina)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>After <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a> fell apart, <a href="/wiki/Serbs_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Serbs of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Serbs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> wanted to stay with <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Serbia_(1990%E2%80%932006)" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Serbia (1990–2006)">Serbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Montenegro_(1992%E2%80%932006)" title="Republic of Montenegro (1992–2006)">Montenegro</a>. <a href="/wiki/Croats_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Croats of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Croats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bosniaks" title="Bosniaks">Bosniaks</a>, on the other hand, wanted to create an independent state of Bosnia and Herzegovina. On 9 January 1992, Bosnian Serb authorities declared the creation of the Serbian Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina, now called <a href="/wiki/Republika_Srpska" title="Republika Srpska">Republika Srpska</a> ("Serb Republic", not to be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Republic of Serbia</a>) as a state within the country of Bosnia of Herzegovina. Republika Srpska now celebrates Republic Day on the anniversary of the state's creation. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="26_January_in_India">26 January in India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 26 January in India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(India)" title="Republic Day (India)">Republic Day (India)</a>
      </div> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Indian_Army-Madras_regiment.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Indian_Army-Madras_regiment.jpeg/250px-Indian_Army-Madras_regiment.jpeg" decoding="async" width="250" height="179" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Indian_Army-Madras_regiment.jpeg/375px-Indian_Army-Madras_regiment.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Indian_Army-Madras_regiment.jpeg/500px-Indian_Army-Madras_regiment.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="2608" data-file-height="1866"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">

         </div>Soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/Madras_Regiment" title="Madras Regiment">Madras Regiment</a> during the annual Republic Day Parade in 2004.
        </div>

      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_India" title="Constitution of India">Constitution of India</a> came into force, and India declared itself a Republic on 26 January 1950, a day thereafter celebrated annually as <b>Republic Day</b> in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. The Constitution had been drafted by the <a href="/wiki/Constituent_Assembly" class="mw-redirect" title="Constituent Assembly">Constituent Assembly</a> headed by Dr. <a href="/wiki/B.R._Ambedkar" class="mw-redirect" title="B.R. Ambedkar">B.R. Ambedkar</a> which was set up when India gained its independence from the British in 1947. This, in fact, was a deliberate act: The 26 January was initially India's "Independence Day", one of Mahatma Gandhi's many symbolic acts during India's struggle for freedom against British colonial rule, and the adoption of the Constitution on this date was felt able to strengthen its initial meaning, one calling for Indians of all ages to declare their freedom from the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a> by Mahatama Gandhi. It is one of three <a href="/wiki/Holidays_in_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Holidays in India">national holidays</a> in India, the other two being the nation's <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(India)" title="Independence Day (India)">Independence Day</a> on 15 August (since 1947) and the <a href="/wiki/Gandhi_Jayanti" title="Gandhi Jayanti">birthday of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi</a> on 2 October. </p>
      <p>To mark this occasion, a grand parade is held near <a href="/wiki/Rajpath" title="Rajpath">Rajpath</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Capital_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital of India">capital of India</a>, beginning from <a href="/wiki/Raisina_Hill" title="Raisina Hill">Raisina Hill</a> near the <a href="/wiki/Rashtrapati_Bhavan" title="Rashtrapati Bhavan">Rashtrapati Bhavan</a> (Presidential Palace), along the <a href="/wiki/Rajpath" title="Rajpath">Rajpath</a>, past <a href="/wiki/India_Gate" title="India Gate">India Gate</a> and on to the historic <a href="/wiki/Red_Fort" title="Red Fort">Red Fort</a> in the old quarter of the city. Different infantry, cavalry and mechanized regiments of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Army" title="Indian Army">Indian Army</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Marines" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Marines">Indian Marines</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Air_Force" title="Indian Air Force">Indian Air Force</a> march in formation, decked in all their finery and official decorations. The <a href="/wiki/President_of_India" title="President of India">President of India</a>, who is also the <a href="/wiki/Commander_in_Chief" class="mw-redirect" title="Commander in Chief">Commander in Chief</a> of the Indian Armed Forces, takes the salute. The <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(India)#Chief_Guest" title="Republic Day (India)">Chief Guest of the parade</a> is the Head of State or Head of Government of another nation. The parade also includes many traditional dance troupes, to symbolize the cultural heritage of India. It traditionally ends with a colourful <a href="/wiki/Flypast" title="Flypast">flypast</a> by <a href="/wiki/Indian_Air_Force" title="Indian Air Force">Indian Air Force</a> jets in a <a href="/wiki/Tiranga" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiranga">tiranga</a> formation. Similar parades are held in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_state_and_union_territory_capitals_in_India" title="List of state and union territory capitals in India">capitals of all the states of India</a>, where the Governors of the respective states take the salute. The official conclusion of republic day festivities is much later on 30 January, four days after the Republic day. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1_February_in_Hungary">1 February in Hungary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1 February in Hungary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 1 February 1946 <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> commemorates the proclamation of the Republic of Hungary. Since 2004, this day is a national commemoration day, without being a public or national Holiday. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="March">March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="23_March_in_Pakistan">23 March in Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 23 March in Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(Pakistan)" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic Day (Pakistan)">Republic Day (Pakistan)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> this day marks the passing of the <a href="/wiki/Lahore_Resolution" title="Lahore Resolution">Lahore Resolution</a>. Republic Day of Pakistan was first observed in 1956 when Pakistan officially became a Republic and shunned the former status of <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Pakistan" title="Dominion of Pakistan">Dominion</a>. The main events of this day include a full <a href="/wiki/Military_parade" title="Military parade">military parade</a> and the awarding of honours at the <a href="/wiki/Aiwan-e-Sadr" title="Aiwan-e-Sadr">Presidency</a> (Presidential Palace) by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Pakistan" title="President of Pakistan">President</a>. Every year, on 23 March, the Pakistani people commemorate their National Day in remembrance of "The Pakistan Resolution" passed on 23 March 1940, in the historic city of Lahore which is also the day the country was declared a republic. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="May">May</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: May">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span id="28_May_in_Armenia.2C_Azerbaijan_and_Nepal"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="28_May_in_Armenia,_Azerbaijan_and_Nepal">28 May in Armenia, Azerbaijan and Nepal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 28 May in Armenia, Azerbaijan and Nepal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 28 May 1918 <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> declared independence from the <a href="/wiki/Transcaucasian_Democratic_Federative_Republic" title="Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic">Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic</a>, thus forming the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Armenia" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Republic of Armenia">Democratic Republic of Armenia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan_Democratic_Republic" title="Azerbaijan Democratic Republic">Azerbaijan Democratic Republic</a>. It was the first sovereign republic in the history of both countries and Azerbaijan the first democratic <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_republic" title="Parliamentary republic">parliamentary republic</a> in the Muslim world.. However the holiday was not celebrated during <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> times, and it only achieved consistency after the <a href="/wiki/Collapse_of_the_USSR" class="mw-redirect" title="Collapse of the USSR">collapse of the USSR</a>. </p>
      <p>A decade-long People's Revolution by the Communist Party of Nepal (Maoist) along with several weeks of mass protests by all major political parties of Nepal in 2006, culminated in a peace accord and the ensuing elections for the constituent assembly voted overwhelmingly in favor of the abdication of the last Nepali monarch Gyanendra Shah and the establishment of a federal democratic republic on 28 May 2008. </p> 
      <h3><span id="31_May_in_South_Africa_.281961.E2.80.931994.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="31_May_in_South_Africa_(1961–1994)">31 May in South Africa (1961–1994)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 31 May in South Africa (1961–1994)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Between 1961 and 1994, 31 May was celebrated in South Africa as <b>Republic Day</b>. This practice was discontinued in 1995 following the attainment of majority rule and the reorganisation of public holidays as a consequence. On the last Republic Day, in 1994, South Africa rejoined the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="June">June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2_June_in_Italy">2 June in Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2 June in Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(Italy)" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic Day (Italy)">Festa della Repubblica</a> is celebrated on the second day of June, and it commemorates the institutional referendum of 1946 when (by <a href="/wiki/Universal_suffrage" title="Universal suffrage">universal suffrage</a>) the Italian population was called to decide what form of government (<a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">monarchy</a> or <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>) to give to the country after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and the fall of <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">Fascism</a>. </p>
      <p>After 85 years of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_(1861%E2%80%931946)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Italy (1861–1946)">monarchy</a>, with 12,717,923 votes for and 10,719,284 votes against, <a href="/wiki/Birth_of_the_Italian_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Birth of the Italian Republic">Italy became a Republic</a>, and the monarchs of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Savoy" title="House of Savoy">House of Savoy</a> were deposed and exiled. This is one of the most important Italian public holidays which, like 14 July in France and 4 July in the USA, celebrates the birth of the nation. A grand military parade is held in central Rome. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="5_October_in_Portugal">5 October in Portugal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 5 October in Portugal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="10_October_in_Republic_of_China">10 October in Republic of China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: 10 October in Republic of China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>10 October in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> is a national holiday commemorating the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> in 1911, the symbolic start of the Chinese revolution with the <a href="/wiki/Wuchang_uprising" class="mw-redirect" title="Wuchang uprising">Wuchang uprising</a>. It is also known as the <a href="/wiki/Double_Ten_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Double Ten Day">Double Ten Day</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span id="24_October_in_Rhodesia_.281970-1979.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="24_October_in_Rhodesia_(1970-1979)">24 October in Rhodesia (1970-1979)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: 24 October in Rhodesia (1970-1979)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Although the government of <a href="/wiki/Ian_Smith" title="Ian Smith">Ian Smith</a> declared <a href="/wiki/Rhodesia" title="Rhodesia">Rhodesia</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>) a republic on 2 March 1970, it was officially commemorated on 24 October. Following independence in 1980, the holiday was abolished. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="25_October_in_Kazakhstan">25 October in Kazakhstan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: 25 October in Kazakhstan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the waning days of Soviet rule, individual <a href="/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Republics of the Soviet Union">republics of the Soviet Union</a> sought greater autonomy. The <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> agreed in early 1990 to give up its monopoly of political power. Following the lead of <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_SSR" class="mw-redirect" title="Lithuanian SSR">Lithuanian SSR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_SFSR" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian SFSR">Russian SFSR</a> and others, <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_SSR" class="mw-redirect" title="Kazakh SSR">Kazakh SSR</a> declared its sovereignty on 25 October 1990, and <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> subsequently became independent on 16 December 1991 as the Soviet Union collapsed. 25 October, the anniversary of the adoption of the "Declaration on State Sovereignty of Kazakh SSR" by the Kazakh legislature in 1990, is now commemorated as Republic Day (<a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh</a>: <span lang="kk">Республика күні</span>, <small>romanized:&nbsp;</small><i lang="kk-Latn" title="Kazakh-language romanization">Respwblïka küni</i>), a <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan#Public_holidays" title="Kazakhstan">public holiday in Kazakhstan</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="29_October_in_Turkey">29 October in Turkey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: 29 October in Turkey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 29 October 1923, the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Turkey" title="Constitution of Turkey">Turkish constitution</a> was amended and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> became a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. This formally declared the dissolution of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>. Republic Day (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i>Cumhuriyet Bayramı</i>) is celebrated throughout Turkey and Northern Cyprus every year. Commemorative events usually begin in the afternoon on the previous day. In observance of the holiday, government offices and schools close for a day. Also, there are fireworks shows in all cities of Turkey.That day everyone commemorates Mustafa Kemal Atatürk. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="November">November</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: November">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="11_November_in_the_Maldives">11 November in the Maldives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: 11 November in the Maldives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 11 November 1968, <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a> the then existing monarchy was abolished and replaced by a republic. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="15_November_in_Brazil">15 November in Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: 15 November in Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 15 November 1889, in the city of Rio de Janeiro (the Brazilian capital at that time), a military coup led by Field Marshal <a href="/wiki/Deodoro_da_Fonseca" title="Deodoro da Fonseca">Deodoro da Fonseca</a> overthrew Emperor <a href="/wiki/Pedro_II_of_Brazil" title="Pedro II of Brazil">Pedro II</a> and declared <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> a republic. </p> 
      <h3><span id="29_November_in_former_Yugoslavia_.281945.E2.80.931990.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="29_November_in_former_Yugoslavia_(1945–1990)">29 November in former Yugoslavia (1945–1990)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: 29 November in former Yugoslavia (1945–1990)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 29 November 1943 the <a href="/wiki/AVNOJ" class="mw-redirect" title="AVNOJ">Anti-Fascist Council of National Liberation of Yugoslavia</a> established the foundations of post-war <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Socialist_republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist republic">socialist republic</a>, which was officially proclaimed on the same date in 1945. <b>Republic Day</b> (local name: <i>Dan Republike</i> or Дан Републике) marked the occasion two consecutive days, 29 and 30 November, and was likely the most important holiday (the other two-day holidays were <a href="/wiki/New_Year" title="New Year">New Year</a> and <a href="/wiki/May_Day" title="May Day">May Day</a>). </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Elementary_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Elementary school">elementary schools</a> first graders were inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_Movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Pioneer Movement">Pioneer Movement</a> on or around Republic Day. Employees merged the holiday with weekends and extra days off to form 3-, 4- or even 5-day weekends. Urban dwellers took the occasion to visit their relatives in the country, who marked the event with <a href="/wiki/Domestic_pig" title="Domestic pig">pig</a> slaughter and the ensuing feast. </p>
      <p>In 1980s, as central and <a href="/wiki/League_of_Communists_of_Yugoslavia" title="League of Communists of Yugoslavia">Communist Party</a> authority eroded, dissenters targeted Republic Day celebrations for criticism. In 1987 <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Republic_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Socialist Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnian</a> <a href="/wiki/Garage_rock" title="Garage rock">garage rock</a> band <i><a href="/wiki/Zabranjeno_pu%C5%A1enje" class="mw-redirect" title="Zabranjeno pušenje">Zabranjeno pušenje</a></i> published a song entitled <i>Dan Republike</i>, in which they criticized the state of the economy and protested the general indifference to the ideals behind the holiday. The band had to <a href="/wiki/Censorship" title="Censorship">change some of the lyrics</a> before being allowed to air the song. </p>
      <p>In 1989, <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Republic_of_Slovenia" title="Socialist Republic of Slovenia">Slovenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Republic_of_Croatia" title="Socialist Republic of Croatia">Croatia</a> were the first federal republics to cease observing the holiday. Other seceded republics followed suit as <a href="/wiki/Breakup_of_Yugoslavia" title="Breakup of Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia dissolved</a>. <a href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a> kept the holiday until 2002. </p>
      <p>The date "29.XI.1943" figured prominently on the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_the_Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" class="mw-redirect" title="Coat of arms of the Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Yugoslav coat of arms</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="December">December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="13_December_in_Malta">13 December in Malta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: 13 December in Malta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 13 December 1974, the <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> was substantially revised, transforming the former British <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> from a <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Realm" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth Realm">Commonwealth Realm</a> into a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a>. The British Monarch stopped being called <i>Reġina ta' Malta</i> (<a href="/wiki/Queen_of_Malta" title="Queen of Malta">Queen of Malta</a>) and the new Head of State was President Sir <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Mamo" title="Anthony Mamo">Anthony Mamo</a>. This occasion is marked every year as <i>Republic Day</i> (Maltese: <i>Jum ir-Repubblika</i>) in Malta. The monument of Republic Day is at <a href="/wiki/Marsa_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Marsa (city)">Marsa</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="18_December_in_Niger">18 December in Niger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: 18 December in Niger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>18 December 1958 is commemorated in the <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Republic of Niger</a> as <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(Niger)" title="Republic Day (Niger)">Republic Day</a>, the national holiday. Although not the date of formal, complete independence from <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, 18 December marks the founding of the Republic and creation of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Niger" class="mw-redirect" title="President of Niger">Presidency of the Republic of Niger</a>, following the constitutional changes of the <a href="/wiki/French_Fifth_Republic" title="French Fifth Republic">French Fifth Republic</a>, and the elections of 4 December 1958 held across <a href="/wiki/French_Colonial_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="French Colonial Empire">The French Colonial possessions</a>. Nigeriens consider this date to be the founding of their nation. Between 18 December 1958 and 5 August 1964, Niger remained a semi-autonomous Republic within the <a href="/wiki/French_Community" title="French Community">French Community</a>. </p>
      <p>The 16th is celebrated in Niger with official festivals and appearances of political leaders, as well as public parties and festivities. The 50th anniversary celebrations were held in 2006, centered not in the capitol, but in the regional center of <a href="/wiki/Tillab%C3%A9ry" class="mw-redirect" title="Tillabéry">Tillabéry</a>, and surrounded by sports, musical and arts competitions, the opening of new buildings, a National Youth Festival, and other public festivities.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[1]</a></sup> </p>
