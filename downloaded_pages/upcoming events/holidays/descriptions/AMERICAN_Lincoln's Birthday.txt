      <p><b>Lincoln's Birthday</b> is <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_the_United_States" title="Public holidays in the United States">a legal, public holiday in some U.S. states</a>, observed on the anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>'s birth on February 12, 1809 in Hodgenville (Hodgensville, Hodgen's Mill), Kentucky.<sup id="cite_ref-calif_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-calif-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> observe the holiday. </p>
      <p>In many other states, Lincoln's birthday is not celebrated separately, as a stand-alone holiday. Instead Lincoln's Birthday is combined with a celebration of President <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a>'s birthday (also in February) and celebrated either as <a href="/wiki/Washington%27s_Birthday" title="Washington's Birthday">Washington's Birthday</a> or as <a href="/wiki/Presidents%27_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidents' Day">Presidents' Day</a> on the third Monday in February, concurrent with the <a href="/wiki/Federal_holidays_in_the_United_States" title="Federal holidays in the United States">federal holiday</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln%27s_Birthday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The earliest known observance of Lincoln's birthday occurred in <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>, in either 1873 or 1874. Julius Francis (d. 1881), a Buffalo druggist, made it his life's mission to honor <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln_assassination" class="mw-redirect" title="Abraham Lincoln assassination">the slain president</a>. He repeatedly petitioned <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> to establish Lincoln's birthday as a <a href="/wiki/Civic_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Civic holiday">legal holiday</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The day is marked by traditional wreath-laying ceremonies at <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln_Birthplace_National_Historic_Site" class="mw-redirect" title="Abraham Lincoln Birthplace National Historic Site">Abraham Lincoln Birthplace National Historic Site</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hodgenville,_Kentucky" title="Hodgenville, Kentucky">Hodgenville, Kentucky</a>, and at the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Memorial" title="Lincoln Memorial">Lincoln Memorial</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> The latter has been the site of a ceremony ever since the Memorial was dedicated. Since that event in 1922, observances continue to be organized by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_Birthday_National_Commemorative_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lincoln Birthday National Commemorative Committee (page does not exist)">Lincoln Birthday National Commemorative Committee</a> and by the <a href="/wiki/Military_Order_of_the_Loyal_Legion_of_the_United_States" title="Military Order of the Loyal Legion of the United States">Military Order of the Loyal Legion of the United States</a> (MOLLUS). A wreath is laid on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, a custom also carried out at the grave sites of all deceased U.S. presidents on their birthdays. <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Tomb" title="Lincoln Tomb">Lincoln's tomb</a> is in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Illinois" title="Springfield, Illinois">Springfield, Illinois</a>. </p>
      <p>On February 12, 2009, the annual wreath-laying ceremony at the Lincoln Memorial commemorated Lincoln's 200th birthday in grand fashion. An extended ceremony, organized by the <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln_Bicentennial_Commission" title="Abraham Lincoln Bicentennial Commission">Abraham Lincoln Bicentennial Commission</a> (ALBC) and with help from MOLLUS, featured musical performances from four-time Grammy-nominated singer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Feinstein" title="Michael Feinstein">Michael Feinstein</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Band" title="United States Marine Band">U.S. Marine Corps Band</a>. The morning celebration also featured remarks by Illinois Sen. <a href="/wiki/Dick_Durbin" title="Dick Durbin">Dick Durbin</a>; Lincoln scholar and ALBC Co-Chair <a href="/wiki/Harold_Holzer" title="Harold Holzer">Harold Holzer</a>; recently retired <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_Supreme_Court" title="Rhode Island Supreme Court">Rhode Island Supreme Court</a> Chief Justice – and ALBC Commissioner – <a href="/wiki/Frank_J._Williams" title="Frank J. Williams">Frank J. Williams</a>; and author <a href="/wiki/Nikki_Giovanni" title="Nikki Giovanni">Nikki Giovanni</a> reciting her newest work, which was written especially for the <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/bicentennial" class="extiw" title="wikt:bicentennial">Bicentennial</a>. </p>
      <p>As part of Lincoln's birthday bicentennial, the U.S. Mint released four new <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_cent" title="Lincoln cent">Lincoln cents</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Modern_United_States_commemorative_coins" class="mw-redirect" title="Modern United States commemorative coins">commemorative coins</a> have new designs on the <a href="/wiki/Obverse_and_reverse" title="Obverse and reverse">reverse</a> showing stages of his life. The first went into <a href="/wiki/Circulation_(currency)" class="mw-redirect" title="Circulation (currency)">circulation</a> on September 12, 2009. The standard portrait of Lincoln's head remains on the front. The new designs include a <a href="/wiki/Log_cabin" title="Log cabin">log cabin</a> representing his birthplace, Lincoln as a young man reading while sitting on a log that he was taking a break from splitting, Lincoln as a state legislator in front of the <a href="/wiki/Old_State_Capitol_State_Historic_Site" title="Old State Capitol State Historic Site">Illinois Capitol</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Capitol_dome" title="United States Capitol dome">partially built dome</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Capitol" title="United States Capitol">U.S. Capitol</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> stopped observing the holiday on May 23, 2005 with the enactment of the Public Employee Pension and Benefits Reform Act of 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_Black_History_Month">Origin of Black History Month</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln%27s_Birthday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin of Black History Month">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Black_History_Month" title="Black History Month">Black History Month</a> has its origin in 19th-century celebrations of Lincoln's Birthday by <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> communities in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-Scott_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-8">[8]</a></sup> By the early 20th century, black communities were annually celebrating Lincoln's birthday in conjunction with the birthday of former slave and abolitionist <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Douglass" title="Frederick Douglass">Frederick Douglass</a> on February 14.<sup id="cite_ref-Scott_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-8">[8]</a></sup> The precursor to Black History Month was created in 1926 when historian <a href="/wiki/Carter_G._Woodson" title="Carter G. Woodson">Carter G. Woodson</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_the_Study_of_Negro_Life_and_History" class="mw-redirect" title="Association for the Study of Negro Life and History">Association for the Study of Negro Life and History</a> announced that the second week of February would be "Negro History Week" to coincide with the traditional Black commemorations of both men's birthdays.<sup id="cite_ref-Scott_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-8">[8]</a></sup> By the 1970s, "Negro History Week" had become "Black History Month".<sup id="cite_ref-Scott_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-8">[8]</a></sup> Black History Month has expanded further to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, where it is also celebrated in February, and to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, which celebrates it in October. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_government_holidays">Official government holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln%27s_Birthday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Official government holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Lincoln's Birthday was never a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Federal_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Federal Government">U.S. Federal Government</a> holiday. The third Monday in February remains only "Washington's Birthday" in federal law.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> However, many state governments have officially renamed their Washington's Birthday state holiday as "Presidents' Day", "Washington and Lincoln Day", or other such designations which explicitly or implicitly celebrate Lincoln's birthday. Regardless of the official name and purpose, celebrations and commemorations on or about the third Monday often include honoring Lincoln. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, while Washington's Birthday is a federal holiday, Lincoln's Birthday is still a state holiday, falling on February 12 regardless of the day of the week. California still lists Lincoln's Birthday as a holiday,<sup id="cite_ref-calif_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-calif-1">[1]</a></sup> but as of 2009 no longer gives State employees a paid holiday on February 12.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> However, it is considered a "Court holiday" and state courts are closed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the following states, the third Monday in February is an official state holiday and known as: </p>
      <p><i>Using "president"</i> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Presidents' Day in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> and <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a><sup id="cite_ref-nlrodgers_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nlrodgers-13">[13]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>President's Day in <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>, <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a></li> 
       <li>Presidents Day in <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a></li> 
       <li>Washington's Birthday/President's Day in <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a></li> 
       <li>Lincoln/Washington/Presidents' Day in <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p><i>Washington and Lincoln</i> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Washington and Lincoln Day in <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a></li> 
       <li>Washington–Lincoln Day in <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>Washington's and Lincoln's Birthday in <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>Lincoln's and Washington's Birthday in <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a></li> 
       <li>Washington's and Lincoln's Birthday in <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <p><i>Washington alone</i> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>George Washington Day in <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p><i>Washington and another person</i> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>George Washington/Thomas Jefferson Birthday in <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>George Washington's Birthday and <a href="/wiki/Daisy_Bates_(civil_rights_activist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Daisy Bates (civil rights activist)">Daisy Gatson Bates</a> Day in <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p><i>Unspecified</i> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>"The third Monday in February" in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Several states honor presidents with official state holidays that do not fall on the third Monday of February. In <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, Presidents' Day, at least as a state-government paid holiday, is observed on the <a href="/wiki/Black_Friday_(shopping)" title="Black Friday (shopping)">Friday following</a> <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving" title="Thanksgiving">Thanksgiving</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, Presidents' Day, at least as a state-government paid holiday, is observed on <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">Christmas Eve</a>. (Observed on the prior Thursday if <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> falls on Saturday; observed on the prior Friday if <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> falls on a Sunday. If December 24 is a Wednesday, then this holiday is observed on Friday December 26.)<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
