      <p><b>National Pearl Harbor Remembrance Day</b>, also referred to as <b>Pearl Harbor Remembrance Day</b> or <b>Pearl Harbor Day</b>, is observed annually in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on December 7, to remember and honor the 2,403 Americans who were killed in the Japanese surprise <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a> in Hawaii on December 7, 1941, which led to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_declaration_of_war_on_Japan" title="United States declaration of war on Japan">United States declaring war on Japan</a> the next day and thus entering <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. </p>
      <p>In 1994, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Public_Law_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Law (United States)"><abbr title="Public Law (United States)">Pub.L.</abbr></a>&nbsp;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://uslaw.link/citation/us-law/public/103/308">103–308</a>, 108&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/United_States_Statutes_at_Large" title="United States Statutes at Large">Stat.</a>&nbsp;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://legislink.org/us/stat-108-1169">1169</a>, designated December 7 of each year as National Pearl Harbor Remembrance Day.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The joint resolution was signed by President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> on August 23, 1994. It became <a href="/wiki/Title_36_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 36 of the United States Code">36&nbsp;U.S.C.</a>&nbsp;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/36/129">§&nbsp;129</a> (Patriotic and National Observances and Ceremonies) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Code" title="United States Code">United States Code</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On November 29, Clinton issued a proclamation declaring December 7, 1994, the first National Pearl Harbor Remembrance Day.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On Pearl Harbor Day, the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">American flag</a> should be flown at half-staff until sunset to honor those who died as a result of the attack on U.S. military forces in Hawaii.<sup id="cite_ref-timeanddate.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeanddate.com-4">[4]</a></sup> Pearl Harbor Day is not a federal holiday – government offices, schools, and businesses do not close. Some organizations may hold special events in memory of those killed or injured at Pearl Harbor.<sup id="cite_ref-timeanddate.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeanddate.com-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_attack_on_Pearl_Harbor">The attack on Pearl Harbor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Pearl_Harbor_Remembrance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The attack on Pearl Harbor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">Attack on Pearl Harbor</a>
      </div> 
      <p>On Sunday morning, December 7, 1941, the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy_Air_Service" title="Imperial Japanese Navy Air Service">Imperial Japanese Navy Air Service</a> attacked the <a href="/wiki/Neutral_country" title="Neutral country">neutral</a> United States at <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Pearl_Harbor" title="Naval Station Pearl Harbor">Naval Station Pearl Harbor</a> near <a href="/wiki/Honolulu" title="Honolulu">Honolulu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Territory_of_Hawaii" title="Territory of Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, killing 2,403 Americans and injuring 1,178 others. The attack sank four <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">U.S. Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleships</a> and damaged four others. It also damaged three <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruisers</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a>, and one <a href="/wiki/Minelayer" title="Minelayer">minelayer</a>. Aircraft losses were 188 destroyed and 159 damaged. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Pearl_Harbor_Remembrance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Consequences_of_the_attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Consequences of the attack on Pearl Harbor">Consequences of the attack on Pearl Harbor</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Canada declared war on Japan within hours of the attack on Pearl Harbor,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the first Western nation to do so. On December 8, the United States declared war on Japan and entered World War II on the side of <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">the Allies</a>. In a speech to Congress, President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> called the bombing of Pearl Harbor "<a href="/wiki/Infamy_Speech" title="Infamy Speech">a date which will live in infamy</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-timeanddate.com_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeanddate.com-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pearl_Harbor_and_Ford_Island_historic_sites">Pearl Harbor and Ford Island historic sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Pearl_Harbor_Remembrance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pearl Harbor and Ford Island historic sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Pearl Harbor: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/USS_Arizona_Memorial" title="USS Arizona Memorial">USS <i>Arizona</i> Memorial</a> in Pearl Harbor is a marble memorial over the sunken battleship <a href="/wiki/USS_Arizona_(BB-39)" title="USS Arizona (BB-39)">USS&nbsp;<i>Arizona</i></a>, which was dedicated on May 30, 1962 ("<a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a>"), in honor of the 1,177 crew members who were killed. The memorial remembers all military personnel who were killed in the Pearl Harbor attack. Note: This site is open to the public with boat tours to the memorial provided by the US Navy from the visitors center.</li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/USS_Oklahoma_(BB-37)" title="USS Oklahoma (BB-37)">USS&nbsp;<i>Oklahoma</i></a> Memorial is in remembrance of a battleship that was sunk which lost 429 men on December 7, 1941.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/USS_Utah_(BB-31)" title="USS Utah (BB-31)">USS&nbsp;<i>Utah</i></a> Memorial, is in remembrance of a former battleship that had been converted to a target ship in 1931 (thus, at the time of the Pearl Harbor attack carried the designation of AG-16), that was sunk in the attack on December 7, 1941.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A memorial to honor the crew including the 58 who died aboard USS <i>Utah</i> was dedicated on the northwest shore of <a href="/wiki/Ford_Island" title="Ford Island">Ford Island</a>, near the ship's wreck, in 1972. The ship, along with USS <i>Arizona</i>, was added to the National Register of Historic Places and declared a National Historic Landmark in 1989.</li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/USS_Bowfin_(SS-287)" title="USS Bowfin (SS-287)">USS&nbsp;<i>Bowfin</i></a> Submarine Museum and Park, is in remembrance of an American submarine that sank 44 ships in World War II.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Note: This site is adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/USS_Arizona_Memorial" title="USS Arizona Memorial">USS <i>Arizona</i> Memorial</a> Visitors Center.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Also on Ford Island. Note: Ford Island remains an active military installation therefore public access is restricted to approved tours, military personnel, military retirees, NOAA personnel and their family members only. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/USS_Missouri_(BB-63)" title="USS Missouri (BB-63)">USS&nbsp;<i>Missouri</i></a> Memorial.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The surrender of the Japanese on the deck of USS <i>Missouri</i> brought the end of World War II.</li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Aviation_Museum_Pearl_Harbor" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Aviation Museum Pearl Harbor">Pacific Aviation Museum Pearl Harbor</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pearl_Harbor_tributes">Pearl Harbor tributes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Pearl_Harbor_Remembrance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pearl Harbor tributes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>In 1990, leading up to the 50th anniversary of the attack on Pearl Harbor, Congress established the <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor_Commemorative_Medal" title="Pearl Harbor Commemorative Medal">Pearl Harbor Commemorative Medal</a>. This is also known as the Pearl Harbor Survivor's medal and was awarded to anyone who was in the U.S. Armed Forces and who was present in Hawaii on December 7, 1941, and participated in combat operations that day against the attack. The medal was also awarded to civilians who were killed or injured in the attack. A few years later, Congress amended the law to allow any person who was present in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> on December 7, 1941, and was involved in combat operations against Japanese military forces attacking Hawaii, to receive the award. In both instances, there was a limited time period to apply for the award, and it is no longer issued.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
