      <p><b>All Souls' Day</b>, also known as the <b>Commemoration of All the Faithful Departed</b> and the <b>Day of the Dead</b>, is a day of prayer and remembrance for the <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">souls</a> of those who have died, which is observed by <a href="/wiki/Latin_Church" title="Latin Church">Latin Catholics</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Christian_denominations" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian denominations">Christian denominations</a> annually on <a href="/wiki/2_November" class="mw-redirect" title="2 November">November 2</a>. All Souls' Day is often celebrated in <a href="/wiki/Western_Christianity" title="Western Christianity">Western Christianity</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saturday_of_Souls" title="Saturday of Souls">Saturday of Souls</a> is a related tradition more frequently observed in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Christianity" title="Eastern Christianity">Eastern Christianity</a>. Practitioners of All Souls' Day traditions often remember deceased loved ones in various ways on the day.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrossLivingstone200542_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrossLivingstone200542-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBall200333_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBall200333-3">[3]</a></sup> Beliefs and practices associated with All Souls' Day vary widely among Christian denominations. </p>
      <p>The annual celebration is the last day of <a href="/wiki/Allhallowtide" title="Allhallowtide">Allhallowtide</a>, after <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Day" title="All Saints' Day">All Saints' Day</a> and its eve.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBannatyne199812_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBannatyne199812-4">[4]</a></sup> Prior to the standardization of Catholic observance on 2 November by St. Odlio of Cluny during the 10th century, many Catholic congregations celebrated All Souls Day on various dates during the Easter season as it is still observed in some <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox Churches</a> and associated <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Catholic_Churches" title="Eastern Catholic Churches">Eastern Catholic Churches</a>. Churches of the <a href="/wiki/East_Syriac_Rite" title="East Syriac Rite">East Syriac Rite</a> (<a href="/wiki/Syro-Malabar_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Syro-Malabar Catholic Church">Syro-Malabar Catholic Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chaldean_Catholic_Church" title="Chaldean Catholic Church">Chaldean Catholic Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_Church_of_the_East" title="Assyrian Church of the East">Assyrian Church of the East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Church_of_the_East" title="Ancient Church of the East">Ancient Church of the East</a>) commemorate all the faithful departed on the Friday before <a href="/wiki/Great_Lent" title="Great Lent">Lent</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_customs">Popular customs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Popular customs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Many All Souls' Day traditions are associated with popular notions about <a href="/wiki/Purgatory" title="Purgatory">purgatory</a>. Bell tolling is meant to comfort those being cleansed. Lighting candles serves to kindle a light for the poor souls languishing in the darkness. <a href="/wiki/Soul_cakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Soul cakes">Soul cakes</a> are given to children coming to sing or pray for the dead (cf. <a href="/wiki/Trick-or-treating" title="Trick-or-treating">trick-or-treating</a>), giving rise to the traditions of "going souling" and the baking of special types of bread or cakes (cf. <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A3o-por-Deus" title="Pão-por-Deus">Pão-por-Deus</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchousboe201210-13_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchousboe201210-13-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Tyrol_(state)" title="Tyrol (state)">Tirol</a>, cakes are left for them on the table and the room kept warm for their comfort. In <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>, people flock to the cemeteries at nightfall to kneel, bareheaded, at the graves of their loved ones, and to anoint the hollow of the <a href="/wiki/Tomb_stone" class="mw-redirect" title="Tomb stone">tombstone</a> with <a href="/wiki/Holy_water" title="Holy water">holy water</a> or to pour libations of milk on it. At bedtime, the supper is left on the table for the souls.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, on All Souls' Day (known in <a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a> as <i>Jum il-Mejtin</i>), a traditional supper includes roasted pig, based on a custom of letting a pig loose on the streets with a bell around its neck, to be fed by the entire neighborhood and cooked on that day to feed the poor.<sup id="cite_ref-ToM_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ToM-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Linz" title="Linz">Linz</a>, funereal musical pieces known as <a href="/wiki/Aequale" class="mw-redirect" title="Aequale">aequales</a> were played from tower tops on All Souls' Day and the evening before.<sup id="cite_ref-triton_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triton-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Day#Philippines" title="All Saints' Day">All Saints' Day §&nbsp;Philippines</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In the Philippines, Hallow mas is variously called "<i>Undás</i>", "<i>Todos los Santos</i>" (Spanish, "All Saints"), and sometimes "<i>Araw ng mga Patay / Yumao</i>" (<a href="/wiki/Tagalog_language" title="Tagalog language">Tagalog</a>, "Day of the dead / those who have passed away"), which incorporates All Saints' Day and All Souls' Day. Filipinos traditionally observe this day by visiting the family dead to clean and repair their tombs. Offerings of prayers, flowers, candles,<sup id="cite_ref-guardian-world-saints_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-world-saints-9">[9]</a></sup> and food. <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Filipinos" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese Filipinos">Chinese Filipinos</a> additionally burn <a href="/wiki/Joss_stick" class="mw-redirect" title="Joss stick">incense</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Joss_paper" title="Joss paper">kim</a></i>. Many also spend the day and ensuing night holding reunions at the cemetery with feasts and merriment. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_observance_by_denomination">Religious observance by denomination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religious observance by denomination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span id="Byzantine_.28Greek.29_Catholic_and_the_Eastern_Orthodoxy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_(Greek)_Catholic_and_the_Eastern_Orthodoxy">Byzantine (Greek) Catholic and the Eastern Orthodoxy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Byzantine (Greek) Catholic and the Eastern Orthodoxy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Saturday_of_Souls" title="Saturday of Souls">Saturday of Souls</a> (or Soul Saturday) is a day set aside for the commemoration of the dead within the <a href="/wiki/Liturgical_year" title="Liturgical year">liturgical year</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Orthodox">Eastern Orthodox</a> and Byzantine Catholic Churches. Saturday is a traditional day of prayer for the dead, because Christ lay dead in the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Sepulchre" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Sepulchre">Tomb</a> on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-GOArch_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOArch-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>These days are devoted to prayer for departed relatives and others among the faithful who would not be commemorated specifically as saints. The <a href="/wiki/Divine_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Divine Services">Divine Services</a> on these days have special hymns added to them to commemorate the departed. There is oftena <a href="/wiki/Panikhida" class="mw-redirect" title="Panikhida">Panikhida</a> (Memorial Service) either after the <a href="/wiki/Divine_Liturgy" title="Divine Liturgy">Divine Liturgy</a> on Saturday morning or after <a href="/wiki/Vespers" title="Vespers">Vespers</a> on Friday evening, for which <a href="/wiki/Koliva" title="Koliva">Koliva</a> (a dish made of boiled wheatberries or rice and honey) is prepared and placed on the Panikhida table. After the Service, the priest blesses the Koliva. It is then eaten as a memorial by all present.<sup id="cite_ref-orthodoxpath_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orthodoxpath-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Radonitsa">Radonitsa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Radonitsa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Radonitsa" title="Radonitsa">Radonitsa</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Another Memorial Day in the East, Radonitsa, does not fall on a Saturday, but on either Monday or Tuesday of the second week after Pascha (Easter).<sup id="cite_ref-Averky_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Averky-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bulgakov_Handbook_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bulgakov_Handbook-13">[13]</a></sup> Radonitsa does not have special hymns for the dead at the Divine Services. Instead a Panikhida will follow the Divine Liturgy, and then all will bring paschal foods to the cemeteries to greet the departed with the joy of the Resurrection.<sup id="cite_ref-Averky_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Averky-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Syriac_tradition">East Syriac tradition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: East Syriac tradition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>East Syriac churches including the <a href="/wiki/Syro_Malabar_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Syro Malabar Church">Syro Malabar Church</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chaldean_Catholic_Church" title="Chaldean Catholic Church">Chaldean Catholic Church</a> commemorates the feast of departed faithful on the last Friday of <a href="/wiki/Epiphany_season" title="Epiphany season">Epiphany season</a> (which means Friday just before start of <a href="/wiki/Great_Lent" title="Great Lent">Great Lent</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-nasranifoundation.org_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nasranifoundation.org-14">[14]</a></sup> The season of Epiphany remembers the revelation of Christ to the world. Each Friday of Epiphany season, the church remembers important evangelistic figures.<sup id="cite_ref-syromalabarchurch.in_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-syromalabarchurch.in-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Syro_Malabar_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Syro Malabar Church">Syro Malabar Church</a>, the Friday before the parish festival is also celebrated as feast of departed faithful when the parish remembers the activities of forebearers who worked for the parish and faithful. They also request the intercession of all departed souls for the faithful celebration of parish festival. In East Syriac liturgy, the church remembers departed souls including saints on every Friday throughout the year since the Christ was crucified and died on Friday. </p>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Faithful_(baptized_Catholic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Faithful (baptized Catholic)">"the faithful"</a> refers specifically to baptized Catholics; "all souls" commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Church_penitent" class="mw-redirect" title="Church penitent">church penitent</a> of souls in <a href="/wiki/Purgatory" title="Purgatory">purgatory</a>, whereas "all saints" commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Church_triumphant" class="mw-redirect" title="Church triumphant">church triumphant</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saints</a> in Heaven. In the <a href="/wiki/Liturgical" class="mw-redirect" title="Liturgical">liturgical</a> books of the western <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Latin_Church" title="Latin Church">Latin Church</a>) it is called the Commemoration of All the Faithful Departed (<a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i lang="la">Commemoratio omnium fidelium defunctorum</i>), and is celebrated annually on 2 November. In the ordinary form of the Roman Rite, as well as in the <a href="/wiki/Personal_Ordinariates" class="mw-redirect" title="Personal Ordinariates">Personal Ordinariates</a> established by <a href="/wiki/Benedict_XVI" class="mw-redirect" title="Benedict XVI">Benedict XVI</a> for former Anglicans, it remains on 2 November if this date falls on a Sunday;<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> in the 1962–1969 form of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Rite" title="Roman Rite">Roman Rite</a>, use of which is still authorized, it is transferred to Monday, 3 November.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On this day in particular, Catholics <a href="/wiki/Pray_for_the_dead" class="mw-redirect" title="Pray for the dead">pray for the dead</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBregman201045_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBregman201045-19">[19]</a></sup> Every priest is allowed to celebrate three holy masses on All Souls' Day. The Catholic Church teaches that the purification of the souls in purgatory can be assisted by the actions of the faithful on earth. Its teaching is based also on the practice of prayer for the dead mentioned as far back as 2 Maccabees 12:42–46.<sup id="cite_ref-CCC_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCC-20">[20]</a></sup> In the West there is ample evidence of the custom of praying for the dead in the inscriptions of the <a href="/wiki/Catacomb" class="mw-redirect" title="Catacomb">catacombs</a>, with their constant prayers for the peace of the souls of the departed and in the early liturgies, which commonly contain commemorations of the dead. Tertullian, Cyprian and other early Western Fathers witness to the regular practice of praying for the dead among the early Christians.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrossLivingstone2005459_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrossLivingstone2005459-21">[21]</a></sup> The theological basis for the feast is the doctrine that the souls which, on departing from the body, are not perfectly cleansed from venial sins, or have not fully atoned for past transgressions, are debarred from the Beatific Vision, and that the faithful on earth can help them by prayers, alms deeds and especially by the sacrifice of the Mass.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMershman1907_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMershman1907-22">[22]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>In the sixth century, it was customary in Benedictine monasteries to hold a commemoration of the deceased members at Whitsuntide. In the time of <a href="/wiki/St._Isidore_of_Seville" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Isidore of Seville">St. Isidore of Seville</a> (d. 636) who lived in what is today Spain, the Monday after Pentecost was designated to remember the deceased. At the beginning of the ninth century, Abbot <a href="/wiki/Eigil_of_Fulda" title="Eigil of Fulda">Eigil of Fulda</a> set 17 December as commemoration of all deceased in part of what is today Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMacDonald1967119_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMacDonald1967119-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>According to <a href="/wiki/Widukind_of_Corvey" title="Widukind of Corvey">Widukind of Corvey</a> (c. 975), there also existed a ceremony praying for the dead on 1 October in Saxony.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMershman1907_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMershman1907-22">[22]</a></sup> But it was the day after <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Day" title="All Saints' Day">All Saints' Day</a> that Saint <a href="/wiki/Odilo_of_Cluny" title="Odilo of Cluny">Odilo of Cluny</a> chose when in the 11th century he instituted for all the monasteries dependent on the <a href="/wiki/Abbey_of_Cluny" class="mw-redirect" title="Abbey of Cluny">Abbey of Cluny</a> an annual commemoration of all the faithful departed, to be observed with alms, prayers, and sacrifices for the relief of the suffering souls in purgatory.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEButler199012_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEButler199012-24">[24]</a></sup> Odilo decreed that those requesting a Mass be offered for the departed should make an offering for the poor, thus linking almsgiving with fasting and prayer for the dead. </p>
      <p>The 2 November date and customs spread from the Cluniac monasteries to other Benedictine monasteries and thence to the Western Church in general.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2013_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2013-25">[25]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Liège">Diocese of Liège</a> was the first diocese to adopt the practice under <a href="/wiki/Notker_of_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Notker of Liège">Bishop Notger</a> (d. 1008).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMershman1907_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMershman1907-22">[22]</a></sup> 2 November was adopted in Italy and Rome in the thirteenth century.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMacDonald1967119_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMacDonald1967119-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the 15th century the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominicans</a> instituted a custom of each priest offering three Masses on the Feast of All Souls. During World War I, given the great number of war dead and the many destroyed churches where mass could no longer be said, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XV" title="Pope Benedict XV">Pope Benedict XV</a>, granted all priests the privilege of offering three Masses on All Souls Day,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESaunders2003_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESaunders2003-26">[26]</a></sup> a permission that still stands. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Liturgical_practice">Liturgical practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Liturgical practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>In the Roman Rite as revised in 1969, if 2 November falls on a Sunday, the Mass is of All Souls, but the <a href="/wiki/Liturgy_of_the_Hours" title="Liturgy of the Hours">Liturgy of the Hours</a> is that of the Sunday. However, public celebration of Lauds and Vespers of the Dead with the people participating is permitted. While celebration of a Sunday, a solemnity or a feast of the Lord replacing a Sunday begins on the previous evening with Vespers and perhaps evening Mass, the general norms do not allow for anticipation on Saturday evening of the liturgy of All Souls' Day falling on a Sunday, and so they suggest that the formula of the Mass on that Saturday evening is that of the solemnity of All Saints, which outranks the Sunday of Ordinary Time whose Mass would be celebrated on that evening.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2013_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2013-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> However, in 2014, the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops decided that for that year the Saturday evening (Sunday vigil) Mass in that country was to be that of All Souls; in countries such as Italy the situation was less clear.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2014_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2014-28">[28]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In countries where All Saints' Day is not a <a href="/wiki/Holy_day_of_obligation" title="Holy day of obligation">holy day of obligation</a> attendance at an evening Mass of All Saints on Saturday 1 November satisfies the Sunday obligation.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2013_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2013-25">[25]</a></sup> In England and Wales, where holy days of obligation that fall on a Saturday are transferred to the following day, if 2 November is a Sunday, the solemnity of All Saints is transferred to that date, and All Souls Day is transferred to 3 November.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2014_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcNamara2014-28">[28]</a></sup> In pre-1970 forms of the Roman Rite, still observed by some, if All Souls Day falls on a Sunday, it is always transferred to 3 November. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Divine_Worship:_The_Missal" title="Divine Worship: The Missal">Divine Worship: The Missal</a> the minor propers (Introit, Gradual, Tract, Sequence, Offertory, and Communion) are those used for Renaissance and Classical musical <a href="/wiki/Requiem" title="Requiem">requiem</a> settings, including the <a href="/wiki/Dies_Irae" class="mw-redirect" title="Dies Irae">Dies Irae</a>. This permits the performance of traditional requiem settings in the context of the Divine Worship Form of the Roman Rite on All Souls Day as well as at funerals, votive celebrations of all faithful departed, and anniversaries of deaths.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h4><span id="All_Souls.27_indulgence"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="All_Souls'_indulgence">All Souls' indulgence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: All Souls' indulgence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>According to The Enchiridion of Indulgences, An indulgence, applicable only to the souls in purgatory (commonly called the poor souls), is granted to the faithful, who devoutly visit a cemetery and pray for the departed. The indulgence is plenary, under the usual conditions, each day from the first to the eighth of November; a partial indulgence is granted on any other days of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-catholic.org_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-catholic.org-30">[30]</a></sup> "A plenary indulgence, applicable ONLY to the souls in purgatory, may be obtained by those who, on All Souls Day, piously visit a church, public oratory, or - for those entitled to use it, a semi public oratory. It may be acquired either on the day designated as All Souls Day or, with the consent of the bishop, on the preceding or following Sunday or the feast of All Saints. On visiting the church or oratory it is required that one Our Father and the Creed be recited."<sup id="cite_ref-EnchIndul_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EnchIndul-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-catholic.org_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-catholic.org-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Among continental <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestants</a> its tradition has been more tenaciously maintained. During <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_(religious_leader)" class="mw-redirect" title="Martin Luther (religious leader)">Luther's</a> lifetime, All Souls' Day was widely observed in <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> although the Roman Catholic meaning of the day was discarded;<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-6">[6]</a></sup> ecclesiastically in the <a href="/wiki/Lutheran_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Lutheran Church">Lutheran Church</a>, the day was merged with, and is often seen as an extension of All Saints' Day, with many Lutherans still visiting and decorating graves on all the days of Allhallowtide, including All Souls' Day.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarkussen2013183_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarkussen2013183-32">[32]</a></sup> Just as it is the custom of <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French people</a>, of all ranks and creeds, to decorate the graves of their dead on the <i>jour des morts</i>, so <a href="/wiki/German_people" class="mw-redirect" title="German people">German</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm1911-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Polish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hungarians" title="Hungarians">Hungarian</a> people stream to the graveyards once a year with offerings of flowers and special grave lights. Among <a href="/wiki/Czech_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Czech people">Czech people</a> the custom of visiting and tidying graves of relatives on the day is quite common. In 1816, <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a> introduced a new date for the remembrance of the Dead among its Lutheran citizens: <a href="/wiki/Totensonntag" title="Totensonntag">Totensonntag</a>, the last Sunday before <a href="/wiki/Advent" title="Advent">Advent</a>. This custom was later also adopted by the non-Prussian Lutherans in Germany, but it has not spread much beyond the Protestant areas of Germany. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anglican_Communion">Anglican Communion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Anglican Communion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG/220px-Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG/330px-Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG/440px-Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="2560"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Leichhardt_All_Souls_Anglican_Church.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div><a href="/wiki/All_Souls_Anglican_Church,_Leichhardt" title="All Souls Anglican Church, Leichhardt">All Souls Anglican Church</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Diocese_of_Sydney" title="Anglican Diocese of Sydney">Diocese of Sydney</a>, a parish dedicated to All Souls
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> it is called The Commemoration of the Faithful Departed and is an optional celebration; Anglicans view All Souls' Day as an extension of the observance of All Saints' Day and it serves to "remember those who have died", in connection with the theological doctrines of the <a href="/wiki/Resurrection_of_the_dead" class="mw-redirect" title="Resurrection of the dead">resurrection of the body</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Communion_of_Saints" class="mw-redirect" title="Communion of Saints">Communion of Saints</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBaysHancock2012128_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBaysHancock2012128-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum19997_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum19997-34">[34]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican Communion</a>, All Souls' Day is known liturgically as the Commemoration of All Faithful Departed, and is an optional observance seen as "an extension of All Saints' Day", the latter of which marks the second day of <a href="/wiki/Allhallowtide" title="Allhallowtide">Allhallowtide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum19997_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum19997-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDickison2014_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDickison2014-35">[35]</a></sup> Historically and at present, several Anglican churches are dedicated to All Souls. During the <a href="/wiki/English_Reformation" title="English Reformation">English Reformation</a>, the observance of All Souls' Day lapsed, although a new Anglican theological understanding of the day has "led to a widespread acceptance of this commemoration among Anglicans".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMichno1998160_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMichno1998160-36">[36]</a></sup> Patricia Bays, with regard to the Anglican view of All Souls' Day, wrote that:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBaysHancock2012128_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBaysHancock2012128-33">[33]</a></sup> </p> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>All Souls Day (November 2) is a time when we particularly remember those who have died. The prayers appointed for that day remind us that we are joined with the <a href="/wiki/Communion_of_Saints" class="mw-redirect" title="Communion of Saints">Communion of Saints</a>, that great group of Christians who have finished their earthly life and with who we share the hope of <a href="/wiki/Resurrection_of_the_dead" class="mw-redirect" title="Resurrection of the dead">resurrection from the dead</a>. </p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        — <cite><a href="#CITEREFBaysHancock2012">Bays &amp; Hancock 2012</a>, p.&nbsp;128</cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>As such, Anglican parishes "now commemorate all the faithful departed in the context of the All Saints' Day celebration", in keeping with this fresh perspective.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum19997_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum19997-34">[34]</a></sup> Contributing to the revival was the need "to help Anglicans mourn the deaths of millions of soldiers in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEnglish20044_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEnglish20044-37">[37]</a></sup> Members of the <a href="/wiki/Guild_of_All_Souls" title="Guild of All Souls">Guild of All Souls</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Anglican_devotional_society" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican devotional society">Anglican devotional society</a> founded in 1873, "are encouraged to pray for the dying and the dead, to participate in a requiem of All Souls' Day and say a Litany of the Faithful Departed at least once a month".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum1999232_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmentroutSlocum1999232-38">[38]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>At the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant Reformation">Reformation</a> the celebration of All Souls' Day was fused with <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Day" title="All Saints' Day">All Saints' Day</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a><sup id="cite_ref-Episcopal_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Episcopal-39">[39]</a></sup> or, in the judgement of some, it was "deservedly abrogated".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBCP1850_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBCP1850-40">[40]</a></sup> It was reinstated in certain parishes in connection with the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Movement" title="Oxford Movement">Oxford Movement</a> of the 19th century<sup id="cite_ref-Episcopal_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Episcopal-39">[39]</a></sup> and is acknowledged in United States Anglicanism in the <i>Holy Women, Holy Men</i> calendar<sup id="cite_ref-Episcopal_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Episcopal-39">[39]</a></sup> and in the Church of England with the 1980 <i><a href="/wiki/Alternative_Service_Book" title="Alternative Service Book">Alternative Service Book</a></i>. It features in <i><a href="/wiki/Common_Worship" title="Common Worship">Common Worship</a></i> as a <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Lesser Festival">Lesser Festival</a> called "Commemoration of the Faithful Departed (All Souls' Day)". </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Methodist_Churches">Methodist Churches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Souls%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Methodist Churches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Methodist_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist Church">Methodist Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saints_in_Methodism" title="Saints in Methodism">saints</a> refer to all <a href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christians</a> and therefore, on <a href="/wiki/All_Saint%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="All Saint's Day">All Saint's Day</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Church_Universal" class="mw-redirect" title="Church Universal">Church Universal</a>, as well as the deceased members of a <a href="/wiki/Local_congregation" class="mw-redirect" title="Local congregation">local congregation</a> are honoured and remembered.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHileman2003_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHileman2003-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeck2011_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeck2011-42">[42]</a></sup> In Methodist congregations that celebrate the liturgy on All Souls Day, the observance, as with Anglicanism and Lutheranism, is viewed as an extension of All Saints' Day and as such, Methodists "remember our loved ones who had died" in their observance of this feast.<sup id="cite_ref-sheringhammethodist.org.uk_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sheringhammethodist.org.uk-43">[43]</a></sup> </p>
