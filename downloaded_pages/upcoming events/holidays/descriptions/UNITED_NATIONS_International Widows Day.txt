      <p class="mw-empty-elt"> </p>
      <p><b>International Widows Day</b> is a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> ratified <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#June" title="List of minor secular observances">day of action</a> to address the "poverty and injustice faced by millions of <a href="/wiki/Widow" title="Widow">widows</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Dependant" title="Dependant">dependents</a> in many countries".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The day takes place annually on 23 June. </p>
      <p>International Widows Day was established by <a href="/wiki/The_Loomba_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="The Loomba Foundation">The Loomba Foundation</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (June 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> to <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raise awareness</a> of the issue of widowhood. The significance of 23 June is that it was on that day in 1954 that Shrimati Pushpa Wati Loomba, mother of the foundation's founder, <a href="/wiki/Raj_Loomba,_Baron_Loomba" title="Raj Loomba, Baron Loomba">Lord Loomba</a>, became a widow.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> One of the foundation’s key goals is to highlight what it describes as an invisible calamity. A 2010 book, <i>Invisible, Forgotten Sufferers: The Plight of Widows Around the World</i>, estimates that there are 245 million widows worldwide, 115 million of whom live in poverty and suffer from <a href="/wiki/Social_stigma" title="Social stigma">social stigmatization</a> and economic deprivation purely because they have lost their husbands.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As part of the Loomba Foundation’s awareness campaign, this study was presented to UN Secretary General Ban Ki-moon on 22 June 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The first International Widows Day took place in 2005 and was launched by Lord Loomba and the foundation's president, <a href="/wiki/Cherie_Blair" title="Cherie Blair">Cherie Blair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> By the sixth International Widows Day in 2010, events were held in <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, the United States, the UK, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, India, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Nations_recognition">United Nations recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Widows_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: United Nations recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>On 21 December 2010, the United Nations General Assembly formally adopted 23 June as International Widows Day, endorsing by unanimous acclaim a proposal introduced by President <a href="/wiki/Ali_Bongo_Ondimba" title="Ali Bongo Ondimba">Ali Bongo Ondimba</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As well as formally recognizing 23 June as a day of observance, the accompanying resolution called upon "Member States, the United Nations system and other international and regional organizations to give special attention to the situation of widows and their children."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
