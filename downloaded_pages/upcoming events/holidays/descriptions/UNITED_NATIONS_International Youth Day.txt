      <p><b>International Youth Day</b> (<b>IYD</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#August" title="List of minor secular observances">awareness day</a> designated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>. The purpose of the day is to draw attention to a given set of cultural and legal issues surrounding <a href="/wiki/Youth" title="Youth">youth</a>. The first IYD was observed on <a href="/wiki/12_August" class="mw-redirect" title="12 August">12 August</a>, 2000. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Youth_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>International Youth Day is observed annually on August 12th. It is meant as an opportunity for governments and others to draw attention to youth issues worldwide. During IYD, concerts, workshops, cultural events, and meetings involving national and local government officials and youth organizations take place around the world. </p>
      <p>IYD was designated by the United Nations in 1999 with the adoption of <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.un.org/en/comun/docs/?symbol=A/RES/54/120">Resolution 54/120</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>International Youth Day's Slogan for 2014 was Youth and <a href="/wiki/Mental_health" title="Mental health">Mental Health</a>. For 2015, it was Youth and Civic Engagement. The theme of the 2016 International Youth Day was “The Road to 2030: <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">Eradicating Poverty</a> and Achieving Sustainable Consumption and Production." <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> For 2017, the theme of IYD is "Youth Building Peace". The theme for IYD 2018 was "Safe Spaces for Youth". In this way it will go on which recognises the contributions of young people to <a href="/wiki/Conflict_resolution" title="Conflict resolution">preventing conflict</a>, supporting inclusion, <a href="/wiki/Social_justice" title="Social justice">social justice</a>, and sustain peace.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> For 2019, the theme of IYD is "Transforming education" to make education <a href="/wiki/Inclusion_(education)" title="Inclusion (education)">inclusive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Accessibility" title="Accessibility">accessible</a> for all youth. And for 2020, the theme of IYD is "Youth Engagement for Global Action". </p>

      <p>Each youth day is associated with a number of events around the world. These included, in 2013: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>International Youth Conference 2013: held on August 10-August 11, 2013 before the International Youth Day on August 12. This conference comprised speakers from various countries and fields of work, followed by youth awards ceremony, and spirit kindling <a href="/wiki/Debate" title="Debate">debates</a> and discussions. It was hosted by, YOUTHINK, Youth Exnora and the US consulate general, Chennai.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>IYC'13 hosted by: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>YOUTHINK</li> 
       <li>Youth Exnora</li> 
       <li>US consulate general, Chennai</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>IYD '18 and IYD '19 hosted by Indian Youth Cafe, Chennai </p>
      <p>The event has its own specialty in empowering the youth power. </p>
