      <p><b>World Hepatitis Day</b>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#July" title="List of minor secular observances">observed</a> on July 28 every year, aims to <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raise global awareness</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis" title="Hepatitis">hepatitis</a> — a group of infectious diseases known as Hepatitis <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_A" title="Hepatitis A">A</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_B" title="Hepatitis B">B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_C" title="Hepatitis C">C</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_D" title="Hepatitis D">D</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_E" title="Hepatitis E">E</a> — and encourage prevention, diagnosis and treatment. Hepatitis affects hundreds of millions of people worldwide, causing acute and chronic disease and killing close to 1.34 million people every year.<sup id="cite_ref-WHOcampaign_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WHOcampaign-1">[1]</a></sup>Hepatitis causes liver diseases and can also kill a person.In some countries hepatitis B is commonest cause of cirrhosis and may also cause liver cancer(HCC) </p>
      <p>World Hepatitis Day is one of 11 official <a href="/wiki/Global_health" title="Global health">global public health</a> campaigns marked by the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (<a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">WHO</a>), along with <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Day" title="World Health Day">World Health Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Chagas_Disease_Day" title="World Chagas Disease Day">World Chagas Disease Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Blood_Donor_Day" title="World Blood Donor Day">World Blood Donor Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Malaria_Day" title="World Malaria Day">World Malaria Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Immunization_Week" title="World Immunization Week">World Immunization Week</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Tuberculosis_Day" title="World Tuberculosis Day">World Tuberculosis Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_No_Tobacco_Day" title="World No Tobacco Day">World No Tobacco Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Patient_Safety_Day" title="World Patient Safety Day">World Patient Safety Day</a>, World Antimicrobial Awareness Week and <a href="/wiki/World_AIDS_Day" title="World AIDS Day">World AIDS Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Hepatitis_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Hepatitis groups, patients and advocates worldwide take part in events on 28 July. Notably in 2012, a Guinness World Record was created when 12,588 people from 20 countries did the <a href="/wiki/Three_Wise_Monkeys" class="mw-redirect" title="Three Wise Monkeys">Three Wise Monkeys</a> actions on World Hepatitis Day to signify the willful ignorance of the disease.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Hepatitis_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The inaugural International Hepatitis C Awareness day, coordinated by various European and Middle Eastern Patient Groups and Baby Muriel, took place on October 1, 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> However, many patient groups continued to mark 'hepatitis day' on disparate dates.<sup id="cite_ref-url_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-url-4">[4]</a></sup> For this reason in 2008, the World Hepatitis Alliance in collaboration with patient groups declared May 19 the first global World Hepatitis Day. </p>
      <p>Following the adoption of a resolution during the 63rd <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Assembly" title="World Health Assembly">World Health Assembly</a> in May 2010, World Hepatitis Day was given global endorsement as the primary focus for national and international awareness-raising efforts and the date was changed to July 28 (in honour of Nobel Laureate <a href="/wiki/Baruch_Samuel_Blumberg" title="Baruch Samuel Blumberg">Baruch Samuel Blumberg</a>, discoverer of the hepatitis B virus, who celebrates his birthday on that date). The resolution resolves that "28 July shall be designated as World Hepatitis Day in order to provide an opportunity for education and greater understanding of <a href="/wiki/Viral_hepatitis" title="Viral hepatitis">viral hepatitis</a> as a global public health problem, and to stimulate the strengthening of preventive and control measures of this disease in Member States." </p>
      <p>World Hepatitis Day is now recognised in over 100 countries each year through events such as free screenings, poster campaigns, demonstrations, concerts, talk shows, flash mobs and vaccination drives, amongst many others.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Each year a report is published by the WHO and the World Hepatitis Alliance detailing all the events across the world. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Hepatitis_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>World Hepatitis Day provides an opportunity to focus on actions such as:<sup id="cite_ref-WHOcampaign_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WHOcampaign-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Raising awareness of the different forms of hepatitis and how they are transmitted;</li> 
       <li>Strengthening prevention, screening and control of viral hepatitis and its related diseases;</li> 
       <li>Increasing hepatitis B vaccine coverage and integration into national <a href="/wiki/Immunization" title="Immunization">immunization</a> programmes; and</li> 
       <li>Coordinating a global response to hepatitis.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Each year focuses on a specific theme. The list of themes is as follows: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>2011</b>: Hepatitis affects everyone, everywhere. Know it. Confront it. Confront her.</li> 
       <li><b>2012</b>: It's closer than you think.</li> 
       <li><b>2013</b>: More must be done to stop this silent killer.</li> 
       <li><b>2014</b>: Hepatitis: Think Again</li> 
       <li><b>2015</b>: Prevention of viral Hepatitis. Act now.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><b>2016</b>: Know Hepatitis-Act now.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><b>2017</b>: Eliminate Hepatities.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><b>2018</b>: Test. Treat. Hepatitis.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><b>2019</b>: Invest in eliminating hepatitis.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><b>2020</b>: Hepatitis Free Future</li>
      </ul>
