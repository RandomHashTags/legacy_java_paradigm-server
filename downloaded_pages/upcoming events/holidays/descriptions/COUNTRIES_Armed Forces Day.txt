      <p>Many nations around the world observe some kind of <b>Armed Forces Day</b> to honor their <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> forces. This day is not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Veterans_Day" title="Veterans Day">Veterans Day</a> or <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Egypt">Egypt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Egypt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Egypt, <b>Armed Forces Day</b> is celebrated on 6 October, the date on which the <a href="/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War" title="Yom Kippur War">October War</a> of 1973 began with the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_Army" title="Egyptian Army">Egyptian Army</a>'s successful crossing of the <a href="/wiki/Suez_Canal" title="Suez Canal">Suez Canal</a> that culminated in the capture of the <a href="/wiki/Bar_Lev_Line" title="Bar Lev Line">Bar Lev Line</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BAFD_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BAFD-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liberia">Liberia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Liberia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Liberia" title="Public holidays in Liberia">Armed Forces Day</a> is celebrated on 11 February.<sup id="cite_ref-liberia_holidays_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-liberia_holidays-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mali">Mali</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mali">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b>Mali Army Day</b> is celebrated on 20 January.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mauritania">Mauritania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mauritania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Mauritanian_Armed_Forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Mauritanian Armed Forces">Mauritanian Armed Forces</a> Day</b> is celebrated on 10 July. Armed forces day is marked with events of national importance, such as flag raising ceremonies, awards ceremonies and military parades. It was established in 1960 by President <a href="/wiki/Moktar_Ould_Daddah" title="Moktar Ould Daddah">Moktar Ould Daddah</a> as a professional holiday for the armed forces, replacing the holidays of the separate branches of the armed forces, as well as resembling the <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Day" title="Remembrance Day">Remembrance Day</a> celebrations in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It is not a public holiday in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria">Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nigeria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Nigeria, <b>Armed Forces Day</b>, also known as <b><a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Remembrance_Day" title="Armed Forces Remembrance Day">Remembrance Day</a></b>, is celebrated on 15 January. It was formerly celebrated on 11 November of every year to coincide with the Remembrance Day (<a href="/wiki/Poppy_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Poppy Day">Poppy Day</a>) for the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> veterans in the British <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. But it was changed to 15 January in Nigeria in commemoration of the surrender of <a href="/wiki/Biafran" class="mw-redirect" title="Biafran">Biafran</a> troops to the Federal troops on 15 January 1970, thus concluding the <a href="/wiki/Nigerian_Civil_War" title="Nigerian Civil War">Nigerian Civil War</a> that sought to tear apart the unity of Nigeria.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b>Armed Forces Day</b> for the <a href="/wiki/South_African_National_Defence_Force" title="South African National Defence Force">South African National Defence Force</a> is celebrated on 21 February, the day of the 1993 reconstitution of the <a href="/wiki/South_African_Defence_Force" title="South African Defence Force">South African Defence Force</a> into its current identity. It has been celebrated with parades nationwide since 2012. Since 2017, the Armed Forces Day event also honors the fallen of the tragic 1917 sinking of <a href="/wiki/SS_Mendi" title="SS Mendi">SS Mendi</a>, which carried South African forces into the frontlines during the First World War, resulting in one of the biggest military losses ever in South African military history. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Canada, <b><a href="/wiki/Canadian_Armed_Forces" title="Canadian Armed Forces">Canadian Armed Forces</a> Day (CAFD)</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Journée des forces armées canadiennes</i>) is celebrated on the first Sunday in June and is a celebration of Canada's armed services, their heritage, and their personnel.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It was established as a result of passing of a motion in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_Canada" title="House of Commons of Canada">House of Commons</a> on 25 April 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Canadian Armed Forces Day is not a public holiday in Canada. Official congratulations are given by the <a href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief_of_the_Canadian_Forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Commander-in-Chief of the Canadian Forces">Commander-in-Chief of the Canadian Forces</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Canada" title="Prime Minister of Canada">Prime Minister of Canada</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_National_Defence_(Canada)" title="Minister of National Defence (Canada)">Minister of National Defence</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_the_Defence_Staff_(Canada)" title="Chief of the Defence Staff (Canada)">Chief of the Defence Staff</a>. An inspection of an inter-service <a href="/wiki/Guard_of_honour" title="Guard of honour">guard of honour</a> is commonplace during the holiday. Tributes to CF personnel usually happen at the <a href="/wiki/National_War_Memorial_(Canada)" title="National War Memorial (Canada)">National War Memorial</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Tomb_of_the_Unknown_Soldier" title="Canadian Tomb of the Unknown Soldier">Canadian Tomb of the Unknown Soldier</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">Ottawa</a>. The event is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Day" title="Remembrance Day">Remembrance Day</a> celebrations in the fall. In 2018, <a href="/wiki/CFB_Borden" title="CFB Borden">CFB Borden</a> organised an air show for the holiday<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chile">Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_Patrias_(Chile)" title="Fiestas Patrias (Chile)">Fiestas Patrias (Chile)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In Chile, Army Day (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Día de las Glorias del Ejército</i>) is a national holiday celebrated every 19 September, a day after the independence day, with the Gran Parada Militar, a <a href="/wiki/Military_parade" title="Military parade">military parade</a> where all the branches of the armed forces display some of their troops and equipment in a special part of <a href="/wiki/O%27Higgins_Park" title="O'Higgins Park">O'Higgins Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santiago,_Chile" class="mw-redirect" title="Santiago, Chile">Santiago</a>. Several other smaller parades can be seen on other cities of the country, as well as air displays by the air force, on Independence Day, 18 September. </p>
      <p>For the Chilean Navy, its counterpart is the national <a href="/wiki/Navy_Day_(Chile)" title="Navy Day (Chile)">Navy Day</a> (Spanish: <i lang="es">Día de las Glorias de la Armada</i>) celebrations on 21 May, in honor of the double anniversaries of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Iquique" title="Battle of Iquique">Battle of Iquique</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Punta_Gruesa" title="Battle of Punta Gruesa">Battle of Punta Gruesa</a> in 1879. <a href="/wiki/Valparaiso" class="mw-redirect" title="Valparaiso">Valparaiso</a> is where the main celebrations are concentrated, with a military parade in the morning and the President of Chile's State of the Nation address in the afternoon. Similar parades are hosted in major and minor cities and towns nationwide. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Day_of_the_Cuban_Armed_Forces" title="Day of the Cuban Armed Forces">Day of the Cuban Armed Forces</a> is celebrated on 2 December to commemorate the landing of the <a href="/wiki/Granma_(yacht)" title="Granma (yacht)">Granma</a> in 1956.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The first ever <a href="/wiki/Military_parade" title="Military parade">military parade</a> in years to be held on the holiday was marked in 2006. The parade on 2 January 2017 (postponed for 1 month due to the <a href="/wiki/Death_and_state_funeral_of_Fidel_Castro" title="Death and state funeral of Fidel Castro">Death and state funeral of Fidel Castro</a>) was the second and the final event marking the diamond jubilee since the events of 1956. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominican_Republic">Dominican Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Dominican Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Armed forces day (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Día de las Fuerzas Armadas</i>) is celebrated on 25 February to commemorate the anniversary of the armed forces of the Dominican Republic. It is also the day of birth of Matías Ramón Mella, who is regarded as a national hero in the Dominican Republic and fired the first shot with his blunderbuss to proclaim, along with other patriots, the Independence from Haiti on 27 February 1844. On 27 February a military parade is held to commemorate the Independence anniversary. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guatemala">Guatemala</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Guatemala">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Guatemala, <b>Día del Ejército</b> is celebrated on 30 June. It is remembered because in 1871 the Liberal Revolution or "Revolución Liberal", led by <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Garc%C3%ADa_Granados" title="Miguel García Granados">Miguel García Granados</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justo_Rufino_Barrios" title="Justo Rufino Barrios">Justo Rufino Barrios</a>, and composed of personnel of the <a href="/wiki/Guatemalan_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Guatemalan Army">Guatemalan Army</a>, marched on the streets of Guatemala City and captured the Presidential palace, putting an end to the <i>Conservadora</i> administration formerly headed by <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Carrera" title="Rafael Carrera">Rafael Carrera</a>, marking the start of a new era in Guatemalan history. Parades are held in honor of the holiday in Guatemala City and in other major towns nationwide. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Haiti">Haiti</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Haiti">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Haiti, <b><i lang="fr" title="French-language text">Le Jour de La Battaille Des Vertieres</i></b> is celebrated on 18 November in celebration of the victory of the indigenous slave African and <a href="/wiki/Gens_de_couleur" class="mw-redirect" title="Gens de couleur">Gens de couleur</a> versus the European forces led by France with support from United States and Spain. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Mexican_Armed_Forces" title="Mexican Armed Forces">Mexican Armed Forces</a> Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: Día de las Fuerzas Armadas Mexicanas) is celebrated on 19 February since 1950, in commemoration of the decree that created them in 1917. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peru">Peru</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Peru">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Peru, the <b>Día de las Fuerzas Armadas del Perú</b> (English: <i>Peruvian Armed Forces Day</i>) is celebrated on 24 September, the feast of the Virgin of Mercy, patroness of the Armed Forces. The day for the <a href="/wiki/Peruvian_Army" title="Peruvian Army">Peruvian Army</a> itself is 9 December, commemorating Peru's victory in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ayacucho" title="Battle of Ayacucho">Battle of Ayacucho</a>, which ended the <a href="/wiki/Peruvian_War_of_Independence" title="Peruvian War of Independence">Peruvian War of Independence</a>, while for the <a href="/wiki/Peruvian_Navy" title="Peruvian Navy">Peruvian Navy</a>, Navy Day is on 8 October, the double anniversary of the 1821 foundation of the Navy and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Angamos" title="Battle of Angamos">Battle of Angamos</a> in 1879. </p>
      <p>Air Force Day is held on 23 July, the anniversary of the death of <a href="/wiki/Peruvian_Air_Force" title="Peruvian Air Force">Peruvian Air Force</a> Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Qui%C3%B1ones_Gonzales" title="José Quiñones Gonzales">José Quiñones Gonzales</a> during the 1941 <a href="/wiki/Ecuadorian%E2%80%93Peruvian_War" title="Ecuadorian–Peruvian War">Ecuadorian–Peruvian War</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Great_Military_Parade_of_Peru" title="Great Military Parade of Peru">Great Military Parade of Peru</a> is held on the day after Independence Day, 29 July, and this is where all 3 services of the Armed Forces are also honored. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Venezuela">Venezuela</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Venezuela">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Venezuela celebrates <b>Army Day</b> on 24 June, the anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Simón Bolívar">Simón Bolívar</a>'s victory in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Carabobo" title="Battle of Carabobo">Battle of Carabobo</a>, which led to Venezuela's independence from Spain. </p>
      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Navy_Day" title="Navy Day">Navy Day</a></b>, honoring the 1823 <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lake_Maracaibo" title="Battle of Lake Maracaibo">Battle of Lake Maracaibo</a>, is celebrated on the same day as the birthday of Simón Bolívar, 24 July.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>The Venezuelan Air Force marks <b>Air Force Day</b> on 27 November every year, honoring the role of Venezuelan military aviation in national history (the date, used since 2010, is in remembrance of the 2nd of the <a href="/wiki/1992_Venezuelan_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempts" title="1992 Venezuelan coup d'état attempts">1992 Venezuelan coup d'état attempts</a> in which the Air Force took part). From 1946 till 2009, 10 December was celebrated as Air Force Day in honor of the birth of national military aviation with the 1920 opening of the Air Force Academy in Maracay.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p><b>National Guard Day</b> is celebrated on 3 August, the date of the 1936 founding of the Venezuelan National Guard. The entire Venezuelan National Armed Forces are also honored on Independence Day, 5 July, which is also earmarked as <b>National Armed Forces Day</b>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the United States, <b>Armed Forces Day</b> is celebrated on the third Saturday in May. It falls near the end of <b>Armed Forces Week</b>, which begins on the second Saturday of May and ends on the third Sunday of May (the fourth if the month begins on a Sunday, as in 2016).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>First observed on 20 May 1950, the day was created on 31 August 1949, to honor Americans serving in the five U.S. military branches – the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">U.S. Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">U.S. Navy</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marines" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Marines">U.S. Marine Corps</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">U.S. Air Force</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a> – following the consolidation of the military services in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Defense" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Department of Defense">U.S. Department of Defense</a>. It was intended to replace the separate Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps and Coast Guard Days, but the separate days are still observed, especially within the respective services.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The first Armed Forces Day was celebrated by <a href="/wiki/Parade" title="Parade">parades</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doors_Open_Days" title="Doors Open Days">open houses</a>, receptions and <a href="/wiki/Air_show" title="Air show">air shows</a>. The United States' <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Day_Parade_(Bremerton)" title="Armed Forces Day Parade (Bremerton)">longest continuously running Armed Forces Day Parade is held in Bremerton, Washington</a>. In 2019 Bremerton will celebrate the 71st year of the Armed Forces Day Parade. </p>
      <p>Because of their unique training schedules, <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="National Guard of the United States">National Guard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reserve_components_of_the_United_States_armed_forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Reserve components of the United States armed forces">Reserve units</a> may celebrate Armed Forces Day/Week over any period in the month of May. </p>
      <p>On 19 May 2017, President <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> reaffirmed the Armed Forces Day holiday, marking the 70th anniversary since the creation of the Department of Defense.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Aside from the Armed Forces Day the Armed Forces and the National Guard Bureau are honored on the following days: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>29 March: Vietnam Veterans Day (All US Military Branches)<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>Last Monday of May: <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a></li> 
       <li>14 June: Flag Day and Army Day (United States Army)</li> 
       <li>4 August: Coast Guard Day (United States Coast Guard)</li> 
       <li>18 September: Air Force Day (United States Air Force)</li> 
       <li>13 October: US Navy Birthday (United States Navy)</li> 
       <li>27 October: Navy Day (United States Navy)</li> 
       <li>10 November: Marine Corps Birthday (United States Marine Corps)</li> 
       <li>11 November: <a href="/wiki/Veterans_Day" title="Veterans Day">Veterans Day</a></li> 
       <li>13 December: National Guard Day (<a href="/wiki/National_Guard_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="National Guard of the United States">National Guard of the United States</a>)</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Azerbaijan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Day_of_the_Armed_Forces_of_Azerbaijan" title="Day of the Armed Forces of Azerbaijan">Day of the Armed Forces of Azerbaijan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>: <i lang="az">Silahlı Qüvvələr Günü</i>) is celebrated on 26 June. The events are centered around a <a href="/wiki/Military_parade" title="Military parade">military parade</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a>, the national capital. The annual parade is one of the biggest in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Armenia">Armenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Armenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Army_Day_(Armenia)" title="Army Day (Armenia)">Army Day (Armenia)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Army Day (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy">Բանակի օր</span>) is celebrated on 28 January to commemorate the formation of the armed forces of the newly independent Armenia in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Bangladesh observes Armed Forces Day on 21 November to mark the occasion of the Tri-Services joint operation against occupying Pakistani forces in the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War" title="Bangladesh Liberation War">Liberation War</a>, 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-bss_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bss-24">[24]</a></sup> The day starts with laying of a floral wreath at '<b>Sikha Anirban'</b> (Eternal Flame) at <a href="/wiki/Dhaka_Cantonment" title="Dhaka Cantonment">Dhaka Cantonment</a> by the President, the Prime Minister and the service chiefs. In the afternoon a reception is held at <b>Senakunja</b>, Dhaka Cantonment where the Prime Minister, ministers, the leader of the opposition and other high civil and military officials attend. In other <a href="/wiki/Cantonment" title="Cantonment">cantonments</a>, naval bases, and air bases, similar receptions are held. A special TV programme <i>Anirban</i> is broadcast on different TV channels the previous evening, and special newspaper supplements are published with national <a href="/wiki/Daily_newspaper" class="mw-redirect" title="Daily newspaper">dailies</a>. Receptions are also held by the Prime Minister and the service chiefs for recipients of the gallantry award Freedom Fighter Award. Special meals for family members are served in all military stations. The Armed Forces Division also brings out a special publication with articles related to the War of Independence and the armed forces. </p> 
      <h3><span id="China_.28People.27s_Republic_of_China.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="China_(People's_Republic_of_China)">China (People's Republic of China)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: China (People's Republic of China)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/PLA_Day" title="PLA Day">People's Liberation Army Day</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh"><b>中国人民解放军建军纪念日</b></span>) is celebrated in the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> on 1 August in commemoration of the founding of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Nanchang_Uprising" class="mw-redirect" title="Nanchang Uprising">Nanchang Uprising</a> of 1927. On 30 June 1933, the Central Committee for Military Revolutionary Cases of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> (CPC) voted to declare 1 August an annual holiday. This was solidified on 11 July of that same year, when this decision was approved by the government of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Soviet_Republic" title="Chinese Soviet Republic">Chinese Soviet Republic</a>. Since then, the date has been celebrated as the professional holiday and birthday of the PLA. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Georgia">Georgia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Georgia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Georgia marks its Armed Forces Day (<a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>: <span lang="ka" title="Georgian-language text">შეიარაღებული ძალების დღე</span>) on 30 April to commemorate the foundation of the <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Armed_Forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgian Armed Forces">Georgian Armed Forces</a> in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In India, <a href="/wiki/Army_Day_(India)" title="Army Day (India)">Army Day</a> is celebrated on 15 January, <a href="/wiki/Navy_Day_(India)" title="Navy Day (India)">Navy Day</a> is celebrated on 4 December and Air Force Day is celebrated on 8 October every year and 7 December is celebrated as <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Flag_Day" title="Armed Forces Flag Day">Armed Forces Flag Day</a>. This <i>Armed Forces Flag Day</i> is a day dedicated to the collection of funds from people of India for the welfare of the <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Armed Forces of India">Indian Armed Forces personnel</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia">Indonesia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Indonesia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_National_Armed_Forces_Day" title="Indonesian National Armed Forces Day">Indonesian National Armed Forces Day</a>
      </div> 
      <p><b>Hari Tentara Nasional Indonesia</b> (English: <i>Indonesian National Armed Forces Day</i>) abbreviated HUT TNI is celebrated on the 5 October, the day of the foundation of the <b>Tentara Keamanan Rakyat</b> (<i>People's Security Armed Forces</i>), the predecessor of the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_National_Armed_Forces" title="Indonesian National Armed Forces">TNI</a>, in 1945, itself a replacement for the <i>Badan Keamanan Rakyat</i> (<i>People's Security Corps</i>) established on 29 August on the same year. Military parades are held nationwide in major cities and provincial capitals in honor of the TNI's serving men and women and military veterans. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Iran">Iran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Iran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Islamic_Republic_of_Iran_Army_Day" title="Islamic Republic of Iran Army Day">Rouz-e Artesh</a></b> (English: <i>Army Day</i>) is celebrated on 18 April. Exhibitions of the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Iranian Army">Iranian Army</a>, such as a military parade of active personnel and veterans in the presence of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Iran" title="President of Iran">President of Iran</a> in front of the <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum_of_Ruhollah_Khomeini" title="Mausoleum of Ruhollah Khomeini">Mausoleum of Ruhollah Khomeini</a>, takes place during the holiday events. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Iraq">Iraq</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Iraq">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b>Iraqi Army Day</b> is celebrated on 6 January, and marks the anniversary of the activation of the <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_Army" title="Iraqi Army">Iraqi Army</a> on 6 January 1921.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Soldiers typically hold military parades in the <a href="/wiki/Green_Zone" title="Green Zone">Green Zone</a> of <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a> to mark the holiday, although it is not celebrated in the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Region" title="Kurdistan Region">Kurdistan Region</a>, due to many Kurds accusing the Iraqi Army of genocide.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The 2021 Army Day celebrations honored the Army's 100th anniversary.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Israel">Israel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Israel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Yom_Hazikaron" class="mw-redirect" title="Yom Hazikaron">Yom Hazikaron</a></b> (English: <i>Memorial Day</i>) is observed on the 4th day of the month of <a href="/wiki/Iyar" title="Iyar">Iyar</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_calendar" title="Hebrew calendar">Hebrew calendar</a> or May in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_Calendar" class="mw-redirect" title="Gregorian Calendar">Gregorian Calendar</a>, always preceding the next day's celebrations of Israel <a href="/wiki/Yom_Ha%27atzma%27ut" class="mw-redirect" title="Yom Ha'atzma'ut">Independence Day</a>, Yom Ha-Atzma'ut, on the 5th day of Iyar, the anniversary of the Proclamation of the State of Israel in 1948. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Japan, following the end of World War II, the <b><a href="/wiki/Japan_Self-Defense_Forces" title="Japan Self-Defense Forces">Self-Defense Forces</a> Day</b> (Japanese: 自衛隊記念日; <a href="/wiki/Romaji" class="mw-redirect" title="Romaji">Romaji</a>: <i>Jiei-tai Kinen'bi</i>) is held every year since 1966.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> It celebrates the foundation of the Japan Self-Defense Forces. The <a href="/wiki/Ground_Self-Defense_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Ground Self-Defense Force">GSDF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Self-Defense_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritime Self-Defense Force">MSDF</a> and <a href="/wiki/Air_Self-Defense_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Self-Defense Force">ASDF</a> hold annual reviews in rotation (the GSDF a full military parade, the ASDF an airshow and the MSDF a fleet review) set in a designated day in October.<sup id="cite_ref-SDF-Day-2018_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SDF-Day-2018-30">[30]</a></sup> There is also a three-day music event called the <a href="/wiki/JSDF_Marching_Festival" title="JSDF Marching Festival">SDF Marching Festival</a>. The date varies per year.<sup id="cite_ref-SDF-Marching-2014_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SDF-Marching-2014-31">[31]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Empire_of_Japan">Empire of Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Empire of Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a>, <b>Army Commemoration Day</b> (Japanese: 陸軍記念日; Romaji: <i>Riku-gun Kinen'bi</i>) was celebrated every 10 March, in commemoration of the Japanese victory in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mukden" title="Battle of Mukden">Battle of Mukden</a>. Similarly, <b><a href="/wiki/Navy_Day#Japan" title="Navy Day">Navy Commemoration Day</a></b> (Japanese: 海軍記念日; Romaji: <i>Kai-gun Kinen'bi</i>) was celebrated every 24 May in commemoration of the Japanese victory in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Tsushima" title="Battle of Tsushima">Battle of Tsushima</a>. These days were celebrated from 1906 until the end of World War II in 1945. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Kazakhstan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>The <b>Defender of the Fatherland Day</b> of Kazakhstan is observed on 7 May to mark the founding of the <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_Kazakhstan" class="mw-redirect" title="Armed Forces of Kazakhstan">Armed Forces of Kazakhstan</a> on 7 May 1992 after the fall of the USSR. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Laos">Laos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Laos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <b>Anniversary of The Lao Army</b> was observed annually every 20 January by the <a href="/wiki/Lao_People%27s_Armed_Forces" title="Lao People's Armed Forces">Lao People's Armed Forces</a> to celebrate the creation of the independent Lao army on 20 January 1949. Every year, large posters are placed to remind that date which was marks the first step towards full independence and domination of the <a href="/wiki/Lao_People%27s_Revolutionary_Party" title="Lao People's Revolutionary Party">Lao Communist Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> A military parade is held in the early morning in the capital of <a href="/wiki/Vientiane" title="Vientiane">Vientiane</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Laos" title="Kingdom of Laos">Kingdom of Laos</a>, 1 July was the anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Lao_Army" title="Royal Lao Army">Royal Lao Army</a>. </p>

      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Men%27s_and_Soldiers%27_Day" title="Men's and Soldiers' Day">Soldier's Day</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a>: <span lang="mn">Цэргийн баяр</span>) is celebrated on 18 March annually. On 18 March 1921, Sükhbaatar's troops succeeded in taking the town, despite being heavily outnumbered. This day is now the official holiday of Mongolia's army, and is usually celebrated as the equivalent of Defender of the Fatherland Day in Russia, or the male version of International Women's Day.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Myanmar">Myanmar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: Myanmar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Day_(Myanmar)" title="Armed Forces Day (Myanmar)">Armed Forces Day (Myanmar)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Day_(Myanmar)" title="Armed Forces Day (Myanmar)">Armed Forces Day</a></b> (<span lang="my" title="Burmese-language text">တပ်မတော်နေ့</span>) is celebrated on 27 March in commemoration of the start of <a href="/wiki/Burmese_army" class="mw-redirect" title="Burmese army">Burmese army</a>'s resistance to Japanese occupation in 1945. Originally, it was known as Resistance Day (<span lang="my" title="Burmese-language text">တော်လှန်ရေးနေ့</span>). </p>
      <p>Notably, the Myanmar Armed Forces (Tatmadaw) killed at least 114 unarmed civilians, including at least six children, on the 76th Armed Forces Day celebrated on 27th March 2021. <sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_Korea">North Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: North Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Military_Foundation_Day" title="Military Foundation Day">Military Foundation Day</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Military_Foundation_Day" title="Military Foundation Day">Military Foundation Day</a> is celebrated on 8 February, in commemoration of the day of the creation of the modern <a href="/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Army" title="Korean People's Army">Korean People's Army</a> (KPA)<sup id="cite_ref-SeolSongAh2015_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SeolSongAh2015-36">[36]</a></sup> in 1948. On that holiday North Korea holds a national commemorative assembly in Pyongyang and various commemorative events, firepower demonstrations, concerts and the biannual military parade. Since 23 April 1996 when the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_People%27s_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Central People's Committee (page does not exist)">Central People's Committee</a> issued an ordinance making it a national holiday, both the entire military and civilians are permitted to take off from work to celebrate all those serving in the KPA and its veterans. </p>
      <p>Additionally, since 2015, the KPA and its veterans are honored on <i>People's Revolutionary Army Day</i>, held every 25 April, to commemorate the official formation of the predecessor forces of the Army, the Revolutionary Army, in the spring of 1932. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistan">Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Pakistan, the Federal <a href="/wiki/Pakistani_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistani Army">Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistani_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistani Navy">Navy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pakistani_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistani Air Force">Air Force</a> celebrate <a href="/wiki/Defence_Day" title="Defence Day">Defence Day</a> (6 September), the <a href="/wiki/Navy_Day#Pakistan" title="Navy Day">Navy Day</a>/<a href="/wiki/Victory_Day#8_September_in_Pakistan" title="Victory Day">Victory Day</a> (8 September) and the <a href="/wiki/Air_Force_Day_(Pakistan)" title="Air Force Day (Pakistan)">Air Force Day</a> (7 September) respectively. Usually the ceremony takes place on the <i><b>Resolution</b></i> or <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Day" title="Pakistan Day">Pakistan Day</a> (23 March) when all three services display their full colours and guards of honour, as well as on Independence Day (14 August). </p>

      <h3><span id="Taiwan_.2F_Republic_of_China_.28ROC.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Taiwan_/_Republic_of_China_(ROC)">Taiwan / Republic of China (ROC)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=46" title="Edit section: Taiwan / Republic of China (ROC)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <b>Armed Forces Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh"><b>軍人節</b></span>) is celebrated in the Republic of China on the western <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> (formerly called <a href="/wiki/Formosa" class="mw-redirect" title="Formosa">Formosa</a> when under <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a> occupation), on 3 September, on the same day as their <a href="/wiki/Victory_over_Japan_Day" title="Victory over Japan Day">Victory over Japan Day</a> (1945). Pursuant to Article 5 of the <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/zh:%E7%B4%80%E5%BF%B5%E6%97%A5%E5%8F%8A%E7%AF%80%E6%97%A5%E5%AF%A6%E6%96%BD%E8%BE%A6%E6%B3%95" class="extiw" title="s:zh:紀念日及節日實施辦法"><i>Order to Implement Commemoration Days and Holidays</i> (紀念日及節日實施辦法)</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Defense_(Republic_of_China)" title="Ministry of National Defense (Republic of China)">Ministry of National Defense</a> (國防部) determines how to allow a day off for the <a href="/wiki/Military_of_the_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Military of the Republic of China">military personnel</a>. This is not a <a href="/wiki/Holidays_in_the_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Holidays in the Republic of China">public holiday in the Republic of China</a> on Taiwan, but relevant institutions, groups, and schools may hold celebrating activities.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thailand">Thailand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=48" title="Edit section: Thailand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Thailand honours the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_Armed_Forces" title="Royal Thai Armed Forces">Royal Thai Armed Forces</a> on 18 January, <b>Royal Thai Armed Forces Day</b>. The RTAF honors on that day the anniversary of the victory won by the King <a href="/wiki/Naresuan" title="Naresuan">Naresuan</a> in the epic Yuddhahatthi or Elephant Battle against <a href="/wiki/Mingyi_Swa" title="Mingyi Swa">Mingyi Swa</a> (grandson of <a href="/wiki/Bayinnaung" title="Bayinnaung">Bayinnaung</a>) in 1592 at Nong Sarai, <a href="/wiki/Suphanburi" class="mw-redirect" title="Suphanburi">Suphanburi</a>. This is honored with massive military parades in various parts of the country. The main celebrations are in Bangkok and on behalf of the Royal Family of Thailand, the Chief of Defence Forces takes the salute on this day's parade. In 2020, King Vajiralongkorn, as Chief of the Armed Forces, attended the Armed Forces Day parade for the very first time. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singapore">Singapore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=49" title="Edit section: Singapore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Singapore, <b>Singapore Armed Forces Day</b> falls on 1 July each year.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The practice began in 1969, just several years after Singapore <a href="/wiki/Singapore_in_Malaysia" title="Singapore in Malaysia">separated from Malaysia</a> to become a sovereign nation. The day is marked by a parade and a re-affirmation of the pledge of loyalty<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> by all members of the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Armed_Forces" title="Singapore Armed Forces">Singapore Armed Forces</a> on <a href="/wiki/SAF_Day_Parade" title="SAF Day Parade">parade</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Trooping_the_Colour" title="Trooping the Colour">Trooping the Colour</a> like event.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Each year, a minute of silence is observed to pay tribute to the servicemen.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On that day, the outgoing colour bearer of the Singapore Army hands over the State Colour of the Army to a new colour bearer from the Army's Best Combat Unit of the year. The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Singapore" title="President of Singapore">President of Singapore</a> gives a holiday address to the SAF and the nation. Since 1987, rededication ceremonies have been held across the country so that employers can pledge their support for SAF.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>In <b>South Korea</b>, 국군의 날 (English: <i>Armed Forces Day</i>) falls on 1 October, the day that South Korean forces broke through the <a href="/wiki/38th_parallel_north" title="38th parallel north">38th parallel</a> in 1950 during the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>. It is not a national holiday or public day off, but a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_South_Korea" title="Public holidays in South Korea">National Flag Raising Day</a> (국기게양일) to recognize and honor the active and reserve servicemen and women and veterans of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Armed_Forces" title="Republic of Korea Armed Forces">Republic of Korea Armed Forces</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=51" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Sri Lanka each armed services celebrates its own Army Day (10 October), the Navy Day (9 December) and the Air Force Day (2 March) respectively. However all armed services celebrate Independence Day (4 February) with a military parade in which they display their full colours.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Since 2010, the armed services also hold parades on <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Day_(Sri_Lanka)" title="Remembrance Day (Sri Lanka)">Victory and Remembrance Day (18 May)</a>, in honour of the armed forces fallen, heroes and veterans of the <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Civil_War" title="Sri Lankan Civil War">Sri Lankan Civil War</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vietnam">Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=52" title="Edit section: Vietnam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Vietnam, <b>People's Army Day</b> is celebrated on 22 December, the day of the 1944 foundation of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Army_of_Vietnam" title="People's Army of Vietnam">People's Army of Vietnam</a>. This is not a public holiday, but relevant celebrations are held nationwide to celebrate the occasion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Vietnam">South Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=53" title="Edit section: South Vietnam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>The Armed Forces Day of <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a> was celebrated from 1965 to 1974 on 19 June; the holiday is still celebrated internationally by former South Vietnamese military veterans.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=54" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=55" title="Edit section: Bulgaria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <b>Day of Bravery and <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Armed_Forces_Day" title="Bulgarian Armed Forces Day">Bulgarian Armed Forces Day</a></b> is commemorated every year on 6 May, The Feast of <a href="/wiki/Saint_George" title="Saint George">Saint George</a>, who is the <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Armed_Forces" title="Bulgarian Armed Forces">Bulgarian Armed Forces</a>. The national parade is held on <a href="/wiki/Prince_Alexander_of_Battenberg_Square" title="Prince Alexander of Battenberg Square">Prince Alexander of Battenberg Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>, the national capital city, on this day, with the salute taken by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Bulgaria" title="President of Bulgaria">President of Bulgaria</a>, the Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Croatia">Croatia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=56" title="Edit section: Croatia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Croatia, the commemorative dates of the Armed Forces are as follows: </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Croatia_Armed_Forces" title="Republic of Croatia Armed Forces">Republic of Croatia Armed Forces</a> (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <i lang="hr">Oružane snage Republike Hrvatske</i>): </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>28 May – Croatian Armed Forces Day (Croatian: <i lang="hr">Dan Oružanih snaga Republike Hrvatske</i>), marking the day of the first-ever public parade of the military forces on 28 May 1991 on <a href="/wiki/Stadion_Kranj%C4%8Devi%C4%87eva" title="Stadion Kranjčevićeva">Stadion Kranjčevićeva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Army" title="Croatian Army">Croatian Army</a> (Croatian: <i lang="hr">Hrvatska kopnena vojska</i>); </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>28 May – Croatian Army Day (Croatian: <i lang="hr">Dan Hrvatske kopnene vojske</i>).</li>
      </ul> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Navy" title="Croatian Navy">Croatian Navy</a> (Croatian: <i lang="hr">Hrvatska ratna mornarica</i>); </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>18 September – Croatian Navy Day (Croatian: <i lang="hr">Dan Hrvatske ratne mornarice</i>);</li>
      </ul> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Air_Force_and_Defense" class="mw-redirect" title="Croatian Air Force and Defense">Croatian Air Force and Defense</a> (Croatian: <i lang="hr">Hrvatsko ratno zrakoplovstvo i protuzračna obrana</i>). </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>12 December – Croatian Air Force Day (Croatian: <i lang="hr">Dan Hrvatskog ratnog zrakoplovstva</i>).</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The entire Croatian Armed Forces are also honored on 5 August, <a href="/wiki/Victory_Day_(Croatia)" title="Victory Day (Croatia)">Victory and Homeland Defenders Day and Day of the Defenders of Croatia</a>, celebrating the anniversary of the 1995 <a href="/wiki/Operation_Storm" title="Operation Storm">Operation Storm</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finland">Finland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=57" title="Edit section: Finland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Finland, Day of the <a href="/wiki/Finnish_Defence_Forces" title="Finnish Defence Forces">Defence Forces</a> (<a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>: <i lang="fi">Puolustusvoimain lippujuhlan päivä</i>, <small><a href="/wiki/Literal_translation" title="Literal translation">lit.</a> </small>'The Day of the Defence Forces' flag fest') is celebrated on 4 June, which is the birthday of <a href="/wiki/Marshal_of_Finland" class="mw-redirect" title="Marshal of Finland">Marshal of Finland</a> <a href="/wiki/Gustaf_Mannerheim" class="mw-redirect" title="Gustaf Mannerheim">Gustaf Mannerheim</a>. During 1919–1939 it was called the Day of the War People's flag fest (Finnish: <i lang="fi">Sotaväen lippujuhlan päivä</i>) and was celebrated on 16th as the Victory Day of the Troops of the Republic of Finland, i. e. the <a href="/wiki/Whites_(Finland)" title="Whites (Finland)">Whites</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Red_Guards_(Finland)" title="Red Guards (Finland)">Reds</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Finnish_Civil_War" title="Finnish Civil War">Civil War</a> 1918. After <a href="/wiki/The_Winter_War" class="mw-redirect" title="The Winter War">the Winter War</a> there were no need to maintain the dichotomy of Finnish society and the celebrations of 16 May were ended. During Mannerheim's 75th birthday the Finnish Government declared that from then on 4 June would officially celebrated as the Birthday of Marshal of Finland (Finnish: <i lang="fi">Suomen marsalkan syntymäpäivä</i>). Even though the official name of the day changed soon after it is still known, also in official context, as "The Birthday of Marshal of Finland".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oja_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oja-49">[49]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=58" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>During <a href="/wiki/Bastille_Day" title="Bastille Day">France's national day</a>, France honors <a href="/wiki/French_armed_forces" class="mw-redirect" title="French armed forces">its armed forces</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Bastille_Day_Military_Parade" class="mw-redirect" title="Bastille Day Military Parade">military parade of the 14 July</a>, which is the oldest and largest military parade in Europe.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=59" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Greece marks its Armed Forces Day (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el">Ημέρα των Ενόπλων Δυνάμεων</span>) on 21 November, on the day of the <a href="/wiki/Presentation_of_Mary" title="Presentation of Mary">Presentation of Mary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hungary">Hungary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=60" title="Edit section: Hungary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Hungary, the <b>Patriots and Homeland Defenders Day</b> is celebrated on 21 May, honoring all those serving in the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Defence_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Hungarian Defence Force">Hungarian Defence Force</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=61" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b>Giorno delle Forze Armate</b> (English: <i>Armed Forces Day</i>) is celebrated on 4 November to remember the Italian victory in the First World War. On 4 November 1918, Austrian-Hungarian forces agreed to a cease fire, thus ending the war on Italian front. </p>

      <ul>
       <li>Army Day: 4 May (<a href="/wiki/Italian_Army" title="Italian Army">Italian Army</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Navy_Day" title="Navy Day">Navy Day</a>: 10 June (<a href="/wiki/Italian_Navy" title="Italian Navy">Italian Navy</a>)</li> 
       <li>Air Force Day: 28 March (<a href="/wiki/Italian_Air_Force" title="Italian Air Force">Italian Air Force</a>)</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Carabinieri" title="Carabinieri">Carabinieri</a> Days: 5 June and 13 July</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Latvia">Latvia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=62" title="Edit section: Latvia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Latvian_National_Armed_Forces" title="Latvian National Armed Forces">Latvian National Armed Forces</a> Day</b> is marked every 10 July, the day when in 1919 Latvia's Independent and North Latvian brigades where united in one formation under the command of General <a href="/w/index.php?title=D%C4%81vids_S%C4%ABmansons&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dāvids Sīmansons (page does not exist)">Dāvids Sīmansons</a>. Similar celebrations occur on <a href="/wiki/L%C4%81%C4%8Dpl%C4%93sis_Day" title="Lāčplēsis Day">Lāčplēsis Day</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lithuania">Lithuania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=63" title="Edit section: Lithuania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_Armed_Forces" title="Lithuanian Armed Forces">Lithuanian Armed Forces</a> Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a>: <i lang="lt">Lietuvos kariuomenės diena</i>) is celebrated on 23 November. It honors the issuing of the first laws regarding the army on 23 November 1918 at the height of the <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian%E2%80%93Soviet_War" title="Lithuanian–Soviet War">Lithuanian–Soviet War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> The holiday is traditionally celebrated with the noon <a href="/wiki/Military_parade" title="Military parade">military parade</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_Square,_Vilnius" title="Cathedral Square, Vilnius">Cathedral Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a>, which runs through <a href="/wiki/Gediminas_Avenue" title="Gediminas Avenue">Gediminas Avenue</a> to Independence Square after being reviewed by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Lithuania" class="mw-redirect" title="President of Lithuania">President of Lithuania</a>. <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>, alongside Lithuanian troops take part in the parade, with contingents coming from <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Moldova">Moldova</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=64" title="Edit section: Moldova">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 3 September, the <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_National_Army" title="Moldovan National Army">Moldovan National Army</a> marks the <i>Ziua Armatei Naționale</i> (Day of the National Army), with the Ministry of Defense organizing large demonstrations on the occasion of holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Military and civilian staff lay flowers at the <a href="/wiki/Stephen_the_Great_Monument" title="Stephen the Great Monument">Stephen the Great Monument</a> and there is also a ceremony of the decoration of National Army distinctions. On 2 March, the entire <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="Armed Forces of the Republic of Moldova">Armed Forces of Moldova</a> celebrates Remembrance Day, which honours the veterans of the <a href="/wiki/Transnistria_War" title="Transnistria War">Transnistria War</a>, with events being organized from 1-4 March. Participants in years past have also organized the Memory March, walking from <a href="/wiki/Great_National_Assembly_Square,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="Great National Assembly Square, Chișinău">Great National Assembly Square</a> to the Maica Indurerata (Grieving Mother) at the <a href="/wiki/Eternity_Memorial_Complex,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="Eternity Memorial Complex, Chișinău">Eternity Memorial Complex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_Macedonia">North Macedonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=65" title="Edit section: North Macedonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <b>Day of <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Republic_of_North_Macedonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Army of the Republic of North Macedonia">Macedonian Army</a></b> is celebrated on 18 August. This date is chosen because on 18 August 1943, the battalion <i>Mirče Acev</i> was formed at the Slavej Mountain. It was the first organized battalion to fight against the Axis forces in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Macedonia" title="North Macedonia">North Macedonia</a>. The day is not a national holiday, but is celebrated with a manifestation at one of the Army's barracks, where the <a href="/wiki/President_of_North_Macedonia" title="President of North Macedonia">President</a>, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Speakers_of_the_Assembly_of_the_Republic_of_North_Macedonia" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Speakers of the Assembly of the Republic of North Macedonia">Speaker of the Assembly</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_North_Macedonia" title="Prime Minister of North Macedonia">Prime Minister</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Defense_(North_Macedonia)" title="Minister of Defense (North Macedonia)">Minister of Defense</a> are always present. Usually there is a parade of the armed forces and an exhibition of the weapons and vehicles of the Army.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poland">Poland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=66" title="Edit section: Poland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Day_(Poland)" title="Armed Forces Day (Poland)">Armed Forces Day (Poland)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In Poland, the <b>Święto Wojska Polskiego</b> (English: <i>Polish Armed Forces Day</i>) is celebrated annually on 15 August. Begun in 1923, the day commemorated the anniversary of Poland's 1920 victory over <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Soviet Russia</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Warsaw_(1920)" title="Battle of Warsaw (1920)">Battle of Warsaw</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Polish-Soviet_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish-Soviet War">Polish-Soviet War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PolskieRadio2013_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PolskieRadio2013-58">[58]</a></sup> The holiday was discontinued during the <a href="/wiki/Polish_People%27s_Republic" title="Polish People's Republic">communist era</a> in 1947 and replaced with 12 October, the anniversary of the 1943 <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lenino" title="Battle of Lenino">Battle of Lenino</a>, but it was revived again after the overthrow of communism in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Romania">Romania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=67" title="Edit section: Romania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Romania, the <b>Armed Forces Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a>: <i lang="ro">Ziua Armatei</i>) is celebrated on 25 October. On this day, in 1944, the <a href="/wiki/Romanian_Land_Forces" title="Romanian Land Forces">Romanian Land Forces</a> retook <a href="/wiki/Carei" title="Carei">Carei</a>, the last Romanian city under joint German–Hungarian occupation. </p>
      <p>The Romania–Hungary border had been reached a few days earlier, but the troops rested, and then completed the reoccupation of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Transylvania" title="Northern Transylvania">Northern Transylvania</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Carei" title="Battle of Carei">Battle of Carei</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russian_Federation">Russian Federation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=68" title="Edit section: Russian Federation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Russia, the <b>День защитника Отечества / Dyen' zaschitnika Otechestva</b> (English: <i><a href="/wiki/Defender_of_the_Fatherland_Day" title="Defender of the Fatherland Day">Defender of the Fatherland Day</a></i>) is celebrated on 23 February, honoring all those serving in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Armed_Forces" title="Russian Armed Forces">Russian Armed Forces</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> there was <b>День Советской Армии / Dyen' Sovetskoy Armii</b> (<i>Day of the Soviet Army</i>), celebrated on the same date. It is the very day of the anniversary since the formation of the modern armed forces of the Soviet Union in 1918, the traditions of which are continued by the current Russian Armed Forces, in conjunction with the heritage of its Imperial past. </p>
      <p>In Russia the following holidays are celebrated by military personnel, veterans and the general public: </p>

       <li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Special_Operations_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Russian Special Operations Forces Day (page does not exist)">Russian Special Operations Forces Day</a> on 27 February, commemorating the annexation of the Crimean Peninsula in Ukraine, 2014<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Victory_Day" title="Victory Day">Victory Day</a> on 9 May: Victory Day marks Germany's surrender to the Soviet Union in 1945</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Navy_Day_(Russia)" title="Navy Day (Russia)">Navy Day</a> on the last Sunday of July</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Paratroopers%27_Day" title="Paratroopers' Day">Paratroopers' Day</a> on 2 August</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Russian_Air_Force_Day" title="Russian Air Force Day">Russian Air Force Day</a> on 12 August</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Russian_Ground_Forces" title="Russian Ground Forces">Russian Ground Forces</a> Day on 1 October, in honor of the 1550 raising of the first <a href="/wiki/Streltsy" title="Streltsy">Streltsy</a> detachments</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Russian_Aerospace_Defence_Forces" title="Russian Aerospace Defence Forces">Russian Aerospace Defence Forces</a> Day on 4 October, the date of the 1957 launching of <a href="/wiki/Sputnik" class="mw-redirect" title="Sputnik">Sputnik</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Naval_Infantry_(Russia)" class="mw-redirect" title="Naval Infantry (Russia)">Russian Naval Infantry</a> Day on 27 November, the date of its 1705 founding</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Strategic_Missile_Forces" title="Strategic Missile Forces">Strategic Missile Forces</a> Day on 17 December</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Serbia">Serbia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=69" title="Edit section: Serbia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b>Dan Vojske Srbije</b> (<i><a href="/wiki/Serbian_Armed_Forces" title="Serbian Armed Forces">Serbian Armed Forces</a> Day</i>) is marked on 23 April, the anniversary of the beginning of the 1815 <a href="/wiki/Second_Serbian_Uprising" title="Second Serbian Uprising">Second Serbian Uprising</a> which began the long road towards the restoration of Serbian independence after years of Ottoman occupation. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=70" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b>Armed Forces Day</b> (Spanish: <i>Día de las Fuerzas Armadas</i>) is observed in Spain since 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> It started as a purely military celebration, but became with time a more colourful and popular event, the central acts of which are held each year at a different city.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Since 1987 it is observed the Saturday nearest to 30 May, feast-day of <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_III_of_Castile" title="Ferdinand III of Castile">Saint Ferdinand, King</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Armed Forces and those killed in service are also honoured at the celebrations of <a href="/wiki/Fiesta_Nacional_de_Espa%C3%B1a" class="mw-redirect" title="Fiesta Nacional de España">Spain's National Day</a> (12 October). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ukraine">Ukraine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=71" title="Edit section: Ukraine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Ground Forces Day</b> for the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Ground_Forces" title="Ukrainian Ground Forces">Ukrainian Ground Forces</a> is celebrated on 12 December.</li> 
       <li><b>Navy Day</b> for the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Navy" title="Ukrainian Navy">Ukrainian Navy</a> is celebrated on the first Sunday of July since June 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-pndfsj_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pndfsj-66">[66]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><b>Air Force Day</b> is the first Saturday in August.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>For the entire <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_Ukraine" title="Armed Forces of Ukraine">Armed Forces of Ukraine</a>, <b>Armed Forces Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">День Збройних сил України</span>) is celebrated on 6 December, with fireworks displays and gun salutes nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-CMUArmyDAy_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CMUArmyDAy-70">[70]</a></sup> This holiday was established in 1993 by a resolution passed by the <a href="/wiki/Verkhovna_Rada" title="Verkhovna Rada">Verkhovna Rada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> </p>

       <li>14 October – <a href="/wiki/Defender_of_Ukraine_Day" title="Defender of Ukraine Day">Defender of Ukraine Day</a></li> 
       <li>29 October – Finance Officers Day</li> 
       <li>3 November – <a href="/wiki/Rocket_Forces_and_Artillery_(Ukraine)" title="Rocket Forces and Artillery (Ukraine)">Rocket Forces and Artillery</a> Day</li>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=72" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Day_(United_Kingdom)" title="Armed Forces Day (United Kingdom)">Armed Forces Day (United Kingdom)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The first Armed Forces Day in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> took place on 27 June 2009. It replaced the previous <a href="/wiki/Veterans%27_Day_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Veterans' Day UK">Veterans' Day</a>, first observed in 2006. <sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The date was chosen as it marked the day after the anniversary of the first investiture ceremony for the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Cross" title="Victoria Cross">Victoria Cross</a> military medal for heroism, held on 26 June 1857.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The 2009 celebrations were centred on <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Historic_Dockyard" title="Chatham Historic Dockyard">Chatham Historic Dockyard</a> – a former <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> base. The Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Jane_Brown" title="Sarah Jane Brown">Sarah</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Richard,_Duke_of_Gloucester" title="Prince Richard, Duke of Gloucester">Prince Richard, the Duke and his Duchess of Gloucester</a>, attended as the official party, along with the head of the unified Armed Forces, <a href="/wiki/Air_Chief_Marshal" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Chief Marshal">Air Chief Marshal</a> <a href="/wiki/Sir_Jock_Stirrup" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Jock Stirrup">Sir Jock Stirrup</a>, and Defence Minister <a href="/wiki/Kevan_Jones" title="Kevan Jones">Kevan Jones</a>. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Crown_Dependency" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown Dependency">Crown Dependency</a>, held its events a month later on 26 July 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The 2010 event was centred on <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> and in 2011 it was Edinburgh's turn. Smaller events were held throughout the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.armedforcesday.org.uk/">UK Armed Forces Day 2012</a> was centred on <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> and took place on Saturday 30 June. Smaller events were held throughout the United Kingdom. The Isle of Man holds <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130621162652/http://www.gov.im/event.gov?eventid=3047">its event</a> on Sunday 24 June. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a> was chosen to be the host location for the 2020 UK Armed Forces Day event on Saturday 27 June.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> Scarborough was announced as the venue in June 2018, after submitting a bid which was described by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)">MoD</a> as “outstanding”. </p>
      <p>The event was due to take place on 27 June 2020, and would have contained a large programme of events taking place on the day and in the build up to the event.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> The AFD became a "virtual event" after the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom">COVID-19 pandemic</a> and so Scarborough Borough Council created a website hosting military related content instead. The AFD for 2021, will be held in Scarborough in lieu of the 2020 event.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oceania">Oceania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=73" title="Edit section: Oceania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia_and_New_Zealand">Australia and New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=74" title="Edit section: Australia and New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b><a href="/wiki/ANZAC_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="ANZAC Day">ANZAC Day</a></b> is a public holiday commemorated on 25 April. It is a national day of remembrance in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> that broadly commemorates all Australians and New Zealanders "who served and died in all wars, conflicts, and peacekeeping operations" and "the contribution and suffering of all those who have served."<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> The date commemorates the landings in 1915 at <a href="/wiki/Anzac_Cove" class="mw-redirect" title="Anzac Cove">Anzac Cove</a> on the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Dardanelles" title="Dardanelles">Dardanelles</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a> of the old <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>) by <a href="/wiki/Australian_Army" title="Australian Army">Australian</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Army" title="New Zealand Army">New Zealand</a> combined military forces in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_and_New_Zealand_Army_Corps" title="Australian and New Zealand Army Corps">Australian and New Zealand Army Corps</a>, the beginning of the costly casualties of the <a href="/wiki/Gallipoli_campaign" title="Gallipoli campaign">Gallipoli campaign</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. </p>
      <p>There is also a Reserve Forces Day in Australia first celebrated in 1998 for "the 50th anniversary of the reforming of the Citizen Military Forces after World War II on 1 July 1948 and Reserve service. The date of the first of July was chosen as the official date of Reserve Forces Day with the celebrations being held on that day or the weekend before or following that date."<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> </p>
