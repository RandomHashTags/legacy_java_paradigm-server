      <p><b>World Book Day</b>, also known as <b>World Book and Copyright Day</b>, or <b>International Day of the Book</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#April" title="List of minor secular observances">annual event</a> organized by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization</a> (UNESCO) to promote <a href="/wiki/Reading_(activity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Reading (activity)">reading</a>, <a href="/wiki/Publishing" title="Publishing">publishing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyright</a>. The first World Book Day was celebrated on 23 April in 1995, and continues to be recognized on that day. A related <a href="/wiki/World_Book_Day_(UK_and_Ireland)" title="World Book Day (UK and Ireland)">event in the United Kingdom and Ireland</a> is observed in March.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_selection">Date selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Book_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Date selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The original idea came from the Catalan “Dia del Llibre” or day of the Book celebrated in Catalonia each April 23rd. In 1995 <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> decided that the World Book and Copyright Day would be celebrated on 23 April, as the date is also the anniversary of the death of <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inca_Garcilaso_de_la_Vega" title="Inca Garcilaso de la Vega">Inca Garcilaso de la Vega</a>, as well as that of the birth or death of several other prominent authors.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (In a historical coincidence, Shakespeare and Cervantes died on the same <i>date</i> — 23 April 1616 — but not on the same <i>day</i>, as at the time, Spain used the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a> and England used the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>; Shakespeare actually died 10 days after Cervantes died, on 3 May of the Gregorian calendar). </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catalonia">Catalonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Book_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Catalonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a> (Spain), <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St. George's Day">St. George's Day</a> (Diada de Sant Jordi), the patron saint of Catalonia, has been commemorated since 1436, and involves the exchange of gifts between loved ones and respected people. St George's Day in Catalonia is celebrated with giving loved ones books and roses, and is an opportunity for Catalans to honour their patron saint and show their love of culture.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Book_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, Book Day began to be celebrated since 1926 every October 7<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>, the date that <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_Cervantes" title="Miguel de Cervantes">Miguel de Cervantes</a> was believed to have been born. But, it was considered more appropriate to celebrate this day in a more pleasant season for walking and browsing the books in the open-air. Spring was much better than fall. So in 1930 <a href="/wiki/King_Alfonso_XIII" class="mw-redirect" title="King Alfonso XIII">King Alfonso XIII</a> approved the change of celebration of the Book Day on April 23, the supposed date of the death of Cervantes.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Book_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, the day is known as <i>Världsbokdagen</i> ("World Book Day") and the copyright aspect is seldom mentioned. Normally celebrated on 23 April, it was moved to 13 April in the year 2000<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and 2011 to avoid a clash with <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom_and_Ireland">United Kingdom and Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Book_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United Kingdom and Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/World_Book_Day_(UK_and_Ireland)" title="World Book Day (UK and Ireland)">World Book Day (UK and Ireland)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, World Book Day is a <a href="/wiki/World_Book_Day_(UK_and_Ireland)" title="World Book Day (UK and Ireland)">charity event in March</a>, held annually on the first Thursday and coinciding with the <a href="/wiki/List_of_World_Book_Day_books" title="List of World Book Day books">release of special editions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The annual celebration on 23 April is World Book Night, an event organized by independent charity <a href="/wiki/The_Reading_Agency" title="The Reading Agency">The Reading Agency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Book_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Kensington,_Maryland" title="Kensington, Maryland">Kensington</a>, Maryland, the International Day of the Book is celebrated with a <a href="/wiki/Street_festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Street festival">street festival</a> on the Sunday closest to 26 April.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>This year 15th Annual Kensington Day of the Book Festival was cancelled due to <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_the_United_States" title="COVID-19 pandemic in the United States">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
