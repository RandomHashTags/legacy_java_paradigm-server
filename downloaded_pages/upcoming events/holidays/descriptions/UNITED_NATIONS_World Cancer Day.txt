      <p><b>World Cancer Day</b> is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#February" title="List of minor secular observances">international day</a> marked on <a href="/wiki/February_4" title="February 4">February 4</a> to <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raise awareness</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> and to encourage its <a href="/wiki/Cancer_prevention" title="Cancer prevention">prevention</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cancer_screening" title="Cancer screening">detection</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Treatment_of_cancer" title="Treatment of cancer">treatment</a>. World Cancer Day is led by the <a href="/wiki/Union_for_International_Cancer_Control" title="Union for International Cancer Control">Union for International Cancer Control</a> (UICC) to support the goals of the World Cancer Declaration, written in 2008. The primary goal of World Cancer Day is to significantly reduce illness and <a href="/wiki/Epidemiology_of_cancer#Incidence_and_mortality" title="Epidemiology of cancer">death caused by cancer</a><sup id="cite_ref-World_Cancer_Day_2013_Summary_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-World_Cancer_Day_2013_Summary-1">[1]</a></sup> and is an opportunity to rally the international community to end the injustice of preventable suffering from cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The day is observed by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>World Cancer Day targets misinformation,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raises awareness</a>, and reduces <a href="/wiki/Social_stigma" title="Social stigma">stigma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Multiple initiatives are run on World Cancer Day to show support for those affected by cancer. One of these movements are <a href="/wiki/Hashtag_activism" title="Hashtag activism">#NoHairSelfie</a>, a global movement to have "hairticipants" shave their heads either physically or virtually to show a symbol of courage for those undergoing cancer treatment.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Images of participants are then shared all over <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a>. Hundreds of events around the world also take place.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Cancer_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>World Cancer Day was established on 4 February 2000 at the World Cancer Summit Against Cancer for the New Millenium, which was held in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Charter of Paris Against Cancer,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which was created to promote research, prevent cancer, improve patient services, also included an article establishing the anniversary of the document's official signing as World Cancer Day, was signed at the Summit by the then General Director of <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dichir%C5%8D_Matsuura" title="Kōichirō Matsuura">Kōichirō Matsuura</a>, and then French President <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Chirac" title="Jacques Chirac">Jacques Chirac</a> in Paris on 4 February 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Cancer_Day_themes">World Cancer Day themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Cancer_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World Cancer Day themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>The 2019-2021 campaign theme is 'I Am and I Will'. The theme seeks to counter the negative attitude and fatalistic belief that nothing can be done about cancer, and instead promotes how our personal actions can be powerful and impactful.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2016, World Cancer Day started a three-year campaign under the tagline of 'We can. I can.', which explored the power of collective and individual actions to reduce the impact of cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Prior to 2016, the campaign themes included "Not Beyond Us" (2015) and "Debunk the Myths" (2014). </p>

      <p>World Cancer Day is marked by the international cancer community, governments and individuals around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Each year, more than 900 activities take place in over 100 different countries, with the day itself a trending topic on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In recent years, cities have begun to support the day by lighting up important landmarks in orange and blue. In 2019, 55 landmarks in 37 cities participated in the landmark lighting initiative.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-21">[21]</a></sup> At least 60 governments officially observe World Cancer Day. </p>
