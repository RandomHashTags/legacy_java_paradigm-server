      <p><b>World Cities Day</b> is an annual <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances" title="List of minor secular observances">observance day</a> held on 31 October.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The global observance, first held in 2014, is organized by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Settlements_Programme" title="United Nations Human Settlements Programme">United Nations Human Settlements Programme</a> (UN-Habitat) in coordination with each year's selected host city.<sup id="cite_ref-UN_Cities_Day_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_Cities_Day-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Cities_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>World Cities Day was established on 27 December 2013 by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> in its resolution A/RES/68/239,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in which the General Assembly "decides to designate 31 October, beginning in 2014, as World Cities Day, invites <a href="/wiki/Member_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Member State">States</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> system, in particular <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Settlements_Programme" title="United Nations Human Settlements Programme">UN-Habitat</a>, relevant <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">international organizations</a>, civil society and all relevant <a href="/wiki/Organizational_stakeholders" title="Organizational stakeholders">stakeholders</a> to observe and raise awareness of the Day".<sup id="cite_ref-UN_Cities_Day_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_Cities_Day-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The first World Cities Day was held in October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>A legacy of <a href="/wiki/Expo_2010" title="Expo 2010">Expo 2010 Shanghai China</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> World Cities Day aims to promote the international community's interest in global urbanization, push forward cooperation among countries in meeting opportunities and addressing challenges of <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">urbanization</a>, and contributing to sustainable <a href="/wiki/Urban_Planning" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban Planning">urban development</a> around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The observance day ties in with <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goal_11" title="Sustainable Development Goal 11">Sustainable Development Goal 11</a>, to make cities "inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The general theme of World Cities Day is <i>Better City, Better Life</i>, while each year a different sub-theme and a location for its global observance is selected, to either promote successes of urbanization, or address specific challenges resulting from urbanization.<sup id="cite_ref-Liverpool_Express_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Liverpool_Express-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
