      <p>The <b>International Day of Yoga</b> has been <a href="/wiki/List_of_International_Days_of_Yoga" title="List of International Days of Yoga">celebrated annually on 21 June</a> since 2015, following its inception in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Yoga" title="Yoga">Yoga</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Yoga_as_exercise" title="Yoga as exercise">physical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mind" title="Mind">mental</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spiritual_practice" title="Spiritual practice">spiritual practice</a> which originated in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Indian_Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Prime Minister">Indian Prime Minister</a>, <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a>, in his UN address suggested the date of 21 June, as it is the <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">longest day of the year</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> and shares a special significance in many parts of the world.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Yoga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The idea of an International Day of Yoga was first proposed by the current Prime Minister of India, <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a>, during his speech at the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> (UNGA), on 27 September 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">[4]</a></sup> He stated:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>Yoga is an invaluable gift of India's ancient tradition. It embodies unity of mind and body; thought and action; restraint and fulfillment; harmony between <a href="/wiki/Man_and_nature" class="mw-redirect" title="Man and nature">man and nature</a>; a holistic approach to health and well-being. It is not about exercise but to discover the sense of oneness with yourself, the world and the nature. By changing our lifestyle and creating consciousness, it can help in well being. Let us work towards adopting an International Yoga Day.</p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        — <cite>Narendra Modi, <a href="/wiki/UN_General_Assembly" class="mw-redirect" title="UN General Assembly">UN General Assembly</a></cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>Following this initial proposal, the UNGA held informal consultations on the draft resolution, entitled "International Day of Yoga", on 14 October 2014. The consultations were convened by the delegation of India.<sup id="cite_ref-economictimes_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economictimes-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2015 <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Bank_of_India" title="Reserve Bank of India">Reserve Bank of India</a> issued a 10 rupees commemorative coin to mark the International Day of Yoga.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In April 2017, UN Postal Administration (UNPA) issued 10 stamps on Asanas on a single sheet to mark International Day of Yoga.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UN_Declaration">UN Declaration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Yoga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: UN Declaration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>On 11 December 2014, India's Permanent Representative <a href="/wiki/Asoke_Kumar_Mukerji" title="Asoke Kumar Mukerji">Asoke Mukherji</a> introduced the draft resolution in the United Nations General Assembly. The draft text received broad support from 177 Member States who sponsored the text, which was adopted without a vote. This initiative found support from many global leaders. A total of 177 nations co-sponsored the resolution, which is the highest number of co-sponsors ever for any UNGA resolution of such nature.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>When proposing 21 June as the date, Modi said that the date was the longest day of the year in the mass northern hemisphere (shortest in the southern hemisphere), having special significance in many parts of the world. From the perspective of yoga, the summer solstice marks the transition to <a href="/wiki/Dakshinayana" title="Dakshinayana">Dakshinayana</a>. The second <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> full moon after summer solstice is known as <a href="/wiki/Guru_Poornima" class="mw-redirect" title="Guru Poornima">Guru Poornima</a>. <a href="/wiki/Shiva" title="Shiva">Shiva</a>, the first yogi (Adi Yogi), is said to have begun imparting the knowledge of yoga to the rest of mankind on this day, and became the first guru (Adi Guru).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Following the adoption of the UN resolution, several leaders of the spiritual movement in India voiced their support for the initiative. The founder of <a href="/wiki/Isha_Foundation" title="Isha Foundation">Isha Foundation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sadhguru" class="mw-redirect" title="Sadhguru">Sadhguru</a>, stated, "this could be a kind of a foundation stone to make scientific approach to the inner well-being of the human being, a worldwide thing... It's a tremendous step for the world."<sup id="cite_ref-Youtube_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Youtube-12">[12]</a></sup> The founder of <a href="/wiki/Art_of_Living_Foundation" title="Art of Living Foundation">Art of Living</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ravi_Shankar_(spiritual_leader)" title="Ravi Shankar (spiritual leader)">Ravi Shankar</a>, lauded the efforts of Modi, saying, "It is very difficult for any philosophy, religion or culture to survive without state patronage. Yoga has existed so far almost like an orphan. Now, official recognition by the UN would further spread the benefit of yoga to the entire world."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_practice">In practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Yoga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>The first International Day of Yoga was observed around the world on 21 June 2015. The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Ayurveda,_Yoga_and_Naturopathy,_Unani,_Siddha_and_Homoeopathy" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Ayurveda, Yoga and Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha and Homoeopathy">Ministry of AYUSH</a> made the necessary arrangements in India. 35,985 people, including PM Modi and dignitaries from 84 nations, performed 21 <a href="/wiki/Asana" title="Asana">asanas</a> (yoga postures) for 35 minutes at <a href="/wiki/Rajpath" title="Rajpath">Rajpath</a> in New Delhi, becoming the largest yoga class ever held, and with the largest number—84—of participating nations.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-world_record_1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-world_record_1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/List_of_International_Days_of_Yoga" title="List of International Days of Yoga">Similar days have been held</a> in cities in India and around the world each year since then.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newsgraph.net_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsgraph.net-22">[22]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Yoga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>An <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> report in 2015 noted that the first "International Yoga Day"<sup id="cite_ref-AP_2015_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP_2015-23">[23]</a></sup> involved "millions of yoga enthusiasts" who "stretched and twisted", as well as Modi and members of his cabinet. It stated that the main road in Delhi had become an exercise area for the occasion, and reported that while Modi was speaking of "peace and harmony",<sup id="cite_ref-AP_2015_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP_2015-23">[23]</a></sup> some people in India thought the promotion of yoga was a partisan <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> operation. It reported that a sequence of <a href="/wiki/Surya_Namaskar" title="Surya Namaskar">Surya Namaskar</a> (sun salutations) was dropped because Muslims objected to the implication that the sun was the Hindu god of the sun, <a href="/wiki/Surya" title="Surya">Surya</a>; the chanting of the Hindu sacred syllable "<a href="/wiki/Om" title="Om">Om</a>" was also dropped. Others considered that the money spent on the event might have been better spent on cleaning Delhi's streets.<sup id="cite_ref-AP_2015_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP_2015-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Christian_Science_Monitor" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Science Monitor">Christian Science Monitor</a></i> wrote in 2016 that the 2014 United Nations resolution had been "wildly popular"<sup id="cite_ref-CSM_2016_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSM_2016-24">[24]</a></sup> but noted that yoga had a "<a href="/wiki/Meditation" title="Meditation">meditative</a> component"<sup id="cite_ref-CSM_2016_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSM_2016-24">[24]</a></sup> and had become known as not only <a href="/wiki/Yoga_as_exercise" title="Yoga as exercise">a form of physical exercise</a> but also a mental and spiritual practice. It gave as evidence the 2015 sermon by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> cautioning <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholics" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholics">Roman Catholics</a> about the idea that yoga could be a path to God; it noted, too, that Modi had replied to the charge that the Day was intended to promote Hinduism with the words "Yoga is not about the other life. Therefore, it is not a religious practice".<sup id="cite_ref-CSM_2016_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSM_2016-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Week" title="The Week">The Week</a></i> stated in 2015 that the government of India's purpose in holding International Days of Yoga was to have yoga recognised around the world as "India's cultural property",<sup id="cite_ref-TW_2015_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TW_2015-25">[25]</a></sup> citing India's minister of yoga, Shripad Yesso Naik as stating "We're trying to establish to the world that it's ours."<sup id="cite_ref-TW_2015_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TW_2015-25">[25]</a></sup> <i>The Week</i> wrote that this was not likely to succeed, not least because many types of yoga were already being practised in the Western world.<sup id="cite_ref-TW_2015_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TW_2015-25">[25]</a></sup> The article noted that <a href="/wiki/Evangelical_Christian" class="mw-redirect" title="Evangelical Christian">Christian evangelicals</a> agreed with the Indian government that yoga was "primarily a Hindu spiritual practice",<sup id="cite_ref-TW_2015_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TW_2015-25">[25]</a></sup> but quoted the scholar of religion Ann Gleig as saying that most Western yoga was markedly changed by being in the West, and was devoid of religious content; the "ironically"<sup id="cite_ref-TW_2015_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TW_2015-25">[25]</a></sup> agreeing views of strongly religious Hindus and Christians were "historically flawed".<sup id="cite_ref-TW_2015_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TW_2015-25">[25]</a></sup> </p>
