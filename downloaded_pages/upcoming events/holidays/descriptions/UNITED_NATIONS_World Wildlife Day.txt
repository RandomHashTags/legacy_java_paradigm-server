      <p>On 20 December 2013, at its 68th session, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> (UNGA), in its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.un.org/en/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/68/205">resolution UN 68/205</a>, decided to proclaim 3 March, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#March" title="List of minor secular observances">international day</a> of the adoption of the <a href="/wiki/CITES" title="CITES">Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora</a> (CITES) on the planet raise awareness and benefits fauna and flora in 1973, as <b>World Wildlife Day</b>, which was proposed by <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to celebrate and <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raise awareness</a> of the world's wild <a href="/wiki/Fauna" title="Fauna">fauna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flora" title="Flora">flora</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UNGA_Resolution">UNGA Resolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wildlife_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: UNGA Resolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In its resolution,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the General Assembly reaffirmed the intrinsic value of wildlife and its various contributions, including ecological, genetic, social, economic, scientific, educational, cultural, recreational and aesthetic, to <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> and human <a href="/wiki/Well-being" title="Well-being">well-being</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The General Assembly took note of the outcome of the 16th meeting of the Conference of the Parties to CITES, held in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a> from 3 to 14 March 2013, in particular Resolution Conf. 16.1<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> designating 3 March as World Wildlife Day, in order to celebrate and raise awareness of the world’s wild fauna and flora, and recognized the important role of CITES in ensuring that international trade does not threaten the survival of species.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The General Assembly requested the CITES Secretariat, in collaboration with relevant organizations of the United Nations system, to facilitate the implementation of World Wildlife Day.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wildlife_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>2021</b>: The 2021 theme is “Forests and Livelihoods: sustaining people and planet"<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><b>2020</b>: The 2020 theme is “Sustaining all life on earth"<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><b>2019</b>: The 2019 theme is “Life below water: for people and planet"<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><b>2018</b>: The 2018 theme is "Big cats - predators under threat".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><b>2017</b>: The 2017 theme is "Listen to the young voices".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><b>2016</b>: The 2016 theme is "The future of wildlife is in our hands", with a sub-theme "The future of elephants is in our hands". </p>
      <p><b>2015</b>: The 2015 theme is "It’s time to get serious about wildlife crime". </p>
