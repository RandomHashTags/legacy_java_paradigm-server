      <p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> declared <a href="/wiki/May_3" title="May 3">May 3</a> to be <b>World Press Freedom Day</b><sup id="cite_ref-UN_A48PV85_page29_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A48PV85_page29-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UN_A48624_page22_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_A48624_page22-2">[2]</a></sup> or just <b>World Press Day</b>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#May" title="List of minor secular observances">observed</a> to <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raise awareness</a> of the importance of <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_the_press" title="Freedom of the press">freedom of the press</a> and remind governments of their duty to respect and uphold the right to <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_expression" class="mw-redirect" title="Freedom of expression">freedom of expression</a> enshrined under Article 19 of the 1948 <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">Universal Declaration of Human Rights</a> and marking the anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Windhoek_Declaration" title="Windhoek Declaration">Windhoek Declaration</a>, a statement of <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_the_press" title="Freedom of the press">free press</a> principles put together by <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> newspaper journalists in <a href="/wiki/Windhoek" title="Windhoek">Windhoek</a> in 1991. </p>

      <p><a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> marks World Press Freedom Day by conferring the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO/Guillermo_Cano_World_Press_Freedom_Prize" title="UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize">UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize</a> on a deserving individual, organisation or institution that has made an outstanding contribution to the defence and/or promotion of press freedom anywhere in the world, especially when this has been achieved in the face of danger. Created in 1997, the prize is awarded on the recommendation of an independent jury of 14 news professionals. Names are submitted by regional and international <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organisations" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-governmental organisations">non-governmental organisations</a> working for <a href="/wiki/Press_freedom" class="mw-redirect" title="Press freedom">press freedom</a>, and by UNESCO member states.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Prize is named in honour of <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_Cano_Isaza" title="Guillermo Cano Isaza">Guillermo Cano Isaza</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> who was assassinated in front of the offices of his newspaper, <i><a href="/wiki/El_Espectador" title="El Espectador">El Espectador</a>,</i> in <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">Bogotá</a>, on 17 December 1986. Cano's writings had offended Colombia's powerful drug barons. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UNESCO_conference">UNESCO conference</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Press_Freedom_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: UNESCO conference">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>UNESCO also marks World Press Freedom Day each year by bringing together media professionals, press freedom organisations and UN agencies to assess the state of press freedom worldwide and discuss solutions for addressing challenges. Each conference is centred on a theme related to press freedom, including good <a href="/wiki/Governance" title="Governance">governance</a>, media coverage of <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Impunity" title="Impunity">impunity</a> and the role of media in post-<a href="/wiki/War" title="War">conflict</a> countries. </p>
