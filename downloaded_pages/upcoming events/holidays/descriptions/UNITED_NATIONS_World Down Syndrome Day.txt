      <p><b>World Down Syndrome Day</b> (<b>WDSD</b>) is marked each year on March 21,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> beginning in 2006. The 21st day of March (the 3rd month of the year) was selected to signify the uniqueness of the triplication (trisomy) of the 21st chromosome which causes <a href="/wiki/Down_syndrome" title="Down syndrome">Down syndrome</a>. </p>
      <p>Every year on March 21, <b>World Down Syndrome Day</b> is <b>observed</b> to create <b>awareness</b> about <b>down syndrome</b>. It is a condition in which a child is born with an extra 21st chromosome </p>
