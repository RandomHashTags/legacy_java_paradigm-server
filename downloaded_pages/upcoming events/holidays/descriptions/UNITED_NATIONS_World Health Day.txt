      <p><b>World Health Day</b> is a global health <a href="/wiki/Awareness_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Awareness day">awareness day</a> celebrated every year on April 7, under the <a href="/wiki/Sponsor_(commercial)" title="Sponsor (commercial)">sponsorship</a> of the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (WHO), as well as other related organizations. </p>
      <p>In 1948, the WHO held the First <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Assembly" title="World Health Assembly">World Health Assembly</a>. The Assembly decided to celebrate 7 April of each year, with effect from 1950, as the World Health Day. The World Health Day is held to mark WHO's founding and is seen as an opportunity by the <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">organization</a> to draw worldwide attention to a subject of major importance to <a href="/wiki/Global_health" title="Global health">global health</a> each year.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The WHO organizes international, regional and local events on the Day related to a particular theme. World Health Day is acknowledged by various governments and non-governmental organizations with interests in <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> issues, who also organize activities and highlight their support in media reports, such as the <a href="/wiki/Global_Health_Council" title="Global Health Council">Global Health Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>World Health Day is one of 11 official <a href="/wiki/Global_health" title="Global health">global health</a> campaigns marked by WHO, along with <a href="/wiki/World_Tuberculosis_Day" title="World Tuberculosis Day">World Tuberculosis Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Immunization_Week" title="World Immunization Week">World Immunization Week</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Malaria_Day" title="World Malaria Day">World Malaria Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_No_Tobacco_Day" title="World No Tobacco Day">World No Tobacco Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_AIDS_Day" title="World AIDS Day">World AIDS Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Blood_Donor_Day" title="World Blood Donor Day">World Blood Donor Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Chagas_Disease_Day" title="World Chagas Disease Day">World Chagas Disease Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Patient_Safety_Day" title="World Patient Safety Day">World Patient Safety Day</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Antimicrobial_Awareness_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World Antimicrobial Awareness Week (page does not exist)">World Antimicrobial Awareness Week</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_Hepatitis_Day" title="World Hepatitis Day">World Hepatitis Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006:_Working_together_for_health">2006: Working together for health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006: Working together for health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>In 2006, World Health Day was devoted to the <a href="/wiki/Health_Human_Resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Health Human Resources">health workforce</a> crisis, or chronic shortages of health workers around the world due to decades of underinvestment in their education, training, salaries, working environment and management. The day was also meant to celebrate individual <a href="/wiki/Health_care_provider" title="Health care provider">health workers</a> – the people who provide health care to those who need it, in other words those at the heart of <a href="/wiki/Health_system" title="Health system">health systems</a>. </p>
      <p>The Day also marked the launch of the WHO's <i><a href="/wiki/World_Health_Report" title="World Health Report">World Health Report 2006</a></i>, which focused on the same theme. The report contained an assessment of the current crisis in the global health workforce, revealing an estimated shortage of almost 4.3 million physicians, midwives, nurses and other <a href="/wiki/Health_care_provider" title="Health care provider">health care providers</a> worldwide, and further proposed a series of actions for countries and the international community to tackle it.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>In 2008, World Health Day focused on the need to protect health from the adverse effects of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> and establish links between climate change and health and other development areas such as environment, food, energy, transport. </p>

WHO selected this theme in recognition that climate change is posing ever growing threats to global public health security.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>World Health Day 2009 focused on the safety of <a href="/wiki/Health_care_provider" title="Health care provider">health facilities</a> and the readiness of health workers who treat those affected by emergencies. Health centres and staff are critical lifelines for vulnerable people in disasters – treating <a href="/wiki/Trauma_(medicine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trauma (medicine)">injuries</a>, preventing illnesses and caring for people's health needs.       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_Antimicrobial_Resistance">2011: Antimicrobial Resistance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2011: Antimicrobial Resistance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme of World Health Day 2011, marked on 7 April 2011, was "<a href="/wiki/Antimicrobial_resistance" title="Antimicrobial resistance">Antimicrobial resistance</a> and its global spread" and focused on the need for governments and stakeholders to implement the policies and practices needed to prevent and counter the emergence of highly resistant <a href="/wiki/Microorganism" title="Microorganism">microorganisms</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>When <a href="/wiki/Infection" title="Infection">infections</a> caused by resistant microorganisms fail to respond to standard treatments, including <a href="/wiki/Antibacterial" class="mw-redirect" title="Antibacterial">antibiotics</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Anti-microbial" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-microbial">antimicrobial</a> medicines – also known as <a href="/wiki/Multiple_drug_resistance" title="Multiple drug resistance">drug resistance</a> – this may result in prolonged illness and greater risk of death. </p>
      <p>On World Health Day 2011, WHO called for intensified global commitment to safeguard antimicrobial medicines for future generations. The organization introduced a six-point policy package to combat the spread of antimicrobial resistance:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Commit to a comprehensive, financed national plan with accountability and civil society engagement.</li> 
       <li>Strengthen surveillance and laboratory capacity.</li> 
       <li>Ensure uninterrupted access to essential medicines of assured quality.</li> 
       <li>Regulate and promote rational use of medicines, including in animal husbandry, and ensure proper patient care; reduce use of antimicrobials in food-producing animals.</li> 
       <li>Enhance infection prevention and control.</li> 
       <li>Foster innovations and research and development for new tools.</li>
      </ol> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_Ageing_and_Health">2012: Ageing and Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2012: Ageing and Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>World Health Day 2012 was marked with the slogan "Good health adds life to years". Life expectancy is going up in most countries, meaning more and more people live longer and enter an age when they may need <a href="/wiki/Health_care" title="Health care">health care</a>. Meanwhile, birth rates are generally falling. Countries and health care systems need to find innovative and sustainable ways to cope with the demographic shift. As stated by John Beard, director of the WHO Department of Ageing and Life Course, "With the rapid ageing of populations, finding the right model for <a href="/wiki/Long-term_care" title="Long-term care">long-term care</a> becomes more and more urgent."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Different activities were organized by WHO as well as non-governmental and community organizations around the world to mark World Health Day 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Healthy_Blood_Pressure">2013: Healthy Blood Pressure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2013: Healthy Blood Pressure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme of World Health Day 2013, marked on 7 April 2013, was the need to control <a href="/wiki/Hypertension" title="Hypertension">raised blood pressure</a> (hypertension) as a "silent killer, global public health crisis".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The slogan for the campaign was "Healthy Heart Beat, Healthy Blood Pressure”.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The WHO reports hypertension – which is both preventable and treatable – contributes to the burden of <a href="/wiki/Heart_disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Heart disease">heart disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stroke" title="Stroke">stroke</a> and kidney failure, and is an important cause of premature death and disability. The organization estimates one in 3 adults has raised blood pressure. </p>
      <p>Specific objectives of the World Health Day 2013 campaign were to: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>raise awareness of the causes and consequences of high blood pressure;</li> 
       <li>provide information on how to prevent high blood pressure and related complications;</li> 
       <li>encourage adults to check their <a href="/wiki/Blood_pressure" title="Blood pressure">blood pressure</a> and follow the advice of healthcare professionals;</li> 
       <li>encourage <a href="/wiki/Self_care" class="mw-redirect" title="Self care">self care</a> to prevent high blood pressure;</li> 
       <li>to make blood pressure measurement affordable to all;</li> 
       <li>to incite national and local authorities to create enabling environments for <a href="/wiki/Health_promotion" title="Health promotion">healthy behaviours</a>.</li>
      </ul>

      <p>World Health Day 2014 put the spotlight on some of the most commonly known <a href="/wiki/Vector_(epidemiology)" title="Vector (epidemiology)">vectors</a> – such as mosquitoes, sandflies, bugs, ticks and snails – responsible for transmitting a wide range of parasites and pathogens that can cause many different illnesses. Mosquitoes, for example, transmit <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a> – the most deadly vector-borne disease, causing an estimated 660 000 deaths annually worldwide – as well as <a href="/wiki/Dengue_fever" title="Dengue fever">dengue fever</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lymphatic_filariasis" title="Lymphatic filariasis">lymphatic filariasis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chikungunya" title="Chikungunya">chikungunya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_encephalitis" title="Japanese encephalitis">Japanese encephalitis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yellow_fever" title="Yellow fever">yellow fever</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> More than half of the world's population is at risk of these diseases. </p>
      <p>The goal of the World Health Day 2014 campaign was better protection from vector-borne diseases, especially for families living in areas where diseases are transmitted by vectors, and travelers to countries where they pose a health threat. The campaign advocated for health authorities in countries where vector-borne diseases are a <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> problem or emerging threat, to put in place measures to improve surveillance and protection. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Food_Safety">2015: Food Safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2015: Food Safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The WHO promoted improvement of <a href="/wiki/Food_safety" title="Food safety">food safety</a> as part of the 2015 World Health Day campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Unsafe food — food containing harmful bacteria, viruses, parasites or chemical substances — is responsible for more than 200 diseases, and is linked to the deaths of some 2 million people annually, mostly children. Changes in <a href="/wiki/Food_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Food production">food production</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_distribution" title="Food distribution">distribution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Food_consumption" class="mw-redirect" title="Food consumption">consumption</a>; changes to the environment; new and emerging pathogens; and <a href="/wiki/Antimicrobial_resistance" title="Antimicrobial resistance">antimicrobial resistance</a> all pose challenges to food safety systems. </p>
      <p>The WHO works with countries and partners to strengthen efforts to prevent, detect and respond to <a href="/wiki/Foodborne_disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Foodborne disease">foodborne disease</a> outbreaks in line with the <a href="/wiki/Codex_Alimentarius" title="Codex Alimentarius">Codex Alimentarius</a>, advocating that food safety is a shared responsibility — from farmers and manufacturers to vendors and consumers — and raising awareness about the importance of the part everyone can play in ensuring that the food on our plate is safe to eat.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>

Simple lifestyle measures have been shown to be effective in preventing or delaying the onset of type 2 diabetes, including maintaining <a href="/wiki/Normal_body_weight" class="mw-redirect" title="Normal body weight">normal body weight</a>, engaging in regular physical activity, and eating a healthy diet.       <p>World Health Day 2017, celebrated on 7 April, aims to mobilize action on <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depression</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> This condition affects people of all ages, from all walks of life, in all countries.       <h3><span id="2018:_Universal_Health_Coverage:_Everyone.2C_Everywhere"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018:_Universal_Health_Coverage:_Everyone,_Everywhere">2018: Universal Health Coverage: Everyone, Everywhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2018: Universal Health Coverage: Everyone, Everywhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>World Health Day 2018 theme, "Universal Health Coverage: Everyone, Everywhere", emphasized the idea that health is a fundamental human right, and that all people should be able to have access to health care. The slogan for the day was "Health For All".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Several events were held including a panel discussion on universal health coverage which was <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vL-4qihC3QI&amp;feature=youtu.be">live streamed</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> 2018 marked the 70th anniversary of the World Health Organization's founding.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="2019:_Universal_Health_Coverage:_Everyone.2C_Everywhere"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019:_Universal_Health_Coverage:_Everyone,_Everywhere">2019: Universal Health Coverage: Everyone, Everywhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2019: Universal Health Coverage: Everyone, Everywhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The 2019 World Health Day theme was "Universal Health Coverage: Everyone, Everywhere", a repeat of the 2018 theme, with an emphasis on the idea that "Universal Health Coverage is the WHO's number one goal".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> To commemorate the 2019 World Health Day theme, the World Health Organization launched a campaign to sign a petition for health for all, held a Facebook live event, and shared information about primary health care and universal health coverage "statistics and facts".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020:_Support_Nurses_and_Midwives">2020: Support Nurses and Midwives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2020: Support Nurses and Midwives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The 2020 World Health Day theme took place in the context of the COVID-19 global pandemic and was therefore launched as 'Support Nurses and Midwives".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Around the world, people spent the day thanking the nurses and health care workers on the frontlines battling the COVID-19 coronavirus.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The US White House released a presidential message emphasizing the role public health plays in "building strong, prosperous, and free societies around the world".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The celebration of 2020 World Health Day took place around the world in a mostly virtual environment through online press conferences, announcements, and social media due to the worldwide, broad based, "stay at home" order due to the COVID-19 coronavirus pandemic.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Much of the efforts were focused on raising funds for the COVID-19 solidarity response fund.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> </p>
