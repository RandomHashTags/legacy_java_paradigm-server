      <p>The <b>National Aviation Day</b> (August 19) is a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Holidays_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Holidays of the United States">national observation</a> that celebrates the development of <a href="/wiki/Aviation" title="Aviation">aviation</a>. </p>
      <p>The holiday was established in 1939 by <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt" class="mw-redirect" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt">Franklin Delano Roosevelt</a>, who issued a presidential proclamation which designated the anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Orville_Wright" class="mw-redirect" title="Orville Wright">Orville Wright</a>'s birthday to be National Aviation Day (Mr. Wright, born in 1871, was still alive when the proclamation was first issued, and would live another nine years). The proclamation was codified (USC 36:I:A:1:118), and it allows the sitting US President to proclaim August 19 as <i>National Aviation Day</i> each year, if desired. Their proclamation may direct all federal buildings and installations to fly the US flag on that day, and may encourage citizens to observe the day with activities that promote interest in aviation.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>In 2014 <a href="/wiki/No_Sleep_Records" title="No Sleep Records">No Sleep Records</a> commemorated the holiday by releasing Aviator's debut LP <i>Head In The Clouds, Hands In The Dirt</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, and in the same year <a href="/wiki/Rockstar_Games" title="Rockstar Games">Rockstar Games</a> commemorated the holiday by releasing aviation themed downloadable content for <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i>. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Air shows on or near the time of National Aviation Day for August 2018 and into September, include:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Air_and_Water_Show" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago Air and Water Show">Chicago Air and Water Show</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City_Airshow" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic City Airshow">Atlantic City Airshow</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Canadian_International_Air_Show" title="Canadian International Air Show">Canadian International Air Show</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Cleveland_National_Air_Show" title="Cleveland National Air Show">Cleveland National Air Show</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/New_York_Air_Show" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Air Show">New York Air Show</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The Chicago Air and Water show will host a NASA village in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The NASA village, active for August 17 to 19, 2018 also celebrates the 60th anniversary of NASA and includes information about projects like the <a href="/wiki/Space_Launch_System" title="Space Launch System">Space Launch System</a>, a large rocket under development in the 2010s.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> As part of the National Aviation Day festivities at the North beach location, NASA will open its <i>Journey to Tomorrow</i> traveling exhibit.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
