      <p><b>World Logic Day</b> is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#January" title="List of minor secular observances">international day</a> proclaimed by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in association with the <a href="/wiki/International_Council_for_Philosophy_and_Humanistic_Studies" title="International Council for Philosophy and Humanistic Studies">International Council for Philosophy and Human Sciences</a> (CIPSH) in November 2019 to be celebrated on <a href="/wiki/14_January" class="mw-redirect" title="14 January">14 January</a> every year. It was first celebrated on 14 January 2019, before the UNESCO declaration. World Logic Day intends to bring the intellectual history, conceptual significance and practical implications of <a href="/wiki/Logic" title="Logic">logic</a> to the attention of <a href="/wiki/Interdisciplinarity" title="Interdisciplinarity">interdisciplinary</a> science communities and the broader public.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Date">Date</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Logic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Date">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The date chosen to celebrate World Logic Day, <a href="/wiki/14_January" class="mw-redirect" title="14 January">14 January</a>, corresponds to the date of death of <a href="/wiki/Kurt_G%C3%B6del" title="Kurt Gödel">Kurt Gödel</a> and the date of birth of <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Tarski" title="Alfred Tarski">Alfred Tarski</a>, two of the most prominent logicians of the twentieth century.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proclamation">Proclamation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Logic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Proclamation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The proclamation of World Logic Day was proposed to the UNESCO Executive Board in the middle of 2019. It was discussed and adopted at the 207th session of the UNESCO Executive Board in October 2019<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and proposed to the 40th General Conference of UNESCO. On 26 November 2019, the 40th General Conference proclaimed 14 January to be World Logic Day, coordinated by <a href="/wiki/International_Council_for_Philosophy_and_Humanistic_Studies" title="International Council for Philosophy and Humanistic Studies">CIPSH</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations">Celebrations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Logic_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Celebrations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <i>Logica Universalis Association</i>, an informal meta-association promoting logic<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> closely linked to <a href="/wiki/Jean-Yves_B%C3%A9ziau" title="Jean-Yves Béziau">Jean-Yves Béziau</a>, promoted the celebration of World Logic Day 2019 by encouraging logicians worldwide to organise independent events on 14 January 2019. Approximately sixty such events were organised in 33 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The success of this informal first World Logic Day formed part of the deliberations of the 40th UNESCO General Conference in November 2019 which led to the formal proclamation by UNESCO. </p>
      <p>On the first World Logic Day after the UNESCO proclamation, the Director-General of UNESCO, <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Azoulay" title="Audrey Azoulay">Audrey Azoulay</a>, issued a statement highlighting the importance of logic:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <blockquote>
       <p>In the twenty-first century – indeed, now more than ever – the discipline of logic is a particularly timely one, utterly vital to our societies and economies. Computer science and information and communications technology, for example, are rooted in logical and algorithmic reasoning. </p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>World Logic Day 2020 was celebrated by approximately sixty events in 35 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
