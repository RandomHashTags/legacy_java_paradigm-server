      <p><b>Zero Discrimination Day</b> is an annual day celebrated on 1 March each year by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> (UN) and other international organisations. The day aims to promote equality before the law and in practice throughout all of the member countries of the UN. The day was first celebrated on March 1, 2014, and was launched by <a href="/wiki/UNAIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="UNAIDS">UNAIDS</a> Executive Director Michel Sidib√© on 27 February of that year with a major event in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In February 2017, UNAIDS called on people to "make some noise around zero discrimination, to speak up and prevent discrimination from standing in the way of achieving ambitions, goals and dreams."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The day is particularly noted by organisations like UNAIDS that combat discrimination against people living with HIV/AIDS. "HIV related stigma and discrimination is pervasive and exists in almost every part of the world including our Liberia", according to Dr. Ivan F. Camanor, Chairman of the National AIDS Commission of Liberia.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Programme" title="United Nations Development Programme">UN Development Programme</a> also paid tribute in 2017 to LGBTI people with HIV/AIDS who face discrimination.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Campaigners in India have used this day to speak out against laws making discrimination against the LGBTI community more likely, especially during the previous campaign to repeal the law (<a href="/wiki/Section_377_of_the_Indian_Penal_Code" title="Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code">Indian Penal Code, s377</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>) that used to criminalise homosexuality in that country, before that law was overturned by the Indian Supreme Court in September 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2015, Armenian Americans in California held a 'die-in' on Zero Discrimination Day to remember the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
