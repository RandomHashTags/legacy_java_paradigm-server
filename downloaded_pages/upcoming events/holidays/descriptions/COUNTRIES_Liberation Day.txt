      <p><b>Liberation Day</b> is a day, often a <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holiday</a>, that marks the liberation of a place, similar to an <a href="/wiki/Independence_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence day">independence day</a>. Liberation marks the date of either a <a href="/wiki/Revolution" title="Revolution">revolution</a>, as in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, the fall of an oppressive regime, as in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, or the end of an <a href="/wiki/Military_occupation" title="Military occupation">occupation</a> by another state, as in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, thereby differing from independence in the meaning of <a href="/wiki/Secession" title="Secession">secession</a> from another country. </p>
