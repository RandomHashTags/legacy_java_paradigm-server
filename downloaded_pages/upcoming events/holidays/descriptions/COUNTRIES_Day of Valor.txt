      <p><i><b>Araw ng Kagitingan</b></i> (<abbr title="literal translation">lit.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap"> </span>'Day of Valor'), also known as <b>Bataan Day</b> and <b>Bataan and Corregidor Day</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">national observance</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> which commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bataan#The_Fall" title="Battle of Bataan">fall of Bataan</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. It falls on April 9, although in 2009, its celebration was moved to April 6 to avoid it from coinciding with <a href="/wiki/Maundy_Thursday" title="Maundy Thursday">Maundy Thursday</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pn295_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pn295-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gma_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gma-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_and_date_of_observance">Name and date of observance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bataan_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name and date of observance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In April 1961, <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_the_Philippines" title="Congress of the Philippines">Congress</a> passed Republic Act No. 3022 declaring April 9 of every year as "Bataan Day".<sup id="cite_ref-RA3022_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RA3022-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In June 1987, Executive Order No. 203 revised all national holidays in the Philippines, referring to the April 9 holiday as "Araw ng Kagitingan (Bataan and Corregidor Day)".<sup id="cite_ref-EO203s1987_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EO203s1987-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Less than a month later, another executive order (No. 292) revised the holidays anew, again referring to the April 9 holiday as "Araw ng Kagitingan (Bataan and Corregidor Day)".<sup id="cite_ref-EO292s1987_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EO292s1987-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2007, Congress passed Republic Act No. 9492 putting into law the "Holiday Economics" policy of President <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal-Arroyo" class="mw-redirect" title="Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo">Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo</a>; this put the observance of each holiday, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>, to the Monday nearest it. The order referred to the holiday celebrated on the Monday nearest April 9 as "Araw ng Kagitingan (Bataan and Corregidor Day)".<sup id="cite_ref-RA9492_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RA9492-6">[6]</a></sup> Starting in 2008, the holiday was called simply as "Araw ng Kagitingan", and was celebrated on the nearest Monday.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This practice was repeated in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2010, the holiday was still named as such, but was celebrated on April 9.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Starting with the administration of President <a href="/wiki/Benigno_Aquino_III" title="Benigno Aquino III">Benigno Aquino III</a>, celebrations of the holiday have been observed on April 9, instead of being moved to the nearest Monday, and the holiday has been called simply "Araw ng Kagitingan" in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-PP84s2010_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PP84s2010-10">[10]</a></sup> 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">[11]</a></sup> 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">[12]</a></sup> 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-:2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-13">[13]</a></sup> 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-:3_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-14">[14]</a></sup> and 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The administration of President <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Duterte" title="Rodrigo Duterte">Rodrigo Duterte</a> followed Aquino's naming and dating of the holiday in 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-:5_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-16">[16]</a></sup> 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-:6_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-17">[17]</a></sup> 2019,<sup id="cite_ref-:7_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-18">[18]</a></sup> 2020,<sup id="cite_ref-:8_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-19">[19]</a></sup> and 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-20">[20]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fall_of_Bataan">Fall of Bataan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bataan_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fall of Bataan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bataan" title="Battle of Bataan">Battle of Bataan</a>
      </div> 
      <p>At dawn on April 9, 1942, against the orders of Generals <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_M._Wainwright_(general)" title="Jonathan M. Wainwright (general)">Jonathan Wainwright</a>, the commander of the Luzon Force, <a href="/wiki/Bataan" title="Bataan">Bataan</a>, Major General <a href="/wiki/Edward_P._King,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Edward P. King, Jr.">Edward P. King, Jr.</a>, surrendered more than 76,000 starving and disease-ridden soldiers (64,000 Filipinos, and 12,000 Americans) to <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a> troops.<sup id="cite_ref-Tucker2005_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tucker2005-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The majority of these <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_war" title="Prisoner of war">prisoners of war</a> had their belongings confiscated before being forced to endure the infamous 140-kilometre (87&nbsp;mi) <a href="/wiki/Bataan_Death_March" title="Bataan Death March">Bataan Death March</a> to <a href="/wiki/Camp_O%27Donnell" title="Camp O'Donnell">Camp O'Donnell</a> in <a href="/wiki/Capas,_Tarlac" class="mw-redirect" title="Capas, Tarlac">Capas, Tarlac</a>. En route, thousands died from dehydration, heat prostration, untreated wounds, and <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/wanton#Adjective" class="extiw" title="wikt:wanton">wanton</a> execution while walking in deep dust over vehicle-broken <a href="/wiki/Macadam_road" class="mw-redirect" title="Macadam road">Macadam roads</a>, and crammed into rail cars for transport to captivity.<sup id="cite_ref-lansford-159-160_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lansford-159-160-22">[22]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The few who were lucky enough to travel by truck to <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando,_Pampanga" title="San Fernando, Pampanga">San Fernando, Pampanga</a> would still have to endure more than an additional 25 miles (40&nbsp;km) of marching. Prisoners were beaten randomly and often denied promised food and water. Those who fell behind were usually executed or left to die, with the sides of the roads becoming littered with dead bodies and those moaning for help.<sup id="cite_ref-greenberger_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greenberger-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Only some 54,000 of the 76,000 prisoners reached their destination; the exact death toll is difficult to assess because thousands of captives were able to escape from their guards. Approximately 5,000-10,000 Filipino and 600-650 American prisoners-of-war died before they could reach Camp O'Donnell.<sup id="cite_ref-interaksyon1_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interaksyon1-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Philippines">In the Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bataan_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In the Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The observance usually is centered on <a href="/wiki/Mount_Samat_National_Shrine" title="Mount Samat National Shrine">Mount Samat National Shrine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pilar,_Bataan" title="Pilar, Bataan">Pilar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bataan" title="Bataan">Bataan</a>. It is usually attended by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Philippines" title="President of the Philippines">President of the Philippines</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Bataan" title="Governor of Bataan">Governor of Bataan</a>, the ambassadors of the United States and Japan, and surviving veterans groups. </p>
      <p>By 2021, there were only 2,952 defenders of Bataan who are still alive.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bataan_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>In 2012, the 70th anniversary of the Fall of Bataan was commemorated at <a href="/wiki/Mount_Samat" title="Mount Samat">Mount Samat Shrine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pilar,_Bataan" title="Pilar, Bataan">Pilar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bataan" title="Bataan">Bataan</a> by some of the over 18,000 still-living Filipino veterans. </p>
      <p>Then-incumbent President <a href="/wiki/Benigno_Aquino_III" title="Benigno Aquino III">Benigno S. Aquino III</a> and former President <a href="/wiki/Fidel_V._Ramos" title="Fidel V. Ramos">Fidel V. Ramos</a> attended the rites. Japanese ambassador to the Philippines Toshina Urabe expressed "deep apology and a deep sense of remorse to the tragedy", while the United States Deputy Chief of Mission Leslie A. Bassett (representing U.S. ambassador <a href="/wiki/Harry_K._Thomas_Jr." title="Harry K. Thomas Jr.">Harry K. Thomas Jr.</a>) said that <a href="/wiki/United_States_Embassy_in_Manila" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Embassy in Manila">their embassy</a> has provided a total of US$220 million (over <a href="/wiki/Philippine_peso" title="Philippine peso">₱</a>9 billion) to Filipino war veterans.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_United_States">In the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bataan_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Maywood,_Illinois" title="Maywood, Illinois">Maywood, Illinois</a> the second Sunday in September is remembered as Bataan Day.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Maywood provided <a href="/wiki/Illinois_National_Guard" title="Illinois National Guard">Illinois National Guard</a> soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/192nd_Tank_Battalion" title="192nd Tank Battalion">192nd Tank Battalion</a> who served on Bataan. </p>
