      <p><b><i>Star Wars</i> Day</b>, May<span class="nowrap">&nbsp;</span>4, celebrates <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Media_franchise" class="mw-redirect" title="Media franchise">media franchise</a>. Observance of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_commemorative_days" class="mw-redirect" title="List of commemorative days">commemorative day</a> spread quickly through media and <a href="/wiki/Grassroots" title="Grassroots">grassroots</a> celebrations.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The date was chosen for the <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">pun</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Catchphrase" title="Catchphrase">catchphrase</a> "<a href="/wiki/May_the_Force_be_with_you" class="mw-redirect" title="May the Force be with you">May the Force be with you</a>" as "<em>May the Fourth</em> be with you". Even though the holiday was not created or declared by <a href="/wiki/Lucasfilm" title="Lucasfilm">Lucasfilm</a>, many <i>Star Wars</i> fans across the world have chosen to celebrate the holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It has since been embraced by Lucasfilm and parent company <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> as an annual celebration of <i>Star Wars</i>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Wars_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The first recorded reference was the phrase being first used on May 4, 1979, the day <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Thatcher" title="Margaret Thatcher">Margaret Thatcher</a> took the job as <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. An online news article from the Danish public broadcaster says her political party, the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservatives</a>, placed a congratulatory advertisement in <i><a href="/wiki/London_Evening_News" title="London Evening News">The London Evening News</a></i>, saying "May the Fourth Be with You, Maggie. Congratulations."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The saying was used in a <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">UK Parliament</a> defence debate on May 4, 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-maythefourth_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maythefourth-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Astrophysicist and author <a href="/wiki/Jeanne_Cavelos" title="Jeanne Cavelos">Jeanne Cavelos</a> used the saying on page 94 of her 1999 book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Science_of_Star_Wars_(book)" title="The Science of Star Wars (book)">The Science of Star Wars</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2008, the first Facebook groups appeared, celebrating <i>Luke Skywalker Day,</i> with the same catchphrase.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2011, the first organized celebration of <i>Star Wars</i> Day took place in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> at the Toronto Underground Cinema. Produced by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Ward&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sean Ward (page does not exist)">Sean Ward</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alice_Quinn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alice Quinn (page does not exist)">Alice Quinn</a>, festivities included an Original Trilogy Trivia Game Show; a costume contest with celebrity judges; and the web's best tribute films, mash-ups, parodies, and remixes on the big screen. The second annual edition took place on Friday, May 4, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Fans (even government officials, such as <a href="/wiki/Boris_Johnson" title="Boris Johnson">Boris Johnson</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>) have celebrated <i>Star Wars</i> in a variety of ways in social media and on television. </p>
      <p>Since 2013, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> has officially observed the holiday with several <i>Star Wars</i> events and festivities at <a href="/wiki/Disneyland" title="Disneyland">Disneyland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World" title="Walt Disney World">Walt Disney World</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Disney had purchased <a href="/wiki/Lucasfilm" title="Lucasfilm">Lucasfilm</a>, including the rights to <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i>, in late 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The finale of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)">The Clone Wars</a></i> was made available on <a href="/wiki/Disney%2B" title="Disney+">Disney+</a> on May 4, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Rise_of_Skywalker" title="Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker">Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker</a></i>, as well as an eight-episode documentary series titled <i><a href="/wiki/Disney_Gallery:_The_Mandalorian" class="mw-redirect" title="Disney Gallery: The Mandalorian">Disney Gallery: The Mandalorian</a></i>, were also made available on Disney+ the same day. A new animated series, <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Bad_Batch" title="Star Wars: The Bad Batch"><i>Star Wars: The Bad Batch</i></a> will also premiere on Disney+ on this day in 2021. </p>
      <p>Minor League baseball teams such as the <a href="/wiki/Toledo_Mud_Hens" title="Toledo Mud Hens">Toledo Mud Hens</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Durham_Bulls" title="Durham Bulls">Durham Bulls</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> have worn special uniforms as part of <i>Star Wars</i> Day promotions. </p>
      <p>On <i>Star Wars</i> Day 2015, astronauts in the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> watched <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kluger_2015_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kluger_2015-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Also in 2015, the carillon bells inside the <a href="/wiki/Peace_Tower" title="Peace Tower">Peace Tower</a> on <a href="/wiki/Parliament_Hill" title="Parliament Hill">Parliament Hill</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">Ottawa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> played "<a href="/wiki/The_Imperial_March" title="The Imperial March">The Imperial March</a>" theme from <i>Star Wars</i>, among other space-related tunes.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span id="Revenge_of_the_Fifth.2FSixth"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Revenge_of_the_Fifth/Sixth">Revenge of the Fifth/Sixth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Wars_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Revenge of the Fifth/Sixth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Revenge_of_the_Fifth"></span><span class="anchor" id="Revenge_of_the_Sixth"></span><span class="anchor" id="Revenge_of_the_5th"></span><span class="anchor" id="Revenge_of_the_6th"></span><span class="anchor" id="May_5th"></span><span class="anchor" id="May_6th"></span><span class="anchor" id="May_Fifth"></span><span class="anchor" id="May_Sixth"></span><span class="anchor" id="May_Sith"></span><span class="anchor" id="Revenge_of_the_Sith"></span> Some recognize the following day, May 5, as "<b>Revenge of the Fifth</b>", a play on <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_%E2%80%93_Revenge_of_the_Sith" title="Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith">Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith</a></i> and celebrate the <a href="/wiki/Sith_Lord" class="mw-redirect" title="Sith Lord">Sith Lords</a> and other villainous characters from the <i>Star Wars</i> series rather than the Jedi.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Others celebrate this one day later, on May 6, citing "<b>Revenge of the Sixth</b>" as a better play on "<a href="/wiki/Sith" title="Sith">Sith</a>", while others refer to the 6th as "Return of the 6th" as a play on the trilogy aspect of the <a href="/wiki/Star_wars_films" class="mw-redirect" title="Star wars films"><i>Star Wars</i> films</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="May_25">May 25</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Star_Wars_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: May 25">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_City_Council" title="Los Angeles City Council">Los Angeles City Council</a> declared May 25, 2007, as <i>Star Wars</i> Day, in honor of the 30th anniversary release date of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_(film)" title="Star Wars (film)">Star Wars</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LAStarWarsDay_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAStarWarsDay-24">[24]</a></sup> A separate initiative for observing <a href="/wiki/Geek_Pride_Day" title="Geek Pride Day">Geek Pride Day</a> on May 25 is based on the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> connection along with ties to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hitchhiker%27s_Guide_to_the_Galaxy" title="The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy">The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Towel_Day" title="Towel Day">Towel Day</a>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Discworld" title="Discworld">Discworld</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story" title="Solo: A Star Wars Story">Solo: A Star Wars Story</a></i> was also released on May 25, 41 years after <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i>. </p>
