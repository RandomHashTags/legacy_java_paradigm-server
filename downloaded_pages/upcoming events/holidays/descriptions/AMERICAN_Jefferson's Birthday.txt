      <p><b>Jefferson's Birthday</b> officially honors the birth of the third U.S. president, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a> on April 13, 1743.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This day was recognized by <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> as part of Presidential Proclamation 2276, issued on March 21, 1938.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>More recently, President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> issued proclamation 8124 on April 11, 2007, stating that "... on Thomas Jefferson Day, we commemorate the birthday of a monumental figure whose place in our Nation’s history will always be cherished".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
