      <p><b>Evolution Day</b> is a celebration to commemorate the anniversary of the initial publication of <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Origin_of_Species" title="On the Origin of Species">On the Origin of Species</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> on 24 November 1859.<sup id="cite_ref-TES97_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TES97-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Such celebrations have been held for over a century, but the specific term "Evolution Day" for the anniversary appears to be a <a href="/wiki/Neologism" title="Neologism">neologism</a> which was coined prior to 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-TES97_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TES97-1">[1]</a></sup> By highlighting Darwin's contributions to science, the day's events are used to educate about <a href="/wiki/Evolutionary_biology" title="Evolutionary biology">evolutionary biology</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is similar to the better-known <a href="/wiki/Darwin_Day" title="Darwin Day">Darwin Day</a>, held on the anniversary of his birth (12 February 1809).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It is unrelated to the <a href="/wiki/Secularization" title="Secularization">secularization</a> campaign by the <a href="/wiki/Giordano_Bruno_Foundation" title="Giordano Bruno Foundation">Giordano Bruno Foundation</a> to have the <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Germany" title="Public holidays in Germany">German public holiday</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ascension_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Ascension Day">Ascension Day</a> renamed to "Evolutionstag" (Evolution Day).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commemorations">Commemorations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evolution_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Commemorations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>1909, the 50th anniversary of the publication of <i>On The Origin of Species</i> and the 100th anniversary of Darwin's birth, saw several major events celebrating both. At <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, more than 400 scientists and dignitaries from 167 countries met in a widely reported event of public interest to honour Darwin's contributions and discuss the latest discoveries and ideas related to evolution,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Academy_of_Sciences" title="New York Academy of Sciences">New York Academy of Sciences</a> held a celebration at the <a href="/wiki/American_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="American Museum of Natural History">American Museum of Natural History</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Society of New Zealand">Royal Society of New Zealand</a> held an event with "a very large attendance".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Darwin_Centennial_Celebration_(1959)" title="Darwin Centennial Celebration (1959)">Darwin Centennial Celebration (1959)</a> had a major, well publicised event from 24–28 November at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a>. </p>
      <p>In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> aired <a href="/wiki/BBC_Darwin_Season" title="BBC Darwin Season">BBC Darwin Season</a>, a series of television and radio programs, to celebrate Darwin's bicentenary and the 150th anniversary of the publication of <i>the Origin</i>. </p>
