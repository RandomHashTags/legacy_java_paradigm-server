      <p><b>World Poetry Day</b> is <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#March" title="List of minor secular observances">celebrated</a> on <a href="/wiki/21_March" class="mw-redirect" title="21 March">21 March</a>, and was declared by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> (the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization) in 1999, "with the aim of supporting linguistic diversity through poetic expression and increasing the opportunity for <a href="/wiki/Endangered_language" title="Endangered language">endangered languages</a> to be heard".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its purpose is to promote the reading, writing, publishing and teaching of <a href="/wiki/Poetry" title="Poetry">poetry</a> throughout the world and, as the original UNESCO declaration says, to "give fresh recognition and impetus to national, regional and international poetry movements". </p>
      <p>It was generally celebrated in October, but in the latter part of the 20th century the world community celebrated it on the 15th, the birthday of <a href="/wiki/Virgil" title="Virgil">Virgil</a>, the Roman <a href="/wiki/Epic_poetry" title="Epic poetry">epic poet</a> and poet laureate under <a href="/wiki/Augustan_literature_(ancient_Rome)" title="Augustan literature (ancient Rome)">Augustus</a>. The tradition to keep an October date for national or international poetry day celebrations still holds in many countries.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The United Kingdom generally uses the first Thursday in October,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> but elsewhere a different October, or even sometimes a November date, is celebrated. </p>
      <p>The 2021 World Poetry Day in the UNESCO headquarters in Paris was dedicated to the celebration of the 100th anniversary of the birth of the great Macedonian poet, writer, <a href="/wiki/Literary_translator" class="mw-redirect" title="Literary translator">literary translator</a> and linguistic scholar <a href="/wiki/Bla%C5%BEe_Koneski" title="Blaže Koneski">Blaže Koneski</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At the same time, the British poet <a href="/wiki/Carol_Ann_Duffy" title="Carol Ann Duffy">Carol Ann Duffy</a> was announced as the recipient of the Golden Wreath Award of the <a href="/wiki/Struga_Poetry_Evenings" title="Struga Poetry Evenings">Struga Poetry Evenings</a> for 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
