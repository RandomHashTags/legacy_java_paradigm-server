      <p><b>International Dance Day</b> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#April" title="List of minor secular observances">global celebration</a> of dance, created by the Dance Committee of the <a href="/wiki/International_Theatre_Institute" title="International Theatre Institute">International Theatre Institute</a> (ITI), the main partner for the performing arts of <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>. The event takes place every year on 29 April, which is the anniversary of the birth of <a href="/wiki/Jean-Georges_Noverre" title="Jean-Georges Noverre">Jean-Georges Noverre</a> (1727–1810), the creator of modern ballet. The day strives to encourage participation and education in dance through events and festivals held on the date all over the world. <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> formally recognize ITI to be the creators and organizers of the event.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About">About</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Dance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: About">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Every year, since its creation in 1982, an outstanding dance personality is selected to write a message for International Dance Day. ITI also create a flagship event in a selected host city, at which there are dance performances, educational workshops, humanitarian projects and speeches made by ambassadors, dignitaries, dance personalities and the selected Message Author for that year.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The day is a celebration day for those who can see the value and importance of the art form <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dance</a>, and acts as a wake-up-call for governments, politicians and institutions which have not yet recognised its value to the people. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Event">Event</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Dance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Event">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>To mark International Dance Day each year, on 29 April, the International Theatre Institute invites its members along with dancers, choreographers, dance students and enthusiasts to join them in a Gala Celebration. </p> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:ContemporaryBalletLeap.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/ContemporaryBalletLeap.jpg/220px-ContemporaryBalletLeap.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="279" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/ContemporaryBalletLeap.jpg/330px-ContemporaryBalletLeap.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/ContemporaryBalletLeap.jpg/440px-ContemporaryBalletLeap.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1377" data-file-height="1745"></a>

         </div>Ballet dancer on stage
        </div>

      <p>The Gala Celebration takes place in a chosen host city decided on by the Executive Council of the International Theatre Institute – for instance in 2017 it was held in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, and in 2018 it was in <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The programme of the Gala Event can vary, but usually consists of top-quality dance performances from around the world, student performances, keynote speeches, and a recital of the Message for that year’s event read, in person, by that years selected message author. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> 2017 for instance,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the event became a three-day celebration with greater emphasis on education through dance workshops and presentations led by an international cast of dance experts. The evenings were reserved for performances, the finale of which was the Gala Celebration. The Shanghai event also had a humanitarian aspect, with much of its focus being on celebrating the achievements of disabled dancers and encouraging disabled children to dance.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Beyond the Gala itself, ITI Centres across the globe are encouraged to mark 29 April in their own country through special educational initiatives, humanitarian drives, dance performances and festivals.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Message_Authors">Message Authors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Dance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Message Authors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>To help publicise International Dance Day each year the International Theatre Institute selects an outstanding personality from the world of dance to be the Message Author for the event. In the message, it is hoped that the author can underline the relevance and power of dance. Past authors have included <a href="/wiki/Trisha_Brown" title="Trisha Brown">Trisha Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Alonso" title="Alicia Alonso">Alicia Alonso</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Merce_Cunningham" title="Merce Cunningham">Merce Cunningham</a>. </p>
      <p>As 2018 marked the 70th Anniversary of ITI, 5 Message Authors were selected for the 2018 event, one from each of the 5 <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> Regions. The 5 authors were; <a href="/w/index.php?title=Georgette_Gebara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Georgette Gebara (page does not exist)">Georgette Gebara</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arab</a> Countries), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salia_Sanou&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Salia Sanou (page does not exist)">Salia Sanou</a> (<a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marianela_Boan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marianela Boan (page does not exist)">Marianela Boan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>), <a href="/wiki/Willy_Tsao" title="Willy Tsao">Willy Tsao</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">Asia-Pacific</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Ohad_Naharin" title="Ohad Naharin">Ohad Naharin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>). </p>
