      <p><b>World Blood Donor Day</b> (<b>WBDD</b>) is held on <a href="/wiki/June_14" title="June 14">June 14</a> each year. The event was organised for the first time in 2005, by a joint initiative of the World Health Organization, the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Societies" title="International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies">International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies</a> to raise awareness of the need for safe <a href="/wiki/Blood" title="Blood">blood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blood_products" class="mw-redirect" title="Blood products">blood products</a>, and to thank <a href="/wiki/Blood_donation" title="Blood donation">blood donors</a> for their <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_action" title="Voluntary action">voluntary</a>, life-saving gifts of blood. World Blood Donor Day is one of 11 official <a href="/wiki/Global_health" title="Global health">global public health</a> campaigns marked by the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (WHO), along with <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Day" title="World Health Day">World Health Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Chagas_Disease_Day" title="World Chagas Disease Day">World Chagas Disease Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Tuberculosis_Day" title="World Tuberculosis Day">World Tuberculosis Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Immunization_Week" title="World Immunization Week">World Immunization Week</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Patient_Safety_Day" title="World Patient Safety Day">World Patient Safety Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Malaria_Day" title="World Malaria Day">World Malaria Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_No_Tobacco_Day" title="World No Tobacco Day">World No Tobacco Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Hepatitis_Day" title="World Hepatitis Day">World Hepatitis Day</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Antimicrobial_Awareness_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World Antimicrobial Awareness Week (page does not exist)">World Antimicrobial Awareness Week</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_AIDS_Day" title="World AIDS Day">World AIDS Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Blood_donation_needle.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Blood_donation_needle.jpg/300px-Blood_donation_needle.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="100" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Blood_donation_needle.jpg/450px-Blood_donation_needle.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Blood_donation_needle.jpg/600px-Blood_donation_needle.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="640"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Blood_donation_needle.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>A donor's arm at various stages of donation. The two photographs on the left show a blood pressure cuff being used as a tourniquet.
        </div>

      </div> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Transfusion_of_blood" class="mw-redirect" title="Transfusion of blood">Transfusion of blood</a> and blood products helps and save millions of lives every year. It can help patients who suffering from life-threatening conditions live longer and with higher <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a>, and supports complex medical and surgical procedures. It also has an essential, life-saving role in maternal and <a href="/wiki/Perinatal" class="mw-redirect" title="Perinatal">perinatal care</a>. Access to safe and sufficient blood and blood products can help reduce rates of death and disability due to severe bleeding during delivery and after childbirth.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In many countries, there is not an adequate supply of safe blood, and blood services face the challenge of making sufficient blood available, while also ensuring its quality and safety.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>An adequate supply can only be assured through regular donations by voluntary unpaid blood donors. The WHO's goal is for all countries to obtain all their blood supplies from voluntary unpaid donors by 2020. In 2014, 60 countries have their national blood supplies based on 99-100% voluntary unpaid blood donations, with 73 countries still largely dependent on family and paid donors.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>World Blood Donor Day is celebrated every year by people around the world on June 14. It is celebrated on the birthday anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Karl_Landsteiner" title="Karl Landsteiner">Karl Landsteiner</a> on June 14, 1868.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Landsteiner was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> for his discovery of the ABO blood group system.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Importance">Importance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Importance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Blood is a necessary resource for the planned treatments and the urgent interventions. It is helpful for patients who are suffering from life-threatening conditions for living longer and with a higher quality of life. It supports complex medical and surgical procedures.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020:Safe_Blood_Saves_Lives">2020:Safe Blood Saves Lives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2020:Safe Blood Saves Lives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for Blood Donation Day 2020 was 'Safe Blood Saves Lives'. The Slogan of "Blood Donation Day 2020" was 'Give Blood And Make The World A Healthier Place'<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>.This year WHO announced a virtual rally for Covid-19 pandemic.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019:_Safe_Blood_for_All">2019: Safe Blood for All</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2019: Safe Blood for All">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for Blood Donation Day in 2019 was 'Safe Blood For All'.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2018:_Be_there_for_someone_else._Give_blood._Share_life">2018: Be there for someone else. Give blood. Share life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2018: Be there for someone else. Give blood. Share life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for Blood Donation Day in 2018 was "Be there for someone else. Give blood. Share life."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017:_Give_Blood._Give_Now._Give_Often">2017: Give Blood. Give Now. Give Often</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2017: Give Blood. Give Now. Give Often">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for Blood Donation Day in 2017 focused on blood donation in emergency situations. The first response when an emergency happens is "What Can You Do?" and the answer is "Give Blood. Give Now. Give Often" which is taken by World Health Organization.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016:_Blood_connects_us_all">2016: Blood connects us all</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2016: Blood connects us all">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme of the World Blood Donor Day, blood connects us all, highlights the notions of "sharing" and "connection" amongst blood donors and patients. The WBDD 2016 focused on thanking blood donors while emphasizing the role of the voluntary donation. The 2016 campaign brought to light the stories of the different people saved through blood donation to promote awareness and inspire people to donate.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The host country of WBDD 2016 was The Netherlands.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">Williem-Alexander</a>, the king of the Netherlands, opened the ceremony by thanking donors.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Thank_you_for_saving_my_life">2015: Thank you for saving my life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2015: Thank you for saving my life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>It emphasizes thanking of blood donors who save lives every day through their blood donations and inspires more people all over the world to donate blood voluntarily and regularly with the slogan “Give freely, give often". Blood donation matters. This year campaign pays attention to stories from people whose lives have been saved through blood donation. Activities include memorable events, meetings, publication of relevant stories on media, scientific conferences, publication of articles on national, regional and international scientific journals, and other activities that would help in encouraging the title of this year's World Blood Donor Day.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The host country for World Blood Donor Day 2015 is China through its blood center in Shanghai,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Shanghai Blood Centre, also the <a href="/wiki/WHO" class="mw-redirect" title="WHO">WHO</a> Collaborating Center for Blood Transfusion Services.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_Safe_blood_for_saving_mothers">2014: Safe blood for saving mothers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2014: Safe blood for saving mothers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The focus of the WBDD 2014 campaign was “Safe blood for saving mothers”. The goal of the campaign was to increase awareness about why timely access to safe blood and blood products is essential for all countries, as part of a comprehensive approach to prevent maternal deaths. </p>
      <p>According to the World Health Organization, 800 women die every day from pregnancy and childbirth-related complications.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Severe bleeding is the cause of 34% of maternal deaths in Africa, 31% in Asia and 21% in Latin America and the Caribbean. </p>
      <p>The global host for the WBDD 2014 event was <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Through its <a href="/wiki/Blood_transfusion_in_Sri_Lanka" title="Blood transfusion in Sri Lanka">national blood transfusion service</a>, Sri Lanka promotes voluntary unpaid donation to increase access to safe and sufficient blood and blood products. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Give_the_gift_of_life_:_donate_blood">2013: Give the gift of life&nbsp;: donate blood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2013: Give the gift of life&nbsp;: donate blood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The focus for the WBDD 2013 campaign – which marked the 10th anniversary of World Blood Donor Day – was blood donation as a gift that saves lives. The WHO encouraged all countries to highlight stories from people whose lives have been saved through blood donation, as a way of motivating regular blood donors to continue giving blood and people in good health who have never given blood, particularly young people, to begin doing so. </p>
      <p>The host country for World Blood Donor Day 2013 was <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Through its national blood service, the Etablissement Français du Sang (EFS), France has been promoting voluntary non-remunerated blood donation since the 1950s.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_Every_blood_donor_is_a_hero">2012: Every blood donor is a hero</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Blood_Donor_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2012: Every blood donor is a hero">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The 2012 campaign focused on the idea that any person can become a hero by giving blood. Blood cannot yet be <a href="/wiki/Artificial_blood" class="mw-redirect" title="Artificial blood">manufactured artificially</a>, so voluntary blood donation remains vital for healthcare worldwide. Many anonymous blood donors save lives every day through their blood donations. </p>
