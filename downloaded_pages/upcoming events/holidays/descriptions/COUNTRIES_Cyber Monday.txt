      <p><b>Cyber Monday</b> is a marketing term for e-commerce transactions on the Monday after <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving_(United_States)" title="Thanksgiving (United States)">Thanksgiving</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It was created by retailers to encourage people to <a href="/wiki/Online_shopping" title="Online shopping">shop online</a>. The term was coined by <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Davis_(businesswoman)" title="Ellen Davis (businesswoman)">Ellen Davis</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Retail_Federation" title="National Retail Federation">National Retail Federation</a> and Scott Silverman, and made its debut on November 28, 2005, in a <a href="/wiki/Shop.org" class="mw-redirect" title="Shop.org">Shop.org</a> press release entitled <span style="padding-right:.15em;">"</span>'Cyber Monday' Quickly Becoming One of the Biggest Online Shopping Days of the Year".<sup id="cite_ref-Cyber_Monday_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cyber_Monday-1">[1]</a></sup> Cyber Monday takes place the Monday after Thanksgiving; the date falls between <a href="/wiki/November_26" title="November 26">November 26</a> and <a href="/wiki/December_2" title="December 2">December 2</a>, depending on the year. </p>
      <p>According to the Shop.org/<a href="/wiki/Bizrate" class="mw-redirect" title="Bizrate">Bizrate</a> Research 2005 eHoliday Mood Study, "77 percent of online retailers said that their sales increased substantially on the Monday after Thanksgiving, a trend that is driving serious online discounts and promotions on Cyber Monday this year (2005)". </p>
      <p>In 2017, Cyber Monday online sales grew to a record of $6.59 billion, compared with $2.98 billion in 2015, and $2.65 billion in 2014. However, the average order value was $128, down slightly from 2014's $160.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Cyber Monday has become the online equivalent to <a href="/wiki/Black_Friday_(shopping)" title="Black Friday (shopping)">Black Friday</a> and offers a way for smaller retail websites to compete with larger chains.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Since its inception, it has become an international marketing term used by online retailers across the world. </p>
      <p>The Cyber Monday on November 30, 2020 was the biggest online shopping day in U.S. history with a total of $10.7 billion in online spending.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The term "Cyber Monday" was coined by <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Davis_(businesswoman)" title="Ellen Davis (businesswoman)">Ellen Davis</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and was first used within the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_commerce" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic commerce">ecommerce</a> community during the 2005 holiday season.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> According to Scott Silverman, the head of Shop.org, the term was coined based on 2004 research showing "one of the biggest online shopping days of the year" was the Monday after Thanksgiving (12th-biggest day historically).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Retailers also noted the most significant shopping period was December 5 through 15 of the previous year. In late November 2005, <i>The New York Times</i> reported: "The name Cyber Monday grew out of the observation that millions of otherwise productive working Americans, fresh off a Thanksgiving weekend of window shopping, were returning to high-speed Internet connections at work Monday and buying what they liked."<sup id="cite_ref-Online_sales_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Online_sales-9">[9]</a></sup> At the time, a lot of people had slow Internet at home.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The idea for having such a holiday was created by Tony Valado, in 2003 while working at <a href="/wiki/1800Flowers.com" class="mw-redirect" title="1800Flowers.com">1800Flowers.com</a>, and coined "White Wednesday" to be the day before Thanksgiving for online retailers.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/ComScore" class="mw-redirect" title="ComScore">comScore</a> reported that online spending on Cyber Monday jumped 25% to $608 million,<sup id="cite_ref-Spending_Beats_Forecast_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spending_Beats_Forecast-12">[12]</a></sup> 21% to $733 million in 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-Strong_Week_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strong_Week-13">[13]</a></sup> and 15% to $846 million in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Spending_Jumps_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spending_Jumps-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2009, comScore reported that online spending increased 5 percent on Cyber Monday to $887 million and that more than half of dollars spent online at US Web sites originated from work computers (52.7 percent), representing a gain of 2.3 percentage points from last year.<sup id="cite_ref-Heaviest_Online_Spending_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heaviest_Online_Spending-15">[15]</a></sup> Buying from home comprised the majority of the remaining share (41.6 percent) while buying from international locations accounted for 5.8 percent. According to comScore chairman Gian Fulgoni, "comScore data have shown that Cyber Monday online sales have always been driven by considerable buying activity from work locations. That pattern hasn't changed. After returning from the long Thanksgiving weekend with a lot of holiday shopping still ahead of them, many consumers tend to continue their holiday shopping from work. Whether to take advantage of the extensive Cyber Monday deals offered by retailers or to buy gifts away from the prying eyes of family members, this day has become an annual ritual for America's online holiday shoppers."<sup id="cite_ref-Heaviest_Online_Spending_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heaviest_Online_Spending-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2010, comScore reported the first-ever $1 billion online shopping day ($1028M), an increase of 16 percent over 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2011, comScore reported that Cyber Week saw US consumers spend over $6 billion online from November 28 to December 2.<sup id="cite_ref-post_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-post-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2012, comScore reported that Cyber Monday saw a 16% increase in sales from 2011, totaling $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-2012_Prediction_Results_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2012_Prediction_Results-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2013, Cyber Monday sales continued their growth and recorded their highest grossing day ever at $2.29 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2014, the average planned expenditure was $361 per person. 46 percent of people expect to pay with credit cards and 43 percent expect to pay with debit cards.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Sales are up 8.1%, according to IBM Digital Analytics. The average order is $131.66, flat with last year, though the number of transactions is up and people are buying more items on average per order.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2016, according to Adobe Digital Insights,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Cyber Monday hit a new record with $3.45 billion, and which was the first time that online sales in one single day surpassed $3 billion in US history. The numbers went up 12.1% from the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2018, according to Adobe Analytics Cyber Monday hits a record $7.9 billion of online spending which is a 19.3% increase from a year ago.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2019, according to Adobe Analytics, Cyber Monday mobile transactions totaled $3.1 billion with total online sales reaching a record $9.4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> </p>

      <p><br> U.S. employers have been cracking down on employees using company equipment and company time for non-work-related purposes, including Cyber Monday. As of November 2011, 22% of employers had fired an employee for using the Internet for non-work related activity; 7% of human resource managers surveyed had fired an employee for holiday shopping; and 54% of employers were blocking employees from accessing certain websites.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> According to CareerBuilder's annual Cyber Monday survey, more than half of workers (53%) say they spend at least some work time holiday shopping on the Internet, up 3% from 2015. Of this group, 43% spend an hour or more doing so, compared to 42% from 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Argentina">Argentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Argentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>According to Argentine press, Cyber Monday was celebrated on November 11, 2014, and marked a tenfold growth in users taking advantage of online sales over the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Cyber Monday came to Canada in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Star_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Star-32">[32]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/National_Post" title="National Post">National Post</a></i> featured an article published on November 25, 2010, stating that the parity of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">Canadian dollar</a> with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US dollar</a> caused many Canadian retailers to have <a href="/wiki/Black_Friday_(shopping)" title="Black Friday (shopping)">Black Friday</a> and Cyber Monday sales of their own. According to the article, an estimated 80% of Canadians were expected to participate in Black Friday and Cyber Monday sales.<sup id="cite_ref-arts.nationalpost.com_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arts.nationalpost.com-33">[33]</a></sup> Speculation has been made that with all major US television broadcasters—which are typically available to Canadians—emphasizing Black Friday and Cyber Monday sales for stores that are also doing business in Canada, Canadian retailers needed to mimic sales offerings in order to keep Canadian dollars from being spent in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-arts.nationalpost.com_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arts.nationalpost.com-33">[33]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>By 2011, around 80% of online retailers in Canada were participating in Cyber Monday.<sup id="cite_ref-Star_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Star-32">[32]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chile">Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Chile's first Cyber Monday took place on November 28, 2011. The companies participating in the event are participants in the Santiago Chamber of Commerce's Electronic Commerce Committee.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In 2015 the Chilean Cyber Monday had 85 stores participating, 390.000 transactions and US$83 million in sales. 36% of that was mobile.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In 2016 Cyber Monday will be held on November 7. 140 companies are registered as official partners. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colombia">Colombia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Colombia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The first Cyber Monday in Colombia took place on November 26, 2012. It was organized by the Colombian Chamber of Electronic Commerce and sponsored by the Ministry of IT and Telecommunications.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/E-commerce_in_India" title="E-commerce in India">E-commerce in India</a>
      </div> 
      <p>India got its own version of the Cyber Monday (<a href="/wiki/Great_Online_Shopping_Festival" title="Great Online Shopping Festival">Great Online Shopping Festival</a>) on December 12, 2012 when <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> India partnered with many e-commerce companies including <a href="/wiki/Flipkart" title="Flipkart">Flipkart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snapdeal" title="Snapdeal">Snapdeal</a>, <a href="/wiki/HomeShop18" class="mw-redirect" title="HomeShop18">HomeShop18</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiatimes_shopping" title="Indiatimes shopping">Indiatimes shopping</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MakeMyTrip" title="MakeMyTrip">MakeMyTrip</a>. Google said that this was the first time an industry-wide initiative of this scale was undertaken.<sup id="cite_ref-Google_partners_e-commerce_sites_for_'Cyber_Monday'_on_December_12_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Google_partners_e-commerce_sites_for_'Cyber_Monday'_on_December_12-37">[37]</a></sup> In November 2015, Google announced that the event would not be repeated.<sup id="cite_ref-Google_logs_out_of_its_flagship_Online_Shopping_Festival_GOSF_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Google_logs_out_of_its_flagship_Online_Shopping_Festival_GOSF-38">[38]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Amazon.co.jp announced it registered as Cyber Monday with Japan Anniversary Association in 2012. Amazon.co.jp ran the Cyber Monday Seven Day Sale from Dec 10 through December 16, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Amazon.co.jp_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon.co.jp-39">[39]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Belgium">Belgium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Belgium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Belgium, Cyber Monday has gained popularity since 2016. Due to efforts off several major online shops launching large Cyber Monday campaigns, the average revenue during Cyber Monday has increased with 50% compared to the 2015 edition.<sup id="cite_ref-Cyber_Monday_België_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cyber_Monday_België-40">[40]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-Black_Friday_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Black_Friday-41">[41]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Inspired by the U.S. phenomenon, the term Cyber Monday was first used in France in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-France_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-France-42">[42]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Amazon.de announced that it brought Cyber Monday to Germany in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Amazon.de_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon.de-43">[43]</a></sup> As of 2014, amazon.de continues to advertise Cyber Monday and has extended it to an 8-day period ("Cyber Monday week") beginning on the Monday before <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving" title="Thanksgiving">Thanksgiving</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands">Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In the Netherlands, the term Cyber Monday was first used in 2012. Since then, Dutch online retailers have taken advantage of Cyber Monday in promotional purposes, because it is perfectly timed before the celebration of <a href="/wiki/Sinterklaas" title="Sinterklaas">Sinterklaas</a> which is celebrated by buying gifts for each other in Netherlands.<sup id="cite_ref-Cyber_Monday_Nederland_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cyber_Monday_Nederland-44">[44]</a></sup> Since 2012, the popularity of Cyber Monday has grown strongly every year in the Netherlands. In total there were 105 participating online stores during the Cyber Monday period of 2017. Last year, during Cyber Monday 2019 the total of shops increased with 40 to 189 shops in total <sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Portugal">Portugal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Portugal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Portugal, the term Cyber Monday was first used in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Lojas_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lojas-46">[46]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Romania">Romania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Romania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Romania, Cyber Monday is the first Monday after Black Friday, which is held by the biggest retailers one week before the US Black Friday. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Sweden Cyber Monday is growing rapidly and several of the largest online retailers regularly launch Cyber Monday campaigns.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Cyber Monday was first established on larger online retailers in Sweden 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>According to a 2009 <i>The Guardian</i> article, UK online retailers are now referring to "Cyber Monday" as the busiest internet shopping day of the year that commonly falls on the same day as the US Cyber Monday.<sup id="cite_ref-Amazon_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon-49">[49]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Beginning at 7&nbsp;pm <a href="/wiki/AEDT" class="mw-redirect" title="AEDT">AEDT</a> on November 20 in 2012, Australian online retailers decided to hold a similar event for the first time, dubbed "Click Frenzy". Many websites immediately crashed, went offline, or had major server issues, including the Click Frenzy promotion website. David Jones, a major retailer, ran a competing sale dubbed 'Christmas Frenzy' on the same date. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyber_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Online retailer Belly Beyond held the first Cyber Monday Sale in New Zealand on November 29, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-History_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-50">[50]</a></sup> The sale lasted for five days, from Monday to Friday. </p>
