      <p><b>Darwin Day</b> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#February" title="List of minor secular observances">celebration</a> to <a href="/wiki/Memorialization" title="Memorialization">commemorate</a> the <a href="/wiki/Birthday" title="Birthday">birthday</a> of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> on 12 February 1809. The day is used to highlight Darwin's contributions to <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> and to promote science in general. Darwin Day is celebrated around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darwin_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The celebration of Darwin's work and tributes to his life have been organised sporadically since his death on 19 April 1882, at age 73. Events took place at <a href="/wiki/Down_House" title="Down House">Down House</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Downe" title="Downe">Downe</a> on the southern outskirts of London where Darwin and members of his family lived from 1842 until the death of his wife, <a href="/wiki/Emma_Darwin" title="Emma Darwin">Emma Darwin</a>, in 1896.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1909, more than 400 scientists and dignitaries from 167 countries met in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a> to honour Darwin's contributions and to discuss vigorously the recent discoveries and related theories contesting for acceptance. This was a widely reported event of public interest.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Also in 1909, on 12 February, the 100th birth anniversary of Darwin and the 50th anniversary of the publication of <i><a href="/wiki/On_The_Origin_of_Species" class="mw-redirect" title="On The Origin of Species">On The Origin of Species</a></i> were celebrated by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Academy_of_Sciences" title="New York Academy of Sciences">New York Academy of Sciences</a> at the <a href="/wiki/American_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="American Museum of Natural History">American Museum of Natural History</a>. A bronze bust of Darwin was unveiled.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 2 June 1909 the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Society of New Zealand">Royal Society of New Zealand</a> held a "Darwin Celebration". "There was a very large attendance."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On 24–28 November 1959, The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a> held a major celebration of Darwin and the publication of <i>On the Origin of Species</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> the largest event of the <a href="/wiki/Darwin_Centennial_Celebration_(1959)" title="Darwin Centennial Celebration (1959)">Darwin Centennial Celebration</a>. Scientists and academics sometimes celebrated 12 February with "<a href="/wiki/Phylum" title="Phylum">Phylum</a> Feast" events—a meal with foods from as many different phyla as they could manage, at least as early as 1972, 1974, and 1989 in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the United States, <a href="/wiki/Salem_State_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Salem State College">Salem State College</a> in Massachusetts has held a "Darwin Festival"<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> annually since 1980,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and in 2005, registered "Darwin Festival" as a service mark with the US Patent and Trademark Office.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Humanist Community<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto" class="mw-redirect" title="Palo Alto">Palo Alto</a>, California, was motivated by Dr. Robert Stephens in late 1993 to begin planning for an annual Darwin Day celebration. Its first public Darwin Day event was a lecture by Dr. <a href="/wiki/Donald_Johanson" title="Donald Johanson">Donald Johanson</a> (discoverer of the early hominid "Lucy"), sponsored by the Stanford Humanists student group<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and the Humanist Community on 22 April 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The Humanist Community continues its annual celebration.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Independently, in 1997, Professor <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Pigliucci" title="Massimo Pigliucci">Massimo Pigliucci</a> initiated an annual Darwin Day event at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tennessee" title="University of Tennessee">University of Tennessee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The event included public lectures and activities as well as a teachers' workshop meant to help elementary and secondary school teachers better understand evolution and how to communicate it to their students, as well as how to deal with the pressures often placed on them by the <a href="/wiki/Creationism" title="Creationism">creationism</a> movement.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>

      <p>2009 was the 200th anniversary of Darwin's birth and it also marked the 150th anniversary of the publication of Darwin's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Origin_of_Species" class="mw-redirect" title="The Origin of Species">On the Origin of Species</a></i>. Events were planned, with the most prominent celebrations in <a href="/wiki/Shrewsbury" title="Shrewsbury">Shrewsbury</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/Natural_History_Museum,_London" title="Natural History Museum, London">Natural History Museum</a> in London.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>Darwin's <i>alma mater</i>, <a href="/wiki/Christ%27s_College,_Cambridge" title="Christ's College, Cambridge">Christ's College, Cambridge</a>, commemorated the bicentenary with the unveiling of a life-sized bronze statue of the <i>Young Darwin</i>, sculpted by their graduate <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Smith_(sculptor)" title="Anthony Smith (sculptor)">Anthony Smith</a>. Prince Philip (then-Chancellor of the University) unveiled the statue and it was later shortlisted for the Marsh Award for Excellence in Public Sculpture 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The same year, two well known evolutionary biologists, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dawkins" title="Richard Dawkins">Richard Dawkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Coyne" title="Jerry Coyne">Jerry Coyne</a>, published book length treatments covering the <a href="/wiki/Evidence_of_common_descent" title="Evidence of common descent">evidence for evolution</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Perth_Mint" title="Perth Mint">Perth Mint</a>, Australia launched a 2009 dated commemorative 1-ounce silver legal tender coin depicting Darwin, young and old; HMS <i>Beagle</i>; and Darwin's signature.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later">Later</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darwin_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 9 February 2011, <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">California Representative</a> <a href="/wiki/Pete_Stark" title="Pete Stark">Pete Stark</a> introduced H. Res 81 to Congress designating 12 February 2011 as Darwin Day, calling Darwin "a worthy symbol of scientific advancement... and around which to build a global celebration of science and humanity."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The resolution was a culmination of collaboration between Stark and the American Humanist Association, which had awarded Stark the Humanist of the Year award in 2008. In a statement on the House floor, Rep. Stark said, "Darwin's birthday is a good time for us to reflect on the important role of science in our society." In a press release from the American Humanist Association, executive director Roy Speckhardt said, "Stark's Darwin Day resolution is a thrilling step forward for the secular movement. Not only is this an opportunity to bring the scientific impact of Charles Darwin to the forefront, but this also signifies the potential for greater respect for scientific reasoning on Capitol Hill."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On 22 January 2013, <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">New Jersey Representative</a> <a href="/wiki/Rush_D._Holt,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Rush D. Holt, Jr.">Rush D. Holt, Jr.</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Quaker_Christian" class="mw-redirect" title="Quaker Christian">Quaker Christian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_physicist" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuclear physicist">nuclear physicist</a>, introduced a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress#Bills_and_resolutions" title="United States Congress">resolution</a> to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> designating 12 February 2013 (Charles Darwin's 204th birthday) as "Darwin Day" to recognise "the importance of sciences in the betterment of humanity".<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-20130201_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20130201-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2015, Delaware's governor <a href="/wiki/Jack_Markell" title="Jack Markell">Jack Markell</a> declared 12 February "Charles Darwin Day", making Delaware the first state in America to formally mark the occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>House Resolution 67, introduced by Representative <a href="/wiki/Jim_Himes" title="Jim Himes">Jim Himes</a> in the United States House of Representatives on 2 February 2015 would designate 12 February as Darwin Day in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-24">[24]</a></sup> It would recognise Darwin as "a worthy symbol on which to focus...a global celebration of science and humanity."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-24">[24]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Darwin_Day_Program_and_Darwin_Day_Celebration">Darwin Day Program and Darwin Day Celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darwin_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Darwin Day Program and Darwin Day Celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In the late 1990s, two Darwin enthusiasts, Amanda Chesworth and Robert Stephens, co-founded an unofficial effort to promote Darwin Day. In 2001, Chesworth moved to New Mexico and incorporated the "Darwin Day Program".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Stephens became chairman of the board and President of this nonprofit corporation with <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Pigliucci" title="Massimo Pigliucci">Massimo Pigliucci</a> as Vice-President and Amanda Chesworth as member of the Board, Secretary, and Executive Director. Stephens presented the objectives of the organisation in an article titled "Darwin Day An International Celebration."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2002, Chesworth compiled and edited a substantial book entitled <i>Darwin Day Collection One: the Single Best Idea, Ever</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The objectives of the book were to show the multidisciplinary reach of Charles Darwin and to meld academic work with popular culture.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>In 2004, the New Mexico corporation was dissolved and all its assets assigned to the "Darwin Day Celebration", a <a href="/wiki/501(c)(3)" class="mw-redirect" title="501(c)(3)">non-profit organisation</a> incorporated in California in 2004<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> by Dr. Robert Stephens and others<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and the Mission Statement was expanded.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Darwin Day Celebration redesigned the website, from a static presentation of information about the Darwin Day Program to a combination of education about Darwin and the Darwin Day Celebration organisation, including automated registration and publication of planned and past celebratory Events and the automated registration of people who want to receive emailings or make public declaration of support for Darwin Day. The website is now operated by the International Darwin Day Foundation, an autonomous program of the <a href="/wiki/American_Humanist_Association" title="American Humanist Association">American Humanist Association</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>Darwin Day is also celebrated by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a>. The event is co-sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Franklin_College_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Franklin College of Arts and Sciences">Franklin College of Arts and Sciences</a>, Division of Biological Sciences, <a href="/wiki/Odum_School_of_Ecology" title="Odum School of Ecology">Odum School of Ecology</a> and the departments of cellular biology, plant biology, and genetics.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Mark Farmer, a professor and division chair of biological sciences and organiser of Darwin Day at UGA. Farmer said he got the idea from the International Darwin Day Foundation and brought the event to UGA in 2009 in time for the 150th anniversary of the publication of "Origin of Species" and the 200th anniversary of Darwin's birth. The University celebrates the impact that Darwin's work had on the scientific community through a series of lectures around campus.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Ethical Humanist Society of Long Island celebrates with programs that appeal to children and adults both.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Clergy_Letter_Project" title="Clergy Letter Project">Clergy Letter Project</a> encourages evolution-affirming churches to participate in Evolution Weekend, on the Sunday closest to this date, to learn about and discuss evolution.<sup id="cite_ref-Weekend_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weekend-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ns_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ns-35">[35]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Darwin Day and Darwin week is also celebrated at Southern Illinois University,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and has been celebrated annually since 2006 when it commenced with a talk by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Berra" title="Tim Berra">Tim Berra</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darwin_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Various events are conducted on Darwin Day around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> They have included dinner parties with special recipes for <a href="/wiki/Primordial_soup" title="Primordial soup">primordial soup</a> and other inventive dishes, protests with <a href="/wiki/School_boards" class="mw-redirect" title="School boards">school boards</a> and other governmental bodies, workshops and symposia, distribution of information by people in ape costumes, lectures and debates, essay and art competitions, concerts, poetry readings, plays, artwork, comedy routines, re-enactments of the <a href="/wiki/Scopes_Trial" title="Scopes Trial">Scopes Trial</a> and of the debate between <a href="/wiki/Thomas_H._Huxley" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas H. Huxley">Thomas H. Huxley</a> and Bishop <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Wilberforce" title="Samuel Wilberforce">Samuel Wilberforce</a>, library displays, museum exhibits, travel and educational tours, recreations of the journey of <a href="/wiki/HMS_Beagle" title="HMS Beagle">HMS&nbsp;<i>Beagle</i></a>, church sermons, movie nights, outreach, and nature hikes. The Darwin Day Celebration Web site offers free registration and display<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> of all Darwin Day events. Some celebrants also combine Darwin Day with a celebration of <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>, who was also born on 12 February 1809. Still others celebrate the many noted individuals that influenced or were influenced by Darwin's work, such as <a href="/wiki/Thomas_H._Huxley" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas H. Huxley">Thomas H. Huxley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lyell" title="Charles Lyell">Charles Lyell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Russel_Wallace" title="Alfred Russel Wallace">Alfred Russel Wallace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Sagan" title="Carl Sagan">Carl Sagan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Mayr" title="Ernst Mayr">Ernst Mayr</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters">Supporters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darwin_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Supporters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Support for Darwin Day comes from both secular and religious organisations. Many Christians who support the concept of <a href="/wiki/Evolutionary_creation" title="Evolutionary creation">evolutionary creation</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Biologos_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Biologos Foundation">Biologos Foundation</a> and GC Science, celebrate Darwin Day, believing that evolution was a tool used by God in the creation process.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-20130201_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20130201-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Some free-thought organisations that support Darwin Day include <a href="/wiki/Council_for_Secular_Humanism" class="mw-redirect" title="Council for Secular Humanism">Council for Secular Humanism</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Freedom_from_Religion_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Freedom from Religion Foundation">Freedom from Religion Foundation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Humanist_Association_of_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Humanist Association of Canada">Humanist Association of Canada</a><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Inquiry" title="Center for Inquiry">Center for Inquiry</a><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Humanist_Association" title="American Humanist Association">American Humanist Association</a><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> in the United States, as well as the <a href="/wiki/British_Humanist_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="British Humanist Association">British Humanist Association</a><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> in the UK, have helped to spread awareness about Darwin Day. In 1999, the <a href="/wiki/Campus_Freethought_Alliance" class="mw-redirect" title="Campus Freethought Alliance">Campus Freethought Alliance</a><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> and the Alliance for Secular Humanist Societies<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> began promoting Darwin Day among members. <a href="/wiki/Category:Humanist_associations" title="Category:Humanist associations">Humanist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Skeptic_organizations" title="Category:Skeptic organizations">sceptic groups</a> welcomed the event and an increase in celebrations on or around 12 February spread across the US and in several other countries. The organizers behind this effort included the <a href="/wiki/International_Humanist_and_Ethical_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="International Humanist and Ethical Union">International Humanist and Ethical Union</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Pigliucci" title="Massimo Pigliucci">Massimo Pigliucci</a>, Amanda Chesworth, and Joann Mooney. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/D._J._Grothe" title="D. J. Grothe">D. J. Grothe</a> continues to champion this effort among groups associated with the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Inquiry" title="Center for Inquiry">Center for Inquiry</a>. The Center's branches across the world also organise Darwin Day events. <i><a href="/wiki/Free_Inquiry" title="Free Inquiry">Free Inquiry</a> </i>magazine, the flagship publication of the <a href="/wiki/Council_for_Secular_Humanism" class="mw-redirect" title="Council for Secular Humanism">Council for Secular Humanism</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Skeptical_Inquirer" title="Skeptical Inquirer">Skeptical Inquirer</a>,</i> the flagship publication of the <a href="/wiki/Committee_for_Skeptical_Inquiry" title="Committee for Skeptical Inquiry">Committee for Skeptical Inquiry</a>, advertised the event and attracted further individuals and groups. The <a href="/wiki/Secular_Student_Alliance" title="Secular Student Alliance">Secular Student Alliance</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> and other organisations committed to <a href="/wiki/Reason" title="Reason">reason</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rationality" title="Rationality">rationality</a> also participate in the annual celebration. </p>
      <p>With Robert Stephens, a scientist, as its President, Darwin Day Celebration has received support from scientists and science enthusiasts across the globe. Educators began to participate by offering special lessons to their students on or around 12 February. Darwin Day Celebration has joined COPUS, the Coalition on the Public Understanding of Science,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> reflecting an increased emphasis on science education and appreciation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>Public relations emails to major publications led to coverage by media such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> in the UK, <i><a href="/wiki/Scientific_American" title="Scientific American">Scientific American</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/New_Scientist" title="New Scientist">New Scientist</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a>. In 2006 Darwin Day was covered by major news syndicates such as <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a>, <a href="/wiki/Knight-Ridder" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight-Ridder">Knight-Ridder</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>. Over 150 articles appeared in major newspapers across the world and helped to attract more participants.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Scientific organisations such as the <a href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Science_Education" title="National Center for Science Education">National Center for Science Education</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Linnaean_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="Linnaean Society">Linnaean Society</a>, have endorsed the holiday. Scientists, philosophers, historians, and physicians lent their names in support of the effort, including <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Dennett" title="Daniel Dennett">Daniel Dennett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Pinker" title="Steven Pinker">Steven Pinker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eugenie_Scott" title="Eugenie Scott">Eugenie Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Jones_(biologist)" title="Steve Jones (biologist)">Steven Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Sober" title="Elliott Sober">Elliott Sober</a>, Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Maddox" title="John Maddox">John Maddox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helena_Cronin" title="Helena Cronin">Helena Cronin</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Calvin" class="mw-redirect" title="William Calvin">William Calvin</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Rennie_(editor)" title="John Rennie (editor)">John Rennie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Kurtz" title="Paul Kurtz">Paul Kurtz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Zimmer" title="Carl Zimmer">Carl Zimmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_O._Wilson" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward O. Wilson">Edward O. Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Shermer" title="Michael Shermer">Michael Shermer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Blackmore" title="Susan Blackmore">Susan Blackmore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ruse" title="Michael Ruse">Michael Ruse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Leakey" title="Richard Leakey">Richard Leakey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niles_Eldridge" class="mw-redirect" title="Niles Eldridge">Niles Eldridge</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Colin_Tudge" title="Colin Tudge">Colin Tudge</a>. Musicians and entertainers such as Richard Miller and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Baird" title="Stephen Baird">Stephen Baird</a> also participated.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>In 2004, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Zimmerman_(biologist)" title="Michael Zimmerman (biologist)">Michael Zimmerman</a>, a professor of biology and dean of the <a href="/wiki/Butler_University#College_of_Liberal_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Butler University">College of Liberal Arts and Sciences</a> at <a href="/wiki/Butler_University" title="Butler University">Butler University</a>, founded the <i><a href="/wiki/Clergy_Letter_Project" title="Clergy Letter Project">Clergy Letter Project</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> in which over 11,100 clergy, as of 18 April 2008, have signed a declaration that a person of faith does not have to choose either belief in God or belief in evolution. In 2006 Zimmerman developed the <i><a href="/wiki/Evolution_Sunday" class="mw-redirect" title="Evolution Sunday">Evolution Sunday</a></i> movement. In 2007 lectures and sermons were presented to roughly 618 congregations across the United States and five other countries, on Darwin's birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Evolution Sunday is intended to show that faith and evolutionary science are compatible in many religious traditions. In 2008, Evolution Sunday was expanded to an Evolution Weekend to incorporate a wider range of faith traditions and 814 congregations from nine countries participated.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
