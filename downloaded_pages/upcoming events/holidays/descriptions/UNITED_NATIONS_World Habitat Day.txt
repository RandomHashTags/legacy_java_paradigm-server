   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading">World Habitat Day</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub" class="noprint">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="contentSub2"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a> 
    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p><b>World Habitat Day</b> is marked on the first Monday of October each year,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and is recognized by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> to reflect on the state of <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">towns</a> and <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">cities</a>, and on the basic right of all to adequate <a href="/wiki/Shelter_(building)" title="Shelter (building)">shelter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The day is also intended to remind the world that everyone has the power and the responsibility to shape the future of towns and cities.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> World Habitat Day was first celebrated in 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, and the theme chosen for that year was "Shelter is My Right". <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/The_United_Nations_General_Assembly" class="mw-redirect" title="The United Nations General Assembly">The United Nations General Assembly</a> decided that this should be an annual event and the first Monday of October was chosen. The day is celebrated in many countries around the world and various activities are organized to examine the problems of rapid <a href="/wiki/Urbanisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Urbanisation">urbanisation</a> and its impact on the environment and human <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Annual themes for World Habitat Day have been diverse and have included "Shelter for the Homeless", "Our Neighbourhood", "Safer Cities," "Women in Urban Government," Cities without Slums" and "Water and Sanitation for Cities." <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>UN Habitat makes plain the need to plan cities in order to avoid the chaotic development of <a href="/wiki/Urban_sprawl" title="Urban sprawl">urban sprawl</a> and all the associated problems that are created as a result. </p>
      <p>Cities are engines of growth. Many people from <a href="/wiki/Rural_areas" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural areas">rural areas</a> globally long to move to cities to realize their dreams of a better life. Often this dream is not realized, but people continue to flock to cities for no other reason than a vague promise of a better future and prosperity.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>A well-planned city can bring just that. Cities can be centres for economic activities and urban challenges can be addressed and opportunities can continue to be afforded to both current and future residents.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Those who are successful succeed in getting jobs or starting their own businesses, which in turn creates more <a href="/wiki/Employment" title="Employment">employment</a> opportunities. </p>
      <p>On the other hand, cities can also become a setting in which <a href="/wiki/Social_exclusion" title="Social exclusion">marginalisation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_inequality" title="Social inequality">inequality</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_exclusion" title="Social exclusion">social exclusion</a> can abound. Access to adequate housing is an important factor in ensuring this is avoided.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Another major issue is the ever-increasing risk posed by natural disasters as the climate crisis continues to develop. This risk is particularly significant in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean Region</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, where countries such as <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> have higher levels of poverty and where their cities are exceptionally vulnerable due to their population density and diversity. </p>
      <p>High levels of population density, coupled with poor building techniques have given rise to <a href="/wiki/Shanty_towns" class="mw-redirect" title="Shanty towns">shanty towns</a> that have no proper infrastructure,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> community organization or <a href="/wiki/Security_of_tenure" title="Security of tenure">security of tenure</a>. In the event of a disaster of any kind, a complete breakdown can result in a chaotic situation and enormous loss of life. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_Scroll_of_Honour">Habitat Scroll of Honour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Habitat_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Habitat Scroll of Honour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/UN-Habitat_Scroll_of_Honour_Award" title="UN-Habitat Scroll of Honour Award">UN-Habitat Scroll of Honour Award</a> was launched by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Settlements_Programme" title="United Nations Human Settlements Programme">United Nations Human Settlements Programme</a> (UN-Habitat) in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It is currently the most prestigious human settlements award in the world. Its aim is to acknowledge initiatives which have made outstanding contributions in various fields such as shelter provision, highlighting the plight of the homeless, leadership in post conflict reconstruction and developing and improving human settlements and the quality of urban life. </p>
      <p>The award, a plaque engraved with the name of the winner and their achievement, is presented to the winners during the Global Observance of the World Habitat Day. </p>
