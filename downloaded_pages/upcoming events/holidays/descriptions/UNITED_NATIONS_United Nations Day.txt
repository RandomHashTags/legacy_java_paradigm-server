      <p><b>United Nations Day</b> is an annual commemorative day, reflecting the official creation of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> on 24 October 1945. In 1947, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> declared 24 October, the anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Charter">Charter of the United Nations</a>, as which "shall be devoted to making known to the people of the world the aims and achievements of the United Nations and to gaining their support for" its work.<sup id="cite_ref-UN_ARES168II_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES168II-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1971, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> adopted a further resolution (<a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_resolution" title="United Nations General Assembly resolution">United Nations Resolution</a> 2782) declaring that United Nations Day shall be an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#October" title="List of minor secular observances">international observance</a> or international holiday and recommended that it should be observed as a public holiday by <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_member_states" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations member states">United Nations member states</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UN_ARES2782XXVI_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES2782XXVI-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span id="World_War_II_Allies.27_day"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II_Allies'_day">World War II Allies' day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: World War II Allies' day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The first event called "United Nations Day" was a <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">World War II Allies</a>' day of solidarity and <a href="/wiki/Military_parade" title="Military parade">military parades</a> launched by US President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> tied to <a href="/wiki/Flag_Day_(United_States)" title="Flag Day (United States)">US Flag Day</a> on June 14, 1942, six months after the <a href="/wiki/Declaration_by_United_Nations" title="Declaration by United Nations">Declaration by United Nations</a>. It was observed in New York City as the "<a href="/wiki/New_York_at_War" title="New York at War">New York at War</a>" parade, in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, and by the Soviet and Chinese governments.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>It was observed throughout World War II, during 1942–1944. Prior to the foundation of the UN itself, it was not directly connected to the current international observance. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commemoration">Commemoration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Commemoration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>U.N. Day has traditionally been marked throughout the world with meetings, discussions and exhibits about the achievements and goals of the organization. In 1971, the General Assembly recommended that <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_member_states" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations member states">member states</a> observe it as a <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holiday</a>. </p>
      <p>Several <a href="/wiki/International_schools" class="mw-redirect" title="International schools">international schools</a> throughout India and other countries throughout the world would also celebrate the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_diversity" title="Cultural diversity">diversity</a> of their student body on United Nations Day (although the event is not necessarily celebrated on 24 October). Celebrations often include a show of cultural performances in the evening and a food fair, where food is available from all over the world. </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> has issued a <a href="/wiki/List_of_observances_in_the_United_States_by_presidential_proclamation" title="List of observances in the United States by presidential proclamation">proclamation</a> each year for United Nations Day since 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>, United Nations Day is an official non-working day, as the province is administered by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Interim_Administration_Mission_in_Kosovo" title="United Nations Interim Administration Mission in Kosovo">Interim Administration Mission</a>. </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, local schoolchildren customarily dress in the national costumes of member states and hold a programme on U.N. Day, which is the last school day before semestral break. Individual students, classes, or grade levels are assigned a country to represent and study; students handcraft their assigned country's flag, and prepare cultural presentations and food as part of the day's educational activities. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_Commemorations">History Commemorations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History Commemorations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>On United Nations Day in 1951, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Postal_Administration" title="United Nations Postal Administration">United Nations Postal Administration</a> issued the first UN Stamps, which were issued in <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">U.S. Dollars</a> at the <a href="/wiki/UN_Headquarters" class="mw-redirect" title="UN Headquarters">U.N. Headquarters</a> in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Development_Information_Day">World Development Information Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: World Development Information Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The UN's <a href="/wiki/World_Development_Information_Day" title="World Development Information Day">World Development Information Day</a> has also been held on 24 October since 1972 </p>
      <p>Humanity has entered the era of <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> – with a global commitment to fulfill the great promise of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. It is celebrated on 24 October worldwide. Many institutions celebrate it by conducting quizzes and elocutions. Da Potta Group of Companies in Kenya, Tanzania and India celebrate it with various discussions. </p>
