      <div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">
       Day to raise autism awareness
      </div> 
      <p class="mw-empty-elt"> </p>
      <p><b>World Autism Awareness Day</b> is an <a href="/wiki/International_observance" class="mw-redirect" title="International observance">internationally recognized day</a> on April 2 every year, encouraging <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">Member States</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> to take measures to raise awareness about people with <a href="/wiki/Autistic_spectrum_disorders" class="mw-redirect" title="Autistic spectrum disorders">autistic spectrum disorders</a> including <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asperger_syndrome" title="Asperger syndrome">Asperger syndrome</a> throughout the world.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was designated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> resolution (A/RES/62/139).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> World Autism Awareness Day", passed in council on 1 November 2007, and adopted on 18 December 2007. It was proposed by <a href="/wiki/Moza_bint_Nasser" title="Moza bint Nasser">Mozah Bint Nasser Al-Missned</a>, the United Nations Representative from <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">consort</a> to <a href="/wiki/Emir_of_Qatar" title="Emir of Qatar">Emir</a> <a href="/wiki/Hamad_bin_Khalifa_Al_Thani" title="Hamad bin Khalifa Al Thani">Hamad Bin Khalifa Al-Thani</a>, and supported by all member states.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>This resolution was passed and adopted without a vote in the UN General Assembly, mainly as a supplement to previous UN initiatives to improve <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>World Autism Day is one of only seven official health-specific UN Days.<sup id="cite_ref-UN-days_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN-days-8">[8]</a></sup> The day itself brings individual autism organizations together all around the world to aid in things like research, diagnoses, treatment, and acceptance for those with a developmental path affected by autism. </p>
      <p>The terms "Autism Awareness Day" and "Autism Awareness Month" are often contested by <a href="/wiki/Autism_rights_movement" title="Autism rights movement">autism rights</a> advocates, who claim that they feed into perceived <a href="/wiki/Ableism" title="Ableism">ableism</a> against autistic people. Such groups, including the <a href="/wiki/Autistic_Self_Advocacy_Network" title="Autistic Self Advocacy Network">Autistic Self Advocacy Network</a>, advocate using the term "Autism Acceptance" as an alternative for both events under the belief that it promotes overcoming anti-autism prejudice rather than simply increasing awareness of autism.<sup id="cite_ref-ASAN_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASAN-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Psychology_Today_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Psychology_Today-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Components">Components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The original resolution had four main components: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>the establishment of the second day of April as World Autism Awareness Day,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> beginning in 2008</li> 
       <li>invitation to Member States and other relevant organizations to the UN or the international <a href="/wiki/Societal_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Societal system">societal system</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organizations" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-governmental organizations">non-governmental organizations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Private_sector" title="Private sector">private sector</a>, to create initiatives to raise public <a href="/wiki/Awareness" title="Awareness">awareness</a> of autism</li> 
       <li>encourages Member States to <a href="/wiki/Awareness_raising" class="mw-redirect" title="Awareness raising">raise awareness</a> of <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autism</a> on all levels in society</li> 
       <li>asks the <a href="/wiki/UN_Secretary-General" class="mw-redirect" title="UN Secretary-General">UN Secretary-General</a> to deliver this message to member states and all other UN organizations<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>For the past years, each World Autism Awareness Day has focused on a specific theme determined by the UN. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>2012: "Launch of Official UN "Awareness Raising" Stamp"<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2013: "Celebrating the ability within the disability of autism"<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2014: "Opening Doors to <a href="/wiki/Inclusion_(education)" title="Inclusion (education)">Inclusive Education</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2015: "Employment: The Autism Advantage"<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2016: "Autism and the 2030 Agenda: Inclusion and <a href="/wiki/Neurodiversity" title="Neurodiversity">Neurodiversity</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2017: "Toward <a href="/wiki/Autonomy" title="Autonomy">Autonomy</a> and Self-Determination"<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2018: "<a href="/wiki/Empowerment" title="Empowerment">Empowering</a> Women and Girls with Autism"<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2019: "<a href="/wiki/Assistive_technology" title="Assistive technology">Assistive Technologies</a>, Active Participation"<sup id="cite_ref-:1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-20">[20]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2020: "The Transition to Adulthood"<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2021: "<a href="/wiki/Inclusion_(disability_rights)" title="Inclusion (disability rights)">Inclusion</a> in the Workplace"<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_initiatives">Notable initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Onesie_Wednesday">Onesie Wednesday</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Onesie Wednesday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In 2014, WAAD coincided with Onesie Wednesday, a day created by the <a href="/wiki/National_Autistic_Society" title="National Autistic Society">National Autistic Society</a> to encourage people in England, Wales and Northern Ireland to show their support for anyone on the <a href="/wiki/Autistic_spectrum" class="mw-redirect" title="Autistic spectrum">autistic spectrum</a>. By wearing a <a href="/wiki/Onesie_(jumpsuit)" title="Onesie (jumpsuit)">onesie</a> or pyjamas, participants are saying, "it's all right to be different".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Outcomes">Outcomes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Outcomes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Autism_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In a 2015 Presidential Proclamation, <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">President Obama</a> highlighted some of the initiatives that the US government was taking to bring rights to those with autism and to bring awareness to the disorder. He highlighted things like The <a href="/wiki/Affordable_Care_Act" title="Affordable Care Act">Affordable Care Act</a>, which prohibits health insurance companies from denying coverage based on a pre-existing condition such as autism. He also pointed out the recent <a href="/wiki/Autism_CARES_Act_of_2014" title="Autism CARES Act of 2014">Autism CARES Act of 2014</a>, which provides higher level training for those who are serving citizens on the autism spectrum.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>
