      <p><b>Heroes' Day</b> or <b>National Heroes' Day</b> may refer to a number of commemorations of national heroes in different countries. It is often held on the <a href="/wiki/Birthday" title="Birthday">birthday</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Folk_hero" title="Folk hero">national hero</a> or heroine, or the anniversary of their great deeds that made them heroes. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Angola">Angola</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Angola">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>National Heroes Day in <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Holidays_in_Angola" class="mw-redirect" title="Holidays in Angola">holiday in Angola</a> on 17 September, the birthday of the national hero <a href="/wiki/Agostinho_Neto" title="Agostinho Neto">Agostinho Neto</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bahamas">Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>National Heroes Day in the <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">Bahamas</a> has been a public holiday since 2013. It replaced Discovery Day, which celebrated the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> to the Americas. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Barbados">Barbados</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barbados">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Barbadian_national_heroes" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbadian national heroes">National Heroes' Day</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Barbados" title="Public holidays in Barbados">public holiday</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> on April 28.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cape_Verde">Cape Verde</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cape Verde">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Heroes Day is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Cape_Verde" title="Public holidays in Cape Verde">public holiday in Cape Verde</a> on 20 January. It commemorates the 1973 assassination of <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%ADlcar_Cabral" title="Amílcar Cabral">Amílcar Cabral</a>, who is remembered as a hero for fighting western <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonialism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Exploitation_of_labour" title="Exploitation of labour">exploitation</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cayman_Islands">Cayman Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cayman Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The fourth Monday in January is National Heroes' Day in the Cayman Islands; it was proclaimed with the National Heroes Law, providing for the declaration of persons who have rendered exceptional service as national heroes. Numerous Caymanians have been declared national heroes, includes the Hon. James (Jim) Manoah Bodden, Mrs. <a href="/wiki/Sybil_I._McLaughlin" title="Sybil I. McLaughlin">Sybil I. McLaughlin</a>, Mr. Thomas William Farrington, Mrs. <a href="/wiki/Sybil_Joyce_Hylton" title="Sybil Joyce Hylton">Sybil Joyce Hylton</a>, Mr. Desmond Vere Watler, Ms Mary Evelyn Wood, Cert. Hon. and <a href="/wiki/William_Warren_Conolly" title="William Warren Conolly">William Warren Conolly</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Timor">East Timor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: East Timor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>December 31 is <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_East_Timor#Public_holidays" title="Culture of East Timor">National Heroes Day</a> in <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a>. It commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_invasion_of_East_Timor" title="Indonesian invasion of East Timor">Indonesian invasion of East Timor</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Guinea-Bissau">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>Heroes' Day</b> is celebrated on <a href="/wiki/January_20" title="January 20">January 20</a> to commemorate the assassination of independence leader <a href="/wiki/Amilcar_Cabral" class="mw-redirect" title="Amilcar Cabral">Amilcar Cabral</a> in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hungary">Hungary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Hungary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Heroes' Day (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <i lang="hu">Hősök napja</i>), National Heroes' Day or the Memorial Day of Hungarian Heroes in <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> is celebrated on the 31 May as a public holiday. It is observed with military ceremonies at monuments such as the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier on <a href="/wiki/H%C5%91s%C3%B6k_tere" title="Hősök tere">Heroes' Square in Budapest</a> all to commemorate soldiers in service to Hungary.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It has been marked every year by the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Defence_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Hungarian Defence Force">Hungarian Defence Force</a> and the public since 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia">Indonesia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Indonesia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>Hari Pahlawan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a> for <b>Heroes' Day</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Day" title="Remembrance Day">Remembrance Day</a> annually celebrated on 10 November in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The day commemorates the 1945 <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Surabaya" title="Battle of Surabaya">Battle of Surabaya</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> in which pro-independence <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> local community, as well as soldiers and militia fought against British and Dutch troops as part of the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_National_Revolution" title="Indonesian National Revolution">Indonesian National Revolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Jamaica">Jamaica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Jamaica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Jamaica, Heroes' Day is celebrated annually on the third Monday of October. It started in 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It honours <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Bustamante" title="Alexander Bustamante">Alexander Bustamante</a>, Jamaica’s first prime minister; <a href="/wiki/Nanny_of_the_Maroons" title="Nanny of the Maroons">Nanny of the Maroons</a>, who led a fight for independence in the early 18th century, and five other national heroes. The government also recognizes modern heroes on this date.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kenya">Kenya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Kenya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>Mashujaa Day</b>, also known as <b>Heroes' Day</b> ("mashujaa" is <a href="/wiki/Swahili_language" title="Swahili language">Swahili</a> for "heroes"), is a national day in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, which is observed on 20 October as a <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holiday</a> to collectively honour all those who contributed towards the struggle for Kenya's independence or positively contributed in the post independence Kenya. It was previously known as <b>Kenyatta Day</b>, which was celebrated to commemorate the detention in <a href="/wiki/Kapenguria" title="Kapenguria">Kapenguria</a> of freedom fighters <a href="/wiki/Achieng_Oneko" title="Achieng Oneko">Achieng' Oneko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bildad_Kaggia" title="Bildad Kaggia">Bildad Kaggia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Kubai" title="Fred Kubai">Fred Kubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jomo_Kenyatta" title="Jomo Kenyatta">Jomo Kenyatta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kungu_Karumba" class="mw-redirect" title="Kungu Karumba">Kung'u Karumba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ngei" title="Paul Ngei">Paul Ngei</a>, often referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Kapenguria_Six" title="Kapenguria Six">Kapenguria Six</a>. However, following the promulgation of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Kenya" title="Constitution of Kenya">Constitution of Kenya</a> in August 2010, Kenyatta Day was renamed.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Malaysia">Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>Warriors' Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a>: <i lang="ms">Hari Pahlawan</i>) is a day in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> that commemorates the servicemen killed during the two <a href="/wiki/World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="World War">World Wars</a> and the Malayan Emergency. By extension, it honours all individuals who lost their lives in the line of duty throughout Malaysia's history. Until 2010, every year on 31 July, the <a href="/wiki/King_of_Malaysia" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Malaysia">King</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia" title="Prime Minister of Malaysia">Prime Minister</a> as well as senior representatives of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Malaysian_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Malaysian Police">Royal Malaysian Police</a> and the Armed Forces gathered at the National Monument in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Gardens,_Kuala_Lumpur" class="mw-redirect" title="Lake Gardens, Kuala Lumpur">Lake Gardens</a> to lay wreaths and pay homage to Malaysia's fallen heroes. They now gather at <a href="/wiki/Merdeka_Square,_Kuala_Lumpur" title="Merdeka Square, Kuala Lumpur">Merdeka Square, Kuala Lumpur</a>, or in Kem Perdana in <a href="/wiki/Sungai_Besi" title="Sungai Besi">Sungai Besi</a>, also in KL (the 2014 event was held there).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mozambique">Mozambique</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Mozambique">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Heroes' Day is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Mozambique" title="Public holidays in Mozambique">public holiday</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> on 3 February. Commemorates those who lost their lives in the Portuguese Colonial War.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Namibia">Namibia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Namibia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Heroes%27_Day_(Namibia)" title="Heroes' Day (Namibia)">Heroes' Day (Namibia)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>26 August is Heroes' Day in Namibia. It marks the beginning of the armed struggle during the <a href="/wiki/Namibian_War_of_Independence" class="mw-redirect" title="Namibian War of Independence">Namibian War of Independence</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>National Heroes' Day, a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_the_Philippines" title="Public holidays in the Philippines">national public holiday in the Philippines</a>, is held on the last Monday of every August (formerly<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Relative_time_references" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="Clarify the applicable timeframe so that it is unambiguous when read at a later date. (October 2020)">timeframe?</span></a></i>]</sup> last Sunday of August)<sup id="cite_ref-Official_Gazette_of_the_Philippines_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Official_Gazette_of_the_Philippines-13">[13]</a></sup> to mark the anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Cry_of_Pugad_Lawin" title="Cry of Pugad Lawin">Cry of Pugad Lawin</a>, the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Revolution" title="Philippine Revolution">Philippine Revolution</a> by the <i><a href="/wiki/Katipunan" title="Katipunan">Katipunan</a></i> and its <i>Supremo</i> <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Bonifacio" title="Andrés Bonifacio">Andrés Bonifacio</a> in 1896.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This national holiday was originally set on the last Sunday of August in 1931, but was moved to the last Monday of August in 2007 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Gloria_Macapagal_Arroyo" title="Presidency of Gloria Macapagal Arroyo">Arroyo Administration</a>'s attempts to reduce work disruptions, allow for longer weekends and boost domestic leisure and tourism.<sup id="cite_ref-Official_Gazette_of_the_Philippines_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Official_Gazette_of_the_Philippines-13">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Romania">Romania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Romania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Heroes' Day in <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> is celebrated on the 40th day (<a href="/wiki/Feast_of_the_Ascension" title="Feast of the Ascension">Feast of the Ascension</a>) from the Orthodox Easter. It is a national holiday;<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> observed with military and religious festivities at the monuments dedicated to the national heroes (such as the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rwanda">Rwanda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Rwanda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>National Heroes Day is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Rwanda" title="Public holidays in Rwanda">public holiday</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a> on 1 February. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis">Saint Kitts and Nevis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Saint Kitts and Nevis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>National Heroes Day</b> is a holiday in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis" title="Saint Kitts and Nevis">Saint Kitts and Nevis</a> that is observed annually on 16 September. It honours individuals who have made significant contributions to the advancement of the nation. There are currently five national heroes: <a href="/wiki/Robert_Llewellyn_Bradshaw" title="Robert Llewellyn Bradshaw">Robert Llewellyn Bradshaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Southwell" title="Paul Southwell">Paul Southwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Nathaniel_France" title="Joseph Nathaniel France">Joseph Nathaniel France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simeon_Daniel" title="Simeon Daniel">Simeon Daniel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Simmonds" title="Kennedy Simmonds">Kennedy Simmonds</a>. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>The holiday was established in 1996 by the Saint Kitts and Nevis Federal Parliament with the passage of the <i>National Honours Act</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The first observance of the holiday was on 16 September 1998 to honour <a href="/wiki/Robert_Llewellyn_Bradshaw" title="Robert Llewellyn Bradshaw">Robert Llewellyn Bradshaw</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Premier" title="Premier">Premier</a> of Saint Kitts and Nevis and a former labour activist.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The holiday is observed on his birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-a_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <ul>
       <li>In 2004, former Premier and Chief Minister <a href="/wiki/Paul_Southwell" title="Paul Southwell">Paul Southwell</a>, and former Social Services Minister and trade unionist <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Nathaniel_France" title="Joseph Nathaniel France">Joseph Nathaniel France</a>, were also affirmed as National Heroes by the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Saint Kitts and Nevis">National Assembly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2013, the first <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Nevis" title="Premier of Nevis">Premier of Nevis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simeon_Daniel" title="Simeon Daniel">Simeon Daniel</a>, was added to the list of National Heroes.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <ul>
       <li>In 2015, <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Simmonds" title="Kennedy Simmonds">Kennedy Simmonds</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister of Saint Kitts and Nevis">Prime Minister of Saint Kitts and Nevis</a>, became the fifth person to be named as a National Hero.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He is the first living person to receive this honour.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>National Heroes Day in Sri Lanka is celebrated every 22 May. It has been celebrated since 1818 Kandyan rebellion against British invasion in Sri Lanka. This year (2013), 195th commemoration of National Heroes Day of Sri Lanka was held by the sponsorship of the Sri Lankan Government.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Turks_and_Caicos_Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Turks and Caicos Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a>, National Heroes' Day takes place on the last Monday in May.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uganda">Uganda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Uganda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Heroes' Day in <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> takes place every 9 June. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> first celebrated a National Heroes Day in 2011, on 21 October (the same day as the victory at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Trafalgar" title="Battle of Trafalgar">Battle of Trafalgar</a>). It was founded by Danny Glavin, a teacher from <a href="/wiki/Fareham,_Hampshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Fareham, Hampshire">Fareham, Hampshire</a>. After hearing about the tragic death of his childhood friend whilst serving in Afghanistan, he decided to fund-raise for a military charity in his memory. The day was endorsed by Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a>. Now Glavin has founded The Inspiration Federation and coordinates the Heroes Day Educational Programme within schools across the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Heroes' Day in <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> is a public holiday in a cluster four days of holidays observed over the first weekend in July. The other holidays are the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zambia_International_Trade_Fair&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zambia International Trade Fair (page does not exist)">Zambia International Trade Fair</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Unity_Day_(Zambia)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Unity Day (Zambia) (page does not exist)">Unity Day</a>, celebrating a country with six main languages. Heroes Day is a memorial day for those who perished during the independence struggles.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heroes%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Zimbabwe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Heroes' Day in <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> is a public holiday observed on the 2nd Monday of August each year.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> This is a day to remember those who died during the liberation war or those who were declared National Heroes when they died and are buried at the National shrine, The National Heroes Acre in Harare. There are also 10 Provincial Heroes Acres where those who have not been accorded the highest honour but are recognised for the role they played during the Liberation War and are buried in the Provinces they would have come from. </p>
