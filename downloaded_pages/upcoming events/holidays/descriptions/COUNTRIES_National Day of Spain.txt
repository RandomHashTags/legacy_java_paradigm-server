      <p>The <b>National Day of Spain</b> (Spanish: <i lang="es">Fiesta Nacional de España</i>) is the official name of the <a href="/wiki/National_day" title="National day">national festivity</a> of Spain. It is held annually on 12 October and is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">national holiday</a>. It is also traditionally referred to as the Día de la Hispanidad (<a href="/wiki/Hispanicity" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanicity">Hispanicity</a>), commemorating the Spanish legacy to the world, especially in <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_America" title="Hispanic America">America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Aguilar_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aguilar-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The celebration of the National Day of Spain is the day the Spanish people commemorates the history of the country, recognising and giving value of what has been achieved together and to reconfirm the commitment for the future as a nation. The 12 of October celebrates union and brotherhood, also showing the ties of Spain with the international community.<sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY2-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Aguilar_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aguilar-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p> Spanish law declares <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style></p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>«&nbsp;the date chosen, October 12, symbolizes the historical anniversary in which Spain, about to conclude a process of State construction based on our cultural and political plurality, and the integration of the kingdoms of Spain into the same monarchy, begins a period of linguistic and cultural projection beyond European limits&nbsp;»<sup id="cite_ref-Decree_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Decree-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>La Fiesta Nacional de España conmemorates the <a href="/wiki/Discovery_of_the_Americas" class="mw-redirect" title="Discovery of the Americas">discovery of the&nbsp;Americas</a>&nbsp;by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> for&nbsp;Spain on October 12, 1492, as a key point for the Spain's overseas projection and legacy to the world and in the Americas in particular and the vast, common heritage with the today's <a href="/wiki/American_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="American countries">American countries</a>, which integrated the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Global_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Global power">global power</a> in the <a href="/wiki/World_history" title="World history">world history</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>October 12 is also the official Spanish language day, the Feasts of both <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Our Lady of the Pillar">Our Lady of the Pillar</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Guadalupe_in_Extremadura" title="Our Lady of Guadalupe in Extremadura">Virgin of Guadalupe</a> as well as the Day of the Spanish Armed Forces.<sup id="cite_ref-Fuller_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fuller-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The 12 of October is also an official festivity in most <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_America" title="Hispanic America">Hispanic America</a>, though under varying names (Día de la Hispanidad, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%ADa_de_la_Raza" class="mw-redirect" title="Día de la Raza">Día de la Raza</a>, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%ADa_del_Respeto_a_la_Diversidad_Cultural" class="mw-redirect" title="Día del Respeto a la Diversidad Cultural">Día del Respeto a la Diversidad Cultural</a>, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%ADa_de_la_Resistencia_Ind%C3%ADgena" class="mw-redirect" title="Día de la Resistencia Indígena">Día de la Resistencia Indígena</a>, etc.) mainly celebrating the historical and cultural ties among them and between them and Spain and their common Hispanic and pre-Hispanic native-American heritage&nbsp;; and further celebrated in the United States, as the <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Day" title="Columbus Day">Columbus Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>The National Day of Spain is a national holiday in the whole country, so all central and autonomous communities <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Spain" title="Politics of Spain">institutions and administration</a> offices are closed on that day as well as banks and commerces. The National Day is massively celebrated in Spain through numerous public and private events organized throughout the country to praise the nation's heritage, history, society and people. The festivity is also celebrated by Spanish communities worldwide. Solemn acts of Tribute to the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Spain" title="Flag of Spain">National Flag</a> take place in different locations of Spain and abroad. The most important by far is the one held in the capital, <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, along with the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armed_Forces" title="Spanish Armed Forces">Armed Forces</a> parade. Other tribute, cultural, religious and vindicative parades and demonstrations are also organised by civil society actors across Spain. </p>
      <p>While the National Day is celebrated exclusively on October 12, the national holidays takes, with no exception, a long weekend, at least 3 days, for the citizens' leisure and enjoyment. Taking the advantage of these short holidays, many people travel these days in Spain, specially to visit other cities and emblematic places within the country. Common destinations are <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a> and its capital, <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, where the festivity of "<a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Our Lady of the Pillar">Our Lady of the Pillar</a>" (La Virgen del Pilar), "Mother of the Hispanic Peoples", also falls on this date. For this reason, these holidays are also known in Spain as the «&nbsp;Long weekend of the Pillar&nbsp;» (Puente del Pilar). This way of celebrating certainly strengthens the sense of national unity and cohesion as well as the sense of national belonging and purpose. </p>
      <p>Indeed, though these holidays are short, many citizens take some days off travelling around Spain thanks to the well developed highway network and specially the <a href="/wiki/AVE" title="AVE">high-speed train</a> network, the largest in Europe and second in the world. Between five and more than six million road travels occur during these holydays in Spain for the State Traffic Office plans and implements specific road and driving safety measures, including alcohol, drugs and speed controls.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>

         </div>Hispania and its provinces – Allegoric sculpture complex on top of the <a href="/wiki/Gate_of_Toledo" class="mw-redirect" title="Gate of Toledo">Gate of Toledo</a> in Madrid

      <p>The roots of today's Spanish identity and cultural diversity are to be found in the interaction and fusion of peoples which have over the course of three millennia arrived and settled in Hispania (in Latin) or Iberia (in Greek), among them <a href="/wiki/Tartessians" class="mw-redirect" title="Tartessians">Tartessians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vascones" title="Vascones">Vascones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ligures" title="Ligures">Ligures</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iberians" title="Iberians">Iberians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cantabri" title="Cantabri">Cantabri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phoenicians" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenicians">Phoenicians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carthaginians" class="mw-redirect" title="Carthaginians">Carthaginians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suevi" class="mw-redirect" title="Suevi">Suevi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alans" title="Alans">Alans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berbers" title="Berbers">Berbers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Arabs</a>...). Hispania was already identified as an autonomous entity by historians from the <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">Antiquity</a> (<a href="/wiki/Strabo" title="Strabo">Strabo</a>, Asclepiades of Mirlea). In Early Middle Ages, Spain, as an entity of its own, and Spanish identity proud can be already traced in 5th and 6th centuries' works by Bishops <a href="/wiki/Paulus_Orosius" class="mw-redirect" title="Paulus Orosius">Paulus Orosius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hydatius" title="Hydatius">Hydatius</a> and most importantly Saint <a href="/wiki/Isidore_of_Seville" title="Isidore of Seville">Isidore of Seville</a> with his <a href="/wiki/Laus_Spaniae" class="mw-redirect" title="Laus Spaniae">Laus Spaniae</a> praising:<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bueno_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bueno-15">[15]</a></sup> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942">
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>Of all the lands from the west to the Indies, you, Spain, O sacred and always fortunate mother of princes and peoples, are the most beautiful.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
      </blockquote>

      <p>At the dawn of the Early Modern Period, the marriage and joint rule of <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella I of Castile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II of Aragon</a> in 1469 marked the <i>de facto</i> unification of Spain, joining what contemporaries referred to as "the Spains". After the conquest and fall of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Granada" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Granada">Kingdom of Granada</a>, the last Muslim-ruled polity in the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a>) in January 1492, the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs_of_Spain" title="Catholic Monarchs of Spain">Hispanic Monarchs</a>, Isabel and Fernando, soon after getting the title of «&nbsp;Catholic King and Queen&nbsp;» from <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_VI" title="Pope Alexander VI">Pope Alexander VI</a>, agreed to undertake the charting of a new commercial maritim route to Asia sailing westwards through the Mare Tenebrosum (medieval name for Atlantic Ocean), a project proposed to them by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">Genovese</a> navigator and cartographer. This move was provoked by the control of the historical land and maritime <a href="/wiki/Spice_trade" title="Spice trade">spice trade</a> routes by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> pursuant to the conquest of <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Constantinople" title="Fall of Constantinople">Constantinople</a> (today's <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>) and the end of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Eastern Roman Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The discovery of the Americas by the Spaniards is for many one of the truly epochal events in world history as it changed dramatically not only the vision and comprehension of the world for ever, but resulted in a new socio-economic and political order affecting millions of people both in the Old and the New World to date.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The conmemoration emphasizes the discovering of America and the pioneering efforts and contributions of Spain in America and to the world through centuries, from the world exploration to the Spanish influence and shaping of Hispanic American societies.<sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY2-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Aguilar_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aguilar-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lummis_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lummis-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In this sense, la Fiesta Nacional traditionally celebrates the Hispanidad (Hispanicity) as a long lasting historic and present day expression of a multiethnic, multicultural community spread worldwide, all sharing a <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Spain" title="Culture of Spain">Spanish cultural heritage</a>, most importantly the Spanish language, and in particular referred to the <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_America" title="Hispanic America">Hispanic American countries</a>, based on the biological and cultural <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">mestizaje</a> of peoples achieved under 400 years of Spanish rule, between the late 15th and the late 19th centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Spanish legacy is evoked by Harvard University scholar <a href="/wiki/Charles_F._Lummis" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles F. Lummis">Charles F. Lummis</a> in his work <i>The Spanish Pioneers</i> when stating that </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942">
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>«&nbsp;the Spanish pioneering of the Americas was the largest and longest and most marvellous feat of manhood in all history.&nbsp;» and that&nbsp;«&nbsp;(t)he honor of giving America to the world belongs to Spain, the credit not only of discovery, but of centuries of such pioneering as no other nation ever paralleled in any land.&nbsp;»<sup id="cite_ref-Lummis_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lummis-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_evolution">Origins and evolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Day_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Origins and evolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Secano-venida_de_la_virgen_del_pilar.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Secano-venida_de_la_virgen_del_pilar.png/250px-Secano-venida_de_la_virgen_del_pilar.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="344" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5a/Secano-venida_de_la_virgen_del_pilar.png 1.5x" data-file-width="326" data-file-height="448"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Secano-venida_de_la_virgen_del_pilar.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Coming of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Our Lady of the Pillar">Our Lady of the Pillar</a> to <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, capital city of <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The far origin of the festivity is to be found in the Catholic tradition of Virgin Mary apparition "in mortal flesh" in <a href="/wiki/Caesaraugusta" class="mw-redirect" title="Caesaraugusta">Caesaraugusta</a> (<a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> name of Zaragoza) to Apostle <a href="/wiki/Saint_James_the_Greater" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint James the Greater">Saint James the Greater</a> in AD 40 where a temple was built by the river <a href="/wiki/Ebro" title="Ebro">Ebro</a>, the first in the world dedicated to the cult of the Blessed Mother. <a href="/wiki/Pilgrimage" title="Pilgrimage">Pilgrimage</a> to Zaragoza to venerate <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Our Lady of the Pillar">Our Lady of the Pillar</a> started in early XIII century. Together with Saint James in <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_Compostela" class="mw-redirect" title="Cathedral of Compostela">Compostela</a>, Our Lady of the Pillar has centered the spirituality of the Christians in the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> for centuries. <sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On May 27, 1642, the city of Zaragoza designated the Virgen del Pilar as symbol of the Hispanidad (Hispanicity) and the date became a feast in commemoration of the "coming of Our Lady" to the city.<sup id="cite_ref-Cenarro_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cenarro-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1730, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_XII" title="Pope Clement XII">Pope Clement XII</a> allowed the feast of Our Lady of the Pillar to be celebrated all over the&nbsp;Spanish Empire thereon.<sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY1-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On September 23, 1892, the <a href="/wiki/Queen_regent" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen regent">queen regent</a> of Spain, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Christina_of_Austria" title="Maria Christina of Austria">Maria Christina of Austria</a>, promulgated a Royal Decree in <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n" title="San Sebastián">San Sebastián</a>, as proposed by the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Spain" title="Prime Minister of Spain">Prime Minister</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_C%C3%A1novas_del_Castillo" title="Antonio Cánovas del Castillo">Antonio Cánovas del Castillo</a>, declaring 12 October 1892 a national day in commemoration of the 4th centenary of the discovery of America.<sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY3_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY3-25">[25]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The preamble of said decree states that the government had "considered appropriate to explore the position of all the American countries and Italy, as homeland of Columbus, to see if, by joint agreement, that commemoration could be vested with greater importance." </p>
      <p>The preamble continues pointing out that "most Governments of America have already adhered to the idea in principle, like that of the United States as well as those of the Hispanic American Republics, with few exceptions, maybe because of temporary circumstances, which shall probably not prevent an unanimous resolution, and, from its part, the Government of his S.M. the King of Italy has also welcomed the invitation ( ) in the most cordial and satisfactory way." It is therein further stressed out the opportunity of "perpetuating" such a commemoration as a National Day of Spain.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Hispanidad was celebrated again in Spain from 1935, when the first festival was held in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-filosofia_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-filosofia-27">[27]</a></sup> The day was known as <i>Día de la <a href="/wiki/Hispanidad" title="Hispanidad">Hispanidad</a></i> ("Day of Hispanicity"), emphasizing Spain's connection to the international Hispanic community.<sup id="cite_ref-Prakke_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prakke-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY3_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY3-25">[25]</a></sup> </p> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg/250px-Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg/375px-Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg/500px-Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456"></a>

          <a href="/wiki/File:Basilica_Pilar_Zaragoza_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div><a href="/wiki/Cathedral-Basilica_of_Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Cathedral-Basilica of Our Lady of the Pillar">Cathedral-Basilica of Our Lady of the Pillar</a> in Zaragoza

      <p>The Hispanidad linked to the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral-Basilica_of_Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Cathedral-Basilica of Our Lady of the Pillar">Cathedral-Basilica of Our Lady of the Pillar</a> as historical background and symbology of the National day of Spain are well expressed in a 1939 letter addressed by an Argentinian donor, Doña Soledad Alonso de Drysdale, to the Spanish government regarding the fundraising to complete the building of the last two of four towers of the Cathedral of the Pillar:<sup id="cite_ref-Cenarro_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cenarro-24">[24]</a></sup> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942">
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>«&nbsp;If, effectively, the funding comes from the Hispanic America, these two towers will become an enviable symbol of hispanicity, raised in the city capital of the kingdom who advanced the money used for the discovery of the New Word. (...) On a January 2, feast of the Coming of the Virgin Mary in mortal flesh to Zaragoza, Granada surrendered to the Catholic Kings so could Isabel and Ferdinand dedicate time and plan the enterprise proposed to them by the great navigator&nbsp;; on an October 12, feast of Our Lady of the Pillar, the three inmortal caravels arrived to the first American lands. (...) If the architecture of the cathedral is completed and culminated with money from overseas, the symbolic value of the Saint Metropolitan Temple as Sanctuary of the Hispanidad will be totally affirmed.&nbsp;»</p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>from the original: </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942">
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>«Si, en efecto, de la América Hispana viene el dinero, esas dos torres serán un símbolo envidiable de hispanidad, levantado en la capital del reino que anticipó el dinero para el descubrimiento del Nuevo Mundo. (...) En un día dos de enero, fiesta de la Venida de la Virgen en carne mortal a Zaragoza, se rendía Granada a los Reyes Católicos y podían dedicar Isabel y Fernando tiempo y meditaciones a la empresa que les proponía el gran navegante; en un día 12 de octubre, fiesta de Nuestra Señora del Pilar, las tres inmortales carabelas llegaban a las primeras tierras americanas. (...) Si es con dinero de Ultramar como (...) se completa y corona la arquitectura de la catedral, el valor simbólico del Santo Templo Metropolitano como Santuario de la Hispanidad habrá quedado consagrado totalmente.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
      </blockquote>

         </div>Spanish Constitution of 1978, the ninth fundamental law in the history of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Constitutions_of_Spain" title="List of Constitutions of Spain">Spanish constitutionalism</a> since the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1812" title="Spanish Constitution of 1812">Spanish Constitution of 1812</a>

      <p>In 1981, soon after the approval of the Spanish Constitution of 1978, the national day is renamed "Fiesta Nacional de España y Día de la Hispanidad" by Royal Decree<sup id="cite_ref-Prakke_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prakke-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On 7 October 1987 the mention to Hispanidad is discarded and the name is changed again to <i>Fiesta Nacional</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Decree_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Decree-5">[5]</a></sup> The 12 October became one of two national celebrations, along with <a href="/wiki/Constitution_Day" title="Constitution Day">Constitution Day</a> on 6 December.<sup id="cite_ref-Prakke_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prakke-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fuller_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fuller-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Spain's "national day" had moved around several times during the various regime changes of the 20th century; establishing it on the day of the Discovery of America international celebration was part of a compromise between conservatives, who wanted to emphasize the status of the monarchy and Spain's history, and the left-wingged parties, who wanted to commemorate Spain's burgeoning democracy with an official holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-Fuller_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fuller-7">[7]</a></sup> While the manifest ties of Spain with Hispanic America are not questioned, the removal of the Hispanidad from the name of the national festivity aims to avoid any controversy with different sensibilities regarding the so-called conquest, projection and rule of the Americas by Spain during the imperial period.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The participation of the Armed forces in the celebration of the National Day was stablished in 1997 by Royal Decree, which regulates the conmemoration and tribute acts to be performed by the Armed forces on the National Day of Spain. It actually transfers the most relevant acts of the Armed forces Day, traditionally celebrated separately in springtime, to October 12. This measure aimed, on the one hand, to integrate in the same festivity all the historic and cultural elements of the Spanich Nation, and on the other hand, to enhance the identification of the Armed forces with the Spanish society which they serve. Other civic-military events are also organised around Oct 12, including military training and exhibitions, cultural and sport events, aiming to facilitate the citizens to get a better knowledge of the Armed forces.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Public and civil society celebrations of the National Day are numerous and of different natures. Solemn Acts of Tribute to the National Flag, symbol of unity and of national coexistence, take place in different locations of Spain. The most important is the one traditionally held in the capital, Madrid, along with the Armed forces and State Security Forces parade. Other tribute and vindicative parades and demonstrations are also organised by civil society actors across Spain. </p>
      <p>While the reference to 'Hispanidad' was removed from the official name of the festivity, the tradition of celebrating somehow this phenomenon is massively observed in Spain as it is celebrated by Hispanic peoples worldwide. Celebrations emphasizing the discovering of America and the pioneering efforts and contributions of Spain in America and to the world through centuries, from the world exploration to the Spanish influence and shaping of Hispanic American societies, are traditionally held.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY1_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MARCILHACY3_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCILHACY3-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lummis_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lummis-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bueno_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bueno-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Officially commemorated until 1987, the Hispanic Day (Dia de la Hispanidad) has continued to be spontaneously celebrated by Spanish people with both a cultural and a religious dimension. It celebrates the Hispanic diversity, brotherhood, common heritage and cultural ties between Hispanic countries. This section should help to seize what Spain celebrates on its National Day and how. </p>

      <p>The celebration traditionally includes a parade of the Spanish Armed forces (military) and the State Security Forces (law enforcement), usually held in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, attended by high institutional and political representatives including the King of Spain, head of state and commander-in-chief of the Armed Forces, together with the Royal Family, the Presidents of the Government, the Parliament and the Judicial Power and other central and autonomous communities administrations' representatives. Political and institutional delegations from other guest countries, including military forces participating in the parade, also attend the event. The parade expresses the commitment of the Armed and the State Security forces with the society and its contribution to the international community.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>But the most important role is usually reserved to the Spanish flag, symbol of unity and of national coexistence. For several consecutive years the Spanish Air Force aerobatics paratroop unity has performed a jump carrying a massive Spanish flag to land by the Royal tribune presiding the parade. Then the tribute to the national flag, with its raising while the national anthem is performed. Then, tribute is paid to the soldiers who gave their lives for Spain which is followed by an aerial and land civil-military parade, which usually includes a display by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Air_Force" title="Spanish Air Force">Spanish Air Force</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Aerobatics" title="Aerobatics">aerobatics</a> team, the <i><a href="/wiki/Patrulla_%C3%81guila" title="Patrulla Águila">Patrulla Águila</a></i>. </p>
      <p>The Armed Forces Parade is one of the most multitudinous event in Spain with thousands of citizens celebrating the feast and gathering along the route parade where they can show their affection to the Royal family and to the Armed Forces and to the State Security forces as they march through. </p>
      <p>As for many other public events, the 2020 Armed Forces Parade was cancelled due to the COVID-19 sanitary crisis and substituted by a civil-military institutional act in the Royal Palace's court where the Tribute to the Spanish flag could be paid. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_culture">Spanish culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Day_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spanish culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>Expression of the rich <a href="/wiki/Spanish_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish culture">cultural diversity of Spain</a>, the National Day is massively celebrated through numerous public and private events to praise the nation's heritage, history, society and people.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> October 12 feast actually celebrates the unique "Spanish lifestyle"<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and shows the essence of the many local, regional and national <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_of_National_Tourist_Interest_of_Spain" title="Fiestas of National Tourist Interest of Spain">festivities and traditions</a> celebrated in Spain throughout the year, some enjoying <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_of_International_Tourist_Interest_of_Spain" title="Fiestas of International Tourist Interest of Spain">international fame</a>. Dozens of thousands of people invade the streets to show their national proud, whether in colorful parades and demonstrations or just spontaneously on their own. People dressed in traditional regional or historic costumes as well as <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Spain" title="Music of Spain">folk, classical and modern music</a> concerts and street shows are unavoidable features of the celebrations. Hispanic communities in Spain also participate in parades displaying their national colors and their countries' typical garments marching along to the sound of their respective upbeat folk tunes. La Hispanidad is in the air. </p>
      <p>No food, no feast: The Spaniards usually gather around a good meal in family and friends reunions whether at home or in bars and restaurants and they do not miss the tradition at the National Day. Open air or indoor meals organized by institutions or civil society actors are very popular as well. On the menu: the marvelous <a href="/wiki/Spanish_cuisine" title="Spanish cuisine">Spanish food</a> with its distinctive regional singularities. A part from the worldwide famous tapas, paellas, jamon serrano and tortilla de patatas, an amazing festival of rich dishes, salads, soups,&nbsp;stews and&nbsp;roasts, fish and seafood, etc. can be enjoyed anywhere in Spain as they are a manifestation&nbsp;not only of the Spanish gastronomy but also of the great agricultural, cattle-raising and fishering millenary tradition of Spain.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Then, over two thousand years old <a href="/wiki/Spanish_wine" title="Spanish wine">viniculture</a> ensure to harmoniously pair those meals with a good wine produced, virtually, in any corner of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_wine_regions" title="Spanish wine regions">Spanish country land</a>. Spanish people do know how to celebrate around the table and they do it specially on October 12. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Art_of_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Art of Spain">Spanish art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_architecture" title="Spanish architecture">Architecture</a> also play an important role in the celebration of the National Day. October 12 is an Open Doors Day in many museums and historical sites. From Stone, Bronze and Iron Ages, through Roman, Visigothic, Al-Andalus, Romanesque, Gothic, Mudéjar, Renaissance, Baroque, Neoclassical, to XXth century Modernism and Contemporary, Spanish architecture legacy is simply awesom reflecting the great historical and geographical diversity of the country. </p>
      <p>Ranking third worldwide in the UNESCO World Heritage Sites' list, with 48 sites therein inscribed, is only a tiny hint of the countless artistic and architechtonical treasures spread all over the Spanish territory, truly. Many citizens take the opportunity each October 12 to visit them as well as to admire the artworks of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_art" title="Spanish art">classical and contemporary Maestros</a> (Picasso, Dali, Goya, Velazquez, Miro, etc.) and other artists held in the Spanish museums, some being world-class art institutions, like the Madrid triad <a href="/wiki/El_Prado_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="El Prado Museum">El Prado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reina_Sofia_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Reina Sofia Museum">Reina Sofia</a> or&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Thyssen-Bornemisza_Museum" title="Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum">Thyssen-Bornemisza Museums</a>, the so-called "Golden Triangle of Art", simply the best pictoric collection of the world.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Hispanic dimension of the festivity gives place too to recall the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Colonial_architecture" title="Spanish Colonial architecture">Spanish Art and Arquitecture legacy overseas</a>, mainly in the Americas at the Imperial age, including the present day United States soil. Spanish manufacture and design of Renaissance and Baroque styles erected historic quarters and buildings of most beautiful American towns and cities, today symbols of identity and object of proud of the Hispanic people.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> As an expression of this Hispanic cultural heritage, several dozens of sites all along the Americas are also listed in the UNESCO World Heritage Sites.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Much of the above has been achieved with a common instrument, the Spanish language, the world's second-most spoken native language today (soon 500 million people) and the world's third-most spoken language.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Institutions such as the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Cervantes" title="Instituto Cervantes">Instituto Cervantes</a> organize events, workshops and other cultural activities in different locations worldwide to celebrate the Hispanic legacy through the Spanish language phenomena and its fructiferous <a href="/wiki/Spanish_literature" title="Spanish literature">literature</a> through centuries. For some years since 2010, the <a href="/wiki/UN_Spanish_Language_Day" title="UN Spanish Language Day">Spanish Language Day</a> was also celebrated on Oct 12 by the United Nations, later moved to April 23 to commemorate <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_Cervantes" title="Miguel de Cervantes">Miguel de Cervantes</a>' death anniversary on that day. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_dimension">Religious dimension</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Day_of_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Religious dimension">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Spanish legacy has a strong religious component expressed with the traditional celebration of the Day of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Our Lady of the Pillar">Our Lady of the Pillar</a>, "Mother of the Hispanic Peoples" and very popular in Spain; and of the <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Guadalupe_in_Extremadura" title="Our Lady of Guadalupe in Extremadura">Our Lady of Guadalupe</a>, "Queen of the Hispanidad" who enjoys a high popularity in Hispanic America, both festivities being held as well on October 12. </p>
      <p>Our Lady of the Pillar (la Virgen del Pilar) is patroness saint of both the autonomous community of <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a> and its capital, the city of <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a> as well as of the Guardia Civil (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_Guard" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Civil Guard">Spanish Civil Guard</a>) and the Spanish Navy submarine force among others. It commemorates the coming of the Virgin Mary to Zaragoza in AD 40, according to tradition, her only apparition while she was still alive and the first to the Hispanic people. Since the religious feast day coincides with the discovery of the Americas (12 October 1492), Pope John Paul II praised El Pilar as "Mother of the Hispanic Peoples" during both his visits to the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral-Basilica_of_Our_Lady_of_the_Pillar" title="Cathedral-Basilica of Our Lady of the Pillar">Cathedral-Basilica of Our Lady of the Pillar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Custodia_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Custodia-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The 12 October, also popularly known in Spain as the «&nbsp;Día del Pilar&nbsp;», attracks many people from other Spanish regions to Zaragoza, where the traditional «&nbsp;Ofrenda de flores a la Virgen&nbsp;» (Offering of flowers&nbsp;to the Virgin Mary), a multitudinous parade with massive social participation, including Hispanic American communities, takes place all the day long. Every nation of Hispanic colonial origin has donated national vestments for the fifteenth-century statue of the Virgin, which is housed in the chapel of the cathedral.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Another relevant symbol of Hispanic heritage both in Spain and in Hispanic America is the cult of Our Lady of Guadalupe, which feast is celebrated in Extremadura on the Dia de la Hispanidad, each October 12, since she holds the title of Queen of Hispanicity since 1928. The origin of this religious tradition is the Christian legend of the apparition of the Blessed Mother to a shepherd in the XIV century indicating the place to find a wooden statue of the Virgin Mary carved by Luke the Evangelist buried centuries ago by local clergymen to prevent any damage of it from the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_Hispania" title="Umayyad conquest of Hispania">Moorish invaders</a>. The Monastery built thereafter on the site where the statue was found soon became the most important place of pilgrimage in Castile and fair rich.<sup id="cite_ref-Texas_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Texas-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Trexler_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trexler-47">[47]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Catholic Kings met in the Monastery with Columbus in June 1492, which resulted in the Monarchs' authorization of the <a href="/wiki/Voyages_of_Christopher_Columbus" title="Voyages of Christopher Columbus">exploration endeavor</a> and the dispatch of the first Royal injunctions to organize the "voyage into the unknown". In 1493, Columbus went back to the Monastery to thank Our Lady of Guadalupe for his successful first voyage and in his second, he baptized a newly found Caribbean island as <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadalupe</a>, in honor to the Spanish Virgin. This, together with the fact that many Spanish explorers came from Extremadura and the utmost popularity of Our Lady of Guadalupe in Castile, are the reason behind the spread of the <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Guadalupe" title="Our Lady of Guadalupe">worship</a> throughout America and her canonical coronation as Queen of Hispanicity.<sup id="cite_ref-Texas_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Texas-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Trexler_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trexler-47">[47]</a></sup> </p>
