      <p><b>Super Bowl Sunday</b> is the day on which the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl" title="Super Bowl">Super Bowl</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> (NFL)'s annual championship game, is played. It is currently observed on the first Sunday in February, although in 2022 will be moved to the second Sunday, and is sometimes referred to as an unofficial national holiday. The 55th annual event, <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_LV" title="Super Bowl LV">Super Bowl LV</a>, occurred on February 7, 2021. Festivities for Super Sunday typically involve groups of people gathering to watch the game. Both "Super Sunday" and "Super Bowl Sunday" are registered <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademarks</a> of the National Football League.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On Guam, it is known as Super Bowl Monday, due to the time zone, the game airs live Monday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivities">Festivities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bowl_Sunday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Festivities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Although not an <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_the_United_States" title="Public holidays in the United States">official holiday</a>, Super Sunday in an occasion when many families and friends gather together to watch the game, including those who are not normally football fans.<sup id="cite_ref-tftu_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tftu-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Although <a href="/wiki/Sports_bar" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports bar">sports bars</a> have historically been busy on Super Sunday in the past, it is becoming more common for people to watch the game from home. This is due in part to the increasing size of home televisions in the United States and Canada as well as the attempts of budget conscious consumers to save money.<sup id="cite_ref-twsj02_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twsj02-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twsj01_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twsj01-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Because watching the Super Bowl is so popular, stores are often empty during the game, particularly in the regions represented by the two teams playing in the Super Bowl,<sup id="cite_ref-tn_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tn-7">[7]</a></sup> and water usage drops, with significant rises in use during halftime and after the game, as fans use the bathroom.<sup id="cite_ref-calder20120209_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-calder20120209-8">[8]</a></sup> Additionally, churches sometimes cancel afternoon or evening services on Super Sunday, hold football-themed charity drives, or deliver sermons designed to appeal to male members of the congregation.<sup id="cite_ref-tva_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tva-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dp_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dp-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hs_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hs-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>NFL executives have called for a three-day weekend in order to allow fans to celebrate the event, and there is thought to be a loss of <a href="/wiki/Productivity" title="Productivity">productivity</a> in the American work force on Monday after the event.<sup id="cite_ref-LAT_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The television network carrying the game (either <a href="/wiki/NFL_on_CBS" title="NFL on CBS">CBS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fox_NFL" class="mw-redirect" title="Fox NFL">Fox</a>, or <a href="/wiki/NBC_Sunday_Night_Football" title="NBC Sunday Night Football">NBC</a>) will usually devote the entire day's programming schedule to the game, with extended pregame shows, <a href="/wiki/NFL_Films" title="NFL Films">NFL Films</a> retrospectives of the previous season, and special versions of the <a href="/wiki/Sunday_morning_talk_show" title="Sunday morning talk show">Sunday morning talk shows</a> in the morning and afternoon hours leading into the game. Competing networks, due to the severe loss of viewers to the Super Bowl festivities and a gentlemen's agreement not to compete against the game (<a href="/wiki/Media_cross-ownership_in_the_United_States" title="Media cross-ownership in the United States">most cable and broadcast networks are owned by a limited number of companies</a>, <a href="/wiki/NFL_on_television" class="mw-redirect" title="NFL on television">most of which hold NFL rights</a>), generally resort to low-cost <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_counterprogramming" title="Super Bowl counterprogramming">counterprogramming</a> measures like the <i><a href="/wiki/Puppy_Bowl" title="Puppy Bowl">Puppy Bowl</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Food">Food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Bowl_Sunday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Food">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>Large amounts of food and alcohol are consumed on Super Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-LAT_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-si_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wgn_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wgn-16">[16]</a></sup> The event is the second-largest food consumption event in the United States, behind only <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving_dinner" title="Thanksgiving dinner">Thanksgiving dinner</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tac_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tac-17">[17]</a></sup> and some police departments have noticed a dramatic increase in <a href="/wiki/Drunk_driving" title="Drunk driving">drunk driving</a> on Super Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-wgn_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wgn-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Super Sunday food is usually served <a href="/wiki/Buffet" title="Buffet">buffet</a> style, rather than as a sit-down meal. Foods traditionally eaten on Super Sunday include <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_wing" title="Buffalo wing">buffalo wings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chili_con_carne" title="Chili con carne">chili</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pork_ribs" title="Pork ribs">baby back ribs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dip_(food)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dip (food)">dipping sauces</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pizza" title="Pizza">pizza</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Potato_chip" title="Potato chip">potato chips</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tac_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tac-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ttl_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ttl-18">[18]</a></sup> Many <a href="/wiki/Pizza_delivery" title="Pizza delivery">pizza delivery</a> businesses see their order numbers double as roughly 60 percent of the take out ordered on Super Sunday is pizza. Roughly 28&nbsp;million pounds (13,000&nbsp;t) of chips, 1.25 billion chicken wings, and 8&nbsp;million pounds (3,600&nbsp;t) of <a href="/wiki/Guacamole" title="Guacamole">guacamole</a> are consumed during Super Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-ttl_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ttl-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wpde_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpde-19">[19]</a></sup> </p>
