      <p><b>Global Handwashing Day</b> (<b>GHD</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_international_observances#October" class="mw-redirect" title="List of international observances">international handwashing promotion campaign</a> to motivate and mobilize people around the <a href="/wiki/World" title="World">world</a> to improve their <a href="/wiki/Hand_washing" title="Hand washing">handwashing</a> habits. Washing hands at critical points during the day and washing with <a href="/wiki/Soap" title="Soap">soap</a> are both important. </p>
      <p>Global Handwashing Day occurs on 15 October of each year. The global campaign is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Consciousness_raising" title="Consciousness raising">raising awareness</a> of handwashing with soap as a key factor in <a href="/wiki/Preventive_healthcare" title="Preventive healthcare">disease prevention</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Respiratory and intestinal diseases can be reduced by 25-50%. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Implementation_and_management">Implementation and management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Implementation and management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The Global Handwashing Partnership (GHP) (formerly called "<a href="/wiki/Public-private_partnership" class="mw-redirect" title="Public-private partnership">Public Private Partnership</a>&nbsp;for Handwashing" (PPPHW)) established Global Handwashing Day in 2008 as a way to promote a global and local vision of handwashing with soap.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Steering Committee members of the GHP include <a href="/wiki/Colgate-Palmolive" title="Colgate-Palmolive">Colgate-Palmolive</a>; <a href="/wiki/FHI_360" title="FHI 360">FHI 360</a>; The <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Hygiene_and_Tropical_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine">London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine</a>; <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a>; <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>; <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a>; <a href="/wiki/University_at_Buffalo" title="University at Buffalo">University at Buffalo</a>; <a href="/wiki/USAID" class="mw-redirect" title="USAID">USAID</a>; the Water and Sanitation Programme at the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Water_Supply_and_Sanitation_Collaborative_Council" title="Water Supply and Sanitation Collaborative Council">Water Supply and Sanitation Collaborative Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Continued research on handwashing habits and practices is commissioned in conjunction with GHD. In 2011, <a href="/wiki/Svenska_Cellulosa_Aktiebolaget" class="mw-redirect" title="Svenska Cellulosa Aktiebolaget">Svenska Cellulosa Aktiebolaget</a> (SCA), sponsored a study to assess the handwashing habits of American and Canadian adults, finding that many were not using soap when washing their hands.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims">Aims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The stated aims of Global Handwashing Day are to:<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Foster and support a general culture of handwashing with soap in all societies</li> 
       <li>Shine a spotlight on the state of handwashing in each country</li> 
       <li>Raise awareness about the benefits of handwashing with soap.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Each year, over 200 million people celebrate Global Handwashing Day.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li>On 15 October 2014, <a href="/wiki/Madhya_Pradesh" title="Madhya Pradesh">Madhya Pradesh</a>, an Indian state, won the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Record</a> for the most massive handwashing program.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup> There were 1,276,425 children in 51 different districts participating.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>Sometimes, groups choose to celebrate GHD on other dates than 15 October. In <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, 300 people celebrated Global Handwashing Day in <a href="/wiki/Addis_Ababa" title="Addis Ababa">Addis Ababa</a> on 1 November in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>On 15 October 2015, Lupok Central Elementary School, Guiuan Eastern Samar, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, celebrated the Global Handwashing Day by doing the proper handwashing before starting classes.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Global Handwashing Day was initiated by the Global Handwashing Partnership (GHP) in August 2008 at the annual <a href="/wiki/World_Water_Week" class="mw-redirect" title="World Water Week">World Water Week</a> in Stockholm, Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This means that the first Global Handwashing Day took place on 15 October 2008. The date was appointed by the UN General Assembly. The year 2008 was also the <a href="/wiki/International_Year_of_Sanitation" title="International Year of Sanitation">International Year of Sanitation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The founding bodies in 2008 included: FHI360 (a nonprofit human development organization based in the US),<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Centers_for_Disease_Control_and_Prevention" title="Centers for Disease Control and Prevention">US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention</a>, <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a>, <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_and_sanitation_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Water and sanitation program">World Bank Water &amp; Sanitation Program</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Agency_for_International_Development" title="United States Agency for International Development">United States Agency for International Development</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes_for_annual_Global_Handwashing_Day">Themes for annual Global Handwashing Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Themes for annual Global Handwashing Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

       <li>2020 - Hand Hygiene for All.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>

       <li>2018 - Clean hands - a recipe for health.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2017 - Our hands, our future.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2016 - Make handwashing a habit.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2015 - Raise a hand for hygiene.<sup id="cite_ref-peacecorps.gov_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-peacecorps.gov-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2014 - Clean hands save lives. In 2014, Global Handwashing Day was used as an opportunity to fight Ebola.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In Nigeria, for example, Concern Universal and Carex sponsored events featuring singer Sunny Neji.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2013 - The power is in your hands.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2012 - I am a handwashing advocate.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2011 - Clean hands save lives.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2010 - Children and Schools.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2009 - Spread the word, not the germs.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2008 - The focus for Global Handwashing Day's inaugural year in 2008 was school children. In that year, the members pledged to get the maximum number of school children handwashing with soap in more than 70 countries. In <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> in 2008, <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> legend <a href="/wiki/Sachin_Tendulkar" title="Sachin Tendulkar">Sachin Tendulkar</a> and his teammates joined an estimated 100 million schoolchildren around the country in lathering up for better health and hygiene as part of the first Global Handwashing Day.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>The campaign was initiated to reduce childhood mortality rates and related <a href="/wiki/Respiratory" class="mw-redirect" title="Respiratory">respiratory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diarrhea" title="Diarrhea">diarrheal diseases</a> by introducing simple <a href="/wiki/Behaviour_change_(public_health)" class="mw-redirect" title="Behaviour change (public health)">behavioral changes</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Handwashing" class="mw-redirect" title="Handwashing">handwashing</a> with <a href="/wiki/Soap" title="Soap">soap</a>. This simple action can reduce the <a href="/wiki/Mortality_rate" title="Mortality rate">mortality rate</a> of respiratory disease by 25%. Death from diarrheal diseases can be reduced by 50%.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Across the world, more than 60 percent of health workers do not adhere to proper hand hygiene. According to the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, US health care providers, on average, wash their hands less than half of the time they should. On any given day, one in 25 US hospital patients has at least one health care-associated infection.<sup id="cite_ref-CDC_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDC-33">[33]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Importance_of_handwashing">Importance of handwashing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Importance of handwashing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">

      <p>Handwashing with soap is very effective and the least expensive way to prevent <a href="/wiki/Diarrhea" title="Diarrhea">diarrhea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acute_respiratory_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Acute respiratory infection">acute respiratory infections</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">Pneumonia</a>, a major ARI (acute respiratory infection), is the number one cause of mortality among children under five years old, killing an estimated 1.8&nbsp;million children per year. <a href="/wiki/Diarrhea" title="Diarrhea">Diarrhea</a> and pneumonia together account for almost 3.5&nbsp;million child deaths annually.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Handwashing with soap is estimated to reduce cases of diarrhea by 30% and respiratory infections by 21% in children under the age of five.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>It is important to make handwashing into a <a href="/wiki/Habit" title="Habit">habit</a>. Making good handwashing with soap before <a href="/wiki/Eating" title="Eating">eating</a> and after using the toilet into a regular habit can save more lives than any single <a href="/wiki/Vaccine" title="Vaccine">vaccine</a> or medical intervention, cutting deaths from diarrhea by almost half and deaths from acute <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Respiratory infection">respiratory infections</a> by one-quarter.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Handwashing is usually done together with other <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a> interventions as part of water, sanitation and hygiene <a href="/wiki/WASH" title="WASH">WASH</a> programmes. </p>
      <p>The Global Handwashing Day helps raise awareness of the importance of washing with soap, but it also makes it fun for children to get involved.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Proper hygiene requires that individuals know the importance of good hygiene and develop the habits to carry it out. There are people with plenty of money who nonetheless lack the important habits of timely handwashing with soap, and thereby unknowingly endanger themselves and others around them.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Peer_pressure" title="Peer pressure">Peer influence</a> is significant for seeing increased handwashing. In a study conducted in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, researchers found that students were much more likely to wash their hands when another student is present.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Peer influence is only successful, however, when students know that handwashing is a desirable action. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_awareness_days">Related awareness days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Related awareness days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (WHO) celebrates a World Hand Hygiene Day on 5 May. In 2018 the theme was prevention of <a href="/wiki/Sepsis" title="Sepsis">sepsis</a> in health care.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The theme of the year before was to combat <a href="/wiki/Antimicrobial_resistance" title="Antimicrobial resistance">antibiotic resistance</a> (AMR).<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaign_effectiveness">Campaign effectiveness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Handwashing_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Campaign effectiveness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A 2012 study from China attempted to qualitatively assess Chinese social media users’ reactions to Global Handwashing Day (GHD) 2012, in particular, and to health promotion campaigns in general.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> They concluded that social media data in China can be used to evaluate public health campaigns in China.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> </p>
