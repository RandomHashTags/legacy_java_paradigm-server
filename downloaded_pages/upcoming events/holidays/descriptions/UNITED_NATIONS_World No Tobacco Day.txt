      <p><b>World No Tobacco Day</b> (<b>WNTD</b>) is <a href="/wiki/International_observance#Days" class="mw-redirect" title="International observance">observed</a> around the world every year on 31 May. This yearly celebration informs the public on the dangers of using tobacco, the business practices of tobacco companies, what the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (WHO) is doing to fight against the use of tobacco, and what people around the world can do to claim their right to health and healthy living and to protect future generations. </p>
      <p>The Member States of the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">WHO</a> created World No Tobacco Day in 1987 to draw global attention to the tobacco epidemic and the preventable death and disease it causes. The day is further intended to draw attention to the widespread prevalence of tobacco use and to negative <a href="/wiki/Health_effects_of_tobacco" title="Health effects of tobacco">health effects</a>, which currently lead to more than 8&nbsp;million deaths each year worldwide, including 1.2&nbsp;million are the result of non-smokers being exposed to second-hand smoke.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The day has been met with both enthusiasm and resistance around the globe from governments, public health organizations, smokers, growers, and the tobacco industry. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="WHO_and_World_No_Tobacco_Day">WHO and World No Tobacco Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: WHO and World No Tobacco Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>WNTD is one of 11 official <a href="/wiki/Global_health" title="Global health">global public health</a> campaigns marked by the WHO, along with <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Day" title="World Health Day">World Health Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Blood_Donor_Day" title="World Blood Donor Day">World Blood Donor Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Immunization_Week" title="World Immunization Week">World Immunization Week</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Tuberculosis_Day" title="World Tuberculosis Day">World Tuberculosis Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Malaria_Day" title="World Malaria Day">World Malaria Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Hepatitis_Day" title="World Hepatitis Day">World Hepatitis Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Chagas_Disease_Day" title="World Chagas Disease Day">World Chagas Disease Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Patient_Safety_Day" title="World Patient Safety Day">World Patient Safety Day</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Antimicrobial_Awareness_Week&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World Antimicrobial Awareness Week (page does not exist)">World Antimicrobial Awareness Week </a> and <a href="/wiki/World_AIDS_Day" title="World AIDS Day">World AIDS Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li>In 1987, the WHO's <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Assembly" title="World Health Assembly">World Health Assembly</a> passed Resolution WHA40.38, calling for 7 April 1988 to be "a world no-smoking day". The objective of the day was to urge tobacco users worldwide to abstain from using tobacco products for 24 hours, an action they hoped would provide assistance for those trying to quit.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 1988, Resolution WHA42.19 was passed by the World Health Assembly, calling for the celebration of World No Tobacco Day, every year on 31 May. Since then, the WHO has supported World No Tobacco Day every year, linking each year to a different tobacco-related theme.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 1998, the WHO established the Tobacco Free Initiative (TFI), an attempt to focus international resources and attention on the <a href="/wiki/Global_health" title="Global health">global health</a> issue of tobacco. The initiative provides assistance for creating global public health policy, encourages mobilization between societies, and supports the <i><a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization_Framework_Convention_on_Tobacco_Control" class="mw-redirect" title="World Health Organization Framework Convention on Tobacco Control">World Health Organization Framework Convention on Tobacco Control</a></i> (FCTC).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The WHO FCTC is a global public health treaty adopted in 2003 by countries around the globe as an agreement to implement policies that work towards tobacco cessation.</li> 
       <li>In 2008, on the eve of the World No Tobacco Day, the WHO called for a worldwide ban on all tobacco advertising, promotion, and sponsorship. The theme of that year's day was "Tobacco-free youth"; therefore, this initiative was especially meant to target advertising efforts aimed at youth. According to the WHO, the tobacco industry must replace older quitting or dying smokers with younger consumers. Because of this, marketing strategies are commonly observed in places that will attract youth such as movies, the Internet, billboards, and magazines. Studies have shown that the more youth are exposed to tobacco advertising, the more likely they are to smoke.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 2015, WNTD highlighted the health risks associated with tobacco use and advocated for effective <a href="/wiki/Health_policy" title="Health policy">policies</a> to reduce tobacco consumption, including ending the <a href="/wiki/Cigarette_smuggling" class="mw-redirect" title="Cigarette smuggling">illicit trade of tobacco products</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 2016, on World No Tobacco Day, the WHO called on governments to get ready for <a href="/wiki/Plain_tobacco_packaging" title="Plain tobacco packaging">plain packaging</a> of tobacco products.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 2017, the focus is "A threat to development". The campaign aims to demonstrate the threats that the tobacco industry poses to <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>, including the health and economic <a href="/wiki/Well-being" title="Well-being">well-being</a> of citizens in all countries.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 2018, the focus is "Tobacco breaks hearts: choose health, not tobacco" #NoTobacco.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 2019, the focus is on "Tobacco and lung health".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>In 2020, the focus is on "Tobacco and related industry tactics to attract younger generations".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Each year, the WHO selects a theme for the day in order to create a more unified global message for WNTD. This theme then becomes the central component of the WHO's tobacco-related agenda for the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-WNTD2010_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WNTD2010-13">[13]</a></sup> The WHO oversees the creation and distribution of publicity materials related to the theme, including brochures, fliers, posters, websites, and press releases.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Videos were created as a part of the 2008 WNTD awareness campaign for the theme "Tobacco-free youth" and published on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, and podcasts were first used in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In many of its WNTD themes and related publicity-materials, the WHO emphasizes the idea of "truth." Theme titles such as "Tobacco kills, don't be duped" (2000) and "Tobacco: deadly in any form or disguise” (2006) indicate a WHO belief that individuals may be misled or confused about the true nature of tobacco; the rationale for the 2000 and 2008 WNTD themes identify the marketing strategies and “illusions” created by the tobacco industry as a primary source of this confusion.<sup id="cite_ref-WNTD2010_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WNTD2010-13">[13]</a></sup> The WHO's WNTD materials present an alternate understanding of the “facts” as seen from a global public health perspective. WNTD publicity materials provide an "official" interpretation of the most up-to-date tobacco-related research and statistics and provide a common ground from which to formulate anti-tobacco arguments around the world. The theme for World No Tobacco Day 2017 was "Tobacco – a threat to development".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2018, it is "Tobacco breaks hearts".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2019, the theme is "Make Every Day World No Tobacco Day".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Event_coordination">Event coordination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Event coordination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The WHO serves as a central hub for fostering communication and coordinating WNTD events around the world. The WHO website provides a place for groups to share news of their activities, and the organization publishes this information online by country.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Since 1988 the WHO has presented one or more awards to organizations or individuals who have made exceptional contributions to reducing tobacco consumption. World No Tobacco Day Awards are given to individuals from six different world regions (Africa, Americas, Eastern Mediterranean, Europe, South-East Asia, and Western Pacific), and Director-General Special Awards and Recognition Certificates are given to individuals from any region.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_observance">Global observance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Global observance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">

      <p>Groups around the world – from local clubs to city councils to national governments – are encouraged by the WHO to organize events each year to help communities celebrate World No Tobacco Day in their own way at the local level. Past events have included letter writing campaigns to government officials and local newspapers, marches, public debates, local and national publicity campaigns, anti-tobacco activist meetings, educational programming, and public art.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In addition, many governments use WNTD as the start date for implementing new <a href="/wiki/Tobacco_smoking" title="Tobacco smoking">smoking</a> bans and <a href="/wiki/Tobacco_control" title="Tobacco control">tobacco control</a> efforts. For example, on 31 May 2008, a section of the <i>Smoke Free Ontario Act</i> came into effect banning tobacco "power walls" and displays at stores in this <a href="/wiki/Smoking_in_Canada" title="Smoking in Canada">Canadian</a> province, and all hospitals and government offices in Australia became smoke-free on 31 May 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The day has also been used as a springboard for discussing the current and future state of a country as it relates to tobacco—for example in <a href="/wiki/Smoking_in_India" title="Smoking in India">India</a> which, with 275&nbsp;million tobacco users, has one of the highest levels of tobacco consumption in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Government of India</a> has also launched a Smoking Cessation Helpline to help curb the widespread addiction in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resistance">Resistance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Resistance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>For some, WNTD is seen as a challenge to individual freedom of choice or even a culturally acceptable form of discrimination. From ignoring WNTD, to participating in protests or acts of defiance, to bookending the day with extra rounds of pro-tobacco advertisements and events, smokers, tobacco growers, and the tobacco industry have found ways to make their opinions heard. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smoker_response">Smoker response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Smoker response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>There has been no sustained or widespread effort to organize counter-WNTD events on the part of smokers. However, some small groups, particularly in the United States, have created local pro-smoking events. For example, the <i><a href="/wiki/Oregon_Commentator" title="Oregon Commentator">Oregon Commentator</a></i>, an independent conservative journal of opinion published at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oregon" title="University of Oregon">University of Oregon</a>, hosted a "Great American Smoke-in" on campus as a counter to the locally more widespread <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Smokeout" title="Great American Smokeout">Great American Smokeout</a>: "In response to the ever-increasing vilification of smokers on campus, the Oregon Commentator presents the Great American Smoke-in as an opportunity for students to join together and enjoy the pleasures of fine tobacco products."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Similarly, "Americans for Freedom of Choice", a group in <a href="/wiki/Honolulu,_Hawaii" class="mw-redirect" title="Honolulu, Hawaii">Honolulu, Hawaii</a>, organized "World Defiance Day" in response to WNTD and Hawaii's statewide ban on smoking in restaurants.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_response">Industry response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Industry response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Historically, in America the tobacco industry has funded state initiatives that provide resources to help smokers quit smoking as per the <a href="/wiki/Master_Settlement_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Master Settlement Agreement">Master Settlement Agreement</a> regulated by the U.S. government.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> For example, <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Morris_USA" class="mw-redirect" title="Phillip Morris USA">Phillip Morris USA</a> operates a website that acts as a guide for those who choose to quit smoking.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>World No Tobacco Days have not induced a positive vocal response from the tobacco industry. For example, a memo made publicly available through the Tobacco Archives website was sent out to executives of <a href="/wiki/R.J._Reynolds_Tobacco_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="R.J. Reynolds Tobacco Company">R.J. Reynolds Tobacco Company</a> in preparation for the third annual World No Tobacco Day,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> which had the theme of "Childhood and Youth Without Tobacco". The memo includes a warning about the upcoming day, a document that explains the arguments they anticipate the WHO making, and an explanation of how the company should respond to these claims. For example, in response to the anticipated argument that their advertisements target children, the company's response includes arguments that claim their advertisements are targeted towards adults by using adult models, and that advertisements lack the power to influence what people will actually purchase.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, since the World No Tobacco Day is the one day that the media is obligated to publicize tobacco control issues, the British American Tobacco company uses the eve of the day to administer counter-publicity. In 2001, their strategy included events such as a visit with the President of the International Tobacco Growers Association.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Unlike the tobacco industry, some major pharmaceutical companies do publicly support WNTD. For example, <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a> was a large sponsor for many WNTD events in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> in 2008. At the time, Pfizer was preparing to release its drug Chantix (<a href="/wiki/Varenicline" title="Varenicline">varenicline</a>) into the Middle Eastern market. The drug was "designed to activate the nicotinic receptor to reduce both the severity of the smoker's craving and the withdrawal symptoms from nicotine."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grower_response">Grower response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Grower response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Many tobacco growers feel that anti-tobacco efforts by organizations such as the WHO jeopardize their rights. For example, the International Tobacco Growers Association (ITGA) argues that poor farmers in Africa may suffer the consequences if WHO anti-tobacco movements succeed. They also argue that these efforts may gang up on manufacturers of tobacco and be an attack on the industry, therefore hurting the growers.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Potential_for_Indigenous_American_opposition">Potential for Indigenous American opposition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_No_Tobacco_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Potential for Indigenous American opposition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>As some traditions and ceremonies of a number of cultures and ethnicities of <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans in the United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_Nations" title="First Nations">First Nations</a> in North America have been based on tobacco since pre-Columbian times, some potential for unintended abrogation of such traditions may exist from authorities seeking to eliminate tobacco from worldwide use – traditions of <i>non</i>-combustive use of tobacco for some forms of indigenous American ceremonial purposes have begun to be used for <a href="/wiki/Smoking_cessation" title="Smoking cessation">cessation of cigarette use</a> among indigenous tribal members,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> while members of the <a href="/wiki/Oglala_Lakota" class="mw-redirect" title="Oglala Lakota">Oglala Lakota</a> have had their struggles to retain important historic tribal artifacts used for tobacco's traditional role in their ethnicity's traditions, to prevent their illegal sale.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> It is possible that a 2015 survey from <a href="/wiki/Health_Canada" title="Health Canada">Health Canada</a> concerning future tobacco control legislation in Canada, having a section requesting advice from indigenous peoples within Canada, showed the potential of concern over such issues.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> </p>
