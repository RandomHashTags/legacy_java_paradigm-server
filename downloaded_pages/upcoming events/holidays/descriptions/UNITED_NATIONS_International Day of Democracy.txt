      <p>In 2007 the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> resolved <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#September" title="List of minor secular observances">to observe</a> 15 September as the <b>International Day of Democracy</b>—with the purpose of promoting and upholding the principles of <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>—and invited all member states and organizations to commemorate the day in an appropriate manner that contributes to <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raising public awareness</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UN_ARES627_page3_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES627_page3-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>…while democracies share common features, there is no single model of democracy and that democracy does not belong to any country or region... …democracy is a <a href="/wiki/Universal_value" title="Universal value">universal value</a> based on the freely-expressed will of people to determine their own political, economic, social and cultural systems, and their full <a href="/wiki/Participation_(decision_making)" class="mw-redirect" title="Participation (decision making)">participation</a> in all aspects of life.</p>
      </blockquote>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In September 1997 the <a href="/wiki/Inter-Parliamentary_Union" title="Inter-Parliamentary Union">Inter-Parliamentary Union</a> (IPU) adopted a Universal Declaration on Democracy.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> That Declaration affirms the principles of democracy, the elements and exercise of democratic government, and the international scope of democracy. </p>
      <p>The international conferences on new and restored democracies<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (ICNRD process) began in 1988 under the initiative of President <a href="/wiki/Corazon_C._Aquino" class="mw-redirect" title="Corazon C. Aquino">Corazon C. Aquino</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> after the so-called peaceful "<a href="/wiki/People_Power_Revolution" title="People Power Revolution">People Power Revolution</a>" overthrew the 20-year dictatorship of <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Marcos" title="Ferdinand Marcos">Ferdinand Marcos</a>. Initially an inter-governmental forum, the ICNRD process developed into a tripartite structure with participation of governments, parliaments and civil society. The sixth conference (ICNRD-6) that took place in <a href="/wiki/Doha" title="Doha">Doha</a>, Qatar, in 2006 reinforced the tripartite nature of the process and concluded with a declaration and Plan of Action which reaffirmed the fundamental principles and values of democracy. </p>
      <p>Following up on the outcome of ICNRD-6, an advisory board set up by the chair of the process – Qatar – decided to promote an International Day of Democracy. Qatar took the lead in drafting the text of a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> resolution and convened consultations with UN member states. At the suggestion of the IPU, on 15 September (date of the Universal Declaration on Democracy) was chosen as the day when the international community would celebrate each year the International Day of Democracy. The resolution entitled "Support by the United Nations system of efforts of Governments to promote and consolidate new or restored democracies",<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> was adopted by consensus on 8 November 2007. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations_in_2008">Celebrations in 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Celebrations in 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The IPU has urged parliaments to celebrate the International Day of Democracy through some form of special activity, to be held on or as close to 15 September as possible depending on circumstances. The day will be an opportunity for parliaments to: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Emphasize the importance of democracy, what it involves, the challenges it faces as well as the opportunities it offers, and the central responsibility that all parliaments have as the key <a href="/wiki/Institution" title="Institution">institution</a> of democracy;</li> 
       <li>Examine and discuss how well parliament performs its democratic functions, possibly on the basis of a <a href="/wiki/Self-assessment" title="Self-assessment">self-assessment</a>, and identify what steps it may take to strengthen its effectiveness.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>To mark the first International Day of Democracy on 15 September 2008, the IPU will hold a special event at the House of Parliaments in Geneva. National parliaments are invited to organise their own democracy-related activities on that day to highlight the role of parliament as the cornerstone of democracy . </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations_in_2009">Celebrations in 2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Celebrations in 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations_in_2013">Celebrations in 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Celebrations in 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In 2013, IPU promoted the International Day of Democracy through its Member Parliaments in 162 countries around the world. As a result, a number of parliaments from all over the globe announced their events to be held on or close to 15 September.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The year's theme was "Strengthening Voices for Democracy" and IPU launched an online contest<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> to hear and gather stories from local democracy champions that managed to make their voices heard. These stories were to inspire people to take action in their own community. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations_in_2014">Celebrations in 2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Celebrations in 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The theme for the International Day of Democracy in 2014 is "Engaging youth on democracy".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>IPU has urged action and changes in mindset if disillusioned and <a href="/wiki/Social_alienation" title="Social alienation">alienated</a> youth the world over are to be engaged in political <a href="/wiki/Decision-making" title="Decision-making">decision-making</a>. </p>
      <p>In its press release, IPU President Abdelwahad Radi said, "It is a cliché to always link youth to the future. Young people not only have the power to define the future, but also decide on the present. However, they are largely absent from formal decision-making politics and this has to change". </p>
      <p>The IPU says youth participation has a special meaning for it and that a programme to promote young men and women’s involvement in the democratic process is getting underway, in follow-up to the resolution<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> adopted by the IPU Assembly in 2010. The IPU announced it would organize the first Global Conference of Young Parliamentarians on 10 and 11 October 2014 and all parliaments were invited to attend. </p>
      <p>A photo contest<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> titled "Engage for Change" encourages youth to show what action they take to bring about positive change in society by sending a photo that shows them working for positive change in their community, region, country or the world. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations_in_2018">Celebrations in 2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Celebrations in 2018">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The theme for this year’s observance is "Democracy under Strain: Solutions for a Changing World". Coinciding with the 70th anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">Universal Declaration of Human Rights</a>, the Day is also an opportunity to highlight the values of freedom and respect for human rights as essential elements of democracy. <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations_in_2019">Celebrations in 2019</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Celebrations in 2019">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The 2019 theme for democracy day was "participation".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>This year's International Day of Democracy is an opportunity to recall that democracy is about people. Democracy is built on inclusion, equal treatment and participation—and it is a fundamental building block for peace, sustainable development, and human rights.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations_in_2020">Celebrations in 2020</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Democracy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Celebrations in 2020">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The theme for International Day of Democracy 2020 is "COVID-19: A Spotlight on Democracy."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>Almost every Country is implementing measures to fight this pandemic. It is also important that the countries uphold the rule of law, protect and respect international standards and basic principles of <a href="/wiki/Legality" title="Legality">legality</a>, and the right to access justice, remedies, and <a href="/wiki/Due_process_of_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Due process of law">due process of law</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
