      <p><b>Leif Erikson Day</b> is an annual observance that occurs on October 9.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It honors <a href="/wiki/Leif_Erikson" title="Leif Erikson">Leif Erikson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Old Norse</a>: <i>Leifr Eir√≠ksson</i>, <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_language" title="Icelandic language">Icelandic</a>: <i lang="is">Leifur Eir√≠ksson</i>, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <i lang="no">Leiv Eiriksson</i>, <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>: <i>Leif Eriksson</i>), the <a href="/wiki/Norse_colonization_of_North_America" title="Norse colonization of North America">Norse explorer</a> who led the first <a href="/wiki/European_ethnic_groups" class="mw-redirect" title="European ethnic groups">Europeans</a> thought <a href="/wiki/L%27Anse_aux_Meadows" title="L'Anse aux Meadows">to have set foot</a> in continental <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> (other than <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leif_Erikson_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The 1874 book <i>America Not Discovered by Columbus</i> by <a href="/wiki/Norwegian-American" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian-American">Norwegian-American</a> <a href="/wiki/Rasmus_B._Anderson" title="Rasmus B. Anderson">Rasmus B. Anderson</a> helped popularize the idea that <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a> were the <a href="/wiki/Norse_colonization_of_the_Americas" class="mw-redirect" title="Norse colonization of the Americas">first Europeans in the New World</a>, an idea <a href="/wiki/L%27Anse_aux_Meadows" title="L'Anse aux Meadows">that was verified in 1960</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> During his appearance at the <a href="/wiki/Norse-American_Centennial" title="Norse-American Centennial">Norse-American Centennial</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_State_Fair" title="Minnesota State Fair">Minnesota State Fair</a> in 1925, President <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Coolidge" title="Calvin Coolidge">Calvin Coolidge</a> gave recognition to Leif Erikson as the discoverer of America due to research by Norwegian-American scholars such as <a href="/wiki/Knut_Gjerset" title="Knut Gjerset">Knut Gjerset</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludvig_Hektoen" title="Ludvig Hektoen">Ludvig Hektoen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1929, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> to officially adopt Leif Erikson Day as a <a href="/wiki/State_holiday#Legal_holidays_by_states" class="mw-redirect" title="State holiday">state holiday</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> thanks in large part to efforts by Rasmus Anderson.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1931, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> did also.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Thanks to the efforts of the Leif Erikson Memorial Association of Saskatchewan, the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Saskatchewan" title="Legislative Assembly of Saskatchewan">Legislative Assembly of Saskatchewan</a> proclaimed‚Äîthrough an order-in-council in 1936‚Äîthat Leif Ericsson Day would be observed on October 9.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> By 1956, Leif Erikson Day had been made an official observance in seven states (Wisconsin, Minnesota, <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, and <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>) and one Canadian province (<a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1963, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Senator" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Senator">Senator</a> <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Humphrey" title="Hubert Humphrey">Hubert Humphrey</a> and <a href="/wiki/U.S._Representative" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Representative">Representative</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Blatnik" title="John Blatnik">John Blatnik</a>, both from Minnesota, introduced bills to observe Leif Erikson Day nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On September 2, 1964, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> unanimously authorized and requested the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> to create the observance through an annual <a href="/wiki/List_of_observances_in_the_United_States_by_presidential_proclamation" title="List of observances in the United States by presidential proclamation">proclamation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson" title="Lyndon B. Johnson">Lyndon B. Johnson</a> did so that year,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> as has each president in the years since,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="explorelacrosse.com is not a reliable source, and it cirtes no other sources itself (July 2020)">better&nbsp;source&nbsp;needed</span></a></i>]</sup> often using the proclamation to praise the contributions of Americans of Nordic descent generally and the spirit of discovery.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Bills have been introduced in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Canada" title="Parliament of Canada">Parliament of Canada</a> to observe Leif Erikson Day.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Date">Date</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leif_Erikson_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Date">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>October 9 is not associated with any particular event in Leif Erikson's life.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The date was chosen because the ship <i><a href="/wiki/Restauration_(ship)" title="Restauration (ship)">Restauration</a></i> coming from <a href="/wiki/Stavanger" title="Stavanger">Stavanger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, arrived in <a href="/wiki/New_York_Harbor" title="New York Harbor">New York Harbor</a> on October 9, 1825, beginning a wave of immigration from Norway to America.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Observance">Observance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leif_Erikson_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Observance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">federal government</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> observes the holiday and some U.S. states officially commemorate Leif Erikson Day. It is celebrated in many communities, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Upper_Midwest" title="Upper Midwest">Upper Midwest</a> and other places where large numbers of people from the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_countries" title="Nordic countries">Nordic countries</a> settled.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> It has long been observed in <a href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" class="mw-redirect" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In 2012, the day was made official in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Westby,_Wisconsin" title="Westby, Wisconsin">Westby, Wisconsin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway,_Michigan" title="Norway, Michigan">Norway, Michigan</a> have held festivals near the day.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> There have been Canadian commemorations, including in <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Charlottetown" title="Charlottetown">Charlottetown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The day is also celebrated in <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> </p>
