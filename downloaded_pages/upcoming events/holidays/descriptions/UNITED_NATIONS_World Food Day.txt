      <p><b>World Food Day</b> is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#October" title="List of minor secular observances">international day</a> celebrated every year worldwide on 16 October to commemorate the date of the founding of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="Food and Agriculture Organization">Food and Agriculture Organization</a> in 1945. The day is celebrated widely by many other organizations concerned with hunger and <a href="/wiki/Food_security" title="Food security">food security</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/World_Food_Programme" title="World Food Programme">World Food Programme</a> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Fund_for_Agricultural_Development" title="International Fund for Agricultural Development">International Fund for Agricultural Development</a>. WFP received the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobel Prize in Peace">Nobel Prize in Peace</a> for 2020 for their efforts to combat hunger, contribute to peace in conflict areas, and for playing a leading role in stopping the use of hunger in the form of a weapon for war and conflict.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The World Food Day theme for 2014 was Family Farming: "Feeding the world, caring for the earth"; in 2015 it was "Social Protection and Agriculture: Breaking the Cycle of Rural Poverty"; in 2016 it is Climate Change: "Climate is changing. Food and agriculture must too",<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup> which echoes the theme of 2008, and of 2002 and 1989 before that. The theme of 2020 was "Grow, nourish, sustain. Together. Our actions are our future."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <p><b>World Food Day</b> (WFD) was established by FAO's Member Countries at the Organization's 20th General Conference in November 1979. The Hungarian Delegation, led by the former Hungarian Minister of Agriculture and Food Dr. <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1l_Rom%C3%A1ny" title="Pál Romány">Pál Romány</a>, played an active role at the 20th Session of the FAO Conference and suggested the idea of celebrating the WFD worldwide. It has since been observed every year in more than 150 countries, <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raising awareness</a> of the issues behind poverty and hunger. </p>

      <p>Since 1981, World Food Day has adopted a different theme each year in order to highlight areas needed for action and provide a common focus. FAO issued World Food Day medals each year to commemorate and promote the anniversary. </p>
      <p>Most of the themes revolve around agriculture because only investment in agriculture – together with support for education and health – will turn this situation around. The bulk of that investment will have to come from the <a href="/wiki/Private_sector" title="Private sector">private sector</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Public_investment" class="mw-redirect" title="Public investment">public investment</a> playing a crucial role, especially in view of its facilitating and stimulating effect on private investment. </p>
      <p>In spite of the importance of agriculture as the driving force in the economies of many developing countries, this vital sector is frequently starved of investment. In particular, foreign aid to agriculture has shown marked declines over the past 20 years. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Food_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In over 150 countries, events mark World Food Day. Examples of events across the world held in recent years are listed. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Food_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><i>World Food Day</i> is celebrated in honour of the date of the founding of the FAO of the United Nations in 1945. It is also followed in India. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Food_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>World Food Day has been a tradition in the United States since the first World Food Day in 1982. In the United States the endeavor is sponsored by 450 national, private voluntary organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-world_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-world-4">[4]</a></sup> One example for World Food Day events is the World Food Day Sunday Dinners that <a href="/wiki/Oxfam_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxfam America">Oxfam America</a> sponsors in collaboration with several other non-profits.<sup id="cite_ref-oxfam_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxfam-5">[5]</a></sup> Emeritus <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_Desmond_Tutu" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishop Desmond Tutu">Archbishop Desmond Tutu</a><sup id="cite_ref-tutu_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tutu-6">[6]</a></sup> and author <a href="/wiki/Francis_Moore_Lappe" class="mw-redirect" title="Francis Moore Lappe">Francis Moore Lappe</a><sup id="cite_ref-lappe_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lappe-7">[7]</a></sup> have teamed up with Oxfam America to promote World Food Day Sunday Dinners. The Iowa Hunger Summit has been held on or near World Food Day since 2007, and is organized by the <a href="/wiki/World_Food_Prize" title="World Food Prize">World Food Prize</a> in conjunction with their annual symposium in Des Moines, Iowa.<sup id="cite_ref-iowa_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iowa-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Food_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Italy, ministries, universities, research agencies, international agencies, and <a href="/wiki/NGOs" class="mw-redirect" title="NGOs">NGOs</a> have organized many conferences as well as exhibitions and symposia. The Italian Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry Policies organized a meeting which focused on women's rights in rural areas in 2005. </p>
      <p>In Germany, the Federal Ministry of Consumer Protection, Food and Agriculture have all become involved via press conferences. Spanish television has been active in broadcasting events. FAO Goodwill Ambassador – Spanish soccer star Raul – has taken part in events and helped highlight food-security issues across his country. </p>
      <p>The UK Food Group has also been active through conferences and media broadcasts. In the emerging economies of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> – i.e., <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, Czech Republic, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, Hungary, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Slovak Republic">Slovak Republic</a> – a variety of activities have been held. </p>
      <p>In Hungary, renowned experts have given presentations in the Hungarian Agricultural Museum and FAO, and WFD medals have been awarded to well-known Hungarian experts by the FAO Sub-Regional Representative. </p>
      <p>On behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>, Popes <a href="/wiki/John_Paul_II" class="mw-redirect" title="John Paul II">John Paul II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benedict_XVI" class="mw-redirect" title="Benedict XVI">Benedict XVI</a> have sent an annual message for food producers and consumers on World Food Day.<sup id="cite_ref-vatican_XIV_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vatican_XIV-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vatican_XII_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vatican_XII-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fao_150_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fao_150-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fao_2004_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fao_2004-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-catholic_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-catholic-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cathnews_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cathnews-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-radio_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radio-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> celebrated WFD in 2005 through the 4th Forum on Rural Women, while in <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a> the second Vice-President planted potatoes to provide a symbolic example about food production. In <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>, the President of the Republic has inaugurated a bridge at Boda to coincide with World Food Day, making the agricultural production area more accessible. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>, thousands of people have attended debates, conferences and activities including theatre, films, folk dance, visits to project sites and visits by agricultural companies. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, the Ministry of Food and Agriculture has hosted a food security conference, while <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> has run an awareness campaign through national media. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>, the National Food technology research center recently exhibited its products and services at the World Food Day commemoration held at Kalakamati Farm on 19 October 2017. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> has hosted a Forum on nutrition issues. <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> have held seminars and exhibitions. In <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, organizations and individuals involved in feeding programs (e.g. Foodbank Nigeria) connect with other stakeholders in food production, agro-allied industries, wholesalers and community-based organizations to address food security challenges. For example, since 2009, Northern Nigeria is unstable. According to the humanitarian organisation <a href="/wiki/Action_Against_Hunger" title="Action Against Hunger">Action Against Hunger</a> (AAH), the ongoing and deepening humanitarian crisis in Northeast Nigeria has led to the displacement of over 1.5 million people, causing four million people to experience acute food insecurity and be in need of humanitarian assistance (Action Against Hunger). Since 2010, the AAH have been working with both "national agencies" and "local communities" to build capacity to treat deadly malnutrition caused by food insecurity (Action Against Hunger).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>The Government of <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> has been involved through organizing a food festival; in China in 2005, celebrations were organized in <a href="/wiki/Qujing_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Qujing City">Qujing City</a>, where numerous ethnical minorities live, by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Agriculture">Ministry of Agriculture</a> and the Government of Qujing City, with the participation of a number of senior officials of the Government. </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_People%27s_Republic_of_Korea" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic People's Republic of Korea">Democratic People's Republic of Korea</a>, seminars have been held and visits made to various project sites. The Ministry of Agriculture of <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> has in the past organized a major Food Expo in Bandung, West Java, while a Farmers' and Fishermen's Workshop of NGOs was held in <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">Bali</a>. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, staff from the Ministry of Agriculture, non-governmental organizations, Armenian State Agriculture University, the donor community, international organizations, and the mass media have participated in the World Food Day ceremony. In <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, representatives of Ministries, embassies, UN agencies, International Financial Organizations, National and International NGOs, and FAO staff have attended the World Food Day ceremony. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, special ceremonies have been organized in primary and secondary schools, where teachers explained the significance of World Food Day. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, a Society Named as MAPS (Mentor Amiable Professional Society) celebrates world food day by providing food packages to poor &amp; née-dies and tells the importance of food to the people by organizing workshops. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> on 16 October 2015, writer and realty entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Wilson_Lee_Flores" title="Wilson Lee Flores">Wilson Lee Flores</a> started celebrating "World Pandesal Day" at the non-partisan Pandesal Forum of his popular Kamuning Bakery Cafe in Judge Jimenez Street corner K-1st Street, Quezon City. He and celebrities like <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="GMA Network, Inc.">GMA Network, Inc.</a> Chairman <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Gozon" title="Felipe Gozon">Atty. Felipe Gozon</a>, Senator <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Angara" title="Sonny Angara">Sonny Angara</a> and actor <a href="/wiki/Dingdong_Dantes" title="Dingdong Dantes">Dingdong Dantes</a> gave away 30,000 "pugon" or wood-fired brick oven breads plus other gifts to urban poor families. In 2016, he repeated this civic project with celebrities like Quezon City Vice-Mayor <a href="/wiki/Joy_Belmonte" title="Joy Belmonte">Joy Belmonte</a> and business leader Dr. James Dy of the Philippine Chinese Charitable Association, plus undertaking free medical, dental and optical missions for urban poor families. </p>
      <p>On 16 October 2017, Wilson Lee Flores increased gifts to urban poor families to 50,000 pugon-baked breads, Mega sardines, King Sue hams, Fly Ace and Hobe noodles, OK Koolers juices, plus two dates for free medical, dental and optical missions on 8 October and 29 October. Special guests at this 3rd "World Pandesal Day" were led by Supreme Court Chief Justice <a href="/wiki/Maria_Lourdes_Sereno" title="Maria Lourdes Sereno">Maria Lourdes Sereno</a>, Vice-President <a href="/wiki/Leni_Robredo" title="Leni Robredo">Leni Robredo</a> and Philippine National Police (PNP) chief General <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Dela_Rosa" class="mw-redirect" title="Ronald Dela Rosa">Ronald Dela Rosa</a> accompanied by Quezon City senior superintendent Guillermo Eleazar. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, for the World Food Day celebration in the country, it has become a tradition that the research conference "Food security" is annually organized by Ministry of Food, Agriculture and Light Industry and UN FAO representative office in Mongolia cooperated with Mongolian Food Industry Association. This traditional event gives a significant opportunity to promote research works, to value the contributions on the country's food security of scholars and researchers, to strengthen the cooperation and collaboration between research institutions, NGOs and food related public organization, to transfer the technological research in the industry and to develop the research-based policy and regulations etc. </p>
