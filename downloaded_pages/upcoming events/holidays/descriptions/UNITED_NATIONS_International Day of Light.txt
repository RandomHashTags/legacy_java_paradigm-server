      <p>The <b>International Day of Light</b> is an international observance established by the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization</a> (UNESCO) <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to provide an annual opportunity to raise awareness of the importance of light science and technology in sustainable development, and to promote the broader place of light in art and culture. </p>
      <p>The International Day of Light is a follow-up to the <a href="/wiki/International_Year_of_Light" title="International Year of Light">International Year of Light and Light-based Technologies</a> 2015, and is celebrated annually on <a href="/wiki/May_16" title="May 16">May 16</a>, the anniversary of the first successful operation of a <a href="/wiki/Laser#Laser" title="Laser">laser</a> in 1960. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Light&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The International Day of Light proposal was first examined by the UNESCO Executive Board during its 200th Session in Paris held from 4<a href="/wiki/Caracas,_Venezuela" class="mw-redirect" title="Caracas, Venezuela">Caracas, Venezuela</a>, 18 October 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> introduced by sponsors <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian Federation</a>. A favorable decision was taken with 15 additional co-sponsors from: <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>. The UNESCO General Conference formally proclaimed the International Day of Light during its 39th session on the 7 November 2017 with additional support being expressed by <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of Congo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Togo" title="Togo">Togo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Light&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The International Day of Light is administered from the International Basic Science Programme (IBSP) of UNESCO by a Steering Committee that includes representatives from a broad range of international partners:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/American_Institute_of_Physics" title="American Institute of Physics">American Institute of Physics</a> (AIP), the <a href="/wiki/American_Physical_Society" title="American Physical Society">American Physical Society</a> (APS), Bosca, the China International Optoelectronic Exhibition (CIOE), <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Optical_Society" title="Chinese Optical Society">Chinese Optical Society</a> (COS), the European Centres for Outreach in Photonics (ECOP), the <a href="/wiki/European_Photonics_Industry_Consortium" title="European Photonics Industry Consortium">European Photonics Industry Consortium</a> (EPIC), the <a href="/wiki/European_Physical_Society" title="European Physical Society">European Physical Society</a> (EPS), the <a href="/wiki/Illuminating_Engineering_Society" title="Illuminating Engineering Society">Illuminating Engineering Society</a> (IES), the <a href="/wiki/International_Centre_for_Theoretical_Physics" title="International Centre for Theoretical Physics">International Centre for Theoretical Physics</a> (ICTP), the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Photonics_Society" title="IEEE Photonics Society">IEEE Photonics Society</a> (IPS),&nbsp; the <a href="/wiki/International_Commission_on_Illumination" title="International Commission on Illumination">International Commission on Illumination</a> (CIE), lightsources.org - the international network of accelerator based light sources, Light: Science and Applications, The <a href="/wiki/The_Optical_Society" title="The Optical Society">Optical Society</a> (OSA), Tampere University, the International Society for Optics and Photonics (SPIE), SESAME, Signify, Transitions, the <a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_de_Franche-Comt%C3%A9" class="mw-redirect" title="Université de Franche-Comté">Université de Franche-Comté</a> and Velux. </p>
