      <p>In April 2018, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> declared <b><a href="/wiki/June_3" title="June 3">June 3</a></b> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#June" title="List of minor secular observances">International</a> <b>World Bicycle Day</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The resolution for World Bicycle Day recognizes "the uniqueness, longevity and versatility of the <a href="/wiki/Bicycle" title="Bicycle">bicycle</a>, which has been in use for two centuries, and that it is a simple, affordable, reliable, clean and <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmentally</a> fit <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable</a> means of transport."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_founding_of_World_Bicycle_Day">The founding of World Bicycle Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Bicycle_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The founding of World Bicycle Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Professor <a href="/wiki/Leszek_Sibilski" title="Leszek Sibilski">Leszek Sibilski</a>, Polish social scientist working in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, led a grassroots campaign with his sociology class to promote a UN Resolution for World Bicycle Day, eventually gaining the support of <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a> and 56 other countries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The original UN Blue and White #June3WorldBicycleDay logo was designed by Isaac Feld and the accompanying animation was done by Professor John E. Swanson. It depicts bicyclists of various types riding around the globe. At the bottom of the logo is the <a href="/wiki/Hashtag" title="Hashtag">hashtag</a> #June3WorldBicycleDay. The main message is to show that the bicycle belongs to and serves all of humanity.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_significance_of_World_Bicycle_Day">The significance of World Bicycle Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Bicycle_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The significance of World Bicycle Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>World Bicycle Day is a global holiday meant to be enjoyed by all people regardless of any characteristic. The bicycle as a symbol of human progress and advancement "[promotes] <a href="/wiki/Toleration" title="Toleration">tolerance</a>, mutual <a href="/wiki/Understanding" title="Understanding">understanding</a> and respect and [facilitates] <a href="/wiki/Social_inclusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Social inclusion">social inclusion</a> and a culture of peace."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> The bicycle further is a "symbol of sustainable transport and conveys a positive message to foster sustainable consumption and production, and has a positive impact on climate."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>World Bicycle Day is now being associated with promoting a healthy <a href="/wiki/Lifestyle_(sociology)" title="Lifestyle (sociology)">lifestyle</a> for those with Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
