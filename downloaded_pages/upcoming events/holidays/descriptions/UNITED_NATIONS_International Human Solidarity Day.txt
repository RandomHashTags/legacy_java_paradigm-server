   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading">International Human Solidarity Day</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub" class="noprint">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="contentSub2"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a> 
    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">
       United Nations annual unity day
      </div> 
      <p>The <b>International Human Solidarity Day</b> (IHSD), <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#December" title="List of minor secular observances">observed</a> on December 20, is an international annual unity day of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and its member states introduced by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">general assembly</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Summit" title="2005 World Summit">2005 World Summit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hindustan_Times_2019_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hindustan_Times_2019-1">[1]</a></sup> It was established on December 22, 2005 by resolution 60/209.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Its main goal is to recognize the <a href="/wiki/Universal_value" title="Universal value">universal values</a> of poor by making the <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">associated countries</a> aware of <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">reducing poverty</a> and to formulate its countermeasures as signed by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states" title="List of sovereign states">independent states</a>. IHSD is a part of <a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Solidarity_Fund&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World Solidarity Fund (page does not exist)">World Solidarity Fund</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Programme" title="United Nations Development Programme">United Nations Development Programme</a> focused on to achieve goals set for worldwide poverty eradication.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Human_Solidarity_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The International Human Solidarity Day was established under the influence of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Millennium_Declaration" title="United Nations Millennium Declaration">U.N. Millennium Declaration</a> that determines the <a href="/wiki/Civil_and_political_rights" title="Civil and political rights">civil and political rights</a> of an individual in the modern era by establishing the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations" title="Foreign relations">foreign relations</a> between the member states and the U.N.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The General Assembly of December 22, 2005 by resolution 60/209 recognized <a href="/wiki/Solidarity" title="Solidarity">solidarity</a> as one of the fundamental and <a href="/wiki/Universal_value" title="Universal value">universal values</a> and proclaimed the International Human Solidarity Day. IHSD is held every year on December 20 to promote the importance of sharing and the culture of solidarity to combat poverty and to encourage governments, NGOs, and almost every individual to organise programmes and <a href="/wiki/Debate" title="Debate">debates</a> either on a national or international level so that <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">poverty can be reduced</a> by spreading the <a href="/wiki/Awareness" title="Awareness">awareness</a>. An individual can participate or celebrate the day either by contributing to education or help the poor. This day is also celebrated by helping <a href="/wiki/Social_anxiety" title="Social anxiety">socially</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mental_disorder" title="Mental disorder">mentally challenged</a> people. However, governments are much expected to respond the poverty and other barriers in society through <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals" title="Sustainable Development Goals">Sustainable Development Goals</a>, designed to facilitate good future for all.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-timeanddate.com_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeanddate.com-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the International Human Solidarity Day is </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>a day to celebrate our <a href="/wiki/Unity_in_diversity" title="Unity in diversity">unity in diversity</a>;</li> 
       <li>a day to remind governments to respect their commitments to international agreements;</li> 
       <li>a day to raise public awareness of the importance of solidarity;</li> 
       <li>a day to encourage debate on the ways to promote solidarity for the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals including poverty eradication;</li> 
       <li>a day of action to encourage new initiatives for poverty eradication.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
      </ul>
