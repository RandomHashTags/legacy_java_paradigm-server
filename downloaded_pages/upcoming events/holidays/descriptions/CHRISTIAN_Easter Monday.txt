      <p><b>Easter Monday</b> is the day after <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter Sunday</a> and is a public holiday in some countries. It is the second day of <a href="/wiki/Eastertide" title="Eastertide">Eastertide</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Western_Christianity" title="Western Christianity">Western Christianity</a>, it is also the second day of the <a href="/wiki/Octave_of_Easter" title="Octave of Easter">Octave of Easter</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Christianity" title="Eastern Christianity">Eastern Christianity</a>, it is also the second day of <a href="/wiki/Bright_Week" title="Bright Week">Bright Week</a>. </p>
      <p>Easter Monday is celebrated in the Catholic church and also called the Monday of the Angel "because we recall the meeting of the angel with the women who arrived at Jesus’s tomb (see Mt 28:1-15). The angel said to them: “I know that you seek Jesus who was crucified. He is not here; for he has risen (vv. 5-6). This expression “He has risen” goes beyond human capacity. Even the women who had gone to the tomb and had found it open and empty could not confirm “He has risen”, but they could only say that the tomb was empty. “He has risen” is a message… Only an angel could say that Jesus had risen, only an angel with the authority to be the bearer of a heavenly message, with the power given by God to say it, just as an angel – only an angel – had been able to say to Mary: “you will conceive a son, [….] and he will be called the Son of the Most High” (Lk 1:31-32). Because of this we call it Monday of the Angel because only an angel with the power of God could say that Jesus had risen".(Regina Caeli, 2021)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox Church</a> and <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Rite" title="Byzantine Rite">Byzantine Rite</a> <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Catholic_Churches" title="Eastern Catholic Churches">Catholic Churches</a>, this day is called "Bright Monday" or "Renewal Monday". The services, as in the rest of Bright Week, are quite different from during the rest of the year and are similar to the services on Pascha (Easter Sunday) and include an outdoor <a href="/wiki/Procession#Outdoors" title="Procession">procession</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Divine_Liturgy" title="Divine Liturgy">Divine Liturgy</a>; while this is prescribed for all days of that week, often they are only celebrated on Monday and maybe a couple of other days in parish churches, especially in non-Orthodox countries. Also, when the calendar date of the <a href="/wiki/Feast_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Feast day">feast day</a> of a major saint, <i>e.g.</i>, <a href="/wiki/St._George" class="mw-redirect" title="St. George">St. George</a> or the patron saint of a church or one's <a href="/wiki/Name_day" title="Name day">name day</a>, falls during <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week" title="Holy Week">Holy Week</a> or on Easter Sunday, the saint's day is celebrated on Easter Monday.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Australia, Easter Monday is a public holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Some people enjoy outdoor sporting events, such as the <a href="/wiki/Oakbank_Easter_Racing_Carnival" title="Oakbank Easter Racing Carnival">Oakbank Easter Racing Carnival</a> in South Australia,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Stawell_Gift" title="Stawell Gift">Stawell Gift</a> in Victoria,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> as well as a traditional <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_League" title="Australian Football League">AFL</a> match between <a href="/wiki/Geelong_Cats" class="mw-redirect" title="Geelong Cats">Geelong Cats</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hawthorn_Hawks" class="mw-redirect" title="Hawthorn Hawks">Hawthorn Hawks</a> at the MCG. The <a href="/wiki/Australian_Three_Peaks_Race" title="Australian Three Peaks Race">Australian Three Peaks Race</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a> until 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Austria">Austria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Austria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Emmausgang" title="Emmausgang">Emmausgang</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In Austria and Southern Germany, there is the traditional "<a href="/wiki/Emmausgang" title="Emmausgang">Emmausgang</a>", commemorating the walk of the <a href="/wiki/Disciple_(Christianity)" title="Disciple (Christianity)">disciples</a> to <a href="/wiki/Emmaus" title="Emmaus">Emmaus</a>, to which Jesus followed them without being recognized. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Easter Monday (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Le Lundi de Pâques</i>) is the Monday immediately following Easter Sunday and is a statutory holiday for federal employees. Although not mandatory by federal regulation, some employers also give this day off to employees out of common practice. Additionally, this holiday succeeds Good Friday (the Friday preceding Easter), which is a mandatory holiday for all employees, giving those workers an extra long weekend in March or April. In provinces where Family Day, Islander Day, or Louis Riel Day are not observed, Easter Weekend is the first provincial holiday after New Year's Day. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_Europe">Central Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Central Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">

      <p><i><a href="/wiki/%C5%9Amigus-dyngus" title="Śmigus-dyngus">Śmigus-dyngus</a></i> (or <i>lany poniedziałek</i>, Polish for <i>Wet Monday</i>) is the name for Easter Monday in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> and the diaspora. In the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> it is called <i>velikonoční pondělí</i>, in <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> <i>veľkonočný pondelok</i> and in <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> <i>Vízbevető</i>. All these <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Catholic</a> countries (and some others) practice the unique ancient custom on this day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Traditionally, boys and men pour a bucket of water or perfume on girls and women and/or spank their buttocks and legs with long thin twigs (<a href="/wiki/Pussy_willow" title="Pussy willow">pussy willow</a>) or <a href="/wiki/Easter_whip" title="Easter whip">switches</a> made from <a href="/wiki/Willow" title="Willow">willow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a> or decorated tree branches. A legend says that it keep women healthy, beautiful, and fertile during the whole next year. </p>
      <p>Another related custom, unique to Poland, is that of sprinkling bowls (garce) of ashes on people or houses, celebrated a few weeks earlier at the "półpoście". This custom is almost forgotten, but still practiced in the area around the borders of <a href="/wiki/Masuria" title="Masuria">Masuria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masovia" class="mw-redirect" title="Masovia">Masovia</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Egypt">Egypt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Egypt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sham_Ennessim" title="Sham Ennessim">Sham Ennessim</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In Egypt, the ancient festival of <b><a href="/wiki/Sham_Ennessim" title="Sham Ennessim">Sham Ennessim</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">شم النسيم</span>‎, literally meaning "smelling of the breeze") is celebrated on the <a href="/wiki/Coptic_Orthodox_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Coptic Orthodox Church">Coptic</a> (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Christianity" title="Eastern Christianity">Eastern</a>) Easter Monday, though the festival dates back to <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Pharonic times</a> (about 2700 BC). It is an Egyptian national holiday. Traditional activities include painting eggs, taking meals outdoors, and eating <a href="/wiki/Feseekh" class="mw-redirect" title="Feseekh">feseekh</a> (fermented <a href="/wiki/Mullet_(fish)" title="Mullet (fish)">mullet</a>). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Germany, people go out into the fields early in the morning and hold <a href="/wiki/Easter_egg" title="Easter egg">Easter egg</a> races.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> For Roman Catholics, Easter Monday is also a <a href="/wiki/Holy_Day_of_Obligation" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Day of Obligation">Holy Day of Obligation</a> in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the Republic of Ireland it is a day of remembrance for the men and women who died in the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Rising" title="Easter Rising">Easter Rising</a> which began on Easter Monday 1916. Until 1966, there was a parade of veterans, past the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Army" title="Irish Republican Army">Irish Republican Army</a> at the General Post Office (GPO) on O'Connell Street, and a reading of the <a href="/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_Irish_Republic" title="Proclamation of the Irish Republic">Proclamation of the Irish Republic</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Italy, Easter Monday is an official public holiday and is called “Pasquetta”. It is customary to prepare a family picnic in the countryside or barbecues with friends. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In New Zealand it is a National Public Holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Schools often extend the weekend to the Tuesday to give students a 5 day break.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>In Spain, the Easter Monday is an official public holiday in the autonomous communities of <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Land_of_Valencia" class="mw-redirect" title="Land of Valencia">Land of Valencia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Autonomous_Community" class="mw-redirect" title="Basque Autonomous Community">Basque Autonomous Community</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">Cantabria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castilla%E2%80%93La_Mancha" title="Castilla–La Mancha">Castilla–La Mancha</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" class="mw-redirect" title="La Rioja (Spain)">La Rioja</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In Catalonia, the Land of Valencia and Murcia is typical a kind of cake called <a href="/wiki/Easter_mona" title="Easter mona">Easter mona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It's usually given by godparents to their godchild and it is a tradition for families or groups of friends to gather together and to go somewhere, specially to the countryside, to eat the <i>mona</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Netherlands">The Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: The Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In The Netherlands Easter Monday is an official public holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Apart from church services in some locations on the Dutch "bible belt", there are no widespread festivities or tradtions for the "Second Easter Day" ("Tweede Paasdag"). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">

        </div>

      <p>In the United States, Easter Monday is not a federal holiday,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and is generally not observed on a nationwide level, apart from a few traditions such as the <a href="/wiki/Egg_rolling#United_States" title="Egg rolling">White House Easter egg roll</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On a local level, the day is informally observed in some areas such as the state of <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a>, and some cities in <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>. Easter Monday was a public holiday in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> from 1935 to 1987, due to the early-20th-century tradition of state government workers taking the day off to attend the annual baseball game between <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_State_University" title="North Carolina State University">North Carolina State College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wake_Forest_University" title="Wake Forest University">Wake Forest College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a> schools often have two holidays on <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a> and Easter Monday. In some states and districts, public schools and universities are closed on Easter Monday, often part of <a href="/wiki/Spring_break" title="Spring break">spring break</a>. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dyngus_Day">Dyngus Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Dyngus Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Amigus-dyngus" title="Śmigus-dyngus">Śmigus-dyngus</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Traditionally Polish areas of the United States observe Easter Monday as <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Amigus-dyngus" title="Śmigus-dyngus">Dyngus Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Dyngus Day celebrations are widespread and popular in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>; <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>; <a href="/wiki/Wyandotte,_Michigan" title="Wyandotte, Michigan">Wyandotte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hamtramck,_Michigan" title="Hamtramck, Michigan">Hamtramck</a> in <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>; <a href="/wiki/South_Bend,_Indiana" title="South Bend, Indiana">South Bend</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Porte,_Indiana" title="La Porte, Indiana">La Porte</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Hanover,_New_Hampshire" title="Hanover, New Hampshire">Hanover, New Hampshire</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easter_Monday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Three of the four <a href="/wiki/Countries_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Countries of the United Kingdom">countries of the United Kingdom</a> have Easter Monday as a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Public holidays in the United Kingdom">bank holiday</a>: England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. In <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a>, England, the people of <a href="/wiki/Hallaton" title="Hallaton">Hallaton</a> hold a <a href="/wiki/Bottle-kicking" title="Bottle-kicking">bottle-kicking</a> match and Hare Pie Scramble.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>
