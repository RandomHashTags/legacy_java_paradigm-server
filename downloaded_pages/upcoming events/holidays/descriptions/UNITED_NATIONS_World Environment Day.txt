      <p><b>World Environment Day</b> (<b>WED</b>) is celebrated on 5 June every year, and is the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>' principal vehicle for encouraging awareness and action for the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">protection of the environment</a>. First held in 1974, it has been a flagship campaign for <a href="/wiki/Awareness_raising" class="mw-redirect" title="Awareness raising">raising awareness</a> on <a href="/wiki/Environmental_issue" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental issue">environmental issues</a> emerging from <a href="/wiki/Marine_pollution" title="Marine pollution">marine pollution</a>, human <a href="/wiki/Overpopulation" title="Overpopulation">overpopulation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Global warming">global warming</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_consumption" title="Sustainable consumption">sustainable consumption</a> and wildlife crime. World Environment Day has grown to become a global platform for public outreach, with participation from over 143 countries annually. Each year, WED has provided a new theme that major corporations, NGOs, communities, governments and all celebrities worldwide adopt to advocate environmental causes.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>World Environment Day was established in 1972 by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> on the first day of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment" title="United Nations Conference on the Human Environment">Stockholm Conference on the Human Environment</a>, resulting from discussions on the integration of human interactions and the environment. Two years later, in 1974 the first WED was held with the theme "Only One Earth". Even though WED celebration have been held annually since 1974, in 1987 the idea for rotating the centre of these activities through selecting different host countries began.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>For almost five decades, World Environment Day has been raising awareness, supporting action, and driving changes for the environment. Here is a timeline of key accomplishments in WEDs' history: </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005">2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2005">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg/150px-SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg/225px-SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg/300px-SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="300"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:SFWed2005Maddruid.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Standard WED banners hung in San Francisco in May 2005
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The theme for the 2005 World Environment Day was "Green Cities" and the slogan was "Plant for the Planet!".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006">2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The topic for WED 2006 was <a href="/wiki/Deserts" class="mw-redirect" title="Deserts">Deserts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desertification" title="Desertification">Desertification</a> and the slogan was "Don't desert drylands".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The slogan emphasised the importance of protecting <a href="/wiki/Dryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Dryland">drylands</a>. The main international celebrations of the World Environment Day 2006 were held in <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007">2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The topic for World Environment Day for 2007 was "Melting Ice – a Hot Topic?" During <a href="/wiki/International_Polar_Year" title="International Polar Year">International Polar Year</a>, WED 2007 focused on the effects that climate change is having on polar ecosystems and communities, on other ice- and snow-covered areas of the world, and the resulting global impacts. </p>
      <p>The main international celebrations of the WED 2007 were held in the city of <a href="/wiki/Troms%C3%B8" title="Tromsø">Tromsø</a>, Norway, a city north of the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Egypt issued a postage stamp for the 2007 World Environment Day.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The host for World Environment Day 2008 was New Zealand, with the main international celebrations scheduled for <a href="/wiki/Wellington,_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Wellington, New Zealand">Wellington</a>. The slogan for 2008 was "<a href="/wiki/CO2" class="mw-redirect" title="CO2">CO<sub>2</sub></a>, Kick the Habit! Towards a <a href="/wiki/Low_carbon_economy" class="mw-redirect" title="Low carbon economy">Low Carbon Economy</a>." New Zealand was one of the first countries to pledge to achieve <a href="/wiki/Carbon_neutrality" title="Carbon neutrality">carbon-neutrality</a>, and will also focus on forest management as a tool for reducing <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Botanic_Garden" title="Chicago Botanic Garden">Chicago Botanic Garden</a> served as the North American host<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> for World Environment Day on 5 June 2008. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for WED 2009 was 'Your Planet Needs You – UNite to Combat Climate Change', and Michael Jackson's 'Earth Song' was declared 'World Environment Day Song'. It was hosted in Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>'Many Species. One Planet. One Future', was the theme of 2010. </p>
      <p>It celebrated the diversity of life on Earth as part of the 2010 International Year of <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">Biodiversity</a>. It was hosted in <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>. Thousands of activities were organized worldwide, with beach clean-ups, concerts, exhibits, film festivals, community events and much more.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Each continent (except <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>) had a "regional host city", the U.N. chose <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, Pennsylvania as the host for all North.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The world environment day of 2011 was hosted by India. It was the 1st time for India to host the day. The theme for 2011 was Forests&nbsp;– Nature At Your Service. Thousands of activities were organized worldwide, with beach clean-ups, concerts, exhibits, film festivals, community events, tree plantings<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and much more. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for the 2012 World Environment Day was <i>Green Economy.</i><sup id="cite_ref-WED2012_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WED2012-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The theme aimed to invite people to examine their activities and lifestyle and see how the concept of a "Green Economy" fits into it. The host country for the year's celebrations was Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-WED2012_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WED2012-17">[17]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>The 2013 theme for World Environment Day was <i>Think.Eat.Save.</i><sup id="cite_ref-WED2013_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WED2013-18">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The campaign addressed the huge annual wastage and losses in food, which, if conserved, would release a large quantity of food as well as reduce the overall carbon footprint. The campaign aimed to bring about awareness in countries with lifestyles resulting in food wastage. It also aimed to empower people to make informed choices about the food they eat so as to reduce the overall ecological impact due to the worldwide production of food.<sup id="cite_ref-WED2013_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WED2013-18">[18]</a></sup> The host country for the year's celebrations was Mongolia. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Theme of the 2014 WED was International Year of Small Islands Developing States (SIDS). By choosing this Theme the UN General Assembly aimed to highlight the development Challenges and successes of the SIDS. In 2014, the World Environment Day focused on global warming and its impact on ocean levels.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The Slogan of the WED 2014 is "Raise your voice not the sea level", as <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> hosted the global celebrations of the 42nd edition of the World Environment Day.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The UN Environment Programme named actor <a href="/wiki/Ian_Somerhalder" title="Ian Somerhalder">Ian Somerhalder</a> as the official Goodwill ambassador of the WED 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Slogan of the 2015 edition of the World Environment Day is "Seven Billion Dreams. One Planet. Consume with Care". The slogan was picked through a voting process on social media.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In Saudi Arabia, 15 women recycled 2000 plastic bags to crochet a mural in support of the WED 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In India, <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a> planted a Kadamb sapling to celebrate the World Environment Day and raise awareness for Environment.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Italy is the host country of the 43rd edition of the WED. The celebrations took place as part of <a href="/wiki/Expo_2015" title="Expo 2015">Milan Expo</a> around the theme: Feeding the Planet – Energy for Life.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
      <p> The 2016 WED was organized under the theme "Go wild for life". This edition of the WED aims to reduce and prevent the illegal trade in wildlife.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> was chosen as the host country of the 2016 WED during the <a href="/wiki/2015_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2015 United Nations Climate Change Conference">COP21</a> in Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017">2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2017">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for 2017 was 'Connecting People to Nature – in the city and on the land, from the poles to the equator'. The host nation was Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2018">2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 2018">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for 2018 was "Beat Plastic Pollution". The host nation was India.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> By choosing this theme, it is hoped that people may strive to change their everyday lives to reduce the heavy burden of <a href="/wiki/Plastic_pollution" title="Plastic pollution">plastic pollution</a>. People should be free from the over-reliance on <a href="/wiki/Disposable_product" title="Disposable product">single-use</a> or disposables, as they have severe environmental consequences. We should liberate our natural places, our wildlife and our own health from plastics.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The Indian government pledged to eliminate all single use of plastic in India by 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019">2019</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: 2019">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for 2019 was "Beat Air Pollution". The host nation was <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. This theme was chosen as air pollution kills about 7 million people annually.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Réunion Island">Réunion Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miss_Earth_2018" title="Miss Earth 2018">Miss Earth 2018</a> <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Ph%C6%B0%C6%A1ng_Kh%C3%A1nh" title="Nguyễn Phương Khánh">Nguyễn Phương Khánh</a> from <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> delivered her speech during World Environment Day with the theme "How to fight <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Global warming">global warming</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020">2020</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2020">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for 2020 was "Time for Nature", and was hosted in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Biodiversity_of_Colombia" title="Biodiversity of Colombia">Colombia</a> is one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Megadiverse_countries" title="Megadiverse countries">megadiverse countries</a> in the world and holds close to 10% of the planet's <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>. Since it is part of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_rainforest" title="Amazon rainforest">Amazon rainforest</a>, Colombia ranks first in bird and orchid species diversity and second in plants, butterflies, freshwater fish, and amphibians.<br> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="WORLD_Environment_Day_Anthem">WORLD Environment Day Anthem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Environment_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: WORLD Environment Day Anthem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>An Earth Anthem penned by poet <a href="/wiki/Abhay_K" class="mw-redirect" title="Abhay K">Abhay K</a> is sung to celebrate World Environment Day.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> </p> 
      <blockquote> 
       <div class="poem"> 
        <p>Our cosmic oasis, cosmic blue pearl<br> the most beautiful planet in the universe<br> all the continents and all the oceans <br> united we stand as flora and fauna<br> united we stand as species of one earth<br> different cultures, beliefs and ways<br> we are humans, the earth is our home<br> all the people and the nations of the world<br> all for one and one for all<br> united we unfurl the blue marble flag. </p> 
       </div> 
       <p><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> </p> 
      </blockquote> 
      <p>It was launched in June 2013 on the occasion of the World Environment Day by <a href="/wiki/Kapil_Sibal" title="Kapil Sibal">Kapil Sibal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shashi_Tharoor" title="Shashi Tharoor">Shashi Tharoor</a>, then Union Ministers of India, at a function organized by the Indian Council of Cultural Relations in New Delhi.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> It is supported by the global organization <a href="/wiki/Habitat_For_Humanity" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat For Humanity">Habitat For Humanity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> </p>
