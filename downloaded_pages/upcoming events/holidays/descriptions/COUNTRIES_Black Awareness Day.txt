      <p>In Brazil, <b>Black Awareness Day</b> or <b>Black Consciousness Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Dia da Consciência Negra</i>) is observed annually on November 20 as a day "to celebrate a regained awareness by the <a href="/wiki/Afro-Brazilians" title="Afro-Brazilians">black community</a> about their great worth and contribution to the country".<sup id="cite_ref-Date_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Date-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Black Awareness Day has been celebrated since the 1960s and has amplified its events in the last few years. Originally, it was celebrated on May 13 (the date of <a href="/wiki/Lei_%C3%81urea" title="Lei Áurea">abolition of slavery in Brazil</a>). It was later moved to November 20 to honour <a href="/wiki/Zumbi" title="Zumbi">Zumbi</a>'s death, and is sometimes called <b>Zumbi Day</b>. It is an official state holiday in <a href="/wiki/Alagoas" title="Alagoas">Alagoas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amazonas_(Brazilian_state)" title="Amazonas (Brazilian state)">Amazonas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amap%C3%A1" title="Amapá">Amapá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso" title="Mato Grosso">Mato Grosso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro_(state)" title="Rio de Janeiro (state)">Rio de Janeiro</a>, and marked elsewhere by multiple city councils. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Members of the organization "<a href="/wiki/Black_Movement_of_Brazil" title="Black Movement of Brazil">Black Movement</a>" (the largest of its kind in Brazil) organize educational and fun events involving mainly children of African descent. Their focus during these events is to dissolve the perception of Africans' inferiority in society. Other "hot topics" in the Black community during the Day of Black Awareness are the assimilation of African-Brazilian laborers with Caucasian-Brazilian and other laborers, ethnic identity, and black pride. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Similar_days_celebrating_racial_groups_in_Brazil">Similar days celebrating racial groups in Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Similar days celebrating racial groups in Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The "Day of the <a href="/wiki/Caboclo" title="Caboclo">Caboclo</a>" (<i>Dia do Caboclo</i>) is observed annually on June 24, in celebration of the contributions and identity of the <a href="/wiki/Mameluco" title="Mameluco">original caboclos</a> and their descendants. This date is an official public holiday in the <a href="/wiki/Amazonas_(Brazilian_state)" title="Amazonas (Brazilian state)">State of Amazonas</a>. </p>
      <p>"<a href="/wiki/Mixed_Race_Day" title="Mixed Race Day">Mixed Race Day</a>" (<i>Dia do <a href="/wiki/Mesti%C3%A7o" title="Mestiço">Mestiço</a></i>) is observed annually on June 27, three days after the <a href="/wiki/Day_of_the_Caboclo" class="mw-redirect" title="Day of the Caboclo">Day of the Caboclo</a>, in celebration of all <a href="/wiki/Mixed-race_Brazilian" title="Mixed-race Brazilian">mixed-race Brazilians</a>, including the caboclos. The date is an official public holiday in three <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_states" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian states">Brazilian states</a>. </p>
      <p>"<a href="/wiki/Indian_Day_(Brazil)" title="Indian Day (Brazil)">Indian Day</a>" (<i>Dia do Índio</i>), observed annually on April 19, recognizes and honours the <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Indigenous peoples of Brazil">indigenous peoples of Brazil</a>. </p>
