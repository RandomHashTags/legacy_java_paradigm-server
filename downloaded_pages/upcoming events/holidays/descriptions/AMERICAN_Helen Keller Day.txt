      <p><b>Helen Keller Day</b> is a <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/commemorative" class="extiw" title="wikt:commemorative">commemorative</a> holiday to celebrate the birth of <a href="/wiki/Helen_Keller" title="Helen Keller">Helen Keller</a>, observed on June 27 annually. The holiday observance was created by presidential proclamation in 2006, as well as by international organizations, particularly those helping the <a href="/wiki/Blindness" class="mw-redirect" title="Blindness">blind</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Deaf" class="mw-redirect" title="Deaf">deaf</a>. The holiday is generally known for its fashion show held on June 27 annually for fundraising purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-wnep_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wnep-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Helen_Keller_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The holiday was first proclaimed on March 23, 1960, by <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a> mayor Leo P. Carlin.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On June 7, 1960, by then <a href="/wiki/Annapolis,_Maryland" title="Annapolis, Maryland">Annapolis, Maryland</a> mayor Arthur G. Ellington where he announced Keller's 80th birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On June 19, 1980, President <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a> issues Proclamation #4767 to honor Helen Keller and her accomplishment.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Outside of the proclamations, the holiday is also observed by private organizations. Since 1971, the <a href="/wiki/Lions_Club_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Lions Club International">Lions Club International</a> has declared the holiday on June 1 annually to commemorate the speech Keller gave on June 30, 1925, to spread awareness of the <a href="/wiki/American_Foundation_for_the_Blind" title="American Foundation for the Blind">American Foundation for the Blind</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The day is generally observed internationally through the Blind Association and other international organizations who help people with this disability. Every year on June 27, an annual fashion show is held as a fundraiser in <a href="/wiki/Lackawanna_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Lackawanna County, Pennsylvania">Lackawanna County, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Helen_Keller_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Helen_Keller" title="Helen Keller">Helen Keller</a>
      </div> 
      <p><b>Helen Adams Keller</b> (June 27, 1880 – June 1, 1968) was an American author, <a href="/wiki/Political_activist" class="mw-redirect" title="Political activist">political activist</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lecturer" title="Lecturer">lecturer</a>. She was the first <a href="/wiki/Deafblindness" title="Deafblindness">deafblind</a> person to earn a bachelor of arts degree.<sup id="cite_ref-rnib_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rnib-8">[8]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The story of how Keller's teacher, <a href="/wiki/Anne_Sullivan" title="Anne Sullivan">Anne Sullivan</a>, broke through the isolation imposed by a near complete lack of language, allowing the girl to blossom as she learned to communicate, has become widely known through the dramatic depictions of the 1959 play and 1962 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Miracle_Worker" title="The Miracle Worker">The Miracle Worker</a></i>. Her birthplace in West Tuscumbia, Alabama, is now a museum<sup id="cite_ref-birthplace_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthplace-10">[10]</a></sup> and sponsors an annual "Helen Keller Day". Her birthday on June 27 is commemorated as Helen Keller Day in the U.S. state of <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> and was authorized at the federal level by presidential proclamation by President <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a> in 1980, the 100th anniversary of her birth. </p>
