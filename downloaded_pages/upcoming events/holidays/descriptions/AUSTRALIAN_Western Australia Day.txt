      <p><b>Western Australia Day</b> or simply <b>WA Day</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (formerly known as <b>Foundation Day</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-watoday_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-watoday-3">[3]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">public holiday</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>, celebrated on the first Monday in June each year to commemorate the founding of the <a href="/wiki/Swan_River_Colony" title="Swan River Colony">Swan River Colony</a> in 1829. Because of the celebration of Western Australia Day, WA does not celebrate the <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Official_Birthday" title="Queen's Official Birthday">Queen's Birthday Holiday</a> in June, as do the other Australian states; it is held in September or October instead.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Australia_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/HMS_Challenger_(1826)" title="HMS Challenger (1826)">HMS <i>Challenger</i></a>, under Captain <a href="/wiki/Charles_Fremantle" title="Charles Fremantle">Charles Fremantle</a>, anchored off <a href="/wiki/Garden_Island,_Western_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Garden Island, Western Australia">Garden Island</a> on 25 April 1829. Fremantle officially claimed the western part of Australia for Britain on 2 May. The merchant vessel <i><a href="/wiki/Parmelia_(barque)" title="Parmelia (barque)">Parmelia</a></i> – with the new colony's administrator Lieutenant-Governor <a href="/wiki/James_Stirling_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="James Stirling (Royal Navy officer)">James Stirling</a>, other officials, and civilian <a href="/wiki/Settler" title="Settler">settlers</a> on board – arrived on the night of 31 May and sighted the coast on 1 June. It finally anchored in <a href="/wiki/Cockburn_Sound" title="Cockburn Sound">Cockburn Sound</a> on 6 June. The warship <a href="/wiki/HMS_Sulphur_(1826)" title="HMS Sulphur (1826)">HMS <i>Sulphur</i></a> arrived on 8 June, carrying the British Army <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a>. The Swan River Colony was officially proclaimed by Stirling on 11 June.<sup id="cite_ref-wa_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wa-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-calder_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-calder-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Ships carrying more civilian settlers began arriving in August, and on the <a href="/wiki/George_IV_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="George IV of the United Kingdom">King</a>'s birthday, 12 August, the wife of the captain of <i>Sulphur</i>, Mrs Helena Dance, standing in for Mrs Ellen Stirling, cut down a tree to mark the founding of the colony's capital, <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wa_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wa-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1832, Stirling decided that an annual celebration was needed to unite the colony's inhabitants, including both settlers and <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_Australians" title="Aboriginal Australians">Aboriginal Australians</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Employer" class="mw-redirect" title="Employer">masters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Employees" class="mw-redirect" title="Employees">servants</a>". He decided that the commemoration would be held on 1 June each year (or if a Sunday, on the following Monday), the date originally planned by Stirling for Parmelia's arrival in recognition of the first and greatest British naval victory over the French in 1794, the "<a href="/wiki/Glorious_First_of_June" title="Glorious First of June">Glorious First of June</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-calder_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-calder-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The holiday was celebrated as Foundation Day up until 2011; in 2012 it was renamed Western Australia Day as part of a series of law changes recognising Aboriginal Australians as the original inhabitants of Western Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-watoday_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-watoday-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
