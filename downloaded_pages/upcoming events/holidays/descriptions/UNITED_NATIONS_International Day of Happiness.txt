      <p>The <b>International Day of Happiness</b> is <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#March" title="List of minor secular observances">celebrated</a> throughout the world on the 20th of March.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was established by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> on 28 June 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The International Day of Happiness aims to make people around the world realize the importance of happiness within their lives. </p>
      <p>In 2015, the United Nations launched 17 Sustainable Development Goals to make people's lives happier. Its main development goals are to eradicate poverty, reduce inequality and protect our planet. </p>
      <p>The United Nations invites people of all ages to join in celebrating the International Day of Happiness. </p>
      <p>Assembly Resolution A/RES/66/281 states in pertinent part: </p> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>The General Assembly, Conscious that the pursuit of happiness is a fundamental human goal, Recognizing also the need for a more inclusive, equitable and balanced approach to economic growth that promotes <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">poverty eradication</a>, happiness and the <a href="/wiki/Well-being" title="Well-being">well-being</a> of all peoples, Decides to proclaim 20 March the International Day of Happiness, Invites all Member States, organizations of the United Nations system and other international and regional organizations, as well as civil society, including non-governmental organizations and individuals, to observe the International Day of Happiness in an appropriate manner, including through education and public awareness-raising activities[…]</p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        — <cite><a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a>, Resolution adopted by the General Assembly on 28 June 2012<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote>
      <p><b><big>The Official Information by United Nations General Assembly</big></b> </p>
      <p>Ten Steps To Global Happiness: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Do things at makes you happy</li> 
       <li>Tell Everyone</li> 
       <li>Participate in and celebrate the World Happiness Contest</li> 
       <li>Give and spread happiness to others</li> 
       <li>To celebrate</li> 
       <li>Share the things that make you happy on social media</li> 
       <li>Promote the resolution</li> 
       <li>We will advance the United Nations global goals for sustainable development</li> 
       <li>Enjoy nature</li> 
       <li>Adopt hedonism</li>
      </ol> 
      <p><br> <b><big>Mission</big></b> </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Happiness as a fundamental human right and goal for all</li> 
       <li>Happiness as a universal aspiration in the lives of all</li> 
       <li>Happiness as a way of living, being, and serving communities and society</li> 
       <li>Happiness as a north star for individuals, communities, governments, and society.</li> 
       <li>Happiness path toward achieving the sustainable development goals</li> 
       <li>Happiness as a “new paradigm’ for human development</li> 
       <li>Worldwide celebration of the international day of happiness that is democratic, diverse, organic, and inclusive</li>
      </ol>
