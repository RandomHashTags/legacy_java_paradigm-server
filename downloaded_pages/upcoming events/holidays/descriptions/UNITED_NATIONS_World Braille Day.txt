   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading">World Braille Day</h1>

       Day raising awareness for the blind
      </div> 
      <p><b>World Braille Day</b> is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#January" title="List of minor secular observances">international day</a> on 4 January and celebrates <a href="/wiki/Awareness" title="Awareness">awareness</a> of the importance of <a href="/wiki/Braille" title="Braille">braille</a> as a means of communication in the full realization of the human rights for blind and <a href="/wiki/Visual_impairment" title="Visual impairment">visually impaired</a> people.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The date for the event was chosen by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> via a proclamation in November 2018, and marks the birthday of <a href="/wiki/Louis_Braille" title="Louis Braille">Louis Braille</a>, creator of this writing system.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The first World Braille Day was celebrated on January 4, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
