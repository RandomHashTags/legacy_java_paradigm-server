   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading">World Tourism Day</h1>

      <p>Since 1980, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_World_Tourism_Organization" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations World Tourism Organization">United Nations World Tourism Organization</a> has celebrated <b>World Tourism Day</b> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#September" title="List of minor secular observances">international observances</a> on <a href="/wiki/September_27" title="September 27">September 27</a>. This date was chosen as on that day in 1970, the Statutes of the UNWTO were adopted. The adoption of these Statutes is considered a milestone in global tourism. The purpose of this day is to <a href="/wiki/Raising_awareness" class="mw-redirect" title="Raising awareness">raise awareness</a> on the role of <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a> within the international community and to demonstrate how it affects social, cultural, political and economic values worldwide. The theme of the day was "<a href="/wiki/Sustainable_tourism" title="Sustainable tourism">sustainable tourism</a>", in 2017. In 2018 the theme was "Tourism and the Digital Transformation". In 2019 the theme was "Tourism and Jobs: a better future for all" and in 2020 the theme was: "Tourism and Rural Development". </p>
      <p>At its Twelfth Session in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, in October 1997, the UNWTO General Assembly decided to designate a host country each year to act as the Organization's partner in the celebration of World Tourism Day. At its Fifteenth Session in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, in October 2003, the Assembly decided the following geographic order to be followed for World Tourism Day celebrations: 2006 in Europe; 2007 in South Asia; 2008 in the Americas; 2009 in Africa and 2011 in the Middle East. </p>
      <p>The late Ignatius Amaduwa Atigbi, a Nigerian national, was the one who proposed the idea of marking September 27 of every year as World Tourism Day. He was finally recognized for his contribution in 2009. The colour of World Tourism Day is Blue. </p>
