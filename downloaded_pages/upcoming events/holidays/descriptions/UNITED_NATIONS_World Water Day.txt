      <p><b>World Water Day</b> is an annual <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> (UN) observance day held on 22 March that highlights the importance of <a href="/wiki/Fresh_water" title="Fresh water">fresh water</a>. The day is used to advocate for the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_management" title="Sustainable management">sustainable management</a> of <a href="/wiki/Water_resources" title="Water resources">freshwater resources</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2-1">[1]</a></sup> The theme of each day focuses on topics relevant to <a href="/wiki/Drinking_water" title="Drinking water">clean water</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hygiene" title="Hygiene">hygiene</a> <a href="/wiki/WASH" title="WASH">(WASH</a>), which is in line with the targets of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goal_6" title="Sustainable Development Goal 6">Sustainable Development Goal 6</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-JMP2017_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JMP2017-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/UN_World_Water_Development_Report" title="UN World Water Development Report">UN World Water Development Report</a> (WWDR) is released each year around World Water Day. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/UN-Water" title="UN-Water">UN-Water</a> is the convener for World Water Day and selects the theme for each year in consultation with UN organizations that share an interest in that year's focus.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2-1">[1]</a></sup> The theme for 2021 was "Valuing Water" and the public campaign invited people to join a global conversation on social media to "tell us your stories, thoughts and feelings about water".<sup id="cite_ref-:5_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p> In 2020, the theme was "Water and Climate Change".<sup id="cite_ref-:6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-4">[4]</a></sup> Previous themes for the years 2016 to 2019 were "Water and Jobs'",<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> "Why waste water?"<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> "Nature for Water",<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">[7]</a></sup> and "Leaving no one behind".<sup id="cite_ref-:1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup> World Water Day is celebrated around the world with a variety of events. These can be theatrical, musical or <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbying</a> in nature. The day can also include campaigns to raise money for water projects. The first World Water Day, designated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, was in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2-1">[1]</a></sup><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886046785">.mw-parser-output .toclimit-2 .toclevel-1 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-3 .toclevel-2 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-4 .toclevel-3 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-5 .toclevel-4 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-6 .toclevel-5 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-7 .toclevel-6 ul{display:none}</style></p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives_and_structure">Objectives and structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Objectives and structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>World Water Day is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_international_observances" class="mw-redirect" title="List of international observances">international observance day</a>. The intention is to inspire people around the world to learn more about water-related issues and to take action to make a difference.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2020, due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, there was an additional focus on <a href="/wiki/Hand_washing" title="Hand washing">hand washing</a> and hygiene.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Relevant issues include <a href="/wiki/Water_scarcity" title="Water scarcity">water scarcity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">water pollution</a>, inadequate <a href="/wiki/Water_supply" title="Water supply">water supply</a>, lack of <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a>, and the impacts of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> (which is the theme of World Water Day 2020<sup id="cite_ref-:4_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-10">[10]</a></sup>). The day brings to light the inequality of access to <a href="/wiki/WASH" title="WASH">WASH</a> services and the need to assure the <a href="/wiki/Right_to_water" class="mw-redirect" title="Right to water">human right to water and sanitation</a>. </p>
      <p>The World Water Day website announces events, activities and volunteer opportunities. In 2020, featured stories are about adapting to the water effects climate change and using water more efficiently.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Convener">Convener</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Convener">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/UN-Water" title="UN-Water">UN-Water</a> coordinates activities with UN member organisations who share an interest in that year's theme. UN-Water mobilizes organizations of all kinds to action, whether globally or locally.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples_of_activities">Examples of activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Examples of activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">Non-governmental organizations</a> active in the <a href="/wiki/WASH" title="WASH">WASH</a> sector, such as <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>, <a href="/wiki/WaterAid" title="WaterAid">WaterAid</a> and Water and Sanitation for the Urban Poor (WSUP), use the day to raise public awareness, get <a href="/wiki/Media_attention" class="mw-redirect" title="Media attention">media attention</a> for water issues and inspire action.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Activities have included releasing publications and films, as well as organizing round tables, seminars and expositions.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/End_Water_Poverty" title="End Water Poverty">End Water Poverty</a>, a global civil society coalition with 250 partner organizations, sponsors Water Action Month each year and offers an event-planning guidebook.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/UN_World_Water_Development_Report" title="UN World Water Development Report">UN World Water Development Report (WWDR)</a> is released each year on World Water Day. Information related to the annual theme gives decision-makers tools to implement sustainable use of <a href="/wiki/Water_resources" title="Water resources">water resources</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>More and more initiatives in schools and universities are educating people about the importance of conserving and managing water resources.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TES_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TES-19">[19]</a></sup> For example, <a href="/wiki/Michigan_State_University" title="Michigan State University">Michigan State University</a> held a contest for "best World Water Day poster" in 2017. Primary school children in <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">the Philippines</a> participated in a "My School Toilet" contest in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>This day was first formally proposed in <a href="/wiki/Agenda_21" title="Agenda 21">Agenda 21</a> of the 1992 <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit" title="Earth Summit">United Nations Conference on Environment and Development</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>. In December 1992, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> adopted resolution A/RES/47/193 by which 22 March of each year was declared World Day for Water.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1993, the first World Water Day was observed.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2-1">[1]</a></sup> An archive of previous World Water Day campaign websites also exists.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_themes">Annual themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Annual themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prior_to_2014">Prior to 2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Prior to 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the years prior to 2014, the annual themes were as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-:4_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-10">[10]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>1994: Caring for our Water Resources is Everybody's Business</li> 
       <li>1995: Women and Water</li> 
       <li>1996: Water for Thirsty Cities</li> 
       <li>1997: The World's Water: Is there enough?</li> 
       <li>1998: Groundwater– The Invisible Resource. The UN identified gaps in <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">groundwater</a> management which have enormous implications for sustainable development.</li> 
       <li>1999: Everyone Lives Downstream</li> 
       <li>2000: Water for the 21st century</li> 
       <li>2001: Water for Health</li> 
       <li>2002: Water for Development. The poor and deteriorating state of water resources in many parts of the world demand <a href="/wiki/Integrated_water_resources_management" title="Integrated water resources management">integrated water resources planning and management</a>.</li> 
       <li>2003: Water for Future. Maintain and improve the quality and quantity of fresh water available to future generations.</li> 
       <li>2004: Water and Disasters. Weather, climate and water resources can have a devastating impact on socio-economic development and on the well-being of humankind.</li> 
       <li>2005: <a href="/wiki/Water_for_Life_Decade" title="Water for Life Decade">Water for Life Decade 2005–2015</a>. The United Nations General Assembly at its 58th session in December 2003 agreed to proclaim the years 2005 to 2015 the International Decade for Action, beginning with World Water Day, 22 March 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The phrase Water for Life Decade was also used.</li> 
       <li>2006: Water and Culture. The theme drew the attention to the fact that there are as many ways of viewing, using, and celebrating water as there are cultural traditions across the world.</li> 
       <li>2007: Coping With Water Scarcity. Highlighted <a href="/wiki/Water_scarcity" title="Water scarcity">water scarcity</a> worldwide and the need for increased integration and cooperation to ensure sustainable, efficient and equitable management of scarce water resources, both at international and local levels.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2008: <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">Sanitation</a>. 2008 was also the <a href="/wiki/International_Year_of_Sanitation" title="International Year of Sanitation">International Year of Sanitation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2009: Trans Waters. Special focus placed on <a href="/wiki/International_waters" title="International waters">trans-boundary waters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2010: Clean Water for a Healthy World. Dedicated to water quality, reflecting its importance alongside quantity of the resource in water management.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2011: Water for cities: responding to the urban challenge. The aim was to spotlight and encourage governments, organizations, communities, and individuals to actively engage in addressing the challenges of urban water management.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2012: Water and Food Security: The World is Thirsty Because We are Hungry. On the occasion of 2012 World Water Day, the International Committee of the Red Cross (<a href="/wiki/International_Committee_of_the_Red_Cross" title="International Committee of the Red Cross">ICRC</a>) called attention to the water-related challenges faced by civilians caught up in fighting and intense civil unrest.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>2013: International Year of Cooperation. In December 2010, the United Nations General Assembly declared 2013 as the United Nations International Year of Water Cooperation. In reflection of this declaration, the 2013 World Water Day was dedicated to water cooperation.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span id="2014_.E2.80.93_Water_and_Energy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_–_Water_and_Energy">2014 – Water and Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014 – Water and Energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The 2014 theme of Water and Energy emphasized the close linkages and interdependence of water and energy and brought attention to the <a href="/wiki/Water-energy_nexus" title="Water-energy nexus">water-energy nexus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2014_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2014-29">[29]</a></sup> About 8% of the energy generated globally is used for pumping, treating and transporting water to various consumers. Furthermore, generating and transmitting energy requires the use of water resources, particularly for <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydroelectric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thermal_energy" title="Thermal energy">thermal energy</a> sources. </p>
      <p>The aim of that year's theme was to facilitate the development of policies and crosscutting frameworks that would bridge ministries and sectors. It was meant to lead the way to energy security and sustainable water use in a <a href="/wiki/Green_economy" title="Green economy">green economy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2014_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2014-29">[29]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Journalists from 11 countries in Asia met in Tokyo from 20 to 21 March 2014 to discuss the importance of water. The event included discussion panels on topics such as privatization of services, integration between water and energy and modernization of water services. The journalists also developed four joint stories and 20 individual story ideas for a network of Asian journalists writing on water (and energy) in social media.<sup id="cite_ref-UN2016_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN2016-30">[30]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="2015_.E2.80.93_Water_and_Sustainable_Development"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_–_Water_and_Sustainable_Development">2015 – Water and Sustainable Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2015 – Water and Sustainable Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>With the theme 'Water and Sustainable Development', the year 2015 provided an important opportunity to consolidate and build upon the previous World Water Days to highlight water's role in the sustainable development agenda.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER-31">[31]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a> (MDGs) were to have been achieved by 2015, so the year lent itself to discussions of the post-MDG period and aspirations for water and sustainable development. With the launch of the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals" title="Sustainable Development Goals">Sustainable Development Goals</a> (SDGs), World Water Day gave specific emphasis to SDG 6, which calls for water and sanitation for all, by encouraging discussion of how SDG 6 could be achieved by 2030.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWATER2_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWATER2-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="2016_.E2.80.93_Better_Water.2C_Better_Jobs"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_–_Better_Water,_Better_Jobs">2016 – Better Water, Better Jobs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2016 – Better Water, Better Jobs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The 2016 theme of "Better water, better jobs" highlighted the correlation between water and job creation, both directly and indirectly, by water sources around the globe.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> The theme led to a collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/International_Labour_Organization" title="International Labour Organization">International Labour Organization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> As water scarcity becomes more of a reality, industries heavily dependent on water like textiles and agriculture are at risk of increased costs, which threatens salaries and jobs. Increased costs may then be passed on to consumers. </p>
      <p>The theme also highlights how an abundance of quality water can change people's jobs and lives for the better. The 2016 celebration created recognition for those working to improve water quality and availability, and the need for many to transition to other and better jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> Three out of four of jobs worldwide are water-dependent. Water shortages and lack of access may limit economic growth in the years to come, according to the 2016 United Nations World Water Development Report.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="2017_.E2.80.93_Why_Waste_Water.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017_–_Why_Waste_Water?">2017 – Why Waste Water?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2017 – Why Waste Water?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In 2017, the theme was "Why Waste Water?" which was about reducing and <a href="/wiki/Reclaimed_water" title="Reclaimed water">reusing wastewater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> The theme was a play on words as it related to both the aspect of wasting <a href="/wiki/Drinking_water" title="Drinking water">water</a> and issues around <a href="/wiki/Wastewater" title="Wastewater">wastewater</a>, namely <a href="/wiki/Wastewater_treatment" title="Wastewater treatment">treatment</a> and reuse. Wastewater is a valuable resource to help achieve the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goal_6" title="Sustainable Development Goal 6">Sustainable Development Goal Number 6</a>. One aspect of Target 6.3 is to halve the proportion of untreated wastewater and also to increase the recycling and safe reuse of water across the globe. After appropriate treatment, wastewater can be used for a variety of purposes. Industry, for example, can reuse water in <a href="/wiki/Cooling_towers" class="mw-redirect" title="Cooling towers">cooling towers</a> and agriculture can reuse water for <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>An example activity for 2017 was the Wikipedia <a href="/wiki/Edit-a-thon" title="Edit-a-thon">edit-a-thon</a> organized by members of the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Sanitation_Alliance" title="Sustainable Sanitation Alliance">Sustainable Sanitation Alliance</a> on 19–21 March 2017. The purpose of the activity was to improve water and <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a> related content on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a> just ahead of World Water Day. The goal was to improve the quantity and quality of sanitation information available on Wikipedia for the use of teachers, journalists and the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="2018_.E2.80.93_Nature_for_Water"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_–_Nature_for_Water">2018 – Nature for Water</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2018 – Nature for Water">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme in 2018 explored how nature can be used to overcome the water challenges of the 21st century.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">[7]</a></sup> This could be in the form of <a href="/wiki/Nature-based_solutions" title="Nature-based solutions">nature-based solutions</a> to water-related challenges. For example, reducing <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">floods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">droughts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">water pollution</a> and protecting <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystems</a> could be solved using natural means, which nature uses, rather than man-made approaches. Restoring <a href="/wiki/Wetlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Wetlands">wetlands</a>, implementing <a href="/wiki/Constructed_wetlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Constructed wetlands">constructed wetlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_roof" title="Green roof">green roofs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_infrastructure" title="Green infrastructure">green infrastructure</a>, planting new forests, reconnecting rivers to floodplains, are some examples. Each of these use natural processes to rebalance the <a href="/wiki/Water_cycle" title="Water cycle">water cycle</a> and improve human health and livelihoods.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="2019_.E2.80.93_Leaving_No_One_Behind"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019_–_Leaving_No_One_Behind">2019 – Leaving No One Behind</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2019 – Leaving No One Behind">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme of 2019 was about tackling the water crisis by addressing the reasons why so many people are being left behind. <a href="/wiki/Social_exclusion" title="Social exclusion">Marginalized groups</a> – women, children, <a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">refugees</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">indigenous peoples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disability" title="Disability">disabled people</a> – are often overlooked, and may face discrimination, as they try to access safe water.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WWDR2019_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWDR2019-37">[37]</a></sup> UN-Water asserts that "water services must meet the needs of marginalized groups and their voices must be heard in decision-making processes".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span id="2020_.E2.80.93_Water_and_Climate_Change"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_–_Water_and_Climate_Change">2020 – Water and Climate Change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2020 – Water and Climate Change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme of World Water Day 2020 was about water and <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Global warming">climate change</a> – and how the two are inextricably linked. UN-Water states that "adapting to the water effects of climate change will protect health and save lives". Also, using water more efficiently will reduce <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse-gas_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenhouse-gas emissions">greenhouse gas emissions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-4">[4]</a></sup> Due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, the 2020 campaign also promoted messages of <a href="/wiki/Hand_washing" title="Hand washing">hand washing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hygiene" title="Hygiene">hygiene</a> and gave guidance on staying safe while supporting the campaign. </p> 
      <h3><span id="2021_.E2.80.93_Valuing_Water"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021_–_Valuing_Water">2021 – Valuing Water</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2021 – Valuing Water">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The theme for 2021 is "Valuing Water".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> People were invited to join a global conversation to "tell us your stories, thoughts and feelings about water" on social media using the hashtag <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/water2me?ref_src=twsrc%5Egoogle%7Ctwcamp%5Eserp%7Ctwgr%5Ehashtag">#Water2me</a>. The campaign looked beyond the issue of pricing, asking the public: "How is water important to your home and family life, your livelihood, your cultural practices, your wellbeing, your local environment?".<sup id="cite_ref-:5_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-3">[3]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impacts">Impacts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Water_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Impacts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Every year, World Water Day campaign messages and publications reach millions of people through <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a>, dedicated websites and other channels. In 2021, the World Water Day public campaign invited people to take part in a social media conversation (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/water2me?ref_src=twsrc%5Egoogle%7Ctwcamp%5Eserp%7Ctwgr%5Ehashtag">#Water2me</a>) about the value of water. More than 6,000 public conversations took place in over 140 countries on social media from mid-November 2020 to mid-February 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2017, 700 individual events were held in 110 countries and there were over 500,000 authors on social media using the hashtag '#WorldWaterDay'.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In 2018, there was a 25% increase in both the number of website visits and the maximum potential reach on social media largely due to celebrity support and a coordinated communications approach across the United Nations.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2016, the UN-Water annual report stated that social media engagement (hashtag #WorldWaterDay) had a maximum potential reach of 1.6 billion people worldwide in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWater2016_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWater2016-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>21</span></sup> Over 500 events in 100 countries were registered on the World Water Day website that year.<sup id="cite_ref-UNWater2016_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNWater2016-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>21</span></sup> </p>
