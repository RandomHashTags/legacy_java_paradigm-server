      <p><b>Holy Saturday</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i lang="la">Sabbatum Sanctum</i>), also known as <b>Great and Holy Saturday</b> (also <b>Holy and Great Saturday</b>), the <b>Great Sabbath</b>, <b>Hallelujah Saturday</b> (in Portugal and Brazil), <b>Saturday of the Gloria</b> and <b>Black Saturday</b> (in the Philippines) or <b>Easter Eve</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and called "Joyous Saturday" or "the Saturday of Light" among <a href="/wiki/Coptic_Orthodox_Church_of_Alexandria" title="Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria">Coptic Christians</a>, is the final day of <a href="/wiki/Holy_Week" title="Holy Week">Holy Week</a>, between <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter Sunday</a>, when Christians prepare for the latter. The day commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Harrowing_of_Hell" title="Harrowing of Hell">Harrowing of Hell</a> while <a href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesus Christ">Jesus Christ</a>'s body lay in the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Sepulchre" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Sepulchre">tomb</a>. Christians of the Catholic, Lutheran, Methodist, Anglican and Reformed denominations begin the celebration of the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Vigil" title="Easter Vigil">Easter Vigil</a> service on Holy Saturday, which provides a transition to the season of <a href="/wiki/Eastertide" title="Eastertide">Eastertide</a>; in the <a href="/wiki/Moravian_Church" title="Moravian Church">Moravian Christian</a> tradition, graves are decorated with flowers during the day of Holy Saturday and the celebration of the <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_service" title="Sunrise service">sunrise service</a> starts before dawn on Easter Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-Steenwyk_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steenwyk-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Home_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Home-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holy_Saturday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jewish_Nazarenes">Jewish Nazarenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holy_Saturday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Jewish Nazarenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Christianity" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish Christianity">Jewish Christianity</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Whereas the <a href="/wiki/Great_Sabbath" title="Great Sabbath">Great Sabbath</a> in Jewish liturgy occurs the <a href="/wiki/Sabbath" title="Sabbath">sabbath</a> before the <a href="/wiki/Feast_of_Unleavened_Bread" class="mw-redirect" title="Feast of Unleavened Bread">Feast of Unleavened Bread</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sabbath" title="Sabbath">sabbath</a> in the midst of the feast is celebrated as a <a href="/wiki/Special_Shabbat#Shabbat_Chol_Hamoed_Pesach" title="Special Shabbat">Special Shabbat §&nbsp;Shabbat Chol Hamoed Pesach</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Hebrews" class="mw-redirect" title="Book of Hebrews">book of Hebrews</a> chapter 4, Jewish christians are admonished to make every effort to enter this sabbath and every sabbath in repentance, and psalm 95 is excerpted by the author of Hebrews: "today if you hear his voice, do not harden your hearts." The entire Psalm 95 is read on friday afternoon every week in synagogue prayers immediately before <a href="/wiki/Kabbalat_Shabbat" class="mw-redirect" title="Kabbalat Shabbat">receiving the sabbath</a> in rabbinic Judaism. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_traditions">Eastern traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holy_Saturday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Eastern traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><b>Eastern Orthodoxy</b> </p>
      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodoxy" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Orthodoxy">Eastern Orthodoxy</a> this day, known as <b>Holy and Great Saturday</b>, is also called <b>The Great <a href="/wiki/Biblical_Sabbath" title="Biblical Sabbath">Sabbath</a></b> since it is on this day that <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Christ</a> "rested" physically in the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_the_Holy_Sepulchre" title="Church of the Holy Sepulchre">tomb</a>. But it is also believed that it was on this day he performed in spirit the <a href="/wiki/Harrowing_of_Hell" title="Harrowing of Hell">Harrowing of Hell</a> and raised up to <a href="/wiki/Paradise" title="Paradise">Paradise</a>, having liberated those who had been held captive. </p>
      <p><b>Oriental Orthodoxy</b> </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Coptic_Orthodox_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Coptic Orthodox Church">Coptic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Orthodox_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethiopian Orthodox Church">Ethiopian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eritrean_Orthodox_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Eritrean Orthodox Church">Eritrean</a> Orthodox Churches, this day is known as <b>Joyous Saturday</b>, otherwise known as the night of light and joy.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It is known as the <b>Saturday of Good Tidings</b> or <b>Gospel Saturday</b> in the <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Orthodox_Church" title="Syriac Orthodox Church">Syriac</a> Church, which is also a day where Syriac Christians remember their departed. <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_traditions">Western traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holy_Saturday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Western traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In Western traditions, the day is usually called <b>Holy Saturday</b>, although in the <a href="/wiki/Moravian_Church" title="Moravian Church">Moravian Church</a>, the day is referred to as the <b>Great Sabbath</b> and in the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican Communion</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Common_Prayer" title="Book of Common Prayer">Book of Common Prayer</a> refers to the day as <b>Easter Even</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker2014_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker2014-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Although the term <a href="/wiki/Easter_Saturday" title="Easter Saturday">Easter Saturday</a> is usually applied to the Saturday in <a href="/wiki/Easter_week" class="mw-redirect" title="Easter week">Easter week</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> in English-speaking countries it is sometimes applied to Holy Saturday, including in legislation in the Australian states of <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and by Australian government agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In the Catholic tradition, the <a href="/wiki/Blessed_Virgin_Mary" class="mw-redirect" title="Blessed Virgin Mary">Blessed Virgin Mary</a> as <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Sorrows" title="Our Lady of Sorrows">Our Lady of Sorrows</a> is on this day assigned the title <i>Our Lady of Solitude</i>, referring to her solace and grief at the death of her son. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_and_cultural_practices">Religious and cultural practices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holy_Saturday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religious and cultural practices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_traditions_2">Eastern traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holy_Saturday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Eastern traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">

         </div>The <a href="/wiki/Icon" title="Icon">icon</a> of Holy and Great Saturday, portraying the <a href="/wiki/Harrowing_of_Hades" class="mw-redirect" title="Harrowing of Hades">Harrowing of Hades</a>
        </div>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Matins" title="Matins">Matins</a> of Holy and Great Saturday (in parishes usually held on Friday evening)<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> takes the form of a <a href="/wiki/Funeral" title="Funeral">funeral</a> service for Christ. The entire service takes place around the <i><a href="/wiki/Epitaphios_(liturgical)" title="Epitaphios (liturgical)">Epitaphios</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/Icon" title="Icon">icon</a> in the form of a cloth embroidered with the image of Christ prepared for burial. The first part of the service consists of chanting <a href="/wiki/Psalm_119" title="Psalm 119">Psalm 118</a>, as usual at both Saturday matins and at funerals, but interspersed with hymns (<i>enkomia</i> or <i>lamentations</i>) between the verses. The predominant theme of the service is not so much one of mourning, but of watchful expectation:<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p> 
      <blockquote>
       <p>Today Thou dost keep holy the seventh day,<br> Which Thou has blessed of old by resting from Thy works.<br> Thou bringest all things into being and Thou makest all things new,<br> Observing the Sabbath rest, my Saviour, and restoring strength.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>Near the end of matins, at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Doxology" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Doxology">Great Doxology</a>, the Epitaphios is taken up and carried in <a href="/wiki/Procession#Eastern_Orthodox" title="Procession">procession</a> around the outside of the church, while the <a href="/wiki/Trisagion" title="Trisagion">Trisagion</a> is sung, as is done when carrying the body to the cemetery in an Orthodox <a href="/wiki/Christian_burial" title="Christian burial">burial</a>. </p> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg/170px-HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg/255px-HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg/340px-HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3663" data-file-height="2933"></a>

         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:HolySaturdayDivineLiturgy.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Divine Liturgy of Holy Saturday in a Greek Orthodox church in the United States

      <p>On Saturday, a <a href="/wiki/Vespers" title="Vespers">vesperal</a> <a href="/wiki/Divine_Liturgy" title="Divine Liturgy">Divine Liturgy</a> of Saint <a href="/wiki/Basil_the_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Basil the Great">Basil the Great</a> is celebrated, called the First Resurrection Service (Greek: <span lang="el" title="Greek-language text">Ἡ Πρώτη Ἀνάστασις</span>), named so because chronologically it was composed earlier than the Paschal Canon by <a href="/wiki/St_John_Damascene" class="mw-redirect" title="St John Damascene">St John Damascene</a>, rather than because it occurs earlier liturgically.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> This is the longest Divine Liturgy of the entire year and is performed later than on any other day of the year, "at the tenth hour of the day".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Little_Entrance" class="mw-redirect" title="Little Entrance">Little Entrance</a> there are 15 <a href="/wiki/Old_Testament" title="Old Testament">Old Testament</a> readings that recall the history of salvation. In the Russian tradition, just before the Gospel reading (<a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Bible_(King_James)/Matthew#28:1" class="extiw" title="s:Bible (King James)/Matthew">Matthew 28:1–20</a>) the <a href="/wiki/Antependium" title="Antependium">hangings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Altar_cloth" title="Altar cloth">altar cloths</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vestment" title="Vestment">vestments</a> are changed from <a href="/wiki/Dark" class="mw-redirect" title="Dark">dark</a> to <a href="/wiki/Brightness" title="Brightness">bright</a> and the deacon performs a <a href="/wiki/Thurible" title="Thurible">censing</a> of the church. In the Greek tradition, the <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">clergy</a> strew <a href="/wiki/Bay_laurel" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay laurel">laurel</a> leaves and flower petals all over the church to symbolize the shattered gates and broken chains of <a href="/wiki/Hell" title="Hell">hell</a> and Jesus' victory over death. While the liturgical atmosphere changes from sorrow to joy at this service, the faithful continue to <a href="/wiki/Fasting" title="Fasting">fast</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Paschal_greeting" title="Paschal greeting">Paschal greeting</a>, "Christ is risen!", is not exchanged until after midnight during the <a href="/wiki/Paschal_Vigil" class="mw-redirect" title="Paschal Vigil">Paschal Vigil</a> since this service represents the proclamation of Jesus' victory over death to those in Hades, but the <a href="/wiki/Resurrection_of_Jesus" title="Resurrection of Jesus">Resurrection</a> has not yet been announced to those on earth which takes place during the Paschal Vigil. </p>

       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Epitaphios_Procession_Beginning_at_Great_Saturday_Mattins.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Epitaphios_Procession_Beginning_at_Great_Saturday_Mattins.jpg/220px-Epitaphios_Procession_Beginning_at_Great_Saturday_Mattins.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Epitaphios_Procession_Beginning_at_Great_Saturday_Mattins.jpg/330px-Epitaphios_Procession_Beginning_at_Great_Saturday_Mattins.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Epitaphios_Procession_Beginning_at_Great_Saturday_Mattins.jpg/440px-Epitaphios_Procession_Beginning_at_Great_Saturday_Mattins.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4700" data-file-height="3200"></a>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Great_Lent" title="Great Lent">Great Lent</a> was originally the period of <a href="/wiki/Catechesis" title="Catechesis">catechesis</a> for new converts in order to prepare them for <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chrismation" title="Chrismation">chrismation</a> and when there are converts received, that occurs during the Old testament readings during the vesperal divine liturgy. Before the midnight service, the faithful gather in church for the reading of the <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_the_Apostles" title="Acts of the Apostles">Acts of the Apostles</a> in its entirety. Preceding midnight the <a href="/wiki/Paschal_Vigil" class="mw-redirect" title="Paschal Vigil">Paschal Vigil</a> begins with the <a href="/wiki/Midnight_Office" class="mw-redirect" title="Midnight Office">Midnight Office</a>, during which the <a href="/wiki/Canon_(hymnography)" title="Canon (hymnography)">Canon</a> of Holy Saturday is repeated, toward the end of which the epitaphios is removed from the center of the church and placed on the altar table where it remains until the <a href="/wiki/Ascension_of_Jesus" title="Ascension of Jesus">Ascension</a><sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_a"><a class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Saturday#endnote_a">[1]</a></sup>. Then, all of the candles and lights in the church are extinguished, and all wait in darkness and silence for the proclamation of the Resurrection of Christ. </p>
      <p>Prior to the composition of the current <a href="/wiki/Easter_Vigil" title="Easter Vigil">Paschal Vigil</a> of St. <a href="/wiki/John_of_Damascus" title="John of Damascus">John of Damascus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> this day's vesperal liturgy was the main Easter celebration.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>

      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Altar" title="Altar">altar</a> remains stripped completely bare (following the <a href="/wiki/Mass_(liturgy)" title="Mass (liturgy)">Mass</a> on <a href="/wiki/Maundy_Thursday" title="Maundy Thursday">Maundy Thursday</a>). The celebration of the Sacraments is extremely limited: <a href="/wiki/Holy_Communion" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Communion">Holy Communion</a> is given only as <a href="/wiki/Viaticum" title="Viaticum">Viaticum</a> to the dying; while <a href="/wiki/Penance" title="Penance">Penance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anointing_of_the_Sick" class="mw-redirect" title="Anointing of the Sick">Anointing of the Sick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a> may be administered because they, like Viaticum, are helpful to ensuring salvation for the dying. The day is the second day of the Paschal fast as outlined in <a href="/wiki/Sacrosanctum_Concilium" title="Sacrosanctum Concilium">Sacrosanctum Concilium</a>, Article 110.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Many of the <a href="/wiki/Church_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Church body">churches</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheranism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglicanism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodism</a>, and some other Churches observe most of the same customs of the Catholic Church; however, their altars may be covered in black instead of being stripped. In some Anglican churches, including the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Episcopal_Church_in_the_United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant Episcopal Church in the United States of America">Protestant Episcopal Church in the United States of America</a>, provision is made for a simple <a href="/wiki/Liturgy_of_the_Word" class="mw-redirect" title="Liturgy of the Word">Liturgy of the Word</a> on this day, with readings commemorating the burial of Christ. Daily Offices are still observed. In the <a href="/wiki/Moravian_Church" title="Moravian Church">Moravian Church</a>, the day is known as Great Sabbath.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker2014_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker2014-6">[6]</a></sup> The Anglican <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Common_Prayer" title="Book of Common Prayer">Book of Common Prayer</a> uses Easter Even to designate the day. </p>

      <p>In the Roman Catholic, Lutheran, Anglican and Methodist traditions, Holy Saturday lasts until nightfall, after which the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Vigil" title="Easter Vigil">Easter Vigil</a> is celebrated, marking the official start of the <a href="/wiki/Eastertide" title="Eastertide">Easter season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The rubrics<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> state that the Easter Vigil must take place in the night; it must begin after nightfall and end before dawn. The service may start with a fire and the lighting of the new <a href="/wiki/Paschal_candle" title="Paschal candle">Paschal candle</a>. In Roman Catholic and some Anglican observance, the Mass is the first Mass since that of <a href="/wiki/Maundy_Thursday" title="Maundy Thursday">Maundy Thursday</a>, and during it, the "<a href="/wiki/Gloria_in_excelsis" class="mw-redirect" title="Gloria in excelsis">Gloria</a>" — which has been absent during Lent — is used as the statues and icons, covered with purple veils during <a href="/wiki/Passiontide" title="Passiontide">Passiontide</a>, are dramatically unveiled. Some Anglican churches prefer to celebrate Easter and the lighting of the new Paschal candle at dawn on Easter Day. Baptisms may take place in this service and Baptismal vows are often renewed. </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Moravian_Church" title="Moravian Church">Moravian Church</a>, people decorate the graves in <a href="/wiki/God%27s_Acre" title="God's Acre">God's Acre</a> with flowers; the <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_service" title="Sunrise service">sunrise service</a>, the first liturgy of Paschaltide, begins before dawn of Easter Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-Home_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Home-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_the_Philippines" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism in the Philippines">predominantly Roman Catholic</a> Philippines, the day is legally and colloquially known in <a href="/wiki/Philippine_English" title="Philippine English">English</a> as <i>Black Saturday</i>, given the colour's role in mourning. Traditional taboos from the previous day are carried over and are sometimes broken; swimming is allowed in the afternoon. Most commercial establishments resume operations, with smaller enterprises remaining closed until Easter. Television and radio stations broadcast on shorter hours with special programming or remain off-air. In predominantly Roman Catholic Poland, <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Awi%C4%99conka" title="Święconka">Święconka</a> (<small>Polish pronunciation:&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Polish" title="Help:IPA/Polish">[ɕvʲɛnˈtsɔnka]</a></span>), meaning "the blessing of the Easter baskets", on Holy Saturday, is one of the most enduring and beloved traditions. </p>
