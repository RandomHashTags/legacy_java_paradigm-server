      <p><b>World <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">Philosophy</a> Day</b> is an <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#November" title="List of minor secular observances">international day</a> proclaimed by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> to be celebrated every 3rd Thursday of November. It was first celebrated on 21 November 2002. </p>
      <p>By celebrating <b>World Philosophy Day</b> each year, on the third Thursday of November, <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> underlines the enduring value of philosophy for the development of human thought, for each culture and for each individual. UNESCO has always been closely linked to philosophy, not speculative or normative philosophy, but critical questioning which enables it to give <a href="/wiki/Meaning_of_life" title="Meaning of life">meaning to life</a> and action in the international context. </p>
      <p>In <b>establishing World Philosophy Day in 2005</b>, UNESCO’s General Conference highlighted the importance of this discipline, especially for young people, underlining that “philosophy is a discipline that encourages <a href="/wiki/Critical_thinking" title="Critical thinking">critical</a> and independent thought and is capable of working towards a better understanding of the world and promoting tolerance and peace”. UNESCO's General Conference was convinced that “the <a href="/wiki/Institutionalisation" title="Institutionalisation">institutionalisation</a> of Philosophy Day at UNESCO as ‘World Philosophy Day’ would win recognition for and give strong impetus to philosophy and, in particular, to the <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_education" title="Philosophy education">teaching of philosophy</a> in the world”. </p>
