      <p><b>Veterans Day</b> (originally known as <b>Armistice Day</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Federal_holidays_in_the_United_States" title="Federal holidays in the United States">federal holiday in the United States</a> observed annually on November 11, for honoring <a href="/wiki/Veteran" title="Veteran">military veterans</a>, who are people who have served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a> (that were discharged under conditions other than dishonorable).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It coincides with other holidays including <a href="/wiki/Armistice_Day" title="Armistice Day">Armistice Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Day" title="Remembrance Day">Remembrance Day</a> which are celebrated in other countries that mark the anniversary of the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup>Major hostilities of World War I were formally ended at the 11th hour of the 11th day of the 11th month of 1918, when the <a href="/wiki/Armistice_with_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Armistice with Germany">Armistice with Germany</a> went into effect. At the urging of major U.S. veteran organizations, Armistice Day was renamed Veterans Day in 1954.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Veterans Day is distinct from <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a>, a U.S. public holiday in May. Veterans Day celebrates the service of all U.S. military veterans, while Memorial Day honors those who had <i>died</i> while in military service.<sup id="cite_ref-kelber_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kelber-5">[5]</a></sup> Another military holiday that also occurs in May, <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Day#United_States" title="Armed Forces Day">Armed Forces Day</a>, honors those <i>currently serving</i> in the U.S. military. Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Women_Veterans_Day" title="Women Veterans Day">Women Veterans Day</a> is recognized by a growing number of U.S. states that specifically honors women who have served in the U.S. military. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2> 
      <p>On November 11, 1919, U.S. president <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">Woodrow Wilson</a> issued a message to his countrymen on the first Armistice Day, in which he expressed what he felt the day meant to Americans: </p> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote"> 
       <p>ADDRESS TO FELLOW-COUNTRYMEN </p>
       <p>The White House, November 11, 1919. </p>
       <p>A year ago today our enemies laid down their arms in accordance with an armistice which rendered them impotent to renew hostilities, and gave to the world an assured opportunity to reconstruct its shattered order and to work out in peace a new and juster set of international relations. The soldiers and people of the European Allies had fought and endured for more than four years to uphold the barrier of civilization against the aggressions of armed force. We ourselves had been in the conflict something more than a year and a half. </p>
       <p>With splendid forgetfulness of mere personal concerns, we remodeled our industries, concentrated our financial resources, increased our agricultural output, and assembled a great army, so that at the last our power was a decisive factor in the victory. We were able to bring the vast resources, material and moral, of a great and free people to the assistance of our associates in Europe who had suffered and sacrificed without limit in the cause for which we fought. </p>
       <p>Out of this victory there arose new possibilities of political freedom and economic concert. The war showed us the strength of great nations acting together for high purposes, and the victory of arms foretells the enduring conquests which can be made in peace when nations act justly and in furtherance of the common interests of men. </p>
       <p>To us in America the reflections of Armistice Day will be filled with solemn pride in the heroism of those who died in the country's service, and with gratitude for the victory, both because of the thing from which it has freed us and because of the opportunity it has given America to show her sympathy with peace and justice in the councils of nations. </p>
       <p> WOODROW WILSON<sup id="cite_ref-cmh_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmh-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>The United States Congress adopted a resolution on June 4, 1926, requesting that President <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Coolidge" title="Calvin Coolidge">Calvin Coolidge</a> issue annual proclamations calling for the observance of November 11 with appropriate ceremonies.<sup id="cite_ref-cmh_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmh-6">[6]</a></sup> A Congressional Act (52 Stat. 351; 5 U.S. Code, Sec. 87a) approved May 13, 1938, made November 11 in each year a legal holiday: "a day to be dedicated to the cause of world peace and to be thereafter celebrated and known as 'Armistice Day'".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>In 1945, <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> veteran Raymond Weeks from <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama" title="Birmingham, Alabama">Birmingham, Alabama</a>, had the idea to expand Armistice Day to celebrate all veterans, not just those who died in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. Weeks led a delegation to Gen. Dwight Eisenhower, who supported the idea of National Veterans Day. Weeks led the first national celebration in 1947 in Alabama and annually until his death in 1985. President Reagan honored Weeks at the White House with the Presidential Citizenship Medal in 1982 as the driving force for the national holiday. Elizabeth Dole, who prepared the briefing for President Reagan, determined Weeks as the "Father of Veterans Day".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>U.S. representative <a href="/wiki/Edward_Herbert_Rees" title="Edward Herbert Rees">Ed Rees</a> from <a href="/wiki/Emporia,_Kansas" title="Emporia, Kansas">Emporia, Kansas</a>, presented a bill establishing the holiday through Congress. President <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a>, also from Kansas, signed the bill into law on May 26, 1954. It had been eight and a half years since Weeks held his first Armistice Day celebration for all veterans.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Congress amended the bill on June 1, 1954, replacing "Armistice" with "Veterans," and it has been known as Veterans Day since.<sup id="cite_ref-VAhistory_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VAhistory-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The National Veterans Award was also created in 1954. Congressman Rees of Kansas received the first National Veterans Award in Birmingham, Alabama, for his support offering legislation to make Veterans Day a federal holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Although originally scheduled for celebration on November 11 of every year, starting in 1971 in accordance with the <a href="/wiki/Uniform_Monday_Holiday_Act" title="Uniform Monday Holiday Act">Uniform Monday Holiday Act</a>, Veterans Day was moved to the fourth Monday of October (October 25, 1971;<sup id="cite_ref-oldvets_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oldvets-13">[13]</a></sup> October 23, 1972; October 22, 1973; October 28, 1974; October 27, 1975; October 25, 1976, and October 24, 1977). In 1978, it was moved back to its original celebration on November 11. While the legal holiday remains on November 11, if that date happens to be on a Saturday or Sunday, then organizations that formally observe the holiday will normally be closed on the adjacent Friday or Monday, respectively.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Observance">Observance</span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Veterans_Day_poster_2018.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Veterans_Day_poster_2018.jpg/170px-Veterans_Day_poster_2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Veterans_Day_poster_2018.jpg/255px-Veterans_Day_poster_2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Veterans_Day_poster_2018.jpg/340px-Veterans_Day_poster_2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5400" data-file-height="7200"></a>

      <p>Because it is a <a href="/wiki/Federal_holidays_in_the_United_States" title="Federal holidays in the United States">federal holiday</a>, some American workers and many students have Veterans Day off from work or school. When Veterans Day falls on a Saturday then either Saturday or the preceding Friday may be designated as the holiday, whereas if it falls on a Sunday it is typically observed on the following Monday. When it falls on the weekend many private companies offer it as a floating holiday where employee can choose some other day.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A <a href="/wiki/Society_for_Human_Resource_Management" title="Society for Human Resource Management">Society for Human Resource Management</a> poll in 2010 found that 21 percent of employers planned to observe the holiday in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Legally, two minutes of silence is recommended to be observed at 2:11pm <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Standard Time">Eastern Standard Time</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Non-essential federal government offices are closed. No mail is delivered. All federal workers are paid for the holiday; those who are required to work on the holiday sometimes receive holiday pay for that day in addition to their wages.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Armistice_Day">Armistice Day</span></h3> 
      <p>In his Armistice Day address to Congress, Wilson was sensitive to the psychological toll of the lean War years: "Hunger does not breed reform; it breeds madness," he remarked.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith_2007_290_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith_2007_290-17">[17]</a></sup> As Veterans Day and the birthday of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> (November 10, 1775) are only one day apart, that branch of the Armed Forces customarily observes both occasions as a 96-hour liberty period.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Election_Day_(United_States)" title="Election Day (United States)">Election Day</a> is a regular working day, while Veterans Day, which typically falls the following week, is a federal holiday. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Commission_on_Federal_Election_Reform&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Commission on Federal Election Reform (page does not exist)">National Commission on Federal Election Reform</a> called for the holidays to be merged, so citizens can have a day off to vote. They state this as a way to honor voting by exercising democratic rights.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spelling_of_Veterans_Day">Spelling of Veterans Day</span></h3> 
      <p>While the holiday is commonly printed as <i>Veteran's Day</i> or <i>Veterans' Day</i> in calendars and advertisements, the United States Department of Veterans Affairs website states that the <a href="/wiki/Attributive_noun" class="mw-redirect" title="Attributive noun">attributive</a> (no apostrophe) rather than the <a href="/wiki/Possessive_case" class="mw-redirect" title="Possessive case">possessive case</a> is the official spelling "because it is not a day that 'belongs' to veterans, it is a day for honoring all veterans."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
