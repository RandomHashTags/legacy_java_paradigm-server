      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Afghanistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Afghanistan)" title="Martyrs' Day (Afghanistan)">Martyrs' Day</a> is observed on September 8 or 9th (<a href="/wiki/Solar_Hijri_calendar" title="Solar Hijri calendar">variable</a>), the anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Shah_Massoud" title="Ahmad Shah Massoud">Ahmad Shah Massoud</a>'s 2001 assassination. It commemorates all those who died <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–present)">fighting</a> for <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Armenia">Armenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Armenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide_Remembrance_Day" title="Armenian Genocide Remembrance Day">Armenian Genocide Remembrance Day</a>, also known as <b>Armenian Christian Martyr day</b>, on April 24 in Armenia, commemorating the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_genocide" class="mw-redirect" title="Armenian genocide">Armenian genocide</a> from 1915 to 1923 in <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> Turkey. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Azerbaijan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Martyrs' Day in Azerbaijan is observed on January 20, in memory of those killed in the <a href="/wiki/Black_January" title="Black January">Black January</a> events. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Language_Movement_Day" title="Language Movement Day">Language Movement Day</a>, also known as <b>Language Martyrs' Day</b>, on <b>21 February</b> in Bangladesh, commemorates Bengali as a national language.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Bengali_Genocide_Remembrance_Day" title="Bengali Genocide Remembrance Day">Bengali Genocide Remembrance Day</a> is observed on <b>25 March</b> in Bangladesh to commemorate the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Bengali_Genocide_of_1971" class="mw-redirect" title="Bengali Genocide of 1971">Bengali Genocide of 1971</a>, initially in 2017.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Martyred_Intellectuals_Day" title="Martyred Intellectuals Day">Martyred Intellectuals Day</a> is observed on <b>14 December</b> in Bangladesh to commemorate those intellectuals <a href="/wiki/1971_killing_of_Bengali_intellectuals" title="1971 killing of Bengali intellectuals">who were killed</a> by Pakistani forces and their collaborators during the <a href="/wiki/Liberation_War_of_Bangladesh" class="mw-redirect" title="Liberation War of Bangladesh">1971 Liberation War</a>, particularly on 14 December 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-Rahman_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rahman-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Banglapedia_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Banglapedia-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Burkina_Faso">Burkina Faso</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Burkina Faso">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">Martyrs' Day</a>, on October 31, honors victims of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Burkinab%C3%A9_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="2015 Burkinabé coup d'état">2015 Burkinabé coup d'état</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Burma">Burma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Burma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Burmese_Martyrs%27_Day" title="Burmese Martyrs' Day">Burmese Martyrs' Day</a>, on July 19, the day when nine Burmese independence leaders were assassinated in 1947. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(China)" title="Martyrs' Day (China)">Martyrs' Day (China)</a> is celebrated on September 30, the eve of the <a href="/wiki/National_Day_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="National Day of the People's Republic of China">National Day of the People's Republic of China</a>, to commemorate those who lost their lives for the national and territorial integrity of the people of China. It was created by the <a href="/wiki/National_People%27s_Congress" title="National People's Congress">National People's Congress</a> in 2014. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eritrea">Eritrea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Eritrea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Eritrea)" class="mw-redirect" title="Martyrs' Day (Eritrea)">Martyrs' Day (Eritrea)</a>, is observed on June 20 of every year to honor the fallen heroes of Eritrea's warriors from the Yikealo and Warsay generations . </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(India)" title="Martyrs' Day (India)">Martyrs' Day (India)</a>, is observed on January 30, the anniversary of the Assassination of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi|assassination of Mohandas Gandhi in 1948. Martyrs' day is also observed across the Indian state of Punjab on March 23, on the death anniversary of Bhagat Singh in 1931 . 30 January is the date observed at the national level. The date was chosen as it marks the assassination of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi in 1948 by Nathuram Godse.[1] On Martyr’s Day the president, the vice president, the prime minister, the defence minister, and the three Service Chiefs gather at the samadhi at Raj Ghat memorial and lay wreaths decorated with multi-colour flowers. The armed forces personnel blow bugles sounding the Last Post. The inter-services contingent reverse arms as a mark of respect. A two-minute silence in memory of Indian martyrs is observed throughout the country at 11 AM. Participants hold all-religion prayers and sing tributes.[2] </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lebanon">Lebanon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Lebanon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Lebanon_and_Syria)" title="Martyrs' Day (Lebanon and Syria)">Martyrs' Day (Lebanon and Syria)</a>, commemorates the execution of Lebanese and Syrian nationalists in Beirut (on what's now called Martyrs Square in DownTown) by the Ottoman occupants on May 6, 1916. Also commemorates the martyrs of the Lebanese civil war from 1975 till 1990. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Libya">Libya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Libya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>16 September <a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Libya)" class="mw-redirect" title="Martyrs' Day (Libya)">Martyrs' Day</a> remembers Libyans killed or exiled under <a href="/wiki/History_of_Libya_as_Italian_Colony" class="mw-redirect" title="History of Libya as Italian Colony">Italian rule</a> and those who were killed in the 17 of February revolution. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Madagascar">Madagascar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Madagascar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day_(Madagascar)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Martyrs' Day (Madagascar) (page does not exist)">Martyrs' Day</a>, every 29 March, commemorates the beginning of the 1947 <a href="/wiki/Malagasy_Uprising" title="Malagasy Uprising">Malagasy Uprising</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Malawi">Malawi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Malawi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Malawi, Martyrs' Day is celebrated on March 3 to honor the political heroes who gave their lives in the struggle against British colonialism.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Malaysia">Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Warriors%27_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Warriors' Day">Warriors' Day</a> (<a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a>: <i lang="ms">Hari Pahlawan</i>； <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">国家纪念日</span>) is a day in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> that commemorates the servicemen killed during the two <a href="/wiki/World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="World War">World Wars</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Malayan_Emergency" title="Malayan Emergency">Malayan Emergency</a>. By extension, it honours all individuals who lost their lives in the line of duty throughout Malaysia's history. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mali">Mali</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Mali">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Mali" title="Public holidays in Mali">Public holidays in Mali</a>
      </div> 
      <p><b>Martyrs' Day</b> or <b>Democracy Day</b> is celebrated on <a href="/wiki/March_26" title="March 26">March 26</a> and honors the victims of the <a href="/wiki/1991_Malian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1991 Malian coup d'état">1991 Malian coup d'état</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span id="Mizoram_.28India.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mizoram_(India)">Mizoram (India)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Mizoram (India)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Mizoram)" class="mw-redirect" title="Martyrs' Day (Mizoram)">Martyrs' Day</a>, every 20 September observed by <a href="/wiki/Mizo_National_Front" title="Mizo National Front">MNF</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nepal">Nepal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Nepal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Nepal Martyrs day(<a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a>: <span lang="ne">शहीद दिवस</span>)is celebrated in (<a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a>: <span lang="ne"><a href="/wiki/Magh_(Nepali_calendar)" title="Magh (Nepali calendar)">माघ</a> १६ गते</span>)Magh 16 of <a href="/wiki/Vikram_Samvat" title="Vikram Samvat">Vikram Samvat calendar</a>. The month of January and February correspond to Magh. <b>Martyr</b> (<a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a>: <span lang="ne">शहिद</span>; Shahid) in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> is a term for some one who is executed while making contributions for the welfare of the country or society. The term was originally used for individuals who died while opposing the <a href="/wiki/Rana_dynasty" title="Rana dynasty">Rana Regime</a> which was in place in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Nepal" title="Kingdom of Nepal">Kingdom of Nepal</a> from 1846 until 1951.There are five(5)martyrs' in Nepal. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Lakhan_Thapa" title="Lakhan Thapa">Lakhan Thapa</a> is regarded as the first <a href="/wiki/Martyr" title="Martyr">martyr</a> of Nepal.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistan">Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Martyrs' Day (Pakistan) or Youm-e-Shuda is a Pakistani holiday held on 30 April to pay tribute to Pakistani military who died in service of their country. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Panama">Panama</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Panama">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Panama)" title="Martyrs' Day (Panama)">Martyrs' Day (Panama)</a>, is a Panamanian holiday which commemorates the January 9, 1964 riots over sovereignty of the Panama Canal Zone. </p> 
      <h2><span id="S.C3.A3o_Tom.C3.A9_and_Pr.C3.ADncipe"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="São_Tomé_and_Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: São Tomé and Príncipe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><i>Dia dos Mártires da Liberdade</i>, 3 February, commemorates the 1953 <a href="/wiki/Batep%C3%A1_massacre" title="Batepá massacre">Batepá massacre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Sudan">South Sudan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: South Sudan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>30 July is <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_South_Sudan" title="Public holidays in South Sudan">Martyr's Day in South Sudan</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/John_Garang_de_Mabior" class="mw-redirect" title="John Garang de Mabior">John Garang de Mabior</a> led the <a href="/wiki/Sudan_People%27s_Liberation_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Sudan People's Liberation Army">Sudan People's Liberation Army</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sudanese_Civil_War" title="Second Sudanese Civil War">Second Sudanese Civil War</a>. Following a peace agreement, he briefly served as First Vice President of Sudan for three weeks until his death in a helicopter crash on July 30, 2005. Foul play in his death has never been proven, but July 30 is marked as Martyr's Day in <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Syria">Syria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Syria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(Lebanon_and_Syria)" title="Martyrs' Day (Lebanon and Syria)">Martyrs' Day (Lebanon and Syria)</a>, commemorates the execution of Syrian nationalists in Damascus by the Ottoman occupants on May 6, 1916. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Togo">Togo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Togo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>June 21. The day honors all who struggled for the freedom of Togo. It is celebrated with a military parade in <a href="/wiki/Lom%C3%A9" title="Lomé">Lomé</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tunisia">Tunisia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Tunisia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>April 9, Martyr's Day remembers Tunisians killed in 1938. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Turkey">Turkey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Turkey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>March 18, in remembrance of Turkish soldiers fallen in action.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Military_of_the_Ottoman_Empire" title="Military of the Ottoman Empire">Ottoman forces</a> defeated a naval attack of the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I" title="Allies of World War I">Allied Powers</a> in <a href="/wiki/Naval_operations_in_the_Dardanelles_Campaign#The_Battle_of_March_18" class="mw-redirect" title="Naval operations in the Dardanelles Campaign">Gallipoli Campaign</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> on March 18, 1915. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vietnam">Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Vietnam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Vietnam" title="Public holidays in Vietnam">Memorial Day for War Martyrs</a>, on July 27, in Vietnam. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uganda">Uganda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Uganda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Uganda, Martyrs' Day is celebrated on June 3, mainly in honor of the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_Martyrs" title="Uganda Martyrs">Uganda Martyrs</a>; Christian converts who were murdered for their religion in Uganda's biggest kingdom, Buganda in the late-1880s. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Arab_Emirates">United Arab Emirates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martyrs%27_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: United Arab Emirates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Martyrs%27_Day_(United_Arab_Emirates)" class="mw-redirect" title="Martyrs' Day (United Arab Emirates)">Martyrs' Day (United Arab Emirates)</a> also known as <a href="/wiki/Commemoration_Day_(United_Arab_Emirates)" class="mw-redirect" title="Commemoration Day (United Arab Emirates)">Commemoration Day (United Arab Emirates)</a> will be marked annually on November 30, recognising the sacrifices and dedication of <a href="/wiki/Emirati_martyrs" class="mw-redirect" title="Emirati martyrs">Emirati martyrs</a> who have given their life in the UAE and abroad in the field of civil, military and humanitarian service.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
