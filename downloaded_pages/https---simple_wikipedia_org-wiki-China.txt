      <p><b>China</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">中国</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">中國;</span> <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">Pinyin</a>: Zhōngguó) is a <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">cultural</a> <a href="/wiki/Region" title="Region">region</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">ancient</a> <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilization</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> in <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>. The official name is <b>People's Republic of China</b>. </p>
      <p>The last <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> (1927–1949) resulted in two different political powers today: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><span class="flagicon"><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China"><img alt="Republic of China" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Flag_of_the_Republic_of_China.svg/23px-Flag_of_the_Republic_of_China.svg.png" decoding="async" width="23" height="15" class="thumbborder" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Flag_of_the_Republic_of_China.svg/35px-Flag_of_the_Republic_of_China.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Flag_of_the_Republic_of_China.svg/45px-Flag_of_the_Republic_of_China.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600"></a></span> The <i><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China (ROC)</a> </i>(since 1911), commonly known as <i>China since 1 January.,1911 to 25 October,1971.</i> Now commonly known as <i><a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a></i>, has controlled over the islands of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penghu" title="Penghu">Penghu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Kinmen</a>, and Matsu.</li> 
       <li><span class="flagicon"><a href="/wiki/China" title="China"><img alt="China" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Flag_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China.svg/23px-Flag_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China.svg.png" decoding="async" width="23" height="15" class="thumbborder" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Flag_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China.svg/35px-Flag_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Flag_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China.svg/45px-Flag_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600"></a></span> The <i><a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China (PRC)</a> </i>(since 1949), commonly known as <i>China</i>, has control over <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland China</a> and the largely self-governing territories of <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> (since 1997) and <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> (since 1999).</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>China is one of the world's oldest civilizations: it has the oldest continuous civilization.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[18]</a></sup> There is <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeological</a> evidence over 5,000 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-Hagg_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hagg-27">[19]</a></sup> China also has one of the world's oldest <a href="/wiki/Chinese_character" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese character">writing systems</a> (and the oldest in use today). China has been the source of many major <a href="/wiki/Inventions" class="mw-redirect" title="Inventions">inventions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hagg_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hagg-27">[19]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Origins">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Origins">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The first recorded use of the word "China" is dated 1555.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[nb 1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OnlineEty_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OnlineEty-29">[20]</a></sup> It is derived from <i>chīnī</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> adjective meaning 'Chinese' which was popularized in Europe by <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo" title="Marco Polo">Marco Polo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OnlineEty_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OnlineEty-29">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wood_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-30">[21]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_China" title="History of China">History of China</a>

      <h3><span id="Ancient_.282100_B.C._.E2.80.93_1500_A.D..29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_(2100_B.C._–_1500_A.D.)">Ancient (2100 B.C. – 1500 A.D.)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Ancient (2100 B.C. – 1500 A.D.)">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Ancient (2100 B.C. – 1500 A.D.)">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Ancient_China" title="Ancient China">Ancient China</a> was one of the first <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilizations</a> and was active since the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium_BC" title="2nd millennium BC">2nd millennium BC</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudal</a> <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">society</a>. Chinese civilization was also one of the few to invent <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writing</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Hagg_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hagg-27">[19]</a></sup> with the others being <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_civilization" title="Indus Valley civilization">Indus Valley civilization</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Maya civilization">Maya civilization</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Minoan_civilization" title="Minoan civilization">Minoan civilization</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">ancient Greece</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[22]</a></sup> It reached its golden age during the <a href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a> (c. A.D. 10th century). Home of <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daoism" class="mw-redirect" title="Daoism">Daoism</a>, it had great influence on nearby countries including Japan, Korea, and Vietnam in the areas of political system, philosophy, religion, art, writing and literature. China is home to some of the oldest artwork in the world. Statues and pottery, as well as decorations made of <a href="/wiki/Jade" title="Jade">jade</a>, are some classic examples. </p>
      <p>Before the <a href="/wiki/Qin_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qin Dynasty">Qin Dynasty</a> united China, there were hundreds of small states that fought each other for hundreds of years in a war to control China. This is known as the <a href="/wiki/Warring_States_Period" class="mw-redirect" title="Warring States Period">Warring States Period</a>. Although the continuing wars made people suffer, it was at this time when many great philosophies were born, including <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daoism" class="mw-redirect" title="Daoism">Daoism</a>. Confucianism and Daoism alone have been the foundation of many social values seen in modern eastern-Asian cultures today. </p>
      <p>Its geography mostly looked like that of modern China, except with northern and western edges that varied. It was often attacked by northern <a href="/wiki/Nomadic_people" title="Nomadic people">nomadic people</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic peoples</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mongol" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongol">Mongols</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Genghis_Khan" title="Genghis Khan">Genghis Khan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kublai_Khan" title="Kublai Khan">Kublai Khan</a>. During the history of ancient China, the northern nomadic people and the Chinese people had been fighting each other and taking turns to rule the land and the people of China. However, when the northern people beat the Chinese people and came to rule the kingdom, they also Incorporated the Chinese way of living and became like the Chinese. Many of the strongest dynasties of China were ruled by the northern people, including the <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qing Dynasty">Qin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan</a> (Mongolian), and <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing</a>. Each time, they also brought new elements into the Chinese culture. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A_new_age">A new age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: A new age">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: A new age">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>While China achieved many things in the <a href="/wiki/First_millennium" class="mw-redirect" title="First millennium">First millennium</a> and early <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>, it became an <a href="/w/index.php?title=Isolationism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Isolationism (not yet started)">isolationist</a> country in the <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">15th century C.E.</a> This was because Spain found enormous silver in the new continent, which was the main currency (money) in China and Europe at the time, and China did not want to be bought by the foreigners. </p>
      <p>By the time of the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>, European powers started to take over other countries in Asia. While China was never actually taken over, many <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> <a href="/wiki/Countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Countries">countries</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Britain">Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> built <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spheres_of_influence&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spheres of influence (not yet started)">spheres of influence</a> in China. Since China had cut itself off from the world over the previous few <a href="/wiki/Centuries" class="mw-redirect" title="Centuries">centuries</a>, by the <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a>, it had fallen behind other countries in <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, and was helpless to stop this from happening. This had become clear when it lost the <a href="/wiki/Opium_Wars" title="Opium Wars">Opium Wars</a> to <a href="/wiki/Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Britain">Britain</a> in the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>. </p>
      <p>Still influenced by Western sources, China faced internal strife. The Taiping Rebellion or Taiping War occurred in China from 1851 through 1864. The Taiping Rebellion was led by Hong Xiuquan from Guangdon. Hong Xiuquan was influenced by Christian missionaries and declared himself the brother of Jesus. Hong made his mission to bring down the Qing Dynasty. Gaining influence on the southern Chinese population, the Taiping Rebellion attracted tens of thousands of supporters. The Taiping regime successfully created a state within the Qing Empire with the capital at Nanjing. Hong called his new state the Taiping Tianguo or "The Heavenly State of Great Peace". Local armies eventually suppressed the rebellion at the final battle of Nanjing. <sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[23]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 1911, the Republic of China was founded by <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen" title="Sun Yat-sen">Sun Yat-sen</a>, but its government was very weak. <a href="/wiki/Warlord" title="Warlord">Warlords</a> controlled many areas. <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek" title="Chiang Kai-shek">Chiang Kai-shek</a> led wars against them, and he became President and dictator. </p>
      <p>In 1931, Japan invaded <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a>, a place in the northeastern part of China. On July 7, 1937, the Japanese attacked the rest of the country, starting what was called the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">Second Sino-Japanese War</a>. The war later became part of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The war was fought for eight years and millions of Chinese people were killed. </p>
      <p>However, the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> later started between the <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> (Nationalists) of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> (ROC) and the Communists of the People's Republic of China (PRC). The Communists wanted to make China like the Soviet Union, whereas the other side wanted to keep China in its current state at the time. The Communists were led by <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liu_Shaoqi" title="Liu Shaoqi">Liu Shaoqi</a> ,Zhou Enlai and others. The Communists eventually won the war by uniting all the people from different positions. The Nationalists (led by Chiang Kai-shek) fled to the island of Taiwan and set up their new capital city in Taipei. After the Chinese Civil War, the Communist leader Mao Zedong declared a new country, the People's Republic of China (PRC), in Beijing on October 1, 1949. </p>
      <p>Under Mao the country stayed poor while Taiwan became richer. His attempt at <a href="/wiki/Industrialization" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialization">industrialization</a> and collectivization with the <a href="/wiki/Great_Leap_Forward" title="Great Leap Forward">Great Leap Forward</a> led to the deaths of many people from <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">famine</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a> caused great social upheaval. After 1976, China underwent <a href="/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">market economy</a> reforms under <a href="/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping" title="Deng Xiaoping">Deng Xiaoping</a>, and experienced rapid economic growth, which made the former progress made by Taiwan became overshadowed. China is now one of the largest economies in the world, relying mainly on exports. </p>
      <p>In recent history, China has had problems with protests, blocking of information on the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Censorship" title="Censorship">censorship</a> of news. 1989 was notable for the controversial Tian An Men Event. But also known for its social stability and low crime rate. Since the 2008 Olympics, China has hosted many major international events, and the 2022 Winter Olympics will be held in Beijing, China. </p>

      <p>China's landscape is vast and diverse. It ranges from the <a href="/wiki/Gobi_Desert" title="Gobi Desert">Gobi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taklamakan_Desert" title="Taklamakan Desert">Taklamakan Deserts</a> in the north to subtropical forests in the south. The <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalaya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karakoram" title="Karakoram">Karakoram</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pamir_mountain_range&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pamir mountain range (not yet started)">Pamir</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tian_Shan" title="Tian Shan">Tian Shan</a> mountain ranges separate China from much of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Yangtze</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow Rivers</a> run from the Tibetan Plateau to the densely populated eastern coast. The Yangtze River is the third-longest river in the world while the Yellow River is the sixth-longest. China's coastline along the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> is 14,500 kilometers (9,000 mi) long. It is bounded by the <a href="/wiki/Bohai_Sea" title="Bohai Sea">Bohai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China seas</a>. China connects through the <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakh</a> border to the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Steppe" title="Eurasian Steppe">Eurasian Steppe</a>. The Eurasian Steppe has been an artery of communication between East and West since the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> through the Steppe route. The Steppe Route is the ancestor of the terrestrial <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road(s)</a>. </p>

      <p>China's constitution states that The People's Republic of China "is a socialist state under the people's democratic dictatorship led by the working class and based on the alliance of workers and peasants". It also states the state organs "apply the principle of democratic centralism." The PRC is one of the world's only socialist states openly being communist. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Military">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Military">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>With 2.3 million active troops, the People's Liberation Army (PLA) is the largest standing military force in the world. The PLA is commanded by the Central Military Commission (CMC).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[24]</a></sup> China has the second-biggest military reserve force, only behind North Korea. The PLA consists of the Ground Force (PLAGF), the Navy (PLAN), the Air Force (PLAAF), and the People's Liberation Army Rocket Force (PLARF). According to the Chinese government, China's military budget for 2017 was US$151,5 billion. China has the world's second-largest military budget. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_technology">Science and technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Science and technology">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Science and technology">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

      <p>China was once a world leader in science and technology up until the Ming dynasty. There are many Ancient Chinese discoveries and inventions. For example, papermaking, printing, the compass, and gunpowder are known as the Four Great Inventions. They became widespread across East Asia, the Middle East and later to Europe. Chinese mathematicians were the first to use negative numbers. By the 17th century, Europe and the Western world became better than China in science and technology. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Demographics">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Demographics">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The national census of 2010 recorded the population of the People's Republic of China to be about 1,370,536,875. About 16.60% of the population were 14 years old or younger, 70.14% were between 15 and 59 years old, and 13.26% were over 60 years old. The population growth rate for 2013 is estimated to be 0.46%. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>China is the origin of Eastern <a href="/wiki/Martial_art" class="mw-redirect" title="Martial art">martial arts</a>, called <a href="/wiki/Kung-fu" title="Kung-fu">Kung Fu</a> or its first name Wushu. China is also the home of the well-respected <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spa_Monastery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spa Monastery (not yet started)">Spa Monastery</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wudang_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wudang Mountains (not yet started)">Wudang Mountains</a>. Martial art started more for the purpose of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Survival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Survival (not yet started)">survival</a>, defense, and <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">warfare</a> than <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a>. Over time some <a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_form&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Art form (not yet started)">art forms</a> have branched off, while others have retained their distinct Chinese flavor. </p>
      <p>China has had renowned <a href="/wiki/Artist" title="Artist">artists</a> including <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wong_Fei_Hung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wong Fei Hung (not yet started)">Wong Fei Hung</a> (Huang Fei Hung or Hwang Fei Hung) and many others. <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">Art</a> has also co-existed with a variety of <a href="/wiki/Paint" title="Paint">paints</a> including the more standard 18 colors. Legendary and controversial moves like <a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Mak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Big Mak (not yet started)">Big Mak</a> are also praised and talked about within the <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a>. </p>
      <p>China has many traditional <a href="/wiki/Festival" title="Festival">festivals</a>, such as Spring Festival, Dragon Boat Festival, Mid-autumn Festival and so on. The most important is <a href="/wiki/Chinese_New_Year" title="Chinese New Year">Chinese New Year</a>. People in China will have holidays to celebrate these festivals. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Festivals">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Change section: Festivals">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Spring Festival is the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_New_Year" title="Chinese New Year">Chinese New Year</a>. </p>
      <p>Dragon Boat Festival is celebrated to commemorate the death of Qu Yuan, a patriotic poet of the State of Chu during the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Warring_States&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Warring States (not yet started)">Warring States</a> period. He persuaded his emperor not to accept Qin's diplomats' offers several times but his emperor did not listen to him. He was very sad and ended up jumping into the river to end his life. The people loved him so much that they did not want the fish to eat his corpse. They made and threw rice dumplings into the river. They hope the fish eat these dumplings instead of the poet's corpse. They also rowed dragon boats in the river to get rid of the fish. Such practices, eating rice dumplings and holding dragon boat races, become what Chinese do in this festival nowadays. </p>
      <p>Held on the fifteenth day of the eighth lunar month, Mid-Autumn Festival is a festival for families. Now when the festival sets in, people would sit together to eat moon cakes, appreciate the bright full moon cakes, appreciate the bright full moon, celebrate the bumper harvest and enjoy the family love and happiness. To the Chinese people, the full moon symbolizes family reunion, as does the "moon cakes." Hence the Mid-Autumn Festival is also called the Family Reunion Festival. </p>
