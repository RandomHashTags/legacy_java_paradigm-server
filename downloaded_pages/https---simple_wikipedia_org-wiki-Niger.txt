      <p><b>Niger</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in western <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Niamey" title="Niamey">Niamey</a>, and the official language is <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. It is surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a> to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> to the west. Niger is landlocked, meaning it has no coastline. Niger gets its name from the <a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a>, whose name possibly comes from the Berber word "<a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">River of Rivers</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Mali_Empire" title="Mali Empire">Mali Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Songhai_Empire" title="Songhai Empire">Songhai Empire</a> both had land in what is now Niger. Later <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> controlled the land that is now Niger. Niger has a population, or the number of people living in an area, of 15,306,252 and an area of 1,267,000 <a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">square kilometres</a>. Much of Niger is <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">desert</a>. </p>
      <p>After a military <a href="/wiki/Coup" class="mw-redirect" title="Coup">coup</a> in 2010, Niger is now a democratic, multi-party state. Most of the people live in rural areas, and have little access to advanced education. </p>
      <p>Niger is one of the poorest countries in the world with a lack of <a href="/wiki/Contraception" class="mw-redirect" title="Contraception">contraception</a>. 41.4% live in <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hunger" title="Hunger">Hunger</a> plagues the country. </p>
      <p>Niger’s main exports are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uranium_ore&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Uranium ore (not yet started)">uranium ore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cowpeas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cowpeas (not yet started)">cowpeas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Onions" class="mw-redirect" title="Onions">onions</a>. <a href="/wiki/Millet" title="Millet">Millet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sorghum" title="Sorghum">sorghum</a> are grown in the south, as well as cassava and sugarcane. Rice is grown in the Niger River valley. Peanuts are the most important cash crop. Other important crops are cotton and pulses. </p>
      <p>Niger is home to the critically endangered <a href="/w/index.php?title=Addax_antelope&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Addax antelope (not yet started)">addax antelope</a>. West African giraffes are the only <a href="/wiki/Giraffes" class="mw-redirect" title="Giraffes">giraffes</a> in West Africa that exist in Niger. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niger&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Niger is on the border between the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan</a> regions. Niger's area is 1,267,000 square kilometres (489,191&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) of which 300 square kilometres (116&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) is water. It is the world's twenty-second largest country. Niger borders seven countries. The longest border is with <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> to the south. </p>
      <p>The lowest point is the <a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a>, with an elevation of 200 metres (656&nbsp;ft). The highest point is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mont_Idoukal-n-Tagh%C3%A8s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mont Idoukal-n-Taghès (not yet started)">Mont Idoukal-n-Taghès</a> in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=A%C3%AFr_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aïr Mountains (not yet started)">Aïr Mountains</a> at 2,022&nbsp;m (6,634&nbsp;ft). </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niger&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Climate">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Climate">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Niger's <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> climate is mainly very hot and dry. Much of it is covered by <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a>. In the extreme south there is a tropical climate on the edges of the <a href="/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a> basin. The land is mostly desert plains and <a href="/wiki/Sand_dune" class="mw-redirect" title="Sand dune">sand dunes</a>. There is flat to rolling savanna in the south and hills in the north. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niger&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Literacy_rate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Literacy rate (not yet started)">literacy rate</a> (the amount of the adult population who can read or write) of Niger is among the lowest in the world. In 2005 it was estimated to be only 28.7% (42.9% male and 15.1% female).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Primary education in Niger is required for six years.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-10">[10]</a></sup> The primary school enrollment and attendance rates are low, particularly for girls.<sup id="cite_ref-ilab_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilab-10">[10]</a></sup> Children are often made to work rather than attend school. This is particularly true during planting or <a href="/wiki/Harvest" title="Harvest">harvest</a> periods. </p>
