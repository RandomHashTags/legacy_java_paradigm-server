      <p><b>Azerbaijan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>: <i lang="az">Azərbaycan</i>; officially called the <b>Republic of Azerbaijan</b>) is a country in the <a href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">South Caucasus</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. It is next to <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> in the north, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, in the west, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> in the south, and <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> on the east. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (city)">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a>. Azerbaijan became <a href="/wiki/Independent" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent">independent</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> when it ended in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>. </p>
      <p>Azerbaijan also includes Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic, an <a href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">enclave</a>, which is next to Armenia to the north and east, Iran to the south and west, and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> to the northwest. </p>
      <p>Most of Azerbaijan's land is in <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. It is called an Eurasian country by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>. </p>
      <p>However, because it is close and its history is related to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, Azerbaijan is also a member of a number of European groups, including the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> since <a href="/wiki/2001" title="2001">2001</a>. Azerbaijan has diplomatic relations with 158 countries. They have membership in 38 international organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-FCO_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FCO-6">[6]</a></sup> On May 9, 2006 Azerbaijan was elected to membership in the newly made <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Human Rights Council">Human Rights Council</a> by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Un.org_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Un.org-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>More than 90% of the people are ethnic <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijanis" class="mw-redirect" title="Azerbaijanis">Azerbaijanis</a>. Minorities include <a href="/wiki/Russians" class="mw-redirect" title="Russians">Russians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgians" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgians">Georgians</a> and other minorities. The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Azerbaijan" title="Constitution of Azerbaijan">Constitution of Azerbaijan</a> does not say there is an official religion. But, <a href="/wiki/Shia" class="mw-redirect" title="Shia">Shia</a> <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, is by far the largest <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> in the country, followed by <a href="/wiki/Sunni" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunni">Sunni</a> Islam. There are also a small number of <a href="/wiki/Christians" class="mw-redirect" title="Christians">Christians</a> (mainly <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Orthodox">Eastern Orthodox</a>), <a href="/wiki/Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Jews">Jews</a> (mainly <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashkenazi">Ashkenazi</a>), <a href="/wiki/Agnostics" class="mw-redirect" title="Agnostics">agnostics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atheists" class="mw-redirect" title="Atheists">atheists</a>. </p>
      <p>The name of Azerbaijan comes from <i><a href="/wiki/Atropates" title="Atropates">Atropates</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Schippmann_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schippmann-9">[9]</a></sup> It was a <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" title="Persian people">Persian</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Satrap" title="Satrap">satrap</a> under the Achaemenid Empire. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Azerbaijan" title="History of Azerbaijan">History of Azerbaijan</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Ancient Azerbaijan was named "<a href="/wiki/Caucasian_Albania" title="Caucasian Albania">Caucasian Albania</a>" and was relatively independent under Roman/Byzantine (<a href="/wiki/Roman_Azerbaijan" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Azerbaijan">Roman Azerbaijan</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Persian</a> control. </p>
      <p>After the <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arab</a> conquest in the <a href="/wiki/7th_century" title="7th century">7th century</a> the country has been always under <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> influence, when its people became Muslim, until the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> entered the Caucasus region. </p>
      <p>From <a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a> to <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, Azerbaijan was a <a href="/wiki/Communist" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist">Communist</a> country as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. </p>
      <p>Since the early <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a> many Russians settled in Azerbaijan, but after the end of the Soviet Union with the independence of Azerbaijan which is back in control, most Russians and other minorities have left the country and are continuing to leave the country. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biodiversity">Biodiversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Biodiversity">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Biodiversity">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are 106 species of mammals, 97 species of fish, 363 species of birds, 10 species of amphibians and 52 species of reptiles which have been recorded and classified in Azerbaijan.<sup id="cite_ref-GEO_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GEO-13">[13]</a></sup> The national animal of Azerbaijan is the <a href="/wiki/Karabakh_horse" title="Karabakh horse">Karabakh horse</a>. It is a mountain-steppe racing and riding horse native to Azerbaijan. It is one of the oldest breeds, with ancestry dating to the ancient world. However today the horse is an endangered species.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Azerbaijan's flora is more than 4,500 species of <a href="/wiki/Higher_plants" class="mw-redirect" title="Higher plants">higher plants</a>. Due the unique climate in Azerbaijan, the flora is much richer in the number of species than the flora of the other countries of the South Caucasus.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> About 67 percent of the species growing in the whole <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> can be in Azerbaijan. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Education">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Education">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Many Azerbaijanis have some form of higher education, most notably in scientific and technical subjects.<sup id="cite_ref-AZ_study_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AZ_study-16">[16]</a></sup> According to Soviet data, 100 percent of males and females (ages nine to forty-nine) were literate (able to read) in 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-AZ_study_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AZ_study-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2009, the literacy rate in Azerbaijan was 99.5 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Culture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Culture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The culture of Azerbaijan has come about as a result of many influences. Today, <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">Western</a> influences, including <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalized</a> <a href="/wiki/Consumer" title="Consumer">consumer</a> culture, are strong. National traditions are well kept in the country. Some of the main parts of the Azerbaijani culture are: music, literature, folk dances and art, cuisine, architecture, and movies. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_and_folk_dances">Music and folk dances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Music and folk dances">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Music and folk dances">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Music of Azerbaijan builds on <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk traditions</a> that goes back nearly a thousand years.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Among national musical instruments there are 14 <a href="/wiki/String_instruments" class="mw-redirect" title="String instruments">string instruments</a>, eight <a href="/wiki/Percussion_instruments" class="mw-redirect" title="Percussion instruments">percussion instruments</a> and six <a href="/wiki/Wind_instruments" class="mw-redirect" title="Wind instruments">wind instruments</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Mugham, meykhana and Ashiq are some of the many musical traditions of Azerbaijan. Mugham is music with <a href="/wiki/Poetry" title="Poetry">poetry</a> and instrumental interludes. When performing Mugham, the singers have to bring their emotions into singing and music. Mugham singer <a href="/wiki/Alim_Qasimov" title="Alim Qasimov">Alim Qasimov</a> is one of the five best singers of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Meykhana is a song with no music. It is usually done by several people. They make up the words about a particular subject. Ashiq joins poetry, storytelling, dance and vocal and instrumental music. It is as a symbol of Azerbaijani culture. </p>
      <p>Azerbaijan was at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2008" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2008">Eurovision Song Contest</a> for the first time in 2008. They hosted the contest in <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2012" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2012">2012</a>, in Baku.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are dozens of Azerbaijani <a href="/wiki/Folk_dance" title="Folk dance">folk dances</a>. They are performed at formal <a href="/wiki/Festival" title="Festival">festivals</a>. The dancers wear national clothes like the <a href="/wiki/Chokha" title="Chokha">Chokha</a>. Most dances have a very fast <a href="/wiki/Rhythm" title="Rhythm">rhythm</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Architecture">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Architecture">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Azerbaijani architecture typically joins <a href="/wiki/Eastern_world" title="Eastern world">East</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Western culture">West</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Many ancient treasures such as the <a href="/wiki/Maiden_Tower_(Baku)" title="Maiden Tower (Baku)">Maiden Tower</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_the_Shirvanshahs" title="Palace of the Shirvanshahs">Palace of the Shirvanshahs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Walled City of Baku</a> survive in modern Azerbaijan. Plans have been shown for the building of the Azerbaijan Tower. It will reportedly replace the Burj Khalifa as the tallest building in the world. The planned height is 1,050 metres (3,440&nbsp;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Movies">Movies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Movies">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Movies">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The movie industry in Azerbaijan dates back to 1898. In fact, Azerbaijan was among the first countries involved in making movies.<sup id="cite_ref-mishon_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mishon-25">[25]</a></sup> In 1991, after Azerbaijan gained its freedom from the Soviet Union, the first Baku International Film Festival East-West was held in Baku. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food">Food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Food">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Food">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The traditional food is famous for many vegetables and greens used seasonally in the dishes. Fresh herbs, including mint, cilantro (coriander), dill, basil, parsley, tarragon, leeks, chives, thyme, marjoram, green onion, and watercress, are very popular. They are often served with main dishes on the table. National dishes show the variety of the landscape. They are based on fish from the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a>, local meat (mainly mutton and beef), and the many seasonal vegetables and greens. Saffron-rice <a href="/w/index.php?title=Plov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plov (not yet started)">plov</a> is the flagship food in Azerbaijan and <a href="/wiki/Black_tea" title="Black tea">black tea</a> is the national beverage.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">Literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Literature">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Literature">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The earliest known person in Azerbaijani literature was <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hasanoghlu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hasanoghlu (not yet started)">Hasanoghlu</a> or Pur Hasan Asfaraini. He made a <a href="/wiki/Diwan_(poetry)" title="Diwan (poetry)">divan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Turkic language">Turkic</a> <a href="/wiki/Ghazal" title="Ghazal">ghazals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-beale_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beale-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-caferoglu_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-caferoglu-28">[28]</a></sup> Classical literature in Azerbaijani was formed in 14th century. Among the poets of this period were <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gazi_Burhanaddin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gazi Burhanaddin (not yet started)">Gazi Burhanaddin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jahan_Shah" title="Jahan Shah">Haqiqi</a>. The famed <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Dede_Korkut" title="Book of Dede Korkut">Book of Dede Korkut</a> has two manuscripts copied in the 16th century.<sup id="cite_ref-Meeker_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meeker-29">[29]</a></sup> It is a collection of 12 stories showing the oral tradition of Oghuz nomads.<sup id="cite_ref-IranicaOguzKhan_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IranicaOguzKhan-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In the span of the 17th century and 18th century, Fizuli's unique types as well <a href="/wiki/Ashik" title="Ashik">Ashik</a> poetry were taken up by poets and writers such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Qovsi_of_Tabriz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Qovsi of Tabriz (not yet started)">Qovsi of Tabriz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abbas_II_of_Persia" title="Abbas II of Persia">Shah Abbas Sani</a>. </p>
      <p>The first newspaper in Azerbaijani, <i><a href="/wiki/Akinchi" title="Akinchi">Akinchi</a></i> was published in 1875. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Sports">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Azerbaijan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Change section: Sports">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Sport in Azerbaijan is very old. Even now, both traditional and modern sports are still practiced. <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_wrestling" class="mw-redirect" title="Freestyle wrestling">Freestyle wrestling</a> has been traditionally said to be Azerbaijan's <a href="/wiki/National_sport" title="National sport">national sport</a>. The most popular sports in Azerbaijan are <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan_national_football_team" title="Azerbaijan national football team">national football team</a> does not do well in international competitions. On March 19, 2010, Azerbaijan won the bid to host the <a href="/wiki/2012_FIFA_U-17_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2012 FIFA U-17 Women's World Cup">2012 FIFA U-17 Women's World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Futsal" title="Futsal">Futsal</a> is another popular sport in Azerbaijan. <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan_national_futsal_team" title="Azerbaijan national futsal team">Azerbaijan national futsal team</a> got fourth place in <a href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_Futsal_Championship" title="2010 UEFA Futsal Championship">2010 UEFA Futsal Championship</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Backgammon" title="Backgammon">Backgammon</a> plays a major role in Azerbaijani culture.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> This game is very popular in Azerbaijan and is widely played by the local public.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> </p>

      </div> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> of Azerbaijan is based on <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, and on <a href="/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a> including <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/sector" class="extiw" title="wikt:sector">sector</a> based on the large <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/reserve" class="extiw" title="wikt:reserve">reserves</a> of <a href="/wiki/Crude_oil" class="mw-redirect" title="Crude oil">crude oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>, is the main source of <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a> in Azerbaijan today, though half of the Azerbaijani people earn their <a href="/wiki/Income" title="Income">income</a> directly or indirectly through services and a third earn their income through agriculture.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-40">[40]</a></sup> The energy <a href="/wiki/Boom" class="mw-disambig" title="Boom">boom</a> has led to massive <a href="/wiki/Foreign_direct_investment" title="Foreign direct investment">foreign direct investment</a> and the growth rate of the Azerbaijani economy is one of the world's highest.<sup id="cite_ref-LOC_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LOC-41">[41]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>After gaining independence in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> with the end of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, Azerbaijan made the long and difficult change from a <a href="/wiki/Command_economy" title="Command economy">command economy</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">market economy</a>. The government has largely completed <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" class="mw-redirect" title="Agriculture">agricultural lands</a> and small, medium and large <a href="/wiki/State" title="State">state</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ownership" title="Ownership">owned</a> <a href="/wiki/Company" title="Company">companies</a>. Azerbaijan is continuing making economic <a href="/wiki/Reform" title="Reform">reforms</a>, and old economic ties and structures have been slowly replaced. With independence, Azerbaijan became a member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a>, the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Bank_for_Reconstruction_and_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (not yet started)">European Bank for Reconstruction and Development</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Islamic_Development_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Islamic Development Bank (not yet started)">Islamic Development Bank</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Development_Bank" title="Asian Development Bank">Asian Development Bank</a>. Azerbaijan's <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_manat" title="Azerbaijani manat">Azerbaijani manat</a> (AZN) which is divided into 100 <a href="/wiki/Q%C9%99pik" class="mw-redirect" title="Qəpik">qəpik</a>. It became the national currency in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a> and replaced the old <a href="/wiki/Soviet_ruble" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet ruble">Soviet ruble</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Central_Bank_of_Azerbaijan" title="Central Bank of Azerbaijan">Central Bank of Azerbaijan</a> was created in 1992. The Central Bank serves as Azerbaijan's <a href="/wiki/Central_bank" title="Central bank">central bank</a>, and is responsible for <a href="/wiki/Printing" title="Printing">printing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Distribution" class="mw-disambig" title="Distribution">distributing</a> the national currency, the Azerbaijani manat, and to control all <a href="/wiki/Commercial" class="mw-disambig" title="Commercial">commercial</a> <a href="/wiki/Bank" title="Bank">banks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AZE_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AZE-42">[42]</a></sup> </p>
