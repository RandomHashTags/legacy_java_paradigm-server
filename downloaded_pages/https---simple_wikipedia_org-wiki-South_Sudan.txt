      <p><b>South Sudan</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Its official name is the <b>Republic of South Sudan</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-8">[8]</a></sup> It used to be a part of <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>. A civil war began in 2013. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Landlocked" class="mw-redirect" title="Landlocked">landlocked</a> country is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> to the east; <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> to the southeast; <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> to the south; the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> to the southwest; the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> to the west; and the Republic of Sudan to the north. South Sudan includes the vast swamp region of the Sudd formed by the <a href="/wiki/White_Nile" class="mw-redirect" title="White Nile">White Nile</a>, locally called the <i>Bahr al Jabal</i>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Sudan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Sudan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>What is now South Sudan was once part of the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Egyptian_Sudan" title="Anglo-Egyptian Sudan">Anglo-Egyptian Sudan</a>. This part of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> became the Republic of Sudan when independence was achieved in 1956. After the First Sudanese Civil War, the Southern Sudan Autonomous Region was formed in 1972 and lasted until 1983. A second Sudanese civil war soon developed and ended with the Comprehensive Peace Agreement of 2005. Later that year, the Autonomous Government of Southern Sudan was formed. South Sudan became an independent state on 9 July 2011 at midnight local time,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> after a <a href="/wiki/Referendum" class="mw-redirect" title="Referendum">referendum</a> held in January 2011. In the referendum, nearly 99% of voters wanted to separate from the rest of Sudan.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The United Nations Security Council met on 13 July 2011 to formally discuss membership for the Republic of South Sudan. The next day, 14 July 2011, South Sudan became a United Nations member state.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> South Sudan has also applied to join the Commonwealth of Nations,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> the East African Community,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> the Intergovernmental Authority on Development,<sup id="cite_ref-sudantribune1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sudantribune1-17">[17]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The country was declared eligible to apply for membership in the Arab League as well.<sup id="cite_ref-sdntribuneArabLg_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sdntribuneArabLg-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>At the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, one athlete from South Sudan competed under the flag of the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2013 a civil war broke out. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Sudan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Sudan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Juba" title="Juba">Juba</a>. Between eight and twelve million people live there. Over 200 languages are spoken, but the official language is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> is also spoken by many people. </p>
      <p>The main religion is <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>, practised by nearly 78% of the population. Another 20% practise <a href="/wiki/African_traditional_religions" class="mw-redirect" title="African traditional religions">African traditional religions</a>, and just 3% are <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Muslim</a>. </p>
      <p>Much of South Sudan's economy is based on <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a>, but they also have a large <a href="/wiki/Lumber" title="Lumber">lumber</a> industry mainly consisting of <a href="/wiki/Teak" title="Teak">teak</a>. It is a very poor and under-developed country. There is very little <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a>, and the civil wars have caused a lot of damage. </p>
