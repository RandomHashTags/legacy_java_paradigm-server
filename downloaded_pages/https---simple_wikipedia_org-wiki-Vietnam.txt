      <p><b>Vietnam</b> (<a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>: <i lang="vi">Cộng hòa Xã hội chủ nghĩa Việt Nam</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in Southeast Asia. The long-form name of the country is the <b>Socialist Republic of Vietnam</b>. The neighboring countries of Vietnam are China, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>. Vietnam is one of five remaining countries that believe in <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communism</a>. The capital of Vietnam is <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Hanoi</a>. The biggest city is <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a> (formerly <a href="/wiki/Saigon" class="mw-redirect" title="Saigon">Saigon</a>). There are about 94,444,200 people living in Vietnam. </p>
      <p>After the Japanese occupation in the 1940s, the Vietnamese fought French colonial rule during the <a href="/wiki/First_Indochina_War" title="First Indochina War">First Indochina War</a> between the Viet Minh and the French in 2 September 1945. <a href="/wiki/H%E1%BB%93_Ch%C3%AD_Minh" class="mw-redirect" title="Hồ Chí Minh">Hồ Chí Minh</a> declared Vietnam's independence from France under the new name of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, but was fought by French colonialists. In 1954, the Vietnamese declared victory in Dien Bien Phu which took place between March and May 1954 and culminated in a major French defeat. Then Vietnam was divided into two political states, <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a> (officially the Democratic Republic of Vietnam) and <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a> (officially the Republic of Vietnam). Conflicts between the two sides intensified in the so-called <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a> with strong influence from the US in South Vietnam. The war ended in 1975 with a North Vietnamese victory. </p>
      <p>Vietnam was then united under a communist government. In 1986, the government launched a number of economic and political reforms that began Vietnam's path to integration into the world economy.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2004_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2004-11">[9]</a></sup> By 2000, it had established diplomatic relations with all nations. Since 2000, Vietnam's economic growth has been among the highest in the world,<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2004_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2004-11">[9]</a></sup> and in 2011 it had the highest global growth generator index among 11 major economies.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[10]</a></sup> Its successful economic reforms resulted in its becoming a member of the World Trade Organization in 2007. It is also a member of economic cooperation between Asia and the Pacific and the International de la Francophonie Organization. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population"><a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">Population</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Population">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: Population">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In Vietnam, the approximate population is 97,094,658.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup> 25.2% of these people are aged between 0-14, with 11,954,354 being male and 10,868,610 being female. 69.3% of the population are between the ages of 15-64. The male-to-female ratio is almost evenly split, with 31,301,879 being male and 31,419,306 being female. 5.5% are 65 and over, with 1,921,652 being male and 3,092,589 being female. So within the older two categories, there are more women than men.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[13]</a></sup> <br> The population is not from one origin. There are many ethnic tribes that developed in the history of Vietnam. This makes Vietnam's history and culture very diverse. It's not the same as a country where every family landed on the country's shores in the same century. French and Chinese colonization didn't involve an excessive migration of people to Vietnam. <br> Nowadays, the blend of cultures has been increasing with the influence of globalization and world interest. Many Vietnamese that have been living overseas are described as the Viet Kieu. The population has several communities in many countries around the world. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Geography">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: Geography">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The length of the country, from North to South, is 1,650&nbsp;kilometers (1,025&nbsp;miles).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[14]</a></sup> "At its <a href="/w/index.php?title=Narrow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Narrow (not yet started)">narrowest</a> point, Vietnam is only 30&nbsp;miles (48&nbsp;kilometers) wide".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[15]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The country is covered in <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforests</a> that are currently going through rapid <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>. It borders the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a> to the west, and China to the north. The country is slightly larger than <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.The country is slightly smaller than <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: History">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: History">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Vietnam's history has long been characterized by the neighborhood of China in the north. For about 1,000 years, northern Vietnam belonged to China, but from 938 the country became independent and later expanded southward at the expense of the Champa kingdom. In the 19th century the country was colonized by France and during the Second World War, the country was occupied by Japan. After this war, the colonial empire did not have the resources to restore the regime and lost the military battle against the liberation forces. This led to the division of the country, which in turn led to the Vietnam War with major human and material losses for the country. The war ended on 30 April 1975 by the fact that <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a> took the southern part. After experimental planning in the 1970s and 1980s, the economy was reformed in a market economy direction. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Viet">Viet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Viet">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Viet">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>About 5000 years ago, the two ethnic tribes of the Lac Viet and Au Viet lived together in many areas with other inhabitants. Due to increasing needs to control floods, fights against invaders, and culture and trade exchanges, these tribes living near each other tended to gather together and integrate into a larger mixed group. </p>
      <p>Among these Lac Viet tribes was the Van Lang, which was the most powerful tribe. The leader of this tribe later joined all the tribes together to found Van Lang Nation in 2897&nbsp;BC, addressing himself as the King Hung. The next generations followed in their father's footsteps and kept this appellation. Based on historical documents, researchers correlatively delineated the location of Van Lang Nation to the present day regions of North and north of Central Vietnam, as well as the south of present-day Kwangsi (China). The Van Lang Nation lasted to the 3rd century B.C. </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%93c_Eo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Óc Eo (not yet started)">Óc Eo</a> may have been a busy port of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom" title="Kingdom">kingdom</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Funan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Funan (not yet started)">Funan</a> between the 1st and 7th centuries. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dong_Son_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dong Son culture (not yet started)">Dong Son civilization</a> that covered much of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> was also the beginning of Vietnam's history. In 221&nbsp;BC, the <a href="/wiki/Qin_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qin Dynasty">Qins</a> invaded the land of the Viet tribes. Thuc Phan, leader of the alliance of Au-Viet tribes managed to expel the enemies and declared himself King An Duong Vuong and his territory Au Lac Nation (257-207&nbsp;BC). In <a href="/w/index.php?title=208_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="208 BC (not yet started)">208 BC</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Qin_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qin Dynasty">Qin Dynasty</a> general named <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zhao_Tuo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zhao Tuo (not yet started)">Triệu Đà</a> invaded Au Lac. An Duong Vuong failed this time. As a result, the northern feudalist took turns dominating the country over the next eleven centuries, establishing their harsh regime in the country and dividing the country into administrative regions and districts with unfamiliar names. However, the country's name of Au Lac could not be erased from the people's minds in their everyday life. </p>
      <p>In <a href="/w/index.php?title=207_BC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="207 BC (not yet started)">207 BC</a> Triệu Đà established a state called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nam_Vi%E1%BB%87t&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nam Việt (not yet started)">Nam Việt</a> which encompassed southern China and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_River_Delta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Red River Delta (not yet started)">Red River Delta</a>. The historical significance of the original Nam Việt remains controversial because some historians consider it a Chinese occupation while others believe it was an independent era. For most of the period from <a href="/w/index.php?title=111_BCE&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="111 BCE (not yet started)">111 BCE</a> to the early 10th century, Vietnam was under the rule of successive <a href="/wiki/Chinese_dynasties" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese dynasties">Chinese dynasties</a>. Sporadic independence movements were attempted, but were quickly suppressed by Chinese forces. </p>
      <p>The kings of <a href="/wiki/Champa" title="Champa">Champa</a> (Chiêm Thành in Vietnamese) started <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hindu_temple" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu temple">Hindu temples</a> at <a href="/w/index.php?title=M%E1%BB%B9_S%C6%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mỹ Sơn (not yet started)">Mỹ Sơn</a> in the 4th century AD.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%E1%BB%99i_An&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hội An (not yet started)">Hội An</a> was founded as a trading port by the Nguyễn Lord <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Ho%C3%A0ng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nguyễn Hoàng (not yet started)">Nguyễn Hoàng</a> sometime around 1595. </p>
      <p>Work on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Imperial_City,_Hu%E1%BA%BF&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Imperial City, Huế (not yet started)">Imperial City, Huế</a> started in 1804. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IndoChina">IndoChina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: IndoChina">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: IndoChina">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">

      <p>In September 1858, France occupied Đà Nẵng. Cochinchina was a French colony from 1862 to 1948. </p>
      <p>In 1930 <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh" title="Ho Chi Minh">Nguyễn Ái Quốc</a> established the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_Independence_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Vietnamese Independence League">Vietnamese Independence League</a> (<i>Việt Nam Ðộc Lập Ðồng Minh Hội</i>) which is also known as the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_Independence_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Vietnamese Independence League">Việt Minh</a>. </p>
      <p>The Japanese took over Vietnam in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The Việt Minh fought against both the Japanese and the <a href="/wiki/Vichy" title="Vichy">Vichy</a> French. </p>
      <p>When the Japanese were defeated, the Vietnamese people, led by the Việt Minh started the <a href="/wiki/August_Revolution" title="August Revolution">August Revolution</a>. </p>
      <p>On 2 September 1945, Nguyễn Ái Quốc (who was now calling himself Hồ Chí Minh, meaning 'Hồ (a common last name) with the will of light') read the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Vietnamese Declaration of Independence">Declaration of Independence of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ba_%C3%90%C3%ACnh_Square" title="Ba Ðình Square">Ba Ðình Square</a>, in Hànội. It was based on the American Declaration of Independence. </p>
      <p>Hồ Chí Minh led the Việt Minh in a war for independence from France. </p>
      <p>The "Autonomous Republic of Cochinchina" (<i>République Autonome de Cochinchine</i>) was proclaimed 1 June 1946 to frustrate the Việt Minh's desire to rule all of Vietnam. </p>
      <p>The War between France and the Việt Minh <a href="https://simple.wiktionary.org/wiki/lasted" class="extiw" title="wikt:lasted">lasted</a> from 1946 to 1954. The French were defeated in 1954 after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dien_Bien_Phu" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Dien Bien Phu">Battle of Dien Bien Phu</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_and_South_Vietnam">North and South Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: North and South Vietnam">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: North and South Vietnam">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The nation was then divided into <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a>. After independence was achieved, the French gave the land of the <a href="/wiki/Mekong_River" title="Mekong River">Mekong</a> delta that was part of <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a> to <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a>. The anti-communist United States had a lot of influence in the South, and the communist and nationalist Việt Minh controlled the North. Hồ Chí Minh was extremely popular in the whole nation, as he was the only remaining leader after years of fighting, so he became <a href="/wiki/President_of_Vietnam" title="President of Vietnam">President</a> of the Democratic Republic of (North) Việtnam. It was agreed that the nation would be reunited by elections in 1956. But, the Americans and the Southern government stopped the elections from happening because they expected Hồ Chí Minh to win because communist North Vietnam refused to hold free elections. Dwight Eisenhower said he thought Hồ would win with around 80% of the vote if elections were held because of the majority of the population being in the north added with Ho's few supporters in the South.<sup id="cite_ref-nixon_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nixon-20">[18]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Soon, the USA was at war with Vietnam. This war was known as the American War, the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, or the Second Indochinese War. Soon, South Vietnam became a military dictatorship with some basic freedoms. The Southern army removed the controversial<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ngo_Dinh_Diem" title="Ngo Dinh Diem">Ngo Dinh Diem</a> from power and killed him. </p>

      <p>On 2 September 1969, Independence Day, President Hồ Chí Minh died of heart failure. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unification">Unification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Change section: Unification">change</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Unification">change source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>On 30 April 1975, the National Liberation Front with the help of the N.V.A.<sup id="cite_ref-nixon_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nixon-20">[18]</a></sup> overtook Sàigòn and quickly renamed it Hồ Chí Minh City, which is the capital of Vietnam. The nation was fully reunified as Socialist Republic of Vietnam on 2 July 1976. </p>
